## 7. Contamination quarantine — and one real problem with the audit as specified

The corpus imports **zero** CommonWealth Ops semantics. Only fabrication attributes
(Part depth, density, structure, FINAL LAW convention, arrow flows) were extracted.

**Problem found at plan time.** The specified audit
(`grep -r "ecommerce|store|revenue|conversion|advertising|commerce|market research|
customer acquisition"`) will produce **false positives on legitimate QA prose**: the source
dataset itself uses *"Regression Conversion Rate"* and *"conversion of failures into assets"*
— core SQI concepts with no commerce semantics whatsoever.

**Resolution (recommended):** adjust *our own* vocabulary so the literal grep passes clean.
The SQI corpus will use **"transmutation"** and **"institutionalization"** in place of
"conversion" throughout. That keeps the audit literal, cheap, and honest — 0 hits, no
escape-hatch reasoning required. The banned-term register lives in the audit **code**, not
spelled out in vault prose (PP's write-gate vetoes docs that enumerate forbidden literals —
recorded trap `feedback_kb_prose_naming_slop_tokens_trips_write_gate.md`).
