**Date:** 2026-06-12 · **Subject firm:** costaluzlawyers.com (María Luisa de Castro, ICA Cádiz 2745) · **Scope:** State of AI-search visibility for cross-border Spanish legal services (off-plan property buyer protection + immigration), the citation mechanics that govern it in mid-2026, the regulatory overlay, and a prioritized action set for the next round.

## 1. Executive Summary

The center of gravity in legal-services discovery has moved off the blue-link SERP and into AI answer engines. Three findings dominate this round's research and should drive R96 decisions:

1. **Traditional SEO is now a prerequisite, not the prize.** Roughly half (one source: 76%+) of Google AI Overview citations come from pages already in the organic top 10, and Google AIO requires page-one ranking before it will cite. But for **ChatGPT — which delivers 87.4% of all AI referral traffic — Google's top-10 mirrors less than 25% of the time on legal queries.** Page-1 Google does *not* transfer to ChatGPT citation for the majority of legal queries. This is the single most strategically important asymmetry of the round.

2. **The highest-leverage, least-saturated lever for CostaLuz is off-site presence.** Off-site mentions show a **0.67 correlation** with appearing in AI results — the strongest single factor measured — and **seven directories (Chambers, Legal 500, Super Lawyers, Best Lawyers, Martindale-Hubbell, Avvo, Justia) functionally own the legal AI citation layer.** Martindale-Avvo narrows the ChatGPT-specific set to **Super Lawyers, Avvo, Martindale-Hubbell, FindLaw.** Given the project's R51 saturation note (on-page content is done), the next real gains are off-page: directory placement, consistent NAP, reviews, and Knowledge Graph/Wikidata.

3. **CostaLuz is well-positioned but under-leveraged in AI's eyes.** It is ranked #6 in Movingto's "15 best immigration law firms in Spain 2026," carries strong E-E-A-T (2012 landmark anti-bank judgment, Embassy listings, Telegraph/FT/BBC/Forbes features, Google 4.8★, 620+ successful Ley 57/1968 claims), and is now anchored in Wikidata (**Q140185591**, created R91). The gap is not credibility — it is *machine-extractable, cross-directory consistency* of that credibility.

**Bottom line for R96:** the on-page GEO work has hit diminishing returns. The defensible next moves are (a) directory/NAP consistency across the ChatGPT-citation-owning platforms, (b) review-signal hygiene to clear AI quality thresholds, (c) Knowledge Graph reinforcement, and (d) jurisdictional-specificity audits of existing content. These are off-page and structural — exactly the levers the saturation memo leaves open.

## 3. How AI Engines Select and Cite Legal Content

### 3.1 The traditional-SEO prerequisite (and its limits)

- ~50% (one source 76%+) of Google AIO citations come from the **organic top 10.**
- **Off-site mentions: 0.67 correlation** with AI appearance — the strongest single factor.
- Google AIO requires **page-one ranking first**; Perplexity crawls **near real-time** and does not.

### 3.2 The engine-by-engine mirroring asymmetry (the round's key insight)

Martindale-Avvo's measurement of how closely each engine mirrors Google's top-10 organic on legal queries:

| Engine | Mirrors Google top-10 | Implication for CostaLuz |
|---|---|---|
| **ChatGPT** | **<25%** | Page-1 Google ≠ ChatGPT citation. Off-page + directories drive this. |
| Perplexity | ~75% | Classic SEO + freshness transfers well. |
| Claude | ~75% | Classic SEO transfers. |
| Gemini | ~50% | Mixed. |

Because ChatGPT owns 87.4% of AI referrals *and* mirrors Google least, **the dominant traffic engine is precisely the one least reachable through traditional SEO.** It is reached through extractable factual claims, NAP consistency, directory presence, review thresholds, and Knowledge Graph/Wikidata — not keyword density or directory rhetoric.

### 3.3 What AI selection actually rewards

- **Extractable factual claims**: bar admissions, JD/graduation year, jurisdiction-specific statute citations (e.g. "California Code of Civil Procedure §335.1" — for CostaLuz the analogues are "Ley 57/1968," "Article 1.2," "Article 1964 CC," "STS 443/2026").
- **Consistent NAP** across all directories.
- **Review thresholds** (SOCi 2026 LVI): AI favors **≥4.3★ ratings** and **≥5% review-response rate.** CostaLuz's 4.8★ clears the rating bar; response-rate hygiene should be verified.
- **Knowledge Graph / Wikidata presence** — now satisfied via Q140185591.
- **Named author attribution and named-source statistics** (§4).
- AI engines **de-prioritize machine-translated/Google-Translate content** — authentic original-language (Spanish *and* English, separately authored) depth is the new ranking lever. CostaLuz's English/Spanish-only, human-reviewed model is aligned with this.

### 3.4 The directory citation layer

5WPR & Haute Lawyer Network 2026 (tested ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Google AI Mode, pub. 29 Apr 2026):

- **Seven directories functionally own the legal AI citation layer**: Chambers, Legal 500, Super Lawyers, Best Lawyers, Martindale-Hubbell, Avvo, Justia.
- Even elite firms (Cravath, Kirkland, Skadden) appear *inside* directories, rarely as independent voices.
- **Zero law-focused editorial sources** appeared in top results for any tested query.
- ChatGPT-specific top four (Martindale-Avvo internal 2025–26): **Super Lawyers, Avvo, Martindale-Hubbell, FindLaw.**
- For UK/expat-facing immigration/property, add the legal directories the research flags as competitor anchors: **Avvo, Martindale-Hubbell, Justia, FindLaw, Super Lawyers.**

**Action implication:** CostaLuz's AI visibility is gated less by its own site (saturated) and more by its presence and NAP consistency on these directories. This is the round's clearest unsaturated lever.

### 3.5 Bot-access audit (silent blocking risk)

CDN/WAF bot-protection (Cloudflare Bot Fight Mode, Sucuri, Wordfence, DataDome) can **silently block GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, OAI-SearchBot, Google-Extended before robots.txt is ever consulted.** For a **SiteGround/WAF-protected** site with a documented **sgcaptcha WAF blocker** (flags local IP after >15 req/min), this is a live risk. **R96 should explicitly verify these AI crawler user-agents are not WAF-blocked** — a single silent block would zero out all on-page GEO work for the dominant engines.

## 4. Content Doctrine — What Earns Citation

BrightEdge 2026 GEO Study + 5WPR/Ahrefs:

| Lever | GEO visibility lift / effect |
|---|---|
| Named author attribution | **+41%** |
| Named-source statistics | **+30%** |
| Inline citations with hyperlinked sources | **+30%** |
| Jurisdictional specificity | Clearest cited/uncited differentiator |
| "Laws vary by state/country" vagueness | **Single most reliable AI-citation disqualifier** |

Structural rules:

- **Direct answers in the first 40–60 words** of each section.
- **FAQ sections of 8–12 questions, 40–80 word answers, exact-match FAQPage schema.**
- Headings phrased as **client questions**; answers ≤60 words.
- **Author E-E-A-T high in Article schema** (credential + bar admission in the author field).

**Tension with CostaLuz authorship rule:** the firm's confirmed policy is **no individual byline** — only "Reviewed by María Luisa de Castro, Expert in Off-plan Property Investment." The +41% named-author lift is therefore captured through the *reviewer* attribution and the corporate entity, not a personal byline. This is a deliberate, client-sealed constraint; do not "fix" it by adding bylines. Maximize the lever within the rule by ensuring María Luisa's credential (ICA Cádiz 2745, off-plan expertise, 620+ claims) appears as structured, machine-extractable reviewer attribution.

CostaLuz already structures content with explicit **AI-disclosure** ("prepared with assistance of generative AI, edited/reviewed by María Luisa"), FAQ schema, and **policy-verification stamps** (R11-POLICY-VERIFIED-2026-06-03) — aligned with best practice.

### Recommended schema stack (legal)

Organization, **LegalService (with areaServed)**, Service/makesOffer, FAQPage/QAPage, **Attorney/Person**, LocalBusiness, Review, BreadcrumbList, HowTo. Note the documented Costaluz-install limitation: **`_yoast_wpseo_canonical` and Yoast canonical are NOT REST-writable** (silently dropped on POST 200) — only title + meta are. Schema injection must route accordingly.

## 8. Regulatory Overlay (Immigration / Cross-Border AI)

This matters both as **content opportunity** (timely, citable explainers) and as **operational compliance** if CostaLuz deploys any client-facing AI.

### 8.1 EU AI Act (Reg. (EU) 2024/1689)

- Migration/asylum/border AI (risk assessment, asylum/visa/residence examination, biometric ID) = **high-risk under Annex III.**
- **Article 6(3)** permits provider self-assessment to exempt out; internal-control conformity assessment (no notified body); systems registered in a **"secure non-public section"** of the EU database (Art. 49) — training data and FRIAs need not be entered at all.
- **High-risk obligations enforceable 2 August 2026** (imminent). Fines up to **€35M or 7% of global turnover.**
- **Article 111** grants legacy IT (SIS, VIS, Eurodac, EES, ETIAS, ECRIS-TCN) until **31 Dec 2030.**
- "Administration of justice," legal research, and document analysis for judicial matters are also covered.
- EU Commission high-risk clarifying guidelines were **due 2 Feb 2026.**

### 8.2 Stacked regimes for cross-border legal AI

- **GDPR**: extraterritorial; DPIA (Art. 35), automated-decision rights (Art. 22), SCCs + Schrems II Transfer Impact Assessments.
- **US state patchwork**: Colorado AI Act (SB 24-205, delayed to **30 June 2026**, covers legal services as "consequential decisions"); Texas TRAIGA (eff. **1 Jan 2026**); California AB 2013 / SB 942.

### 8.3 ETIAS — live, citable, contested

- **ETIAS takes effect end of 2026.**
- Under **CJEU challenge**: Belgian case (Ligue des Droits Humains, lawyer Catherine Forget) referred Dec 2025 argues ETIAS's broad/ill-defined "risk" definition is "disproportionate interference" with migrants' fundamental rights ("crimmigration"); CJEU could rule within the year.
- **UK's renewed adequacy decision (19 Dec 2025) excludes immigration-control data transfers** — a domain-specific regulatory carve-out.

**Content opportunity:** ETIAS is a high-volume, imminent, question-shaped immigration topic with a live legal controversy — ideal answer-first GEO content, framed in the high-trigger immigration vertical. **Caution:** stay strictly within CostaLuz's scope and the no-invented-data rule; cite the case and dates as facts, do not editorialize the CJEU outcome.
