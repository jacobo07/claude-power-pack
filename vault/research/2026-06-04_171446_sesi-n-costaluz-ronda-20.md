## 1. Executive Summary

The strategic environment has crossed a structural threshold: AI-mediated search is no longer an emerging channel but a **material share of high-intent legal demand**. AI Overviews now render on **~20.5% of all SERPs** and **23.6% of legal/YMYL queries** — and almost the entirety of CostaLuz's demand surface (off-plan legal protection, bank-guarantee recovery, buying-process questions) is *informational, question-shaped, long-tail* intent, which is precisely the trigger profile for AI answers (informational intent triggers AIOs 99.9% of the time; question-style 57.9%; 7+ word queries 46.4%).

The R19 pivot was correctly diagnosed. CostaLuz's bottleneck is **not more content** — it is **entity authority and citation-worthiness**. The evidence base shows the highest-leverage moves now are: (1) becoming a **disambiguated Knowledge-Graph entity** (Wikidata + consistent NAP + `sameAs`), (2) restructuring existing content to be **extraction-friendly** (answer-first 40–60 words, statistics, quotations, citations, question-phrased H2s), and (3) instrumenting **AI-channel measurement** so R20 onward is governed by observed citation/referral data, not assumption.

**The three statistically strongest GEO levers, all directly applicable to CostaLuz:**

| Lever | Measured uplift | CostaLuz fit |
|---|---|---|
| Add **citations** (statutes, rulings, official sources) | **+41%** (BrightEdge 2026) / +30–40% (Princeton) | Ley 20/2015, STS 21-Dec-2015, ITP rates — natively citable |
| Add **statistics** | **+30%** visibility (+22% per Princeton) | Buying-cost %, tax rates, deadlines |
| Add **quotations** | **+32%** (+37% per Princeton) | Quote the law verbatim; quote María Luisa |

Critically, the research shows **lower-SERP-ranked sites benefit MORE from GEO than top-ranked ones**, and **keyword stuffing is penalized** — meaning CostaLuz can win AI citations on long-tail off-plan queries *before* winning the classic organic #1, provided the content is structured for extraction.

## 4. Cross-Platform Citation Architecture (Citation Overlap Is Low — Plan Per-Engine)

The critical, counter-intuitive finding: **the engines barely agree on who to cite.** Optimisation cannot be monolithic.

- Only **~11% of domains are cited by both ChatGPT and Perplexity** for similar prompts (Profound/Digital Bloom, 680M+ citations).
- Google **AI Overviews vs AI Mode share only 13.7% of cited domains** despite **86% semantic similarity** — same question, different sources.

**Source-preference profiles diverge sharply:**

| Engine | Favoured sources | Strategic lever for CostaLuz |
|---|---|---|
| **ChatGPT** | Wikipedia **47.9%** of top-10 citations; Reddit 11–13%; YouTube 3–8.6% | **Wikidata→Wikipedia/Knowledge-Graph entity** is decisive; needs Bing visibility (browse mode) |
| **Perplexity** | Reddit 24–46.7% (declining); recency-weighted | Freshness + parse-ability; consider community/Q&A presence |
| **Google AIO / Gemini** | YouTube 18.8–23.3%; Google self-citations **17.42%** (tripled) | Page-1 organic + GBP + possibly video |
| **Claude** | **~70% brand/owned-site dependent** | Strong, well-structured owned content cited directly |

**R20 takeaway:** the ChatGPT path runs *through entity/Wikipedia presence and Bing*, the Google AIO path runs *through page-1 organic + Google's own properties*, and the Claude path rewards *clean owned content*. The Wikidata/entity work (R19) is the **single move that most directly unlocks the largest engine (ChatGPT, 47.9% Wikipedia-weighted)** — validating the pivot.

## 5. Citation-Accuracy Risk (Why E-E-A-T and Verbatim Law Matter)

The Columbia Journalism Review / Tow Center benchmark (March 2025, 1,600 queries, 8 tools, 200 articles, 20 publishers) found:

- **Collective citation-error rate >60%.** Best: Perplexity **37%**; ChatGPT Search **67%**; Grok-3 **94%**.
- Tools "presented inaccurate answers with **alarming confidence**."
- ChatGPT **base mode (~60% of queries) cites no external source in up to 82% of responses**, with **18–55% source-fabrication rates**.
- **<10 distinct URLs account for ~80% of citations per query** — winner-take-most concentration.

**Caveat (directional):** these predate GPT-5.5 (launched **2026-04-23**) and Perplexity Sonar Reasoning Pro; accuracy is presumably improving.

**Two implications for a YMYL legal firm:**
1. **Concentration is opportunity:** because <10 URLs carry ~80% of citations, *becoming one of those URLs* on a narrow off-plan topic yields outsized, durable share.
2. **Accuracy is liability AND moat:** in a field where AIs hallucinate law, CostaLuz's verbatim-statute, correctly-cited content is both more citation-worthy and a brand-trust differentiator. Every legal claim must be exact (Ley 20/2015 DA1ª, STS 21-Dec-2015, 30-day window) — errors propagate and damage authority.

## 6. Crawling & Retrieval Mechanics (Engine-Specific Technical Levers)

| Engine | Retrieval mechanism | Technical requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **ChatGPT (browse)** | Queries **Bing** — 87% of SearchGPT citations match Bing top-10 (vs 56% Google), Seer 500+ study. Weighting ≈ domain authority **40%** / content quality **35%** / trust **25%** (ZipTie.dev) | Allow **OAI-SearchBot** + **GPTBot** in robots.txt; build **Bing** organic presence |
| **Perplexity** | Live **RAG over 200B+ URL index every query** (~780M monthly queries, zero ads since 2026). Scores semantic clarity, **freshness**, parse-ability, entity authority | Clean parse-able HTML, fresh dates, strong entity signals |
| **Google AIO (Gemini 3, since Jan 2026)** | **Fan-out sub-queries**; 92% of citations from top-10-ranking domains but only **4.5% match the exact page-1 URL**; domain-authority correlation dropped to **r=0.18** (from 0.23) | Win page-1 *organic* first (AIO citation requires it), then structure for sub-query extraction |

**Key nuance:** Google AIO citations **require page-1 organic ranking as a precondition** but the *specific URL cited is rarely the #1 URL* (4.5% exact match) — fan-out pulls the best-matching passage from any top-10 domain. So CostaLuz needs *both* organic page-1 presence *and* passage-level answer capsules that the fan-out can lift.

**robots.txt action for R20:** verify OAI-SearchBot, GPTBot, PerplexityBot, and Google-Extended are not blocked (a single misconfigured line silently zeroes out ChatGPT-channel eligibility).

## 8. Entity-Authority & Wikidata Strategy (The R19→R20 Core)

This is the highest-leverage, lowest-cost program and the spine of the authority pivot.

### 8.1 Why entity, not links
Entity SEO **replaces link-counting with NAP consistency** to consolidate a single Knowledge-Graph entity. BrightLocal: top local businesses maintain **~85 accurate citations**. Even **"Suite 200" vs "Ste 200" splits a firm into two entities** and dilutes authority. Moz/Whitespark rank citation accuracy among the strongest local signals.

### 8.2 Wikidata — the keystone
- Wikidata is the **single most important entity-definition source for Google's Knowledge Graph** (~500B facts, ~5B entities).
- A basic entry can **improve Knowledge Panel appearance within 60–90 days**.
- **`sameAs`** (linking Wikidata ↔ LinkedIn ↔ Crunchbase ↔ directories) is the **primary entity-consolidation mechanism**.
- Brands with strong entity footprints see **40–60% higher AI-answer citation rates**.

**Quantified entity payoff (Kalicube 1,200-company tracking; SCALEBASE/Formative Digital):**
- Wikidata entries ingested into Google KG in **2–8 weeks**; referenced by ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, **Apple Intelligence** for disambiguation.
- Companies with entries cited **2.1× more**; effect **strongest for mid-size firms (50–500 employees)** — CostaLuz's band.
- Entries with **15+ referenced properties → Knowledge Panel 73% of the time** vs **28% for <8 properties** → **R20 target: ≥15 sourced properties**.
- **~65% of well-sourced entries approved on first submission; ~35% flagged/deleted** for promotional language, weak independent refs, or undisclosed COI.

### 8.3 Wikidata notability (the bar is LOW — but sourcing is strict)
WD:N requires **only ONE** of three: (a) a sitelink to a Wikimedia project, (b) being a "clearly identifiable entity" describable with serious public references, or (c) structural need. **Far lower than Wikipedia.** BUT: **self-published sources (own website) do NOT count** — needs government registries, news outlets, established directories.

## 11. Off-Plan Legal Demand — The Dominant Theme

This is CostaLuz's strongest demand cluster and most citable legal substance.

### 11.1 The legal evolution (cite verbatim)
- **Ley 57/1968** — original off-plan deposit protection, **systematically ignored historically**.
- **Replaced/strengthened by Ley 20/2015** (de 14 de julio, **effective January 2016**); its **Disposición Adicional Primera** absorbed and modernised the prior regime — replacing weak **aggregate/collective** guarantees with **mandatory INDIVIDUAL per-buyer** bank guarantees (aval bancario) or equivalent insurance.
- **Landmark STS 21 December 2015** shifted liability to **financial institutions**, enabling refunds of **principal + interest**.
- **Ley 20/2015** made **banks jointly liable** for deposits accepted into **unguaranteed accounts**.

### 11.2 Guarantee mechanics (the citable substance)
- Issuing **Spanish bank or DGSFP/Bank-of-Spain-licensed insurer** is **directly liable** to pay the buyer the **full paid amount (incl. IVA) + interest** within a **30-day statutory window** of a qualifying event.
- **Qualifying events:** failure to start/deliver construction, developer insolvency/bankruptcy, project termination.
- **Independent of the insolvency estate** — buyer does **not queue as a creditor** (a powerful, reassuring, conversion-driving fact).
- **Typical contractual grace period before late-delivery termination: 3–6 months.**
- Covers **every payment between reservation and notary handover + legal interest from each payment date**.

### 11.3 Why claims are contested (the service hook)
Developers issued **incomplete/collective/no guarantees**; banks **refuse to release funds without a court judgment** → requires legal action (formal notices, negotiation, litigation) — including **direct claims against the bank** for accepting funds into special accounts without ensuring guarantees existed. **This is exactly the service CostaLuz sells** and the content gap competitors monetise via No-Win-No-Fee.

### 11.4 Demand vocabulary
Bank guarantee verification · due diligence · NIE · Nota Simple · contrato de arras · escritura pública · Registro de la Propiedad.
