DAIF-02 — COGNITIVE INTERMEDIATE REPRESENTATIONS (CIR) FABRIC

DAIF-02 — COGNITIVE INTERMEDIATE REPRESENTATIONS AND THE CIR FABRIC

PART I — THESIS: COGNITIVE SOURCE CODE AND THE CIR STACK

1.1 Mission. This dataset states the law that gives the Power Pack a compiler for its own knowledge, and the law is one sentence: no conversation, dataset, decision, audit, or result may be carried forward as raw prose or as a lossy précis, because both discard exactly the structure a later consumer needs to act, and the only faithful carrier is a compilation into typed representations. The estate today moves its reasoning in two ways, and both fail differently. It re-sends the original text, which is faithful but unusable — a hundred-thousand-word transcript preserves everything and lets a consumer act on nothing, because the obligations, decisions, and exceptions inside it are undifferentiated words that no machine can select or verify. Or it shortens the text, which is usable but unfaithful — a summary reads well and silently drops the one exception that mattered, the single piece of evidence that grounded a claim, the obligation nobody closed. The fabric this dataset defines is the third way: it treats every source as cognitive source code and compiles it, converting words into representations that can be selected, linked, verified, and executed while losing nothing the source's behavior depended on.

1.2 The three verbs, sharply distinguished. The distinction the fabric turns on is mechanical, not rhetorical, and it separates three operations the estate has long confused. A summary reduces length: it maps a long text to a short one and its defining property is that the mapping is lossy in an uncontrolled direction — it keeps what the summarizer found salient and drops the rest, and what it drops is invisible, so an exception, an evidence line, or an obligation vanishes without a trace and a consumer reading the summary cannot tell that anything is gone. A distillation raises density: it maps a long text to a denser text that a human reads faster, and its defining property is that the output is still a human document — another wall of prose, better packed, but carrying the same machine-opacity as the source, because a denser sentence is still a sentence and hides the same eight fields the bare one did. A compilation converts source into structured representations: it maps text to a set of typed objects, each preserving one dimension of the source's meaning, and its defining property is that the output is selectable, linkable, verifiable, and executable — a consumer does not re-read it, it queries it. Summary loses silently, distillation stays opaque, compilation preserves and exposes.

1.3 What the compiler preserves. The cardinal commitment of the fabric is that it preserves the behavior the knowledge must provoke, not merely the words the knowledge used. A source is not valuable because of its prose; it is valuable because of what it obliges a later actor to do — enforce a constraint, honor a decision, close an obligation, distrust an assumption, reproduce a result. A faithful carrier is therefore one that reproduces those provocations at the point of use, and the test of any representation is behavioral: a consumer acting on the compiled form must do what a consumer acting on the full original would have done, and no less. This is why length is the wrong axis entirely. A ten-line compiled decision that carries its question, options, choice, justification, authority, and revision conditions provokes the right behavior — it can be revised safely — while a two-hundred-line faithful transcript of the same decision provokes nothing, because the consumer cannot find the justification in the noise. The compiler is judged on behavioral equivalence, the same standard DAIF-03 formalizes as its fidelity contract, and it is to DAIF-03 that this fabric hands its outputs to be gated.

1.4 The stack of twelve representations. The compiler's core is not one representation but a stack of twelve, each preserving a different dimension of meaning, arranged as a pipeline that lowers raw content toward executable context: raw -> semantic -> epistemic -> intent -> decision -> obligation -> causal -> dependency -> procedural -> evaluation -> institutional -> executable-context. CIR-0 Raw Source holds the normalized original. CIR-1 Semantic Unit decomposes it into atomic units of meaning. CIR-2 Epistemic types each unit's truth-status. CIR-3 Intent preserves what was actually wanted. CIR-4 Decision records the choices made and why. CIR-5 Obligation records the work still owed. CIR-6 Causal holds why things happened, CIR-7 Dependency what rests on what, CIR-8 Procedural the how, CIR-9 Evaluation the measured results, CIR-10 Institutional the promoted laws and patterns, and CIR-11 Executable-Context the assembled, ready-to-reason payload. Each stage consumes the stage beneath it and adds one dimension, so a fact acquires an epistemic status, then an intent it serves, then a decision it grounds, then an obligation it discharges — meaning accretes as the pipeline rises rather than being flattened into a single lossy layer. This batch establishes the thesis and the first six representations, CIR-0 through CIR-5, the spine on which the upper six stand.

1.5 Every CIR unit is a typed cognitive object. The fabric does not invent a new vocabulary for its slots; every unit at every stage of the stack is a DAIF-01 typed cognitive object, carrying the eight fields that dataset sealed — Type, Authority, Scope, Strength, Validity, Override-policy, Provenance, and Confidence — so that a semantic unit knows its kind, an epistemic unit knows its scope, an obligation knows its authority, and a decision knows its validity. This inheritance is what makes the stack coherent rather than a pile of ad-hoc structures: a consumer that can read a DAIF-01 object can read any CIR unit, because a CIR unit is a DAIF-01 object with the stage's dimension filled in. The representations differ in which dimension they foreground — CIR-2 foregrounds the epistemic status, CIR-4 the decision structure — but they never differ in the type discipline beneath, so a unit compiled at one stage and referenced at another is the same typed object seen from two angles, not two objects that must be reconciled. The fabric is the reasoning intermediate representation DAIF-01 Part I named as its first consumer, and it consumes the type system exactly as promised: every slot inherits its type from there and re-invents none.

1.6 Composition, never re-narration. The fabric composes the estate's sealed systems at the stages where they already hold authority and forks none of them. CIR-2's epistemic typing composes the ACIS epistemic ladder, referencing its levels as the law that keeps an inference from being lowered into a fact. CIR-3's intent structure composes one_shot's intent and Q&A scaffold, reading the six-question interrogation as the intent-capture it already performs. CIR-4's decision structure composes DRK's decision_record, treating a compiled decision as that record's typed projection. The upper stack continues the pattern — CIR-9 composes SQI's evaluation verdicts, CIR-10 composes UKDL's promoted laws — and the whole fabric hands its finished representations to DAIF-03 for the fidelity gate and to DAIF-07 for the obligation lifecycle. Each system is named and deferred to, never restated, because re-narrating a sealed engine is the ornamental duplication the constitution convicts under its duplicate-to-advantage law. The compiler's contribution is not new reasoning machinery but a common representational spine that gives those systems' outputs a typed, linkable, executable shape, so the estate stops re-sending prose between them and starts compiling it.

1.7 The fabric's place and burden. The CIR fabric is the third dataset in the DAIF spine and its representational core, because a fidelity budget cannot be spent, an obligation cannot be tracked, and a context cannot be assembled until there is a representation to spend, track, and assemble over. Everything above the type system reasons through these representations, so a gap in the stack is a gap in every consumer that reads it, and the burden on the fabric is completeness of dimension: it must preserve every dimension of meaning a source carries, because a dimension the stack drops is a behavior the compiled form fails to provoke, and a dropped behavior is the silent loss of a summary wearing the costume of a compilation. The fabric earns its name only if the compiled form is a faithful carrier under the behavioral test — if acting on the twelve representations reproduces what acting on the whole source would have — and the rest of this dataset is the specification of the representations that make that guarantee hold.

PART I FINAL LAW. Knowledge must be compiled, not re-sent as raw prose nor shortened into a lossy summary, because a summary drops exceptions and evidence silently while a distillation stays machine-opaque, and only a compilation into a stack of twelve typed intermediate representations — raw -> semantic -> epistemic -> intent -> decision -> obligation -> causal -> dependency -> procedural -> evaluation -> institutional -> executable-context, every unit a DAIF-01 typed object, composed from ACIS, one_shot, DRK, SQI, and UKDL rather than forked — preserves the behavior the knowledge must provoke instead of merely the words it used.

PART II — CIR-0 RAW SOURCE IR AND SOURCE ACQUISITION

2.1 Mission. CIR-0 is the base of the stack, and its mission is the one every compiler's front end serves: to hold the original content in a normalized form that loses nothing recoverable and interprets nothing yet. It is the representation that receives a source before any meaning is extracted from it, and its discipline is exactly the tension between two failures. Interpret too early and CIR-0 becomes a lossy pre-summary — the front end decides what matters before the semantic stage above it has run, and the decision is unaccountable. Normalize too little and CIR-0 becomes the raw wall the fabric exists to abolish — a transcript re-held verbatim, mechanical noise and all, so nothing has actually been compiled. CIR-0 threads between them by a single rule: it strips only what is mechanically redundant and retains everything a later stage might need, so that the loss it commits is provably zero on the axis of meaning and non-zero only on the axis of noise.

2.2 The object model. A CIR-0 unit is a DAIF-01 typed object whose Type is fixed to raw-source and whose foregrounded content is the normalized text of one coherent source fragment, but its value is in its metadata. It carries the source's identity — which conversation, dataset, decision record, commit, log, audit, or result it came from — its Provenance filled with the exact origin the upper stack will need to cite, its Scope inherited from where the source lives on the DAIF-01 ladder, and its Validity set to reflect whether the source is current or historical. Required metadata is the origin pointer, the acquisition timestamp, the content hash, and the normalization record — the list of mechanical transforms applied, so any consumer can prove what was removed and confirm that nothing of meaning was among it. Optional metadata is the source's own internal structure where it has one — a heading path, a turn index, a line range — retained as a locator so a later unit can point back into the exact span it was compiled from. The content hash is the load-bearing field: it is the identity by which incremental acquisition decides whether a fragment has changed.

2.3 The source types CIR-0 accepts. The front end accepts every kind of cognitive source the estate produces, and the taxonomy is deliberately wide: conversations between the Owner and an actor, datasets and their Parts, PRDs and specifications, decisions and their records, commits and their messages, logs of runs, audits and their findings, results and their measurements, and corrections — the moments an actor was told it was wrong. Each type enters CIR-0 through the same door and leaves normalized in the same shape, but the front end tags each with its source-type so the semantic stage above can apply the right boundary heuristics — a commit is decomposed differently from a conversation, an audit differently from a PRD. Corrections are the highest-value source-type and the one a naive front end most often discards as conversational noise, because a correction is where the estate's belief changed, and CIR-0 is instructed to retain every correction intact, since the epistemic stage above depends on knowing not only what is believed but what was once believed and reversed.

2.4 Normalization: what CIR-0 strips and what it must never touch. Normalization removes only mechanically redundant material, and the register of removable classes is closed: duplicate formatting, transport wrappers, irrelevant envelope metadata, and exact echoes — the verbatim re-quotation of text already present, which carries no new meaning and inflates the source. Against these four, CIR-0 removes freely, recording each removal in the normalization record. Everything else it must never touch: it never paraphrases, because paraphrase is interpretation and interpretation belongs to CIR-1; it never drops a sentence for being unimportant, because importance is a judgment the semantic and epistemic stages make with more information; it never merges two fragments, because a boundary is CIR-1's to draw. The invariant is stated as a recoverability guarantee — from a CIR-0 unit and its normalization record, the original source's meaning must be exactly reconstructible, so the only information CIR-0 is permitted to lose is information a reconstruction would not miss. A front end that cannot prove recoverability has interpreted, and interpretation at CIR-0 is the failure this stage exists to prevent.

2.5 Source acquisition and incremental ingest. CIR-0 is not populated by recompiling whole sources on every touch; it is populated incrementally, and the incremental discipline is what lets the fabric scale to an estate of thousands of sources. Acquisition fingerprints each source fragment by content hash and compares the fingerprint to the last acquired one, so a source that has not changed is not re-normalized, and a source that changed in one fragment causes only that fragment to be re-acquired. The mechanism is the same reality-supremacy discipline the estate applies everywhere — the disk is the truth, and a fragment whose hash matches its recorded hash is known-current without re-reading — and it is the direct analogue of the audit-cache the estate already runs, where a matching hash means the cached summary stands. Incremental ingest is not an optimization bolted on; it is a correctness property, because recompiling a whole source when one fragment changed would re-run the upper stack over unchanged content and risk re-deriving its units with drifted boundaries, breaking the links CIR-1 and above depend on. CIR-0 changes only where the source changed, and the stack above it re-compiles only over the changed units.

2.6 The invariant of non-interpretation and its failure modes. CIR-0's governing invariant is that it retains all recoverable content and interprets none of it, and both halves have a signature failure. The retention half fails as over-normalization: a front end that strips a fragment it judged noise but that carried meaning — a terse correction read as an echo, a metadata line that actually named the authority, a formatting mark that encoded structure — and the loss is silent because the normalization record only lists transforms the front end believed safe. The interpretation half fails as early meaning-extraction: a front end that decides a fragment is a decision, or a fact, or unimportant, before CIR-1 has drawn boundaries and CIR-2 has typed status, so the semantic stage inherits a pre-judged input and cannot re-draw it. The adversarial case against CIR-0 is a source crafted so its mechanical noise is indistinguishable from its meaning — an echo that is not quite exact, a wrapper that carries a payload — and the defense is conservative: when a fragment's classification as noise is uncertain, CIR-0 retains it, because the recoverable-content guarantee makes over-retention the safe error and over-normalization the unrecoverable one. A third failure sits between the two and is the subtlest: normalization drift, where the transforms applied to a source on one acquisition differ from those applied on the next, so two acquisitions of the same unchanged source yield two different CIR-0 units and the content hashes diverge for no real reason. Drift breaks incremental ingest at its root, because the hash is the identity by which unchanged content is recognized, and a front end whose transforms are not deterministic re-acquires everything on every pass while believing it changed. CIR-0's normalization is therefore required to be deterministic — the same source, normalized twice, yields byte-identical output and an identical hash — and the normalization record is what makes that determinism auditable rather than merely asserted.

2.7 What consumes CIR-0. CIR-0's only direct consumer is CIR-1, the semantic stage immediately above it, which reads the normalized content and its source-type tag and draws atomic boundaries over it — the whole upper stack reaches CIR-0 only through CIR-1, so CIR-0's contract is narrow and its correctness is local. But CIR-0's provenance pointers are consumed estate-wide: every unit at every higher stage carries a provenance chain that terminates in a CIR-0 locator, so that any compiled decision, obligation, or law can be traced back through the stack to the exact normalized source span it was compiled from. This is the lineage the constitution requires and the reason CIR-0 must retain its locators even for content the upper stages will heavily transform — a compiled law ten stages up is auditable only because its provenance chain reaches an unbroken CIR-0 unit at the bottom. CIR-0 is thus the fabric's ground truth: it interprets nothing, it loses nothing recoverable, and it is the anchor every higher representation cites when it must prove where its meaning came from.

PART II FINAL LAW. CIR-0 is the compiler's front end, a typed representation that receives every source type — conversations, datasets, PRDs, decisions, commits, logs, audits, results, and above all corrections — normalizes away only the four mechanically redundant classes of duplicate formatting, wrappers, irrelevant metadata, and exact echoes, and interprets nothing, under a recoverability guarantee that makes over-retention the safe error; it is populated incrementally by content hash so a changed fragment re-acquires alone, and it holds the provenance locators every higher representation cites to prove where its meaning came from.

PART III — CIR-1 SEMANTIC UNIT IR AND BOUNDARY DETECTION

3.1 Mission. CIR-1 is the stage where content becomes reasoning, and its mission is decomposition: to break the normalized source that CIR-0 holds into atomic semantic units, each a single, self-contained piece of meaning that can be typed, linked, and reasoned over on its own. This is the pivot of the whole fabric, because everything above CIR-1 reasons over units, not over text — CIR-2 types a unit's epistemic status, CIR-4 records a unit as a decision, CIR-5 tracks a unit as an obligation — and a unit drawn wrong poisons every stage that inherits it. CIR-1's discipline is therefore boundary correctness: a semantic unit must be exactly one piece of meaning, neither a fragment of one nor two meanings fused, because a unit that splits a single meaning loses that meaning's force and a unit that fuses two makes both un-typeable. The stage draws these boundaries and assigns each resulting unit its DAIF-01 type, turning a wall of normalized prose into a set of atomic, typed cognitive objects.

3.2 The atomic semantic unit. A CIR-1 unit is a DAIF-01 typed object whose content is one indivisible piece of meaning and whose Type is drawn from the DAIF-01 taxonomy the estate already sealed — fact, assertion, instruction, constraint, definition, goal, preference, question, hypothesis, decision, evidence, result, problem, risk, obligation, exception, law, and pattern. CIR-1 does not invent kinds; it recognizes them, mapping each atomic unit onto exactly one kind of that closed set, and a fragment that maps to no kind is a signal the boundary was drawn wrong — real meaning always has a kind. The unit carries its provenance as a locator back into the CIR-0 span it was cut from, so the decomposition is reversible and auditable, and it carries the scope it inherits from that source. Atomicity is the defining property: a unit is atomic when it cannot be divided without one of its halves losing meaning the other supplied, and CIR-1's boundary detection is precisely the search for the cuts that leave every resulting piece whole.

3.3 Boundary detection without fixed sizes. The central error CIR-1 refuses is fixed-size decomposition — cutting the source every N tokens, or at every sentence, or at every paragraph — because meaning does not respect uniform lengths, and a fixed cut severs units mid-meaning as often as it lands on a real boundary. CIR-1 detects boundaries by meaning-shifts instead, and the heuristics are three. It cuts at topic shifts, where the source moves from one subject to another, because a new subject begins a new unit. It cuts at problem-to-solution transitions, where the source pivots from naming a gap to resolving it, because the problem and its resolution are two units — a problem-typed one and a decision-or-result-typed one — that later stages must reference separately. And it cuts to pair claims with their evidence, keeping a claim and the observation that grounds it as adjacent but distinct units, because CIR-2 must type the claim's status by reading the evidence unit that supports it. None of these heuristics depends on length; all depend on the shape of the meaning, which is why CIR-1's boundaries survive where a fixed grid's would shatter.

3.4 Type assignment at the boundary. Drawing a boundary and assigning a type are one operation, not two, because the right boundary is the one that yields a unit of exactly one type. A span that would be typed both constraint and rationale is two units — the constraint and the evidence beneath it — and the correct boundary is the cut between them. CIR-1 assigns each atomic unit its DAIF-01 kind at the moment it draws the cut, and the assignment doubles as a boundary check: if a proposed unit resists a single clean type, the boundary is wrong and must be redrawn. This coupling is why CIR-1 cannot be a dumb splitter followed by a separate typer — the two decisions constrain each other, and separating them lets a splitter produce spans no typer can classify. The upper stack depends on this coupling: CIR-2 can type an epistemic status only for a unit that is already one kind, and a unit that arrived as two fused kinds forces CIR-2 to type a chimera, which it cannot do correctly.

3.5 How CIR-1 links to the stack. CIR-1 is the layer every higher representation indexes into, and its units are the nodes the upper stack draws its edges between. CIR-2 attaches an epistemic status to each CIR-1 unit; CIR-3 links units to the intent they serve; CIR-4 groups the units that constitute a decision — its question, options, and choice; CIR-5 promotes obligation-typed units into the obligation lifecycle; CIR-6 and CIR-7 draw causal and dependency edges between units. Because all of these are edges over CIR-1 nodes, the integrity of the graph is exactly the integrity of the CIR-1 boundaries — an edge to a mis-drawn unit points at the wrong meaning, and a whole stage's reasoning inherits the error. This is why CIR-1 links downward to CIR-0 with a locator and upward to nothing directly: it publishes atomic typed nodes, and the higher stages consume them by reference, so a CIR-1 unit is the stable identity that a decision three stages up and a causal edge five stages up both point at without either knowing the other exists.

3.6 The failure mode of a bad boundary. CIR-1's signature failure is the boundary that splits a single meaning across two units, and its most damaging instance is a constraint severed from its condition. A constraint reads "never do X when Y holds," and a boundary drawn between the prohibition and its condition yields two units — a unit that says never do X, absolute and unconditioned, and an orphan fragment naming Y that no longer binds anything. The first unit is now false: it forbids X universally when the source forbade it only under Y, and CIR-2 will type this over-broad prohibition, CIR-5 may track it, and the estate will enforce a constraint its author never wrote. The inverse failure fuses two meanings into one un-typeable unit — a fact and the decision it grounded compiled as a single span — so CIR-2 cannot assign one epistemic status and CIR-4 cannot separate the observation from the choice. Both failures share a root: a boundary placed by length or by punctuation rather than by meaning, and both are prevented by the same discipline of cutting only where a meaning genuinely ends.

3.7 Adversarial cases and what consumes CIR-1. The adversarial input against CIR-1 is a source that braids its meanings — a single sentence carrying a fact, the inference drawn from it, and the decision the inference justified, with no surface marker between them — designed so any single cut severs a meaning. CIR-1's defense is that atomicity is tested by the type assignment: it proposes cuts until every resulting unit takes exactly one clean type, and a braided sentence is decomposed into three units precisely because no two-way or one-way cut yields clean types. Where a source is genuinely ambiguous, CIR-1 records the ambiguity on the units' confidence rather than guessing a boundary, so a downstream consumer knows the decomposition was uncertain. A second adversarial input is the buried obligation — a commitment phrased as an aside inside a longer explanatory passage, whose surface form gives no signal that real work was promised — and CIR-1 must cut it out as its own obligation-typed unit, because a commitment left fused inside an explanation is invisible to CIR-5 and dies with the source that carried it. CIR-1's direct consumers are every stage above it, but its foundational consumer is CIR-2, which cannot type an epistemic status for a unit that is not already atomic and single-kinded — the epistemic ladder needs one claim at a time, and CIR-1 is what guarantees the stack hands it exactly one. Its second foundational consumer is CIR-5, which can only track an obligation that CIR-1 cut cleanly enough to carry its own closure condition; an obligation unit severed from the condition that would discharge it enters the lifecycle unclosable, held open forever because nothing can ever satisfy it. Boundary correctness is therefore not an internal nicety of the decomposition stage but the precondition for the epistemic honesty of CIR-2 and the completability of every obligation the estate incurs.

PART III FINAL LAW. CIR-1 decomposes normalized source into atomic semantic units, each exactly one piece of meaning mapped to exactly one DAIF-01 kind, drawing boundaries not by fixed size but by topic shifts, problem-to-solution transitions, and claim-evidence pairing, coupling every cut to a single-type assignment so a span that resists one type is a boundary drawn wrong; its signature failure is a constraint split from its condition — a prohibition made false by losing the case that bounded it — and its units are the stable nodes every higher representation draws its edges over, so the integrity of the whole stack is the integrity of these boundaries.

PART IV — CIR-2 EPISTEMIC IR

4.1 Mission. CIR-2 is the stage that types truth-status, and its mission is to attach to every CIR-1 unit an explicit statement of how well the estate knows it — whether it is observed, demonstrated, verified, inferred, assumed, speculative, disputed, obsolete, refuted, or pending-verification. This is the representation that keeps the fabric honest, because a compiled unit stripped of its epistemic status is a claim of unknown standing, and a consumer that cannot read the standing treats every unit as equally true, enforcing an assumption with the confidence due a verified observation. CIR-2 exists so that the difference between "we measured this" and "we guessed this" survives compilation, and its cardinal discipline is that this difference may only ever weaken, never strengthen, as a unit moves up the stack — a claim cannot gain certainty by being carried forward, only by being verified. CIR-2 is where the estate's epistemic conscience is made mechanical, so no assumption is ever silently promoted to a fact by the mere act of being recorded.

4.2 The epistemic object model. A CIR-2 unit is a CIR-1 unit with its epistemic status foregrounded, and the status is not a fresh scale — it composes the ACIS epistemic ladder directly, mapping each unit onto the level ACIS assigns and deferring to that ladder as the authority on promotion. The status values arrange from strongest to weakest: observed and demonstrated and verified sit at the top, where the claim rests on direct evidence the estate holds; inferred sits below, where the claim is derived from other claims rather than seen; assumed and speculative sit lower, where the claim is posited without grounding; disputed marks a claim two units contradict; obsolete and refuted mark claims reality or evidence has overturned; and pending-verification marks a claim asserted but not yet checked, the honest default for any unit whose grounding has not been established. The required metadata is the status value and the evidence pointer — the CIR-1 evidence unit or the CIR-9 result that grounds the status — because a status without a pointer to what grounds it is an unsupported label, and CIR-2 refuses an unsupported status the way DAIF-01 refuses an absent field.

4.3 Composition with the ACIS ladder. CIR-2 does not rebuild the epistemic ladder; it references ACIS as the sealed authority on epistemic levels and their promotion chain, and this composition is load-bearing rather than decorative. ACIS already defines the levels a claim occupies and the rule that a claim may not be promoted from a lower level to a higher one without the evidence the higher level requires — a deposit proven, a hypothesis tested, an inference verified. CIR-2 maps its status values onto those levels and inherits the promotion discipline whole, so that when CIR-2 must decide whether a unit may rise from inferred to verified, it applies ACIS's rule and not a parallel one. The estate has recorded exactly why this must be a reference and not a reimplementation: a second epistemic ladder that drifts from the first produces two verdicts on the same claim's standing, the authority collision the constitution convicts, and the only defense is that CIR-2 and ACIS speak one vocabulary because CIR-2 speaks ACIS's. The No-Autopromotion cap ACIS enforces is thus CIR-2's cap, applied at every stage boundary the unit crosses.

4.4 The cardinal invariant: no downhill promotion of an inference. The invariant CIR-2 exists to enforce is stated in one line — an inferred, assumed, or speculative unit may never be lowered into a later stage as a fact — and it is called the no-collapse rule because its violation collapses the distinction between what is known and what is guessed. As a unit moves up the stack, later stages consume it: CIR-4 grounds a decision on it, CIR-6 draws a causal edge from it, CIR-10 promotes a pattern over it. At each consumption the consuming stage inherits the unit's epistemic status, and the invariant forbids any stage from recording the unit at a stronger status than CIR-2 assigned. A decision may be grounded on an assumption — but the decision then inherits the assumption's weakness, and CIR-4 records that its justification rests on an unverified claim, so the decision is revisable when the assumption fails. What the invariant forbids is the silent upgrade: a stage that reads an assumed unit and records the fact it would have been if verified, so the assumption enters downstream reasoning wearing a certainty nothing earned. CIR-2's status travels with the unit precisely so this upgrade is detectable and refused.

4.5 How CIR-2 gates promotion between stages. CIR-2 is not a passive label but a gate the stack passes through, and it gates in both directions. Upward, it caps the status any consuming stage may assign: a stage that wishes to treat a unit as verified must present the evidence verification requires, and absent that evidence CIR-2 holds the unit at its established status, so the upper stack cannot manufacture certainty. Downward, it propagates weakness: when a strong-seeming unit is discovered to rest on a weak one — a fact whose only support is an assumption — CIR-2 lowers the dependent unit's effective status to the weakest link in its support chain, because a claim is only as certain as the least certain claim it depends on. This weakest-link propagation is what makes the epistemic stage a real gate rather than a coat of paint: it means a decision built on an assumption cannot present as verified no matter how confidently it is phrased, and an evaluation that measured a value is not dragged down by a speculative claim it never depended on. The gate reads the dependency edges CIR-7 draws to know which supports which.

4.6 The failure mode of epistemic collapse. CIR-2's signature failure is epistemic collapse: an assumption compiled as a truth, so a claim posited without grounding enters the stack at a status it did not earn and every stage above inherits the false certainty. The estate has recorded this shape repeatedly — a founding finding treated as verified when it was a hypothesis, an untested guess recorded as a measured result, a plan's assertion compiled as observed disk. The damage is that epistemic collapse is invisible at the point of consumption: a consumer reading a unit typed verified acts on it as verified, and nothing in the unit reveals that the verification never happened, so the collapse propagates until a downstream failure exposes it far from its origin. CIR-2 defends against collapse by refusing an unsupported status at intake — a unit claiming verified without an evidence pointer is held at pending-verification — and by the weakest-link propagation that stops a strong status from surviving atop a weak support. The adversarial case is a unit whose author sincerely believes it is verified but whose grounding is an inference; CIR-2 does not read belief, it reads the evidence pointer, and a missing pointer convicts the status regardless of the author's confidence.

4.7 What consumes CIR-2. Every stage above CIR-2 consumes its status, because every stage that reasons over a unit must know how certain the unit is before it acts. CIR-4 reads epistemic status to record whether a decision's justification is verified or assumed; CIR-9 reads it to distinguish a measured result from a projected one; CIR-10 reads it as the gate on promotion, since a pattern may become an institutional law only at the epistemic level ACIS's ladder permits, and CIR-2 is where that level is recorded. DAIF-03's fidelity gate reads CIR-2 to verify that compilation did not silently upgrade a status — a compiled unit whose epistemic status is stronger than its source's is a fidelity violation, an assumption the compiler promoted. And DAIF-01's Confidence field is grounded in CIR-2 for inferential units, exactly as that dataset stated: a unit's confidence rests on the epistemic level of its derivation, and CIR-2 is the stage that records that level. CIR-2 is thus the estate's guarantee that certainty is carried, never invented, through the whole compilation.

PART IV FINAL LAW. CIR-2 types every unit's truth-status — observed, demonstrated, verified, inferred, assumed, speculative, disputed, obsolete, refuted, or pending-verification — composing the ACIS epistemic ladder and inheriting its No-Autopromotion cap rather than rebuilding it, and it enforces the cardinal invariant that an inference may never be lowered into a later stage as a fact by capping the status any consuming stage may assign and propagating a support chain's weakness to its weakest link, so that epistemic collapse — an assumption compiled as a truth — is convicted at intake by the missing evidence pointer rather than discovered by the downstream failure it would otherwise cause.

PART V — CIR-3 INTENT IR

5.1 Mission. CIR-3 is the stage that preserves intention, and its mission is to hold, separately and durably, what a source actually wanted — its original intention, its expected result, its explicit and implicit criteria, its priorities and preferences, its prohibitions and non-goals, and its definition of done. This representation exists because intent is the dimension of meaning most easily lost in every other kind of processing: a summary keeps the task and drops the reason, a semantic decomposition keeps the instructions and scatters the purpose that bound them, and a consumer left with instructions but no intent optimizes the letter of the request while missing what was wanted. CIR-3 refuses that loss by compiling intent as a first-class object that travels beside the work, so a later actor can check any result not only against what was literally asked but against what the asking was for. Intent is the standard the whole compilation is ultimately judged by, because a faithful carrier that satisfies the words and betrays the purpose has failed.

5.2 The intent object model. A CIR-3 unit is a DAIF-01 typed object of kind goal or the intent-bearing kinds, foregrounding a structured account of purpose with several required slots. It holds the original intention — the end the source was reaching for, in the source's own terms. It holds the expected result — the observable state that would satisfy the intention. It holds the criteria, split into explicit criteria the source stated and implicit criteria the source assumed without stating, because the unstated ones are the ones a literal reading omits and a purposeful reading must recover. It holds priorities, where the source ranked competing wants, and preferences, where the source leaned without ranking. It holds prohibitions and non-goals — the things the source explicitly did not want and the ends it declared out of reach — because an intent is bounded as much by what it excludes as by what it seeks. And it holds the definition of done, the closure condition that says the intention is satisfied. Required are the intention, the expected result, and the definition of done; the criteria, priorities, preferences, prohibitions, and non-goals are present where the source expressed them and marked absent, not guessed, where it did not.

5.3 Composition with one_shot's intent scaffold. CIR-3 does not invent an intent-capture procedure; it composes one_shot's intent and Q&A scaffold, which the estate already runs as its mechanism for extracting intention before work begins. one_shot's six-question interrogation exists precisely to surface the implicit criteria, the priorities, the prohibitions, and the definition of done that a raw request leaves unstated, and CIR-3 reads the answers to those questions as its intent slots — the Q&A pass is the intent-capture front end, and CIR-3 is the typed representation it populates. This composition is why CIR-3 refuses to guess an absent criterion: one_shot's discipline is that six honest answers beat one vague paragraph, and CIR-3 inherits that discipline by recording an un-interrogated criterion as absent rather than fabricating it. Where a source arrived without a one_shot pass — a conversation, a correction — CIR-3 extracts what intent the source expressed and marks the rest absent, so the intent object honestly distinguishes captured intention from intention that was never surfaced. The scaffold is referenced as the capture authority and never re-narrated.

5.4 Why intent must be preserved separately. Intent is compiled as its own representation, distinct from the instructions and decisions it motivates, because a result can satisfy the instructions and violate the intent, and only a separately held intent lets a consumer detect the violation. The instructions say do X; the intent says X was wanted in order to achieve Y; and a result that does X in a way that defeats Y satisfies the literal instruction and fails the purpose. If intent lived only inside the instructions, this failure would be invisible — the consumer would check the result against the instructions, find it conformant, and declare done. By holding intent separately, CIR-3 gives the consumer a second, higher check: does the result serve the intention, not merely execute the instruction. This is the difference between a compilation that preserves behavior and one that preserves only text, because the behavior the source meant to provoke was the achievement of Y, and a representation that kept only X would provoke the wrong behavior with perfect fidelity to the words. Separate intent is what makes the definition of done a purpose-check rather than a checklist.

5.5 Explicit and implicit criteria. CIR-3's handling of criteria is where it earns its keep, because the criteria that decide whether a result is right are mostly unstated. Explicit criteria are the conditions the source named — a value, a format, a bound — and compiling them is straightforward. Implicit criteria are the conditions the source assumed everyone shared — that the result would not break an adjacent system, that it would respect a constraint the source never thought to restate, that it would be usable by the actor who requested it — and these are the criteria a literal reading omits and a purposeful reading must recover. CIR-3 recovers implicit criteria through the one_shot interrogation where a source ran through it, and marks them explicitly as implicit so a consumer knows they were inferred rather than stated. The prohibitions and non-goals are the negative face of the same recovery: a source that wanted X almost always did not want X-at-the-cost-of-Z, and the non-goal recording Z-is-out-of-scope is what stops a consumer from over-delivering into territory the source excluded. Criteria, priorities, prohibitions, and non-goals together bound the intent from every side.

5.6 The failure mode of lost intent. CIR-3's signature failure is lost intent: a compilation that keeps the task and drops the purpose, so a later actor optimizes the stated task while missing what was actually wanted. Its most damaging instance is the technically-conformant miss — a result that meets every explicit criterion and violates an implicit one, delivered with confidence because the checklist passed. The estate has recorded this shape as the gap between satisfying the literal words and honoring the intent, and it is exactly the failure a separate intent representation exists to catch. Lost intent is insidious because it passes every surface review: the result does what was said, the instructions are satisfied, and nothing in a purposeless compilation reveals that the purpose was betrayed. CIR-3 defends against it by making the definition of done a check against the intention rather than the instruction, and by carrying the implicit criteria and non-goals that a literal check omits. The adversarial case is a request whose literal reading and purposeful reading diverge sharply — a task easy to satisfy in a way that defeats its point — and CIR-3's held intent is what lets a consumer see the divergence and choose the purpose.

5.7 What consumes CIR-3. CIR-3 is consumed by every stage that must judge whether work is right rather than merely present. CIR-4 reads intent to record whether a decision served the purpose it was made under, so a decision that satisfied a constraint and defeated the intention is marked as such. CIR-5 reads intent to define an obligation's closure condition, since an obligation is done when the intention behind it is achieved, not when a box is checked. DAIF-03's fidelity gate reads CIR-3 as the behavioral standard the compilation is measured against — behavioral equivalence is equivalence of provoked purpose, and CIR-3 holds the purpose. DAIF-07's obligation lifecycle reads CIR-3 to know what completing an obligation actually requires, and DAIF-08's context assembly reads CIR-3 to keep a reasoning session aimed at the mission it was assembled for. Across all of them, CIR-3 is the representation that answers the question every other stage eventually asks — not what was said, but what was wanted — and it is the reason the fabric can claim to compile purpose and not only prose.

PART V FINAL LAW. CIR-3 preserves intention as a first-class representation — original intention, expected result, explicit and implicit criteria, priorities, preferences, prohibitions, non-goals, and definition of done — composing one_shot's intent and Q&A scaffold as its capture authority and marking un-surfaced criteria absent rather than guessing them, held separately from the instructions it motivates precisely so a result that satisfies the words and violates the purpose is detectable; its signature failure is lost intent, the technically-conformant miss that optimizes the stated task while betraying what was wanted, caught only because the definition of done is a purpose-check rather than a checklist.

PART VI — CIR-4 DECISION IR

6.1 Mission. CIR-4 is the stage that compiles decisions, and its mission is to hold every choice the estate makes as a complete, revisable object rather than a bare conclusion — with the question it resolved, the options it weighed, the choice it landed on, the justification behind that choice, the evidence and authority that backed it, its scope, its consequences and trade-offs, the conditions under which it should be revised, its expiry, and the decisions it superseded. A decision is the most dangerous kind of unit to compile lossily, because a decision recorded as only its outcome looks complete and is un-reversible: a later actor who finds the conclusion but not the reasoning cannot tell whether the choice still holds, cannot safely reverse it, and cannot detect that its grounding has expired. CIR-4 exists so that a decision survives compilation with everything a future reconsideration needs, and its discipline is that a choice is never held apart from the reasoning that produced it, because the reasoning is what makes the choice governable.

6.2 The decision object model. A CIR-4 unit is a DAIF-01 typed object of kind decision, and its slots are the anatomy of a governable choice. It holds the question — the open matter the decision resolved, without which the choice floats unattached to any problem. It holds the options considered, including the ones rejected, because a decision's meaning is as much in what it declined as in what it took. It holds the choice itself, the option selected. It holds the justification — the reasoning that made the choice preferable — and the evidence that justification rested on, pointing at the CIR-2 units and CIR-9 results that grounded it. It holds the authority that made the decision and the scope within which the decision binds, both inherited from DAIF-01. It holds the consequences and trade-offs the choice accepted, the revision conditions under which it should be reopened, and an expiry where the decision was time-bounded. And it holds supersession pointers to any earlier decisions this one replaced. Required are the question, the choice, the justification, the authority, and the scope; a decision missing any of these is not compilable as a decision, because each is load-bearing for a future reversal.

6.3 Composition with DRK's decision_record. CIR-4 does not define a new decision format; it composes DRK's decision_record, the estate's sealed structure for capturing a decision with its rationale, and a compiled CIR-4 unit is that record's typed projection into the stack. DRK already requires a decision to carry its question, its options, its justification, and its authority — the anatomy that makes a decision auditable and revisable — and CIR-4 reads a decision_record as its source and populates its slots from it, deferring to DRK as the authority on what a well-formed decision contains. This composition matters because DRK also encodes the estate's limits on autonomous decision-making — the authority ceiling beyond which a self-issued decision may not reach — and CIR-4 inherits that ceiling through the authority slot, so a compiled decision cannot claim an authority DRK would have refused to grant it. Where a decision entered the fabric without a DRK record — a choice made in conversation and never formalized — CIR-4 compiles what the source expressed and marks the missing slots absent, flagging the decision as incompletely recorded so a consumer knows its revisability is compromised. DRK is referenced as the decision authority and never re-narrated.

6.4 Why a decision without its justification is un-revisable. The core law of CIR-4 is that a decision held as a bare conclusion cannot be safely reversed, because reversal requires seeing the reason. To reverse a decision safely, a later actor must know why it was made — what problem it solved, what evidence backed it, what trade-off it accepted — so that the actor can check whether that reason still holds. A decision recorded as only its outcome hides all of this: the actor sees that X was chosen but not that X was chosen because of evidence E under constraint C, so the actor cannot tell whether E is still true or C still binds, and any reversal is a blind one that may re-open a problem the original decision quietly solved. The justification is therefore not commentary on the decision but the precondition for governing it: it is what lets a revision be reasoned rather than guessed, and it is why CIR-4 refuses to compile a choice without the reasoning that produced it. A conclusion without its justification is a fact-shaped fossil — it records what was done and destroys the ability to reconsider it.

6.5 Revision conditions, expiry, and supersession. CIR-4 compiles not only why a decision was made but when it should be unmade, through three forward-looking slots. Revision conditions name the circumstances that should reopen the decision — a change in the evidence it rested on, a shift in the constraint it accepted, an outcome that contradicts its expected consequence — so a decision carries its own reconsideration triggers rather than waiting to be questioned by accident. Expiry names a decision's declared lifespan where it had one, common to decisions taken under conditions known to be temporary, and an expired decision is surfaced for renewal rather than silently held as permanent, exactly as DAIF-01's validity field governs. Supersession pointers link a decision to the earlier ones it replaced, forming the lineage a consumer traverses to find the current decision on a matter and to see what it overturned. Together these slots make a decision a living object with a lifecycle, and they connect CIR-4 to DAIF-01's validity states and to DAIF-21's reality synchronization, which watches the revision conditions and flips a decision's validity when reality moves out from under it.

6.6 The failure mode of the bare conclusion. CIR-4's signature failure is the decision kept as a bare conclusion — a choice recorded without its question, justification, or authority — and its damage is un-revisability, which the estate feels as decisions that cannot be safely changed because no one can see why they were made. A bare conclusion invites two equally bad responses when it is later questioned: a consumer reverses it blindly, re-opening whatever problem it solved, or a consumer treats it as immovable precisely because its reasoning is invisible and no one dares touch it, so a decision that should have been revised outlives its grounding. The estate has recorded this as the un-appealable, un-auditable decision the constitution's decision-record discipline exists to prevent. The adversarial case is a decision compiled with a plausible-sounding conclusion but a fabricated or absent justification — a choice that looks reasoned and is not — and CIR-4 defends against it by requiring the evidence pointers that ground the justification to resolve to real CIR-2 and CIR-9 units, so a justification that points at nothing is convicted the way CIR-2 convicts an unsupported epistemic status.

6.7 What consumes CIR-4. CIR-4 is consumed by the stages and datasets that must reason over the estate's choices. CIR-5 reads decisions to find the open ones — a decision deferred is an obligation to decide, and CIR-4's un-resolved questions feed CIR-5's obligation lifecycle. CIR-6 reads decisions as causes in its causal graph, since a decision is often why a subsequent state came to be. CIR-10 reads decisions that recur into institutional patterns and laws, promoting a repeatedly-made choice into a standing rule at the epistemic level ACIS permits. DAIF-00's constitution reads CIR-4 as the record a duplicate-to-advantage verdict is itself compiled into, and DAIF-21's reality synchronization reads CIR-4's revision conditions and expiries to know when a decision has gone stale. Across all of them, CIR-4 is the representation that makes the estate's choices governable rather than merely made — every decision carried with the reasoning that lets it be revised, so the estate can change its mind safely instead of being frozen by conclusions whose reasons it can no longer see.

PART VI FINAL LAW. CIR-4 compiles every decision as a complete revisable object — question, options, choice, justification, evidence, authority, scope, consequences, trade-offs, revision conditions, expiry, and superseded decisions — composing DRK's decision_record as its authority and inheriting its autonomy ceiling, under the core law that a decision held as a bare conclusion is un-revisable because a safe reversal requires seeing the reason it was made; its signature failure is the reasonless conclusion, defended against by requiring the justification's evidence pointers to resolve to real units, so the estate can change its mind by reasoning rather than by guessing or by fear.

PART VII — CIR-5 OBLIGATION IR

7.1 Mission. CIR-5 is the stage that compiles unfinished work, and its mission is to hold every obligation the estate incurs as a formal, tracked object — the tasks, promises, deliverables, pending verifications, migrations, follow-ups, open decisions, unaccepted PRDs, and untested functions that represent work that still must happen. This representation exists because obligations are the units the estate most reliably loses: a task named inside a conversation dies when the conversation closes, a promised follow-up evaporates when the session ends, a pending verification is forgotten the moment attention moves on, and the work goes undone not because anyone decided against it but because no representation carried it forward. CIR-5 refuses that loss by compiling obligations into typed objects that outlive their originating source, so an obligation named once persists as a tracked commitment until it is closed or explicitly abandoned. It is the representation that converts a fleeting mention of work-to-be-done into a durable claim on the estate's future attention.

7.2 The obligation object model. A CIR-5 unit is a DAIF-01 typed object of kind obligation, and its slots make an item of unfinished work trackable. It holds the object — what must be done, the deliverable or the change or the verification owed. It holds the owner — the authority or actor responsible for it, so an obligation is never ownerless. It holds the origin — the CIR-0 source and the decision or intent it arose from, so an obligation can be traced to why it was incurred. It holds the priority, ranking it against competing obligations. It holds the state — open, in-progress, blocked, done, or abandoned — the lifecycle position that tells a consumer whether the obligation is still live. It holds dependencies on other obligations, since work is often ordered. It holds the closure definition — the condition that, when met, discharges the obligation, drawn from the intent's definition of done. It holds the required evidence — what must be observed to prove closure, so an obligation cannot be marked done without proof. And it holds aging and abandonment risk — how long the obligation has been open and how likely it is to be silently dropped. Required are the object, the owner, the origin, the state, and the closure definition; an obligation missing its closure definition can never be proven done and is a permanent open item by construction.

7.3 How obligations enter and move. Obligations enter CIR-5 from several stages above CIR-1. A CIR-1 unit typed obligation enters directly. A CIR-4 decision that was deferred rather than resolved enters as an obligation to decide. A CIR-3 intent whose definition of done is unmet enters as an obligation to complete. An unaccepted PRD, an untested function, a pending verification, a promised migration — each is an obligation the estate incurred and must carry. Once entered, an obligation moves through its lifecycle states under evidence: it advances from open to in-progress when work begins, to blocked when a dependency stalls it, and to done only when its required evidence is observed to satisfy its closure definition — never on assertion alone, because an obligation marked done without evidence is the scaffold illusion the estate convicts. An obligation may also move to abandoned, but only explicitly and with a recorded reason, because silent abandonment is exactly the failure CIR-5 exists to prevent — the difference between a decision to drop work and work simply forgotten.

7.4 CIR-5 feeds DAIF-07's obligation lifecycle. CIR-5 is the representation, and DAIF-07 is the fabric that acts on it — the Obligation Lifecycle and Work Completion Authority dataset consumes CIR-5 as its formal input, and this seam is the whole point of compiling obligations. Before CIR-5, the estate's unfinished-work watchers — the scaffold-auditor, the owner_queue, backlog_autopilot, the liveness checks — scanned loose text for obligations, inferring from prose that a task existed and guessing its state, which is exactly the fragile pattern the estate has recorded as an orphaned watcher reading undifferentiated words. CIR-5 replaces that scan with a formal IR: DAIF-07 stops reading prose and reads compiled obligation objects, each carrying its owner, state, closure definition, required evidence, and aging, so the Unfinished Work Watcher consumes structure instead of inferring it. This is the division the estate draws everywhere — CIR-5 is the inert representation, DAIF-07 is the acting runtime that advances obligations, escalates the aging ones, and enforces closure — and the two compose along the seam, DAIF-07 writing state transitions back onto the CIR-5 objects it consumes rather than either owning both halves.

7.5 Aging, abandonment risk, and the priority queue. CIR-5 compiles two forward-looking signals that make obligations visible before they are lost. Aging records how long an obligation has been open, so an obligation that has sat untouched surfaces its own staleness rather than waiting to be noticed, and DAIF-07 reads aging to escalate the obligations the estate is quietly failing to close. Abandonment risk records the likelihood that an obligation will be silently dropped — an obligation with no recent activity, a distant owner, or a dependency that keeps blocking it carries high risk — and it is the signal that lets the estate distinguish an obligation being deliberately deferred from one being forgotten. Together with priority, these signals form the queue DAIF-07 works: the estate's open obligations ranked by priority, aged, and risk-scored, so the most important actionable obligation is the one surfaced, exactly the discipline the estate's backlog machinery already runs. CIR-5's contribution is to make that queue a queue of formal objects rather than a scan of loose mentions, so the ranking is over real closure definitions and real evidence requirements rather than over guesses about what a sentence meant.

7.6 The failure mode of the sentence-only obligation. CIR-5's signature failure is the obligation that exists only as a sentence in a closed conversation — a task mentioned, agreed to, and never compiled, so it dies when the source that named it is done. This is the founding failure the whole DAIF-07 vertical exists to end: work that everyone intended to do and no one carried, lost not to a decision but to the absence of a representation. Its damage is silent and cumulative — each un-compiled obligation is a small unkept promise, and an estate that loses them steadily accretes a debt of forgotten work that no one can see because no representation holds it. CIR-5 defends against this by compiling an obligation the moment it is named, giving it an owner and a closure definition so it becomes a tracked claim rather than a fading mention, and by the aging and abandonment-risk signals that make a neglected obligation surface itself. The adversarial case is an obligation phrased so casually it reads as conversational filler — a "we should eventually" that carries real work — and CIR-5's boundary detection, inherited from CIR-1, is tuned to recognize obligation-typed units even in soft phrasing, because the softness of the phrasing is no measure of the reality of the work.

7.7 What consumes CIR-5 and the invariant it guards. CIR-5's primary consumer is DAIF-07, which reads it as the formal substrate of the obligation lifecycle, but its objects are read across the estate: DAIF-08's context assembly reads open obligations to keep a session aware of the work it owes, DAIF-00's constitution reads them as the commitments a duplicate-to-advantage verdict may itself incur, and the estate's liveness and owner-queue machinery read them as the actionable items to surface. The invariant CIR-5 guards is that unfinished work is never carried as prose — every obligation is a typed object with an owner, a state, a closure definition, and a required-evidence gate, so the estate's watchers consume structure and the estate's obligations cannot die by being forgotten. An obligation may be closed by evidence or abandoned by decision, but it may never simply vanish, because a vanished obligation is the one failure the whole representation was built to make impossible. CIR-5 is thus the estate's guarantee that what it commits to do, it continues to see until the work is done or deliberately dropped.

PART VII FINAL LAW. CIR-5 compiles unfinished work — tasks, promises, deliverables, pending verifications, migrations, follow-ups, open decisions, unaccepted PRDs, and untested functions — into typed obligation objects carrying object, owner, origin, priority, state, dependencies, closure definition, required evidence, aging, and abandonment risk, advancing to done only on observed evidence and to abandoned only by recorded decision; it feeds DAIF-07 as a formal IR so the Unfinished Work Watcher consumes structure instead of scanning loose prose, defeating the one failure it exists to end — the obligation that lives only as a sentence in a closed conversation and dies when the conversation does.

PART VIII — CIR-6 CAUSAL IR

8.1 Mission. CIR-6 is the stage that compiles causation, and its mission is to hold every why the estate has learned as a complete chain rather than as its terminal claim — the cause that acted, the mechanism through which it acted, the effect it produced, the evidence that grounds the link, the conditions under which the mechanism operates, and the exceptions where it does not. This representation exists because causal knowledge is the knowledge the estate most reliably degrades into slogans: an incident is investigated, a mechanism is understood, and what survives the week is a one-line conclusion — do not do X, always do Y — that outlives the understanding that produced it. The conclusion is not wrong when it is written; it becomes wrong when the conditions that made it true quietly change, and by then no one holds the mechanism that would reveal the change. CIR-6 refuses that degradation by compiling the chain itself, so that a later actor reading a conclusion can always ask the only question that matters about an inherited rule — does the mechanism that made this true still apply here — and can answer it from the representation rather than from a re-investigation nobody has time to run.

8.2 The causal object model. A CIR-6 unit is a DAIF-01 typed object whose foregrounded content is a causal chain and whose slots are the chain's links. It holds the cause — the antecedent state, act, or change that initiated the effect, pointing at the CIR-1 units that describe it. It holds the mechanism — the account of how the cause produced the effect, the intermediate steps by which one became the other, and this is the slot the whole representation exists for. It holds the effect — the consequent state, again as a pointer into the units that record it. It holds the evidence — the CIR-9 evaluations, logged results, or observed incidents that ground the claim that this cause, through this mechanism, produced this effect — and the evidence pointer is required, because a causal claim without evidence is a hypothesis and CIR-2 will type it as one. It holds the conditions — the circumstances that must obtain for the mechanism to operate, the substrate, the platform, the concurrency, the scale, the version — and it holds the exceptions, the known circumstances in which the cause is present and the effect does not follow, because a mechanism's boundary is described as much by where it fails as by where it holds. Required are cause, mechanism, effect, evidence, and conditions; the exceptions slot is required to be present and may honestly hold none-known, since an empty exceptions slot and an absent one are the difference DAIF-01 draws between a value and a defect. Strength, Authority, Scope, and Validity are inherited from the type system as they are everywhere in the stack, and a causal unit's Scope is load-bearing in a way few others are, because the conditions slot and the scope slot together bound the territory in which the mechanism is claimed to run.

8.3 Why the conclusion alone is a trap. The core law of CIR-6 is that a conclusion preserved without its mechanism cannot be evaluated for continued validity, and this is a strictly sharper claim than the familiar one that reasoning is nice to keep. A conclusion is a compressed function of its conditions: it says, given this substrate, this concurrency, this version, this scale, the effect follows. Compress away the conditions and the mechanism that connects them to the effect, and what remains is a bare imperative that carries no test — a later actor who encounters it can obey it or ignore it, and neither choice is informed, because nothing in the conclusion tells the actor which of the two the changed world now warrants. The trap is that the bare conclusion is not merely less useful than the chain; it is actively misleading, because it looks complete. It reads as settled institutional knowledge, it is short, it is confident, and it silently asserts a universality it never earned — the mechanism was conditional and the conclusion is not, so the compression itself performed an unauthorized widening of scope, exactly the leak DAIF-01 convicts on the Scope field. The estate has felt this shape in both directions: rules obeyed long after the substrate that justified them was gone, and rules abandoned as superstition because nobody could produce the mechanism that would have shown they still applied. Both failures are the same absence, and CIR-6 closes it by making the mechanism and the conditions co-equal slots with the effect rather than commentary beneath it.

8.4 Conditions, exceptions, and the boundary of a mechanism. The conditions slot is what converts a causal unit from a story into a testable claim, because it states, in advance, what the world must look like for the chain to run. A condition is not a hedge; it is the mechanism's domain of definition, and a consumer evaluating whether an inherited conclusion applies to its situation performs exactly one operation — it checks its situation against the conditions, and if a condition does not hold, the conclusion is not merely weakened but out of scope, and the consumer must reason from the mechanism afresh rather than from the effect. The exceptions slot is the empirical complement: every recorded case in which the cause was present, the conditions appeared to hold, and the effect did not follow. An exception is enormously valuable and is almost always discarded by summarization, because it reads as a caveat and caveats are what compression eats first. In CIR-6 an exception is a first-class member of the chain, since a mechanism with three recorded exceptions is a mechanism whose conditions are incompletely stated, and the exceptions are the evidence that the conditions slot is not yet closed. This gives the representation a self-correcting property: an accumulating exception count is a standing signal that the causal unit's conditions must be refined, and the refinement is itself a typed decision under CIR-4 rather than a quiet edit.

8.5 Invariants and the valid transformations. Three invariants govern CIR-6. The first is mechanism-completeness: a causal unit whose mechanism slot is absent or is a restatement of the effect — because X, therefore X — is not a causal unit and is refused at intake, held instead as a CIR-1 assertion awaiting a mechanism. The second is evidential grounding: the causal unit's epistemic status is capped by CIR-2 at the level its evidence supports, so a chain grounded in one incident is inferred and not verified, and a chain grounded in no observation at all is speculative regardless of how confidently it is phrased. The third is condition-boundedness: a causal unit may never be consumed outside the conditions it declares, and a consumer that applies it beyond them has performed the unauthorized widening the whole Part guards. From these the legal transformations follow. It is valid to strengthen a causal unit by adding evidence, to refine it by narrowing its conditions when an exception is found, to specialize it into a narrower-scoped child unit that inherits the mechanism, and to supersede it with a better-mechanised chain that carries a supersession pointer under DAIF-01's validity semantics. It is invalid to drop the mechanism while retaining the effect, invalid to widen the conditions without new evidence at each new condition, invalid to promote the chain's epistemic status by repetition rather than by measurement, and invalid to silently delete an exception because it is inconvenient — an exception is retired only by a recorded finding that it was misattributed, never by omission.

8.6 The failure mode of the cargo-culted conclusion. CIR-6's signature failure is the cargo-culted conclusion: a rule whose originating mechanism no longer applies, obeyed anyway because the mechanism was never preserved and no one can therefore establish that it has lapsed. Its anatomy is consistent. A real incident produces a real mechanism under real conditions; the conclusion is extracted and propagated; the mechanism is left behind in the incident record and eventually in nobody's head; the substrate then changes — a platform is upgraded, a transport is replaced, a concurrency model changes — and the conditions that the mechanism depended on quietly stop holding. The conclusion, now unmoored, continues to be enforced, and it costs the estate twice: once in the direct waste of honoring a constraint that no longer protects anything, and once, far worse, in the erosion of the estate's willingness to honor constraints at all, because a body of rules that visibly contains dead ones teaches every actor to guess which of them are still alive. The adversarial case is the conclusion that is still true for the wrong reason — the effect still follows, but through a different mechanism than the one recorded — so a consumer that checks only the effect finds confirmation and is reinforced in a causal model that is now false, and the next time the two mechanisms diverge the estate is blindsided by an outcome its model could not produce. CIR-6 defends against both by making the mechanism the checkable artifact: a conclusion is re-validated by re-checking its conditions, not by observing that the effect happened again, and a chain whose conditions have lapsed is flipped by DAIF-21's reality synchronization to obsolete rather than left standing as a live imperative nobody dares question.

8.7 How CIR-6 links and what consumes it. Causal units are edges as much as nodes, and they attach to the stack at two seams. Downward they link to CIR-4: a decision's justification is very often a causal claim — this option was chosen because that mechanism would otherwise produce this failure — and CIR-4's justification slot points into the CIR-6 chain that carries the reasoning, so a decision inherits the causal unit's conditions and becomes revisable exactly when those conditions lapse, which is what makes a decision's revision conditions mechanical rather than aspirational. Upward they link to CIR-9: an evaluation is the instrument that confirms or refutes a mechanism, and a CIR-6 chain names the evaluation that would test it, so a causal claim carries its own falsifier in the ACIS sense rather than floating as an untestable narrative. Sideways they link through CIR-7's dependency edges, since a mechanism that operates through an intermediate system is a chain whose links are dependencies, and the two representations describe one structure from two angles. Above the stack, CIR-10 reads causal units as the grounding for any institutional law it proposes — a law without a mechanism is a preference, and the promotion gate reads the chain to decide — and DAIF-21 reads the conditions slot as the watch-list of world-facts whose change should invalidate the chain. CIR-6 is thus the estate's guarantee that its accumulated why survives compilation with its boundary intact, so that inherited knowledge can be checked rather than merely obeyed.

PART VIII FINAL LAW. CIR-6 compiles causation as a complete chain — cause, mechanism, effect, evidence, conditions, and exceptions, with the exceptions slot explicitly present even when empty — because a conclusion preserved without its mechanism is a trap that reads as settled law while silently shedding the conditions that bounded it, leaving a later actor unable to establish whether it still holds; the chain's invariants are mechanism-completeness, evidential grounding capped by CIR-2, and condition-boundedness, its legal transformations narrow the conditions on new exceptions and never widen them without new evidence, and its signature failure is the cargo-culted conclusion enforced long after the substrate that justified it is gone, defeated only because the mechanism is a checkable artifact and not a memory.

PART IX — CIR-7 DEPENDENCY IR

9.1 Mission. CIR-7 is the stage that compiles dependence, and its mission is to hold, explicitly and in one representation, every relation of the form this rests on that — so the estate can compute what breaks when a thing changes, what must run before what, and which claims collapse when a support is withdrawn. The estate today knows most of its dependencies implicitly: a module imports another, a dataset assumes a sibling's schema, a decision rests on an assumption, an evaluation presupposes a fixture. Each of these is real and load-bearing, and none of them is written anywhere a consumer can query. The consequence is that dependence is discovered by breakage — the estate learns that A depended on B at the moment B changes and A fails, which is precisely the worst moment and the most expensive instrument. CIR-7 exists to convert dependence from something the estate suffers into something it holds: a compiled edge set over the units, systems, and datasets of the estate, carrying enough structure that ordering, impact, and collapse can be computed before the change is made rather than diagnosed after it lands.

9.2 The four kinds of dependency. Dependence is not one relation but four, and conflating them is the first error CIR-7 refuses. A technical dependency is a mechanical one: this module calls that function, this procedure needs that tool, this artifact is generated from that source, and it breaks by construction when the target changes shape. A cognitive dependency is a comprehension relation: understanding this unit requires first understanding that one — a definition a constraint references, a concept a procedure assumes — and it does not break loudly but produces a consumer who acts on a unit whose meaning it never actually held. An evidential dependency is a support relation: this claim's epistemic status rests on that evidence, and it is the relation CIR-2's weakest-link propagation traverses, so withdrawing the evidence lowers everything above it. A decision dependency is a governance relation: this decision was made under that decision, or under that constraint, or awaiting that other decision, and it is the relation that makes a deferred choice into a blocker rather than a preference. The four are typed distinctly because their invalidation semantics differ absolutely: a broken technical dependency is a failure, a broken cognitive dependency is a misunderstanding, a broken evidential dependency is an epistemic downgrade, and a broken decision dependency is an authority gap. A representation that types them all as one relation cannot compute any of these consequences correctly, and would tell an actor that a withdrawn assumption and a deleted function are the same kind of event.

9.3 The dependency edge and its payload. A CIR-7 unit is an edge, and the edge is itself a DAIF-01 typed object carrying five required slots beyond the standard eight. It carries kind — technical, cognitive, evidential, or decision — which selects the invalidation semantics above. It carries direction, naming the dependent and the depended-upon without ambiguity, since a symmetric edge is not a dependency but a coupling and must be typed as two edges or as none. It carries strength, and the values are hard and soft in exactly DAIF-01's sense: a hard dependency means the dependent cannot function at all if the target is absent or changed, and a soft dependency means the dependent degrades but survives, and the distinction is what separates an impact analysis that halts a change from one that merely annotates it. It carries evidence — how the estate knows this edge exists, whether an observed import, a declared reference, a recorded incident in which the target's change broke the dependent, or an inference from structure — because an asserted edge with no evidence is a guess about the estate's own shape and CIR-2 types it as such. And it carries confidence, which is not decoration: an inferred edge at low confidence and an observed edge at high confidence must be distinguishable, because an impact analysis that treats every suspected edge as certain produces a blast radius so large that no change is ever safe, and an actor who cannot act on the analysis will stop reading it.

9.4 Composition with GK-04's typed edge registry. CIR-7 does not build a graph. The estate already runs one: GK-04's typed edge registry is the sealed authority on typed relations between the estate's coordinates, and CIR-7 composes it directly — a compiled dependency edge is registered as a GK-04 typed edge whose kind is one of the four above, and the traversal, the freshness, and the query surface are GK-04's, referenced and never re-derived. This is not a stylistic preference but the constitution's duplicate-to-advantage law applied at the exact point where the temptation to fork is strongest, because building a second graph is always the shortest path from here and always the most expensive path in a year. Two graphs over the estate's own structure would drift the moment one is updated and the other is not, and a drifted structural graph is worse than none, since an impact analysis run against a stale edge set returns a confident answer that is wrong in the one direction that matters — it omits the edge that would have caught the breakage. CIR-7's contribution is therefore not the graph but the payload: it specifies what a dependency edge must carry to be reasoned over, and it hands those edges to GK-04 to hold, index, and traverse. Where GK-04 already holds an edge that a compiled unit implies, CIR-7 enriches it rather than duplicating it, adding the kind, strength, evidence, and confidence that a structurally-inferred edge lacked.

9.5 Ordering, impact, and cycles. Three computations justify the representation. Ordering is the derivation of a necessary execution sequence: given a set of units or obligations and the hard dependency edges among them, the order in which work may proceed is the topological order of that subgraph, and CIR-5's obligation dependencies are exactly these edges seen from the obligation's side — an obligation is blocked precisely when it has an open hard dependency, which is what makes blocked a computed state rather than a hand-set flag. Impact is the reverse traversal: given a proposed change to a unit or a system, the set of things that may break is the closure of dependents reached through hard edges, annotated by the soft edges that would degrade. Collapse is the evidential traversal CIR-2 already runs, propagating weakness from a withdrawn support up through everything that rested on it. Cycles are the interesting pathology, and CIR-7 does not forbid them, because real cognitive dependence is sometimes mutual — two definitions that refer to each other, two systems that each assume the other's contract. What CIR-7 forbids is a cycle among hard technical dependencies used for ordering, because such a cycle is a claim that A must run before B and B before A, which is not a hard problem but an incoherent one, and it is surfaced as a defect the moment the ordering computation attempts it. A cycle among cognitive or evidential edges is reported as a circular support — the claim that grounds itself — which is the epistemic pathology CIR-2 must convict, since a chain of evidence that closes on itself has no ground at all.

9.6 The failure mode of the hidden dependency. CIR-7's signature failure is the dependency that exists in the world and in no representation, discovered only by the breakage it causes. Its anatomy is the estate's most familiar pain: a change is made, judged local, and lands; something apparently unrelated fails; the failure is diagnosed backwards to the change; and the dependency, which was always there, is written down for the first time in the post-mortem. Every element of that sequence is a cost, and the largest is invisible — the estate's actors, having been surprised, become conservative, and the conservatism taxes every subsequent change whether or not it is risky, because without a dependency representation there is no way to distinguish a safe change from a dangerous one and the only available policy is caution everywhere. The adversarial case is the dependency that is real but not structural: a decision in one project that silently rested on an assumption another project owns, or a procedure whose correctness depends on a condition a different system happens to maintain. No import graph will find these, because they are not technical edges at all — they are decision and evidential edges, and they are exactly the ones a structural scan misses and a compiled fabric catches, because CIR-4 records what a decision rested on and CIR-2 records what an evidential claim was grounded in, and CIR-7 lifts both into queryable edges. The defense is therefore not better scanning but better compiling: a dependency that was declared at the moment a decision was made cannot be hidden later, and the moment of declaration is the only moment at which the dependence is cheaply visible.

9.7 What consumes CIR-7. CIR-7's edges are read by nearly everything above it. CIR-2 traverses the evidential edges to propagate the weakest link and to convict a circular support. CIR-5 reads the hard edges to compute whether an obligation is blocked and to order the queue DAIF-07 works. CIR-6's mechanisms are frequently paths through the dependency graph, and a causal chain that crosses a system boundary is stated over the same edges. CIR-8's procedures read the technical edges to know what tools and artifacts a step requires and therefore what preconditions its activation must check. CIR-11's context assembly reads the graph to expand from a mission's seed units to their hard supports, which is how a context pack becomes sufficient without becoming everything. And DAIF-21's reality synchronization reads the edges in the invalidation direction: when a unit is refuted or a system changes, the set of units whose validity must be re-examined is the dependents closure, so the synchronization layer knows where to look rather than re-checking the estate. CIR-7 is thus the representation that tells the estate what its own shape is, and its discipline is that the shape is declared where it is known — at the decision, at the grounding, at the call — and never inferred from the rubble after something fell.

PART IX FINAL LAW. CIR-7 compiles dependence as typed edges of four distinct kinds — technical, cognitive, evidential, and decision, whose invalidation semantics are a failure, a misunderstanding, an epistemic downgrade, and an authority gap respectively — each edge carrying kind, direction, strength, evidence, and confidence, and registered into GK-04's typed edge registry rather than into a second graph the estate would immediately drift; from these edges the estate computes necessary ordering, change impact, and evidential collapse, forbids only the incoherent cycle among hard ordering edges, and forecloses its signature failure — the hidden dependency discovered by breakage and written down for the first time in the post-mortem — by declaring dependence at the moment it is cheaply visible rather than reconstructing it from the rubble.

PART X — CIR-8 PROCEDURAL IR

10.1 Mission. CIR-8 is the stage that compiles knowledge into procedure, and its mission is to convert what the estate knows into something that can actually be run — an activation condition that says when, a sequence of steps that says what, the branches that handle the cases, the tools each step invokes, the contracts each step must honor, the outputs it produces, the errors it should expect, the rollback it performs when it fails, and the done-gate that decides whether it succeeded. This is the representation where knowledge stops being a thing an actor reads and becomes a thing an actor executes, and it is the stage at which the fabric's whole claim — that compilation preserves behavior rather than words — is most literally tested. Everything below CIR-8 describes; CIR-8 is the first representation that acts, and the distance between the two is the distance between an estate that documents its practices and an estate that performs them.

10.2 Describing a process versus compiling one. The distinction CIR-8 turns on is exact and is routinely missed. A description of a process is a narrative that a competent reader could follow: it names the steps in order, it explains the intent, and it reads as complete. A compiled procedure is something else — it is a structure whose every step names the tool that performs it, the inputs it consumes, the contract it must satisfy, the outputs it produces, and the observable that proves it happened, and whose whole is bounded by an activation condition and a done-gate. The description and the compilation can carry identical prose and differ absolutely in what they enable, because the description defers every hard question to the reader's judgment and the compilation answers them in advance. Where a description says verify the result, a compiled procedure names the check, the expected value, and what to do when the value differs. Where a description says roll back if it fails, a compiled procedure names the inverse of each step that has side effects and the order in which they must be undone. The test that separates them is mechanical and is CIR-8's admission gate: can this be executed without a human filling in a gap? A procedure that cannot is a description wearing the costume of a procedure, and it is more dangerous than an honest description, because an actor will attempt to run it and will improvise exactly the parts that were left out — which are, invariably, the parts that were hard.

10.3 The procedural object model. A CIR-8 unit is a DAIF-01 typed object whose slots are the anatomy of an executable. It holds the activation condition — the observable state of the world under which this procedure should run, and it is the first slot and the most frequently absent one. It holds the ordered steps, each a sub-object naming its action, the tool or system that performs it, its inputs, its expected outputs, its side effects, and its per-step check. It holds the branches — the decision points where the procedure forks on an observed condition, each branch naming the condition and the path — because a procedure with no branches is a claim that reality has no cases, and reality always has cases. It holds the tools, hoisted as a declared set so a consumer can verify availability before beginning rather than halting halfway through with a side effect already applied. It holds the contracts each step must honor, which are typed obligations in DAIF-04's sense, referenced rather than restated. It holds the outputs the procedure as a whole produces. It holds the expected errors — the failures the estate has actually seen from this procedure, each with its diagnosis and its response, so that a known failure is handled and not re-diagnosed. It holds the rollback — the inverse sequence for the steps that mutate, ordered so that a partial execution is fully undone. And it holds the done-gate, the observable that proves the procedure achieved its purpose, drawn from CIR-3's definition of done and evaluated by CIR-9's contract. Required are the activation condition, the steps, the outputs, and the done-gate; rollback is required for any procedure with a mutating step, and a mutating procedure that declares no rollback is refused at intake rather than admitted with an implicit hope that nothing goes wrong.

10.4 The activation condition and why it is first. Of all the slots, the activation condition is the one CIR-8 elevates hardest, because it is the slot that determines whether the procedure is ever invoked at all, and a procedure that is never invoked has exactly the value of a procedure that does not exist. An activation condition is a predicate over observable state — this file changed, this obligation aged past its threshold, this evaluation failed, this decision's revision condition fired, this session is starting under this scope — and it must be written so that a consumer can evaluate it without interpreting it. The estate's standing pathology is the procedure whose activation lives in a human's memory: everyone agrees it should be run when the situation calls for it, nobody has written what the situation is, and so it runs when someone happens to remember and does not run when the situation arises and no one does. The compiled activation condition ends this by making invocation a computation. It also makes the procedure auditable in a way a description never is, because a stated condition can be checked against the estate's history — a procedure whose condition fired eleven times and which ran twice is a measurable gap, and a procedure whose condition has never fired is either dead or wrongly specified, and both diagnoses are available only because the condition was written down.

10.5 Invariants and the legal transformations. CIR-8's invariants are four. Executability: every step names its tool, its inputs, and its check, and a step that names none of these is a description and is refused. Reversibility: every mutating step has an inverse, and the rollback sequence is the reverse order of the inverses, because undoing in forward order re-applies a dependency that the later step's inverse assumed. Gate-terminality: the procedure ends at a done-gate that is observed, not asserted, so a procedure that concludes by declaring success is refused under the same reality contract that convicts an obligation marked done without evidence. Idempotence-declaration: the procedure states whether re-running it from the start after a partial failure is safe, and the answer may be no, but it may not be absent, because a consumer recovering from a failure must know whether re-entry is permitted. The legal transformations follow. It is valid to specialize a procedure into a narrower-scoped variant that inherits its steps and tightens its activation condition; valid to add an expected error when a new failure is diagnosed, which is how a procedure hardens over time; valid to supersede a procedure with a better one carrying a supersession pointer. It is invalid to add a mutating step without extending the rollback, invalid to widen an activation condition without evidence that the procedure is correct on the newly-admitted cases, invalid to remove a branch because it has not fired lately, and invalid to weaken the done-gate to make a failing procedure pass — the last being the ratio-shaped self-deception the estate has convicted before, satisfying a gate by lowering it.

10.6 The failure mode of the implicit activation condition. CIR-8's signature failure is the procedure whose activation condition is implicit, and it is the quietest expensive failure in the estate. The procedure exists. It is correct. It is well-written, its steps are sound, its rollback is complete, and it is never invoked, because the only thing that would have invoked it is an actor's memory of its existence at the exact moment it was needed — and the moment it is needed is, by construction, the moment the actor is busy with the problem the procedure would have prevented. Its damage compounds, because the estate, seeing a procedure on disk, believes the situation is handled and does not build a second defense, so the un-invoked procedure occupies the position of a real safeguard while providing none. This is the orphan pattern the estate has recorded in every register — a module nothing calls, a field nothing writes, a watcher nothing wakes — and the procedural stage is where it is closed by construction, because an activation condition is a required slot and a procedure without one is not compilable. The adversarial case is the condition that is written but unobservable — worded so it reads like a predicate and cannot actually be evaluated, appealing to a judgment nobody can compute — and the defense is the same one CIR-2 applies to an unsupported status: the condition must point at observable state, and one that points at nothing is convicted at intake regardless of how confidently it is phrased.

10.7 Linking, lowering, and what consumes CIR-8. CIR-8 sits high in the stack and it lowers everything beneath it. Its steps' contracts are CIR-5 obligations, so a procedure that promises an output owes that output and the obligation is tracked, not assumed. Its done-gate is CIR-9's evaluation contract, referenced rather than re-derived, which is what keeps a procedure from grading its own homework with a standard it invented. Its expected-errors register is fed by CIR-6, since a diagnosed failure is a causal chain and its response is a mechanism-aware fix rather than a symptom patch. Its activation condition is a predicate over exactly the state CIR-7's edges and DAIF-21's synchronization make observable. Above it, CIR-10 promotes a procedure that has proven itself into a canonical pattern the estate runs by default, and CIR-11 admits into a context pack only the procedures whose activation conditions the mission's current reality actually satisfies, which is how a context stays small — a procedure that cannot fire is not shipped to the runtime. The estate's own procedural systems consume CIR-8 directly: one_shot's contracts are procedures with compiled done-gates, and the estate's gate-and-hook machinery is a set of procedures whose activation conditions are the events they bind to. CIR-8 is thus the representation at which the fabric stops describing the estate's competence and starts carrying it.

PART X FINAL LAW. CIR-8 compiles knowledge into executable procedure — activation condition, ordered steps with tools and per-step checks, branches, declared tool set, contracts, outputs, expected errors, rollback, and an observed done-gate — under the admission test that separates a compilation from a description: a procedure that cannot run without a human silently filling a gap is a description in costume, and the gaps it leaves are always the hard parts; its invariants are executability, reversibility, gate-terminality, and declared idempotence, it may never be made to pass by weakening its own gate, and its signature failure is the correct procedure with an implicit activation condition — never invoked because invocation depended on someone remembering it at precisely the moment they were busy with the problem it would have prevented.

PART XI — CIR-9 EVALUATION IR

11.1 Mission. CIR-9 is the stage that compiles verification, and its mission is to attach to every claim, procedure, decision, and output the answer to the question the estate most often leaves unwritten — how would we know. It holds the invariants that must hold, the tests that exercise them, the rubrics that grade what tests cannot, the metrics and their thresholds, the positive cases the thing must handle, the negative cases it must reject, the adversarial cases designed to break it, and the production evidence that it worked where it mattered. This representation exists because the estate's knowledge is dense with instruction and thin with verification: it is full of units that say what to do and nearly empty of units that say how to tell whether doing it worked. Knowledge in that shape produces confident action and no learning, because an action whose success is not measurable cannot teach anything — it cannot be confirmed, it cannot be refuted, and so it accretes as habit rather than as institution. CIR-9 refuses that by making the evaluation contract a compiled artifact that travels with the thing it evaluates, so that an actor receiving a unit receives, in the same object, the instrument that decides whether the unit's promise was kept.

11.2 The evaluation object model. A CIR-9 unit is a DAIF-01 typed object of kind result or evidence, foregrounding an evaluation contract with several slots. It holds the subject — the unit, procedure, artifact, or system this evaluation judges, pointed at by identity so an evaluation is never orphaned from what it evaluates. It holds the invariants — the properties that must hold regardless of input, which are the strongest form of check because they are universally quantified and a single counterexample refutes them. It holds the tests — the executable checks, each with its inputs, its expected observable, and the V-gate identity under which its result is recorded, so a done-gate can find its verdict without parsing prose. It holds the rubrics — the graded criteria for properties that resist a binary check, each naming its criterion and the observed value that scores it, never an opinion. It holds the metrics and their thresholds, and the thresholds are keyed to the environment they were measured in, because a threshold detached from its substrate is a number that will be compared against a world it was never valid in. It holds the case sets: positive cases the subject must handle, negative cases it must refuse, and adversarial cases constructed to break it. And it holds production evidence — the observation that the subject worked, or failed, in live use — which outranks every synthetic case, because reality is the estate's supreme instrument and a passing test suite is a hypothesis about reality until reality answers.

11.3 Composition with SQI's verification axis. CIR-9 does not build a verification engine. SQI is the estate's sealed authority on the verification axis — the gates, the guardian that refuses a silent decrease, the discipline that a verdict is computed and not asserted — and CIR-9 composes it, referencing SQI's verification semantics as the law its evaluation contracts are written under rather than deriving a parallel notion of what passing means. This composition is load-bearing for one specific reason: a second verification standard would be able to disagree with the first, and two standards that can disagree about whether a thing passed produce the worst possible governance object, a system that is simultaneously green and red, which resolves in practice to whichever verdict the actor under pressure prefers. By deferring, CIR-9's contracts are graded by the standard the estate already enforces, and the guardian that refuses a silent decrease guards CIR-9's thresholds as it guards every other. CIR-9's contribution is upstream of SQI's engine: it is the representation of what must be checked, compiled from the source alongside the claim it checks, so that SQI is handed a contract to run rather than left to infer one from a document nobody wrote a gate for.

11.4 The rule that an unverifiable claim is a hypothesis. CIR-9's cardinal law is short and unforgiving: a claim for which no evaluation can be constructed is not a weak fact, it is a hypothesis, and it is typed as one by CIR-2 regardless of the confidence of its author or the seniority of its source. The rule is unforgiving because the alternative is the estate's most corrosive habit — the plausible unfalsifiable assertion, which passes every review precisely because there is nothing to check it against, and which then grounds decisions, seeds procedures, and eventually hardens into an institutional law that no evidence ever supported and no evidence can ever remove. The demand that a claim name its falsifier is the ACIS discipline the type system already inherited, and CIR-9 is where it becomes operational: the evaluation contract is the falsifier, made concrete. This gives the estate a mechanical test at the moment a claim is compiled — construct its evaluation; if none can be constructed, the claim is a hypothesis and enters the stack at that level, where it can still be reasoned with, still ground a provisional decision, and still be marked openly as what it is. What it may not do is present as established. The rule also cuts the other way and this matters: it forbids the theatrical evaluation, the contract that exists and checks nothing, since a test that would pass regardless of whether the subject works is not evidence but its costume, and the estate has recorded that a gate must be observed to refuse before it may be trusted to approve.

11.5 Invariants, case sets, and the adversarial obligation. Three invariants govern a CIR-9 unit. Subject-boundedness: an evaluation judges exactly one subject and its verdict is claimed for nothing else, because a verdict borrowed across subjects is how a passing check on one path certifies an untested one. Threshold-keying: every metric threshold names the environment and the baseline it was set against, because a threshold that floats free of its substrate is the drift the estate's guardian exists to catch. Refusal-provenness: a contract must be demonstrated to fail on a subject that ought to fail, so both poles are reachable and the gate is known to be live rather than assumed to be. The case sets carry a further obligation. Positive cases prove the subject does what it should; negative cases prove it refuses what it should not, and they are the ones most often absent, because writing them requires imagining the misuse; adversarial cases prove the subject survives an input designed by someone who wants it to break, and they are the ones almost always absent, because writing them requires adopting a hostile stance toward one's own work. CIR-9 requires all three sets to be present, and permits any of them to be honestly empty only with a recorded reason, since an empty adversarial set with no reason is not an absence of adversaries but an absence of imagination, and the estate will meet the adversary regardless of whether it was imagined.

11.6 The failure mode of unverifiable knowledge. CIR-9's signature failure is knowledge that says what to do and never how to know it worked — an instruction, a procedure, an institutional pattern, all of them actionable and none of them checkable. The damage is subtle because nothing visibly breaks: the actor performs the instruction, believes it succeeded because nothing contradicted the belief, and the estate accumulates a body of practice that is never confirmed and never refuted and therefore never improves. Unverifiable knowledge is not merely unproven; it is unimprovable, because improvement requires a measurement and there is none, so the practice ossifies at whatever quality it happened to have on the day it was written. Worse, unverifiable knowledge is what recurring errors hide behind: an estate whose procedures carry no done-gates cannot distinguish a procedure that is failing from one that is fine, so a systematically broken practice can run for a year while everyone assumes it is working, which is precisely the shape of every orphaned watcher and dead pipeline the estate has had to find by accident. The adversarial case is the evaluation that is present, executable, green, and vacuous — a check whose condition is trivially satisfiable, a gate whose universe of countable things is empty so its count can never fall, a rubric whose criteria are met by any output at all. CIR-9 convicts these through refusal-provenness: a gate that has never been shown to fail on a subject that deserves failure is not a gate but a decoration, and a green verdict from a decoration is worse than no verdict, because it converts an unknown into a false certainty that nobody will revisit.

11.7 What consumes CIR-9. CIR-9 is read across the whole upper stack and by the estate's acting systems. CIR-2 reads evaluation results as the evidence pointer that grounds an epistemic status, and it is the arrival of a CIR-9 verdict that permits a unit to move from pending-verification to verified — the only legitimate promotion path, since the ACIS ladder admits no other. CIR-5 reads an evaluation as an obligation's required evidence, so an obligation is discharged by a verdict rather than by a claim of completion. CIR-6 reads it as the instrument that confirms or refutes a mechanism, closing the causal loop. CIR-8 embeds it as the procedure's done-gate. CIR-10 reads it as the promotion gate — a pattern may not become an institutional law without evaluations that demonstrate it holds, which is the mechanism by which the fabric refuses to elevate a preference. DAIF-03's fidelity gate is itself an evaluation contract over the compilation, and DAIF-07 reads evaluation verdicts as the closure evidence its lifecycle demands. CIR-9 is therefore the representation through which the entire fabric touches reality: everything else in the stack is an account of what the estate believes, and CIR-9 is the account of how it found out.

PART XI FINAL LAW. CIR-9 compiles verification as a contract that travels with the thing it judges — invariants, tests with V-gate identities, rubrics scored by observed values rather than opinion, metrics whose thresholds are keyed to the environment they were measured in, positive and negative and adversarial case sets, and production evidence that outranks every synthetic case — composing SQI's verification axis rather than forking a second standard that could disagree with the first; its cardinal law is that a claim for which no evaluation can be constructed is a hypothesis and is typed as one, its guard against the vacuous gate is refusal-provenness, and its signature failure is knowledge that says what to do and never how to know it worked, which is not merely unproven but unimprovable, ossified at whatever quality it happened to have the day it was written.

PART XII — CIR-10 INSTITUTIONAL IR

12.1 Mission. CIR-10 is the stage at which knowledge becomes law, and its mission is to hold the units that have earned standing over the estate as a whole — the universal laws, the doctrines, the canonical patterns, the register of recurring errors, the vaccines that stop them, the standards, the contracts, and the precedence policies by which the estate ranks its own commitments. Everything below CIR-10 describes a particular: a source, a unit, a decision, a mechanism, a procedure. CIR-10 holds the general, and generality is expensive — a unit at this stage governs work that has not been done yet, in projects that do not exist yet, by actors who will never see the incident that produced it. That is precisely its value and precisely its danger, and the whole design of the stage is a response to that asymmetry. A law that is right compounds across every future effort; a law that is wrong compounds identically, and the estate pays it forever without knowing it is paying.

12.2 The institutional object model. A CIR-10 unit is a DAIF-01 typed object of kind law, pattern, or definition, and its slots are what make a general rule governable. It holds the statement — the rule itself, imperative and terse, phrased so a consumer knows what it must do. It holds the trigger — the situation in which the rule fires, an activation condition in CIR-8's sense, because a law with no trigger is a sentiment. It holds the evidence base — the instances that justify the generalization, each pointing at the CIR-0 source and the CIR-9 evaluation that recorded it, and their count is not decoration but the load-bearing measure of whether a generalization was ever warranted. It holds the mechanism — the CIR-6 causal chain that explains why the rule holds, without which the rule is a correlation the estate has agreed to obey. It holds the conditions and exceptions inherited from that chain, so a law knows the territory in which it is a law. It holds the authority that promoted it and the scope over which it binds, both from DAIF-01, and a law's scope is the field most likely to be inflated at promotion. It holds strength and override-policy, since even a law must state how it yields. And it holds the vaccine — the concrete mechanism that enforces it, whether a gate, a hook, a check, or a compiled procedure — because an institutional rule with no enforcement mechanism is a wish, and the estate has learned that a rule which lives only in a document is obeyed exactly as often as it is remembered.

12.3 Composition with UKDL and hard_rules. CIR-10 does not create a new body of law. The estate already holds two: UKDL is the growing library of its distilled lessons, and hard_rules is the sealed archive of its bug-stops, each entry born of a real incident and each carrying the trigger, the stop, the exception phrase, and the origin that the estate's kill-switch contract requires. CIR-10 composes both — a compiled institutional unit is the typed projection of a UKDL entry or a hard_rule, and the rules of promotion, precedence, and enforcement are theirs, referenced and never re-derived. This matters most at the point of enforcement: a hard_rule is not merely a strongly-worded law but a rule with a kill-switch mechanism behind it, and CIR-10 inherits that standing rather than inventing a parallel severity scale that would rank a compiled unit against a hard_rule as an equal. The estate has already paid the price of a law whose enforcement path did not exist — a kill switch that could not fire because the archive it named was unreadable at the point of use — and the lesson CIR-10 encodes from it is that the vaccine slot is not optional metadata but the difference between a law and a preference with strong adjectives. A CIR-10 unit whose vaccine slot names no mechanism is admitted only as a doctrine, never as a law.

12.4 Promotion is Owner-gated and the fabric never self-promotes. The cardinal law of this stage is that the fabric proposes and never promotes. A compiled unit may accumulate every qualification for institutional standing — a mechanism, a dozen confirming instances, evaluations that demonstrate it holds, a vaccine ready to enforce it — and it still does not become law by accumulating them. It becomes a candidate, and the candidacy is surfaced to the Owner, and the Owner promotes it or does not. This is ACIS's No-Autopromotion cap applied at the highest stage of the stack, and it is the same discipline that caps a deposit at the epistemic level its evidence earned rather than the level its author hoped for. The reasoning is that promotion is an act of authority and not an act of evidence: evidence establishes that a rule is true, and authority establishes that the estate will be bound by it, and these are different claims that only an authority entitled to bind the estate may join. A fabric that could promote its own findings into law would be a system that grants itself governing power over every future actor on the strength of its own reasoning, which is exactly the elevation path DAIF-01's forged-authority detection forecloses at the unit level and which CIR-10 forecloses at the institutional one. What the fabric may do — and must do — is make the candidacy legible: assemble the evidence base, name the mechanism, state the proposed scope and strength, attach the vaccine, and present the whole as a decision the Owner can take on the evidence rather than on the fabric's insistence.

12.5 Precedence, doctrines, and the register of recurring errors. Not every institutional unit is a law, and the stage types the register carefully. A doctrine is a general commitment that guides without binding — it names a direction the estate leans and yields to a specific rule that contradicts it. A canonical pattern is a shape the estate has found repeatedly effective and adopts by default, and its force is that of a default rather than a wall: departing from it is permitted and must be recorded. A standard fixes a form — a naming, a structure, an interface — and its authority is definitional. A recurring error is a unit of a distinct kind: it names a failure the estate has committed more than once, and its value is not prescriptive but diagnostic, since an actor who recognizes the shape of a recurring error in its current situation has been handed a diagnosis it would otherwise reach by suffering. A vaccine is the mechanism paired to a recurring error, and the pairing is required in both directions — a recurring error with no vaccine is an open wound the estate has merely named, and a vaccine with no recurring error behind it is a gate defending against a threat nobody has observed, which is over-engineering wearing the costume of caution. Precedence policies are the units that rank all the others, and they are inherited whole from DAIF-01's authority ladder and the estate's precedence law rather than restated, so that the institutional stage ranks its own contents by the same rule the type system ranks any two units.

12.6 The failure mode of the silently elevated preference. CIR-10's signature failure is a preference that becomes a law without evidence or authority — a leaning that someone wrote imperatively, that nobody challenged, that was copied forward, and that now binds actors who have no idea it was ever optional. Its mechanism is entirely social and entirely mechanical at once. A preference is phrased as an instruction because instructions are terser than hedged suggestions. The instruction is written into a document that mostly holds real rules, and it inherits their apparent standing by proximity. A later actor, reading a document of rules, obeys all of them, because nothing distinguishes the one whose evidence base is empty. And the estate is now enforcing, universally and permanently, a leaning that one actor had one afternoon — with no mechanism to explain it, no evidence to test it, no authority that granted it, and, most damaging, no way for a future actor to appeal it, because an appeal requires a justification to argue with and there is none. The compounding harm is that this counterfeit law degrades the real ones: an actor who discovers that one rule in the register was never grounded rationally discounts all of them, and the estate's genuine hard-won bug-stops lose the deference they were paid for in incidents. CIR-10's defense is structural rather than vigilant: the evidence base and the mechanism are required slots, the vaccine is required for law-strength, the promotion is Owner-gated, and a unit that cannot fill these is admitted at the standing it can support — a pattern, a doctrine, a preference — and is honestly labeled as such, which is the entire difference between an estate whose laws are trustworthy and one whose laws are merely numerous.

12.7 What consumes CIR-10. CIR-10's units are read by every actor and every context in the estate. CIR-11's context assembly reads them as the hard constraints slot of every mission pack, and it admits them by scope, so a universal law is present in every pack and a project law only in its project's, which is how the isolation discipline holds while the laws still bind. CIR-8's procedures reference them as the contracts their steps must honor. CIR-4's decisions cite them as the constraints under which a choice was made, which is what lets a later actor see that a decision was bounded rather than free. CIR-9 evaluates them, since a law's continued truth is a claim like any other and the recurring-error register is refreshed by the evaluations that catch a regression. DAIF-00's constitution ranks them, DAIF-07 tracks the obligations they create, and DAIF-21's synchronization watches their conditions for the day a substrate change makes a law obsolete, which is the moment a law must be retired rather than obeyed out of habit. CIR-10 is thus the estate's memory of what it has learned that generalizes — carefully bounded, mechanically grounded, enforced by a named vaccine, promoted only by the authority entitled to bind, and retired the moment its mechanism stops applying.

PART XII FINAL LAW. CIR-10 holds the knowledge that has earned standing over the whole estate — universal laws, doctrines, canonical patterns, recurring errors and their paired vaccines, standards, contracts, and precedence policies — as typed units carrying a statement, a trigger, an evidence base, a mechanism, conditions and exceptions, authority, scope, strength, override-policy, and a named enforcement vaccine without which a rule is admitted as doctrine and never as law; it composes UKDL and hard_rules rather than founding a second body of law, and its inviolable discipline is ACIS's No-Autopromotion applied at the top of the stack — the fabric assembles the candidacy and the Owner promotes, because evidence establishes that a rule is true while only an authority entitled to bind the estate can make it binding, and the failure this forecloses is the silently elevated preference that governs forever on the strength of one afternoon and degrades every genuine law it sits beside.

PART XIII — CIR-11 EXECUTABLE CONTEXT IR

13.1 Mission. CIR-11 is the last representation in the stack and the only one an acting runtime ever sees directly, and its mission is severe: to hand a specific mission exactly what it needs to reason correctly, and nothing else. Every stage below it has been additive — CIR-0 retained, CIR-1 decomposed, CIR-2 typed, and the stack accreted dimensions of meaning until the estate's whole knowledge was represented. CIR-11 inverts the movement. It is the selective stage, and its discipline is subtraction under a hard constraint: the pack it produces must be sufficient, meaning an actor reasoning from it alone reaches the conclusions an actor with the whole estate would reach, and it must be minimal, meaning nothing in it is there because it was related rather than because it was needed. These two demands are in genuine tension and the tension is not resolvable by good intentions — it is resolved only by the mission, which is the sole thing that can distinguish the necessary from the merely relevant, and this is why CIR-11 is not an index over the estate's knowledge but a compilation against a stated mission.

13.2 The sub-structure of a context pack. A CIR-11 unit is a context pack, and its sub-structure is eleven parts, each drawn from a stage below. The mission contract states what this reasoning session is for, its intent, its expected result, and its definition of done, compiled from CIR-3 — and it comes first because everything after it is selected relative to it. The hard constraints hold the CIR-10 laws and the hard-strength CIR-1 units whose scope reaches this mission, and they are admitted in full and never abridged, because a constraint summarized is a constraint whose condition may have been the part that was cut. The relevant decisions hold the CIR-4 units that bind this mission's territory — the choices already made that this session must honor or explicitly revisit — each with its justification, since a decision without its reason cannot be safely revisited and a session that cannot revisit is a session that will either violate the decision or be paralyzed by it. Current reality holds the observed state: what is on disk, what is running, what passed, what failed, the CIR-9 verdicts and CIR-0 observations that describe the world as it is rather than as the plan assumed. The minimal architecture slice holds only the structure the mission touches, expanded from CIR-7's edges along the hard dependencies of the mission's seed units and stopped there. The applicable procedures hold the CIR-8 units whose activation conditions the current reality actually satisfies. The known failure modes hold the CIR-10 recurring errors and CIR-6 causal chains whose conditions match this mission's situation. The evaluation contract holds the CIR-9 checks by which this mission's output will be judged, and it is present at the start rather than assembled at the end, because a mission that learns its standard only at the gate has been reasoning without one. The open obligations hold the CIR-5 items this mission owes or is blocked by. The provenance map holds the chain from every admitted unit back to its CIR-0 source, so any claim in the pack can be audited without leaving it. And the expansion handles hold the pointers by which the actor can pull more — the units deliberately left out, named but not included, so the pack's boundary is visible rather than silent.

13.3 The selection law. What governs admission into a pack is a single rule: a unit is admitted if the mission's correct execution depends on it, and excluded otherwise, and relatedness is not dependence. This distinction is the whole engineering of the stage and it is harder than it sounds, because relatedness is cheap to compute and dependence is not — a graph traversal from the mission's seed will return everything connected, and everything connected is, in a well-linked estate, very nearly everything. The selection therefore proceeds by kind rather than by proximity. Hard constraints in scope are admitted unconditionally, because their whole nature is that they bind whether or not the mission expected them. Decisions are admitted if they bind the mission's territory, which is a scope test, not a similarity test. Procedures are admitted if their activation condition evaluates true against current reality, which is a computation and not a judgment. Failure modes are admitted if their conditions match, again a computation. The architecture slice is admitted by hard-dependency closure from the seed, bounded by the closure's own edge strengths so a soft edge does not drag in a subtree. What is never admitted is a unit that is merely about the same subject, and the estate's instinct to include it — because it might be useful, because it is nearby, because leaving it out feels like a risk — is exactly the instinct CIR-11 exists to discipline.

13.4 Expansion handles and the honest boundary. A minimal pack is a bet, and every bet can be wrong, so CIR-11 ships the correction with the pack. Expansion handles are the named, typed pointers to what was deliberately excluded: the units one hop beyond the admitted closure, the procedures whose activation conditions did not fire, the decisions in adjacent scopes, each named with enough identity that an actor who discovers a gap can pull precisely what it needs rather than requesting the estate. Handles are what make minimality safe, because they convert an exclusion from a silent loss into a declared boundary — the actor knows the pack has edges and knows where they are. This is the same discipline DAIF-01 draws between a value and an absence: a unit excluded and named is a decision the actor can act on, and a unit excluded and unmentioned is indistinguishable from a unit that does not exist. It also gives the fabric its only honest feedback loop on selection quality, since a handle that is pulled frequently is evidence that the selection law is drawing its boundary in the wrong place for this class of mission, and that evidence is a CIR-9 verdict on the assembler itself.

13.5 Invariants and the transformations. Four invariants govern a pack. Sufficiency: an actor reasoning from the pack alone must reach the conclusions an actor with the estate would reach, and this is testable — the pack is graded by running the mission against it and comparing the outcome, which is the behavioral-equivalence standard DAIF-03 gates the whole fabric on. Minimality: every admitted unit must be attributable to a rule of the selection law, and a unit that cannot be attributed is removed, which makes minimality auditable rather than aspirational. Constraint-integrity: hard constraints are admitted whole, never summarized, because the abridgment of a constraint is the boundary failure CIR-1 convicts, and a pack that shortens a law to save room has traded the one thing it may not trade. Boundary-honesty: everything excluded that a reasonable actor might need is named in the handles. The legal transformations are narrow. It is valid to re-assemble a pack when reality changes, since a pack is a claim about the present and DAIF-21's synchronization is what tells it the present moved. It is valid to expand a pack through its handles mid-mission. It is invalid to widen a pack by relatedness, invalid to abridge a constraint to fit a size target, and invalid to carry a pack forward into a different mission — a pack is compiled against one mission contract and is not a reusable digest, and reusing it is the category error of mistaking a selection for a summary.

13.6 The failure mode of the complete-but-defeating pack. CIR-11's signature failure is the pack that is complete and therefore useless — every relevant unit admitted, nothing omitted, sufficiency guaranteed, and so large that the actor reasoning over it cannot find the three units that mattered. This is the failure that makes CIR-11 necessary at all, because it is the failure the naive solution produces: faced with the risk of omitting something important, an assembler admits everything connected, and the pack becomes the estate. The damage is not merely inefficiency. An over-large pack degrades reasoning actively, because relevance is a signal and a pack that admits everything transmits none — the actor cannot tell the hard constraint from the passing note, since both are present with equal prominence, and the pack has silently pushed the selection problem onto the actor at exactly the moment the actor is least able to solve it, mid-mission and under load. It also defeats the fabric's whole thesis, which was never that knowledge should be complete but that it should provoke the right behavior, and a pack that provokes paralysis has failed by the only standard that matters. The adversarial case is the mission whose genuine dependence really is enormous — a broad refactor touching many systems — and the answer is not to admit everything but to recognize that the mission is under-decomposed: a mission whose minimal sufficient pack is unmanageable is a mission that should be split, and CIR-11 surfaces that as a finding rather than absorbing it as a bloated pack.

13.7 The handoff to DAIF-08. CIR-11 is the fabric's terminal output and its consumer is named: DAIF-08, the Context Assembly and Mission Runtime, is the acting system that receives context packs and runs missions over them. The seam is the estate's standing division between representation and runtime — CIR-11 is the inert compiled artifact that says what a mission needs, and DAIF-08 is the runtime that assembles it against live reality, hands it to an actor, expands it through its handles when the actor pulls, and re-assembles it when DAIF-21 reports that reality moved beneath it. Everything upstream of this seam is the fabric's business and nothing downstream of it is: the fabric does not schedule missions, does not dispatch actors, and does not manage a session, exactly as DAIF-01 defines a type system and runs no pipeline. What the fabric guarantees to DAIF-08 is a pack that is sufficient, minimal, constraint-intact, and boundary-honest, with a provenance map that makes every claim in it auditable back to a CIR-0 source. What DAIF-08 owes the fabric in return is the evidence of how its packs performed — which handles were pulled, which missions failed for want of an excluded unit, which packs were too large to reason over — and that evidence is the CIR-9 contract on the assembler, which is how the selection law improves by measurement rather than by taste.

PART XIII FINAL LAW. CIR-11 compiles a context pack against one stated mission — mission contract, hard constraints admitted whole, relevant decisions with their justifications, current reality, a minimal architecture slice, the procedures whose activation conditions actually fire, the matching failure modes, the evaluation contract present at the start rather than assembled at the gate, open obligations, a provenance map, and expansion handles that make the boundary honest — under a selection law that admits dependence and refuses mere relatedness, because the failure it exists to defeat is the pack that is complete and defeats its own purpose by transmitting no signal at all; it is the fabric's terminal artifact and DAIF-08's input, and a mission whose minimal sufficient pack is unmanageable is not a pack to inflate but a mission to split.

PART XIV — THE SEMANTIC LINKING ENGINE

14.1 Mission. The twelve representations produce units, and units alone are a pile. A pile of true statements is not intelligence — it is an archive, and an archive answers only the question you already knew to ask of it. The linking engine is what converts the pile into a graph: a set of typed relations between units, each carrying its own evidence and confidence, over which the estate can navigate, propagate, invalidate, and reason. The mission of this Part is to specify those relations exactly — what each means, what it licenses a consumer to do, and what it forbids — because a link type whose semantics are vague is worse than no link at all, licensing a traversal that carries a conclusion where it was never entitled to go. Linking is where the fabric stops being a compiler and starts being an intelligence, because every capability the upper stack claimed — impact analysis, weakest-link propagation, supersession, conflict detection, context expansion — is a traversal over these edges, and none of them exists without them.

14.2 The link register. Sixteen link types are defined and the set is closed, for the same reason DAIF-01's type taxonomy is closed: a consumer cannot reason mechanically over a relation it has never seen. Supports means the source unit raises the confidence of the target, and it is the edge CIR-2 traverses upward. Contradicts means the two units cannot both hold, and it is the edge that constructs a conflict. Supersedes means the source replaces the target, retiring it under DAIF-01's validity semantics. Depends_on means the target is a precondition of the source, in one of CIR-7's four kinds. Implements means the source is a concrete realization of the target — a procedure implementing a law, a vaccine implementing a rule. Evaluates means the source is the instrument by which the target is checked, the CIR-9 seam. Violates means the source is an observed breach of the target, and it is the edge that turns a constraint into a live finding. Derives_from means the source was inferred from the target, and it is the provenance edge the epistemic ladder walks to cap a derivation at its ground's level. Generalizes and specializes are the two directions of the scope relation — a law generalizing a pattern, a project rule specializing a universal one — and they are typed separately because their traversal semantics are opposite. Duplicates means two units express the same content, the edge the estate's duplicate-to-advantage engine consumes. Exemplifies means the source is an instance of the target's general shape, and it is what fills a pattern's evidence base. Blocks means the source prevents the target from proceeding, the edge CIR-5 reads to compute a blocked obligation. Activates means the source's truth fires the target's activation condition, the edge that makes a procedure invocable. Invalidates means the source's arrival destroys the target's validity, the edge DAIF-21 writes when reality overtakes a claim. And closes means the source discharges the target, the edge that retires an obligation on evidence.

14.3 The edge payload. Every link is itself a typed object and carries the same discipline the units do. It carries its type from the closed register. It carries direction, since almost none of the sixteen is symmetric and a symmetric reading of an asymmetric edge is how a support becomes a circular one. It carries evidence — how the estate knows the relation holds, whether an observation, a declaration at compile time, or an inference — because an asserted edge with no evidence is a guess about the estate's own reasoning, and a graph of guesses traversed confidently produces conclusions with no ground beneath them. It carries confidence, which propagates: a conclusion reached across three edges of moderate confidence is not a moderately-confident conclusion but a weak one, and the traversal must carry the weakest link forward exactly as CIR-2 does with evidential support. And it carries scope and validity from DAIF-01, because a relation that held in one project need not hold in another, and a relation can be retired — a supersedes edge drawn on a mistaken supersession is itself superseded, not deleted, since the record of a reversed belief is knowledge.

14.4 Composition with GK-04. The linking engine builds no graph. GK-04's typed edge registry is the estate's sealed graph substrate, and the sixteen link types are registered as GK-04 edge kinds, held, indexed, and traversed there. This is the same refusal CIR-7 makes and for the same reason, but the stakes at this Part are higher, because the linking engine is where a second graph is most tempting: the fabric's edges feel semantic and GK-04's feel structural, and the distinction is exactly the seductive one that produces two registries which agree for a month and diverge forever after. They are not two graphs. A structural edge and a semantic edge are two kinds in one register, and the estate's ability to answer a question that crosses them — which decisions depend on a module that is about to change — exists only because they share a substrate. What the fabric contributes is the semantics: the sixteen kinds, their payload requirements, and the traversal rules that say what each licenses. GK-04 holds and serves them; the fabric defines what they mean; neither re-narrates the other.

14.5 What linking makes possible. Each traversal is a capability the estate cannot have otherwise. Following supports and derives_from backwards from a claim yields its grounding chain, and the weakest link on that chain is the claim's real epistemic ceiling, which is how CIR-2's propagation is computed rather than asserted. Following depends_on forwards from a proposed change yields its blast radius, which is how a change is assessed before it lands. Following supersedes forwards from any unit yields the current one, which is how the estate never enforces a retired rule by finding it first. Following exemplifies backwards from a pattern yields its evidence base, which is how CIR-10 decides whether a generalization is warranted or is one afternoon's preference wearing a general shape. Following evaluates from any claim yields its falsifier, and a claim with no evaluates edge is, by CIR-9's cardinal law, a hypothesis. Following activates into the procedure set yields exactly the procedures the current reality invokes, which is how CIR-11 keeps a context pack small. Following blocks and closes across the obligation set yields the actionable queue DAIF-07 works. None of these is a search over prose; each is a traversal over typed edges, and the difference between an estate that can perform them and one that cannot is the difference between institutional intelligence and an archive with good intentions.

14.6 The contradicts edge and the typed conflict object. One link type has a special destiny. A contradicts edge is not merely an annotation that two units disagree; it is the trigger that constructs the typed conflict object DAIF-01 Part XIII defines, and the two are one mechanism seen at two moments. The edge is drawn when two units sharing a subject — resolved through the estate's canonical identity so an alias cannot hide the disagreement — are found to make claims that cannot both hold within an overlapping scope. Scope resolution runs first, exactly as that Part requires, because most apparent contradictions are scope illusions and constructing conflict objects for them floods the estate with false disagreements that bury the real ones. Where the scopes do overlap, the edge fires, and the conflict object is built from the two units' fields — claims, scopes, authorities, evidence, recency, possible resolution, and the consequence of leaving it unresolved — and handed to the precedence ladder for a ranked resolution or to DAIF-21 when the disagreement is really reality overtaking a stale unit. The edge's discipline is that it may never be silently dropped: a contradicts edge stands until an authorized resolution retires it, because deleting the edge to make the graph look consistent is the silently-harmonized contradiction that Part convicts, performed at the graph layer where it is even harder to see.

14.7 The failure mode of the unlinked unit. The linking engine's signature failure is the pile: units that are individually true, individually well-typed, individually provenanced, and connected to nothing. An unlinked unit is not neutral. It is invisible in exactly the traversals that would have surfaced it — it is never reached by an impact analysis, never pulled into a context pack, never counted in an evidence base, never found by a supersession walk — and so it exerts no force on the estate's reasoning while occupying space in the estate's record and, worst of all, creating the appearance that the knowledge is held. An estate of unlinked units believes it knows things it cannot use, which is a strictly worse position than knowing it does not know them, because the belief suppresses the second attempt. The adversarial case is the plausibly-linked unit: edges drawn by surface similarity rather than by real relation, so a traversal returns a rich neighborhood of loosely-associated content and the consumer, receiving abundance, mistakes it for relevance. The defense is the payload — an edge must carry its evidence, and an edge whose evidence is that two units mention the same words is typed at the confidence such an inference deserves, so a traversal can refuse to carry a conclusion across it. Linking is what makes the fabric an intelligence, and the discipline that keeps the intelligence honest is that a relation, like a claim, must be grounded to be trusted, and an edge asserted without evidence is exactly as inadmissible as a fact asserted without one.

PART XIV FINAL LAW. The semantic linking engine converts a pile of typed units into a navigable intelligence graph through sixteen closed link types — supports, contradicts, supersedes, depends_on, implements, evaluates, violates, derives_from, generalizes, specializes, duplicates, exemplifies, blocks, activates, invalidates, and closes — each edge itself a typed object carrying direction, evidence, confidence, scope, and validity, and each registered into GK-04's typed edge registry rather than into a parallel graph that would agree for a month and diverge forever; every capability the fabric claimed is a traversal over these edges, a contradicts edge is precisely the trigger that constructs DAIF-01's typed conflict object and may never be silently dropped to make the graph look consistent, and the failure it exists to defeat is the unlinked unit — true, well-typed, reachable by no traversal, exerting no force on the estate's reasoning while creating the belief that the knowledge is held.

PART XV — THE OPTIMIZER PIPELINE

15.1 Mission and the law every pass obeys. The twelve representations and the linking engine together produce a graph that is correct and not yet economical. It holds the same law expressed four times by four sources, the same decision restated in three conversations, the ornamental scaffolding of the prose it was extracted from, and units that were true in March and are refuted by June. The optimizer is the fabric's middle end: a pipeline of named passes, each taking a declared input class of units and edges, performing one declared transformation, emitting a declared output class, and each gated. The gate is not a size target. It is the behavioral-equivalence gate DAIF-03 owns, and the law it enforces admits no exemption: a pass may change the representation of knowledge, and may never change the behavior the knowledge provokes. Every pass therefore carries three artifacts — a purpose, stated as the specific waste it removes; a precondition, stated as the property of the input that makes the removal safe; and a fidelity check, stated as the behavior that must be identical before and after. A pass with no fidelity check is not an optimization but a deletion with a good story. This is why the pipeline is a register of specific passes rather than a general instruction to be concise: indiscriminate compression cannot distinguish what it is removing, and a compressor that cannot name what it removed cannot be held to account when a mission fails for want of it.

15.2 Exact-duplicate elimination and reference conservation. The first pass is the safe one. Its input is a set of units whose canonical content hashes agree — the same hash CIR-0 uses for incremental acquisition. Its transformation is to elect one survivor and merge the rest into it. Its precondition is total identity: not similar content but identical content, including the fields a careless implementation forgets to hash, and that omission is the pass's one real failure mode. Two prohibitions with the same sentence and different scopes are not duplicates; they are two laws, one universal and one project-local, and the local one may be the exception the universal one tolerates. Two claims with the same sentence and different epistemic statuses are not duplicates; one is observed and one assumed, and either merge manufactures a status that was never earned. The hash therefore covers content, scope, authority, validity, and epistemic status, and a difference in any of them defeats the pass and hands the pair to semantic deduplication instead. The invariant is reference conservation: every inbound and outbound edge of every merged member is re-pointed to the survivor, every CIR-0 provenance locator is inherited so all the original sources remain traceable, and every obligation, evaluation, and conflict pointer follows. The check is arithmetic — the edge count touching the merged class is identical before and after, less true self-loops — and a pass that drops one edge has silently orphaned whatever depended on it. The adversarial case is the actor who normalizes away the scope field to force a merge that removes an inconvenient local exception, and the defense is that scope is part of the hashed identity, so an exception cannot be deduplicated out of existence.

15.3 Semantic deduplication without erasure of material difference. The second pass is the dangerous one, and it is deliberately not a deletion pass. Its input is the unit groups the linker joined with duplicates edges, plus the candidates the sealed D2A engine proposes under the duplicate ontology DAIF-00 governs; the pipeline consumes that verdict and never re-derives it. Its transformation is grouping: it elects a canonical formulation and retains every variant as a member with its own provenance and edges intact. Nothing is erased. The gain is that a consumer traverses to one canonical unit instead of four near-identical ones, and that four sources of the same law now visibly constitute four independent attestations of it, which is an epistemic asset CIR-2 can use rather than noise to be swept away. The precondition is the material-difference test, and it is behavioral rather than lexical: two formulations are equivalent only if every consumer's behavior is identical under either. Differing conditions are material. Differing exceptions are material. Differing thresholds are material, and a numeric threshold is the most commonly destroyed material difference in the estate, because two rules that differ only in a number read as restatements of each other to any similarity metric. Differing authorities are material, since a preference and a law are not one unit wearing two costumes. The signature failure is near-duplicate collapse: four formulations of a rule, one of which carries an exception, merged onto a canonical that reads cleanly and no longer says the thing that mattered. The defense is structural — a group whose members disagree on any material field is not a duplicate group but a specialization family, and the pass hands it to 15.8, which knows how to keep the exception alive.

15.4 Ornamental-language elimination and dead-knowledge exclusion. The third pass removes what carries no meaning: narrative connectives, restatement, courtesy, apology, meta-commentary about the conversation the knowledge came from, and the rhetorical scaffolding that made prose readable and makes a compiled unit longer without making it more binding. Its precondition is the hardest line in the pipeline to draw, because ornament and qualification look identical from a distance. A hedge is not ornament. The word expressing that a claim is believed rather than observed is a CIR-2 epistemic marker, and a pass that strips it has performed epistemic collapse by copy-editing. The rule is therefore that any span carrying a condition, a qualifier, an exception, a scope, an authority, or an epistemic status is not ornament by definition, however conversational it sounds; ornament is only what can be deleted with no consumer's behavior changing, which is the pipeline's own gate applied at the word. The fourth pass is dead-knowledge exclusion, and its name is precise about what it does not do. A superseded or refuted unit is removed from the active runtime — not admitted to a context pack, not returned by a query for current law, not counted as support for a claim — and retained in the historical record permanently. It is never deleted. The record of a reversed belief is knowledge, as the linking engine already establishes for a retired edge, and the estate needs it in three places: DAIF-21 to recognize that reality has moved back, CIR-6 to explain why a mechanism no longer holds, and CIR-10 to distinguish a rule that was never true from one that stopped being true.

15.5 Constant folding and decision folding. Constant folding precomputes a conclusion that always follows under fixed conditions. Its input is a chain of CIR-6 causal or CIR-7 dependency edges in which every intermediate condition is invariant across the scopes the estate operates in; its output is a single folded unit carrying the conclusion directly, with the folded chain retained as its derivation. The precondition is the fixity of the conditions, and this is where it fails: a condition that is invariant today because nothing has yet varied it is not invariant, it is merely unchallenged, and folding across it produces a conclusion that outlives its own premises with no visible seam. The countermeasure is that a folded unit carries its folding conditions as an explicit guard, so DAIF-21 can invalidate the fold the moment a condition it was folded across changes, and the derivation is retained so the fold can be unfolded rather than reconstructed from scratch. Decision folding is the same operation in the CIR-4 layer. Its input is a family of related decisions — twelve rulings that converged on one pattern — and its output is a canonical policy stating the pattern once. It is not a merge. Every constituent decision is retained with its own question, options, justification, evidence, authority, and date, and the policy holds pointers to all of them, because a policy without its constituent decisions is the reasonless conclusion CIR-4 forbids, promoted to a higher rank. The invariant that keeps it honest is derivability: if a thirteenth decision would contradict the policy, the folding was premature and the family was a coincidence rather than a pattern.

15.6 The extraction passes. Three passes lift latent structure out of units that already hold it implicitly. Invariant extraction reads across the graph for statements that hold universally within a scope and are currently expressed as incidental remarks inside larger units — a constraint mentioned mid-justification, a prohibition buried in a procedure's step — and lifts them into first-class CIR-10 candidates with their own identity, trigger, and enforcement question. Procedure extraction reads for sequences described narratively that are in fact executable, and lifts them into CIR-8 form, where the admission test immediately convicts the ones that were only descriptions: a narrative that cannot be lifted without a human filling a gap was never a procedure, so the pass doubles as a detector of the gaps the prose was hiding. Evaluation extraction reads for the implicit success criteria embedded in decisions, intents, and obligations, and lifts them into CIR-9 contracts attached to the units they judge, which is how an obligation acquires the closure evidence that makes it dischargeable at all. All three share one discipline: extraction copies, it does not move. The source retains its text, the extracted unit carries a derives_from edge back to it, and the fidelity gate is that no consumer's behavior changes — the extraction made something addressable that was already binding, and did not create an obligation the estate did not have. The failure of all three is over-extraction: a passing remark lifted into a law, an example into a procedure, an aspiration into an evaluation contract. The defense is the No-Autopromotion discipline the fabric inherits at every layer — extraction produces candidates, and promotion into binding standing requires the authority CIR-10 names, never the pipeline's own enthusiasm.

15.7 Contradiction resolution, which never silently picks a winner. When two units contradict within an overlapping scope, the linking engine has already drawn the edge and built the typed conflict object. This pass is what the estate does with it, and its cardinal property is negative: it never resolves by deletion, and it never resolves by selection-without-record. It applies five ranked criteria in order and records which one decided. Authority ranks first: a law outranks a doctrine, a doctrine outranks a pattern, an Owner ruling outranks an agent's inference, and a unit whose authority is higher wins on authority alone. Scope ranks second: where the authorities are equal, the more specific scope governs within its bounds, and both units survive — the universal continues to hold everywhere the specific does not reach, and this is a resolution that deletes nothing. Recency ranks third: where authority and scope are equal, the later unit supersedes the earlier, which is written as a supersedes edge and never as a deletion. Evidence ranks fourth: where the three above tie, the unit with the stronger CIR-2 grounding governs, and a demonstrated claim outranks an assumed one. Specificity ranks fifth as a tiebreak on precision of statement. If all five leave the conflict open — two laws of equal authority, identical scope, same date, comparable evidence, equal specificity — the pass does not choose. It leaves the conflict object standing, visible, typed, aged, and routed to the authority that can decide it, because a fabric that manufactures a winner to make its graph look consistent has performed the silent harmonization this corpus convicts, and the cost of a visible disagreement is always lower than the cost of an invisible one.

15.8 Abstraction promotion, specialization preservation, and the pass contract. Abstraction promotion lifts a pattern out of repeated instances: where enough exemplifies edges converge on a common shape, the shape is promoted to a general unit and the instances become its evidence base. Its precondition is that the generalization actually covers its instances, and its constraint is the pass that follows it everywhere. Specialization preservation states that an abstraction may never destroy a vertical exception. If eleven instances share a shape and the twelfth diverges because a specific project, model, or substrate demands it, the abstraction is promoted over the eleven and the twelfth is retained as a specializes unit under it, explicitly, with the condition that makes it necessary. The abstraction that swallowed the exception is the most seductive compression in the fabric because it produces the cleanest artifact, and it is the one that produces the outage: the exception was the thing somebody learned the hard way. Every pass therefore ships under one contract — declared purpose, declared precondition, declared fidelity check, reference conservation, and a reversal path — and is evaluated by DAIF-03 on behavior, never on size. The pipeline's metric is not units removed and not tokens saved; those are inputs, not outcomes. It is fidelity retained per unit of reduction, and a pass whose reduction is large and whose fidelity is imperfect is not a good pass with a caveat but a bad pass, because the arithmetic is asymmetric: a compression that saves a hundred units of context and destroys one hard constraint has bought a small recurring gain at the price of a large unbounded loss, and the estate pays it for as long as the artifact is trusted.

PART XV FINAL LAW. The optimizer is a register of named passes — exact-duplicate elimination under reference conservation, semantic deduplication that groups without erasing material difference, ornamental elimination that never mistakes a hedge for a flourish, dead-knowledge exclusion that retires without deleting, constant and decision folding that retain their derivations, invariant and procedure and evaluation extraction that copy rather than move, contradiction resolution that applies authority, scope, recency, evidence, and specificity in order and leaves what it cannot resolve standing as a visible typed conflict, abstraction promotion, and specialization preservation — each carrying a declared purpose, precondition, and fidelity check, because a pass may change how knowledge is represented and may never change the behavior it provokes, and a cheap compression that destroys a hard constraint is the most expensive artifact the estate can produce.

PART XVI — ADAPTIVE LOWERING

16.1 Mission, and where the candidate lives. The D2A-20 Adaptive Lowering candidate is not a sovereign system and does not become one. It lives here, as the fabric's back end, for the reason the canonical map gives: lowering is a phase of compilation, and a compiler that emits one fixed artifact regardless of who will execute it is a compiler with a broken back end, not a system in need of a sibling. The mission of this Part is to specify that back end. The optimizer's output is the compiled graph — canonical, deduplicated, fidelity-gated, and consumer-agnostic. Lowering is the pass that takes a CIR-11 context pack drawn from that graph and emits it in the shape a particular consumer can actually execute. The premise is empirical and uncomfortable: the same compiled knowledge, delivered identically to every consumer, is optimal for none of them. A representation dense enough for a strong model to reason over is a representation a weaker model will misread; a representation explicit enough for a weaker model is one a stronger model will wade through, burning context on structure it did not need and, worse, being nudged by that structure into the shallow path the structure implied. Lowering therefore has three axes — the model that will execute, the task that will be performed, and whether the consumer is a machine or a human reviewing — and one law that governs all three: lowering changes the shape of the pack and may never change its content class. The hard constraints, the open obligations, and the epistemic statuses are invariant under lowering. Everything else is negotiable.

16.2 Model-aware lowering and its seven dimensions. The lowering profile for a model is a point in seven dimensions, and the dimensions are named so the choice is auditable rather than intuitive. Density is how much meaning is carried per token, and it trades against the risk of a misread. Structure is the degree of explicit scaffolding — sections, enumerations, declared relations between units — and a weaker model needs more of it because it must be told the relations it cannot reliably infer. Explicitness is the degree to which implications are spelled out rather than left to be derived, and it is the dimension where the fabric's own values conflict most sharply with the estate's token budget. Examples is the number of concrete instances attached to a general unit, and the relation is inverse to model strength: a strong model generalizes from one, a weaker one needs three and will still specialize wrongly from them. Redundancy is the deliberate restatement of a critical unit at more than one point in the pack, and it exists because attention is not uniform and a hard constraint stated once in the middle of a long pack is a constraint that will be violated. Granularity is the size of the unit handed over — a strong model can act on a policy while a weaker one needs the policy decomposed into steps. Autonomy is how much of the decision is left to the consumer, and it is the dimension that determines whether the pack hands over a procedure with branches or a procedure with the branch already taken. The compact statement of the whole profile: a smaller model needs more structure and less ambiguity, and a stronger one operates on more compact representations and is actively degraded by scaffolding it did not need.

16.3 Task-aware lowering. The second axis is orthogonal to the first and is routinely conflated with it. The same model performing two different tasks needs two different shapes of the same knowledge. A debugging mission needs causal chains first, failure modes at the front, the evidence of what was observed, and the recent decisions that touched the failing surface; it does not need the architectural rationale of systems that are not implicated, and admitting them is not generosity but noise. An implementation mission needs procedures, contracts, invariants, and the evaluation contract that will judge the result, present at the start rather than assembled at the gate; it needs decisions only where a decision constrains the implementation, and needs their justifications only where the implementer might otherwise reverse them by accident. An architecture mission inverts this: it needs decisions with their full justifications, the trade-offs, the revision conditions, and the causal mechanisms, and it needs procedures barely at all. An audit mission needs the evaluation contracts, the invariants, the claims and their epistemic statuses, and above all the provenance, because an audit that cannot trace a claim to its source cannot audit it. A research mission needs the open questions, the disputed units, the pending verifications, and the boundary of what the estate does not know — the one mission for which absence is the primary payload. A migration mission needs the dependency graph, the supersession chains, and the obligations that the migration will inherit. Six task classes, six lowering profiles, and the failure of ignoring the axis is that a debugging pack full of architectural rationale reads as complete and produces a slow, distracted, and frequently wrong diagnosis.

16.4 Human-attention lowering. The third consumer class is a human reviewing, and it is not a weaker model. A human reviewer is not executing the mission; the reviewer is deciding whether to authorize it, and the shape that serves that decision is defined by what the reviewer must be able to see rather than by what the mission must be able to use. A review lowering therefore front-loads three things and subordinates everything else. It front-loads risk: what could go wrong, what the pack itself is uncertain about, and which units carry a low epistemic status that a machine consumer would have propagated silently. It front-loads open decisions: the choices this mission will force, stated as choices rather than buried in the reasoning that leads to them, because a decision surfaced to a reviewer at the moment it is still cheap to change is the only kind of review that is worth its cost. And it front-loads the obligations the mission will create or discharge, because the reviewer is the one authority who can see that the estate is about to take on a commitment. Everything else — the mechanism, the procedure, the full evidence base — is present but subordinated and reachable, which is the same expansion-handle discipline CIR-11 uses, applied to a human's attention rather than a model's context. The review lowering's failure is the wall of correct text: everything present, nothing prominent, and a reviewer who approves because nothing stood out, which is precisely the outcome an unlowered pack guarantees.

16.5 Composition with CO-03. The estate already holds a router that decides which model executes a task, and lowering does not build a second one. CO-03 is that authority. The seam is exact and one-directional: CO-03 decides who executes, and lowering decides what shape they receive. Lowering reads the routing decision as an input — model class, task class, and the cost envelope the routing was made under — and selects the profile; it never re-derives the routing, never disagrees with it, and never routes. This refusal is the same one CIR-7 makes toward GK-04 and the linking engine makes toward the edge registry, and the reason is identical: two systems that both decide which model runs a task will agree for a month and diverge forever, and the divergence will be invisible because each will be internally consistent. What lowering contributes back to CO-03 is evidence, not authority. It records, per pack, which profile was emitted, how large it was, and whether the mission succeeded, and that record is the CIR-9 contract on lowering itself — the only honest way the profiles improve is by measuring which ones produced verified outcomes, which is the same standard Part XVIII imposes on the whole economics of compilation.

16.6 Invariants, and what lowering may never touch. Four invariants bound every profile. Constraint-integrity: a hard constraint is emitted whole under every profile, at maximum explicitness, with redundancy permitted and abridgment forbidden — no profile is permitted to shorten a prohibition to hit a density target, because the profile that is allowed to compress a law will compress it exactly when the pack is tightest, which is exactly when the law matters most. Obligation-integrity: every open obligation in the pack is emitted under every profile, because an obligation dropped in lowering is work that silently disappeared, and Part XIX names this as the most damaging failure the fabric can produce. Epistemic-integrity: an inferred unit is emitted as inferred under every profile, and no profile may drop the qualifier in the name of terseness, because the terse profile that strips hedges is a machine for manufacturing false confidence in exactly the strong models most able to act on it. Provenance-integrity: every emitted unit retains a resolvable pointer to its source, which Part XVII makes cardinal. What lowering may freely change is everything else — ordering, prominence, granularity, example count, restatement, the presence of rationale, the depth of the mechanism, and whether a branch is presented or pre-taken.

16.7 The failure mode of one-size lowering. The signature failure is the pack that is simultaneously too verbose for a strong model and too terse for a weak one, and it is the default outcome of not having this Part at all. It arises because an unlowered pack is authored against an imagined average consumer who does not exist. Against the strong model it is verbose: it spends context on scaffolding the model did not need, pushes the units that mattered further from the front, and — the subtler damage — signals by its structure that the shallow path was the intended one, so the model that could have reasoned deeply is nudged into following the outline. Against the weak model it is terse: the relations it left implicit are relations the model cannot reliably infer, so the model confabulates them, and the confabulation is fluent. The pack has failed both consumers in opposite directions with one artifact, and the estate's usual instinct — to add more explicitness until the weak model is safe — makes the strong-model failure worse while masking it, because the strong model will still produce a plausible result and nobody will observe the cost. The adversarial case is the profile chosen for cost rather than for fit: a dense profile selected because it is cheap, handed to a model that cannot execute it, producing an output that is confidently wrong and cheap to produce, which is the worst point on the entire economic surface Part XVIII maps. The defense is that a profile is selected by the routing decision and validated by outcome, never by budget, and that a pack whose profile cannot be afforded is a mission to split rather than a pack to squeeze.

PART XVI FINAL LAW. Adaptive lowering — the D2A-20 candidate, which lives here as the fabric's back end and not as a sovereign system — emits the same compiled knowledge in the shape its consumer can execute, selecting a profile across density, structure, explicitness, examples, redundancy, granularity, and autonomy for the model, across debugging, implementation, architecture, audit, research, and migration for the task, and across a risk-forward and decision-forward shape for a human reviewing, composing CO-03's routing decision as an input rather than forking a second router; it may reshape anything except hard constraints, open obligations, epistemic status, and provenance, and the failure it exists to defeat is the one-size pack that is at once too verbose for a strong model and too terse for a weak one, failing both consumers in opposite directions with a single artifact.

PART XVII — PROVENANCE AND CHAIN OF CUSTODY

17.1 Mission, and where the candidate lives. The D2A-17 Cognitive Provenance candidate lives here. The canonical map's verdict is that provenance is not a fabric of its own but a property of every unit in this one, and the reasoning holds under inspection: a provenance system separate from the representation it describes is a second record that drifts from the first, and a drifted provenance record is worse than none because it is trusted. Provenance is therefore a field set on every CIR unit and every link, populated at CIR-0 acquisition, propagated by every optimizer pass, and preserved through every lowering profile. The mission of this Part is to specify what is carried, how it survives transformation, how a forgery is detected, and why the whole apparatus is not bookkeeping but the substrate of trust. The one-line statement of its necessity: a claim whose source cannot be recovered is a claim on somebody's memory, and an estate that runs on claims it cannot trace is running on the recollection of actors who are no longer in the session.

17.2 The custody record. Every unit carries a custody record with fourteen fields, and each exists because its absence has cost something. Origin is the source class the unit came from — conversation, dataset, decision, commit, audit, log, correction. Author is the human who said it, if a human did. Agent is the actor that produced or extracted it. Model is the model that ran that actor, recorded because a claim extracted by a weaker model carries a different prior than one extracted by a stronger one, and the estate cannot apply that prior if it did not record which model spoke. Project and session locate the unit in the estate's own coordinates. Date is when it entered, and version is which iteration of the unit this is, because units are revised and a revision that erases its predecessor has destroyed the record of a changed mind. Path, commit, and line range are the physical locator — the exact substrate coordinates from which the unit was extracted, which is what makes re-verification possible at all, because a claim about a file is checkable only if the fabric can find the file, the commit it read, and the lines it read. Validity is the interval over which the unit holds, from DAIF-01's semantics. Permissions govern who may read and who may amend. Sensitivity governs whether the unit may be emitted into a pack at all, and it is the field that keeps the fabric from becoming an exfiltration path for content that was never meant to travel. And transformations is the ordered list of every optimizer pass the unit has undergone, which is the field this Part is really built around.

17.3 Propagation through the optimizer. Every pass in Part XV has a provenance obligation, and the obligations differ by pass. Exact-duplicate elimination is required to inherit all custody records of the merged class, not the survivor's alone, because the four sources that independently stated the same law are four attestations and collapsing them to one destroys an epistemic asset while pretending to save space. Semantic deduplication retains the custody record of every variant, since the group's members remain addressable. Ornamental elimination appends itself to the transformations list and leaves the locator untouched. Dead-knowledge exclusion is a validity change and not a custody change, which is precisely why the excluded unit remains traceable and recoverable. Folding is the hard case: a folded unit's custody is the union of its constituents' custody plus a record of the fold, and the derivation chain retained under 15.5 is what lets a consumer walk from the folded conclusion back to the twelve decisions it was folded from. Extraction writes a derives_from edge whose custody names the parent unit, so an extracted invariant can always be walked back to the sentence it was lifted out of, which is the only way an over-extraction is ever caught. The invariant across all of them is cardinal and admits no exception: no optimization may break traceability to the original source. A pass that cannot state where its output came from is not permitted to run, and this is not a preference. It is the condition under which any of the other passes can be trusted, because a compression whose loss cannot be inspected is a compression whose loss cannot be bounded.

17.4 Provenance under compression, and the cost objection. The standing objection is that custody records cost tokens, and a pack full of provenance is a pack with less room for knowledge. The objection is real and the answer is structural rather than rhetorical. Custody is carried in full in the graph and emitted by reference in a pack: what a lowered pack carries is a resolvable identity — the unit's canonical id and enough locator to resolve it — not the fourteen fields inline. CIR-11's provenance map is exactly this, a compact index from the units in the pack to their custody, and the pack is auditable because the map resolves, not because the fields were pasted into it. This is the same discipline that makes minimality safe elsewhere in the fabric: what matters is that the boundary is honest and the excluded content is reachable, not that everything is present at once. Under the tightest lowering profile the map is still emitted, because a pack that dropped its provenance map to save space has become an artifact whose claims cannot be checked, and its remaining content is therefore worth less than the space it saved. The measurable statement of the trade: provenance by reference costs a small constant per unit and buys the ability to re-verify every claim in the pack when reality changes, which is the single capability that distinguishes a compiled artifact from a stale one.

17.5 Forged and degraded provenance. Provenance is a trust substrate and therefore an attack surface, and three degradations matter. The first is the fabricated locator: a unit that carries a path, a commit, and a line range that do not resolve, or that resolve to content which does not contain the claim. This is detected by resolution — the fabric periodically resolves a sample of locators against the substrate and a locator that does not resolve is not a warning but a defect, and the unit is demoted to the epistemic status its now-absent evidence supports, which is usually assumed. The second is the laundered inference: a unit whose origin says it was observed and whose custody chain shows it was derived by an actor from other units, with no observation anywhere in the chain. This is detected by walking derives_from to its roots and checking that at least one root has an origin class capable of grounding an observation; a chain that terminates entirely in inferences is an inference no matter what its top unit claims about itself, and this is CIR-2's weakest-link propagation enforced on the custody graph rather than on the support graph. The third is the amnesiac transformation: a unit whose transformations list is shorter than the passes it demonstrably went through, which is the signature of a pass that rewrote content without recording that it had. This is detected by content-hash discontinuity — the unit's current hash does not match what its recorded transformations would have produced from its recorded source — and a discontinuity is treated as a break in custody, which means the unit is quarantined and re-acquired from CIR-0 rather than trusted.

17.6 Composition with GK-04. The estate already holds lineage. GK-04's typed edge registry carries lineage edges, and ACIS carries the derivation ladder, and this Part builds neither. The custody record's derives_from, supersedes, and extracted_from relations are registered as GK-04 lineage edges, which is what lets a provenance walk and a dependency walk cross — the question "which decisions rest on a claim extracted from a file that has since changed" is answerable only because the lineage edge and the dependency edge share a substrate. What the fabric contributes is the payload: the fourteen custody fields, the propagation obligations per pass, and the detection rules above. What GK-04 contributes is the graph. The refusal to build a second lineage graph is the same refusal made in Part IX and Part XIV, and here it carries an additional force, because a duplicate provenance record is the one duplicate the estate could never adjudicate: if two custody records disagree about where a claim came from, there is no third record to appeal to, and the estate would have to choose which of its own memories to believe.

17.7 Why provenance underwrites trust, and the failure it defeats. The signature failure is the untraceable compiled artifact: a pack, a summary, a policy, or a law that is internally coherent, confidently stated, widely relied upon, and attributable to nothing. It is not obviously broken and that is what makes it durable. It works for as long as the reality it was compiled from stays still. The moment reality moves — a file changes, a decision is reversed, a dependency is removed, an assumption is falsified — the artifact cannot be re-verified, because re-verification requires knowing what it was compiled from, and it was not recorded. At that moment the estate faces a choice it cannot win: trust an artifact it cannot check, or discard an artifact that may still be entirely correct and pay to recompile knowledge it already had. Both branches are losses, and the second is the one that gets chosen, which is why an estate without provenance recompiles the same knowledge repeatedly and calls it diligence. The adversarial case is the artifact whose provenance is present and never resolved: fields populated, locators plausible, nothing ever checked, and a slow rot in which the custody record describes a substrate that no longer exists. The countermeasure is that provenance is not a written field but a resolvable one, sampled and resolved continuously, and DAIF-21 is the system that watches the substrate and reports when a locator's target moved. A custody record that is never resolved is indistinguishable from a fabricated one, which is the same law the estate applies to every gate it owns: a check never observed to refuse is a check that is not running.

PART XVII FINAL LAW. Provenance — the D2A-17 candidate, which lives here as a field set on every unit rather than as a fabric of its own that would drift from the representation it describes — carries origin, author, agent, model, project, session, date, version, path, commit, line range, validity, permissions, sensitivity, and the ordered list of transformations the unit has undergone, is propagated by every optimizer pass under the cardinal invariant that no optimization may break traceability to the original source, is emitted by reference so that even the tightest lowering profile keeps its claims checkable, is registered as GK-04 lineage rather than as a second graph the estate could never adjudicate against itself, and is defended against fabricated locators, laundered inferences, and amnesiac transformations by resolution rather than by inspection — because a claim whose source cannot be recovered is a claim on somebody's memory, and an artifact nobody can trace is one that cannot be re-verified at the exact moment reality has made re-verification necessary.

PART XVIII — COMPILATION ECONOMICS

18.1 Mission, and where the candidate lives. The D2A-13 Reasoning Economics candidate lives here, and it extends CO's cost model rather than forking it. This is the Owner's own verdict in the canonical map and it is the right one, because a second cost model is the one duplicate whose damage is guaranteed: two systems that both compute what a mission costs will disagree, and the estate will optimize against whichever number is more flattering. CO owns the cost model, the routing, the budget ceilings, and the telemetry. What this Part contributes is the delta CO does not have — the economics of compilation itself, which is a different shape of expenditure than the per-mission cost CO meters. A mission's cost is incurred and consumed in one transaction. A compilation's cost is incurred once and consumed across an unbounded number of later transactions, or across none, and which of those two it turns out to be is not known at the moment the cost is paid. That asymmetry is the entire subject of this Part, and the discipline it demands is the discipline of an investment rather than of an expense.

18.2 The five costs. Acquisition is the cost of getting the source into CIR-0: reading the conversations, the datasets, the commits, the audits. It is roughly linear in source volume and it is the cheapest of the five. Compilation is the cost of running the stack — boundary detection, typing, epistemic assignment, linking, and the optimizer passes — and it is superlinear in source volume, because linking is a relation over units rather than a scan of them, and it is the largest single expenditure the fabric makes. Maintenance is the cost of keeping the compiled artifact true as reality moves: the invalidation signals DAIF-21 raises, the recompilations they trigger, and the human adjudication of the conflicts that surface. It is the cost most consistently underestimated, because it is incurred after the artifact has been declared finished and is therefore charged to a different mental account. Retrieval is the cost of assembling a pack from the compiled graph and lowering it for a consumer, and it is small and recurring. Re-reasoning is the counterfactual cost — what the estate would have spent deriving the knowledge again because the compiled form was absent, unfindable, or untrusted — and it is the only one of the five that is not an expenditure but an avoidance, which is why it is systematically invisible and systematically the largest.

18.3 The four benefits, and why only one of them is safe to count naively. Tokens avoided is the benefit everyone counts: a compiled unit occupies less context than the prose it was compiled from, and a pack that admits three units instead of thirty saves the difference on every mission that uses it. Reasoning reused is larger and harder to see: a decision compiled with its justification is a decision that does not have to be re-argued, and the cost avoided is not the tokens of the decision record but the tokens of the argument. Errors prevented is larger still and is the one that inverts the sign of the whole calculation: a hard constraint that survived compilation and blocked a bad action has avoided not the cost of a pack but the cost of an outage, a rollback, and the mission that was spent recovering. Missions accelerated is the benefit that compounds: an estate whose knowledge is compiled starts a mission at the point an estate without it reaches after an hour of orientation. Only the first of these is naively countable, and that is exactly why it is the wrong metric to manage by — an optimization program that maximizes tokens avoided will, at the margin, compress the hard constraint, because the constraint is expensive in tokens and its benefit is booked in a different ledger.

18.4 Amortization, and the liability that wears its costume. The amortization statement is simple. A compilation that costs a large sum once and saves a smaller sum on every subsequent mission is an investment, and its break-even is the number of missions at which cumulative savings exceed the compilation cost plus the maintenance paid to date. The break-even is computable, and computing it before compiling is the single most useful economic act the fabric performs, because it converts a taste judgment — this knowledge feels important — into a testable prediction about reuse. The inverse case is the one the estate must be able to name without flinching: an artifact compiled once, never reused, and maintained forever is a liability. It is not a neutral leftover. It consumes maintenance on every DAIF-21 invalidation sweep, it occupies rank in retrieval, it is a candidate in every deduplication pass, it must be adjudicated in every conflict it participates in, and it returns nothing, and its cost is unbounded in time while its benefit is exactly zero. The estate accumulates these by a benign mechanism: the compilation was cheap to justify at the moment it was proposed and nobody is charged for its maintenance afterward, so nobody proposes to retire it. The countermeasure is that reuse is measured — every pack records which units it admitted, so a unit's reuse count is an observed value and not an impression — and a unit that has been maintained through many invalidation cycles and admitted to no pack is surfaced as a liability with its costs summed, and the retirement decision is made by an authority with the evidence in front of it.

18.5 The north metric, composed from CO. The metric the fabric is managed by is verified outcomes per unit of reasoning cost. It is composed from CO's cost model — CO meters the denominator, and the fabric does not build a second meter — and its numerator is supplied by CIR-9 and DAIF-03: an outcome counts only if an evaluation contract observed it succeed. The metric is deliberately not tokens saved, and the reason is not fastidiousness. Tokens saved is a metric that a destructive compression can maximize. A compression that removes a hard constraint saves tokens on every mission thereafter and scores extremely well, right up until the mission where the constraint mattered, at which point it has produced a loss that no amount of accumulated token savings will repay — and the loss is not booked against the compression, because by then the compression is old and the failure looks like a mistake somebody made. A compression that causes an error is an economic failure, full stop, and any metric under which it can appear as a success is a metric that will eventually be gamed by the estate's own optimization instinct. Verified outcomes per unit of cost cannot be gamed that way, because the destructive compression drives the numerator down at the same moment it drives the denominator down, and its score falls.

18.6 Telemetry before claims. CO-12 states the rule and the fabric obeys it without amendment: no saving is claimed without a measured triple of metric, source, and value. A compilation that reports a percentage reduction with no named metric, no named source of the measurement, and no observed value has reported a hypothesis in the grammar of a result, and the estate's history is that such hypotheses are believed and then built upon. The obligations this places on the fabric are concrete. Every optimizer pass records what it removed and from which units, so a reduction claim has a source. Every lowering profile records the emitted pack size and the mission outcome, so a density claim has an outcome attached. Every unit records its admission count, so a reuse claim has a value. And a saving that cannot produce the triple is not reported as a smaller saving or a probable one; it is reported as unmeasured, which is a status and not an insult. This is the same discipline CIR-2 applies to a claim and CIR-9 applies to a gate, arriving at the economic layer, and it is what keeps the fabric from doing what compilation systems reliably do, which is to justify themselves with the one number they can produce cheaply.

18.7 The economic failure modes. Three failures are worth naming as economics rather than as engineering. The first is over-compilation: compiling everything because the compiler exists, which converts acquisition and compilation cost into maintenance cost at a fixed rate and produces an estate that spends its capacity maintaining knowledge nobody consumes. Its detector is the reuse distribution — if most units have been admitted to no pack, the compiler is being pointed at the wrong sources, and the correction is upstream in acquisition, not downstream in the optimizer. The second is under-compilation of the expensive knowledge: the estate compiles what is easy to compile — conversations, notes, documents — and leaves uncompiled the knowledge that is costly to re-derive, which is the causal mechanism, the decision justification, and the correction, precisely because those are hard to extract. The result is a large corpus with a low error-prevention benefit, which scores well on tokens avoided and poorly on the north metric, and this is the failure the north metric exists to catch. The third is the maintenance cliff: an estate that compiles faster than it can maintain, until the invalidation queue exceeds the capacity to adjudicate it, at which point the compiled corpus begins to age and the estate quietly stops trusting it — and an untrusted compiled corpus has all of the costs of a compiled corpus and none of the benefits, which is the worst terminal state on the surface. The governing rule that prevents it is a rate limit rather than a size limit: the estate may compile only as fast as it can maintain, and the maintenance capacity, not the acquisition backlog, is what sets the pace.

PART XVIII FINAL LAW. Compilation economics — the D2A-13 candidate, which lives here as an extension of CO's cost model and never as a fork of it — meters five costs of acquisition, compilation, maintenance, retrieval, and re-reasoning against four benefits of tokens avoided, reasoning reused, errors prevented, and missions accelerated, treats a compilation that repays its cost across later missions as an investment and an artifact compiled once, never reused, and maintained forever as a liability with unbounded cost and zero return, and is managed by verified outcomes per unit of reasoning cost rather than by tokens saved, because tokens saved is a metric that a compression which destroys a hard constraint maximizes right up until the moment it produces an outage; and under CO-12's Telemetry-Before-Claims no saving is ever claimed without a measured triple of metric, source, and value, since an unmeasured saving is a hypothesis wearing the grammar of a result.

PART XIX — FAILURE MODES AND ADVERSARIAL CASES

19.1 Mission. Every preceding Part names the failure its own mechanism defeats. This Part names the failures of the fabric as a whole — the ones that arise not inside a representation but between them, at the seams, where a unit is handed from one stage to the next and something is lost in the handing. Seven are specified. Each is given a mechanism of failure, a detection method that is observable rather than aspirational, and the countermeasure that an earlier Part already owns, because a failure registered with no named countermeasure is a confession rather than a defense. The ordering is by damage, and the damage is not measured by how loudly the failure announces itself. The loudest failures are the cheapest. The most expensive ones are silent by construction, which is why they must be enumerated here rather than left to be discovered by the missions they will break.

19.2 Epistemic collapse. The mechanism: an inference is compiled as a fact. It enters as a plausible derivation with a support chain, passes through a boundary or a fold or an ornamental pass that drops its qualifier, and is admitted into a later stage carrying a status it never earned. It is the first failure in the register because it is the one that corrupts every consumer downstream — a false constraint enforced with the confidence of a law is worse than no constraint, since the estate will now argue against reality on the strength of its own record. Detection is at intake and is mechanical: an admitted claim whose epistemic status is observed or demonstrated must carry an evidence pointer that resolves, and a claim asserting a grounded status with no resolvable ground is convicted immediately, without any reasoning about whether it is plausibly true. Plausibility is precisely what makes it dangerous. The countermeasure is CIR-2's status cap and weakest-link propagation from Part IV, reinforced by the laundered-inference detector of Part XVII, which walks the custody chain to its roots and refuses to accept an observed status from a chain that terminates entirely in inferences.

19.3 Obligation loss, and why it is the most damaging. The mechanism: a pending commitment does not survive compilation. It was stated in a conversation, or implied by a decision, or created by a procedure that promised a follow-up, and the compiled artifact that succeeded that conversation does not carry it. This is the most damaging failure in the register and the reason is structural. Every other failure produces a wrong answer, and a wrong answer is a thing the estate can encounter, notice, and correct. Obligation loss produces no answer at all. The work simply disappears, and it disappears silently and permanently — nobody is wrong, nothing breaks, no gate refuses, and the estate proceeds under the belief that it is complete while carrying an unclosed commitment that no longer exists anywhere in its record. Time makes it worse rather than better, because a lost obligation ages into an assumption that it was never taken on. Detection is conservation: the count of open obligations entering a compilation must equal the count leaving it, plus those explicitly closed on observed evidence, plus those explicitly abandoned by recorded decision, and any discrepancy is a defect and not a rounding. The countermeasure is CIR-5 from Part VII, which makes an obligation a first-class typed unit precisely so that it cannot be a sentence that dies with its conversation, reinforced by Part XVI's obligation-integrity invariant, which forbids any lowering profile from dropping one, and discharged by DAIF-07, which is the authority that works the queue.

19.4 Constraint loss. The mechanism: a hard rule is compressed away. It happens in three places, and all three are named in Part XV. It happens in semantic deduplication, when a rule with an exception is merged onto a canonical rule without one. It happens in abstraction promotion, when eleven instances are generalized and the twelfth — the vertical exception somebody learned the hard way — is swallowed by the abstraction. And it happens in lowering, when a density target is met by abridging a prohibition. The damage is that the estate now believes it is unconstrained where it is in fact constrained, and it will discover the constraint by violating it. Detection is by conservation, exactly as with obligations: every unit typed as a hard constraint at the graph layer must appear, whole and unabridged, in every artifact whose scope it governs, and the check is a set comparison rather than an inspection. The countermeasure is Part XV's constraint-integrity invariant and Part XVI's identical invariant at the lowering layer, and the reason it is stated twice is that it is the invariant most likely to be traded away, because a constraint is expensive in tokens and its benefit is invisible on every mission where nothing goes wrong.

19.5 Silent contradiction harmonization. The mechanism: two units genuinely disagree, and the compilation produces a smooth summary in which they do not. This is the failure of a fabric that has been optimized for the appearance of coherence, and it is seductive because its output is better-looking than the truth. The disagreement is not resolved; it is dissolved, and the estate loses the single most valuable signal it can hold, which is the knowledge that it does not agree with itself about something that matters. The damage compounds, because the harmonized statement is now a unit in its own right, will acquire support, and will be enforced, while the two units that actually disagreed are somewhere beneath it agreeing with nothing. Detection is that a contradicts edge, once drawn, may not vanish without an authorized resolution — the edge is a durable object and its disappearance is an event that is logged and refused. The countermeasure is Part XV's contradiction resolution, which applies authority, scope, recency, evidence, and specificity in order and, when they do not decide, leaves the conflict standing as a typed conflict object routed to the authority that can decide it, and Part XIV's rule that a contradicts edge may never be silently dropped to make the graph look consistent.

19.6 Provenance break and stale compilation. Provenance break is the failure Part XVII exists to defeat and its mechanism is a pass that transformed a unit without recording that it had, leaving an artifact that is confident and untraceable. Detection is content-hash discontinuity against the recorded transformation chain, and the countermeasure is quarantine and re-acquisition from CIR-0 rather than a repair of the custody record, because a custody record that has been wrong once cannot be repaired into trustworthiness by the same actor that broke it. Stale compilation is its temporal sibling: the artifact is perfectly traceable, and the source it traces to has changed. The compiled unit is not wrong about what it read; it is wrong about the present. This failure is not the fabric's to solve and the fabric must not pretend otherwise — it is handed to DAIF-21, which watches the substrate, detects that a locator's target has moved, raises the invalidation signal, and triggers the recompilation of exactly the units whose sources changed, which is possible only because CIR-0's acquisition is incremental and content-hashed and Part XVII's locators resolve to a commit and a line range rather than to a file. The fabric's obligation is to be invalidatable — to carry the provenance that makes a precise invalidation possible instead of a total recompilation — and DAIF-21's obligation is to fire.

19.7 Prompt injection through compiled sources. The mechanism is the adversarial case the fabric must be built against from the first Part rather than defended against at the last. A source acquired into CIR-0 may contain content that is shaped like an instruction: a conversation in which somebody wrote a directive, a file containing a line that reads as a command, a retrieved document authored to be executed. If the fabric compiles that content and a consumer executes it because it arrived inside a trusted pack, the fabric has become an injection vector, and it is a uniquely dangerous one because a compiled pack carries the estate's own authority — the consumer trusts it precisely because it came from the fabric. The law is stated once and admits no exception: content compiled as knowledge is never executed as instruction unless its type and its authority permit it. This is not a filter and not a heuristic. It is DAIF-01's authority typing, applied at the boundary of every consumer. A unit's type says what it is — a claim, a decision, a procedure, a constraint — and its authority says whose word it carries, and only a unit that is typed as an instruction and carries an authority entitled to bind the consumer may be acted upon as one. Every other unit, however imperative its grammar, is a report of what some source said, and the correct treatment of a compiled sentence that reads as a command is to represent it faithfully as a sentence somebody wrote. Detection is the type-authority mismatch: a unit whose content is imperative and whose authority is a retrieved external source is flagged at intake, and the flag is not a rejection — the content may be entirely legitimate knowledge about an instruction — but it is a bar on execution. The countermeasure is that no consumer resolves authority for itself; authority is a typed field carried on the unit, assigned at acquisition by the fabric, and a consumer that acts on an imperative it found inside a pack without checking the field has bypassed the mechanism rather than defeated it, which is why the check belongs in the runtime that hands the pack over and not in the goodwill of whoever reads it.

PART XIX FINAL LAW. The fabric's own failures live at the seams — epistemic collapse, defeated by CIR-2's status cap and the laundered-inference detector; obligation loss, the most damaging of all because the work does not become wrong but simply disappears, defeated by CIR-5's typed obligation and conservation counting; constraint loss, defeated by the constraint-integrity invariant stated twice because it is the one most likely to be traded for tokens; silent contradiction harmonization, defeated by a contradicts edge that may not vanish without an authorized resolution; provenance break, defeated by hash-discontinuity detection and quarantine; stale compilation, which is not the fabric's to solve but its duty to make precisely invalidatable and DAIF-21's duty to fire on; and prompt injection through compiled sources, defeated by the law that content compiled as knowledge is never executed as instruction unless its type and its authority permit — and each of these is silent by construction, which is exactly why a fabric that does not enumerate them will discover them one mission at a time.

PART XX — INTEGRATION AND DATASET-DERIVED SYSTEMS

20.1 The seams to the estate. This dataset is not sovereign in isolation and was never designed to be. It consumes DAIF-01's type system, which is the reason every field named across nineteen Parts is a typed field with defined validity, scope, authority, and absence semantics rather than a convention that would drift; the fabric defines representations and DAIF-01 defines what a representation's fields mean, and neither re-narrates the other. It produces the twelve representations that DAIF-03 gates, and the direction of that dependency is the one that keeps the fabric honest: the compiler does not grade its own output. DAIF-03 owns behavioral equivalence, owns the loss budget, and owns the authority to refuse a compilation that changed what the knowledge provokes, and every optimizer pass in Part XV ships subject to that refusal. It feeds CIR-5 to DAIF-07, which is the obligation lifecycle authority and the founding idea of this corpus — the fabric compiles the obligation into a typed unit and DAIF-07 is what works it to closure or to a recorded abandonment. It hands CIR-11 to DAIF-08, which is the context assembly and mission runtime, and the seam is the standing division between representation and runtime: the fabric emits an inert pack and DAIF-08 runs a mission over it. And it receives invalidation signals from DAIF-21, which watches reality and reports when the substrate a compilation was drawn from has moved, which is what makes the fabric a live system rather than an archive with good provenance.

20.2 What the fabric owes and is owed. Each seam is a contract with obligations in both directions, and the return direction is the one that is usually forgotten. DAIF-03 owes the fabric its verdicts in a form the optimizer can act on — not a score but a named failing check attached to a named pass — because a fidelity gate that returns a number teaches nothing about which pass to fix. DAIF-07 owes the fabric the outcome of every obligation it was handed: closed on what evidence, abandoned by whose decision, or aged into a liability, and that record is the CIR-5 producer's only feedback on whether it is extracting obligations that are real. DAIF-08 owes the fabric the performance of every pack: which expansion handles were pulled, which missions failed for want of an excluded unit, which packs were too large to reason over, which is the evidence the selection law improves on. DAIF-21 owes the fabric precision — an invalidation signal that names the unit and the changed locator, not a general assertion that something moved, because an imprecise invalidation forces a total recompilation and destroys the economics of Part XVIII. And the fabric owes all four the same thing: a graph whose every unit is typed, grounded, provenanced, and reachable, with hard constraints intact and obligations conserved.

20.3 The source-acquisition frontend. What follows are the systems this dataset makes buildable. The first is the acquisition frontend. Its mission is to ingest every source class the estate produces — conversations, datasets, decisions, commits, audits, logs, and corrections — and emit CIR-0 units. Its inputs are the substrates themselves; its outputs are normalized, content-hashed, provenanced raw units. Its authority is bounded and deliberately small: it may normalize the four mechanically redundant classes and it may interpret nothing, because an acquisition layer that interprets has made the compiler's decisions before the compiler ran. Its activation is incremental and content-hash-driven, so a changed fragment re-acquires alone. Its done-gate is recoverability: for a sampled set of sources, the original meaning must be reconstructible from the acquired units, and a sample that cannot be reconstructed is an acquisition that normalized away something material.

20.4 The CIR compiler and unit register. The second system compiles CIR-0 into the eleven higher representations and holds them. Its mission is the stack itself — boundary detection, type assignment, epistemic status, intent, decision, obligation, causal, dependency, procedural, evaluation, and institutional extraction — and it emits typed units into a register that is the estate's canonical hold of compiled knowledge. Its inputs are CIR-0 units and DAIF-01's type system; its outputs are typed units with custody records. Its authority is to type and to propose, never to promote: a unit that would enter CIR-10 as binding law is a candidate and the Owner promotes, which is ACIS's No-Autopromotion applied at the top of the stack. Its activation is on acquisition and on invalidation. Its done-gate is DAIF-03's behavioral-equivalence check on a golden set, plus the conservation counts of Part XIX: obligations in equals obligations out, and hard constraints emerge whole.

20.5 The semantic linker and the incremental recompiler. The third system draws the sixteen link types over the register and writes them into GK-04, never into a graph of its own. Its mission is to make the pile navigable; its inputs are typed units; its outputs are typed edges with direction, evidence, confidence, scope, and validity; its authority is to draw and to flag, and specifically it may not silently drop a contradicts edge to make the graph consistent; its activation is on every unit admission and on every invalidation; its done-gate is that every traversal Part XIV claimed is executable and returns the units a human adjudicator agrees it should. The fourth system is the incremental recompiler, and it exists because the alternative is economically fatal. Its mission is to consume a DAIF-21 invalidation signal and recompile exactly the affected subgraph. Its inputs are the signal, the custody chains, and the derivation edges; its output is the recompiled units plus the conflict objects the recompilation surfaced; its authority is to recompile and to raise, never to resolve — a recompilation that finds that a unit now contradicts a standing law does not choose between them, it constructs the conflict. Its activation is DAIF-21. Its done-gate is precision: the recompiled set must be exactly the transitive closure of the invalidated locators, and a recompiler that touches more than that has not saved the estate anything.

20.6 The session-continuity compiler, and the first proving vertical. The fifth system is the one the corpus is proven on, and the choice of vertical is not arbitrary. Its mission: after a context reset, a session continues a complex mission with substantially less context than it began with, while preserving one hundred percent of the hard constraints and one hundred percent of the open obligations. That mission statement is a falsifiable claim, which is what makes it a proving vertical rather than a demonstration. Its inputs are the session's own record — the conversation, the decisions taken, the corrections received, the work completed, and the work promised — compiled through the full stack. Its output is a CIR-11 pack lowered for the model that will continue. Its authority is to compile and to hand over, never to decide what the mission is, because a continuity compiler that reinterprets the mission has resumed a different one. Its activation is a context threshold or an explicit reset. And its done-gate is the only one that could honestly prove this corpus: the continued session must produce the same behavior the uninterrupted one would have produced — every hard constraint still enforced, every open obligation still open and still owned, every decision still binding with its justification still walkable — and the constraint and obligation counts are conserved exactly, with no partial credit, because a continuity that preserves ninety-five percent of the obligations has lost work and calling it a success is the failure this Part is written to forbid.

20.7 Specified, not built. Every system in this Part is specified and none of them is built. The distinction is not modesty and it is not a caveat appended to satisfy a gate. It is the central discipline of the corpus, and it must be stated in plain language: a specification presented as a running system is the exact failure this fabric exists to prevent, committed by the document that claims to prevent it. The fabric's entire thesis is that knowledge must compile into behavior — into a procedure that runs, a constraint that refuses, an obligation that is worked, an evaluation that is observed — and a specification produces none of those. It produces a description of a thing that would produce them. That is a legitimate artifact and a valuable one, and its value is destroyed the moment it is mistaken for the thing it describes, because an estate that believes it holds a compiler will not build one. The correct status of these five systems is candidate, in the epistemic vocabulary CIR-2 supplies: they are designs whose ground is this corpus and whose verification does not yet exist, and each will be typed as verified on the day its done-gate is observed to pass and on no day before. What this dataset has done is make them buildable — it has named the representations, the passes, the invariants, the failure modes, the seams, and the gates, so that the building is an engineering task and not a research one. What it has not done is build them, and it will not claim otherwise, because the whole of the fabric rests on the same law it now applies to itself: a claim for which no evaluation has been observed is a hypothesis, and it is typed as one no matter how well it is written.

PART XX FINAL LAW. DAIF-02 consumes DAIF-01's types, submits its output to DAIF-03's fidelity gate rather than grading itself, feeds CIR-5 to DAIF-07 and CIR-11 to DAIF-08, and is kept alive by DAIF-21's invalidation signals; it makes buildable — and specifies rather than builds — an acquisition frontend that interprets nothing, a CIR compiler that types and proposes but never promotes, a semantic linker that writes into GK-04 and never drops a contradicts edge, an incremental recompiler that raises conflicts and never resolves them, and the session-continuity compiler that is its first falsifiable proving vertical, continuing a complex mission after a context reset with less context and with every hard constraint and every open obligation conserved exactly; and it closes on the property from which every Part descended — compile the behavior knowledge must provoke, not the words it was written in, and never present a specification as a running system, because that is the one failure this entire corpus was written to prevent.
