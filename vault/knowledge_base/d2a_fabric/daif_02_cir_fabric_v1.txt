DAIF-02 — COGNITIVE INTERMEDIATE REPRESENTATIONS (CIR) FABRIC

DAIF-02 — COGNITIVE INTERMEDIATE REPRESENTATIONS AND THE CIR FABRIC

PART I — THESIS: COGNITIVE SOURCE CODE AND THE CIR STACK

1.1 Mission. This dataset states the law that gives the Power Pack a compiler for its own knowledge, and the law is one sentence: no conversation, dataset, decision, audit, or result may be carried forward as raw prose or as a lossy précis, because both discard exactly the structure a later consumer needs to act, and the only faithful carrier is a compilation into typed representations. The estate today moves its reasoning in two ways, and both fail differently. It re-sends the original text, which is faithful but unusable — a hundred-thousand-word transcript preserves everything and lets a consumer act on nothing, because the obligations, decisions, and exceptions inside it are undifferentiated words that no machine can select or verify. Or it shortens the text, which is usable but unfaithful — a summary reads well and silently drops the one exception that mattered, the single piece of evidence that grounded a claim, the obligation nobody closed. The fabric this dataset defines is the third way: it treats every source as cognitive source code and compiles it, converting words into representations that can be selected, linked, verified, and executed while losing nothing the source's behavior depended on.

1.2 The three verbs, sharply distinguished. The distinction the fabric turns on is mechanical, not rhetorical, and it separates three operations the estate has long confused. A summary reduces length: it maps a long text to a short one and its defining property is that the mapping is lossy in an uncontrolled direction — it keeps what the summarizer found salient and drops the rest, and what it drops is invisible, so an exception, an evidence line, or an obligation vanishes without a trace and a consumer reading the summary cannot tell that anything is gone. A distillation raises density: it maps a long text to a denser text that a human reads faster, and its defining property is that the output is still a human document — another wall of prose, better packed, but carrying the same machine-opacity as the source, because a denser sentence is still a sentence and hides the same eight fields the bare one did. A compilation converts source into structured representations: it maps text to a set of typed objects, each preserving one dimension of the source's meaning, and its defining property is that the output is selectable, linkable, verifiable, and executable — a consumer does not re-read it, it queries it. Summary loses silently, distillation stays opaque, compilation preserves and exposes.

1.3 What the compiler preserves. The cardinal commitment of the fabric is that it preserves the behavior the knowledge must provoke, not merely the words the knowledge used. A source is not valuable because of its prose; it is valuable because of what it obliges a later actor to do — enforce a constraint, honor a decision, close an obligation, distrust an assumption, reproduce a result. A faithful carrier is therefore one that reproduces those provocations at the point of use, and the test of any representation is behavioral: a consumer acting on the compiled form must do what a consumer acting on the full original would have done, and no less. This is why length is the wrong axis entirely. A ten-line compiled decision that carries its question, options, choice, justification, authority, and revision conditions provokes the right behavior — it can be revised safely — while a two-hundred-line faithful transcript of the same decision provokes nothing, because the consumer cannot find the justification in the noise. The compiler is judged on behavioral equivalence, the same standard DAIF-03 formalizes as its fidelity contract, and it is to DAIF-03 that this fabric hands its outputs to be gated.

1.4 The stack of twelve representations. The compiler's core is not one representation but a stack of twelve, each preserving a different dimension of meaning, arranged as a pipeline that lowers raw content toward executable context: raw -> semantic -> epistemic -> intent -> decision -> obligation -> causal -> dependency -> procedural -> evaluation -> institutional -> executable-context. CIR-0 Raw Source holds the normalized original. CIR-1 Semantic Unit decomposes it into atomic units of meaning. CIR-2 Epistemic types each unit's truth-status. CIR-3 Intent preserves what was actually wanted. CIR-4 Decision records the choices made and why. CIR-5 Obligation records the work still owed. CIR-6 Causal holds why things happened, CIR-7 Dependency what rests on what, CIR-8 Procedural the how, CIR-9 Evaluation the measured results, CIR-10 Institutional the promoted laws and patterns, and CIR-11 Executable-Context the assembled, ready-to-reason payload. Each stage consumes the stage beneath it and adds one dimension, so a fact acquires an epistemic status, then an intent it serves, then a decision it grounds, then an obligation it discharges — meaning accretes as the pipeline rises rather than being flattened into a single lossy layer. This batch establishes the thesis and the first six representations, CIR-0 through CIR-5, the spine on which the upper six stand.

1.5 Every CIR unit is a typed cognitive object. The fabric does not invent a new vocabulary for its slots; every unit at every stage of the stack is a DAIF-01 typed cognitive object, carrying the eight fields that dataset sealed — Type, Authority, Scope, Strength, Validity, Override-policy, Provenance, and Confidence — so that a semantic unit knows its kind, an epistemic unit knows its scope, an obligation knows its authority, and a decision knows its validity. This inheritance is what makes the stack coherent rather than a pile of ad-hoc structures: a consumer that can read a DAIF-01 object can read any CIR unit, because a CIR unit is a DAIF-01 object with the stage's dimension filled in. The representations differ in which dimension they foreground — CIR-2 foregrounds the epistemic status, CIR-4 the decision structure — but they never differ in the type discipline beneath, so a unit compiled at one stage and referenced at another is the same typed object seen from two angles, not two objects that must be reconciled. The fabric is the reasoning intermediate representation DAIF-01 Part I named as its first consumer, and it consumes the type system exactly as promised: every slot inherits its type from there and re-invents none.

1.6 Composition, never re-narration. The fabric composes the estate's sealed systems at the stages where they already hold authority and forks none of them. CIR-2's epistemic typing composes the ACIS epistemic ladder, referencing its levels as the law that keeps an inference from being lowered into a fact. CIR-3's intent structure composes one_shot's intent and Q&A scaffold, reading the six-question interrogation as the intent-capture it already performs. CIR-4's decision structure composes DRK's decision_record, treating a compiled decision as that record's typed projection. The upper stack continues the pattern — CIR-9 composes SQI's evaluation verdicts, CIR-10 composes UKDL's promoted laws — and the whole fabric hands its finished representations to DAIF-03 for the fidelity gate and to DAIF-07 for the obligation lifecycle. Each system is named and deferred to, never restated, because re-narrating a sealed engine is the ornamental duplication the constitution convicts under its duplicate-to-advantage law. The compiler's contribution is not new reasoning machinery but a common representational spine that gives those systems' outputs a typed, linkable, executable shape, so the estate stops re-sending prose between them and starts compiling it.

1.7 The fabric's place and burden. The CIR fabric is the third dataset in the DAIF spine and its representational core, because a fidelity budget cannot be spent, an obligation cannot be tracked, and a context cannot be assembled until there is a representation to spend, track, and assemble over. Everything above the type system reasons through these representations, so a gap in the stack is a gap in every consumer that reads it, and the burden on the fabric is completeness of dimension: it must preserve every dimension of meaning a source carries, because a dimension the stack drops is a behavior the compiled form fails to provoke, and a dropped behavior is the silent loss of a summary wearing the costume of a compilation. The fabric earns its name only if the compiled form is a faithful carrier under the behavioral test — if acting on the twelve representations reproduces what acting on the whole source would have — and the rest of this dataset is the specification of the representations that make that guarantee hold.

PART I FINAL LAW. Knowledge must be compiled, not re-sent as raw prose nor shortened into a lossy summary, because a summary drops exceptions and evidence silently while a distillation stays machine-opaque, and only a compilation into a stack of twelve typed intermediate representations — raw -> semantic -> epistemic -> intent -> decision -> obligation -> causal -> dependency -> procedural -> evaluation -> institutional -> executable-context, every unit a DAIF-01 typed object, composed from ACIS, one_shot, DRK, SQI, and UKDL rather than forked — preserves the behavior the knowledge must provoke instead of merely the words it used.

PART II — CIR-0 RAW SOURCE IR AND SOURCE ACQUISITION

2.1 Mission. CIR-0 is the base of the stack, and its mission is the one every compiler's front end serves: to hold the original content in a normalized form that loses nothing recoverable and interprets nothing yet. It is the representation that receives a source before any meaning is extracted from it, and its discipline is exactly the tension between two failures. Interpret too early and CIR-0 becomes a lossy pre-summary — the front end decides what matters before the semantic stage above it has run, and the decision is unaccountable. Normalize too little and CIR-0 becomes the raw wall the fabric exists to abolish — a transcript re-held verbatim, mechanical noise and all, so nothing has actually been compiled. CIR-0 threads between them by a single rule: it strips only what is mechanically redundant and retains everything a later stage might need, so that the loss it commits is provably zero on the axis of meaning and non-zero only on the axis of noise.

2.2 The object model. A CIR-0 unit is a DAIF-01 typed object whose Type is fixed to raw-source and whose foregrounded content is the normalized text of one coherent source fragment, but its value is in its metadata. It carries the source's identity — which conversation, dataset, decision record, commit, log, audit, or result it came from — its Provenance filled with the exact origin the upper stack will need to cite, its Scope inherited from where the source lives on the DAIF-01 ladder, and its Validity set to reflect whether the source is current or historical. Required metadata is the origin pointer, the acquisition timestamp, the content hash, and the normalization record — the list of mechanical transforms applied, so any consumer can prove what was removed and confirm that nothing of meaning was among it. Optional metadata is the source's own internal structure where it has one — a heading path, a turn index, a line range — retained as a locator so a later unit can point back into the exact span it was compiled from. The content hash is the load-bearing field: it is the identity by which incremental acquisition decides whether a fragment has changed.

2.3 The source types CIR-0 accepts. The front end accepts every kind of cognitive source the estate produces, and the taxonomy is deliberately wide: conversations between the Owner and an actor, datasets and their Parts, PRDs and specifications, decisions and their records, commits and their messages, logs of runs, audits and their findings, results and their measurements, and corrections — the moments an actor was told it was wrong. Each type enters CIR-0 through the same door and leaves normalized in the same shape, but the front end tags each with its source-type so the semantic stage above can apply the right boundary heuristics — a commit is decomposed differently from a conversation, an audit differently from a PRD. Corrections are the highest-value source-type and the one a naive front end most often discards as conversational noise, because a correction is where the estate's belief changed, and CIR-0 is instructed to retain every correction intact, since the epistemic stage above depends on knowing not only what is believed but what was once believed and reversed.

2.4 Normalization: what CIR-0 strips and what it must never touch. Normalization removes only mechanically redundant material, and the register of removable classes is closed: duplicate formatting, transport wrappers, irrelevant envelope metadata, and exact echoes — the verbatim re-quotation of text already present, which carries no new meaning and inflates the source. Against these four, CIR-0 removes freely, recording each removal in the normalization record. Everything else it must never touch: it never paraphrases, because paraphrase is interpretation and interpretation belongs to CIR-1; it never drops a sentence for being unimportant, because importance is a judgment the semantic and epistemic stages make with more information; it never merges two fragments, because a boundary is CIR-1's to draw. The invariant is stated as a recoverability guarantee — from a CIR-0 unit and its normalization record, the original source's meaning must be exactly reconstructible, so the only information CIR-0 is permitted to lose is information a reconstruction would not miss. A front end that cannot prove recoverability has interpreted, and interpretation at CIR-0 is the failure this stage exists to prevent.

2.5 Source acquisition and incremental ingest. CIR-0 is not populated by recompiling whole sources on every touch; it is populated incrementally, and the incremental discipline is what lets the fabric scale to an estate of thousands of sources. Acquisition fingerprints each source fragment by content hash and compares the fingerprint to the last acquired one, so a source that has not changed is not re-normalized, and a source that changed in one fragment causes only that fragment to be re-acquired. The mechanism is the same reality-supremacy discipline the estate applies everywhere — the disk is the truth, and a fragment whose hash matches its recorded hash is known-current without re-reading — and it is the direct analogue of the audit-cache the estate already runs, where a matching hash means the cached summary stands. Incremental ingest is not an optimization bolted on; it is a correctness property, because recompiling a whole source when one fragment changed would re-run the upper stack over unchanged content and risk re-deriving its units with drifted boundaries, breaking the links CIR-1 and above depend on. CIR-0 changes only where the source changed, and the stack above it re-compiles only over the changed units.

2.6 The invariant of non-interpretation and its failure modes. CIR-0's governing invariant is that it retains all recoverable content and interprets none of it, and both halves have a signature failure. The retention half fails as over-normalization: a front end that strips a fragment it judged noise but that carried meaning — a terse correction read as an echo, a metadata line that actually named the authority, a formatting mark that encoded structure — and the loss is silent because the normalization record only lists transforms the front end believed safe. The interpretation half fails as early meaning-extraction: a front end that decides a fragment is a decision, or a fact, or unimportant, before CIR-1 has drawn boundaries and CIR-2 has typed status, so the semantic stage inherits a pre-judged input and cannot re-draw it. The adversarial case against CIR-0 is a source crafted so its mechanical noise is indistinguishable from its meaning — an echo that is not quite exact, a wrapper that carries a payload — and the defense is conservative: when a fragment's classification as noise is uncertain, CIR-0 retains it, because the recoverable-content guarantee makes over-retention the safe error and over-normalization the unrecoverable one. A third failure sits between the two and is the subtlest: normalization drift, where the transforms applied to a source on one acquisition differ from those applied on the next, so two acquisitions of the same unchanged source yield two different CIR-0 units and the content hashes diverge for no real reason. Drift breaks incremental ingest at its root, because the hash is the identity by which unchanged content is recognized, and a front end whose transforms are not deterministic re-acquires everything on every pass while believing it changed. CIR-0's normalization is therefore required to be deterministic — the same source, normalized twice, yields byte-identical output and an identical hash — and the normalization record is what makes that determinism auditable rather than merely asserted.

2.7 What consumes CIR-0. CIR-0's only direct consumer is CIR-1, the semantic stage immediately above it, which reads the normalized content and its source-type tag and draws atomic boundaries over it — the whole upper stack reaches CIR-0 only through CIR-1, so CIR-0's contract is narrow and its correctness is local. But CIR-0's provenance pointers are consumed estate-wide: every unit at every higher stage carries a provenance chain that terminates in a CIR-0 locator, so that any compiled decision, obligation, or law can be traced back through the stack to the exact normalized source span it was compiled from. This is the lineage the constitution requires and the reason CIR-0 must retain its locators even for content the upper stages will heavily transform — a compiled law ten stages up is auditable only because its provenance chain reaches an unbroken CIR-0 unit at the bottom. CIR-0 is thus the fabric's ground truth: it interprets nothing, it loses nothing recoverable, and it is the anchor every higher representation cites when it must prove where its meaning came from.

PART II FINAL LAW. CIR-0 is the compiler's front end, a typed representation that receives every source type — conversations, datasets, PRDs, decisions, commits, logs, audits, results, and above all corrections — normalizes away only the four mechanically redundant classes of duplicate formatting, wrappers, irrelevant metadata, and exact echoes, and interprets nothing, under a recoverability guarantee that makes over-retention the safe error; it is populated incrementally by content hash so a changed fragment re-acquires alone, and it holds the provenance locators every higher representation cites to prove where its meaning came from.

PART III — CIR-1 SEMANTIC UNIT IR AND BOUNDARY DETECTION

3.1 Mission. CIR-1 is the stage where content becomes reasoning, and its mission is decomposition: to break the normalized source that CIR-0 holds into atomic semantic units, each a single, self-contained piece of meaning that can be typed, linked, and reasoned over on its own. This is the pivot of the whole fabric, because everything above CIR-1 reasons over units, not over text — CIR-2 types a unit's epistemic status, CIR-4 records a unit as a decision, CIR-5 tracks a unit as an obligation — and a unit drawn wrong poisons every stage that inherits it. CIR-1's discipline is therefore boundary correctness: a semantic unit must be exactly one piece of meaning, neither a fragment of one nor two meanings fused, because a unit that splits a single meaning loses that meaning's force and a unit that fuses two makes both un-typeable. The stage draws these boundaries and assigns each resulting unit its DAIF-01 type, turning a wall of normalized prose into a set of atomic, typed cognitive objects.

3.2 The atomic semantic unit. A CIR-1 unit is a DAIF-01 typed object whose content is one indivisible piece of meaning and whose Type is drawn from the DAIF-01 taxonomy the estate already sealed — fact, assertion, instruction, constraint, definition, goal, preference, question, hypothesis, decision, evidence, result, problem, risk, obligation, exception, law, and pattern. CIR-1 does not invent kinds; it recognizes them, mapping each atomic unit onto exactly one kind of that closed set, and a fragment that maps to no kind is a signal the boundary was drawn wrong — real meaning always has a kind. The unit carries its provenance as a locator back into the CIR-0 span it was cut from, so the decomposition is reversible and auditable, and it carries the scope it inherits from that source. Atomicity is the defining property: a unit is atomic when it cannot be divided without one of its halves losing meaning the other supplied, and CIR-1's boundary detection is precisely the search for the cuts that leave every resulting piece whole.

3.3 Boundary detection without fixed sizes. The central error CIR-1 refuses is fixed-size decomposition — cutting the source every N tokens, or at every sentence, or at every paragraph — because meaning does not respect uniform lengths, and a fixed cut severs units mid-meaning as often as it lands on a real boundary. CIR-1 detects boundaries by meaning-shifts instead, and the heuristics are three. It cuts at topic shifts, where the source moves from one subject to another, because a new subject begins a new unit. It cuts at problem-to-solution transitions, where the source pivots from naming a gap to resolving it, because the problem and its resolution are two units — a problem-typed one and a decision-or-result-typed one — that later stages must reference separately. And it cuts to pair claims with their evidence, keeping a claim and the observation that grounds it as adjacent but distinct units, because CIR-2 must type the claim's status by reading the evidence unit that supports it. None of these heuristics depends on length; all depend on the shape of the meaning, which is why CIR-1's boundaries survive where a fixed grid's would shatter.

3.4 Type assignment at the boundary. Drawing a boundary and assigning a type are one operation, not two, because the right boundary is the one that yields a unit of exactly one type. A span that would be typed both constraint and rationale is two units — the constraint and the evidence beneath it — and the correct boundary is the cut between them. CIR-1 assigns each atomic unit its DAIF-01 kind at the moment it draws the cut, and the assignment doubles as a boundary check: if a proposed unit resists a single clean type, the boundary is wrong and must be redrawn. This coupling is why CIR-1 cannot be a dumb splitter followed by a separate typer — the two decisions constrain each other, and separating them lets a splitter produce spans no typer can classify. The upper stack depends on this coupling: CIR-2 can type an epistemic status only for a unit that is already one kind, and a unit that arrived as two fused kinds forces CIR-2 to type a chimera, which it cannot do correctly.

3.5 How CIR-1 links to the stack. CIR-1 is the layer every higher representation indexes into, and its units are the nodes the upper stack draws its edges between. CIR-2 attaches an epistemic status to each CIR-1 unit; CIR-3 links units to the intent they serve; CIR-4 groups the units that constitute a decision — its question, options, and choice; CIR-5 promotes obligation-typed units into the obligation lifecycle; CIR-6 and CIR-7 draw causal and dependency edges between units. Because all of these are edges over CIR-1 nodes, the integrity of the graph is exactly the integrity of the CIR-1 boundaries — an edge to a mis-drawn unit points at the wrong meaning, and a whole stage's reasoning inherits the error. This is why CIR-1 links downward to CIR-0 with a locator and upward to nothing directly: it publishes atomic typed nodes, and the higher stages consume them by reference, so a CIR-1 unit is the stable identity that a decision three stages up and a causal edge five stages up both point at without either knowing the other exists.

3.6 The failure mode of a bad boundary. CIR-1's signature failure is the boundary that splits a single meaning across two units, and its most damaging instance is a constraint severed from its condition. A constraint reads "never do X when Y holds," and a boundary drawn between the prohibition and its condition yields two units — a unit that says never do X, absolute and unconditioned, and an orphan fragment naming Y that no longer binds anything. The first unit is now false: it forbids X universally when the source forbade it only under Y, and CIR-2 will type this over-broad prohibition, CIR-5 may track it, and the estate will enforce a constraint its author never wrote. The inverse failure fuses two meanings into one un-typeable unit — a fact and the decision it grounded compiled as a single span — so CIR-2 cannot assign one epistemic status and CIR-4 cannot separate the observation from the choice. Both failures share a root: a boundary placed by length or by punctuation rather than by meaning, and both are prevented by the same discipline of cutting only where a meaning genuinely ends.

3.7 Adversarial cases and what consumes CIR-1. The adversarial input against CIR-1 is a source that braids its meanings — a single sentence carrying a fact, the inference drawn from it, and the decision the inference justified, with no surface marker between them — designed so any single cut severs a meaning. CIR-1's defense is that atomicity is tested by the type assignment: it proposes cuts until every resulting unit takes exactly one clean type, and a braided sentence is decomposed into three units precisely because no two-way or one-way cut yields clean types. Where a source is genuinely ambiguous, CIR-1 records the ambiguity on the units' confidence rather than guessing a boundary, so a downstream consumer knows the decomposition was uncertain. A second adversarial input is the buried obligation — a commitment phrased as an aside inside a longer explanatory passage, whose surface form gives no signal that real work was promised — and CIR-1 must cut it out as its own obligation-typed unit, because a commitment left fused inside an explanation is invisible to CIR-5 and dies with the source that carried it. CIR-1's direct consumers are every stage above it, but its foundational consumer is CIR-2, which cannot type an epistemic status for a unit that is not already atomic and single-kinded — the epistemic ladder needs one claim at a time, and CIR-1 is what guarantees the stack hands it exactly one. Its second foundational consumer is CIR-5, which can only track an obligation that CIR-1 cut cleanly enough to carry its own closure condition; an obligation unit severed from the condition that would discharge it enters the lifecycle unclosable, held open forever because nothing can ever satisfy it. Boundary correctness is therefore not an internal nicety of the decomposition stage but the precondition for the epistemic honesty of CIR-2 and the completability of every obligation the estate incurs.

PART III FINAL LAW. CIR-1 decomposes normalized source into atomic semantic units, each exactly one piece of meaning mapped to exactly one DAIF-01 kind, drawing boundaries not by fixed size but by topic shifts, problem-to-solution transitions, and claim-evidence pairing, coupling every cut to a single-type assignment so a span that resists one type is a boundary drawn wrong; its signature failure is a constraint split from its condition — a prohibition made false by losing the case that bounded it — and its units are the stable nodes every higher representation draws its edges over, so the integrity of the whole stack is the integrity of these boundaries.

PART IV — CIR-2 EPISTEMIC IR

4.1 Mission. CIR-2 is the stage that types truth-status, and its mission is to attach to every CIR-1 unit an explicit statement of how well the estate knows it — whether it is observed, demonstrated, verified, inferred, assumed, speculative, disputed, obsolete, refuted, or pending-verification. This is the representation that keeps the fabric honest, because a compiled unit stripped of its epistemic status is a claim of unknown standing, and a consumer that cannot read the standing treats every unit as equally true, enforcing an assumption with the confidence due a verified observation. CIR-2 exists so that the difference between "we measured this" and "we guessed this" survives compilation, and its cardinal discipline is that this difference may only ever weaken, never strengthen, as a unit moves up the stack — a claim cannot gain certainty by being carried forward, only by being verified. CIR-2 is where the estate's epistemic conscience is made mechanical, so no assumption is ever silently promoted to a fact by the mere act of being recorded.

4.2 The epistemic object model. A CIR-2 unit is a CIR-1 unit with its epistemic status foregrounded, and the status is not a fresh scale — it composes the ACIS epistemic ladder directly, mapping each unit onto the level ACIS assigns and deferring to that ladder as the authority on promotion. The status values arrange from strongest to weakest: observed and demonstrated and verified sit at the top, where the claim rests on direct evidence the estate holds; inferred sits below, where the claim is derived from other claims rather than seen; assumed and speculative sit lower, where the claim is posited without grounding; disputed marks a claim two units contradict; obsolete and refuted mark claims reality or evidence has overturned; and pending-verification marks a claim asserted but not yet checked, the honest default for any unit whose grounding has not been established. The required metadata is the status value and the evidence pointer — the CIR-1 evidence unit or the CIR-9 result that grounds the status — because a status without a pointer to what grounds it is an unsupported label, and CIR-2 refuses an unsupported status the way DAIF-01 refuses an absent field.

4.3 Composition with the ACIS ladder. CIR-2 does not rebuild the epistemic ladder; it references ACIS as the sealed authority on epistemic levels and their promotion chain, and this composition is load-bearing rather than decorative. ACIS already defines the levels a claim occupies and the rule that a claim may not be promoted from a lower level to a higher one without the evidence the higher level requires — a deposit proven, a hypothesis tested, an inference verified. CIR-2 maps its status values onto those levels and inherits the promotion discipline whole, so that when CIR-2 must decide whether a unit may rise from inferred to verified, it applies ACIS's rule and not a parallel one. The estate has recorded exactly why this must be a reference and not a reimplementation: a second epistemic ladder that drifts from the first produces two verdicts on the same claim's standing, the authority collision the constitution convicts, and the only defense is that CIR-2 and ACIS speak one vocabulary because CIR-2 speaks ACIS's. The No-Autopromotion cap ACIS enforces is thus CIR-2's cap, applied at every stage boundary the unit crosses.

4.4 The cardinal invariant: no downhill promotion of an inference. The invariant CIR-2 exists to enforce is stated in one line — an inferred, assumed, or speculative unit may never be lowered into a later stage as a fact — and it is called the no-collapse rule because its violation collapses the distinction between what is known and what is guessed. As a unit moves up the stack, later stages consume it: CIR-4 grounds a decision on it, CIR-6 draws a causal edge from it, CIR-10 promotes a pattern over it. At each consumption the consuming stage inherits the unit's epistemic status, and the invariant forbids any stage from recording the unit at a stronger status than CIR-2 assigned. A decision may be grounded on an assumption — but the decision then inherits the assumption's weakness, and CIR-4 records that its justification rests on an unverified claim, so the decision is revisable when the assumption fails. What the invariant forbids is the silent upgrade: a stage that reads an assumed unit and records the fact it would have been if verified, so the assumption enters downstream reasoning wearing a certainty nothing earned. CIR-2's status travels with the unit precisely so this upgrade is detectable and refused.

4.5 How CIR-2 gates promotion between stages. CIR-2 is not a passive label but a gate the stack passes through, and it gates in both directions. Upward, it caps the status any consuming stage may assign: a stage that wishes to treat a unit as verified must present the evidence verification requires, and absent that evidence CIR-2 holds the unit at its established status, so the upper stack cannot manufacture certainty. Downward, it propagates weakness: when a strong-seeming unit is discovered to rest on a weak one — a fact whose only support is an assumption — CIR-2 lowers the dependent unit's effective status to the weakest link in its support chain, because a claim is only as certain as the least certain claim it depends on. This weakest-link propagation is what makes the epistemic stage a real gate rather than a coat of paint: it means a decision built on an assumption cannot present as verified no matter how confidently it is phrased, and an evaluation that measured a value is not dragged down by a speculative claim it never depended on. The gate reads the dependency edges CIR-7 draws to know which supports which.

4.6 The failure mode of epistemic collapse. CIR-2's signature failure is epistemic collapse: an assumption compiled as a truth, so a claim posited without grounding enters the stack at a status it did not earn and every stage above inherits the false certainty. The estate has recorded this shape repeatedly — a founding finding treated as verified when it was a hypothesis, an untested guess recorded as a measured result, a plan's assertion compiled as observed disk. The damage is that epistemic collapse is invisible at the point of consumption: a consumer reading a unit typed verified acts on it as verified, and nothing in the unit reveals that the verification never happened, so the collapse propagates until a downstream failure exposes it far from its origin. CIR-2 defends against collapse by refusing an unsupported status at intake — a unit claiming verified without an evidence pointer is held at pending-verification — and by the weakest-link propagation that stops a strong status from surviving atop a weak support. The adversarial case is a unit whose author sincerely believes it is verified but whose grounding is an inference; CIR-2 does not read belief, it reads the evidence pointer, and a missing pointer convicts the status regardless of the author's confidence.

4.7 What consumes CIR-2. Every stage above CIR-2 consumes its status, because every stage that reasons over a unit must know how certain the unit is before it acts. CIR-4 reads epistemic status to record whether a decision's justification is verified or assumed; CIR-9 reads it to distinguish a measured result from a projected one; CIR-10 reads it as the gate on promotion, since a pattern may become an institutional law only at the epistemic level ACIS's ladder permits, and CIR-2 is where that level is recorded. DAIF-03's fidelity gate reads CIR-2 to verify that compilation did not silently upgrade a status — a compiled unit whose epistemic status is stronger than its source's is a fidelity violation, an assumption the compiler promoted. And DAIF-01's Confidence field is grounded in CIR-2 for inferential units, exactly as that dataset stated: a unit's confidence rests on the epistemic level of its derivation, and CIR-2 is the stage that records that level. CIR-2 is thus the estate's guarantee that certainty is carried, never invented, through the whole compilation.

PART IV FINAL LAW. CIR-2 types every unit's truth-status — observed, demonstrated, verified, inferred, assumed, speculative, disputed, obsolete, refuted, or pending-verification — composing the ACIS epistemic ladder and inheriting its No-Autopromotion cap rather than rebuilding it, and it enforces the cardinal invariant that an inference may never be lowered into a later stage as a fact by capping the status any consuming stage may assign and propagating a support chain's weakness to its weakest link, so that epistemic collapse — an assumption compiled as a truth — is convicted at intake by the missing evidence pointer rather than discovered by the downstream failure it would otherwise cause.

PART V — CIR-3 INTENT IR

5.1 Mission. CIR-3 is the stage that preserves intention, and its mission is to hold, separately and durably, what a source actually wanted — its original intention, its expected result, its explicit and implicit criteria, its priorities and preferences, its prohibitions and non-goals, and its definition of done. This representation exists because intent is the dimension of meaning most easily lost in every other kind of processing: a summary keeps the task and drops the reason, a semantic decomposition keeps the instructions and scatters the purpose that bound them, and a consumer left with instructions but no intent optimizes the letter of the request while missing what was wanted. CIR-3 refuses that loss by compiling intent as a first-class object that travels beside the work, so a later actor can check any result not only against what was literally asked but against what the asking was for. Intent is the standard the whole compilation is ultimately judged by, because a faithful carrier that satisfies the words and betrays the purpose has failed.

5.2 The intent object model. A CIR-3 unit is a DAIF-01 typed object of kind goal or the intent-bearing kinds, foregrounding a structured account of purpose with several required slots. It holds the original intention — the end the source was reaching for, in the source's own terms. It holds the expected result — the observable state that would satisfy the intention. It holds the criteria, split into explicit criteria the source stated and implicit criteria the source assumed without stating, because the unstated ones are the ones a literal reading omits and a purposeful reading must recover. It holds priorities, where the source ranked competing wants, and preferences, where the source leaned without ranking. It holds prohibitions and non-goals — the things the source explicitly did not want and the ends it declared out of reach — because an intent is bounded as much by what it excludes as by what it seeks. And it holds the definition of done, the closure condition that says the intention is satisfied. Required are the intention, the expected result, and the definition of done; the criteria, priorities, preferences, prohibitions, and non-goals are present where the source expressed them and marked absent, not guessed, where it did not.

5.3 Composition with one_shot's intent scaffold. CIR-3 does not invent an intent-capture procedure; it composes one_shot's intent and Q&A scaffold, which the estate already runs as its mechanism for extracting intention before work begins. one_shot's six-question interrogation exists precisely to surface the implicit criteria, the priorities, the prohibitions, and the definition of done that a raw request leaves unstated, and CIR-3 reads the answers to those questions as its intent slots — the Q&A pass is the intent-capture front end, and CIR-3 is the typed representation it populates. This composition is why CIR-3 refuses to guess an absent criterion: one_shot's discipline is that six honest answers beat one vague paragraph, and CIR-3 inherits that discipline by recording an un-interrogated criterion as absent rather than fabricating it. Where a source arrived without a one_shot pass — a conversation, a correction — CIR-3 extracts what intent the source expressed and marks the rest absent, so the intent object honestly distinguishes captured intention from intention that was never surfaced. The scaffold is referenced as the capture authority and never re-narrated.

5.4 Why intent must be preserved separately. Intent is compiled as its own representation, distinct from the instructions and decisions it motivates, because a result can satisfy the instructions and violate the intent, and only a separately held intent lets a consumer detect the violation. The instructions say do X; the intent says X was wanted in order to achieve Y; and a result that does X in a way that defeats Y satisfies the literal instruction and fails the purpose. If intent lived only inside the instructions, this failure would be invisible — the consumer would check the result against the instructions, find it conformant, and declare done. By holding intent separately, CIR-3 gives the consumer a second, higher check: does the result serve the intention, not merely execute the instruction. This is the difference between a compilation that preserves behavior and one that preserves only text, because the behavior the source meant to provoke was the achievement of Y, and a representation that kept only X would provoke the wrong behavior with perfect fidelity to the words. Separate intent is what makes the definition of done a purpose-check rather than a checklist.

5.5 Explicit and implicit criteria. CIR-3's handling of criteria is where it earns its keep, because the criteria that decide whether a result is right are mostly unstated. Explicit criteria are the conditions the source named — a value, a format, a bound — and compiling them is straightforward. Implicit criteria are the conditions the source assumed everyone shared — that the result would not break an adjacent system, that it would respect a constraint the source never thought to restate, that it would be usable by the actor who requested it — and these are the criteria a literal reading omits and a purposeful reading must recover. CIR-3 recovers implicit criteria through the one_shot interrogation where a source ran through it, and marks them explicitly as implicit so a consumer knows they were inferred rather than stated. The prohibitions and non-goals are the negative face of the same recovery: a source that wanted X almost always did not want X-at-the-cost-of-Z, and the non-goal recording Z-is-out-of-scope is what stops a consumer from over-delivering into territory the source excluded. Criteria, priorities, prohibitions, and non-goals together bound the intent from every side.

5.6 The failure mode of lost intent. CIR-3's signature failure is lost intent: a compilation that keeps the task and drops the purpose, so a later actor optimizes the stated task while missing what was actually wanted. Its most damaging instance is the technically-conformant miss — a result that meets every explicit criterion and violates an implicit one, delivered with confidence because the checklist passed. The estate has recorded this shape as the gap between satisfying the literal words and honoring the intent, and it is exactly the failure a separate intent representation exists to catch. Lost intent is insidious because it passes every surface review: the result does what was said, the instructions are satisfied, and nothing in a purposeless compilation reveals that the purpose was betrayed. CIR-3 defends against it by making the definition of done a check against the intention rather than the instruction, and by carrying the implicit criteria and non-goals that a literal check omits. The adversarial case is a request whose literal reading and purposeful reading diverge sharply — a task easy to satisfy in a way that defeats its point — and CIR-3's held intent is what lets a consumer see the divergence and choose the purpose.

5.7 What consumes CIR-3. CIR-3 is consumed by every stage that must judge whether work is right rather than merely present. CIR-4 reads intent to record whether a decision served the purpose it was made under, so a decision that satisfied a constraint and defeated the intention is marked as such. CIR-5 reads intent to define an obligation's closure condition, since an obligation is done when the intention behind it is achieved, not when a box is checked. DAIF-03's fidelity gate reads CIR-3 as the behavioral standard the compilation is measured against — behavioral equivalence is equivalence of provoked purpose, and CIR-3 holds the purpose. DAIF-07's obligation lifecycle reads CIR-3 to know what completing an obligation actually requires, and DAIF-08's context assembly reads CIR-3 to keep a reasoning session aimed at the mission it was assembled for. Across all of them, CIR-3 is the representation that answers the question every other stage eventually asks — not what was said, but what was wanted — and it is the reason the fabric can claim to compile purpose and not only prose.

PART V FINAL LAW. CIR-3 preserves intention as a first-class representation — original intention, expected result, explicit and implicit criteria, priorities, preferences, prohibitions, non-goals, and definition of done — composing one_shot's intent and Q&A scaffold as its capture authority and marking un-surfaced criteria absent rather than guessing them, held separately from the instructions it motivates precisely so a result that satisfies the words and violates the purpose is detectable; its signature failure is lost intent, the technically-conformant miss that optimizes the stated task while betraying what was wanted, caught only because the definition of done is a purpose-check rather than a checklist.

PART VI — CIR-4 DECISION IR

6.1 Mission. CIR-4 is the stage that compiles decisions, and its mission is to hold every choice the estate makes as a complete, revisable object rather than a bare conclusion — with the question it resolved, the options it weighed, the choice it landed on, the justification behind that choice, the evidence and authority that backed it, its scope, its consequences and trade-offs, the conditions under which it should be revised, its expiry, and the decisions it superseded. A decision is the most dangerous kind of unit to compile lossily, because a decision recorded as only its outcome looks complete and is un-reversible: a later actor who finds the conclusion but not the reasoning cannot tell whether the choice still holds, cannot safely reverse it, and cannot detect that its grounding has expired. CIR-4 exists so that a decision survives compilation with everything a future reconsideration needs, and its discipline is that a choice is never held apart from the reasoning that produced it, because the reasoning is what makes the choice governable.

6.2 The decision object model. A CIR-4 unit is a DAIF-01 typed object of kind decision, and its slots are the anatomy of a governable choice. It holds the question — the open matter the decision resolved, without which the choice floats unattached to any problem. It holds the options considered, including the ones rejected, because a decision's meaning is as much in what it declined as in what it took. It holds the choice itself, the option selected. It holds the justification — the reasoning that made the choice preferable — and the evidence that justification rested on, pointing at the CIR-2 units and CIR-9 results that grounded it. It holds the authority that made the decision and the scope within which the decision binds, both inherited from DAIF-01. It holds the consequences and trade-offs the choice accepted, the revision conditions under which it should be reopened, and an expiry where the decision was time-bounded. And it holds supersession pointers to any earlier decisions this one replaced. Required are the question, the choice, the justification, the authority, and the scope; a decision missing any of these is not compilable as a decision, because each is load-bearing for a future reversal.

6.3 Composition with DRK's decision_record. CIR-4 does not define a new decision format; it composes DRK's decision_record, the estate's sealed structure for capturing a decision with its rationale, and a compiled CIR-4 unit is that record's typed projection into the stack. DRK already requires a decision to carry its question, its options, its justification, and its authority — the anatomy that makes a decision auditable and revisable — and CIR-4 reads a decision_record as its source and populates its slots from it, deferring to DRK as the authority on what a well-formed decision contains. This composition matters because DRK also encodes the estate's limits on autonomous decision-making — the authority ceiling beyond which a self-issued decision may not reach — and CIR-4 inherits that ceiling through the authority slot, so a compiled decision cannot claim an authority DRK would have refused to grant it. Where a decision entered the fabric without a DRK record — a choice made in conversation and never formalized — CIR-4 compiles what the source expressed and marks the missing slots absent, flagging the decision as incompletely recorded so a consumer knows its revisability is compromised. DRK is referenced as the decision authority and never re-narrated.

6.4 Why a decision without its justification is un-revisable. The core law of CIR-4 is that a decision held as a bare conclusion cannot be safely reversed, because reversal requires seeing the reason. To reverse a decision safely, a later actor must know why it was made — what problem it solved, what evidence backed it, what trade-off it accepted — so that the actor can check whether that reason still holds. A decision recorded as only its outcome hides all of this: the actor sees that X was chosen but not that X was chosen because of evidence E under constraint C, so the actor cannot tell whether E is still true or C still binds, and any reversal is a blind one that may re-open a problem the original decision quietly solved. The justification is therefore not commentary on the decision but the precondition for governing it: it is what lets a revision be reasoned rather than guessed, and it is why CIR-4 refuses to compile a choice without the reasoning that produced it. A conclusion without its justification is a fact-shaped fossil — it records what was done and destroys the ability to reconsider it.

6.5 Revision conditions, expiry, and supersession. CIR-4 compiles not only why a decision was made but when it should be unmade, through three forward-looking slots. Revision conditions name the circumstances that should reopen the decision — a change in the evidence it rested on, a shift in the constraint it accepted, an outcome that contradicts its expected consequence — so a decision carries its own reconsideration triggers rather than waiting to be questioned by accident. Expiry names a decision's declared lifespan where it had one, common to decisions taken under conditions known to be temporary, and an expired decision is surfaced for renewal rather than silently held as permanent, exactly as DAIF-01's validity field governs. Supersession pointers link a decision to the earlier ones it replaced, forming the lineage a consumer traverses to find the current decision on a matter and to see what it overturned. Together these slots make a decision a living object with a lifecycle, and they connect CIR-4 to DAIF-01's validity states and to DAIF-21's reality synchronization, which watches the revision conditions and flips a decision's validity when reality moves out from under it.

6.6 The failure mode of the bare conclusion. CIR-4's signature failure is the decision kept as a bare conclusion — a choice recorded without its question, justification, or authority — and its damage is un-revisability, which the estate feels as decisions that cannot be safely changed because no one can see why they were made. A bare conclusion invites two equally bad responses when it is later questioned: a consumer reverses it blindly, re-opening whatever problem it solved, or a consumer treats it as immovable precisely because its reasoning is invisible and no one dares touch it, so a decision that should have been revised outlives its grounding. The estate has recorded this as the un-appealable, un-auditable decision the constitution's decision-record discipline exists to prevent. The adversarial case is a decision compiled with a plausible-sounding conclusion but a fabricated or absent justification — a choice that looks reasoned and is not — and CIR-4 defends against it by requiring the evidence pointers that ground the justification to resolve to real CIR-2 and CIR-9 units, so a justification that points at nothing is convicted the way CIR-2 convicts an unsupported epistemic status.

6.7 What consumes CIR-4. CIR-4 is consumed by the stages and datasets that must reason over the estate's choices. CIR-5 reads decisions to find the open ones — a decision deferred is an obligation to decide, and CIR-4's un-resolved questions feed CIR-5's obligation lifecycle. CIR-6 reads decisions as causes in its causal graph, since a decision is often why a subsequent state came to be. CIR-10 reads decisions that recur into institutional patterns and laws, promoting a repeatedly-made choice into a standing rule at the epistemic level ACIS permits. DAIF-00's constitution reads CIR-4 as the record a duplicate-to-advantage verdict is itself compiled into, and DAIF-21's reality synchronization reads CIR-4's revision conditions and expiries to know when a decision has gone stale. Across all of them, CIR-4 is the representation that makes the estate's choices governable rather than merely made — every decision carried with the reasoning that lets it be revised, so the estate can change its mind safely instead of being frozen by conclusions whose reasons it can no longer see.

PART VI FINAL LAW. CIR-4 compiles every decision as a complete revisable object — question, options, choice, justification, evidence, authority, scope, consequences, trade-offs, revision conditions, expiry, and superseded decisions — composing DRK's decision_record as its authority and inheriting its autonomy ceiling, under the core law that a decision held as a bare conclusion is un-revisable because a safe reversal requires seeing the reason it was made; its signature failure is the reasonless conclusion, defended against by requiring the justification's evidence pointers to resolve to real units, so the estate can change its mind by reasoning rather than by guessing or by fear.

PART VII — CIR-5 OBLIGATION IR

7.1 Mission. CIR-5 is the stage that compiles unfinished work, and its mission is to hold every obligation the estate incurs as a formal, tracked object — the tasks, promises, deliverables, pending verifications, migrations, follow-ups, open decisions, unaccepted PRDs, and untested functions that represent work that still must happen. This representation exists because obligations are the units the estate most reliably loses: a task named inside a conversation dies when the conversation closes, a promised follow-up evaporates when the session ends, a pending verification is forgotten the moment attention moves on, and the work goes undone not because anyone decided against it but because no representation carried it forward. CIR-5 refuses that loss by compiling obligations into typed objects that outlive their originating source, so an obligation named once persists as a tracked commitment until it is closed or explicitly abandoned. It is the representation that converts a fleeting mention of work-to-be-done into a durable claim on the estate's future attention.

7.2 The obligation object model. A CIR-5 unit is a DAIF-01 typed object of kind obligation, and its slots make an item of unfinished work trackable. It holds the object — what must be done, the deliverable or the change or the verification owed. It holds the owner — the authority or actor responsible for it, so an obligation is never ownerless. It holds the origin — the CIR-0 source and the decision or intent it arose from, so an obligation can be traced to why it was incurred. It holds the priority, ranking it against competing obligations. It holds the state — open, in-progress, blocked, done, or abandoned — the lifecycle position that tells a consumer whether the obligation is still live. It holds dependencies on other obligations, since work is often ordered. It holds the closure definition — the condition that, when met, discharges the obligation, drawn from the intent's definition of done. It holds the required evidence — what must be observed to prove closure, so an obligation cannot be marked done without proof. And it holds aging and abandonment risk — how long the obligation has been open and how likely it is to be silently dropped. Required are the object, the owner, the origin, the state, and the closure definition; an obligation missing its closure definition can never be proven done and is a permanent open item by construction.

7.3 How obligations enter and move. Obligations enter CIR-5 from several stages above CIR-1. A CIR-1 unit typed obligation enters directly. A CIR-4 decision that was deferred rather than resolved enters as an obligation to decide. A CIR-3 intent whose definition of done is unmet enters as an obligation to complete. An unaccepted PRD, an untested function, a pending verification, a promised migration — each is an obligation the estate incurred and must carry. Once entered, an obligation moves through its lifecycle states under evidence: it advances from open to in-progress when work begins, to blocked when a dependency stalls it, and to done only when its required evidence is observed to satisfy its closure definition — never on assertion alone, because an obligation marked done without evidence is the scaffold illusion the estate convicts. An obligation may also move to abandoned, but only explicitly and with a recorded reason, because silent abandonment is exactly the failure CIR-5 exists to prevent — the difference between a decision to drop work and work simply forgotten.

7.4 CIR-5 feeds DAIF-07's obligation lifecycle. CIR-5 is the representation, and DAIF-07 is the fabric that acts on it — the Obligation Lifecycle and Work Completion Authority dataset consumes CIR-5 as its formal input, and this seam is the whole point of compiling obligations. Before CIR-5, the estate's unfinished-work watchers — the scaffold-auditor, the owner_queue, backlog_autopilot, the liveness checks — scanned loose text for obligations, inferring from prose that a task existed and guessing its state, which is exactly the fragile pattern the estate has recorded as an orphaned watcher reading undifferentiated words. CIR-5 replaces that scan with a formal IR: DAIF-07 stops reading prose and reads compiled obligation objects, each carrying its owner, state, closure definition, required evidence, and aging, so the Unfinished Work Watcher consumes structure instead of inferring it. This is the division the estate draws everywhere — CIR-5 is the inert representation, DAIF-07 is the acting runtime that advances obligations, escalates the aging ones, and enforces closure — and the two compose along the seam, DAIF-07 writing state transitions back onto the CIR-5 objects it consumes rather than either owning both halves.

7.5 Aging, abandonment risk, and the priority queue. CIR-5 compiles two forward-looking signals that make obligations visible before they are lost. Aging records how long an obligation has been open, so an obligation that has sat untouched surfaces its own staleness rather than waiting to be noticed, and DAIF-07 reads aging to escalate the obligations the estate is quietly failing to close. Abandonment risk records the likelihood that an obligation will be silently dropped — an obligation with no recent activity, a distant owner, or a dependency that keeps blocking it carries high risk — and it is the signal that lets the estate distinguish an obligation being deliberately deferred from one being forgotten. Together with priority, these signals form the queue DAIF-07 works: the estate's open obligations ranked by priority, aged, and risk-scored, so the most important actionable obligation is the one surfaced, exactly the discipline the estate's backlog machinery already runs. CIR-5's contribution is to make that queue a queue of formal objects rather than a scan of loose mentions, so the ranking is over real closure definitions and real evidence requirements rather than over guesses about what a sentence meant.

7.6 The failure mode of the sentence-only obligation. CIR-5's signature failure is the obligation that exists only as a sentence in a closed conversation — a task mentioned, agreed to, and never compiled, so it dies when the source that named it is done. This is the founding failure the whole DAIF-07 vertical exists to end: work that everyone intended to do and no one carried, lost not to a decision but to the absence of a representation. Its damage is silent and cumulative — each un-compiled obligation is a small unkept promise, and an estate that loses them steadily accretes a debt of forgotten work that no one can see because no representation holds it. CIR-5 defends against this by compiling an obligation the moment it is named, giving it an owner and a closure definition so it becomes a tracked claim rather than a fading mention, and by the aging and abandonment-risk signals that make a neglected obligation surface itself. The adversarial case is an obligation phrased so casually it reads as conversational filler — a "we should eventually" that carries real work — and CIR-5's boundary detection, inherited from CIR-1, is tuned to recognize obligation-typed units even in soft phrasing, because the softness of the phrasing is no measure of the reality of the work.

7.7 What consumes CIR-5 and the invariant it guards. CIR-5's primary consumer is DAIF-07, which reads it as the formal substrate of the obligation lifecycle, but its objects are read across the estate: DAIF-08's context assembly reads open obligations to keep a session aware of the work it owes, DAIF-00's constitution reads them as the commitments a duplicate-to-advantage verdict may itself incur, and the estate's liveness and owner-queue machinery read them as the actionable items to surface. The invariant CIR-5 guards is that unfinished work is never carried as prose — every obligation is a typed object with an owner, a state, a closure definition, and a required-evidence gate, so the estate's watchers consume structure and the estate's obligations cannot die by being forgotten. An obligation may be closed by evidence or abandoned by decision, but it may never simply vanish, because a vanished obligation is the one failure the whole representation was built to make impossible. CIR-5 is thus the estate's guarantee that what it commits to do, it continues to see until the work is done or deliberately dropped.

PART VII FINAL LAW. CIR-5 compiles unfinished work — tasks, promises, deliverables, pending verifications, migrations, follow-ups, open decisions, unaccepted PRDs, and untested functions — into typed obligation objects carrying object, owner, origin, priority, state, dependencies, closure definition, required evidence, aging, and abandonment risk, advancing to done only on observed evidence and to abandoned only by recorded decision; it feeds DAIF-07 as a formal IR so the Unfinished Work Watcher consumes structure instead of scanning loose prose, defeating the one failure it exists to end — the obligation that lives only as a sentence in a closed conversation and dies when the conversation does.
