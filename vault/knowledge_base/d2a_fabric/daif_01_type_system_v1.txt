DAIF-01 — COGNITIVE TYPE SYSTEM AND CANONICAL ONTOLOGY

PART I — THESIS AND MISSION: THE TYPED COGNITIVE OBJECT

1.1 Mission. This dataset states the law that gives every unit of knowledge the Power Pack produces a machine-readable identity. The law is one sentence: no piece of reasoning may enter the estate as bare prose, because a bare sentence carries its meaning to a human reader and hides everything a machine needs to act on it — its authority, its reach, its strength, its currency, and the rule by which it may be overridden. Today a constraint like "never do X on this project" lives as a clause inside a paragraph, and a downstream consumer that reads that clause knows what it says and nothing about how to treat it. Is it a law or a leaning? Does it bind the whole estate or a single feature? Who set it, when, and does it still hold? May a session override it, and if so on what evidence? The prose answers none of these, so the consumer does the only two things bare prose permits: it either obeys the sentence blindly, treating a soft preference as an inviolable rule, or it ignores the sentence entirely, treating a hard safety constraint as optional advice. Both are failures, and both are unavoidable while the knowledge is untyped, because typing is exactly the information the two failures lack.

1.2 The liability of the untyped sentence. An untyped sentence is a liability precisely because it looks complete. It reads well, it states a real intention, and it passes every human review — and then it reaches a mechanical consumer that cannot see the eight things it does not say. The cost is deferred to the moment of use, the same deferral the sibling constitution traces for a drifting duplicate: the sentence agrees with everyone the day it is written and detonates the day a consumer must decide whether to enforce it against a conflicting one. A planner that cannot read a unit's strength cannot know whether to halt on it or trade it off; an assembler that cannot read a unit's scope cannot know whether it belongs in this project's context at all; a validator that cannot read a unit's validity cannot know whether it is enforcing a current rule or a retired one. Every one of these consumers is forced to guess, and a guess about a rule's authority is the seed of the authority collision the estate has recorded, the scope leak that contaminates an unrelated project, and the stale rule enforced long after it was refuted. The type system exists to move that information from the reader's head, where it is unrecorded and unshared, onto the object itself, where every consumer reads it the same way.

1.3 The typed cognitive object. The unit this dataset defines is the typed cognitive object: any piece of institutional reasoning — a fact, a constraint, a decision, an obligation — carried not as a sentence but as a structure with eight explicit fields. Type names what kind of unit it is. Authority names who may assert or override it. Scope names where it applies. Strength names whether it may ever be traded off. Validity names whether it is still in force. Override-policy names the rule by which it may be set aside. Provenance names where it came from. Confidence names how sure the estate is of it. These eight fields are the spine of the entire dataset, and every later Part is one field elaborated into its own taxonomy, invariants, and failure modes — Part III the Type, Part IV the Authority, Part V the Scope, Part VI the Validity, Part VII the Strength and Override-policy, with Provenance and Confidence threaded through all of them as the evidence and the certainty every field ultimately rests on. A unit that carries all eight is mechanically interpretable: a consumer reads its fields and knows, without re-reading the prose or asking a human, how to treat it.

1.4 What typing makes possible. The single capability the type system delivers is mechanical interpretability, and everything the later fabric does depends on it. A consumer that can read a unit's type knows what shape of thing it holds and therefore what operations are valid on it — a constraint is enforced, a hypothesis is tested, a preference is weighed, and mistaking one for another is impossible when the type is on the object rather than inferred from its phrasing. A consumer that can read authority, scope, strength, and validity together can resolve a conflict between two units by rule rather than by guess: rank them by authority, check that their scopes actually collide, prefer the hard over the soft, and drop the one whose validity has lapsed. None of this is available to a consumer reading prose, and all of it is available to a consumer reading fields — which is why the type system is not a convenience layer but the precondition for every mechanical reasoning the estate wants to perform.

1.5 Shared infrastructure, not an actor. This dataset defines a substrate and does not act on it. The typed cognitive object is the shared vocabulary that later fabrics consume: the reasoning intermediate representations of DAIF-02 are built from typed fields, so a CIR's every slot inherits its type from here rather than re-inventing one; the institutional contracts of DAIF-04 name typed obligations between systems, so a contract's terms are typed cognitive objects and not fresh prose; the obligation lifecycle of DAIF-07 tracks typed obligations from birth to completion, and it can only do so because the obligation arrives already typed. This dataset itself runs no pipeline, gates no deploy, and issues no verdict. It is the type system the acting systems reason with, the way a language's type system is not the program but the thing that lets the program be checked. A dataset that acts would be a different sovereign object with its own consumers; this one is deliberately inert, a definition the estate reads, so that its correctness is a matter of the schema it states and never of a runtime it does not have.

1.6 Composition, never re-narration. The typed cognitive object composes systems the estate already owns rather than forking their machinery, in strict obedience to the sibling constitution's first law. It composes SQI-00's verdict ontology and its Q0–Q5 authorization ladder — the verdicts and the authority levels are typed values this dataset references, not a parallel set it re-derives. It composes the ACIS epistemic ladder, whose levels are exactly the discipline that keeps an inference from being typed as a fact, so Part III cites the ladder as the enforcement its Type field defers to rather than rebuilding it. It composes the hard_rules precedence, whose sealed bug-stops are the highest-authority units in the estate and whose ranking the Authority field inherits. And it composes GK-02's semantic identity and canonicalization, so that two units naming the same thing two ways are recognized as one typed object rather than two. Each of these is referenced by name and never re-narrated, because re-narrating a sealed system is the ornamental restatement the estate convicts — the type system's job is to give those systems' outputs a common typed shape, not to say again what they already say.

1.7 The dataset's place in the fabric. The type system is the second dataset in the DAIF spine and the foundation the rest stands on, because a representation cannot be typed before there is a type system, a contract cannot name a typed obligation before there is a typed object, and an obligation cannot be tracked before it can be typed. Everything downstream reasons over the eight fields defined here, so an error in this dataset propagates into every consumer, and a gap here is a gap in every fabric that reads it. This is why the burden on the type system is completeness: it must type every kind of unit the estate produces, name every authority that can assert one, bound every scope a unit can hold, and define every validity state a unit can occupy, because a unit the type system cannot describe is a unit the fabric cannot reason over, forced back into the bare prose the whole dataset exists to abolish.

PART I FINAL LAW. An untyped sentence is a liability because it hides the eight things a machine needs — its type, authority, scope, strength, validity, override-policy, provenance, and confidence — leaving every consumer to obey it blindly or ignore it entirely, and the typed cognitive object abolishes that liability by carrying those eight fields on itself, a shared and inert substrate that DAIF-02, DAIF-04, and DAIF-07 reason mechanically over, composed from SQI-00, ACIS, hard_rules, and GK-02 rather than forked from them.

PART II — THE OBJECT MODEL AND ITS FIELDS

2.1 Mission. This Part specifies the eight fields as a schema: what each holds, the values it may take, the value it defaults to, the invariants it must satisfy, and the exact failure that lands downstream when it is absent or wrong. The specification matters because a typed object is only as trustworthy as its weakest field, and a field left vague re-admits through one seam the ambiguity the whole type system exists to close. The eight are not independent columns; they reference one another, and the relationships are load-bearing — Override-policy is meaningless without Authority to rank the overriding party, Validity is meaningless without a temporal frame, Scope bounds where the other seven even apply, and Confidence and Provenance qualify how much any of the rest can be trusted. The schema is therefore stated field by field and then as a web of references, so a consumer reading a unit knows not only what each field says but how the fields constrain each other.

2.2 Type, Authority, Scope. Type holds the kind of the unit, drawn from the closed taxonomy of Part III — fact, constraint, decision, obligation, and the rest — and it has no default, because a unit whose kind is unknown cannot be operated on at all and a defaulted type would silently misclassify it; an untyped unit is rejected at intake rather than admitted with a guessed kind. Authority holds the party entitled to assert or override the unit, ranked on the ladder of Part IV, and its default is the lowest rung, agent-scoped, so that an unattributed claim carries the least power rather than the most — the opposite default would let an anonymous sentence pose as a law. Scope holds the reach of the unit on the ladder of Part V, and its default is the narrowest defensible scope, not the broadest, because a unit that forgets to declare its reach must be assumed local until proven universal, and the reverse default is exactly the leak that contaminates unrelated projects. If Type is absent the unit cannot be dispatched; if Authority is absent it cannot be ranked in a conflict; if Scope is absent it cannot be placed in a context — three distinct downstream breaks from three absent fields.

2.3 Strength, Validity, Override-policy. Strength holds whether the unit is hard, meaning it may never be silently violated, or soft, meaning it may be traded off against other units, and its default is soft, because defaulting to hard would let every casual preference halt the estate while defaulting to soft at worst under-enforces a unit that its author can promote — the safe default is the one whose error is recoverable. Validity holds the lifecycle state of Part VI — active, superseded, obsolete, expired, refuted, pending-verification — and its default is pending-verification, never active, so an unverified unit does not enter live reasoning by omission. Override-policy holds the rule by which the unit may be set aside: who may do it, on what evidence, and with what record, expressed as a reference into the appeal path of DAIF-00 Part XV. Override-policy's default is derived, not blank — a hard unit defaults to no-silent-override and a soft unit to trade-off-under-record — because a blank override-policy is the field whose absence produced the most dangerous failure the estate knows, a hard safety constraint that a consumer treated as freely overridable because nothing on the object said otherwise.

2.4 Provenance and Confidence. Provenance holds where the unit came from — the session, the decision record, the incident, the dataset, or the authority that produced it — and it has no default, because a claim with no origin cannot be verified, re-measured on appeal, or promoted, and a unit without provenance is treated as a rumor the estate declines to act on until its source is named. Confidence holds how sure the estate is of the unit, and it is not a mood but a value grounded in Provenance and, where the unit is an inference, in the ACIS epistemic level of its derivation; its default is the lowest confidence, so an unqualified claim is trusted least. Provenance and Confidence together are the qualifiers the other six fields rest on — a high-authority hard constraint with weak provenance and low confidence is not the immovable object its Authority and Strength suggest, and a consumer must read all four together to know how hard to lean on the unit. This is why the two fields thread through every other Part: an authority claim is only as good as its provenance, a validity state is only as trustworthy as the evidence that set it, and a strength of hard means little if the confidence beneath it is a guess.

2.5 The reference web among the fields. The fields form a directed web, and the edges are the invariants. Override-policy references Authority: a policy that says "may be overridden by project authority" is inert unless Authority names a party the ladder can compare, so an override-policy citing an authority level that does not exist is a schema violation caught at intake. Validity references time: superseded and expired are meaningless without the temporal fields of Part VI — a supersession pointer to the unit that replaced it, an effective-from and an expiry — so a validity state without its temporal anchor is incomplete. Scope bounds all seven: a unit's authority, strength, and validity apply only within its scope, so two units that appear to conflict do not if their scopes do not overlap, and the resolution of Part V runs before the ranking of Part IV. Confidence gates enforcement: a hard unit with low confidence enters reasoning as a flag rather than a wall, because the estate does not halt on a claim it is not sure of. Reading the web is how a consumer converts eight field values into one coherent treatment of the unit.

2.6 Unknown is a value; absent is a defect. The schema draws a hard line the whole type system depends on: a field set to unknown is a valid, typed state, and a field that is absent is a defect. Unknown is an honest declaration — the estate does not yet know this unit's confidence, or its provenance is under investigation, or its validity is pending a verification that has not run — and a consumer reading unknown knows exactly how to treat it, usually by declining to act until the unknown resolves. Absent is the opposite: a field simply missing, so the consumer cannot tell whether the value is unknown, defaulted, or forgotten, and it is forced back into the guessing the type system exists to end. The estate has recorded this distinction as the difference between a zero and a null, and between a decrease-gate that reads an unrecognized idiom as zero and one that reads it as uncountable — treating absent as a value is how whole classes of unit go silently unhandled. The type system therefore forbids the silent absence: every field is present with an explicit value, and unknown is spelled unknown, so that a consumer never confuses a field the estate chose not to fill with a field it failed to.

2.7 The object as a whole. A typed cognitive object is well-formed only when all eight fields are present, each either a legal value or an explicit unknown, the reference web's invariants hold, and the defaults have been applied where the author left a field to the schema rather than to absence. A well-formed object is self-describing: any consumer, in any fabric, reads its eight fields and treats it identically, which is the interoperability the downstream datasets require — DAIF-02 cannot build a representation from fields that are sometimes present and sometimes guessed, DAIF-04 cannot contract on an obligation whose strength is ambiguous, and DAIF-07 cannot track an obligation whose validity is absent. The object model is thus the estate's guarantee that a unit means the same thing to every reader, and the schema's strictness — no absent fields, unknown spelled explicitly, defaults that fail safe — is what makes the guarantee hold under the one condition that matters, when the author was careless and the consumer is a machine.

PART II FINAL LAW. The typed cognitive object is eight present fields — Type, Authority, Scope, Strength, Validity, Override-policy, Provenance, Confidence — each carrying a legal value or an explicit unknown, bound by a reference web in which Override-policy needs Authority, Validity needs time, Scope bounds all, and Confidence rests on Provenance, with fail-safe defaults for the fields an author may omit; and the schema's inviolable line is that unknown is a value a consumer can act on while an absent field is a defect that re-admits the guessing the type system was built to end.

PART III — THE TYPE TAXONOMY OF COGNITIVE OBJECTS

3.1 Mission. The Type field draws from a closed taxonomy, and this Part is that taxonomy: the kinds of unit the estate produces, each distinguished from its neighbors, each carrying the fields its kind requires, each with a named failure mode when it is mistyped. The taxonomy is closed rather than open because an open type set re-admits ambiguity — a consumer cannot reason mechanically over a kind it has never seen — and every unit the estate produces must map to exactly one of these kinds or be rejected as untypable. The kinds are eighteen: fact, assertion, instruction, constraint, definition, goal, preference, question, hypothesis, decision, evidence, result, problem, risk, obligation, exception, law, and pattern. They are not arbitrary; each names a distinct thing a consumer does differently, and the whole value of the Type field is that the operation valid on one kind is invalid on another, so mistyping is not a cosmetic error but a category error that sends a consumer to do the wrong thing with confidence.

3.2 The assertoric kinds — fact, assertion, evidence, result. A fact is a claim about the world held true with high confidence and strong provenance — the disk holds a file, a test passed, a value equals a number — and its required fields are provenance and confidence above all, because a fact whose source cannot be named is not a fact but an assertion. An assertion is a claim advanced but not yet verified, distinguished from a fact by exactly the confidence and provenance it lacks. Evidence is an observation that raises or lowers the confidence of some other unit, required to name the unit it bears on. A result is the recorded output of a run — a measurement, a verdict, an exit code — distinguished from a fact by being tied to the execution that produced it. The signature failure across these four is the inference typed as a fact: an assertion promoted to fact without the verification a fact requires, which is the precise error the ACIS epistemic ladder exists to prevent, and the Type field defers to that ladder rather than re-deriving it — a unit may be typed fact only at the epistemic level ACIS assigns to a verified claim, and an inference riding at a lower level that calls itself a fact is convicted by the ladder, not by this dataset.

3.3 The directive kinds — instruction, constraint, law, obligation. An instruction is a directive to do a specific thing, scoped and usually soft, required to carry authority and scope so a consumer knows whose instruction it is and where it applies. A constraint is a directive forbidding a thing, and its critical required field is Strength, because a constraint's whole treatment turns on whether it is hard or soft. A law is a constraint of the highest authority and hardest strength — a hard_rules bug-stop is a law — distinguished from an ordinary constraint by its authority rung and its no-silent-override policy. An obligation is a directive to complete a thing, distinguished from an instruction by carrying a completion condition and a validity that tracks whether it is still owed, which is exactly the field DAIF-07 reads to know an obligation is open. The signature failure here is the constraint mistyped as a preference, stripping its Strength — a hard safety law read as a soft leaning, the failure Part VII traces in full — and the inverse, a preference mistyped as a law, which halts the estate on a leaning nobody was entitled to make binding.

3.4 The intentional kinds — goal, preference, decision, exception. A goal is a desired end state, soft by nature and required to name its scope so competing goals in different scopes do not appear to conflict. A preference is a soft leaning between options, distinguished from a goal by being about how rather than what, and always tradeable — a preference typed hard is a contradiction the schema rejects. A decision is a resolved choice among options, required to carry provenance pointing at the decision record that made it, because a decision without its record is unappealable and unauditable, the failure DAIF-00 Part XIV names. An exception is a decision to set aside another unit for a bounded time, required to carry an expiry and a reference to the unit it excepts, so that it is the recorded, dated amendment of DAIF-00 Part XV and not a silent override. The signature failure is the decision typed as a fact — a choice presented as an observation, so a consumer cannot appeal it because nothing marks it as a decision that could have gone otherwise.

3.5 The investigative kinds — question, hypothesis, problem, risk. A question is an open request for knowledge, required to name what would answer it, so a consumer knows when it is resolved. A hypothesis is a proposed answer not yet tested, distinguished from an assertion by carrying a falsifier — the observation that would refute it — which is the ACIS discipline the Type inherits, and a hypothesis without a falsifier is untypable as a hypothesis. A problem is a named gap between the current and a desired state, required to carry scope so a problem in one project is not read as universal. A risk is a possible future problem carrying a likelihood and an impact, distinguished from a problem by not having happened yet. The signature failure across these is the hypothesis typed as a result — a proposed answer recorded as if it were the measured output — which lets an untested guess enter downstream reasoning wearing the authority of a run that never happened, the founding-finding-as-hypothesis error the estate has recorded.

3.6 The structural kinds — definition, pattern. A definition fixes the meaning of a term, required to carry high authority within its scope because a definition is what other units reference to mean the same thing, and it composes GK-02's canonicalization directly — a definition is the canonical form that resolves a term's aliases to one identity. A pattern is a generalization over repeated instances, required to name the instances that justify it, distinguished from a law by describing what recurs rather than commanding what must be, and its confidence rises with the count of confirming instances the way the sibling constitution's rule of two versus three governs an extraction. The signature failure is the pattern typed as a law — a mere regularity elevated to a binding rule, so the estate enforces as mandatory a thing that was only ever observed to happen, converting a description into a command no authority issued.

3.7 The taxonomy as a dispatch table. The eighteen kinds are a dispatch table: a consumer reads a unit's Type and knows which operation to perform — enforce a constraint, test a hypothesis, weigh a preference, verify an assertion, track an obligation, resolve a question — and the whole discipline of the taxonomy is that these operations are not interchangeable. Enforcing a hypothesis, testing a constraint, or weighing a law is a category error, and the closed type set exists so that no unit reaches a consumer without a kind that names the right operation. Mistyping is therefore the taxonomy's central failure mode, and it is always the same shape: a unit is treated by the operation valid on the kind it was mistyped as, not the kind it is, and the consumer proceeds confidently to do the wrong thing. The taxonomy defends against this not by judgment at the point of use but by fixing the kind on the object at intake, where the author who knows what the unit is records it once, so that every downstream consumer inherits the right operation instead of re-inferring it from prose that never reliably encoded it.

PART III FINAL LAW. The Type field draws from a closed set of eighteen kinds — fact, assertion, instruction, constraint, definition, goal, preference, question, hypothesis, decision, evidence, result, problem, risk, obligation, exception, law, and pattern — each requiring the fields its kind depends on and each naming a distinct operation a consumer performs, so that mistyping is a category error that sends a consumer confidently to the wrong operation, and the sharpest guard is the one the estate already owns: an inference typed as a fact is convicted by the ACIS epistemic ladder, referenced here and never rebuilt.

PART IV — AUTHORITY TYPES

4.1 Mission. The Authority field names who is entitled to assert a unit or to override it, and this Part types that entitlement into a ranked ladder so that a conflict between two units resolves by rule rather than by whoever recorded last. Authority is the field that turns a claim into a governable thing: an untyped sentence carries no authority a machine can read, so a consumer cannot tell a constitutional law from an agent's passing note, and the two collide as equals exactly when they must not. Typing authority does not create the estate's power structure — that already exists in SQI-00's Q0–Q5 ladder, in the hard_rules precedence, and in DRK's limits on autonomous decision — it records that structure on the object, so a unit arrives already knowing its rung. This Part defines the ladder, the rules that refine it, the detection that stops a weak source from forging a strong rung, and the way the Override-policy field consumes an authority value to decide who may set the unit aside.

4.2 The authority ladder. Authority is ranked from the most general and durable commitment to the most local and revisable: constitutional authority outranks an owner directive, which outranks a project rule, which outranks a dataset rule, which outranks an agent instruction. The ordering is the same one DAIF-00 Part XVI states for verdict conflicts, inherited here as the ranking of the units those verdicts reason over, because a fabric cannot rank its decisions on one ladder and the knowledge behind them on another without the two drifting into the authority collision the estate convicts. Constitutional authority is the estate's sealed law — the reality-supremacy hierarchy, the hard_rules that each encode a real incident — and it sits at the top because a growth or convenience decision must never override a truth-or-safety one. An owner directive ranks below the constitution but above every ordinary rule, because the Owner is entitled to decisions the estate is not. Project, dataset, and agent rungs descend from there, each binding only within its reach, so a broad commitment is never overridden by a note scoped to one task.

4.3 The refinements within a level. The vertical ladder is refined by three cross-cutting rules, inherited from the estate's existing precedence law rather than invented here. Specific outranks general: within one rung, a unit written for exactly this case beats one written for cases like it, because the specific author saw this case and the general author only imagined it. Verified outranks speculative: a unit grounded in observed disk beats one grounded in a plan, the reality-supremacy SQI-00 enforces estate-wide, so a claim's authority is capped by the strength of its provenance — a high-rung unit resting on a weak, unverified source does not carry its nominal weight until the source is confirmed. Active outranks superseded: a current unit beats one a later decision retired, so a superseded authority cannot be resurfaced to win a conflict it already lost, which ties the Authority field to the Validity field of Part VI — a unit's rung is inert once its validity lapses. These three refinements are why authority is read together with provenance, confidence, and validity, never alone.

4.4 Composition with the estate's authority mechanisms. The Authority field composes three sealed systems and forks none. It composes SQI-00's Q0–Q5 authorization ladder, whose levels are the authorization semantics this field references — a unit's authority value maps onto a Q-level rather than a parallel scale, so the type system and the authorization system speak one vocabulary. It composes the hard_rules precedence, so that a unit typed as a law at constitutional authority is exactly a hard_rules bug-stop and inherits its kill-switch standing — when such a unit fires, it stops a lower-authority verdict cold, because the hard rule outranks any growth decision. And it composes DRK's authority limits, which bound how far an autonomous decision may reach: a unit asserted by an agent cannot claim an authority above the limit DRK sets for autonomous action, so the ceiling on self-issued authority is DRK's, referenced here and not re-derived. The type system's contribution is not a new power structure but a typed slot that records which existing authority a unit claims, so the estate's one authority law governs the knowledge as it governs the decisions.

4.5 Forged-authority detection. The field's sharpest adversarial case is the forged rung: a unit whose author records a higher authority than the source is entitled to, a preference dressed as a law, an agent note claiming project authority, an unverified assertion claiming the constitution's weight. Detection is mechanical and rests on provenance: an authority claim is valid only if its provenance names a source the ladder actually places at that rung, so a unit claiming constitutional authority whose provenance points at a single session is convicted, because a session cannot assert the constitution. The rule generalizes the estate's standing law that an untrusted source cannot self-elevate — a low-authority actor cannot promote its own unit to a higher rung by declaration, only a higher authority can grant the promotion, and the grant is itself a typed decision with its own provenance. This closes the elevation path the prompt-defense baseline names: a unit that tries to change its own rung is refused the way an untrusted input that tries to override project rules is refused, and the refusal is recorded so the forgery attempt is auditable rather than silent.

4.6 The typed authority object and Override-policy. Authority is not a bare label but a typed sub-object: it names the claiming party, the rung on the ladder, the scope within which the rung holds, and the provenance that entitles the claim, so a consumer reading it can both rank the unit and verify the rank. The Override-policy field consumes this authority object to answer the override question: it names the minimum authority required to set the unit aside, and a consumer proposing an override is checked against that minimum by comparing its own authority object to the policy's requirement. A soft unit's policy may permit override by an equal or higher rung under record; a hard unit's policy requires a strictly higher rung and often a specific granting authority, encoding the rule of Part VII that a low-authority actor can never override a high-authority hard constraint. Because Override-policy references Authority, the two fields are inseparable — an override-policy that named no authority requirement would be inert, permitting anyone to override anything, and an authority that fed no override-policy would rank units without governing how they yield. The typed authority object is thus the pivot on which both conflict resolution and override control turn.

4.7 Authority as the estate's coherence. The Authority field is what lets an estate of many rightful sources act as one, because it converts a crowd of claims — each true in its own scope, each asserted by a different party — into a hierarchy that resolves any collision by a rule set in advance. Without it, two units under pressure obey whoever recorded last, the volume-wins failure that guarantees the wrong claim prevails when the stakes are highest; with it, two units resolve by rung, refinement, and scope, in that order, exactly as DAIF-00 Part XVI resolves two verdicts. The field's discipline is that authority is always read with its qualifiers — capped by provenance, gated by validity, bounded by scope — so a nominal rung is never trusted beyond the evidence beneath it, and a forged rung is convicted at intake. An estate that types its authorities grows into a coherent whole; one that leaves authority in prose grows into feuding claims that collide at every override, and the type system's Authority field is the difference between the two.

PART IV FINAL LAW. The Authority field ranks every unit on the ladder the estate already runs — constitutional over owner over project over dataset over agent, refined by specific-over-general, verified-over-speculative, and active-over-superseded — composed from SQI-00's Q0–Q5, hard_rules precedence, and DRK's autonomy limits rather than forked, with a forged rung convicted mechanically because provenance must entitle the claim, and the typed authority object feeding Override-policy so that who may set a unit aside is decided by rank rather than by whoever holds the pen.

PART V — SCOPE TYPES AND MULTI-PROJECT ISOLATION

5.1 Mission. The Scope field names where a unit applies, and this Part types scope into a nested ladder so that a unit true in one place cannot silently govern another. Scope is the field that makes multi-project isolation mechanical: an untyped sentence carries no boundary, so a preference learned in one project leaks into every project a consumer reads it in, and a rule that should have died at a feature's edge governs the whole estate. Typing scope draws the boundary on the object, so a consumer assembling a context for one project admits only the units whose scope reaches that project and excludes the rest by rule rather than by memory. This is the isolation discipline the estate has long needed expressed on its own internal scopes — never on any external domain — and this Part defines the ladder, the resolution of an ambiguous scope, and the leak that is scope's signature failure.

5.2 The scope ladder. Scope nests from the broadest reach to the narrowest: universal, power-pack, organization, product, project, repo, feature, session, model, and user. Universal is the widest — a unit that holds everywhere the estate reasons, such as a reality-supremacy law. Power-pack scopes a unit to this framework's own conventions. Organization and product scope units to a body of work or a deliverable line above the individual project. Project and repo scope to a single effort and its worktree. Feature scopes below the project to one capability being built. Session scopes to a single working span and dies when it ends. Model scopes a unit to a particular model's behavior, and user scopes it to one person's preferences. The ladder is nested, so a broader scope contains the narrower ones within it, and a unit's reach is exactly its scope and every scope beneath it — a project-scoped unit governs its features, sessions, and repos, but a session-scoped unit governs nothing above the session.

5.3 How scope bounds applicability. Every other field applies only within the unit's scope, which is why scope resolution runs before authority ranking. A unit's authority, strength, and validity are real only inside its reach, so two units that appear to contradict do not conflict at all if their scopes do not overlap — a universal rule and a session rule that seem to disagree are reconciled by recognizing the session rule governs only within its session and the universal rule governs everywhere else, so the narrower resolves within the broader rather than against it. This is the mechanism that makes isolation automatic: a consumer assembling a reasoning context declares the context's scope, and admits a unit only if the context's scope falls within the unit's reach. A project-scoped preference is invisible to a different project's context because the second project does not fall within the first's scope, and a feature-scoped constraint vanishes when its feature is done because no later context falls within a completed feature. Scope is thus the field that lets the estate hold thousands of units without any of them governing where they were never meant to.

5.4 Scope resolution when the scope is ambiguous. A unit whose scope is unclear is not admitted at the broadest reach but resolved to the narrowest defensible one, because the fail-safe direction for scope is inward — under-reaching a unit at worst omits it from a context it should have joined, an error the author can correct by widening the scope, while over-reaching it contaminates every unrelated context, an error discovered only when the leak has already propagated. When a unit's provenance names a single project, its scope resolves to that project unless a higher authority explicitly widens it; when its provenance is a session, it resolves to that session. Widening a scope is itself a typed decision requiring authority proportional to the new reach — promoting a project unit to universal is a claim on the whole estate and requires an authority entitled to make it, exactly as promoting an authority rung does. Ambiguous scope therefore never defaults outward, and a unit that wants a broad reach must earn it through an explicit, authorized widening rather than acquire it through the silence of an unfilled field.

5.5 The isolation failure mode. Scope's signature failure is the leak: a narrow-scoped unit that comes to govern a broader context than its scope permits, and the most damaging instance is the project-scoped preference that reaches universal. The estate has recorded exactly this — a decrease-gate whose baseline was keyed to a session's own token burn failing when read against a different session, a preference learned on one project surfacing as advice on an unrelated one, a rule true for one substrate silently applied to another. Each is a unit whose scope was too broad, or absent and defaulted broad, so a consumer admitted it into a context it never belonged in. The leak is invisible while the contexts happen to agree and injurious the moment they diverge, the same deferred detonation the sibling constitution traces for a drifting duplicate, and it is paid by a later consumer who did not introduce it. The type system defends against the leak by the inward default of resolution and by scope-checking every admission, so a unit can enter a context only through the front door of an explicit scope match, never through the side door of an unfilled or over-broad field.

5.6 The absorbed isolation discipline on internal scopes. This Part absorbs the estate's cross-context isolation discipline and expresses it entirely on the Power Pack's own internal scopes — universal, power-pack, organization, product, project, repo, feature, session, model, user — and on no external domain, because the isolation to be enforced is between the estate's own projects and never between anything outside it. Two projects in the estate are isolated the way the estate isolates a frontier boundary: a unit scoped to one does not cross into the other, and a shared unit must be scoped to a common ancestor on the ladder — power-pack or universal — by an authority entitled to place it there. The discipline is that a unit is shared not by being copied into each project, which is the two-sources-of-truth drift the estate convicts, but by being scoped to the common ancestor once and read from there by every descendant, so a single universal-scoped law governs all projects without any project holding its own divergent copy. Scope thus doubles as the estate's single-sourcing mechanism for shared knowledge: reach is declared once at the right level, and every context beneath it inherits the unit without duplicating it.

5.7 Scope as the estate's map. The Scope field is the estate's map of where each unit lives, and reading it is how a consumer knows the territory a unit governs without guessing. An estate that types scope can hold knowledge at every level from the universal law to the one-session note and keep them from interfering, because each unit's reach is on the object and every admission is checked against it; an estate that leaves scope in prose cannot, because prose carries no boundary and every unit drifts to whatever reach its reader happens to give it. The field's strictness — the inward default, the authorized widening, the scope-check on every admission — is what makes the map trustworthy, so that a consumer assembling a project's context can rely on getting that project's units and only those, and the fabrics above can reason over a context they know to be isolated. Scope is the precondition for the estate scaling across many projects at once without any project's knowledge silently governing another's.

PART V FINAL LAW. The Scope field bounds a unit to a nested reach — universal, power-pack, organization, product, project, repo, feature, session, model, user — so that applicability is decided by whether a context falls within a unit's scope, resolved inward when ambiguous because over-reach contaminates while under-reach merely omits, shared by scoping once to a common ancestor rather than copying into each descendant, and defended against the project-to-universal leak by a scope-check on every admission, so an estate of many projects reasons over isolated contexts instead of a single pool where every unit governs where it was never meant to.

PART VI — VALIDITY AND TEMPORAL TYPES

6.1 Mission. The Validity field names whether a unit is still in force, and this Part types validity into a lifecycle of states and the temporal semantics that move a unit between them, so that a consumer can tell a current unit from a retired one by rule rather than by trusting that whatever is on disk is still true. Validity is the field that makes currency mechanical: an untyped sentence carries no expiry and no supersession, so a rule written a year ago reads exactly like one written today, and a consumer enforces a refuted constraint with the same confidence as a live one. Typing validity records the unit's lifecycle state on the object and anchors it in time, so a consumer admits a unit into live reasoning only if its validity is active, and treats a superseded, obsolete, expired, or refuted unit as the historical record it has become. This Part defines the states, the temporal fields that gate them, the reason an expired-but-present unit is worse than an absent one, and the way Validity guards the door to active reasoning.

6.2 The lifecycle states. A unit occupies one of six validity states. Active means in force and admissible to live reasoning — the default only after verification, never by omission. Superseded means a later unit has replaced it, and the unit carries a supersession pointer to its replacement, so a consumer that encounters the old one is routed to the new. Obsolete means the conditions that made the unit relevant no longer hold — the substrate it governed is gone — so it is neither in force nor replaced, simply no longer applicable. Expired means the unit had a declared lifespan that has elapsed, common to time-boxed exceptions and dated fallbacks, and an expired unit is surfaced for renewal or retirement rather than silently extended. Refuted means evidence has shown the unit false, the strongest retirement, and a refuted unit is retained as a record of what the estate once believed and no longer does. Pending-verification means the unit has been asserted but not yet confirmed, and it sits outside live reasoning until a verification promotes it to active or refutes it. The six are exhaustive, so every unit is always in exactly one known validity state.

6.3 The temporal semantics. Validity rests on three temporal fields. Effective-from names when the unit begins to hold, so a unit can be recorded before it takes force and a consumer reasoning about an earlier moment does not admit it. Expiry names when the unit ceases to hold on its own, the field that drives the expired state and the one whose absence turns a time-boxed exception into the permanent hole DAIF-00 Part XV convicts — an exception without an expiry does not stay an exception, it becomes the standing state of the law. Supersession pointers name the unit that replaced a superseded one, forming a lineage a consumer traverses from any old unit to the current one, so the estate never has to guess which of several versions is live. These three fields are why Validity references time in the reference web of Part II: a validity state without its temporal anchor is incomplete, a superseded unit with no pointer is a dead end, and an expired state with no expiry date cannot be verified. Temporal semantics turn validity from a static label into a lifecycle the estate can advance mechanically as time passes and evidence arrives.

6.4 Why an expired-but-present unit is worse than an absent one. The Part's central danger is the stale unit that is still present, because it is more dangerous than a unit that is simply missing. An absent unit produces a visible gap — a consumer finds nothing and knows it must seek or ask — and the failure is loud and recoverable. An expired-but-present unit produces the opposite: it is there, it reads as current, and a consumer that does not check its validity enforces it as if it still held, so a retired rule governs long after it should have died, a superseded threshold gates against a value nobody uses anymore, a refuted assertion feeds downstream reasoning as though it were true. The harm is silent because nothing in normal operation re-checks a unit that is present; the consumer that reads it trusts its presence, and presence without validity is exactly the false currency the field exists to prevent. This is the same reason the estate treats a stale marker as more dangerous than a missing one, and it is why Validity is not optional metadata but a gate: a present unit is not a current unit, and only the Validity field distinguishes the two.

6.5 How Validity gates active reasoning. Validity is the door to the estate's live reasoning context, and only an active unit passes through it. A consumer assembling a context for a task admits a unit only if its validity is active and its effective-from has arrived and its expiry has not, so a superseded unit is replaced by its successor at the door, an expired unit is held out for renewal, a refuted unit is excluded as false, and a pending-verification unit waits outside until it is confirmed. This gating is what keeps the active context true: without it, a context is the whole disk regardless of currency, and the whole disk includes every retired belief the estate ever recorded. The gate is also why the pending-verification default of Part II matters — an unverified unit does not slip into live reasoning by omission, because the door checks validity and pending-verification does not open it. Validity thus converts the estate's total record, which necessarily includes its history, into a live context that holds only what is current, which is the difference between reasoning with the estate's knowledge and reasoning with its archive.

6.6 The interaction with retention and the other fields. Validity distinguishes a unit that is out of force from a unit that is deleted, and the distinction is retention: a superseded, obsolete, expired, or refuted unit is retained, not erased, because it is the historical record something downstream reads — an audit, a lineage edge, a rationale that explains why the current unit exists. Erasing a retired unit to tidy the record destroys the estate's ability to reconstruct why it once believed otherwise, the temporal duplication the sibling constitution insists on keeping. Validity interacts with Authority — a superseded unit's authority is inert, so it cannot win a conflict it already lost — and with Confidence — a refuted unit's confidence has collapsed, recording not that the estate is unsure but that it is sure the unit is false. Reading Validity together with these fields is how a consumer treats a retired unit correctly: present for history, excluded from force, ranked below its successor, and retained because the record of a reversed belief is exactly the knowledge that keeps the estate from re-making the reversal.

6.7 Foreshadowing reality synchronization. Validity is the field that reality synchronization acts on, and this Part is where that later fabric's hook is placed. A unit's validity is not self-maintaining — a rule becomes obsolete when its substrate changes, an assertion becomes refuted when a new observation contradicts it, a compiled artifact becomes stale when its source moves — and something must detect the change and advance the unit's validity state accordingly. DAIF-21's reality synchronization is that something: it watches the disk, the specs, the compiled artifacts, and the obligations for the changes that invalidate a unit, and it flips validity from active to obsolete, superseded, or refuted when reality moves out from under a unit. The type system defines the states and the temporal fields; the synchronization fabric drives the transitions. This division is the same one the estate draws everywhere — the type system is the inert substrate, the acting fabric is the runtime — so Validity here is the schema DAIF-21 writes to, and the two compose along the seam rather than either owning both halves. A unit's validity is thus always a claim about the present that a later fabric keeps true as the present changes.

PART VI FINAL LAW. The Validity field types a unit's currency into six states — active, superseded, obsolete, expired, refuted, pending-verification — anchored by effective-from, expiry, and supersession pointers, gating the door to live reasoning so only an active unit is admitted while the retired ones are retained as the history something downstream reads, because an expired-but-present unit is worse than an absent one — it reads as current and is enforced long after it died — and the transitions between states are driven not here but by DAIF-21's reality synchronization, to which this field is the schema.

PART VII — STRENGTH AND OVERRIDE POLICY

7.1 Mission. The Strength field names whether a unit may ever be silently violated, and the Override-policy field names the rule by which it may be set aside; this Part types both, because a unit's whole treatment under conflict turns on them and their absence produces the most dangerous failure the estate knows. Strength answers one question — is this hard or soft — and Override-policy answers the follow-on — if it can yield, to whom, on what evidence, and with what record. Together they convert a bare directive into a governable one: a consumer facing two units in tension reads their strengths to know which may bend, and reads their override-policies to know whether a proposed override is even permitted. This Part defines the hard-soft distinction, the override-policy's fields, its interaction with the Authority field of Part IV, its reference to the appeal path of DAIF-00 Part XV, and the signature failure of an un-typed strength — a hard safety constraint treated as a soft preference because nothing on the object said it could not be.

7.2 Hard versus soft. A unit's Strength is hard or soft, and the two are treated in opposite ways under conflict. A hard unit may never be silently violated: a consumer that cannot honor it must halt and surface the conflict, not proceed by trading it away, because a hard unit is a wall and a wall that can be quietly passed through is not a wall. A soft unit may be traded off: a consumer weighing it against other units may set it aside when a stronger consideration wins, provided the trade is recorded. A hard_rules bug-stop is hard; a stylistic leaning is soft; a safety constraint, an authorization gate, a reality-supremacy law are all hard, and the estate's health depends on their hardness surviving into every consumer that reads them. Strength defaults to soft, as Part II establishes, because the recoverable error is to under-enforce a unit its author can promote, while the unrecoverable one is to let every casual preference halt the estate — but the default is a floor, and a unit whose author knows it is hard records it hard, because a hard unit that arrives typed soft is the failure this Part exists to prevent.

7.3 The Override-policy fields. Override-policy is a typed sub-object with three fields. Who names the minimum authority entitled to override the unit, expressed as a rung on the ladder of Part IV, so an override is checked by comparing the overriding party's authority to this minimum. Evidence names what the override requires beyond authority — a re-measurement, a named ground, an Owner directive — so that setting the unit aside is an evidenced act rather than a preference. Record names where the override must be logged, always a reference into the decision-record and appeal machinery, so that no override is silent. A soft unit's policy typically permits override by an equal-or-higher rung under a recorded trade-off; a hard unit's policy requires a strictly higher rung, specific evidence, and a durable record, and a hard safety law's policy names no-silent-override at all, meaning the unit yields only through a recorded, granted exception and never through a consumer's own judgment. The three fields make an override a governed transaction: authorized, evidenced, and recorded, or refused.

7.4 The interaction with Authority. Strength and Override-policy are inseparable from Authority, because the override question is fundamentally about rank. The governing rule is that a low-authority actor can never override a high-authority hard constraint: an agent-rung unit cannot set aside a constitutional hard law, no matter how confident the agent, because the override-policy of a constitutional hard unit requires an authority the agent does not have. This is the mechanism that keeps the estate's safety floor intact under pressure — a session under a deadline cannot type its way past a hard_rules bug-stop, because the bug-stop's override-policy names an authority the session lacks and the attempt is refused at the check. The interaction runs the other way too: a hard unit at a low authority yields more easily than a soft unit at a high one, so strength and authority are read together, never alone, and a consumer computes a unit's true immovability from both fields plus the confidence and provenance beneath them. An override that passes the authority check but fails the evidence or record requirement is still refused, because all three override-policy fields are conjunctive.

7.5 The appeal path. When a consumer believes a unit is wrong for a case but lacks the authority its override-policy requires, the resolution is not a silent override but a recorded appeal, and Override-policy references the appeal path DAIF-00 Part XV defines rather than re-deriving it. An appeal is a typed exception: it names the unit it contests, the ground — a specialization the unit misjudged, an Owner directive, a dated fallback — the evidence, the authority asked to grant it, and, mandatory, an expiry. The appeal is granted by the authority the ladder assigns to its ground and never by the appellant, exactly as Part XV requires, so a consumer cannot judge its own appeal. A granted appeal becomes an exception-typed unit of Part III, carrying its expiry and its reference to the unit it excepts, and it lives as a recorded amendment beside the unit it set aside rather than as a hole in it. This is how the type system admits real counter-cases without eroding its constraints: the strength holds, the override is refused, and the disagreement routes to an appeal that is visible, granted by a ranked authority, and dated — the constitution's discipline that a law is argued with on the record rather than evaded in silence.

7.6 The failure of un-typed strength. The signature failure this Part exists to prevent is the un-typed strength: a unit whose hardness is not recorded, so a consumer must infer it from phrasing and infers wrong. The worst instance is a hard safety constraint read as a soft preference — a constraint that must never be violated treated as a leaning that may be traded off — because the consumer, seeing no Strength field, weighs the safety constraint against convenience and trades it away, and the trade is silent because nothing marked the unit as un-tradeable. The estate has recorded this exact shape: a hard rule demoted in practice to advice because its bare prose did not carry the standing its author intended, and a gate treated as advisory because nothing on it said it blocked. The inverse failure — a soft preference read as a hard law — halts the estate on a leaning nobody was entitled to make binding, wasting the pressure valve on a unit that should have bent. Both come from the same absence, and the Strength field closes both by putting the hard-soft distinction on the object where a consumer reads it rather than guesses it.

7.7 Strength and Override-policy as the estate's spine of enforcement. Strength and Override-policy are what let the estate hold a hard floor and a soft body at once — a small set of inviolable constraints that never bend, surrounded by a large body of preferences that trade off freely — without a consumer ever confusing the two. The hard floor is what keeps the estate safe: the hard_rules, the reality-supremacy laws, the authorization gates, each typed hard with a no-silent-override policy, so that no consumer under any pressure can quietly pass through them, and any attempt routes to a recorded, ranked, dated appeal. The soft body is what keeps the estate flexible: the preferences and leanings that a consumer weighs and trades under record, so the estate is not paralyzed by treating every leaning as a wall. The two fields are the spine of enforcement because they encode, on every unit, the one distinction a consumer must never get wrong — what may bend and what may not — and they encode it mechanically, so the distinction survives the careless author, the pressured session, and the machine consumer that reads fields and not intentions. An estate that types strength enforces its floor and flexes its body by rule; an estate that leaves strength in prose enforces neither reliably, and the failure lands, as always, on the consumer least able to pay it.

PART VII FINAL LAW. The Strength field types a unit as hard, never to be silently violated, or soft, tradeable under record, and the Override-policy field names who may override it, on what evidence, and with what record — conjunctive, authority-gated, and referencing the appeal path of DAIF-00 Part XV — so that a low-authority actor can never override a high-authority hard constraint and a contested hard unit routes to a recorded, ranked, dated exception rather than a silent trade, closing the estate's most dangerous failure: a hard safety constraint read as a soft preference because nothing on the object said it could not bend.

PART VIII — CONFIDENCE AND EPISTEMIC TYPES

8.1 Mission. The Confidence field of Part II is not a mood the author records in passing but a typed measure of how much of the estate's weight a unit may bear, and this Part specifies it as a lattice of epistemic statuses and the discipline that keeps each status honest. The reason Confidence needs its own Part is that the seven other fields describe what a unit says, who set it, where it reaches, and whether it still holds, and none of them describes the one thing a consumer most needs before it acts on the unit: is this known, or merely believed. An untyped sentence hides that distinction absolutely — a verified measurement and a plausible guess read identically as prose, so a consumer either trusts both equally, acting on a guess as though it were a fact, or trusts neither, discarding a hard-won observation because nothing marked it as one. Confidence closes that gap by recording the epistemic status on the object, so a consumer reads not just the claim but the estate's warrant for it. This Part defines the status set, composes the ACIS epistemic ladder that grounds it, states the cardinal rule the type system mechanically enforces, and shows how Confidence reads against Authority and Validity so that a nominally strong unit is never trusted beyond the evidence beneath it.

8.2 The epistemic status set. Confidence draws from a closed set of ten statuses, ordered from the strongest warrant to the weakest and then across the failure edge into the refuted. Observed is the strongest: the estate has seen the thing directly on disk, in a run, in a rendered pixel — the reality SQI-00 holds supreme. Demonstrated is a claim shown true by an executed proof or a reproduced result, one step removed from raw observation but still grounded in a run. Verified is a claim an independent check has confirmed against its source, the status a fact requires before Part III permits the fact type. Inferred is a conclusion derived by valid reasoning from other units, sound only to the degree its premises are, and never itself an observation. Assumed is a premise taken as true without a check because the work must proceed, honest only when marked. Speculative is a possibility entertained but unsupported. Disputed is a unit two authorities disagree on, its confidence suspended pending resolution. Obsolete marks a unit whose warrant has lapsed with its relevance, refuted marks one evidence has shown false, and pending-verification marks one asserted but not yet checked. The ten are exhaustive, so every unit carries exactly one epistemic status, and a consumer reading it knows precisely what kind of warrant stands behind the claim.

8.3 Composing the ACIS epistemic ladder. Confidence does not invent its grading; it composes the ACIS epistemic ladder, whose levels E0 through E7 are the estate's sealed promotion chain from a raw deposit to a proven law, and it maps its statuses onto that ladder rather than deriving a parallel scale. A unit at ACIS's deposit level carries at most an assumed or speculative status; a unit that has been proven at the ladder's E3 gate may carry demonstrated; a unit promoted into UKDL at the higher rungs may carry verified; and the ladder's No-Autopromotion rule — which caps a deposit at E3 until a higher authority advances it — is exactly the ceiling Confidence inherits, so a unit cannot record a status stronger than the epistemic level ACIS assigns to its derivation. This composition is deliberate and load-bearing: the estate already runs one instrument that decides how firmly a claim is known, and the type system's Confidence field is the typed slot that carries that instrument's reading onto the object, not a second grader that could drift from the first. Where the ACIS ladder advances a unit, its Confidence status advances with it; where the ladder refuses promotion, the status stays capped, so the two speak one vocabulary and a unit's confidence is always the epistemic level ACIS already assigned it, referenced here and never rebuilt.

8.4 The cardinal rule. The type system exists to enforce mechanically one rule ACIS states as doctrine: an inference may never be typed as a fact. A fact is a claim at observed, demonstrated, or verified status; an inference is a claim at inferred status, sound only to the degree of its premises and never itself grounded in a run — and the difference between them is not stylistic but the difference between the estate knowing a thing and the estate having reasoned its way to a thing that may be wrong. When an author records a unit as a fact, the checker of Part XII reads its Confidence status and its provenance and refuses the fact type unless the status is observation-grade, so a claim that arrived by inference cannot wear the fact type no matter how the author phrased it. This is the typed form of the estate's own scar, the founding-finding-as-hypothesis error in which a number an effort was built on was treated as measured when it had only been assumed — and the guard is that the epistemic status, drawn from the ACIS ladder, caps what type a unit may claim. An inference that calls itself a fact is convicted not by judgment at the point of use but at intake, where the status and the type are checked against each other, so the estate's downstream reasoning never inherits a guess wearing the authority of an observation.

8.5 Confidence against Authority. Confidence and Authority are orthogonal, and the whole safety of the estate depends on a consumer reading them as two separate questions rather than collapsing them into one impression of strength. Authority names who is entitled to assert a unit; Confidence names how sure the estate is that the unit is true. The two do not move together: a constitutional authority can assert a unit the estate is not yet sure of, and a low-authority agent can observe a fact directly. The dangerous case is the high-authority unit at low confidence — a rule set by an entitled source but resting on an assumed or pending-verification status — because a consumer that reads only the Authority field treats it as immovable when it is merely entitled, and acts on a claim the estate has not confirmed. Part IV's own refinement anticipates this: a claim's authority is capped by the strength of its provenance, so verified outranks speculative within a rung, and a high-rung unit on a weak, unverified warrant does not carry its nominal weight until the warrant is confirmed. Confidence is therefore the qualifier that keeps Authority honest — a consumer computes a unit's true force from both fields together, and a nominally constitutional unit at speculative confidence enters reasoning as a flag to be resolved, never as a wall to be obeyed, exactly as Part II's reference web requires.

8.6 Confidence against Validity. Confidence and Validity are likewise distinct and likewise read together, because a unit can be current but unsure, or sure but retired, and a consumer that conflates the two mishandles both. Validity of Part VI names whether a unit is in force; Confidence names how well it is known. A unit at active validity may sit at pending-verification confidence — in force by default but not yet confirmed — and the estate's pending-verification default exists precisely so such a unit does not slip into live reasoning wearing a certainty it lacks. The two fields also share a boundary: refuted is both a validity state and a confidence status, and the overlap is intentional, because a unit evidence has shown false is simultaneously out of force and known-false, its confidence not merely low but collapsed to a certainty of the opposite. Reading the pair correctly is how a consumer treats the estate's history without re-inheriting its errors: a refuted unit is retained for lineage yet excluded from force, a superseded unit's confidence transfers to its successor, and an obsolete unit's warrant lapsed with its relevance rather than being disproven. Confidence and Validity are thus the two temporal and epistemic qualifiers that together answer whether a consumer may lean on a unit now, and neither alone suffices — a current claim may be unsure, and a sure claim may be dead.

8.7 Confidence as the estate's calibration. Confidence is what lets an estate hold both certainty and doubt without confusing them, and its discipline is calibration: the recorded status must match the actual warrant, so that observed means seen, assumed means unchecked, and neither is inflated to feel more finished. The metric the field answers to is calibration error — the gap between the confidence a body of units claims and the rate at which those units survive later verification — and a systematic overstatement, a register full of assumed claims typed as verified, is a defect the estate audits the way SQI-00 audits a silent decrease, because overstated confidence is the epistemic form of the scaffold illusion: a claim that reads as known and is only believed. The field's honesty is enforced by the same rule that governs the others — unknown is a value, not an absence, so a unit whose warrant the estate has not yet assessed records pending-verification explicitly rather than defaulting to a confidence it did not earn. An estate that types confidence knows the difference between what it has seen and what it has guessed, and can therefore act firmly on the first and cautiously on the second; an estate that leaves confidence in prose treats every claim as equally sure and is wrong, catastrophically, on exactly the guesses it should have doubted.

PART VIII FINAL LAW. The Confidence field types a unit's warrant into ten statuses — observed, demonstrated, verified, inferred, assumed, speculative, disputed, obsolete, refuted, pending-verification — mapped onto the ACIS epistemic ladder it composes rather than rebuilds, enforcing mechanically the one rule ACIS states as doctrine, that an inference may never be typed as a fact, and read always against Authority and Validity so that a high-authority unit at low confidence is a flag and not a wall, because an estate that cannot tell what it has seen from what it has guessed acts most confidently on exactly the claims it should have doubted.

PART IX — IDENTITY, ALIASES, NAMESPACES, AND THE SYMBOL TABLE

9.1 Mission. Every typed cognitive object refers to things — a project, an actor, an engine, a law, a metric, a prior decision — and a reference is worthless unless the name it uses resolves to exactly one thing the estate agrees on. This Part defines the canonical registry of names, the symbol table, and the resolution discipline that keeps one coordinate from fracturing into three labels and one label from fusing two coordinates. The problem is the shallowest kind of duplication the sibling constitution names and the most common false signal: a name is easy to record and easy to mis-handle, so an estate that does not govern its names accumulates synonyms no search reconciles and homonyms no reader disambiguates. An untyped sentence carries a name and no identity behind it, so a consumer reading two units that mention the same engine two ways cannot know they are the same, and a consumer reading two units that mention different concepts by one word cannot know they are different. The symbol table gives every named thing one canonical identity and resolves every reference to it, so that the estate's units, however many authors wrote them, name one world rather than a crowd of private vocabularies that only appear to agree.

9.2 What the symbol table registers. The symbol table is the estate's register of every named thing a unit can refer to, organized by kind: projects and products, actors and agents, engines and systems, concepts and definitions, datasets and their Parts, laws and hard rules, metrics and gates, acronyms, and recorded decisions. Each entry holds a canonical identity — a stable coordinate that does not change when a label does — together with the set of labels that resolve to it, the namespace it lives in, the scope within which the name is meaningful, and the provenance that first registered it. A metric is registered once with its canonical name and its definition, so every unit that cites it cites one thing; an engine is registered once with its coordinate, so a reference to it from any dataset lands on the same identity; a decision is registered with the record that made it, so a unit that depends on the decision can traverse to its rationale. The register is the single source of truth the sibling constitution demands for data: a name is held once, and every other site is a reference to it, never a second copy that can drift, because two recorded spellings of one identity are two identities the moment one is edited and the other is not.

9.3 Alias resolution. The first discipline the symbol table enforces is alias resolution: three labels for one coordinate must resolve to one identity. The estate has recorded exactly this hazard — a coordinate system that appeared as three different labels across three documents — and the resolution is canonicalization, not deletion, because each label is a legitimate way a real author named a real thing and the fix is to point all three at one canonical entry rather than to erase two of them. Alias resolution composes GK-02's semantic identity and canonicalization directly: GK-02 is the estate's sealed mechanism for recognizing that two surface forms denote one underlying thing, and the symbol table is the register its canonicalization writes to, so a unit that names an engine by a nickname, an abbreviation, or its full title is resolved through GK-02 to the one coordinate all three share. The resolution is recorded, so the mapping from label to identity is auditable and a later reader can see why a spelling resolved as it did. Without this discipline the estate fractures into synonyms: a search for the engine by one label misses every unit that named it by another, and the knowledge splits into disconnected pools that each believe they are complete while each holds only a fraction of what the estate knows.

9.4 Homonym separation. The mirror discipline is homonym separation: one label for two concepts must split into two identities. Where alias resolution fuses many labels onto one coordinate, homonym separation forces one overloaded label apart, because a name that denotes two things is a collision that a careless merge resolves by destroying a real distinction. The sibling constitution names this precisely — the homonym that names two concepts one way is resolved by renaming, not by merger — and the symbol table enforces it by refusing to let one label resolve to two canonical identities: when a label is found to denote two things, the table splits it, assigns each concept its own coordinate, and disambiguates the labels so a reference resolves unambiguously. The danger of skipping this is symmetric to the danger of skipping alias resolution: a builder who merges two systems because their names collide destroys the distinction the two names were carrying, and a consumer reading a unit that used the overloaded label cannot tell which concept the author meant. Homonym separation and alias resolution are the two halves of one canonicalization law — collapse the labels that mean one thing, split the label that means two — and together they guarantee that a name in a unit resolves to exactly one identity, never zero and never several.

9.5 Namespaces and scope. A name's meaning is bounded by its namespace, and its namespace is bounded by scope, so the symbol table reads the Scope field of Part V to know where a label is even eligible to resolve. A label that means one thing in a project namespace may mean another in a different project, and the two do not collide because each resolves only within its own namespace — the isolation discipline of Part V applied to names rather than to units. A name registered at universal scope is visible to every namespace beneath it, so a canonical metric defined once at the estate level resolves the same way in every project; a name registered at project scope resolves only within that project and its descendants, so a project-local alias never leaks into a sibling project's resolution. This is how the symbol table holds thousands of names without them interfering: each lives in a namespace whose reach is exactly its scope, and a reference is resolved by first bounding it to the resolving context's scope and then matching within it. A name shared across projects is registered once at a common ancestor namespace and read from there by every descendant, never copied into each, so the single-sourcing that Part V gives to units the symbol table gives to their names.

9.6 The failure of an unresolved alias. The signature failure of this Part is the unresolved alias: a label that never gets canonicalized, so the estate holds two entries for one identity and no search reconciles them. Its harm is the fracture the sibling constitution traces — the estate splits into synonyms, and every unit that named the identity by the un-registered label becomes invisible to every consumer that searched by the canonical one. The failure is silent while nothing depends on completeness and injurious the moment a consumer needs every unit about an identity and gets only the fraction that happened to use its label: a review that misses half the constraints on an engine because they were filed under a nickname, a lineage that dead-ends because a decision was referenced by an alias the traversal did not know, a conflict left unresolved because two units that contradict each other were never recognized as being about the same thing. Like every duplication of a fact, the unresolved alias is discovered late, by a consumer who did not introduce it, at the moment the missing half would have mattered most. The symbol table defends against it by resolving every reference through GK-02 at intake, so a label is canonicalized when the unit enters rather than left to fracture the register from within.

9.7 The symbol table as the estate's shared world. The symbol table is what lets an estate of many authors refer to one world rather than to many private vocabularies that only appear to agree. Every typed cognitive object depends on it, because a unit's Provenance names a source that must resolve to one identity, its Authority names a party that must resolve to one rung-holder, its Scope names a level that must resolve to one place on the ladder, and its references to other units must resolve to their coordinates — so the symbol table is the substrate beneath the substrate, the resolution layer the eight fields lean on whenever they name anything. An estate that governs its names can hold knowledge from thousands of sessions and have it cohere, because every reference lands on the identity its author meant; an estate that leaves names ungoverned holds the same knowledge as a heap of near-synonyms and false-twins that no consumer can reason over, because it can never be sure that two units naming a thing mean the same thing or that one unit naming two means either. Canonical identity is the precondition for every other discipline in this dataset, since a type, an authority, a scope, and a confidence are all claims about a named thing, and a claim about a thing whose name does not resolve is a claim about nothing.

PART IX FINAL LAW. The symbol table registers every named thing the estate refers to under one canonical identity, resolving many labels onto one coordinate and splitting one label off two concepts through the GK-02 canonicalization it composes, bounding each name's meaning by the namespace its scope defines, so that a reference in any unit lands on exactly one identity — because the alternative is the fracture into synonyms no search reconciles and homonyms no reader disambiguates, in which the estate's knowledge splits into private vocabularies that each believe they are complete while each holds only a fraction of the one world the units were meant to share.

PART X — TYPE COMPOSITION, INHERITANCE, AND HIERARCHY

10.1 Mission. The taxonomy of Part III types a unit as one kind, but real institutional knowledge rarely comes as a single pure kind — a constraint is often also a hard safety law, a decision carries its own evidence, a pattern hardens into a rule that governs its instances. This Part specifies how types combine: how a unit inherits the invariants of a more general type, how a compound unit is built from typed sub-units, and how the hierarchy of generality orders the whole. The reason composition needs its own law is that a combined unit's fields cannot be assigned freely — its authority, scope, and strength must derive from its constituents by defined rules, or a composition becomes a seam through which a constituent's strength silently leaks away. An untyped sentence that expresses a compound idea hides its structure entirely, so a consumer cannot see that a single paragraph is really a decision plus its evidence plus a constraint it imposes, and it treats the whole as one flat claim, mishandling every part. The composition law makes the structure explicit and mechanical, so that a compound unit is well-typed only when its derived fields are consistent with its parts, and the estate never builds a unit that is quietly weaker than the constituents it was assembled from.

10.2 Type inheritance. A specialized type inherits the invariants of its parent, so that everything true of the general kind remains true of the special one and the special one adds constraints rather than removing them. A law of Part III is a specialization of constraint — it is a constraint at the highest authority and hardest strength — and it inherits every invariant of constraint, chiefly that its Strength field is load-bearing, then adds its own: a no-silent-override policy and a constitutional authority rung. Because inheritance only tightens, a consumer that knows how to handle the parent kind can always handle the child kind safely, treating it at minimum as its parent and honoring its extra constraints on top; the child never permits an operation the parent forbade. This is the discipline that keeps the taxonomy's dispatch table sound under specialization: a repo-scoped review agent that specializes a universal review doctrine inherits the doctrine's invariants and adds one substrate's traps, exactly the legitimate specialization the sibling constitution's redundancy taxonomy preserves, and it is well-typed only if it strengthens the parent rather than restating it. An inheritance that loosened a parent's invariant would be a contradiction the checker of Part XII rejects, because a child that permits what its parent forbids is not a specialization but a different and weaker unit wearing the parent's name.

10.3 Type composition. Composition builds a compound unit from typed sub-units, each a well-formed typed cognitive object in its own right, wired together so the whole carries a structure a consumer can traverse. A decision that carries its own evidence is a composition: a decision-typed unit with an evidence-typed sub-unit attached, so a consumer that reads the decision can traverse to the evidence that grounds it without leaving the object — which is exactly the provenance a decision requires to be appealable under DAIF-00 Part XIV, made structural. An obligation that carries a completion condition and a verification result is a composition of an obligation-typed unit with a result-typed sub-unit, the structure DAIF-07 reads to know the obligation is discharged. The discipline of composition is that the sub-units keep their own types and fields — the evidence inside a decision is still evidence, with its own confidence and provenance — so the compound does not flatten its parts into an undifferentiated whole but preserves their typing for a consumer that needs to reason over one part alone. A composition is well-formed only when every sub-unit is well-formed and the wiring between them is itself typed, so that the relation between a decision and its evidence, or an obligation and its result, is a named edge a consumer can follow rather than an implication buried in prose.

10.4 The hierarchy of generality. The types and the units form a hierarchy of generality, from the universal law that governs everywhere down to the single session note that governs one span, and the hierarchy is the axis along which inheritance and scope align. A more general unit sits higher and reaches wider; a more specific unit sits lower and reaches narrower, and the specialization relation of Part X runs in parallel with the scope nesting of Part V, so that a specialized child is typically also a narrower-scoped child — a universal constraint specialized into a project constraint both inherits the parent's invariants and narrows the parent's scope. This alignment is what lets the estate reason about a unit's place with one mental model rather than two: a unit's generality, its scope, and its authority tend to move together, the broad commitments held high and wide at strong authority, the local specializations held low and narrow at weaker authority. The hierarchy is also the map the sibling constitution's precedence uses when it ranks specific over general within a rung — the specific unit is the lower, narrower, later-specialized one, and it wins for the case it was written for precisely because it saw that case while the general unit only imagined it. Reading the hierarchy is how a consumer knows, for two related units, which is the parent and which the sharpened child.

10.5 The derived-fields invariant. The central invariant of composition is that a compound unit's authority, scope, and strength are not asserted independently but derived from its constituents by defined rules, so the whole can never claim more than its parts entitle it to. A composition's authority is the highest authority among its constituents, because a compound is at least as authoritative as its strongest part but no consumer may assume it exceeds that. A composition's scope is the intersection of its constituents' scopes, because the compound applies only where all its parts apply — a unit built from a project-scoped constituent and a feature-scoped constituent reaches only the feature, never the whole project. And a composition's strength is the maximum hardness among its constituents, because a compound that contains one hard sub-unit is itself hard: if any part may never be silently violated, the whole may never be silently violated, and a consumer that traded the compound away would trade away the hard part inside it. These derivation rules are the mechanism that makes composition safe — the compound's fields are computed from its parts and checked for consistency at intake, so an author cannot assemble a unit whose declared strength or authority contradicts the constituents it was built from.

10.6 The failure of a silently weakened constituent. The signature failure of composition is the compound that silently weakens a constituent's strength: a hard sub-unit folded into a compound whose derived strength is recorded soft, so a consumer weighing the compound trades it away and, with it, the hard constituent that should never have bent. This is the exact inverse of the strength derivation rule, and it is the composition-layer form of the most dangerous failure Part VII names — a hard safety constraint treated as a soft preference — reached not by mistyping the constraint directly but by burying it inside a compound whose strength was set as if the constraint were not there. The estate has recorded the general shape of this hazard: a light merge that erases a material difference, a compression that discards an exception, a strength that does not survive into the consumer that reads it. A composition that takes the maximum-hardness of its parts is immune, because the hard constituent forces the whole hard and the compound cannot be traded without the trade being refused; a composition that sets its strength by the author's summary rather than by derivation re-admits the leak. The checker of Part XII therefore recomputes a compound's strength from its constituents and rejects any compound whose declared strength is weaker than its hardest part, so a hard unit cannot be neutralized by being made a component of a soft-declared whole.

10.7 Composition as the estate's structure. Type composition and inheritance are what let the estate represent real institutional knowledge, which is compound almost everywhere — decisions carry evidence, obligations carry conditions, constraints carry authorities, patterns harden into laws — without flattening it into the bare prose that hides its structure. A consumer reading a well-composed unit can reason over the whole or descend into any part, follow the edge from a decision to its evidence or from an obligation to its result, and trust that the compound's authority, scope, and strength are the honest derivation of its constituents rather than an author's optimistic summary. This is the interoperability the downstream fabrics require at the level of structure rather than the single field: DAIF-02 builds representations whose slots are themselves composed of typed sub-units, DAIF-04 contracts on obligations that compose a condition with a verification, and DAIF-07 tracks obligations by reading their composed structure. An estate that types composition can build arbitrarily rich units and still reason over them mechanically, because every compound's fields are derived and checked; an estate that composes by prose builds units whose structure only a human reader can see and whose strength leaks at every seam a careless assembly leaves open. The derivation rules are the guarantee that richness never costs safety — a compound is exactly as strong, as authoritative, and as wide as its parts, no more claimed and no less honored.

PART X FINAL LAW. Types combine by inheritance, which only tightens a parent's invariants so a specialized child never permits what its parent forbade, and by composition, which builds a compound from typed sub-units whose types and fields survive into the whole, with the compound's authority, scope, and strength derived by rule — highest authority, intersected scope, maximum hardness — so that the estate's genuinely compound knowledge is represented richly and reasoned over mechanically, and the signature failure is foreclosed: a hard constituent can never be silently weakened by being buried in a soft-declared whole, because the checker recomputes the compound's strength from its hardest part and refuses any assembly that would trade the hard part away.

PART XI — THE SCHEMA REGISTRY

11.1 Mission and absorption. The typed cognitive object has a schema, the reasoning representations of DAIF-02 have schemas, the institutional contracts of DAIF-04 have schemas, and the events every fabric emits have schemas — and unless those schemas themselves have one governed home, the estate holds as many private notions of an object's shape as it has producers, and two producers that disagree on shape each believe they are right. This Part is that home: the schema registry, the register of every object schema, representation schema, contract schema, and event schema the estate uses, with the discovery, identity, versioning, ownership, validation, compatibility, migration, and deprecation disciplines that keep them coherent. It absorbs what a separate effort once proposed as its own schema-registry system: the capability lives here, as a Part of the type system, not as a sovereign sibling, because a register of schemas is inseparable from the type system whose schemas it holds, and forking it into its own system would be the mechanism duplication the sibling constitution routes to shared-infrastructure extraction. Stated plainly by that constitution's own discipline, the schema-registry capability is extracted into the type system that already owns the object model rather than spawned beside it, so the estate has one place where a schema is defined, versioned, and evolved, and every producer reads its shape from there.

11.2 Discovery and identity. A schema is useless if a producer cannot find it, so the registry's first discipline is discovery: every schema is registered under a canonical identity in the symbol table of Part IX, so a producer that needs the shape of a typed cognitive object, a reasoning representation, or a contract resolves its name to one schema entry rather than guessing at the shape or inventing a private copy. A schema's identity is stable across its versions — the schema for the object model is one identity whether it is at its first revision or its tenth — and the versions hang off that identity as a lineage, so a reference to a schema resolves to the identity and then selects a version, never to a floating spelling that could denote any of several shapes. This is the same single-sourcing the symbol table gives to every name: a schema is held once under one identity, and every producer and consumer references it, so the shape of an object is defined in one authoritative place and cannot fork into two producers' divergent notions. Discovery through the register and identity through the symbol table together mean that when a producer builds a unit, it builds it against the one registered schema for that unit's kind, and when a consumer reads it, it validates against the same one — the precondition for two systems agreeing on shape at all.

11.3 Versioning and ownership. Every schema carries a version, and every schema has an owner — an authority of Part IV entitled to change it — because a schema change is not a free edit but a governed act that ripples into every producer and consumer of the shape. Versioning is the mechanism that lets the shape evolve without silently breaking the units built against an earlier shape: a unit records the schema version it was written against, so a consumer knows which shape to validate it by, and a shape that changed under a unit does not retroactively make the unit malformed. Ownership binds the change to an entitled authority, so a schema at a high authority — the object model's own schema is a universal-scoped, high-authority schema — can be changed only by a party the ladder places at that rung, and a low-authority producer cannot alter the shape every other producer depends on. The two disciplines compose: a versioned schema owned by an entitled authority is one whose evolution is both traceable and governed, so the estate can answer, for any unit, which shape it obeys and who last changed that shape and by what right. Without versioning a schema change is a silent redefinition of every unit built against the old shape; without ownership it is a redefinition any producer could impose on all the others.

11.4 Validation and compatibility. The registry's schemas are the contract the type checker of Part XII validates against: a unit is well-typed only if it conforms to the registered schema for its kind at the version it declares, so the schema is not documentation but the executable shape a producer's output is checked by. Compatibility is the discipline that governs whether a new schema version can validate units built against an older one, and it types a change as backward-compatible, forward-compatible, or breaking. A backward-compatible change — a new optional field, a widened range — lets the new schema validate old units, so consumers can move to it without waiting for producers. A breaking change — a removed field, a narrowed range, a field made required — cannot validate old units, and the registry marks it breaking so no consumer silently rejects units that were valid when written. The estate's own scar names the failure this prevents: two producers that disagree on shape, neither wrong, because each built against a different notion of the schema — and compatibility typing is what turns that latent disagreement into an explicit, governed transition rather than a silent divergence discovered when one producer's output fails another's validation for reasons neither can see.

11.5 Migration and deprecation. When a schema must change in a breaking way, the register does not simply replace the old shape and leave every unit built against it malformed; it defines a migration — a typed transformation that carries a unit from the old shape to the new — and a deprecation that retires the old version on a stated schedule rather than deleting it out from under its units. Migration is the schema-level form of supersession: the old version is superseded by the new, carries a supersession pointer to it, and a migration transform lets a producer advance its units across the boundary. Deprecation gives the old version an expiry, the temporal field of Part VI, so a schema version that is being retired is surfaced for migration rather than silently extended past its intended life, and units still built against it are flagged for the transition before the version lapses. The old version is retained through the migration window for exactly the reason Part XIV retains a superseded unit — the lineage and the migration both read it — so the register never destroys a shape that units still obey. Migration and deprecation are what make a schema evolvable in practice: without them a breaking change is either impossible, freezing the shape forever, or catastrophic, invalidating every unit built against the old shape at once with no path across.

11.6 The failure of an unversioned schema. The signature failure of this Part is the unversioned schema: a shape used by producers and consumers but carrying no version, so when it changes there is no way to tell which shape a given unit obeys. Its harm is the estate's own recorded scar — two producers disagree on the shape of an object and neither is wrong, because each built against the schema as it stood at a different moment and nothing on either producer's output records which moment that was. The failure is silent while the shape happens not to change and injurious the instant it does: a consumer validating a unit against the current shape rejects a unit that was valid when written, or worse, accepts it while misreading a field whose meaning the change moved, and the divergence surfaces as a shape mismatch that no producer can explain because none can see which schema the other used. Like every duplication of a fact, the unversioned schema defers its cost to the edit and pays it at the moment a builder believes a change is finished. The register forecloses it by refusing to hold an unversioned schema at all: every schema carries a version, every unit records the version it was built against, and a shape change is a new version with a compatibility type and a migration, never a silent redefinition of the one shape everyone assumed was fixed.

11.7 The schema change as a typed event. The deepest discipline of the register is that a schema change is itself a typed cognitive object — an event with a type, an owning authority, a version, a compatibility classification, a migration, and a provenance — so the estate reasons about its own shapes with the same machinery it reasons about everything else. A schema change is not an edit that happens outside the type system but a decision-typed unit inside it, subject to the authority ranking of Part IV, the validity lifecycle of Part VI, and the conflict handling of Part XIII, so two proposed schema changes that disagree resolve by the same precedence ladder as any other conflict and a schema change made without the owning authority is refused as a forged-authority claim. This reflexivity is what makes the register trustworthy: the shapes the estate depends on are not governed by a mechanism outside the estate's own laws but by the estate's own laws applied to themselves, so a schema audit — the register's periodic check that every registered schema is versioned, owned, validated, and free of breaking drift — is itself a check the type system runs on its own substrate. The schema registry is thus the type system turned upon itself, holding the shapes of the objects it types under the same discipline it imposes on the objects, so that the estate's notion of shape is as governed, as versioned, and as single-sourced as its notion of any fact.

PART XI FINAL LAW. The schema registry holds every object, representation, contract, and event schema the estate uses — absorbing the schema-registry capability into the type system rather than forking it into a sovereign sibling — under one canonical identity, one owning authority, and one version lineage, validated by the type checker, evolved through compatibility-typed changes with migrations and dated deprecations that retain the old shape until its units have crossed, and governed reflexively because a schema change is itself a typed, authority-ranked, versioned event, so the unversioned-schema failure is foreclosed at the root: two producers can never disagree on a shape while each is silently right, because there is exactly one registered, versioned shape and every unit records which one it obeys.

PART XII — TYPE VALIDATION AND THE TYPE CHECKER

12.1 Mission. A schema that is never enforced is a suggestion, and a typed cognitive object that no one checks is prose with fields bolted on, so this Part specifies the mechanism that verifies a unit is well-typed before it enters any reasoning context: the type checker. Its job is to be the gate between an author's claim and the estate's active knowledge, admitting only units that carry every required field, hold every invariant, name a legitimate authority, and resolve a real scope, and quarantining every unit it cannot type rather than letting it pass with a guessed shape. The checker is where the whole dataset's discipline becomes operative, because a type system that defines eight fields and eighteen kinds and a schema register but never checks a unit against them has abolished nothing — the bare prose the type system exists to end simply re-enters wearing fields the checker never verified. The checker's contract is fail-visible: an un-typeable unit is not silently admitted and not silently dropped but quarantined with a stated reason, so the estate knows exactly which units it declined to reason over and why. This Part defines what the checker requires, what it rejects outright, what it flags for review, and the reality-supremacy posture it inherits from SQI-00 — that a passing check is a hypothesis until reconciled with the source, never a proof that the unit is true.

12.2 What the checker requires. The checker admits a unit only when all eight fields of Part II are present with legal values or an explicit unknown, and the distinction between unknown and absent is the checker's first and most important discrimination. An absent field is a defect: the checker cannot tell whether the value was meant to be unknown, was defaulted, or was forgotten, so a unit missing a field is rejected, because admitting it would re-open the guessing the type system was built to close. An unknown field is a valid, typed state the checker admits: the estate does not yet know this unit's confidence, or its provenance is under investigation, and a consumer reading unknown knows exactly how to treat it, usually by declining to act until it resolves. This is the estate's recorded distinction between a zero and a null, between a decrease-gate that reads an unrecognized idiom as zero and one that reads it as uncountable — treating absent as a value is how whole classes of unit go silently mishandled, and the checker's refusal to conflate the two is the enforcement of Part II's inviolable line. Beyond presence, the checker requires that the defaults have been applied where the author left a field to the schema, so a unit arrives with its authority defaulted low, its scope defaulted narrow, its validity defaulted pending, and its strength defaulted soft, never with those fields blank.

12.3 What the checker rejects. The checker rejects a unit outright when a defect makes it un-typeable, and the rejection classes are precise. It rejects a unit with an absent required field, because presence is the precondition for every other check. It rejects a value out of its allowed range — a Type not in the closed set of eighteen, an Authority naming a rung the ladder does not hold, a Validity state outside the six, a Confidence status outside the ten — because a value the schema does not define is a value no consumer can handle. It rejects a unit that violates an invariant of the reference web: an Override-policy that cites an authority level that does not exist, a superseded Validity with no supersession pointer, a composition whose declared strength is weaker than its hardest constituent, an inference typed as a fact. And it rejects a forged authority — a unit whose recorded rung its provenance does not entitle — exactly as Part IV requires, convicting a preference dressed as a law at intake rather than at the contested override. Each rejection carries its reason, so an author sees which field or invariant failed and can correct it, and the rejected unit is quarantined rather than admitted with the defect papered over, because a unit that fails a check is a unit the estate cannot safely reason with regardless of how finished it looks.

12.4 What the checker flags. Between outright rejection and clean admission sits a third outcome: the flag, for units that are well-formed but carry a property the estate should see before it leans on them. A unit at pending-verification confidence is admitted but flagged, because it is not yet confirmed and a consumer should treat it as provisional rather than as fact. A unit at a high authority with a weak provenance is flagged, because its nominal rung outruns the warrant beneath it and Part VIII's rule caps its force until the provenance is confirmed. A unit whose scope resolved to the broadest reach by an authorized widening is flagged, because a claim on the whole estate deserves visibility. A disputed unit is flagged, because two authorities disagree on it and a consumer must know the disagreement is live. The flag is not a rejection — the unit is well-typed and may enter reasoning — but it is a visible mark that travels with the unit, so a consumer reads not only that the unit passed but what the checker noticed about it. Flagging is how the checker enforces the estate's honesty without over-blocking: a provisional or weakly-warranted unit is admitted with its provisionality exposed, rather than either refused for a defect it does not have or admitted with a certainty it has not earned.

12.5 The fail-visible contract. The checker's central contract is that an un-typeable unit is quarantined, never silently admitted and never silently dropped, because both silences are failures the estate has convicted elsewhere. A silently admitted un-typeable unit is the bare prose the type system exists to end, re-entering the active context with fields the checker never verified, so a consumer reasons over a unit whose shape was guessed. A silently dropped unit is the invisible gap — a unit the checker refused and no one was told about — so the estate believes it holds knowledge it actually rejected, and the absence is discovered only when the missing unit would have mattered. Quarantine is the third path that is neither silence: the un-typeable unit is held out of the active context, recorded with the reason it failed, and surfaced so an author can repair it or an authority can decide its fate. This is the same fail-visible posture the estate demands of every gate — a denial is a stated stop, not a puzzle to route around and not a silent no-op — and it is why the checker's every rejection and every quarantine carries a reason. An estate whose checker fails visibly can trust that its active context holds exactly the units that passed and knows exactly which units did not; an estate whose checker fails silently trusts a context that may hold unverified units and may be missing verified ones, and cannot tell which.

12.6 Reality supremacy over the green check. The checker inherits SQI-00's reality-supremacy posture, and the posture is a warning about the checker's own limits: a passing check is a hypothesis that the unit is well-formed, never a proof that the unit is true. The checker verifies shape, not truth — it confirms that a fact-typed unit carries observation-grade confidence and a provenance, but it cannot confirm that the observation actually happened, only that the unit claims it did with the right shape. So a unit can pass every field check, hold every invariant, and still be false, because its provenance names an observation that was misread or a run that did not measure what it claimed. SQI-00's law is that the green check is a hypothesis until reconciled with the source, and the checker obeys it by never treating its own pass as the last word: a passed unit at observed confidence is still subject to the reality synchronization of DAIF-21, which watches the source and can refute the unit if the disk moves out from under it. The checker is thus the necessary first gate and never the sufficient last one — it keeps malformed units out, but keeping well-formed falsehoods out is the reconciliation layer's job, and a consumer that treats a passed check as proof of truth has mistaken the shape of a claim for its correctness, the exact error reality supremacy exists to prevent.

12.7 The checker as the estate's intake gate. The type checker is the single door through which every unit passes from an author's claim into the estate's active reasoning, and its position is what makes the whole type system enforceable rather than aspirational. Every discipline the earlier Parts define — the eight fields, the closed kinds, the ranked authorities, the nested scopes, the validity lifecycle, the strength distinction, the confidence lattice, the canonical identities, the composition derivations, the registered schemas — is checked here, at intake, once, by the author who knows what the unit is, so that every downstream consumer inherits a verified unit rather than re-checking or re-guessing. This is the estate's own doctrine that a static pass proves shape and not runtime behavior, and that integration is tested before units are trusted, applied to knowledge: the checker proves a unit is well-typed, the reconciliation layer proves it is true, and a consumer trusts the pair rather than either alone. An estate with a fail-visible checker at its intake can reason over its active context with confidence that every unit in it is well-typed and every unit that is not is visibly quarantined; an estate without one holds a context of mixed verified and unverified shapes it cannot tell apart, which is precisely the untyped condition this dataset was written to abolish, re-created one unchecked admission at a time.

PART XII FINAL LAW. The type checker is the fail-visible intake gate that admits a unit only when all eight fields are present with legal values or an explicit unknown, every invariant of the reference web holds, the authority is entitled by its provenance, and the scope resolves — rejecting the un-typeable with a stated reason, flagging the well-formed-but-provisional so its weakness travels with it, and quarantining rather than silently admitting or dropping — while inheriting SQI-00's reality supremacy so that a passing check is only ever the hypothesis that a unit is well-shaped, never the proof that it is true, which remains the reconciliation layer's burden and never the checker's.

PART XIII — CONFLICT TYPES AND THE TYPED CONFLICT OBJECT

13.1 Mission. Two well-typed units can disagree, and the worst thing the estate can do with a disagreement is hide it — pick one silently, harmonize the two into a smooth summary that reads as if they never conflicted, and let the buried contradiction detonate later at the contested gate. This Part specifies the alternative: when two units disagree, the system produces a typed conflict object that names both claims, their scopes, authorities, evidence, and recency, the possible resolution, and the consequence of leaving it unresolved — rather than choosing between them and erasing the fact that a choice was made. The conflict object is the estate's refusal to launder a disagreement into false agreement, and it exists because the sibling constitution's most corrosive harmful kind is exactly the contradiction that surfaces at the worst possible moment, paid by whoever is least able to pay it. An untyped estate has no way to represent a disagreement as a first-class thing, so its only options are the two failures: obey the unit recorded last, the volume-wins failure that guarantees the wrong claim prevails under pressure, or smooth the two into a summary that hides that they ever differed. The typed conflict object is the third path, and this Part defines its fields, its kinds, how it feeds the precedence ladder of DAIF-00 Part XVI, and the failure of the silently harmonized contradiction it forecloses.

13.2 The kinds of conflict. Conflicts type into four kinds, each a distinct relation between two units. A contradiction is two units whose claims cannot both be true — one asserts a value the other denies, one permits what the other forbids — and it is the sharpest kind because there is no scope or time in which both hold. An authority collision is two units that each claim the right to decide one question, the jurisdictional dispute of the sibling ontology, legitimate when the scopes are genuinely separate and harmful only when they are contested. A scope overlap is two units whose reaches intersect and whose claims disagree within the intersection, so they conflict only where their scopes meet and are reconciled everywhere else — the mechanism Part V uses to dissolve apparent contradictions between a universal rule and a session rule. A temporal conflict is two units that disagree because one is current and one is retired, resolved by recency and the validity lifecycle of Part VI rather than by ranking their content. Typing the conflict is the precondition for resolving it, because the correct resolution of a contradiction is not the correct resolution of an authority collision, and treating them alike either forces a merger that destroys a real jurisdictional boundary or ranks two claims that a scope check would have shown never to conflict at all.

13.3 The typed conflict object. When the estate detects a disagreement, it constructs a typed conflict object, a first-class typed cognitive object whose fields capture the whole disagreement so a consumer or an authority can resolve it on the evidence rather than on whichever claim it happened to read first. The object names claim A and claim B in full; the scope of each, so a consumer can check whether they actually overlap; the authority of each, so they can be ranked; the evidence and confidence beneath each, so the better-warranted claim can be seen; the recency of each, so a retired claim is not weighed against a current one as an equal; the possible resolution the kind of conflict suggests; and — the field the estate most needs — the consequence of leaving the conflict unresolved, so the cost of inaction is visible rather than deferred. The conflict object is not a resolution; it is the honest representation of a disagreement that a resolution can then act on. Its whole value is that it makes the disagreement a thing the estate holds and can reason over, rather than a fact that lives only in the gap between two units that no consumer reading either one alone can see. A disagreement represented as a typed object is a disagreement the estate can resolve deliberately; a disagreement that exists only implicitly between two units is one it resolves by accident, under pressure, in favor of whoever recorded last.

13.4 How the conflict object is built. The conflict object is produced mechanically, because a disagreement the estate depends on a human to notice is a disagreement it will miss exactly when it is buried. Two units are candidates for conflict when they share a subject — resolved through the symbol table of Part IX, so that a disagreement between two units about one identity is detected even when they name it by different labels, and the unresolved-alias failure that would hide the conflict is foreclosed. Once two units share a subject, the checker of Part XII has already confirmed each is well-typed, so the conflict builder can read their fields directly: it intersects their scopes to see whether they overlap, compares their authorities and recency, and classifies the disagreement into one of the four kinds. A contradiction within an overlapping scope becomes a conflict object; a disagreement between non-overlapping scopes is not a conflict at all and is dissolved rather than recorded, because the two units govern different territories. This is why scope resolution runs before conflict construction — most apparent conflicts are scope illusions, two units that seem to disagree only because a reader forgot they reach different places, and building a conflict object for a scope illusion floods the estate with false disagreements that bury the real ones.

13.5 Feeding the precedence ladder. The typed conflict object is the input to DAIF-00 Part XVI's precedence ladder, which is the estate's sealed law for resolving two claims that genuinely disagree, and this Part hands the ladder a structured disagreement to rule on rather than making the ladder infer one from prose. The ladder ranks by authority first — constitutional over owner over project over dataset over agent — then by the refinements the conflict object exposes: specific over general, verified over speculative, active over superseded. Because the conflict object carries each claim's authority, confidence, and recency as fields, the ladder can apply its rules mechanically: it reads the two authorities and ranks them, reads the two confidences and prefers the verified, reads the two recencies and prefers the active. The resolution the ladder produces is itself recorded — as an exception-typed unit under Part XV's appeal path when a lower-authority claim is set aside, or as a supersession under Part XIV when a current claim retires an older one — so the resolution of a conflict is never silent but leaves a typed trace a later consumer can audit. The conflict object and the precedence ladder are thus two halves of one mechanism: this dataset produces the typed disagreement, the sibling constitution ranks it, and the estate resolves the disagreement by a rule set in advance rather than by whoever holds the pen at the contested moment.

13.6 Foreshadowing DAIF-21. The typed conflict object is where a later fabric's hook is placed, because a conflict is not only a thing to resolve once but a signal that the estate's knowledge has diverged from itself or from reality, and something must watch for that divergence continuously. A contradiction between two units may mean one is simply wrong, or it may mean reality moved and one unit has not caught up — a constraint that contradicts a newer observation is a unit the disk has refuted, not a peer to be ranked against the observation. DAIF-21's reality synchronization is the fabric that reads conflict objects as evidence that a unit's validity should change: when a unit conflicts with a fresh observation at observed confidence, the synchronization layer does not rank the two as equals but flips the older unit's validity toward refuted, because reality outranks a claim that disagrees with it. The type system produces the conflict object; the synchronization fabric decides, for the subset of conflicts that are really reality catching up with a stale unit, that the resolution is a validity transition rather than a precedence ranking. This division is the estate's standing seam — the type system is the inert substrate that represents the conflict, the acting fabric is the runtime that resolves the reality-driven ones — and it is why the conflict object carries confidence and recency fields the synchronization layer reads to tell a genuine peer-disagreement from a unit that reality has simply overtaken.

13.7 The failure of the silently harmonized contradiction. The signature failure this Part exists to prevent is the silently harmonized contradiction: two units that disagree, smoothed into a summary that reads as if they agreed, so the disagreement is hidden rather than resolved. Its harm is precisely the sibling constitution's most corrosive pattern — a real disagreement buried by a smooth surface, dormant while nothing forces the two claims to be compared and injurious the instant something does, at the contested gate where the estate discovers it has been treating a contradiction as a consensus. The smoothing is seductive because it feels like synthesis: a summary that reconciles two views reads as more finished than one that reports they conflict, and an author under pressure to produce a clean answer will harmonize rather than expose. But a harmonized contradiction is not a resolution; it is a resolution's absence disguised as its presence, and the disguise is worse than the disagreement, because a visible conflict routes to the precedence ladder while a hidden one routes to whoever later hits the gap. The conflict object forecloses the failure by making the disagreement a first-class typed thing the estate cannot summarize away: two units that share a subject and disagree within an overlapping scope produce a conflict object at detection, and the object stands until an authorized resolution retires it, so the estate never mistakes a smoothed summary for the resolution of a contradiction it merely hid.

PART XIII FINAL LAW. When two well-typed units disagree, the estate produces a typed conflict object — naming both claims, their scopes, authorities, evidence, recency, possible resolution, and the consequence of leaving it unresolved — classified as contradiction, authority collision, scope overlap, or temporal conflict, built mechanically after scope resolution dissolves the false disagreements, fed to DAIF-00 Part XVI's precedence ladder for a ranked resolution and to DAIF-21 for the conflicts that are really reality overtaking a stale unit, so that the estate's most corrosive failure is foreclosed: a real disagreement can never be laundered into false agreement by a smooth summary, because the conflict is a first-class object that stands until an authorized resolution retires it.

PART XIV — REVOCATION AND SUPERSESSION SEMANTICS

14.1 Mission. A unit that was true and is no longer must leave active force, and the whole discipline of this Part is that it must leave force without being destroyed, because the estate's knowledge compounds only if it can reconstruct why it once believed what it has since abandoned. This Part specifies the three ways a unit exits the active context — revocation, supersession, and expiry — the invariant that a retired unit is retained rather than deleted, the invariant that a retired unit cannot re-enter active reasoning, and the failure of a revoked unit that a stale cache keeps serving. The three exits map onto the validity states of Part VI: revocation and supersession and expiry are the transitions that move a unit out of active validity into superseded, obsolete, expired, or refuted, and this Part gives those transitions their semantics. The reason removal needs its own law is that the naive form of removal — delete the unit and move on — destroys exactly the lineage the sibling constitution's temporal-duplication rule insists on keeping, and the estate has learned that a history erased to tidy a record is an ability to reconstruct intent thrown away. Retirement is therefore not deletion but a change of state, and this Part defines how a unit changes state, why the old state is kept, and how a consumer is prevented from acting on a unit that has left force.

14.2 Revocation. Revocation is the exit in which an authority withdraws a unit it was entitled to assert, and its defining property is that the withdrawal is an act of authority, not of time or of a successor. A constitutional authority revokes a law it set; an owner revokes a directive; a project authority revokes a project rule — and in each case the revocation is valid only if the revoking party holds an authority at least equal to the one that asserted the unit, because a low-authority actor can no more revoke a high-authority unit than it can override one, the same ranking rule Part VII enforces on the override surface applied to the withdrawal surface. A revoked unit moves to a retired validity state, carries the provenance of its revocation — who withdrew it, when, and on what ground — and is retained as the record of a rule the estate once held and an authority has since withdrawn. Revocation is distinct from refutation: a revoked unit is not shown false, it is withdrawn by the party entitled to withdraw it, so its confidence does not collapse to known-false but simply ceases to be in force. The distinction matters to a consumer reading the retired unit for lineage, because a revoked rule may still record a warrant that was valid in its time, while a refuted one records a belief evidence overturned.

14.3 Supersession. Supersession is the exit in which a newer unit replaces an older one, and its defining property is the pointer: the superseded unit carries a supersession pointer to its replacement, so a consumer that encounters the old unit is routed forward to the new rather than left at a dead end. Supersession is how the estate advances a rule without losing the trail from its old form to its current one — a threshold that changes, a constraint that tightens, a decision that revises an earlier decision — and the chain of pointers forms a lineage a consumer can traverse from any historical version to the live one, so the estate never has to guess which of several versions is current. The superseded unit moves to the superseded validity state, and the temporal-duplication rule of DAIF-00 Part II governs it exactly: the versions are legitimately retained for lineage, and destroying the history to save space destroys the ability to reconstruct why a decision was made. Supersession differs from revocation in that it is driven by a successor rather than by a withdrawal — no authority need withdraw the old unit, because the new unit's arrival retires it — and it differs from expiry in that it is driven by a replacement rather than by time. The pointer is the load-bearing field: a superseded unit with no pointer is the dead end Part VI names, a retired unit a consumer cannot advance from because nothing records what replaced it.

14.4 Expiry. Expiry is the exit in which time removes a unit whose lifespan was declared in advance, and its defining property is the expiry field of Part VI, set when the unit was written to name the moment it ceases to hold on its own. Expiry governs the time-boxed units the estate creates deliberately — a bounded exception under Part XV's appeal path, a dated fallback, a temporary authorization — and it is the mechanism that keeps such a unit from silently becoming permanent. The estate's recorded hazard is precisely the exception without an expiry, which does not stay an exception but becomes the standing state of the law, so expiry is not optional metadata on a time-boxed unit but the field that makes it time-boxed at all. An expired unit moves to the expired validity state and is surfaced for renewal or retirement rather than silently extended, because a unit whose declared life has elapsed is a decision the estate should re-make consciously, not one it should let lapse into either a silent death or a silent immortality. Expiry differs from revocation and supersession in requiring no actor at all — no authority withdraws it and no successor replaces it, the passage of time alone moves it out of force — which is why an expired unit is surfaced rather than simply dropped: something must decide whether to renew it, and the estate makes that decision visible rather than letting the clock make it silently.

14.5 Retention over deletion. The invariant that unifies all three exits is retention: a revoked, superseded, expired, obsolete, or refuted unit is retained, never erased, because it is the historical record something downstream reads. The sibling constitution's temporal-duplication rule is the law here — the versions are legitimately retained for lineage, and destroying the history to save space destroys the ability to reconstruct why a decision was made — and the estate has felt the cost of violating it, an erased history being an intent that can no longer be reconstructed. A retained retired unit serves several downstream readers: an audit that reconstructs what the estate believed at a past moment, a lineage edge that a live unit points back to as the rationale for its own existence, a conflict resolution that references the superseded claim to explain why the current one prevailed. Retention is also what distinguishes this dataset's notion of removal from deletion in the crude sense: a unit leaves the active context — the live reasoning set the validity gate of Part VI admits only active units into — but it does not leave the estate's total record, which necessarily includes its history. The estate reasons over its active context and remembers over its total record, and retirement is the transition that moves a unit from the first to the second without ever removing it from the second, so the estate's memory of its own reversals stays intact and it does not re-make a decision it once reversed for reasons it can no longer read.

14.6 The no-re-entry invariant. The second invariant is that a retired unit cannot re-enter an active context, and it is what makes retention safe: a unit is kept for history precisely because it is guaranteed never to be reasoned over as if it were current. The validity gate of Part VI enforces this — only an active unit passes into live reasoning, so a superseded, revoked, expired, or refuted unit is excluded at the door — and Part IV reinforces it, a retired unit's authority being inert so it cannot be resurfaced to win a conflict it already lost. The invariant closes the loop between retention and force: because a retired unit is retained, the estate must guarantee it is never mistaken for a live one, and the guarantee is that the active context is assembled by the validity gate, which admits by state and not by presence. A superseded unit encountered anywhere is routed forward by its pointer rather than acted on; a revoked unit is excluded by its withdrawn state; an expired unit is held out for renewal. This is the mechanism that lets the estate keep its whole history without that history contaminating its current reasoning — the record grows without bound, and the active context stays exactly as small as its live units, because retention and no-re-entry are two guarantees that together let a unit be remembered forever and reasoned over never once it has left force.

14.7 The failure of the stale cache. The signature failure this Part exists to prevent is the revoked unit that a stale cache keeps serving: a unit correctly retired in the estate's record but still held in a cache, a compiled digest, or an assembled context that was built before the retirement and never rebuilt, so a consumer reads the retired unit as current from the cache and acts on a rule the estate has withdrawn. This is the exact hazard the estate has recorded — a compiled artifact going stale when its source moves, a cache serving a value the source has changed — applied to the validity surface, and it is more dangerous than a missing unit for the same reason Part VI gives: the stale unit is present, reads as current, and nothing in normal operation re-checks a unit a cache already holds. The defense is the seam this dataset shares with DAIF-21's reality synchronization: retirement is a validity transition, and a validity transition must invalidate every cache and every assembled context that admitted the unit while it was active, so a retirement is not complete until the caches that hold the unit are rebuilt or invalidated. A unit's retirement in the record is necessary but not sufficient; the sufficient condition is that no active context anywhere continues to serve it, and the synchronization layer that drives validity transitions is the fabric responsible for propagating a retirement out to every cache, so the estate's withdrawn rules stop being served the moment they are withdrawn rather than the next time a cache happens to be rebuilt.

PART XIV FINAL LAW. A unit leaves active force by revocation when an entitled authority withdraws it, by supersession when a newer unit replaces it and carries a pointer forward, or by expiry when a declared lifespan elapses — and in every case it is retained, never deleted, because the temporal-duplication rule of DAIF-00 Part II keeps the history a later audit, lineage, or resolution must read, while the validity gate guarantees no retired unit re-enters live reasoning, so the estate remembers its reversals without acting on them; and the failure the discipline forecloses is the stale cache that keeps serving a withdrawn unit, closed only when a retirement propagates out to invalidate every context that held the unit, because a unit withdrawn in the record but still served from a cache is a rule the estate has revoked and is still, silently, obeying.

PART XV — INTERFACES TO SIBLING DATASETS

15.1 Mission. This dataset acts on nothing; it is inert infrastructure that the sibling fabrics consume, and this Part specifies the consumption contract each sibling honors so that the type system's guarantees survive into the systems that reason with it. The earlier Parts fixed the eight-field typed cognitive object, its closed taxonomy, its intake checker, its conflict object, and its supersession semantics as a substrate — a definition the estate reads, never a runtime it executes — and the value of a substrate collapses the instant a consumer reaches around it, inventing an untyped path that re-admits the bare prose the whole dataset exists to end. This Part therefore names the four consumers that stand on the type system: DAIF-02's reasoning intermediate representations, whose every slot is typed here; DAIF-07's obligation lifecycle, which tracks a typed obligation object; DAIF-04's institutional contracts, whose pre- and postconditions are typed cognitive objects; and DAIF-00's verdicts, which carry typed authority. For each it states what the consumer takes from here and what invariant it upholds in return, and it defines the one law binding all four — no consumer may admit into its own reasoning a unit that did not pass the checker of Part XII — because the seam where an inert definition becomes an enforced discipline is exactly this consumption contract, and this Part is that seam written down.

15.2 DAIF-02 types every semantic unit it produces. The reasoning intermediate representations of DAIF-02 decompose an act of reasoning into typed slots — a premise, a derivation, a decision, an evidence edge — and every one of those slots is a typed cognitive object drawn from the eighteen kinds of Part III rather than a fresh notion DAIF-02 re-derives. A CIR's premise slot holds a fact or an assertion with its confidence and provenance; its derivation slot holds an inference whose epistemic status is capped by the ACIS ladder exactly as Part VIII requires; its decision slot holds a decision-typed unit carrying the record that makes it appealable. The contract DAIF-02 honors is that it never lowers a reasoning step into a representation without typing it first, so a CIR is well-formed only when each slot is a well-typed object the checker of Part XII would admit. In return the type system guarantees DAIF-02 a stable vocabulary: a slot typed fact means the same thing in every representation, so a lowering pass or a re-reasoning step reads a CIR's slots and knows their kinds without re-inferring them from prose. The interface is total — there is no semantic unit in a CIR that escapes typing — because a representation built from half-typed slots is a representation that reasons over guessed shapes, which is precisely the condition CIRs exist to replace.

15.3 DAIF-07 tracks a typed obligation. An obligation in DAIF-07's lifecycle is not a sentence that describes work owed but a typed cognitive object of the obligation kind, carrying the completion condition and the validity that Part III makes mandatory for that kind, so the lifecycle reads the object's fields to know whether the obligation is open, discharged, or lapsed rather than parsing a description. DAIF-07 consumes the Validity field of Part VI directly: an obligation at active validity is still owed, an obligation whose result-typed sub-unit records a verified completion moves to a discharged state, and an obligation whose expiry elapsed without discharge is surfaced the way Part XIV surfaces any expired unit. The contract DAIF-07 honors is that it never marks an obligation complete on a claim it did not type — a completion is a result-typed unit with observation-grade confidence, not an author's assurance — which is the typed form of the estate's own reality contract that a done claim requires observed evidence. In return the type system gives DAIF-07 a lineage: an obligation composed under Part X with its condition and its verification is a structure the lifecycle traverses to prove discharge, and the obligation's supersession pointer routes a consumer from a revised obligation to its replacement. The obligation arrives already typed, which is the only reason the lifecycle can track it mechanically rather than re-reading the prose that first stated the work.

15.4 DAIF-04 types its pre- and postconditions. An institutional contract in DAIF-04 binds two systems by naming what the caller must guarantee before a call and what the callee guarantees after it, and both the precondition and the postcondition are typed cognitive objects rather than prose clauses — each a constraint with a Strength field that decides whether its violation halts the call or is traded under record, each an Authority that names who may waive it, each a Scope that bounds where the contract holds. The contract DAIF-04 honors is that a term is enforceable only if it is typed: a precondition typed hard with a no-silent-override policy is a wall the caller may never quietly pass, exactly as Part VII requires, and a postcondition typed as an obligation feeds straight into DAIF-07's lifecycle as work the callee now owes. In return the type system gives DAIF-04 a resolution rule for conflicting terms — two contracts whose terms disagree produce the typed conflict object of Part XIII and resolve on the precedence ladder — so a contract renegotiation is a governed transition rather than a silent redefinition. Because a contract's terms are typed objects, a contract is itself checkable: DAIF-04 runs the checker of Part XII over its terms before the contract binds, and a term the checker cannot type is a term the contract cannot enforce, so an unenforceable clause is caught at authoring rather than at the breached call.

15.5 DAIF-00's verdicts carry typed authority. The constitution's verdicts — the D2A engine's CREATE, EXTEND, FOLD, and REFERENCE rulings, and the duplicate ontology's classifications — are decisions, and a decision is a typed cognitive object whose Authority field names the rung entitled to make it and whose provenance names the record that made it. DAIF-00 consumes the Authority ladder of Part IV as the ranking its precedence resolution runs on, so that a verdict's standing in a conflict is read from its typed authority rather than from whichever verdict was recorded last. The contract DAIF-00 honors is that a verdict is a typed decision with a provenance that entitles its authority, so a verdict claiming constitutional weight whose provenance points at a single session is convicted as a forged rung exactly as Part IV convicts any such claim. In return the type system inherits DAIF-00's precedence ladder as the resolver its conflict object of Part XIII feeds — the two datasets share one ranking, the constitution ruling on the disagreements the type system represents. This is the closing of the loop the map draws: DAIF-00 governs every verdict, DAIF-01 types the units those verdicts reason over, and neither ranks its decisions on one ladder and the knowledge behind them on another, because a fabric whose verdicts and whose knowledge drift onto separate ladders collides them at exactly the authority dispute both were built to resolve.

15.6 The no-bypass invariant. The single law binding all four consumers is that none may admit a unit into its own reasoning that did not pass the checker of Part XII, because the checker is the one gate where the eight fields are verified and every discipline the earlier Parts define becomes operative. A consumer that reads a well-typed unit inherits a verified shape and need not re-check it; a consumer that admits an unchecked unit inherits a guessed shape and re-creates the untyped condition one bypass at a time. The invariant is not advisory but structural: DAIF-02 lowers only checked units into CIR slots, DAIF-07 tracks only checked obligations, DAIF-04 enforces only checked terms, and DAIF-00 ranks only checked verdicts, so the active reasoning of every fabric above the type system holds exactly the units the checker admitted. This is the estate's own doctrine that a static pass proves shape and not truth applied across the seam — the checker proves a unit well-typed, the consumer trusts that proof rather than re-deriving it, and the reconciliation layer of DAIF-21 proves it true. A consumer may extend the type system by proposing new kinds through the schema registry of Part XI, but it may never route around the checker, because the checker's guarantee is the only thing that lets one fabric trust a unit another fabric produced without re-reading the prose it was typed from.

15.7 The failure of the untyped shortcut. The signature failure this Part forecloses is the consumer that invents an untyped shortcut — a CIR slot filled with a raw sentence to save a typing step, an obligation marked complete on an assurance rather than a result-typed unit, a contract term written as prose because typing it felt like ceremony, a verdict ranked by recency because reading its authority was slower. Each shortcut looks harmless at the moment it is taken, because the untyped unit reads correctly to the human who wrote it, and each defers its cost to the consumer downstream who must act on it mechanically and cannot see the fields it never carried. The harm is the harm the whole dataset traces: the untyped unit obeys blindly or is ignored entirely, leaks across a scope it never declared, launders an inference into a fact across a boundary, or is served stale from a cache that never learned it was retired. A shortcut in one consumer propagates into every fabric that reads that consumer's output, so a single untyped CIR slot corrupts every representation built on it and every obligation, contract, and verdict that reasons over it. The defense is the no-bypass invariant enforced as a gate at each consumer's intake, so that a consumer cannot admit an untyped unit even under pressure, and an untyped shortcut is refused where it is attempted rather than discovered where it detonates — because a type system every fabric may reach around is a type system the estate does not have, however completely its earlier Parts define one.

PART XV FINAL LAW. The type system acts on nothing and is consumed by four siblings under one contract — DAIF-02 types every CIR slot, DAIF-07 tracks a typed obligation object, DAIF-04 types its pre- and postconditions, and DAIF-00's verdicts carry typed authority ranked on the ladder of Part IV — bound by the invariant that no consumer may admit into its own reasoning a unit that did not pass the checker of Part XII, so that the guarantees of the substrate survive into every fabric above it, and the failure foreclosed is the untyped shortcut, which re-admits the bare prose the dataset exists to abolish one bypassed intake at a time and detonates not where it is taken but in the mechanical consumer downstream that cannot see the fields it never carried.

PART XVI — FAILURE MODES AND ADVERSARIAL CASES

16.1 Mission. The earlier Parts defined each field's discipline and named the failure that field forecloses; this Part gathers the system-level failures — the ways the type system as a whole breaks or is attacked — and for each pairs a detection with the countermeasure an earlier Part already names, so the reader sees the defenses not as scattered rules but as one hardened surface. Five failures matter at the system level because each defeats not a single field but the interpretability the whole type system promises: type confusion, where a unit is accepted under the wrong kind and a consumer performs the wrong operation with confidence; authority forgery, where an untrusted source self-elevates a preference to a law; scope leakage, where a project-scoped unit escapes to universal reach; epistemic laundering, where an inference is re-typed as a fact across a boundary; and stale validity, where an expired unit is served as current. None is hypothetical — each is a shape the estate has recorded or a shape its own laws were written to prevent — and each is adversarial in the sense that it can arise by accident or be induced deliberately, so the defense must be mechanical rather than a reviewer's vigilance. This Part treats the five in turn, always in the same form: the attack, how it is detected, and the countermeasure already sealed in an earlier Part, because a failure whose defense is named only once, far from where it strikes, is a failure the estate meets unprepared.

16.2 Type confusion. Type confusion is a unit accepted under the wrong kind, so a consumer reading its Type field performs the operation valid on the kind it was mistyped as rather than the kind it is — a hypothesis enforced as a constraint, a preference weighed as a law, an assertion trusted as a fact. It is the taxonomy's central failure, and it is a category error rather than a cosmetic one, because the eighteen kinds of Part III are a dispatch table in which the operation valid on one kind is invalid on another. Detection is at intake: the checker of Part XII rejects a Type outside the closed set and, crucially, cross-checks a unit's kind against the fields its kind requires — a fact-typed unit lacking observation-grade confidence is refused the fact type, a hypothesis-typed unit lacking a falsifier is untypable as a hypothesis, a constraint-typed unit with no Strength is incomplete. The countermeasure is the closed taxonomy itself plus the ACIS composition of Part VIII: because a kind carries required fields and those fields are checked against the kind, a unit cannot silently wear a kind whose obligations it does not meet. Type confusion survives only where a consumer reads prose and infers a kind; it dies where the kind is fixed on the object at intake by the author who knows what the unit is, and every downstream consumer inherits the right operation rather than re-inferring the wrong one.

16.3 Authority forgery. Authority forgery is an untrusted source recording a higher rung than it is entitled to — a preference dressed as a law, an agent note claiming project authority, an unverified assertion claiming the constitution's weight — and it is the adversarial case Part IV names as the field's sharpest. Its danger is that a forged rung wins conflicts it should lose: a consumer ranking two units by authority hands the contested question to the forger, and the estate's safety floor is breached by a claim no entitled party ever made. Detection rests on provenance: an authority claim is valid only if its provenance names a source the ladder actually places at that rung, so a unit claiming constitutional authority whose provenance points at a single session is convicted at intake, because a session cannot assert the constitution. The countermeasure is the standing law that an untrusted source cannot self-elevate — a low-authority actor cannot promote its own unit, only a higher authority can grant the promotion, and the grant is itself a typed decision with its own provenance — which is the typed form of the prompt-defense baseline's refusal to let an untrusted input override project rules. The forgery attempt is recorded rather than silently refused, so it is auditable, and the same check defends the schema registry of Part XI, where a schema change made without the owning authority is refused as exactly this forgery on the shape surface.

16.4 Scope leakage. Scope leakage is a project-scoped unit escaping to universal reach, so a preference learned on one project surfaces as advice on an unrelated one and a rule true for one substrate silently governs another. It is the isolation failure Part V names, and its most damaging instance is the narrow unit that comes to govern the whole estate — the decrease-gate baseline keyed to one session's own burn failing when read against a different session is the estate's recorded scar. Detection is the scope-check on every admission: a consumer assembling a context declares that context's scope and admits a unit only if the context falls within the unit's reach, so a project unit is invisible to a sibling project's context by construction. The countermeasure is the inward default of Part V — an ambiguous scope resolves to the narrowest defensible reach, never the broadest, because over-reach contaminates while under-reach merely omits — plus the rule that widening a scope is a typed decision requiring authority proportional to the new reach. A unit cannot acquire universal reach through the silence of an unfilled field; it must earn it through an explicit, authorized widening. Scope leakage survives only where scope lives in prose and drifts to whatever reach a reader gives it; it dies where reach is on the object and every admission is checked against it, so a unit enters a context only through the front door of a scope match.

16.5 Epistemic laundering. Epistemic laundering is an inference re-typed as a fact across a boundary, so a claim that arrived by reasoning enters a downstream fabric wearing the authority of an observation it never had. It is the cardinal rule's violation from Part VIII — an inference may never be typed as a fact — occurring not at the moment the inference is drawn but at a hand-off, where a unit at inferred confidence is copied into a new context and its status quietly promoted to verified because the new context's author treats the incoming claim as settled. The estate's founding-finding-as-hypothesis scar is exactly this: a number an effort was built on treated as measured when it had only been assumed. Detection is the checker's cross-check of Type against Confidence: a fact-typed unit must carry observation-grade confidence and a provenance that names the observation, and the epistemic status is capped by the ACIS ladder's No-Autopromotion rule, so a unit cannot record a status stronger than the level ACIS assigned its derivation. The countermeasure across a boundary is that Confidence composes the ACIS ladder rather than being re-declared: when DAIF-02 lowers a claim into a CIR slot or DAIF-04 folds one into a contract term, the claim carries its capped status, and a consumer cannot promote it by re-typing, because the status is the ladder's reading and not the author's mood. Laundering dies where confidence travels with the unit and the checker refuses any fact whose warrant the ladder did not grant.

16.6 Stale validity. Stale validity is an expired or retired unit served as current, so a consumer enforces a rule the estate has withdrawn — the failure Part XIV names and the reason an expired-but-present unit is worse than an absent one, because it reads as current and nothing in normal operation re-checks a unit a cache already holds. The attack surface is the cache, the compiled digest, or the assembled context built while the unit was active and never rebuilt after its retirement, and the recorded scar is the compiled artifact going stale when its source moves. Detection is the validity gate of Part VI: a context admits a unit only if its validity is active, its effective-from has arrived, and its expiry has not, so a superseded unit is routed forward by its pointer and a refuted unit is excluded at the door. But the gate defends only a freshly assembled context; the countermeasure for the stale cache is the seam with DAIF-21's reality synchronization, where a retirement is a validity transition that must invalidate every cache and every context that admitted the unit, so a retirement is not complete until no active context anywhere continues to serve the unit. Stale validity survives only where retirement updates the record but not the caches; it dies where a validity transition propagates out to invalidate every holder, so a withdrawn rule stops being served the moment it is withdrawn rather than the next time a cache happens to rebuild.

16.7 The five as one hardened surface. The five failures are not independent, and reading them together is how the estate sees its defenses as one surface rather than five patches. They share a shape: each is a unit that reads correctly to a human and misleads a machine, each defers its cost to a downstream consumer who did not introduce it, and each is defeated not by vigilance at the point of use but by a mechanical check at intake or at a hand-off. They also chain — a forged authority can carry a laundered inference into universal scope and have it served stale from a cache long after it was refuted, so a single adversarial unit can trip four of the five in sequence — which is why the defenses are layered rather than alternative: the checker of Part XII catches type confusion and forgery at intake, the scope-check catches leakage at every admission, the ACIS cap catches laundering at every hand-off, and the DAIF-21 seam catches staleness at every retirement. No single layer is sufficient, because an attacker or an accident that clears one layer meets the next, and the estate's security is the composition of all four rather than any one. This defense-in-depth is the type system's answer to the fact that its own guarantees are only as strong as the weakest seam a unit crosses, so every seam — intake, admission, hand-off, retirement — carries its own mechanical check, and a unit that is well-typed at every seam is a unit the estate can trust without re-reading the prose it was typed from.

PART XVI FINAL LAW. The type system's five system-level failures — type confusion accepting a unit under the wrong kind, authority forgery self-elevating a preference to a law, scope leakage escaping a project unit to universal reach, epistemic laundering re-typing an inference as a fact across a boundary, and stale validity serving a retired unit as current — are each detected mechanically and each foreclosed by a countermeasure an earlier Part already seals: the closed taxonomy and ACIS cross-check, the provenance-entitles-authority rule, the inward scope default and per-admission check, the ACIS No-Autopromotion cap that travels with the unit, and the DAIF-21 retirement-propagation seam — layered into one hardened surface because a single adversarial unit can chain four failures at once, and the estate's security is the composition of every seam's check, never any one alone.

PART XVII — METRICS AND ANTI-GOODHART

17.1 Mission. A type system that cannot measure its own health is a claim about discipline rather than a discipline, so this Part defines what the type system measures about itself, the data source each metric reads, and — for every metric — the countermetric that defends it against being gamed, because a metric optimized in isolation drifts from the property it was meant to track exactly as Goodhart's law predicts. The measurements are seven: typed-coverage ratio, unknown-versus-absent ratio, authority-resolution rate, scope-isolation violations caught, epistemic-integrity rate, alias-collision rate, and schema-version drift. Each reads a real source on disk — the checker's intake log, the symbol table of Part IX, the conflict resolutions of Part XIII, the schema registry of Part XI — and none asserts a value it has not measured, in strict obedience to CO-12's Telemetry-Before-Claims contract, which this Part composes rather than restates: a metric that reports a number the instrument did not produce is the epistemic form of the scaffold illusion, a claim that reads as measured and was only assumed. The anti-Goodhart discipline is the Part's spine: for every metric there is a way to move the number without improving the property, and for every such gaming path there is a countermetric that catches it, so the estate reads the pair together and never the headline number alone.

17.2 Typed-coverage ratio. The typed-coverage ratio is the fraction of units in a context that carry a full eight-field type versus those that remain bare prose, and its source is the checker's intake log, which records every unit admitted and whether it passed with a complete type or was quarantined. It is the type system's most direct health measure — a context of mostly-typed units is one the fabrics above can reason over mechanically, a context of mostly-prose is the untyped condition the dataset exists to abolish — and it is also the easiest to game, because coverage rises the moment a unit carries eight fields regardless of whether those fields are meaningful. The gaming path is trivial typing: an author faced with a coverage target types noise, filling the eight fields with defaults and unknowns so the unit passes the checker and lifts the ratio without adding any interpretable content. The countermetric is typed-substance: the fraction of typed units whose fields are load-bearing rather than defaulted — a unit whose authority, scope, strength, and confidence are all at their fail-safe defaults carries a type but describes nothing, so a coverage number that rises while typed-substance stays flat is coverage gamed by trivial typing. The estate reads the two together, because a high coverage ratio proves only that units carry fields, and a high typed-substance ratio proves that the fields carry meaning, which is the property coverage was meant to track.

17.3 Unknown-versus-absent ratio. The unknown-versus-absent ratio measures how a context's incompleteness is expressed: honestly, as a field explicitly set to unknown, or dishonestly, as a field simply missing — the distinction Part II makes inviolable, that unknown is a value a consumer can act on while absent is a defect that re-admits guessing. Its source is the checker, which distinguishes the two at intake and rejects the absent while admitting the unknown, so the ratio is read over the units the checker examined, absent counted among the quarantined and unknown among the admitted. A healthy ratio is high unknowns and near-zero absents, meaning the estate declares what it does not know rather than leaving fields blank; a degrading ratio signals authors omitting fields the checker then rejects, or worse, a consumer downstream of a leaky intake reading absents as values. The gaming path is unknown-inflation: an author who cannot supply a field sets it to unknown to pass the checker, so a context fills with unknowns that never resolve, technically honest but substantively empty. The countermetric is unknown-resolution rate — the fraction of unknowns that later advance to a real value versus those that persist — because an unknown is honest only as a temporary declaration awaiting resolution, and a register of permanent unknowns is a context that declared its ignorance and never cured it. The pair distinguishes honest incompleteness in motion from honest incompleteness frozen, which are different healths behind the same headline.

17.4 Authority-resolution rate and scope-isolation violations. Two operational metrics measure whether the ranking and isolation disciplines actually fire. The authority-resolution rate is the fraction of conflicts, from the typed conflict objects of Part XIII, that resolve cleanly on the precedence ladder versus those that stall because two units carry equal, un-refinable authority — its source is the conflict-resolution record, which logs each conflict's inputs and its ranked outcome. A high rate means the ladder is decisive; a falling rate means authorities are under-specified, so genuine disputes cannot be ranked. Its gaming path is spurious decisiveness: resolving conflicts by recency to force a clean outcome, which the countermetric of resolution-by-authority-versus-by-recency catches, because a ladder that resolves by whoever recorded last is the volume-wins failure wearing a resolution's mask. The scope-isolation-violations-caught metric counts the units the scope-check of Part V refused at a context's door because their reach did not include the context — its source is the admission log. A rising catch count is not a failure but a working defense; the danger is a catch count of zero, which reads as perfect isolation and may instead mean the scope-check is inert, never observed to refuse. Its countermetric is the injected-leak test: periodically admit a deliberately over-scoped unit and confirm the check refuses it, because a gate never seen to refuse is indistinguishable from one that cannot, exactly the SQI-00 posture the estate applies to every gate.

17.5 Epistemic-integrity rate. The epistemic-integrity rate is the metric closest to the estate's soul: the fraction of inferences that are never promoted to fact across their lifetime, the direct measure of whether the cardinal rule of Part VIII holds in practice. Its source is the checker's cross-check log paired with the ACIS ladder's promotion record — every unit at inferred confidence is tracked, and any transition to fact-grade confidence is examined for whether an actual observation or proof grounded the promotion or whether the status was simply re-typed at a boundary. A rate near total integrity means inferences stay inferences until real evidence promotes them; a falling rate means epistemic laundering is occurring, inferences acquiring fact status without the warrant a fact requires. The gaming path is definitional evasion: relabeling an inference as an assertion or a result to move it out of the tracked class, so the integrity rate stays high because the laundered units left the denominator. The countermetric is the promotion-audit — a sample of fact-typed units traced back to their provenance to confirm each names a real observation — because integrity measured only over units that kept the inferred label misses exactly the units that escaped it. The two together catch both the direct promotion the rate tracks and the sideways relabeling that evades the rate, which is the only way to measure a discipline whose violation can hide by renaming the thing it violates.

17.6 Alias-collision rate and schema-version drift. Two structural metrics measure the health of the estate's shared vocabulary and its shapes. The alias-collision rate, from the symbol table of Part IX, counts the labels found to resolve to more than one canonical identity before homonym separation splits them, and the unresolved-alias count of labels denoting one identity that were never canonicalized — its source is the symbol table's resolution log. A low collision rate and a low unresolved-alias count mean names cohere; a rising unresolved-alias count is the fracture-into-synonyms failure accumulating, knowledge splitting into pools no search reconciles. Its gaming path is aggressive merging — collapsing labels to drive the unresolved count down, which destroys the real distinctions homonym separation preserves — caught by the countermetric of split-necessity, the rate of merges later reversed because they fused two concepts. Schema-version drift, from the schema registry of Part XI, measures how many units are built against a superseded schema version past its deprecation window, the population the unversioned-schema failure would leave malformed. A low drift means migrations keep pace with schema evolution; a rising drift means shapes are changing faster than units cross to them. Its gaming path is freezing the schema to hold drift at zero, which trades evolvability for a flat metric, caught by pairing drift with schema-evolution rate, because a drift of zero on a frozen schema is a different health from a drift of zero on a schema that evolves and migrates cleanly.

17.7 Telemetry before claims, and the anti-Goodhart law. The Part's governing discipline is CO-12's Telemetry-Before-Claims contract, composed here and never restated: no metric in this Part asserts a value the instrument did not produce, so a report that the typed-coverage ratio is high, the epistemic-integrity rate is total, or the scope-isolation check is working must cite the log the number was read from, and a claimed metric with no telemetry behind it is refused as the scaffold illusion in numeric form. The anti-Goodhart law generalizes across all seven: every metric has a gaming path that moves the number without improving the property, so every metric is published with the countermetric that catches its gaming, and the estate reads the pair rather than the headline. This is the same law the estate learned the hard way — a ratio gate is satisfied by shrinking its denominator, a decrease-gate is bounded by its vocabulary, a coverage score is gamed by trivially typing noise — and the defense is always the same shape: measure the absolute alongside the ratio, measure the substance alongside the coverage, measure the resolution alongside the declaration, and inject a known failure to confirm the gate refuses. A type system that measures itself this way knows the difference between discipline and its appearance; one that reports headline numbers alone optimizes the numbers and loses the property, which is Goodhart's law arriving on schedule at exactly the metric the estate trusted most.

PART XVII FINAL LAW. The type system measures itself with seven telemetried metrics — typed-coverage ratio, unknown-versus-absent ratio, authority-resolution rate, scope-isolation violations caught, epistemic-integrity rate, alias-collision rate, and schema-version drift — each read from a real source and each paired with the countermetric that catches its gaming path, because coverage is gamed by trivially typing noise, isolation-catches of zero may mean an inert gate, and integrity stays high by relabeling the inference that escaped the class; and the whole Part composes CO-12's Telemetry-Before-Claims so that no metric asserts a value the instrument did not produce, the estate reading every headline against its countermetric rather than alone, because a metric optimized in isolation drifts from the property it was built to track exactly as Goodhart predicts.

PART XVIII — THE MATURITY MODEL

18.1 Mission. Type discipline is not a switch an ecosystem flips but a ladder it climbs, and this Part defines the levels so an estate can locate where it stands, see the failure that keeps it there, and know the gate that carries it up. Five levels order the climb: untyped prose, where knowledge is sentences; annotated, where some units carry authority or scope; fully typed, where every unit is mechanically interpretable; self-validating, where a checker gates admission; and self-evolving, where the schema registry learns new types from recurring untyped patterns. Each level is defined by three things — an observable signature a reader can detect on disk, the failure mode characteristic of that level, and the gate that must be passed to reach the next — because a maturity model that names levels without observable signatures is an org-chart, and the estate's own law is that naming a layer adds no mechanism. The levels are cumulative: a fully typed estate still contains prose at its edges, a self-validating one still annotates before it types, and the climb is never complete, because new knowledge enters as prose and must be carried up the ladder continuously. This Part is therefore not a one-time assessment but a standing map, and the value of knowing one's level is knowing which gate is the next real work rather than skipping to a discipline the level below cannot yet support.

18.2 Level zero — untyped prose. At the lowest level knowledge is sentences: a constraint lives as a clause in a paragraph, a decision as a note, a fact and a guess side by side in prose that reads identically for both. The observable signature is that a consumer cannot answer any of the eight questions about a unit without a human reading the surrounding text — the authority, scope, strength, and currency of a claim are nowhere on the object, only inferable by a reader who knows the context. The failure mode is the one the whole dataset opens on: the untyped sentence looks complete, passes every human review, and reaches a mechanical consumer that either obeys it blindly, treating a soft preference as a wall, or ignores it entirely, treating a hard safety constraint as advice. Both failures are unavoidable at this level because typing is exactly the information they lack, and an estate at level zero cannot reason mechanically over its own knowledge at all — it can only present it to humans. The gate to the next level is the first annotation: recording, on at least some units, the one or two fields whose absence hurts most, usually authority and scope, because those are the fields whose guessing produces the authority collision and the scope leak the estate feels first. Passing the gate does not require typing everything; it requires the estate to accept that a field belongs on the object rather than in the reader's head, which is the conceptual break level zero has not yet made.

18.3 Level one — annotated. At the annotated level some units carry some fields: a constraint records its authority, a note records its scope, a decision points at its record — but the annotation is partial, inconsistent, and unchecked, so a unit may carry three fields where its neighbor carries none and neither is validated. The observable signature is fields present but sparse and un-enforced: a reader finds authority on this unit and not that one, scope declared here and absent there, and no gate ensuring any field is legal or any invariant holds. The failure mode is the false confidence of partial typing: a consumer that learns to read the fields when present begins to trust them, and then meets a unit where the field is absent and either halts or guesses, so the inconsistency is worse in some ways than uniform prose, because it teaches reliance the coverage cannot support. Annotation also admits the absent-versus-unknown confusion of Part II in full force, because nothing distinguishes a field left blank from a field meaning unknown. The gate to the next level is completeness with the unknown discipline: every unit carries all eight fields, each a legal value or an explicit unknown, so that a consumer can rely on the fields being present even when their content is unknown. This is the hardest gate on the ladder, because it demands the estate finish the typing it started, and it is where most ecosystems stall — annotation is easy to begin and easy to leave partial, and the partial state feels like progress while delivering the reliability of neither prose nor types.

18.4 Level two — fully typed. At the fully typed level every unit is a mechanically interpretable typed cognitive object: all eight fields present, each a legal value or an explicit unknown, the reference web's invariants holding, the defaults applied where the author left a field to the schema. The observable signature is that a consumer, in any fabric, reads a unit's eight fields and treats it identically to every other consumer — the interoperability the downstream datasets require, where DAIF-02 builds a representation from fields it trusts to be present and DAIF-07 tracks an obligation whose validity it can read. The failure mode at this level is subtler than at the ones below, because the typing is complete but unenforced: an author can still record a malformed unit — an inference typed as a fact, a forged authority, a composition weaker than its hardest part — and nothing stops it entering the active context, because there is as yet no gate, only a convention that units be well-typed. The estate at level two has the vocabulary of mechanical interpretation but not its guarantee, so its contexts are mostly-well-typed with malformed units scattered through them that a consumer discovers only on use. The gate to the next level is the checker: a mechanical intake that admits only well-typed units and quarantines the rest with a stated reason, converting the convention of good typing into the guarantee of it. Passing this gate is the difference between a type system authors are asked to honor and one the estate enforces.

18.5 Level three — self-validating. At the self-validating level the checker of Part XII gates admission: every unit passes through one door where its eight fields are verified, its invariants checked, its authority entitled by provenance, and its scope resolved, and an un-typeable unit is quarantined rather than admitted. The observable signature is a fail-visible intake log — every unit either admitted with a verified type or quarantined with a reason, and no unit in the active context that did not pass — so the estate can state, at any moment, exactly which units it reasons over and which it declined and why. The failure mode at this level is the checker's own limit, the one Part XII names: a passing check proves shape, not truth, so a well-formed falsehood passes, and an estate that treats the green check as proof of correctness inherits units that are well-typed and wrong. The self-validating estate is safe against malformation but not against a unit whose provenance names an observation that was misread, which is why the checker is the necessary first gate and never the sufficient last one. The gate to the next level is reflexivity and evolution: the type system turned upon itself, where the schema registry of Part XI governs the shapes under the same discipline it imposes on units, and where the recurring untyped pattern — the thing authors keep expressing that no existing kind captures — is detected and promoted into a new registered type. Passing this gate is the estate ceasing to be a fixed type system and becoming one that grows its own vocabulary from the pressure of what its authors keep failing to type.

18.6 Level four — self-evolving. At the highest level the schema registry learns new types from recurring untyped patterns: when authors repeatedly express a kind of unit the eighteen kinds do not capture, or repeatedly carry a field the schema does not register, the estate detects the recurrence and promotes it — a new type, a new field, a new invariant — into the registry under the same authority, versioning, and migration the registry governs everything by. The observable signature is a schema that changes on evidence: registered types whose provenance is a measured recurrence of an untyped pattern, each added through the compatibility-typed, migrated, authority-owned process of Part XI, never by silent redefinition. This composes FD-07's flywheel and the estate's evolution machinery rather than inventing a parallel learner — the type system's self-evolution is the flywheel applied to the schema, a recurring pattern promoted to a durable type the way the flywheel promotes a recurring learning to a durable capability. The failure mode at this level is over-fitting the schema: promoting a pattern seen twice into a type, inflating the taxonomy with kinds that describe noise, the type-system form of the anti-inflation gate's concern that CREATE be the rarest verdict. The countermeasure is the same discipline the estate applies to every promotion — a threshold of recurrence, a proof the new type is irreducible to an existing one, and an authority entitled to seal it — so the schema grows only under the evidence that a real pattern recurs, and the self-evolving estate is one whose vocabulary compounds with its experience without bloating with its noise.

18.7 The model as a standing ladder. The five levels are a ladder the estate climbs and re-climbs, not a summit it reaches once, because new knowledge always enters as prose at level zero and must be carried up through annotation, typing, validation, and evolution continuously, so an estate's maturity is not the highest level it has ever touched but the level at which its intake reliably operates. The levels are cumulative and the gates are real work: the conceptual break that a field belongs on the object, the hard completeness gate that finishes partial annotation, the checker that converts convention to guarantee, and the reflexive evolution that grows the schema on evidence. An estate can regress — a self-validating estate whose checker is bypassed by a consumer's untyped shortcut falls back toward annotation, exactly the failure Part XV forecloses — so the ladder must be defended at every level, not just climbed once. The model's value is diagnostic: an estate that knows it stands at annotation knows its next work is completeness, not a self-evolving schema it cannot yet support, and an estate that mistakes partial annotation for maturity skips the gate that would actually help it. This is the estate's own law that an org-chart adds no mechanism applied to its own growth — a maturity level means nothing without its observable signature and its gate — so the model names, for each level, the thing a reader can detect on disk and the concrete gate to the next, and the climb is measured by gates passed rather than by names claimed.

PART XVIII FINAL LAW. Type discipline is a ladder of five levels — untyped prose where knowledge is sentences, annotated where some units carry some fields, fully typed where every unit is mechanically interpretable, self-validating where the checker gates admission, and self-evolving where the schema registry learns new types from recurring untyped patterns — each defined by an observable signature on disk, a characteristic failure mode, and a concrete gate to the next, cumulative and continuously re-climbed because new knowledge always enters as prose and can regress when a consumer bypasses the checker, so an estate's maturity is the level at which its intake reliably operates, measured by gates passed and never by names claimed, because a level without its signature and its gate is an org-chart that adds no mechanism.

PART XIX — WORKED EXAMPLE: TYPING A CONSTRAINT END TO END

19.1 Mission and the specimen. This Part proves the machinery composes by carrying one concrete constraint through the whole system on synthetic data, from the moment an author states it to the moment a successor retires it, so the reader sees the eight fields, the symbol table, the checker, the conflict object, the precedence ladder, and the supersession lineage act on a single unit in sequence rather than as separate abstractions. The specimen is a project-scoped hard architectural rule, stated by an author in prose as: within the session-continuity project, the recovery path must key a session's liveness to the newest internal timestamp inside its transcript, never to the transcript file's modification time. The rule is real in shape and synthetic in content — it names only power-pack-internal scopes and a power-pack-internal recovery concern, contaminated by nothing — and it is chosen because it is exactly the kind of unit that fails as bare prose: a consumer reading the sentence knows what it forbids but not that it is hard, not that it binds only the session-continuity project, not who may override it, and not whether it still holds. Carried through the type system, the same sentence becomes a unit every consumer treats identically, and the walk from prose to typed object, through conflict and supersession, is the demonstration that the eight-field object and its machinery are not a static schema but a composing mechanism.

19.2 Assigning the eight fields. The author types the specimen by assigning its eight fields. Type is constraint, and because the rule is at the highest architectural authority within its project and may never be silently violated, it is the specialization law of Part X — a constraint at the hardest strength and a project's top rung, which the taxonomy names a project-level architectural law rather than an ordinary constraint. Authority is project, the rung entitled to set an architectural rule for the session-continuity project, and its provenance names the decision record that sealed the rule after the liveness-by-modification-time hazard was observed. Scope is project, bounding the rule to session-continuity and its features, repos, and sessions, and no wider, because the rule concerns one project's recovery path and a broader scope would leak it. Strength is hard, with an override-policy of no-silent-override derived from the strength, requiring a strictly higher authority and a recorded, dated exception to set it aside. Validity is active, but only because it has been verified — the author does not default it active but promotes it there after the observation that grounds it. Provenance is the decision record and the observed incident behind it; Confidence is observed, because the rule rests on a directly seen failure of the modification-time path, not on an inference. Eight fields assigned, the sentence is now a unit that answers every question the prose left open.

19.3 Registering identity in the symbol table. Before the unit can be checked or reasoned over, the things it refers to must resolve to canonical identities, so the author registers them through the symbol table of Part IX. The rule names the session-continuity project, which resolves to one project coordinate; the recovery path, which resolves to the canonical identity of that project's session-recovery component; the newest-internal-timestamp concept and the modification-time concept, which resolve to two distinct canonical definitions so a later reader cannot confuse them; and the unit itself, which is registered under a stable identity with its own coordinate so other units can reference it. The registration composes GK-02's canonicalization: the recovery path had been named three ways across prior notes — by a nickname, by an abbreviation, and by its full title — and the symbol table resolves all three onto the one coordinate, so a reference to the rule from any of the three lands on the same identity. The unit's identity is stable across the versions it will later have, so when a successor supersedes it, the supersession pointer will target this coordinate rather than a floating label. Registration is what lets every later step — the checker, the conflict object, the lineage — refer to the specimen and its subjects by identities the estate agrees on rather than by spellings that could fracture.

19.4 Validating through the checker. The unit enters the active context through the checker of Part XII, which validates it at intake. The checker confirms all eight fields are present with legal values — Type in the closed set, Authority a rung the ladder holds, Validity one of the six states, Confidence one of the ten statuses — and applies no defaults, because the author filled every field explicitly. It cross-checks Type against the fields the kind requires: a constraint carries a Strength, and the specimen's Strength is hard, so the check passes; a project-level architectural law carries a no-silent-override policy, and it does. It verifies the reference web's invariants: the override-policy cites the project authority rung, which exists; the confidence is observed and the provenance names the observation that grounds it, so the Type-versus-Confidence cross-check confirms the unit is not an inference wearing a fact-grade claim. It verifies the authority is entitled by its provenance — the project rung is claimed by a decision record the ladder places at that rung, not by a bare session, so no forged-authority conviction fires. And it resolves the scope to the session-continuity project, a real coordinate. The unit passes, and because its confidence is observed and its validity active, it is admitted clean rather than flagged. The intake log records the admission with its verified type, so the estate can later state exactly that this unit entered the active context well-typed.

19.5 A conflicting unit produces a typed conflict object. Some sessions later a second author records a unit in the session-continuity project: a soft preference, at agent authority, that the recovery path key liveness to a file's modification time because it is cheaper to read. This unit is typed too — Type preference, Authority agent, Scope project, Strength soft, Confidence inferred from a reasoning about cost, provenance a single session — and it passes the checker as a well-formed preference. But it disagrees with the specimen: both units govern the session-continuity recovery path, resolved through the symbol table to the same recovery-path identity, and their claims cannot both be honored, because one forbids the modification-time key and the other prefers it. The conflict builder of Part XIII detects the shared subject, confirms the scopes overlap — both are project-scoped to session-continuity — and classifies the disagreement as a contradiction within an overlapping scope. It constructs a typed conflict object naming both claims in full, the scope of each, the authority of each — project versus agent — the confidence of each — observed versus inferred — the recency of each, the possible resolution the kind suggests, and the consequence of leaving it unresolved: a recovery path that might key liveness by modification time and resurface a session the modification-time hazard would misjudge. The disagreement is now a first-class object the estate holds, not a contradiction buried between two units no consumer reading either alone could see.

19.6 Resolution by the precedence ladder. The conflict object feeds DAIF-00 Part XVI's precedence ladder, which resolves it mechanically from the fields the object carries. The ladder ranks by authority first: the specimen is at project authority, the challenger at agent authority, and project outranks agent, so on the vertical ladder alone the specimen wins. The refinements confirm rather than overturn the ranking: verified outranks speculative, and the specimen at observed confidence outranks the challenger at inferred confidence; the specimen is a hard constraint and the challenger a soft preference, and Part VII's rule is decisive — a low-authority actor can never override a high-authority hard constraint, so an agent-rung soft preference cannot set aside a project-rung hard architectural law no matter how much cheaper its path. The ladder resolves in favor of the specimen, and the resolution is recorded, not silent: the challenger is not deleted but marked as overridden by the specimen, its disagreement retained as lineage, and because the challenger raised a real consideration — cost — the resolution notes that a consumer wanting the cheaper path must route it through an appeal under Part XV, a recorded, dated exception granted by an authority entitled to set aside a project architectural law, never a silent trade. The estate has resolved a genuine disagreement by a rule set in advance rather than by whichever unit a consumer read last.

19.7 Supersession and retained lineage. Later still, the session-continuity project's architecture changes: the recovery path gains a signed liveness beacon that is both cheap and reliable, and the project authority seals a new rule — key liveness to the beacon, falling back to the newest internal timestamp when the beacon is absent. This successor is typed and checked exactly as the specimen was, and it supersedes the specimen rather than deleting it: the successor is registered under its own identity, and the specimen's Validity transitions from active to superseded, carrying a supersession pointer to the successor's coordinate so any consumer that encounters the old rule is routed forward to the new one. The specimen is retained, not erased, because it is the lineage a later audit reads to reconstruct why the beacon rule exists — the modification-time hazard the specimen was written against is the rationale the successor inherits, and destroying the specimen would destroy the ability to reconstruct that intent, exactly the temporal-duplication rule of DAIF-00 Part II. The no-re-entry invariant of Part XIV guarantees the superseded specimen never re-enters live reasoning: the validity gate admits only the active successor, and the specimen's authority is now inert, so it cannot resurface to win a conflict it no longer governs. And the retirement propagates through the DAIF-21 seam to invalidate every cache and assembled context that held the specimen while it was active, so no stale context keeps serving the old rule after the beacon rule takes force. The specimen's whole life — typed, registered, checked, contested, resolved, superseded, retained — is the proof that the machinery composes end to end on a single unit.

PART XIX FINAL LAW. A single project-scoped hard architectural rule — key session liveness to the newest internal transcript timestamp, never to a file's modification time — walks the whole system: its eight fields are assigned, its subjects registered to canonical identities in the symbol table, the unit validated clean by the checker, a cheaper agent-authored soft preference producing a typed conflict object that the precedence ladder resolves in the hard rule's favor because a low-authority actor can never override a high-authority hard constraint, and finally a beacon-based successor superseding it with a pointer forward while the original is retained as the lineage a later audit reads and its retirement propagates out to invalidate every stale cache — proving that the eight-field object, the symbol table, the checker, the conflict object, the ladder, and supersession compose end to end on one unit rather than standing as separate abstractions.

PART XX — INTEGRATION AND DATASET-DERIVED SYSTEMS

20.1 Mission and integration. This closing Part states how the type system wires into the estate and what it makes buildable later, and it draws the line the map draws: DAIF-01 is the substrate DAIF-02, DAIF-04, and DAIF-07 stand on, and the systems named here are derived — described so an authority can build them when the estate needs them, never spun up inside this dataset, which acts on nothing. Integration first: the type system sits beneath the spine, because a representation cannot be typed before there is a type system, a contract cannot name a typed obligation before there is a typed object, and an obligation cannot be tracked before it can be typed — so DAIF-02's CIR slots, DAIF-04's contract terms, and DAIF-07's obligations all resolve their shapes here, and the symbol table beneath the eight fields resolves every name they reference. The derived systems are the type system made operational: a type-checker gate at knowledge-admission time, a schema-registry service, a symbol-table canonicalizer, and an authority-forgery detector, each specified by its mission, inputs, outputs, authority, activation, and done-gate, so the estate can build any one of them against a stated contract rather than an intuition. Naming them here is the estate's discipline that a dataset ships only if it names an engine, a gate, and an evaluator — the type system names all three for every derived system, so none is an org-chart layer that adds a title and no mechanism.

20.2 The type-checker gate at knowledge-admission time. The first derived system is the checker of Part XII made a live gate. Its mission is to admit into the estate's active context only well-typed units and to quarantine the rest with a stated reason. Its inputs are a candidate unit's eight fields and the schema registry the fields are validated against; its outputs are an admit-with-verified-type, a quarantine-with-reason, or a flag-and-admit for a well-formed-but-provisional unit, each written to a fail-visible intake log. Its authority is the type system's own, universal-scoped, because the admission discipline binds every fabric that reasons over the active context. Its activation is at knowledge-admission time — when a unit is first recorded, when a fabric lowers a claim into a representation, when a contract binds a term — so the gate fires wherever prose would otherwise enter unchecked, composing the estate's existing PreToolUse-style intake hooks rather than inventing a new transport. Its done-gate is the estate's own standard: the gate is complete only when it has been observed to refuse — an injected malformed unit quarantined, an injected forged authority convicted, an injected inference-as-fact rejected — because a gate never seen to refuse is indistinguishable from an inert one, the SQI-00 posture the estate applies to every gate it ships.

20.3 The schema-registry service. The second derived system is the schema registry of Part XI made a queryable service. Its mission is to hold every object, representation, contract, and event schema under one canonical identity, one owning authority, and one version lineage, so every producer builds against the one registered shape and every consumer validates against it. Its inputs are schema definitions, version records, compatibility classifications, and migration transforms; its outputs are the resolved shape for a given identity and version, a compatibility verdict for a proposed change, and a migration path across a breaking boundary. Its authority is distributed by schema: each schema is owned by the authority of Part IV entitled to change it, so the object model's own schema is universal-scoped and high-authority while a project representation's schema is owned at the project rung. Its activation is on every schema resolution and every proposed schema change — a producer resolves a shape before building, an authority proposes a change that the service classifies backward-compatible, forward-compatible, or breaking and, if breaking, gates behind a migration and a dated deprecation. Its done-gate is that no unversioned schema is held and every schema change is a typed, authority-ranked, versioned event, so the service refuses to admit a shape without a version and refuses a change without an entitled owner, closing the two-producers-disagree-on-shape failure at the root.

20.4 The symbol-table canonicalizer. The third derived system is the symbol table of Part IX made an active canonicalizer. Its mission is to resolve every reference in every unit to exactly one canonical identity — collapsing many labels onto one coordinate and splitting one label off two concepts — so the estate's units name one world rather than a crowd of private vocabularies. Its inputs are the labels a unit uses and the registry of canonical identities with their aliases, namespaces, and scopes; its outputs are a resolved identity for each reference, a recorded label-to-identity mapping, and a split-or-merge action when a collision or an unresolved alias is found. Its authority is the type system's, but a merge or a split that changes an identity is a typed decision requiring an authority entitled to the affected scope, because canonicalizing a universal-scoped name is a claim on the whole estate. Its activation is at intake, composing GK-02's canonicalization: a unit's references are resolved as it enters, so an alias is canonicalized when the unit is recorded rather than left to fracture the register from within. Its done-gate is a low unresolved-alias count paired with a low reversed-merge rate — the alias-collision metric of Part XVII and its split-necessity countermetric — so the canonicalizer is complete only when it collapses the labels that mean one thing without fusing the labels that mean two, proven by both numbers rather than by the merge count alone.

20.5 The authority-forgery detector. The fourth derived system is the forged-authority defense of Part IV made a standing detector. Its mission is to convict, at intake and on periodic audit, any unit whose recorded authority its provenance does not entitle — a preference dressed as a law, an agent note claiming project authority, an unverified assertion claiming the constitution's weight. Its inputs are a unit's Authority field and its Provenance, plus the ladder that maps a source to a rung; its outputs are a conviction with a recorded reason when the provenance does not place the source at the claimed rung, or a clean pass when it does. Its authority is the type system's, universal-scoped, because the forgery defense protects the estate's safety floor across every fabric. Its activation is twofold — at intake, where the checker cross-checks authority against provenance, and as a proactive audit that re-scans the active context for units whose provenance was later found not to entitle their rung, composing the estate's existing proactive-scanner pattern rather than a new loop. Its done-gate is that a forged rung is refused and recorded rather than silently blocked, and that the detector has been observed to convict an injected forgery, because the same law that governs every gate governs this one: a forgery detector never seen to convict is indistinguishable from an absent one, and the estate ships neither.

20.6 Derived, not built now. The four systems are named here and built later by an authority that needs them, never spun up inside this dataset, because DAIF-01 acts on nothing and its correctness is a matter of the schema it states, never of a runtime it does not have. This is the estate's dataset-first discipline: reality, then architecture, then contracts, then construction, so the type system defines the contract each derived system must honor — mission, inputs, outputs, authority, activation, done-gate — and construction follows when the estate reaches the phase for it. Naming the systems without building them is not deferral of undefined work; each is fully specified against a contract an authority can construct against immediately, and each composes an existing estate mechanism rather than forking one — the checker composes intake hooks, the registry composes the schema discipline, the canonicalizer composes GK-02, the detector composes the proactive scanner. The discipline that keeps this honest is the anti-inflation gate the estate applies to its own proposals: a derived system ships only if it is irreducible to an existing one, so the estate builds a type-checker gate because no existing gate validates the eight fields, and it does not build a parallel authority engine because the ladder already exists and the detector merely enforces it. Specifying a system and building it are different phases, and this Part is the specification, complete enough to build from and inert until an authority does.

20.7 The dataset's fundamental property. The whole dataset closes on the property it opened on: an untyped unit is a liability, and a typed unit is mechanically interpretable, and that difference is what lets the rest of the fabric reason without re-reading prose. An untyped sentence carries its meaning to a human and hides the eight things a machine needs — its type, authority, scope, strength, validity, override-policy, provenance, and confidence — so every mechanical consumer is forced to obey it blindly or ignore it entirely, and both are failures the estate has paid for in authority collisions, scope leaks, laundered inferences, and stale rules enforced long after they died. A typed cognitive object carries those eight fields on itself, checked at intake, resolved to canonical identities, ranked by an authority ladder, bounded by a nested scope, gated by a validity lifecycle, and governed by a versioned schema, so a consumer in any fabric reads its fields and treats it identically, and DAIF-02 can build a representation, DAIF-04 a contract, DAIF-07 an obligation, and DAIF-00 a verdict on a substrate they trust rather than re-derive. The type system is the second dataset in the spine and the foundation the rest stands on, deliberately inert, a definition the estate reads, so that the fabrics above it reason over units whose meaning is on the object rather than in the reader's head — which is the one capability the whole estate was built to have, and the one an untyped estate can never have, however well its sentences read.

PART XX FINAL LAW. The type system wires beneath the spine as the substrate DAIF-02, DAIF-04, and DAIF-07 stand on, and it makes four systems buildable later — a type-checker gate at knowledge-admission time, a schema-registry service, a symbol-table canonicalizer, and an authority-forgery detector — each specified by mission, inputs, outputs, authority, activation, and a done-gate that requires the gate be observed to refuse, each composing an existing estate mechanism rather than forking it, and none built inside this inert dataset; and the whole corpus closes on its founding property, that an untyped unit is a liability hiding the eight things a machine needs while a typed unit carries them on itself and is mechanically interpretable, which is exactly what lets the rest of the fabric reason without re-reading the prose the type system was written to abolish.
