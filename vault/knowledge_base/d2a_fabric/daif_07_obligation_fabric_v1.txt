DAIF-07 — OBLIGATION LIFECYCLE AND WORK COMPLETION AUTHORITY

PART I — THESIS: THE SILENT FAILURE

1.1 Mission. This dataset states the law that gives the Power Pack an authority over its own unfinished work, and the law is one sentence: no commitment the estate incurs may leave the estate by silence, because every other defect announces itself and this one does not. A bug throws. A test fails. A deploy errors. A type-check refuses. A gate blocks. Every one of those failures is loud by construction — the failure IS an event, and the event is the detector. Unfinished work is the single failure class defined by the ABSENCE of an event. Work that nobody finished emits nothing. It does not throw, it does not fail, it does not error, and it does not block. The system that abandoned it is exactly as quiet as the system that completed it, and the two are indistinguishable from the outside. This is why obligation loss is the most damaging failure class in the fabric and why DAIF-03 Part V rules obligation fidelity BINARY: a lost obligation raises no alarm, it simply never gets done, and nobody ever notices — not the actor who incurred it, not the authority who was owed it, not the audit that ran the next week. The debt accrues in perfect silence and is discovered, if ever, by accident, years later, as an unexplained gap between what the estate believes it built and what it actually built.

1.2 The founding argument. The brainstorm that founded this entire corpus began with one idea and one idea only — a watcher that finds work nobody finished. The Reality Audit that followed was brutal to its own author: of the thirty guardians that brainstorm proposed, the Power Pack already shipped roughly twenty-two, and the honest verdict on almost all of them was DO-NOT-BUILD. But it did not already ship this one, and the source itself named the reason it did not. A watcher that greps loose text for abandonment markers is a text-scanner, and the estate already runs four text-scanners. Adding a fifth would have been the exact duplication the constitution exists to forbid. The source's own resolution is the sentence this dataset is built on: the real system stops scanning loose text and consumes a formal IR. That single move converts the founding idea from a fifth scanner into something no existing system was: a lifecycle authority. DAIF-07 is that authority. It does not search prose for markers. It consumes the CIR-5 obligation objects that the DAIF-02 compiler already extracted, and it governs their entire lifecycle from birth to verified closure.

1.3 The cardinal distinction: scanner versus authority. A text-scanner finds markers that someone remembered to write. That sentence contains its own indictment. The scanner's recall is bounded by the diligence of the person who abandoned the work — it finds only the abandonment that was politely annotated, and abandonment is precisely the act least likely to be annotated, because a person who abandons work under pressure does not pause to leave a note for the auditor. The scanner therefore has a recall ceiling it can never raise by improving its regular expressions, because the failure is not in the matching, it is in the corpus: the obligations that matter most are the ones nobody marked. An obligation authority has no such ceiling. It does not depend on annotation at all. It governs every commitment the compiler extracted, marked or unmarked, spoken or written, deliberate or offhand — because CIR-5 compiles an obligation at the moment it is CREATED, from the source that created it, before anyone has had the chance to forget it. The scanner asks "what did someone flag?" The authority asks "what did the estate commit to, and has every one of those commitments been discharged or explicitly declared dead?" These are not two strengths of the same instrument. They are different instruments, and only one of them can answer the founding question.

1.4 Why the absence of an event defeats every other guardian. Consider what the estate's existing machinery can and cannot see. The scaffold-auditor sees an incomplete implementation only where the incompleteness is textually present in a file it scans — a stub body, an empty branch, an abandonment marker. It cannot see the function that was never written at all, because there is no file to scan. The liveness ledger sees an asset that is defined but never called — a strong signal, but one that fires only after the asset exists; it says nothing about the asset that was promised and never defined. SQI sees a test authored but never executed, which is a genuine obligation, but it cannot see the test that was promised in a review and never authored. backlog_autopilot ranks the work that was WRITTEN DOWN as backlog and is blind to the work that was agreed to verbally and never entered. owner_queue surfaces the decisions someone bothered to escalate and is blind to the decision that was deferred with a shrug. Each of these guardians is excellent within its surface and each has the same structural blind spot: they detect artifacts, and an unfinished obligation's defining property is that it has no artifact. The estate has built four instruments that see traces and has never built the one instrument that sees commitments.

1.5 What DAIF-07 is, precisely. DAIF-07 is the lifecycle authority for obligations that already exist as compiled CIR-5 objects. Its input is the obligation IR — typed objects carrying object, owner, origin, priority, state, dependencies, closure definition, required evidence, aging, and abandonment risk, exactly as DAIF-02 Part VII sealed them. Its output is state transitions written back onto those objects, plus a set of hand-offs to the systems that already exist to act on them. It is not a register of record for backlog, not a decision queue, not a stub detector, and not a liveness index. It is the missing governor above all four. The seam is the whole design: CIR-5 is the inert representation, DAIF-07 is the acting runtime, and neither owns both halves. DAIF-07 advances an obligation from state to state, escalates the ones that are aging, enforces the closure gate that separates a claim of completion from a proof of it, and refuses — categorically, with no exception phrase — to let an obligation leave the system without an authored, recorded, attributable transition.

1.6 The non-duplication contract. DAIF-07 WRITES INTO the estate's existing systems; it never forks them. This is not a courtesy, it is the condition under which the dataset was permitted to exist at all, and every Part below holds to it. To owner_queue it hands obligations whose next legal transition requires a human authority — the open decisions, the pending acceptances, the abandonment declarations that only the Owner may sign. To backlog_autopilot it hands the obligations that are actionable work, already ranked by the priority, aging, and abandonment-risk fields the IR carries, so that the prioritizer prioritizes over real closure conditions instead of guessing from prose. To the liveness ledger it hands the question of whether a discharged obligation's output is actually alive and used, closing the loop between "we built it" and "it is real." To the scaffold-auditor it hands nothing and takes everything: the auditor's incomplete-implementation findings are an INPUT, a secondary detection path that feeds obligations into the ledger rather than a rival ledger of its own. To Lazarus and the session-continuity layer it hands the obligations that must survive a context reset, because an obligation that dies at a compaction boundary is the founding failure reproduced at machine speed. To learning-sentinel and UKDL it hands the patterns — the classes of obligation this estate reliably abandons — so that a recurring abandonment becomes a sealed law rather than a recurring accident.

1.7 The invariant this dataset guards. One invariant governs everything that follows, and every mechanism in Parts II through VII is a means of enforcing it: an obligation may be closed by evidence, superseded by a later obligation, expired by a declared deadline, refused by an authority, or abandoned by an authored decision — but it may NEVER simply vanish. Abandonment is a legitimate outcome. The estate is not obliged to do everything it ever contemplated, and a system that forbids abandonment would collapse under the weight of its own good intentions. What the estate is obliged to do is DECLARE the abandonment: to name the obligation, name the authority that dropped it, name the reason, and record the transition, so that the drop is a decision the estate made rather than an accident the estate suffered. The difference between those two is the entire subject of this dataset. An obligation that quietly stops being tracked is not a small hygiene failure; it is the exact failure DAIF-07 exists to make structurally impossible, and any mechanism proposed below that would permit a silent exit is rejected on that ground alone, however convenient it may be.

PART I FINAL LAW. Unfinished work is the one failure class defined by the absence of an event — it does not throw, fail, error, or block, and an estate that abandoned it is exactly as quiet as an estate that completed it — so no text-scanner searching for the markers someone remembered to write can ever close the gap; DAIF-07 is therefore not a fifth scanner but the lifecycle authority the estate never had, consuming CIR-5's compiled obligation objects, governing every commitment from birth to verified closure, writing its verdicts into owner_queue, backlog_autopilot, liveness, and the scaffold-auditor rather than forking them, and enforcing the single invariant that an obligation may be closed, superseded, expired, refused, or explicitly abandoned, but may never leave the estate by silence.

PART II — THE OBLIGATION OBJECT

2.1 Mission. An obligation is a DAIF-01 typed cognitive object of kind obligation, and this Part specifies every slot it carries, the invariant each slot holds, and the precise downstream breakage that follows when the slot is empty. The specification matters because the whole authority rests on it: a lifecycle governor can only govern what its objects can express, and every failure in Parts V through VII is traceable to a field the object failed to carry. The object inherits the eight DAIF-01 fields without re-inventing one of them — Type, Authority, Scope, Strength, Validity, Override-policy, Provenance, Confidence — and adds the obligation-specific slots below. Nothing here is a new vocabulary; it is the type system's vocabulary with the obligation dimension filled in, which is what makes an obligation readable by any consumer that can read a typed unit at all.

2.2 Identity, type, and origin. The identifier is a stable, content-independent handle that survives every rewording of the obligation's text, because an obligation renamed is the same obligation and an estate that keys on prose will double-count it the moment someone rephrases the sentence. Its invariant is uniqueness across the ledger and stability across edits; absent it, de-duplication (Part IV) collapses and the same commitment is tracked three times and closed once. The type is the taxonomic kind from Part III — task, promise, open decision, pending verification, and the rest — and it is load-bearing rather than decorative, because the closure semantics of an obligation are a function of its type: a pending verification closes on an observed run, an open decision closes on an authored choice, and treating them alike produces a closure gate that certifies neither. The origin names the CIR-0 source and the CIR-3 intent or CIR-4 decision from which the obligation arose, so that every obligation can answer why it was incurred; absent origin, an aging obligation cannot be adjudicated, because an authority asked to abandon it cannot tell whether it still matters. The source is the narrower pointer — the exact unit, turn, file, or gate output that produced it — and it is what makes an obligation auditable rather than merely asserted.

2.3 Owner, authority, and scope. The owner is the actor accountable for the obligation's advancement. Its invariant is that it is never null: an ownerless obligation is not an obligation, it is a hope held by the collective, and collectives do not close work. When ownership is absent, the obligation ages without an escalation target, and the escalation path (2.7) has nowhere to send it, so the object rots in the ledger while every guardian correctly reports that nothing is wrong. The authority is distinct from the owner and is the DAIF-01 authority type empowered to authorize the obligation's state transitions — critically, the transitions the owner may NOT authorize alone, which Part VI develops into the separation-of-powers rule. Conflating owner and authority is the most common structural defect in an obligation ledger, and it produces exactly one pathology: producers certifying their own claims. The scope is the DAIF-01 scope dimension — universal, power-pack, repo, project, feature, session, model — and it determines who is obliged and where the obligation is visible; a session-scoped obligation that should have been project-scoped dies at the session boundary, which is precisely the Lazarus hand-off case, and a project-scoped obligation mis-typed as universal spams every repo in the estate until the ledger is ignored.

2.4 Priority, risk, and impact. The priority ranks the obligation against its competitors and is the field backlog_autopilot consumes directly rather than re-deriving. The risk records what is threatened while the obligation remains open — a security exposure, a data-loss window, an incorrect claim standing in a sealed document — and it is deliberately distinct from priority, because priority is about ordering and risk is about consequence, and an estate that fuses them will systematically defer high-risk low-urgency obligations forever, which is the ordinary way institutions accumulate their most dangerous debt. The impact records what closing the obligation unlocks — the obligations that are blocked on it, the assets that become live, the claims that become provable — and it is what allows the queue to rank by leverage rather than by loudness. Absent risk and impact, the ranking degenerates into recency: the newest obligation looks most urgent because it is the one someone just mentioned, and the ninety-day-old obligation that gates four others sinks under the noise. That degeneration is not hypothetical; it is the default behavior of every backlog that carries only priority.

2.5 Dependencies, blockers, and consumers. The dependencies are the other obligations that must close before this one legally can, and their invariant is acyclicity: a dependency cycle among obligations is a deadlock the ledger must detect at write time, because a cycle silently converts three live obligations into three permanently blocked ones and blocked obligations do not scream. The blockers are the non-obligation conditions preventing advancement — an unavailable authority, an unreachable substrate, a missing input, an unanswered question — and they are typed separately from dependencies because a blocker is resolved by an event outside the ledger while a dependency is resolved inside it. The consumers are the systems and units that will read this obligation's output when it closes, and this field is what makes supersession safe: an obligation cannot be dropped without knowing who was relying on it. Absent the consumers field, abandonment becomes a blind act — an authority declares an obligation dead without visibility into the four downstream units that assumed it would live, and the abandonment is recorded honestly while its consequences propagate silently, which reproduces the silent failure one level up.

2.6 State, lifecycle stage, and age. The state is the Part V lifecycle position — proposed, accepted, active, blocked, in-progress, pending-verification, verified-closed, superseded, expired, abandoned-with-record, refused — and its invariant is that it is only ever changed by a legal, authored transition, never by inference and never by a batch process reconciling the ledger to reality. The lifecycle stage is coarser and is what reporting consumes: pending, live, terminal. The age is not one number but a triple — time since birth, time since last authored activity, and number of deferrals — because the three answer different questions and an estate that keeps only the first cannot distinguish an obligation that is being actively worked from one that has been open the same duration and touched once. Part VII builds the entire prediction layer on these three, and every one of them is unrecoverable if not recorded at write time; age cannot be reconstructed by archaeology, because the evidence that would date the obligation is exactly the evidence that decayed.

2.7 Evidence, closure conditions, done-gate, expiry, escalation. The closure condition is the field on which the entire dataset turns, and its law is absolute: an obligation without a closure condition is not an obligation, it is a wish. The reason is mechanical rather than rhetorical — if no condition is named, then no observation can ever discharge it, so the object can never legally leave the live states, and it will either sit open forever (poisoning every aging metric it touches) or be closed by fiat (which is the false closure Part VI exists to detect). The done-gate is the executable form of the closure condition: the specific gate, test, probe, or authored artifact whose observed output constitutes proof, composing SQI's verification axis. The evidence field holds the pointers to what was actually observed, and it is empty until closure is attempted. The expiry is the declared date after which the obligation transitions to expired rather than remaining live indefinitely, and it is the estate's defense against an unbounded ledger. The escalation path names where the obligation goes when its aging thresholds trip — ordinarily owner_queue. The abandonment risk is the Part VII score. The supersession pointer names the obligation that replaced this one, and its invariant is that supersession is a directed edge to a REAL, live obligation: an obligation superseded by nothing is an obligation abandoned under a flattering name, and the ledger must refuse the transition.

2.8 The wish clause, stated as a gate. The estate has one gate at obligation birth and it is this: reject any candidate obligation that cannot name a closure condition. This is uncomfortable in practice, because a great deal of what people commit to in conversation genuinely has no closure condition — "we should keep an eye on that", "let us be careful with the migration" — and the gate's refusal is the honest verdict that those are not commitments, they are sentiments. The failure mode of a permissive gate is worse than the failure mode of a strict one: a ledger padded with unclosable wishes trains its readers to ignore it, and an ignored ledger is functionally identical to no ledger, which returns the estate to the silent failure by a longer road. Where the sentiment is real but the closure condition is genuinely unknown, the correct object is not an obligation with an empty closure field but an obligation of type open-decision whose closure condition IS the naming of the closure condition — a bounded, discharge-able act that converts the wish into either a real obligation or an explicit refusal.

PART II FINAL LAW. An obligation is a DAIF-01 typed object carrying identifier, type, origin, source, owner, authority, scope, priority, risk, impact, dependencies, blockers, consumers, state, lifecycle stage, the age triple, evidence, closure condition, done-gate, expiry, escalation path, abandonment risk, and supersession pointer — every slot load-bearing, every absence producing a specific silent breakage downstream — governed by the cardinal clause that an obligation without a closure condition is not an obligation but a wish, because nothing can ever discharge it, and a ledger padded with unclosable wishes is ignored, and an ignored ledger is indistinguishable from no ledger at all.

PART III — THE OBLIGATION TAXONOMY

3.1 Mission. Type is not decoration. The closure semantics, the done-gate, the escalation path, and the characteristic disappearance mode of an obligation are all functions of its kind, and a ledger that carries one undifferentiated kind called "task" will apply one closure rule to twenty different species of commitment and certify almost none of them correctly. This Part names the kinds, and for each one states three things and only three: how it is born, what its closure actually looks like, and its characteristic way of disappearing unnoticed. That third column is the one no existing guardian in the estate records, and it is the column from which Part VII's prediction layer draws its priors.

3.2 The work kinds. A TASK is born when an actor commits to producing a change, and it closes when the change exists and its done-gate observes it; it disappears when the session that named it ends and no representation carried it, which is the founding case CIR-5 was built to defeat. A DEFERRED WORK item is born when a live obligation is explicitly postponed rather than dropped, and it closes exactly as its underlying kind closes; it disappears by the deferral becoming permanent through repetition — nobody ever decided to drop it, they merely declined to do it four times in a row, and the fourth decline looks exactly like the first. An ABANDONED EXPERIMENT is born when exploratory work is begun and left; it closes either by promotion (the finding is deposited into UKDL or ACIS) or by an authored declaration that the experiment yielded nothing worth keeping — and the second is a legitimate, valuable closure. It disappears by leaving artifacts on disk that nobody dares delete because nobody remembers whether they are load-bearing, which is how an estate accumulates code it is simultaneously afraid to use and afraid to remove. An UNFINISHED DATASET is born the moment a multi-part corpus is planned, and it closes only when every planned Part exists and passes its gates; it disappears by the index being written before the content, so the estate reads a table of contents and believes the work is done.

3.3 The verification kinds. A PENDING VERIFICATION is born whenever a claim is made whose truth has not been observed, and it closes on an observed run of its done-gate — never on an assertion that the gate would pass. A PENDING TEST is born when a code path exists without a test, or a test is authored and never executed; SQI already detects the second and hands it to DAIF-07 as an input. Its closure is a green run reconciled against a known-good baseline, because SQI's law holds here too: a green signal is a hypothesis until reconciled, and the absence of a failure signal is UNVERIFIED, never PROVEN. It disappears by the test being marked skipped for a reason that was legitimate on the day it was written and forgotten by the following week, at which point the suite reports full health while carrying a hole. A MISSING EVIDENCE obligation is born when a closure is attempted with an evidence pointer that does not resolve, and it closes when the evidence is produced or the closure is revoked; it disappears when the ledger accepts the closure anyway because the claim was made by a trusted actor — which is precisely the false closure Part VI names as the second-most-damaging failure in the fabric.

3.4 The decision and acceptance kinds. An OPEN DECISION is born when a choice is identified and not made, and it closes only by an authored CIR-4 decision object carrying question, options, choice, justification, and authority — never by the passage of time, and never by one option quietly becoming the only one anybody remembers. It disappears by drift: the estate behaves as though a decision were made, the behavior calcifies, and the decision object is never written, so the estate can no longer revise its own choice because it cannot find the reason it was made. This is the kind DAIF-07 hands to owner_queue without exception. A PENDING ACCEPTANCE is born when a deliverable is produced and offered but not signed off; it closes on an authority's recorded acceptance or refusal. It disappears through the acceptance-by-exhaustion pattern: nobody signed it, nobody objected, and after three weeks the estate treats silence as consent — which converts an unaccepted deliverable into a load-bearing one without any authority ever having accepted the risk. A CLOSURE OBLIGATION is the recursive kind: the obligation to formally close another obligation, born whenever work is finished but its ledger entry is not transitioned, and it exists because the ledger's own hygiene must itself be an obligation or the ledger will silently drift from reality.

3.5 The delivery kinds. A PENDING ROLLOUT and a PENDING DEPLOYMENT are born when a change is merged but not yet live, and they close only on the observed reality of the live substrate — a health probe, an HTTP response from the real domain, a version string read back from the running system — never on the completion of the pipeline that was supposed to produce it. They disappear when the pipeline reports success and nobody checks the substrate, which is the single most common way an estate believes it has shipped something it has not. A PENDING MIGRATION is born when a system is duplicated in preparation for a cutover, and it closes only when the old path is REMOVED, not when the new path works. Its characteristic disappearance is the permanent dual-path: both systems run, both are half-maintained, the migration is nobody's live obligation, and the estate pays double forever. An UNDOCUMENTED IMPLEMENTATION is born whenever a mechanism ships whose behavior exists only in the code; it closes when the behavior is written into the knowledge layer that consumers actually read. It disappears because it is never uncomfortable — the code works, nothing fails, and the cost is paid entirely by future readers who do not yet exist to complain. An INCOMPLETE REQUIREMENT DOCUMENT is born whenever a specification is begun and left partial, and it closes when every section a downstream consumer depends on is authored; it disappears by the partial document being treated as complete, so its silence on a topic is read as "no requirement" rather than "unwritten requirement."

3.6 The knowledge kinds. An UNRESOLVED CONTRADICTION is born whenever two sealed artifacts in the estate assert incompatible things, and it closes when one is superseded, refined, or scoped so the conflict dissolves — an authored act, always. It disappears by each half being read in isolation forever: no reader ever holds both, so no reader ever perceives the conflict, and the estate ships two mutually exclusive behaviors under one law. A FORGOTTEN CONVERSATION is the kind that most nearly names the founding failure: born whenever a session incurs commitments and ends without compiling them, its closure is the compilation itself, and its disappearance is total and instantaneous — the transcript persists, but its obligations were never lifted out of it, so they are present as words and absent as commitments. OPERATIONAL DEBT is born when a manual step is accepted as temporary, and it closes when the step is automated or formally accepted as permanent; it disappears by becoming a habit, at which point the estate no longer perceives it as debt at all. INSTITUTIONAL DEBT is the same pathology at the knowledge layer: a lesson learned and never promoted into UKDL, a false positive discovered and never written into the known-false-positives register, a pattern observed twice and never sealed. It disappears because learning-sentinel can only compound what was deposited, and an undeposited lesson costs the estate the same amount a second time.

3.7 The subtlest kind, and why it defeats every scanner. The most dangerous obligation in the estate is the one phrased as an offhand aside. "We should probably also handle the empty case." "It would be good to check that on the real substrate at some point." "Someone should look at whether that gate ever actually fires." Every one of those is a real obligation wearing the grammar of a musing. It carries a real object, it has a real closure condition latent inside it, and it has no imperative mood, no marker, no ticket, and no owner. A text-scanner cannot see it, because there is no marker to match and its surface form is indistinguishable from conversational filler. A human reader ten turns later cannot see it, because the sentence read as a thought rather than a commitment even at the moment it was uttered. And its damage is not proportional to its casualness — the offhand aside is disproportionately the vehicle for the thing the speaker half-knew was a real gap and did not want to fully confront, which means the aside carries a systematically HIGHER density of real risk than the formally-filed task does. CIR-5's boundary detection is tuned for exactly this: an obligation-typed unit is recognized in soft phrasing, because the softness of the phrasing is no measure of the reality of the work. DAIF-07 inherits that recognition and adds the governance — an aside compiled into the ledger is given an owner, a closure condition, and an age, and from that moment it is exactly as accountable as anything else in the register.

3.8 What the taxonomy is FOR. The kinds are not a naming exercise. Each kind carries a default done-gate shape, a default escalation target, a default expiry, and a prior on abandonment risk drawn from the estate's own observed disappearance history — pending migrations rot, offhand asides evaporate, pending acceptances are consumed by silence — and those priors are what let Part VII predict abandonment before it happens rather than diagnosing it afterward. A ledger with one kind has no priors, and therefore no prediction, and therefore no ability to intervene before the loss.

PART III FINAL LAW. Obligations come in kinds — task, promise, open decision, pending verification, pending test, pending acceptance, pending rollout, pending deployment, pending migration, unfinished dataset, incomplete requirement document, missing evidence, unresolved contradiction, deferred work, abandoned experiment, forgotten conversation, undocumented implementation, operational debt, institutional debt, and the recursive closure obligation — and each has its own birth, its own closure, its own characteristic way of vanishing unnoticed, and its own prior on abandonment risk; the subtlest and most dangerous is the commitment phrased as an offhand aside, a real obligation wearing the grammar of a musing, invisible to every scanner because there is nothing to match, and carrying a higher density of real risk than the formally-filed task precisely because it is what the speaker half-knew and declined to confront.

PART IV — BIRTH AND DETECTION: CONSUMING CIR-5

4.1 Mission. An obligation ledger is only as good as its intake, and this Part specifies how obligations enter DAIF-07: the primary path through CIR-5, the secondary paths through the estate's existing detectors, the de-duplication rule that reconciles them, and the failure mode of the alternative that DAIF-07 categorically rejects — detection by archaeology.

4.2 The primary path: capture at creation. The obligation is compiled at the moment it is CREATED, not discovered later. This is the single architectural commitment from which every advantage of the design follows. When the DAIF-02 compiler processes a source — a session, a review, a decision, a specification, a gate output — it lifts obligation-typed semantic units out of it into CIR-5 objects, with the owner, origin, closure condition, and required evidence populated from the surrounding context WHILE THAT CONTEXT IS STILL PRESENT. The compiler is standing in the room when the commitment is made. It can see who said it, what they were trying to achieve, what decision it hung from, and what would have to be true for it to be done, because all of that is in the source it is compiling. DAIF-07 then receives an object that is already legible. It never has to reconstruct any of it. The distinction from every scanner in the estate is total: a scanner runs later, over residue, and must infer intent from artifacts; the compiler runs at the moment of commitment, over the source, and records intent from the source itself.

4.3 The secondary paths: existing detectors as inputs, never as rivals. Not every obligation is born in a compilable conversation, and DAIF-07 therefore accepts a second class of intake from the guardians the estate already runs — as INPUTS to its ledger, never as parallel ledgers of their own. The scaffold-auditor's incomplete-implementation findings enter as obligations of the unfinished-implementation kind, carrying the file and line as their source. The liveness ledger's defined-but-never-called assets enter as obligations of the undocumented-or-unwired kind — an asset that exists and is never invoked is either dead work to be declared or unfinished wiring to be closed, and either way it is a live commitment the estate has not settled. SQI's authored-but-never-executed tests enter as pending-test obligations, with the test path as source and a green reconciled run as the done-gate. Existing backlog items enter as tasks, back-filled with the owner and closure condition that the backlog format never demanded. Each of these detectors continues to do exactly what it did before; what changes is that its findings now have somewhere to LIVE — an object with an age, an owner, and a state — rather than being re-derived from scratch on every scan and forgotten between runs. This is the precise sense in which DAIF-07 is an authority above the scanners and not a fifth one beside them.

4.4 De-duplication, composing DAIF-00's ontology. The same real obligation will surface from three sources, and it is one obligation with three references — never three obligations. A promised migration mentioned in a session (CIR-5), evidenced by a dual-path finding from the liveness ledger, and filed as a backlog item is one commitment seen from three angles. DAIF-07 resolves this with DAIF-00's duplicate ontology, applied without modification: the candidates are classified as identical, equivalent, overlapping, or genuinely distinct, and the D2A verdict determines the merge. Identical and equivalent candidates collapse to a single object that RETAINS all three source pointers, because the multiplicity of sources is itself signal — an obligation independently detected by three instruments is more real and more urgent than one detected by one, and discarding the extra references would discard that. Overlapping candidates are split at the seam, producing two obligations with a dependency edge. Genuinely distinct candidates that merely share vocabulary are left alone, and this is the case the naive de-duplicator gets wrong: two pending migrations of two different subsystems are not one obligation, and merging them produces a single object that can never be closed because half of it is done. The invariant is that de-duplication never destroys a source pointer and never merges across scope boundaries.

4.5 The three-reference case, worked. The worked example is worth stating because it is the case a naive intake gets wrong in both directions. A pending migration is mentioned once in a planning session and compiled by CIR-5 with an owner, a cutover condition, and a done-gate. Six weeks later the liveness ledger observes that both the old path and the new path are being invoked, which is the dual-path signature of an unfinished migration. Two weeks after that, an actor files a backlog item titled with different words for the same cutover. A ledger without de-duplication now holds three obligations, and the first one to be closed — almost certainly the backlog item, because it is the youngest and the best-scoped — will make the estate believe the migration is done while the CIR-5 object ages on untouched and the liveness signal keeps firing into a channel nobody reads. A ledger with OVER-eager de-duplication makes the opposite error, merging this migration with an unrelated one that shares half its vocabulary, producing a single object whose closure condition can never be satisfied because half of the merged work belongs to a different subsystem with a different owner. The correct outcome is exactly one obligation, carrying three source pointers, with an abandonment risk ELEVATED by the multiplicity — because a commitment that three independent instruments have now noticed, and that none of them has closed, is a commitment the estate is visibly failing to discharge, and the count of its detections is the measure of how visibly.

4.6 The intake gate. Every candidate entering the ledger passes one gate, stated in Part II and enforced here: it must name a closure condition, an owner, and an origin. A candidate that cannot name a closure condition is refused as a wish, and the refusal is itself recorded, because the estate wants to know how many of its expressed intentions are unclosable — that count is a health metric of the estate's own language. A candidate that cannot name an owner is admitted only in the proposed state and cannot advance past it; ownerlessness is a legal birth condition but an illegal live condition, and the assignment of an owner is the accept transition of Part V. A candidate that cannot name an origin is refused outright, because an obligation whose provenance is unknown cannot be adjudicated later, and an unadjudicable obligation is an eternal one.

4.7 The failure mode of archaeology. The alternative to capture-at-creation is discovery-after-the-fact: let the obligations accumulate in the sources, and periodically send an instrument through the wreckage to find them. This is what every scanner in the estate does, and its failure mode is not a matter of recall percentages — it is structural. By the time the archaeologist arrives, the context that made the obligation LEGIBLE is gone. The session that named the task has been compacted. The person who understood why the migration mattered has moved to another surface. The decision the obligation hung from was never written down, so the obligation now reads as an arbitrary chore whose value nobody can assess. The archaeologist can recover the sentence, and it cannot recover the meaning, and an obligation whose meaning cannot be recovered cannot be prioritized, cannot be adjudicated, and cannot be honestly abandoned — because abandoning it requires knowing what is being given up. So it stays in the ledger, unclosable and un-droppable, aging forever, and it teaches every reader of the ledger that the ledger is full of noise. Archaeology does not merely miss obligations; it poisons the ledger with obligations it half-found, and the poisoning is worse than the missing.

4.8 The context-decay clock. This is why intake latency is a first-class metric of the whole system and not a performance detail. The knowledge required to close an obligation decays from the instant the obligation is born, on a curve set by the volatility of its context: an obligation born inside a session decays at compaction speed, one born inside a decision decays as its authority's memory does, one born inside a gate output decays when the gate's baseline changes. The whole value of consuming CIR-5 is that it moves intake to t=0 on that curve, where decay has not begun. Every hour of intake latency is an irreversible loss of the object's future legibility, and no amount of downstream sophistication recovers it — a superb prioritizer operating over illegible obligations produces a superb ranking of noise.

4.9 What DAIF-07 hands out at intake. Intake is not passive. The moment an obligation is admitted, DAIF-07 performs its routing, which is the non-duplication contract in action: obligations whose next legal transition needs a human authority are handed to owner_queue immediately, at birth, rather than after they have aged into a crisis. Obligations that are actionable work are handed to backlog_autopilot with priority, risk, impact, and age already populated, so the prioritizer ranks over structure rather than guessing from a title. Obligations whose scope extends beyond the current session are registered with Lazarus and the session-continuity layer, so that a compaction, a crash, or a context reset cannot kill them — this hand-off is the mechanical answer to the forgotten-conversation kind, and it is the single highest-leverage integration in the dataset, because it converts the estate's most frequent obligation-death event from fatal to survivable. And the classes themselves — which kinds this estate keeps abandoning — are handed to learning-sentinel for eventual promotion into UKDL, so that a recurring abandonment becomes a sealed law rather than a recurring accident.

PART IV FINAL LAW. Obligations enter DAIF-07 primarily through CIR-5, compiled at the moment of creation while the context that makes them legible is still present, and secondarily through the estate's existing detectors — scaffold-auditor incompleteness, liveness's defined-but-uncalled assets, SQI's unexecuted tests, backlog items — which feed the ledger as inputs rather than rival it as ledgers; identical candidates from multiple sources collapse under DAIF-00's duplicate ontology into ONE obligation with THREE references, never three obligations, and every candidate must name a closure condition, an owner, and an origin to be admitted — because the alternative, detection by archaeology, does not merely miss obligations, it half-finds them after their context has decayed and poisons the ledger with commitments nobody can prioritize, adjudicate, or honestly abandon.

PART V — THE LIFECYCLE STATE MACHINE

5.1 Mission. This Part specifies the states an obligation may occupy, every legal transition between them, the entry and exit conditions of each, the DAIF-01 authority type empowered to authorize each transition, and the evidence each transition demands. The machine is the enforcement surface of the entire dataset: the invariant that an obligation may never leave the estate by silence is not a policy statement, it is a property of a state machine that has no unauthored exit.

5.2 The live states. PROPOSED is the birth state: the obligation has been compiled and admitted but has no owner and no commitment behind it. Entry is by intake (Part IV). Exit is by acceptance or refusal. Any actor may propose; proposal is cheap by design, because the cost of a false proposal is one line in a register and the cost of a missed obligation is the silent failure. ACCEPTED means an owner has been assigned and the estate has committed to the work: entry requires a named owner and a closure condition that the accepting authority has read and endorsed, which is the moment the obligation becomes real. ACTIVE means the obligation is live and eligible for scheduling but not yet begun. IN-PROGRESS means an actor is working it now; entry requires an authored start event, and — crucially — exit by inactivity is NOT a legal transition, which is the defense against the most common decay: an obligation that was picked up, half-done, and dropped remains in-progress, aging visibly, rather than silently reverting to a state that looks untouched. BLOCKED means advancement is prevented by a named dependency or blocker; entry requires the blocker to be NAMED, because "blocked" without a named blocker is a synonym for abandoned. Exit from blocked is by the blocker's resolution or by an authored decision to abandon, and never by the blocker being quietly forgotten. PENDING-VERIFICATION means the work is claimed complete and awaits its closure gate — the state in which false closure is caught, and the subject of Part VI.

5.3 The terminal states. VERIFIED-CLOSED is the only success terminal: the closure condition is satisfied AND the done-gate has been observed to produce the evidence it demands, certified by an authority distinct from the producer. SUPERSEDED means a later obligation subsumes this one; the transition requires a pointer to a REAL, live successor, and the ledger refuses supersession by a non-existent or already-terminal obligation, because supersession-into-the-void is abandonment wearing a respectable name. EXPIRED means the obligation's declared expiry passed without closure; it is terminal but it is LOUD — expiry emits an event to owner_queue, because an obligation the estate declared important enough to bound in time and then let lapse is a signal about the estate, not merely about the obligation. ABANDONED-WITH-RECORD is the legitimate drop: the obligation will not be done, an authority has decided so, and the decision carries a reason, a date, an authority identity, and a notification to every unit in the consumers field. REFUSED is the terminal for a proposal never accepted: the estate declines to take on the work, and the refusal is recorded so that the same proposal re-surfacing in six months is recognized as a re-litigation rather than a discovery.

5.4 The transition table, in flows. proposed -> accepted (authority assigns owner + endorses closure condition) -> active (schedulable) -> in-progress (authored start) -> pending-verification (producer claims completion + submits evidence) -> verified-closed (a DISTINCT certifying authority observes the done-gate). The branches: active or in-progress -> blocked (named blocker) -> back to active on resolution. Any live state -> superseded (pointer to a live successor). Any live state -> abandoned-with-record (authority + reason + consumer notification). Any live state -> expired (expiry date reached, event emitted). proposed -> refused (authority declines). pending-verification -> in-progress (the verification FAILED; the obligation reopens, and the failed closure attempt is retained in the evidence history rather than erased, because a claim of completion that did not survive its gate is durable evidence about the producer, the gate, or both). No other transitions are legal, and in particular there is no transition FROM any terminal state back into a live one: an obligation that must be re-opened is a NEW obligation carrying a pointer to the terminal predecessor, so that the estate can count how often it closes work it later has to redo. That count is one of the truest quality signals it possesses.

5.5 Authority: who may move what. Transition authority composes DAIF-01's authority types directly and is not re-invented here. Proposal is open to any actor, including automated detectors. Acceptance requires an authority whose scope contains the obligation's scope — a session-scoped actor may not accept a universal-scoped obligation, which prevents the estate from silently binding itself estate-wide from inside a single pane. Start and progress are the owner's to authorize. The claim of completion is the producer's. The CERTIFICATION of completion is not, and Part VI develops this at length; the state machine encodes it here as a hard structural rule: the transition pending-verification -> verified-closed requires a certifying authority whose identity is not equal to the producing actor's identity, and the ledger REFUSES the transition when they match. Abandonment authority scales with the obligation's risk field: a low-risk obligation may be abandoned by its owner, and a high-risk one may be abandoned only by an authority at or above the risk's escalation tier — which routes, in practice, to owner_queue, and is the mechanism by which the estate is prevented from quietly dropping the work it is most afraid of.

5.6 The cardinal invariant: no silent exit. Every one of the eleven states has exactly one property in common: leaving it requires an authored, recorded, attributable act. There is no transition triggered by time alone except expiry, and expiry is loud. There is no transition triggered by inactivity, ever. There is no batch reconciliation that closes obligations to make the ledger look healthier. There is no garbage collection of old items. An obligation that has been open for six hundred days and touched by nobody remains open, visibly, at the top of the aging register, because its continued presence is TRUE and a ledger that suppresses uncomfortable truths to keep its own metrics clean has become a public-relations instrument rather than an authority. Abandonment is a legitimate, respected, first-class outcome — the estate SHOULD abandon work it will not do, and an estate that never abandons anything is lying about its capacity. What it may not do is allow the abandonment to HAPPEN rather than declare it. The distinction is the whole dataset: a declared abandonment is a decision, recorded, attributable, and reversible by a later authority who can see the reason; an undeclared one is an accident nobody owns, invisible to every audit, and reversible by nobody, because nobody knows it occurred.

5.7 Failure modes of the machine itself. The first is the state that becomes a landfill: in practice, BLOCKED. An obligation moved to blocked with a vague blocker becomes a permanent resident, because blocked feels like a legitimate state and therefore does not shame anybody. The defense is mechanical — blocked requires a NAMED blocker, blocked obligations age faster in the Part VII score than active ones do, and a blocker that has not been touched in the estate's staleness window escalates the obligation to owner_queue with the blocker itself as the subject. The second is the false in-progress: an obligation someone started and dropped, which reads as healthy activity in every report. The defense is that in-progress with no authored activity for the staleness window is the HIGHEST-risk configuration the abandonment scorer knows, ranked above an untouched active obligation, because it carries the false comfort of appearing worked. The third is supersession abuse, addressed by the live-successor requirement. The fourth is the certifying-authority workaround, where a producer designates a nominal certifier who rubber-stamps; the defense is not structural but evidentiary — the certifier must attach the done-gate's OBSERVED output, and a certification with no evidence pointer is refused by the same gate that refuses a producer's self-closure.

5.8 The adversarial case: the ledger-avoidance instinct. The deepest adversarial pressure on this machine does not come from a malicious actor; it comes from the entirely reasonable instinct to not enter an obligation into a ledger that will hold you accountable for it. If admission is expensive and closure is demanding, actors will avoid admitting obligations at all, and the ledger will be immaculate and empty while the estate abandons work exactly as it always did — the metric will look perfect precisely because the system is being bypassed. This is why intake is compiler-driven rather than actor-driven: obligations are lifted from the source by CIR-5, not filed by a person who must first decide to file them, and the estate's commitments therefore enter the ledger whether or not anyone wanted them to. An obligation register that depends on voluntary entry measures diligence, not obligation.

PART V FINAL LAW. The obligation state machine has eleven states — proposed, accepted, active, blocked, in-progress, pending-verification, verified-closed, superseded, expired, abandoned-with-record, refused — and every transition between them is authored, recorded, and attributable to an authority whose scope contains the obligation's, with certification structurally denied to the producing actor, supersession denied without a live successor, and re-opening denied entirely in favor of a new obligation pointing at its terminal predecessor; the cardinal invariant is that no transition is ever triggered by time or inactivity except a loud expiry, so an obligation may be closed by evidence, superseded, expired, refused, or ABANDONED — abandonment being a legitimate and respected outcome that must be DECLARED and may never merely be allowed to happen, because a declared abandonment is a decision the estate owns and an undeclared one is the exact silent failure this dataset exists to make impossible.

PART VI — CLOSURE AND THE VERIFICATION AUTHORITY

6.1 Mission. This Part answers the hardest question in the fabric: what does it mean for work to actually be DONE? Everything before it is bookkeeping — the object, the taxonomy, the intake, the machine — and all of that bookkeeping is worthless if the terminal state can be reached by assertion. An obligation ledger whose closure gate is weak is not merely useless; it is actively harmful, because it converts undone work into a documented, timestamped, official claim that the work was done, and a false record is worse than no record. The estate's audits will now find the obligation closed and stop looking.

6.2 The two-condition closure gate. Closure requires two independent conditions, both satisfied, and the conjunction is not negotiable. The first is that the obligation's own CLOSURE CONDITION — the one it declared at birth, before anyone knew whether it would be convenient — is satisfied. The second is that the obligation's DONE-GATE has been run and has produced the evidence it demands. These are distinct. The closure condition is semantic: it says what must be TRUE. The done-gate is mechanical: it says what must be OBSERVED for the estate to believe that truth. An obligation can satisfy the second and fail the first — the gate ran green because it tests the wrong thing — and an obligation can satisfy the first and fail the second, which is the ordinary case of work that really was done and whose completion was never proven, and which the estate must treat as UNPROVEN rather than as done, because from the ledger's position the two are indistinguishable and it is not the ledger's job to guess. The closure condition is declared at birth deliberately: a condition authored by the producer at the moment of closure, when the shape of the finished work is already known, will be shaped to fit whatever was actually built, and that is not a gate, it is a formality.

6.3 Composing SQI's verification axis. DAIF-07 does not invent a theory of proof; it composes SQI's, without modification and without re-narration. Three of SQI's laws bind every closure in this ledger. First, a green signal is a HYPOTHESIS until reconciled — a gate that passed proves the gate passed, and whether the gate's passing implies the obligation's truth is a separate claim requiring its own grounding, which is why the closure gate demands the gate's identity and baseline, not merely its exit code. Second, the ABSENCE of a failure signal is UNVERIFIED, never PROVEN — a suite that did not fail because it did not run, a probe that returned nothing because it was never reachable, a lint that emitted no findings because it was pointed at an empty path: each of these produces silence, and silence is the same shape as success, and an estate that reads silence as success has re-created the founding failure inside its own verification layer. Third, reality outranks the artifact: a deployment obligation closes on the observed live substrate, never on the pipeline's report that it deployed, because the pipeline reports its own behavior and the obligation is about the world's.

6.4 The separation of powers. The actor who did the work may NOT be the sole authority that certifies its closure. This is the structural core of the Part, and it is not a matter of trust. A producer certifying a producer's own claim is not corrupt, it is EPISTEMICALLY INCAPABLE: the producer's belief that the work is done is precisely the belief the closure gate exists to test, and asking the holder of that belief to test it produces a test whose result was determined before it ran. Every failure mode of self-certification follows from this and none of them requires bad faith — the producer runs the gate they built, on the case they were thinking of, against the baseline they set, and it passes, and the two edge cases they never considered remain broken and now carry an official certification of correctness. The rule is therefore mechanical, encoded in the Part V transition table: the certifying identity must differ from the producing identity. In practice the certifier is a distinct gate-running authority, an independent review agent, an automated done-gate whose definition predates the work, or the Owner via owner_queue for the highest-risk classes. What it may never be is the producer, however senior, however careful, and however inconvenient the alternative.

6.5 The ranked hierarchy of closure evidence. Not all evidence is equal, and DAIF-07 ranks it explicitly so that a closure can be scored rather than merely accepted. At the top: an OBSERVED REALITY PROBE against the live substrate — the running system answering for itself, a health response from the real domain, a value read back from the live record. Below it: an INDEPENDENT GATE RUN, executed by an authority distinct from the producer, against a baseline authored before the work. Below that: a REPRODUCED RESULT — the producer's gate re-run by a second actor who obtains the same output, which is weaker than an independent gate because it inherits the producer's gate design. Below that: an ARTIFACT INSPECTION — the file exists, the function is defined, the section is written; strong for existence claims and worthless for behavior claims, and the confusion of the two is the scaffold illusion. Below that: a PRODUCER'S GATE RUN, self-executed — real evidence, weakly grounded, admissible only for low-risk obligations. And at the very bottom, the weakest form of closure evidence the estate recognizes: a CLAIM OF COMPLETION. Someone said it was done. It is at the bottom not because people lie but because the claim carries no information about the world — it carries information about the claimant's belief, and the entire reason the obligation had a done-gate is that the claimant's belief was never the thing in question.

6.6 False-closure detection. An obligation is falsely closed when it reached verified-closed without satisfying the gate, and the estate detects it by re-derivation rather than by trust. Three probes run continuously over the closed portion of the ledger. The FIRST resolves every evidence pointer on every closed obligation: a pointer to a file that does not exist, a gate run whose record cannot be found, a probe result with no timestamp — any unresolvable pointer re-opens the obligation as a missing-evidence obligation of the kind Part III names, and the re-opening is loud, because a closure whose evidence has evaporated is worse than an open obligation. The SECOND checks that the done-gate was actually RUN: an obligation closed with a done-gate whose execution record does not appear in the estate's gate history was closed on a promise that the gate WOULD pass, and the difference between "the gate would pass" and "the gate passed" is the entire discipline. The THIRD is the liveness reconciliation, and it is the one no other guardian in the estate could perform: a closed obligation whose output is DEFINED BUT NEVER CALLED is work that was completed and is not real, and DAIF-07 hands that finding straight to the liveness ledger, closing the loop between "we built it" and "it is used." A shipped-and-unused deliverable does not falsify the closure, but it does falsify the IMPACT the obligation claimed, and an estate that never reconciles the two will faithfully complete a great deal of work that nobody wanted.

6.7 The metrics, and their countermetrics. The closure layer reports four numbers. CLOSURE RATE — obligations verified-closed per period — whose Goodhart failure is obvious and severe: it is trivially maximized by closing easy obligations and by lowering closure standards, so it is NEVER reported alone. Its countermetric is CLOSURE EVIDENCE STRENGTH, the median rank on the 6.5 hierarchy across closures in the period, which falls immediately if the estate begins closing on claims. FALSE-CLOSURE RATE — closures re-opened by the 6.6 probes — whose Goodhart failure is that it is minimized by never probing, so its countermetric is PROBE COVERAGE, the fraction of the closed ledger actually re-derived in the period. RE-DO RATE — new obligations pointing at a terminal predecessor — whose Goodhart failure is that it is minimized by refusing to admit re-work as re-work, so its countermetric is the count of re-opened obligations filed as fresh work with no predecessor pointer, which is exactly the evasion. And SELF-CERTIFICATION ATTEMPTS — refused transitions where producer equalled certifier — which is not a failure metric at all but a health signal: a system reporting zero attempts is not virtuous, it is either unused or being bypassed, and the correct interpretation of a suspiciously clean gate is always to check whether the gate is running.

PART VI FINAL LAW. Closure is the conjunction of two independent conditions — the obligation's own closure condition, declared at birth before anyone knew what would be convenient, and the observed output of its done-gate — adjudicated under SQI's verification axis where a green signal is a hypothesis until reconciled and the absence of a failure signal is UNVERIFIED and never PROVEN; the producing actor may never be the sole certifying authority, not from distrust but because the producer's belief that the work is done is precisely the belief under test; and the evidence hierarchy runs from an observed reality probe down through independent gate runs, reproduced results, artifact inspection, and self-run gates to its weakest possible form — a claim of completion, which is last because it carries no information about the world, only about the claimant's belief, and the claimant's belief was never the thing in question.

PART VII — AGING, ABANDONMENT RISK, AND PREDICTION

7.1 Mission. Obligations decay, and the decay is the signal. This Part models it: the dimensions along which an obligation rots, the abandonment-risk score built from them, the countermetrics that keep the score honest, and the longitudinal capability that separates an obligation authority from a register — the ability to say "this obligation has been open one hundred seventy-two days and has been deferred four times," rather than merely "it exists."

7.2 The five decay dimensions. AGE SINCE BIRTH is the crude clock and the weakest of the five in isolation, because a long-lived obligation may be under continuous work. TIME SINCE LAST AUTHORED ACTIVITY is far stronger: it measures neglect rather than duration, and it is the dimension that distinguishes a hard obligation being ground down from an obligation nobody has touched since the day it was born. CONTEXT STALENESS measures the decay of the SOURCE — how far the estate has moved from the state in which the obligation was legible, counted in compactions, sessions closed, sealed decisions superseded, and files rewritten beneath it. DEFERRAL COUNT is the number of times the obligation was explicitly postponed, and it is the single most predictive scalar the ledger holds, for a reason worth stating plainly: an obligation deferred four times has been examined four times by an authority who each time declined to do it, and the honest reading of that pattern is not that the work is pending — it is that the estate has effectively decided against the work and has not had the courage to declare it. The fifth is KNOWLEDGE DECAY: whether the actor or session that understood the obligation still exists. This is the cruelest dimension and the least measurable, and it is the one that converts a merely-old obligation into an unclosable one, because when the last carrier of the obligation's meaning is gone, the work can no longer be done as intended — only re-derived from scratch, at full cost, if anyone still believes it is worth it.

7.3 The abandonment-risk score. The score composes the five dimensions with the Part III kind-prior and the Part V state, and the composition is deliberately NOT a simple sum. Its inputs: normalized time since last authored activity, weighted heaviest; deferral count, weighted next and super-linearly, because the third deferral means far more than the first; context staleness; knowledge decay, entered as a step function rather than a gradient, since the departure of the last carrier is a discontinuity and not a slope; age since birth, weighted lightest; the kind-prior, which encodes what this estate has actually observed about how each obligation kind dies; and a state multiplier, in which the highest-risk configuration in the entire machine is IN-PROGRESS WITH NO ACTIVITY — ranked above an untouched active obligation, because it carries the false comfort of appearing worked and therefore evades exactly the human attention that would have saved it. The score's output is an ordering, not a verdict: DAIF-07 does not abandon obligations, it SURFACES the ones about to be abandoned by accident, so that an authority may abandon them on purpose or resurrect them. Every escalation lands in owner_queue, which is the estate's existing surface for exactly this decision, and DAIF-07 forks nothing to deliver it.

7.4 The Goodhart failure, named precisely. An abandonment-risk score is trivially gamed, and the exploit is so natural that it will occur without any intent to cheat. The estate wants the aggregate risk down. The cheapest way to lower it is to close easy obligations — the fresh, small, well-understood ones — in volume, which lifts the closure rate, lowers the mean risk, and leaves every old, hard, high-risk obligation exactly where it was, rotting, while the register reports steady improvement. This is not a hypothetical adversary; it is the default gradient of any actor optimizing a mean. The countermetrics are therefore structural rather than exhortative. The first is the OLD-TAIL COVERAGE metric: the closure rate computed EXCLUSIVELY over the oldest decile of the live ledger, reported beside the aggregate and never merged into it, so that an estate closing only easy work shows a healthy aggregate against a flat tail and the divergence between the two numbers IS the finding. The second is MAXIMUM AGE, reported alongside the mean, because a mean is a device for hiding a maximum and the maximum is the number that matters. The third is DEFERRAL-COUNT DISTRIBUTION rather than its average: an estate with a fat tail at four-plus deferrals has a class of work it will never do and has not admitted it, and no average will ever show that. The fourth is the DECLARED-ABANDONMENT RATE, which the estate wants to be non-zero: a system that never abandons anything is not disciplined, it is dishonest, and a rising declared-abandonment rate against a falling silent-loss rate is the exact signature of the system working.

7.5 The longitudinal capability. The existing guardians in the estate report EXISTENCE. The scaffold-auditor reports that a stub exists. The liveness ledger reports that an asset is uncalled. backlog_autopilot reports that an item is open. Not one of them can tell you HOW LONG, because none of them holds an object with a birth date — each re-derives its findings from the current state of the disk on every run, and a finding re-derived from scratch has no history and therefore no age. This is the most consequential thing the estate threw away, and it threw it away without noticing: age is the most predictive dimension an obligation possesses, and a scanner that re-computes its world every run is structurally incapable of holding it. DAIF-07 holds an object, and so it can say that this migration has been open one hundred seventy-two days, was deferred in four separate sessions, has had no authored activity in ninety-one of those days, and that both actors who understood the cutover plan are gone. That sentence is not a richer version of "the migration is open." It is a different KIND of statement — it is a prediction, it is actionable by an authority who now has enough to decide, and it is the sentence the estate has never been able to produce about any of its unfinished work.

7.6 Prediction, and the intervention that follows. With the five dimensions and the kind-priors, abandonment becomes forecastable rather than merely diagnosable. The estate learns, from its own observed disappearance history, that a pending migration with two deferrals and a departed carrier closes in under one case in ten; that an offhand aside not touched within one session is effectively dead unless surfaced; that a pending acceptance untouched past the estate's silence window will be consumed by acceptance-by-exhaustion and become load-bearing without anybody accepting the risk. These priors are not decoration — each one names an INTERVENTION POINT, and the intervention is always the same shape: surface the obligation to an authority BEFORE its risk becomes terminal, with its full age triple and its kind-prior attached, so the authority makes a decision instead of suffering an accident. And the prior itself is learnable: the classes of obligation this estate reliably abandons are exactly the patterns learning-sentinel exists to compound, and a class that repeats is promoted into UKDL as a sealed law, at which point the estate's tendency to abandon a given kind of work stops being a statistical tendency and becomes a rule with a gate in front of it.

7.7 What the estate must not conclude. Two temptations follow from a good risk score and both must be refused. The first is AUTO-ABANDONMENT: the score is high, the obligation is clearly dead, so let the system close it and clean the ledger. This is forbidden absolutely, and it is forbidden for the reason the entire dataset exists — an obligation dropped by an automatic threshold is an obligation that left the estate without an authored decision, which is the silent failure re-implemented as a feature, and it is worse than the original because it now carries the estate's own imprimatur. Automation may score, rank, surface, escalate, and predict. Only an authority may abandon. The second temptation is score-worship: treating the abandonment-risk number as the obligation's importance, when it is not — it is the probability of loss, and a low-risk obligation may be far more important than a high-risk one. The ranking DAIF-07 hands to backlog_autopilot composes risk with the priority, risk-of-consequence, and impact fields of Part II, and an estate that ranks purely by abandonment risk will spend its attention on the work most likely to be lost rather than the work most costly to lose. Those are different sets, and confusing them is the last way an obligation authority can fail while every one of its own metrics reports that it is succeeding.

PART VII FINAL LAW. Obligations decay along five dimensions — age since birth, time since last authored activity, staleness of the context that made them legible, deferral count, and the knowledge decay that follows when the actor or session who understood the work is gone — and the abandonment-risk score composed from them, weighted super-linearly on deferrals, stepped on knowledge decay, and multiplied to its maximum by the in-progress-with-no-activity state that carries the false comfort of appearing worked, is guarded against its own Goodhart exploit by an old-tail closure rate reported beside the aggregate, a maximum age reported beside the mean, and a declared-abandonment rate the estate WANTS to be non-zero; the score may surface, rank, escalate, and predict, but it may never abandon, because an obligation dropped by an automatic threshold is the silent failure re-implemented as a feature — and the whole reason the authority can predict at all is that it holds an object with a birth date and can therefore say "open one hundred seventy-two days, deferred four times," which is not a richer form of "it exists" but a different kind of statement entirely, and it is the one the estate's scanners, re-deriving their world from scratch on every run, threw away without ever noticing that age was the most predictive dimension they had.

PART VIII — PRIORITIZATION AND THE OBLIGATION QUEUE

8.1 Mission. Parts II through VII establish that every commitment the estate incurs is held as a typed object with an owner, a closure condition, an age triple, and an abandonment-risk score. They do not establish which of those obligations gets discharged next, and that omission is not academic. An estate holding two hundred live obligations with the capacity to close six a week has, by arithmetic alone, already decided to abandon most of them; the only question still open is whether it decides on purpose or by accident. Prioritization is therefore not a scheduling convenience bolted on top of the authority — it IS the authority's operative act, the mechanism by which an inventory of commitments becomes a sequence of discharges, and every obligation ranked below the week's waterline is an obligation the estate is choosing, this week, not to close. That choice is made whether or not anyone authors it. A ledger with no ranking still has an effective ranking: whatever the actor happened to look at. This Part specifies the dimensions of the ranking, the composition rule that turns them into an order, the hand-off that prevents the ranking from mutating into a second backlog, and the precise mechanism by which a queue optimized for the wrong quantity starves exactly the work the queue exists to protect.

8.2 The six ranking dimensions. RISK is what is threatened while the obligation remains open — the exposure window, the incorrect claim standing in a sealed artifact, the substrate drifting from its declared state — and it is the Part II risk field, consumed and never re-derived. IMPACT is what closing it unlocks: the assets that become live, the claims that become provable, the surface that becomes real. AGE is the Part VII triple, and it enters the ranking not as a proxy for urgency but as a proxy for institutional truth-telling — an obligation open one hundred and seventy-two days is either genuinely important, in which case the estate is failing it, or genuinely dead, in which case the estate is lying about it, and both readings demand attention that a fresh obligation does not. BLOCKING-DEGREE is the count of other live obligations that cannot legally advance until this one closes, computed transitively over the Part IX dependency graph, and it is the leverage dimension: an obligation blocking four others is not one unit of work but five units of unlocked motion. DECAY is the rate at which the knowledge required to close this obligation is expiring — whether the actor who understood it is still present, whether the source that made it legible has been compacted, whether the substrate it targets is being rewritten beneath it — and it is the dimension that makes an obligation URGENT WITHOUT BEING IMPORTANT, because a decaying obligation must be closed soon or it can never be closed as intended at all. COST-TO-CLOSE is the estimated effort to discharge it, and it enters the ranking as a divisor, never as a filter.

8.3 Why naive priority fails. A single scalar labelled priority produces one reliable behavior: urgency crowds out importance, and the loudest obligation wins rather than the most consequential. The mechanism is not laziness. An actor asked to rank a hundred obligations by a single number will anchor on the dimension most available to perception, and the most available dimension is always recency — the obligation someone mentioned this morning has a face, a context, and a person waiting, while the obligation incurred four months ago has none of those, and the actor is not comparing two obligations, they are comparing a vivid one against an abstraction. Loudness is the second attractor: the obligation attached to the actor with the most standing gets ranked above the obligation attached to nobody, which is precisely inverted, because an obligation with an attentive advocate is the obligation LEAST at risk of silent loss. A one-dimensional queue therefore systematically ranks by advocacy and freshness and calls the result priority. The estate has seen the outcome and named it in Part II: high-risk low-urgency obligations are deferred forever, which is the ordinary way any institution accumulates its most dangerous debt. The defense is not a better scalar; it is the refusal to collapse six independent quantities into one before the ranking is computed.

8.4 The composition rule. The queue score is an expected-loss-per-unit-effort quantity, not a weighted sum of tidy factors, and it is composed rather than added because the dimensions are not commensurable. Risk and impact multiply into a consequence term: an obligation with high risk and no impact is a hazard to be neutralized, an obligation with high impact and no risk is an opportunity to be taken, and one with both is the estate's most consequential open commitment. Blocking-degree multiplies the impact term, because leverage is genuinely multiplicative — the obligation is worth its own impact plus the impact it is gating, and the transitive closure is used rather than the direct edge count so that a chain of four is not mistaken for a leaf. Decay enters as an urgency multiplier and is deliberately separated from age: age says how long the estate has been failing, decay says how much longer closing is even possible, and an obligation whose last carrier is leaving next week is urgent today at any age. Cost-to-close divides the whole quantity, which is what makes the score a rate rather than a magnitude — but the division is bounded, and the bound is the entire subject of 8.6, because an unbounded divisor is a machine for preferring trivia. Abandonment risk from Part VII does NOT enter this score as a term. It enters as a separate axis reported beside it, for the reason Part VII's closing clause states: abandonment risk is the probability of loss, not the cost of loss, and a queue that ranks by probability of loss spends the estate's attention on the work most likely to vanish rather than the work most expensive to lose.

8.5 The hand-off, and the line the queue may not cross. The queue does not schedule. It ranks, and it hands the ranked output to backlog_autopilot, which remains the estate's single prioritized work surface. This is the non-duplication contract enforced at its most tempting seam, because a ranked list of actionable obligations IS a backlog in every respect except authorship, and an authority that begins issuing one will discover within a month that actors are working from it, that it and the real backlog have diverged, and that the estate now holds two registers of what to do next and no way to reconcile them. What DAIF-07 contributes is precisely what backlog_autopilot never had: obligations that arrive already carrying an owner, a closure condition, a risk, an impact, an age triple, a blocking-degree, and a decay rate, so that the prioritizer prioritizes over structure instead of inferring intent from a title. The direction of the hand-off is the whole design -> the obligation authority supplies the STRUCTURE of the work, backlog_autopilot supplies the ORDER of the work, and neither owns both halves. Obligations whose next legal transition requires a human authority are not ranked at all; they leave the queue sideways into owner_queue, per Part X, because an obligation waiting on a decision is not work waiting on capacity and ranking it against work is a category error that guarantees it will be perpetually outranked.

8.6 The throughput anti-pattern. A queue optimized for throughput is the failure mode this Part exists to name, and it arrives without malice, as the natural gradient of any actor whose performance is read off the count of closures. Cost-to-close is a divisor. Divide by a small enough number and any obligation rises. The cheap obligations are also, reliably, the fresh, small, well-scoped, well-understood ones — the ones born this week, whose context is intact, whose owner is present, whose closure condition is a single gate run. So a throughput-maximizing queue closes them, in volume, and every aggregate metric improves: closure rate up, mean age down, open count flat or falling, the estate visibly discharging its commitments. And underneath, the old, hard, high-value obligations — the migration nobody wants, the contradiction between two sealed artifacts, the requirement document half-authored two quarters ago — are never touched, because each of them costs twenty times a cheap win and each of them therefore sits, permanently, one rank below whatever fresh trivia arrived this morning. This is not starvation as an edge case. It is starvation as the queue's designed behavior, and the estate that built it will report health continuously while its most consequential debt compounds untouched.

8.7 The countermetric that detects it. The aggregate metrics cannot see this, and the reason is worth stating exactly: every one of them is computed over the OPEN ledger, and the open ledger looks better precisely when the cheap items are removed from it. The detector must therefore be computed over the CLOSED ledger, and it is the age distribution of closed obligations — the ages, at the moment of closure, of everything the estate discharged this period. A healthy estate closes a mix: cheap fresh work, and a steady tail of old, hard, expensive obligations that were finally ground down. A throughput-captured estate closes a spike at low age and NOTHING above it, and that shape is unambiguous. It cannot be faked by closing more work, cannot be improved by working faster, and cannot be hidden by any aggregate — a p95 age at closure of four days against a p95 age of the open ledger of one hundred and ninety days is a complete indictment, delivered in two numbers, of a queue that has been eating candy for a quarter. The second countermetric is the old-tail closure rate of Part VII, computed exclusively over the oldest decile of the live ledger and reported beside the aggregate, never merged into it. The third is a HARD FLOOR rather than a metric: a bounded fraction of each period's closure capacity is reserved for the top of the risk-times-impact ordering regardless of cost, so that the expensive consequential obligation is not competing with trivia on a rate it can never win. A queue with no floor will always be captured, because the arithmetic of the divisor guarantees it.

8.8 Adversarial cases. The dimensions are inputs, and inputs authored by the party the ranking judges are attackable. COST INFLATION: an owner who does not want an obligation ranked highly reports a large cost-to-close, and the obligation sinks — detected by the divergence between estimated and observed cost at closure, tracked per owner, and by the simple rule that a cost estimate never revised across four periods is an assertion, not a measurement. BLOCKING-DEGREE FORGERY: an actor adds a spurious dependency edge to inflate an obligation's leverage — detected by the Part IX dependency audit, which requires every edge to name the closure condition of A that B's closure condition actually consumes, and refuses edges that cannot. DECAY-CLAIMING: everything is urgent because everything's context is allegedly expiring — detected because decay is not self-reported, it is DERIVED, from compaction counts, session closures, actor presence, and substrate churn, and a decay rate an actor can type is not a measurement. And the deepest one, LEDGER AVOIDANCE, already named in Part V: if being ranked is punishing, actors will avoid incurring rankable obligations, and the queue will be short, clean, and irrelevant while the estate abandons work exactly as it always did — which is why intake is compiler-driven and why the queue's health is judged by whether the hard obligations move, never by whether the queue is short.

PART VIII FINAL LAW. Obligations are ranked by risk times impact, multiplied by transitive blocking-degree, multiplied by the decay of the knowledge that makes closure possible at all, divided by a BOUNDED cost-to-close — never by a single scalar called priority, because a one-dimensional queue ranks by advocacy and freshness and then calls the result importance; the ranked output is handed to backlog_autopilot and never becomes a second backlog, and obligations awaiting a human authority leave sideways to owner_queue rather than compete against work for capacity they will always lose; and because an unbounded divisor is a machine for preferring trivia, a throughput-optimized queue will close cheap fresh wins in volume while every aggregate metric improves and the old, hard, consequential obligations rot untouched — a capture invisible to every measure taken over the open ledger and visible immediately in the age distribution of the CLOSED one, which in a captured estate spikes at low age and shows nothing above it.

PART IX — DEPENDENCIES, BLOCKERS, AND OBLIGATION CLUSTERS

9.1 Mission. Every mechanism so far has treated the obligation as an independent unit that can be ranked, worked, and closed on its own terms. It cannot. Obligations are bound to each other by structural relations that determine whether closure is even legally possible, and an authority that models them as a flat list will produce three specific pathologies: it will schedule work that cannot advance, it will freeze work forever behind conditions nobody is watching, and it will spend real effort completing four fifths of a body of work whose value is delivered only at five fifths, thereby converting genuine labor into nothing at all. This Part specifies the three relations — hard dependencies, blockers, and clusters — the invariants each must hold, the two structural pathologies they generate (the deadlock and the orphan blocker), and the change to prioritization that follows from the fact that value is realized at the cluster and not at the item.

9.2 Hard dependencies. A hard dependency is an edge from obligation B to obligation A meaning that B cannot legally transition to verified-closed until A has. It is not a hint, not an ordering preference, and not a note about which is more convenient first — it is a legal constraint the state machine enforces, and an attempted closure of B while A is live is refused by the same gate that refuses a self-certified closure. The edge carries an invariant that gives it teeth and prevents its abuse: the edge must name the specific element of A's closure condition that B's closure condition CONSUMES. A dependency that cannot name what it consumes is not a dependency; it is an intuition about sequence, and the ledger admits it as an ordering preference in the queue rather than as a constraint in the graph. This distinction matters because the two have opposite failure modes — an ordering preference wrongly recorded as a hard dependency freezes B for no reason, while a real dependency recorded as a preference lets B close on top of an unbuilt foundation and produces a verified-closed obligation whose evidence is real and whose value is void. The second invariant is acyclicity, checked at write time rather than by a sweep, for the reason Part II gave: a cycle converts live obligations into permanently blocked ones, and blocked obligations do not scream.

9.3 Blockers. A blocker is a non-obligation condition preventing advancement: an authority who is unavailable, a substrate that is unreachable, an input that has not arrived, a question that has not been answered, an upstream artifact outside the estate's control. It is typed separately from a dependency for a mechanical reason — a dependency is resolved INSIDE the ledger, by the closure of another obligation the estate owns, whereas a blocker is resolved by an event OUTSIDE it, which the ledger can observe but cannot cause. That asymmetry drives everything that follows. A dependency's resolution has an owner, an age, and a queue position; a blocker's resolution has none of those by default, and so a blocker, left alone, is the single most reliable way for an obligation to disappear while remaining formally open. The estate's defense is to give every blocker the properties a dependency has for free: a blocker must name a WATCHER — the actor or probe responsible for observing its resolution — and a RECHECK CADENCE, and a blocker with neither is refused at write time, because "blocked" without a named blocker is a synonym for abandoned and a named blocker without a watcher is a synonym for the same thing spelled at greater length. Blocked obligations age faster in the Part VII score than active ones, and a blocker untouched past the staleness window escalates to owner_queue with the BLOCKER as the subject of the escalation rather than the obligation, because it is the blocker, not the work, that the estate has failed to resolve.

9.4 Clusters. A cluster is a set of obligations that delivers its value only together. It is the relation the estate has never modelled and it is the one that silently converts labor into waste. The canonical shape: a mechanism is planned as five obligations — the engine, the wiring into the dispatch surface, the gate that proves it fires, the knowledge deposit that lets a future actor find it, and the removal of the path it replaces. Close four of the five and the estate has spent the full cost and realized none of the value: an engine that is never dispatched is dead code, a mechanism with no gate is an unverified claim, a wired mechanism whose predecessor path still runs is the permanent dual-path of Part III, and a shipped capability nobody can find is institutional debt. The cluster edge therefore carries a VALUE CONDITION — the statement of what becomes true when every member closes, which is by construction not the conjunction of the members' individual closure conditions but a claim about the world that none of them makes alone. Its invariant is that the value condition must be falsifiable by an observation distinct from the members' done-gates, because a cluster whose value condition is "all five closed" is not a cluster, it is a checklist, and it re-admits precisely the failure it exists to prevent: an estate that certifies five closures and never asks whether the thing they were for actually happened.

9.5 The deadlock. A dependency cycle is a set of obligations each of which is blocked on another member of the set, and it is a total, permanent, silent loss of every obligation in the cycle. Its silence is the point: every member is in a legitimate state, every member has a named dependency, every member's owner can honestly report that they are blocked through no fault of their own, and every audit that inspects any single member finds nothing wrong. The estate's aggregate metrics improve, because blocked obligations are excluded from the actionable queue and therefore do not depress the closure rate. Nothing anywhere emits an event. Detection is not a scan for cycles once a quarter — it is a write-time refusal: the ledger runs the acyclicity check on every dependency-edge insertion and refuses the edge that would close a cycle, surfacing the proposed cycle to the authority who attempted it, because at that instant the actor holds the context needed to resolve it and a quarter later nobody will. Where a cycle is genuinely real — two obligations that truly each require the other, which happens when the seam between them was drawn wrong — the resolution is not a policy exception but a re-compilation: the pair is split at the true seam into three obligations with an acyclic order, or merged into one obligation with a single closure condition. A cycle in the graph is a defect in the decomposition, never a fact about the work.

9.6 The orphan blocker. The subtlest pathology in this Part, and the one no existing guardian in the estate can see: obligation B is blocked on obligation A; A is then closed, superseded, expired, or abandoned-with-record; and the edge from B is never resolved. B is now frozen forever, waiting on an obligation that has left the live states, and it will wait with perfect patience until the ledger is deleted. Every variant is silent. If A was CLOSED, B's owner is waiting for a notification that will never arrive, because nobody registered them to receive it. If A was ABANDONED, B is waiting for something the estate has explicitly decided will never happen, and B's continued existence is a contradiction the estate is carrying without noticing. If A was SUPERSEDED, B is blocked on a dead pointer while its real dependency — A's successor — has no edge to it at all. The Part II consumers field exists exactly for this: every terminal transition of A notifies every unit in A's consumers set, and the dependent obligations are the primary members of that set. The invariant is stated as a refusal: no obligation may reach a terminal state while a live obligation holds an unresolved dependency edge to it. The transition is not blocked — the estate must be allowed to abandon A — but it is not COMPLETE until each dependent B has been transitioned to a state that reflects A's death: unblocked, if A's closure satisfied B's need; re-pointed, if A was superseded; and itself escalated to owner_queue, if A was abandoned, because an obligation whose foundation the estate has decided not to build is an obligation the estate must now decide about, and deciding by leaving it frozen is not deciding.

9.7 Cluster-aware prioritization. The queue of Part VIII ranks items. If value is realized at the cluster, then ranking items is wrong in a way no weighting fixes, because the marginal value of the third obligation in a five-member cluster is precisely zero and its ranking will reflect that honestly, which is how a cluster gets abandoned at eighty percent completion by a queue behaving exactly as designed. The correction is structural: the cluster is the ranked unit. Its consequence term is the value condition's risk and impact, not the sum of its members'; its cost-to-close is the sum of its members' remaining costs, which makes a cluster expensive and therefore vulnerable to the throughput capture of Part VIII, which is why the reserved-capacity floor is enforced over clusters and not only over items. And a cluster that is partially closed carries a SUNK-VALUE flag that raises it: an eighty-percent cluster is not eighty percent of the way to value, it is one hundred percent of the way to waste unless it is finished, and the honest options are exactly two — finish it, or abandon it as a whole with a record, which means declaring that the four closed members are dead work and accepting that publicly. What the estate may not do is leave it at four of five, because that state is a lie told by the closure metric: four obligations verified-closed, one open, ninety-five percent of the effort spent, and nothing at all delivered.

9.8 Metrics and their countermetrics. PARTIAL-CLUSTER COUNT — clusters with at least one closed member and at least one live member — is the direct measure of value-in-suspension, and its Goodhart failure is that it is minimized by declaring fewer clusters, so its countermetric is CLUSTER COVERAGE, the fraction of closed obligations that belonged to any declared cluster, which collapses the moment the estate stops declaring them. BLOCKED DURATION, median and maximum, is the health of the blocker layer, and it is gamed by moving obligations out of blocked into active without resolving anything, so its countermetric is the count of obligations that re-enter blocked within a short window, which is the signature of exactly that laundering. ORPHAN-BLOCKER COUNT should be structurally zero by 9.6's refusal, which means a non-zero reading is not a work item but a defect in the authority itself and is escalated as such. And the deepest countermetric is DEPENDENCY-EDGE JUSTIFICATION RATE — the fraction of hard dependencies that actually name the consumed element of the upstream closure condition — because a graph full of unjustified edges is not a model of the work's structure, it is a model of its owners' anxieties, and an estate that schedules against it will freeze itself for reasons no one can reconstruct.

PART IX FINAL LAW. Obligations are bound by hard dependencies, which are legal constraints that must name the element of the upstream closure condition they consume and are refused at write time if they would close a cycle; by blockers, which are external conditions resolved outside the ledger and are therefore refused unless they name a watcher and a recheck cadence, since a blocker with no watcher is abandonment spelled at greater length; and by clusters, which deliver value only whole, carry a falsifiable value condition distinct from the conjunction of their members' done-gates, and must be ranked and abandoned as units — because a queue that ranks items will correctly assign zero marginal value to the third member of a five-member cluster and thereby abandon it at eighty percent, converting the full cost into no value at all; and no obligation may complete a terminal transition while a live dependent still points at it, because the orphan blocker — a dependent frozen forever behind an upstream that was closed, superseded, or abandoned, with nobody watching and nothing emitting — is the silent failure reproduced inside the very graph built to prevent it.

PART X — ESCALATION AND THE OWNER BOUNDARY

10.1 Mission. Every mechanism in this dataset is an automation, and every automation eventually reaches a state it has no standing to resolve. The design question is not whether that happens but what the system does at the boundary, and there are exactly three possible behaviors: it can decide anyway, which is the estate binding itself by machine fiat; it can do nothing, which is the silent failure wearing a mechanical face; or it can STOP, compose a decision request, and hand it to the authority that may legitimately resolve it. This Part specifies the third: the triggers that fire an escalation, the object an escalation produces, the routing that delivers it into owner_queue, the invariant that separates proposal from disposal, and the anti-pattern by which an escalation layer destroys itself — flooding the authority until the authority becomes the bottleneck, which is simply a slower and more respectable way of not doing the work.

10.2 The five triggers. AUTHORITY EXCEEDED: the obligation's next legal transition requires a DAIF-01 authority type the acting agent does not hold, most commonly the abandonment of a high-risk obligation (Part V scales abandonment authority with the risk field) and the acceptance of an obligation whose scope exceeds the accepting actor's — a session-scoped agent may not bind the estate universally, and the attempt is not an error to be logged but a decision to be escalated. BLOCKED PAST THRESHOLD: an obligation has held the blocked state, with its named blocker unresolved, past the staleness window for its kind, which means the estate has an external condition it has failed to resolve and no actor inside the ledger can cause it to resolve — the escalation's subject is the blocker, per Part IX. ABANDONMENT RISK BOUND CROSSED: the Part VII score has crossed the threshold at which the obligation is, on the estate's own observed priors, about to be lost by accident; the escalation exists precisely so that it may instead be abandoned on purpose or resurrected on purpose, and this trigger is the operative mechanism of Part VII's refusal of auto-abandonment. CONTRADICTION: two live obligations assert incompatible closure conditions — one obligation requires the old path removed, another requires it retained — which the ledger can DETECT but cannot ADJUDICATE, because adjudication means choosing what the estate wants, and no automation is entitled to that. CONTESTED CLOSURE: a closure attempt was made, the certifying authority refused, and the producer disputes the refusal, or the Part VI false-closure probes re-opened an obligation whose owner asserts it was legitimately closed. Each trigger is a state the machine can recognize with certainty and none of them is a state the machine may resolve.

10.3 What an escalation produces. An escalation is not a notification. A notification transfers a feeling of urgency and leaves the recipient to reconstruct everything else, which at the boundary is the whole cost: the authority is being asked to decide, and an authority who must first re-derive the context in order to decide will defer, and the deferral will look exactly like the escalation working. So an escalation produces a typed DECISION REQUEST, and it carries four things and refuses to be composed without them. The OBLIGATION, in full — its identity, kind, owner, origin, closure condition, age triple, dependencies, cluster membership, and the source pointer that lets the authority read the moment it was incurred. The EVIDENCE — what has actually been observed: the gate outputs, the resolved and unresolved pointers, the blocker's watcher's last observation, the failed closure attempts retained per Part V. The OPTIONS — the legal transitions available from the obligation's current state, enumerated by the state machine rather than invented by the composer, each with its consequences: which dependents unblock, which cluster is voided, which consumers must be notified. And the COST OF CONTINUED INACTION — the risk that stands while the decision is not made, the decay clock on the knowledge that would allow the obligation to be closed at all, and the dependents currently frozen behind it. That fourth element is the one that distinguishes a decision request from a request for attention, because it makes the DEFAULT explicit: deferring is itself a choice with a price, and an authority who defers having been shown the price has decided, whereas an authority who defers having been shown only a title has merely been interrupted.

10.4 Routing, and the non-duplication contract. The decision request is written into owner_queue. It is not held in a DAIF-07 escalation register, not surfaced through a new channel, and not delivered by a mechanism this dataset invents, because the estate already has exactly one surface for decisions that require the Owner and a second one would guarantee that neither is complete. What DAIF-07 contributes is the composition — owner_queue's entries have historically arrived as whatever the escalating actor thought to write, and the four-element decision request is a schema that makes them adjudicable. The obligation remains in the ledger, in its current live state, with an ESCALATED marker and a pointer to the decision request, and its age continues to accrue while it waits, because an obligation waiting on an authority is still an open obligation and an estate that pauses the clock during escalation has built itself a place to hide work. Critically, escalation removes the obligation from the Part VIII work queue — it is no longer ranked against actionable work, because it is not actionable, and leaving it ranked guarantees it is perpetually outranked while appearing to be in contention.

10.5 The invariant: the system proposes, the authority disposes. DAIF-07 never abandons an obligation on its own authority. It never marks one closed to clear a queue. It never lets one expire quietly to bring an aging metric back into range. It never resolves a contradiction by picking the newer obligation, never satisfies a blocked obligation by deleting its blocker, and never garbage-collects. Every one of those actions would be a state transition performed to improve the system's own numbers, which is the precise definition of the instrument corrupting its own measurement, and every one of them would be an obligation leaving the estate without an authored decision — the silent failure re-implemented as a feature, and worse than the original because it now carries the estate's imprimatur. What the automation may do is everything up to the decision: detect, score, rank, compose, present, and — this is the part that makes the boundary tolerable — RECOMMEND. A decision request may carry a recommended option with its justification, because an authority handed five options and no analysis is being asked to do the system's work, and an escalation that offloads the analysis along with the decision is not a boundary, it is an abdication. The line is exactly between the recommendation and the transition: the system may say what it believes should happen and why; only the authority may make it happen.

10.6 Escalation flooding. An escalation layer with no economy destroys itself, and the mechanism is arithmetic. Escalate every authority-exceeded transition, every blocker past its window, every risk-bound crossing, and the estate's authority receives forty decision requests a week, of which perhaps three are consequential. The authority cannot triage forty, so the authority triages none, so the queue grows, so the authority begins ignoring the queue, and at that point the estate has an escalation mechanism that fires perfectly, a decision surface that is complete and accurate, and an authority who has stopped reading it — which is functionally identical to having no escalation at all, except that the system's own metrics now report a healthy escalation rate. Flooding is not a failure of the authority's diligence. It is a failure of the escalating system, which has confused surfacing a decision with making one and has discovered that surfacing is cheap. The economy that prevents it has four parts. BATCHING: escalations of the same kind and the same underlying cause are composed into one decision request with N obligations attached, because "abandon these eleven obligations from the migration the estate stopped caring about" is one decision, not eleven. DEDUPLICATION against DAIF-00's ontology: an escalation equivalent to one already open is a reference on the existing request, never a new one. THRESHOLD DISCIPLINE: the risk-bound trigger fires at a bound calibrated against the authority's actual decision capacity, and a bound that produces more requests than the authority can adjudicate is not a conservative bound, it is a broken one. And a BUDGET: a bounded number of escalations per period, which forces the layer to rank its own requests by consequence and to answer, for every request it sends, the question of what it declined to send in order to send this.

10.7 The un-escalated obligation. The symmetric failure is quieter and worse, and the budget of 10.6 is what creates it: an obligation that met a trigger and was not escalated because the budget was spent. If it simply stays live and unranked, the estate has invented a new silent exit — the obligation is not abandoned, not escalated, not queued, and not visible, and it is precisely as lost as if nobody had ever compiled it. The defense is that suppression is itself a recorded transition. An escalation that is deferred by budget is written to the ledger as DEFERRED-ESCALATION with its trigger, its rank, and the period in which it was suppressed, its deferral count increments — feeding the super-linear term of the Part VII abandonment score — and it is the FIRST candidate in the next period's budget. An obligation suppressed three periods running has a deferral count that will, by construction, drive it above every other candidate, which means the budget delays escalation and cannot cancel it. A suppressed escalation that is not recorded is the boundary itself becoming a place where obligations go to die.

10.8 Metrics and countermetrics. ESCALATION RATE, measured against the authority's observed decision throughput rather than in the abstract, since the same number is healthy in one estate and a flood in another. DECISION LATENCY — request composed to authority disposed — whose countermetric is the AGE OF THE OPEN REQUEST QUEUE, because latency computed only over resolved requests is trivially improved by leaving the hard ones unresolved forever. RECOMMENDATION-ACCEPTANCE RATE, which the estate wants HIGH but not total: a rate near one hundred percent means either the analysis is excellent or the authority is rubber-stamping, and the two are indistinguishable from the number alone, so the countermetric is the rate of authority-chosen options that DIVERGED from the recommendation, which is the direct measure of whether the boundary is real. SUPPRESSED-ESCALATION COUNT and its age, which is the direct detector of 10.7. And the one that matters most: the count of obligations that met an escalation trigger and were resolved by the system WITHOUT escalation, which by the invariant of 10.5 must be structurally zero, and any non-zero reading is not a work item but evidence that the authority has begun deciding on the Owner's behalf.

PART X FINAL LAW. The system escalates when an obligation's next transition exceeds the agent's authority, when a blocker outlives its threshold, when abandonment risk crosses its bound, when two obligations contradict, or when a closure is contested — and it escalates by composing a typed decision request carrying the obligation, its evidence, its legal options enumerated by the state machine, and the cost of continued inaction, routed into owner_queue and never into a rival surface; the system may detect, score, rank, present, and RECOMMEND, and it may never transition, because an obligation abandoned by an automatic threshold or closed to clear a queue has left the estate without an authored decision, which is the silent failure re-implemented as a feature and carrying the estate's own imprimatur; and because surfacing is cheap while deciding is not, an escalation layer with no budget, no batching, and no deduplication will flood its authority into inattention and thereby achieve, through a mechanism that reports itself perfectly healthy, exactly the same outcome as having no escalation at all.

PART XI — THE OBLIGATION LEDGER

11.1 Mission. Everything this dataset asserts about an obligation — that it was born on a date, incurred by an actor, endorsed by an authority, deferred four times, blocked twice, claimed complete once and refused, and finally closed against a gate whose output was observed — is an assertion about the PAST, and an assertion about the past is worth exactly as much as the record that carries it. The ledger is that record. It is not a projection of the current state of the obligations; it is the append-only sequence of authored transitions from which the current state is derived, and the direction of that derivation is the whole of this Part. An estate that holds the current state as the truth and the history as a convenience has built a system that cannot bear witness against itself, and a system that cannot bear witness against itself is precisely the system this dataset exists to replace.

11.2 The transition record. The ledger's atom is a transition, and it carries seven fields, each of which is refused if absent. The OBLIGATION IDENTITY, the stable handle of Part II, which is why that handle had to be content-independent: a ledger keyed on prose loses the thread the moment a sentence is rephrased. The FROM-STATE and the TO-STATE, drawn from the eleven of Part V, so that the transition can be validated against the legal transition table at write time and an illegal transition is refused rather than recorded. The AUTHOR — the identity that performed the transition, actor or automation, never a role and never a system name, because "the system closed it" is not attribution, it is the absence of attribution wearing a uniform. The AUTHORITY — the DAIF-01 authority type under which the transition was performed, which is distinct from the author and is what makes the separation of powers auditable rather than merely asserted: a closure whose author equals the producing actor is refused by Part V's gate, and a closure whose AUTHORITY is insufficient for the obligation's risk is refused by the same mechanism at a different rank. The TIMESTAMP, from a monotonic estate clock rather than from the author's own reckoning. The EVIDENCE POINTERS — for closures, the gate identity, its baseline, its observed output; for abandonments, the reason and the consumer notifications; for deferrals, the period and the ranking that lost. And the REASON, in prose, mandatory for every terminal transition and for every deferral, because the estate's ability to reconsider an abandonment in a year depends entirely on being able to read why it was made.

11.3 The append-only law. The ledger is append-only, and the reason is not durability or performance — it is that a ledger you can silently edit cannot bear witness against the party doing the editing. Consider what a mutable ledger permits, without any actor intending harm. An obligation closed on weak evidence is later found to have been falsely closed; the honest record shows a closure, a false-closure probe, a re-opening, and a second closure, and that record is a permanent, legible statement about the estate's closure discipline. A mutable ledger permits the first closure to be quietly amended into the second, and the resulting record shows a single clean closure, and the estate's false-closure rate — the metric that exists to detect precisely this — reads zero. Nobody lied. Someone tidied. The metric is now measuring the tidiness of the record rather than the quality of the work, and every downstream conclusion drawn from it is false in a way no audit can detect, because the audit reads the ledger and the ledger has been made to agree. The append-only law makes this structurally impossible: a mistake is corrected by APPENDING a correcting transition that names the transition it corrects, and the erroneous one remains visible forever. The correcting entry is not a blemish on the record; it IS the record — an estate whose ledger contains no corrections has either never made a mistake or has been erasing them, and the second is overwhelmingly more likely. Deletion is not offered. Retraction is a transition. Amendment is a transition. The only operation the ledger supports is APPEND, and the current state of every obligation is a fold over its transitions, computed on demand and cached, never authored.

11.4 What the ledger enables. Four capabilities follow directly and none of them is available to a system that holds only current state. RECONSTRUCTION: for any obligation, the estate can answer what was promised, when, by whom, under what authority, and what happened to it — which is the question every audit, every post-incident review, and every new actor inheriting a surface actually asks, and which no scanner in the estate can answer at all, because a scanner re-derives its world from disk on every run and therefore has no past. CLOSURE AUDIT: because every closure carries its evidence pointers, the Part VI probes can re-derive the closed portion of the ledger without trusting a single claim, resolving each pointer and re-opening what fails to resolve; a closure whose evidence pointers were never recorded is not auditable and, under the intake gate, was never legal. LONGITUDINAL METRICS: the entire Part VII prediction layer and the entire Part XIII measurement layer are folds over the transition sequence — age at closure is a subtraction between two transitions, deferral count is a count of them, time-in-blocked is a sum over intervals, and not one of these can be reconstructed from a current-state table, because the current-state table has forgotten every state the obligation ever left. And ACCOUNTABILITY: the author and authority fields make it possible to ask whether a particular actor's closures survive the false-closure probes at a lower rate than the estate's mean, which is a question about the estate's discipline that no other instrument it owns can even pose.

11.5 Relationship to the estate's existing ledgers. The obligation ledger registers INTO the estate's ledgers and duplicates none of them, and the seams are specific. To liveness: when an obligation reaches verified-closed, its OUTPUT — the asset, the gate, the mechanism it produced — is registered as a liveness subject, so that the Part VI reconciliation can ask whether the completed work is actually used; the obligation ledger does not track liveness, it ASKS liveness, and liveness answers. To owner_queue: the decision requests of Part X are composed by the ledger and held by owner_queue, and the ledger holds only a pointer and the ESCALATED marker; when the authority disposes, the disposition arrives back as a transition and is appended. To backlog_autopilot: the ranked actionable obligations are handed over per Part VIII, and the backlog's own state is not mirrored here — an obligation's advancement is recorded by transitions, and the backlog's internal scheduling is its own business. To learning-sentinel and UKDL: the ledger is the SOURCE of the patterns — the classes of obligation this estate reliably abandons are a query over the abandoned transitions, and a class that recurs is promoted to a sealed law. The rule that governs every one of these seams is the same: the ledger holds TRANSITIONS, and every other system holds the state that its own domain owns. Where the two would overlap, the ledger yields, because a second copy of another system's truth is a divergence with a start date.

11.6 Retention and compaction without loss. A ledger of transitions grows without bound, and an unbounded ledger becomes slow, then unqueried, then ignored — and an ignored ledger is functionally identical to no ledger, which is the road back to the silent failure by a longer route. Compaction is therefore necessary and is bounded by one absolute rule: compaction may compress the REPRESENTATION of the history and may never remove a TRANSITION. What may be compacted is the derived material — cached current-state projections, resolved evidence blobs that can be re-fetched from their source of record, prose reasons on non-terminal transitions older than the retention window, and the intermediate ranking snapshots of Part VIII. What may never be compacted is the transition sequence itself: every state change, its author, its authority, its timestamp, and — for terminal transitions and deferrals — its reason and evidence pointers, which are the exact fields the audits and the metrics fold over. Terminal obligations may be moved to a cold segment, indexed and queryable but not loaded by default, and the segment boundary is chosen so that the metrics of Part XIII, all of which are computed over closed obligations, remain cheap. The test of any compaction scheme is a single question: after compaction, can the estate still answer what was promised, by whom, when, and what happened to it? If the answer is no for any obligation the ledger ever held, the compaction was a deletion with better manners.

11.7 The bloat failure mode, and the adversarial cases. The bloat failure is not that the ledger becomes large; it is that it becomes UNUSABLE, and unusability has a specific signature: the queries the estate actually runs — the aging register, the old-tail closure rate, the abandonment surface — become slow enough that actors stop running them, and a metric nobody runs is a metric that does not exist. The countermeasure is that the ledger's health is measured by QUERY LATENCY on the estate's actual working queries, not by size, and a latency regression is an obligation of the ledger's own owner. The adversarial cases are three. IDENTITY FORGERY: a transition attributed to an authority that did not perform it, which defeats the separation of powers entirely and is the reason the author field is bound to the acting identity by the harness rather than supplied as a parameter by the caller. TIMESTAMP MANIPULATION: an obligation's age is the most predictive field it holds, and an age that can be typed is an age that will be typed, which is why the timestamp is taken from the estate's clock at append time and a transition claiming a past timestamp is refused. And CORRECTION ABUSE: a party who cannot delete a transition can still bury it under a flood of correcting entries, so the correction transition is a distinct type, is counted, and a correction rate above the estate's baseline is itself a signal — an actor whose transitions require frequent correction is either working in a genuinely uncertain surface, which is worth knowing, or is using correction as an eraser, which is worth knowing more.

PART XI FINAL LAW. The obligation ledger is the append-only sequence of authored transitions — identity, from-state, to-state, author, authority, timestamp, evidence pointers, and reason — from which the current state of every obligation is DERIVED and never authored, and it is append-only for one reason that admits no exception: a ledger you can silently edit cannot bear witness against the party editing it, so a falsely-closed obligation that is quietly amended into a clean closure drives the false-closure rate to zero without anybody lying, and the metric then measures the tidiness of the record rather than the quality of the work; a mistake is therefore corrected by APPENDING a correcting transition that names what it corrects, an estate whose ledger holds no corrections has been erasing them, compaction may compress the representation and never remove a transition, and the ledger registers into liveness, owner_queue, backlog_autopilot, and UKDL rather than duplicating any of them — because a ledger that grows unusable is a ledger nobody queries, and a ledger nobody queries is no ledger at all.

PART XII — SURVIVING THE CONTEXT RESET

12.1 Mission. The obligation's hardest test is not closure. It is survival across the boundary where the estate's working memory ends: a session closes, a context is compacted, a process crashes, a pane dies. On the other side of that boundary stands an actor — human or agent — who was not present when the obligation was incurred, who cannot read the transcript that produced it, and who will discharge it only if the obligation itself carries everything required to discharge it. This is where the forgotten-conversation kind of Part III is either defeated or wins, and it is where obligation fidelity, ruled BINARY by DAIF-03 Part V, is actually tested: every open obligation survives the reset, or the compilation failed. There is no partial credit and there is no averaging, because the obligations that fail to survive are not a random sample — they are, systematically, the ones whose context was richest and therefore hardest to compile, which is to say the ones that mattered most.

12.2 The three reset events, which are not the same. A SESSION END is orderly and announced: the boundary is known in advance, a hook can fire, and the estate has a chance to compile. A COMPACTION is orderly and destructive: the session continues, the actor's identity persists, and the SOURCE — the turns in which the obligations were incurred — is summarized away beneath them, which means an obligation compiled at t=0 survives and an obligation still latent in the prose does not, and no announcement is made about which was which. A CRASH is neither orderly nor announced: nothing fires, no hook runs, and whatever was written to disk before the crash is what exists. These three demand different defenses and are routinely conflated. Defending only against the session end produces a system that dies on the crash. Defending against the crash — by writing the obligation to durable ground at the instant it is compiled, before anything else happens — subsumes the other two, and this is the design: obligation durability is not a shutdown ritual, it is a birth ritual. Part IV's capture-at-creation is therefore not merely an argument about context decay; it is the crash defense, because an obligation that reaches the ledger at t=0 survives an event that gives no warning, and an obligation that would have been compiled at session end does not survive it at all.

12.3 The resumption context. An obligation that survives as an identifier and a title has not survived. This must be stated plainly because it is the failure the estate is most likely to congratulate itself for avoiding: the ledger persisted, the entry is there, the count is right, the fidelity gate reports one hundred percent — and the actor on the far side of the reset reads "finish the migration cutover" and has no idea what was being cut over, from what to what, why, what would prove it done, what it depends on, or where the half-finished evidence lives. That obligation has not survived. It has become a HAUNTING: a name that generates guilt without generating work, that cannot be closed because nobody knows what closure means, and that cannot be honestly abandoned either, because abandoning it requires knowing what is being given up and nobody does. It will sit in the ledger forever, aging, poisoning every metric it touches and teaching every reader that the ledger is full of noise — which is the precise poisoning Part IV attributed to archaeology, reproduced here by a system that thought it was doing the opposite. The obligation must therefore carry its own resumption context, and the content of that context is fixed by what closure actually requires: WHAT it is, in a sentence that presumes no shared session; WHY it was incurred, as a pointer to the CIR-3 intent or CIR-4 decision it hangs from; WHAT CLOSES IT, the closure condition and the done-gate, which the obligation has held since birth; WHAT IT DEPENDS ON, the dependency edges, the blockers and their watchers, and its cluster membership, because an obligation resumed outside its cluster will be closed alone and deliver nothing; and WHERE THE EVIDENCE LIVES, the pointers to the partial work already done, without which the resuming actor redoes it.

12.4 Composing Lazarus and the Mission Pack. Neither of these mechanisms is built here. The estate already holds the session-continuity layer that survives the boundary, and it already holds DAIF-08's Mission Pack, which assembles the context an actor needs to do a piece of work. DAIF-07's contribution is to make the obligation a first-class MEMBER of both. At intake, per Part IV, every obligation whose scope exceeds the current session is registered with the continuity layer, which means the obligation is not something the continuity layer must discover in a transcript — it is something it is handed, typed, at t=0. At resumption, the open obligations for the surface being resumed are assembled into the Mission Pack alongside the rest of the actor's context, which is what converts "there are eleven open obligations somewhere in a ledger" into "here are the three open obligations on the thing you are about to touch, with their closure conditions and their evidence." The distinction is total. An obligation the resuming actor must go looking for is an obligation that will be found only by an actor who already suspected it existed, and the entire failure class begins with nobody suspecting.

12.5 The binary gate. DAIF-03 Part V rules obligation fidelity BINARY, and this Part is where that ruling is executed. The gate is stated as an equality and admits no tolerance: the set of obligations open before the reset equals the set of obligations open after it, member for member, with every field intact. Not ninety-nine percent. Not "the important ones." The reason a percentage is refused is the sampling argument of 12.1 — losses are not uniformly distributed across the population, they are concentrated in the obligations whose compilation was hardest, and a ninety-five-percent fidelity budget is therefore not a five-percent loss of typical obligations but a near-total loss of the atypical ones, which is exactly inverted from what any estate would choose if it could see what it was choosing. The gate's second clause is the one that catches the haunting: survival is not measured by identity count. It is measured by CLOSEABILITY — for each surviving obligation, the resumption context must be non-empty in all five of its elements, and an obligation that survives with a name and an empty closure condition is counted as LOST, loudly, by the same gate, because it is lost, and counting it as survived is the estate lying to itself with its own instrument.

12.6 The survival drill. A gate never observed to refuse is indistinguishable from an inert one, so the fidelity claim is not asserted, it is DRILLED. The drill is adversarial by construction: take a live surface with open obligations, snapshot the ledger's open set with full fields, kill the session without warning — no orderly close, no hook, no compilation opportunity — resume on the far side with only what is durable, and compare. The comparison is not on counts, which any implementation passes trivially, but on the five resumption elements per obligation, and it is scored by the only test that matters: can an actor who was NOT present close this obligation using only what survived? The drill's finding is always the same shape and always instructive — the obligations that survive intact are the ones the compiler lifted at t=0, and the ones that arrive as hauntings are the ones that were still latent in the prose when the boundary hit, which is the empirical proof of Part IV's architectural claim, obtained by killing something rather than by asserting a design. An estate that has never run this drill does not know its fidelity; it knows its intention.

12.7 Failure modes at the boundary. MIS-SCOPING is the most common and the quietest: an obligation typed session-scoped that should have been project-scoped dies legitimately at the boundary, and every gate reports correct behavior, because a session-scoped obligation is SUPPOSED to end with its session. The defense is that scope at intake is derived from the obligation's closure condition and consumers rather than from the surface that happened to birth it — an obligation whose closure requires work on a durable substrate is not session-scoped no matter where it was uttered, and the intake gate refuses the mismatch. POINTER ROT: the resumption context is a set of pointers, and pointers into a session that no longer exists resolve to nothing, so evidence pointers must resolve into durable ground — the ledger, the repository, the gate history — and never into a transcript that compaction may summarize away. CONTEXT ATROPHY: the resumption context is written at birth and never refreshed, so an obligation resumed after four months carries a description of a substrate that has since been rewritten beneath it, which is the DAIF-21 invalidation surface and the reason obligations are among the subjects reality-sync must invalidate. And the deepest one, RESUMPTION WITHOUT PRIORITIZATION: all eleven obligations survive perfectly, are all handed to the resuming actor at once, and the actor — facing a wall of undifferentiated commitments with no ranking and no cluster structure — reads none of them. Perfect fidelity delivered without the Part VIII ranking is a fidelity that will be ignored, and being ignored is how the founding failure was always achieved.

PART XII FINAL LAW. An obligation must survive the session end, the compaction, and the crash — three different events demanding three different defenses, subsumed by exactly one: durability is a BIRTH ritual and never a shutdown ritual, because an obligation written to durable ground at the instant it is compiled survives the event that gives no warning while an obligation awaiting a session-end compilation does not survive it at all; and survival is not the persistence of a name but the persistence of CLOSEABILITY, so the obligation carries its own resumption context — what it is, why it was incurred, what closes it, what it depends on, and where its partial evidence lives — assembled into the Mission Pack of DAIF-08 and registered with the session-continuity layer at intake rather than discovered in a transcript afterward, and gated BINARY per DAIF-03 Part V, because losses at the boundary are never a uniform sample but concentrate precisely in the obligations whose context was richest; an obligation that crosses the boundary as a name and loses the context needed to close it has not survived — it has become a haunting, a debt that cannot be discharged because nobody knows what discharging it would mean and cannot be honestly abandoned because nobody knows what would be given up.

PART XIII — METRICS AND ANTI-GOODHART

13.1 Mission. This Part specifies what the obligation authority measures about itself, and it does so under a discipline the estate has already sealed and will not relax here: CO-12's Telemetry-Before-Claims, which holds that a claim without a measured triple — the instrument, the observed value, and the moment of observation — is an opinion wearing the costume of a fact. Every metric below therefore names its data source, and every metric below names its countermetric, because a metric with no countermetric is a target, and a target is a thing an intelligent system optimizes rather than reports. The Part closes on the gaming risk that dominates all the others and that no amount of careful weighting can mitigate, because it attacks the measurement's denominator rather than its numerator.

13.2 The stock metrics. OPEN-OBLIGATION COUNT, decomposed by kind and by age bucket, is the estate's inventory of outstanding commitments, and it is reported as a distribution and never as a scalar, because a scalar count is the least informative number the ledger can produce: an estate with forty fresh tasks and an estate with forty ninety-day-old contradictions read identically, and they are not remotely the same estate. Its source is a fold over the ledger's live states. Its Goodhart failure is that it is minimized by not opening obligations, which is 13.7. AGE DISTRIBUTION OF THE OPEN LEDGER — median, p95, and maximum — is the direct measure of the estate's failure to discharge, and the maximum is reported always, never absorbed into the mean, because a mean is a device for hiding a maximum and the maximum is the obligation the estate is most ashamed of. BLOCKED-OBLIGATION DURATION, median and maximum, measures the health of the Part IX blocker layer; its countermetric is the re-entry rate into the blocked state within a short window, which is the signature of an obligation being laundered out of blocked without its blocker being resolved.

13.3 The flow metrics. CLOSURE RATE — verified-closed transitions per period — is the metric the estate most wants to report and the one it must least trust, because it is trivially maximized in two ways that require no additional work: closing cheap obligations preferentially, and lowering the standard of closure. Its first countermetric, from Part VI, is CLOSURE EVIDENCE STRENGTH, the median rank on the six-level evidence hierarchy across the period's closures, which falls the instant the estate begins closing on claims rather than on observed gate outputs. Its second, from Part VIII, is the AGE DISTRIBUTION OF CLOSED OBLIGATIONS, and this is the countermetric the whole corpus turns on: a healthy estate closes a mix of fresh and old work, a throughput-captured estate closes a spike at low age and nothing above it, and the shape is unfakeable — it cannot be improved by working faster, cannot be hidden by any aggregate over the open ledger, and requires no interpretation. MEDIAN AND P95 AGE AT CLOSURE are the scalar summaries of that distribution, and the p95 is the one that carries the signal; a median of six days with a p95 of eleven days is not a fast estate, it is an estate that has closed nothing hard in a quarter.

13.4 The integrity metrics. ABANDONMENT RATE is reported as two numbers that must never be summed. DECLARED ABANDONMENT — obligations transitioned to abandoned-with-record by an authority — is a number the estate WANTS to be non-zero, because an estate that never abandons anything is not disciplined, it is lying about its capacity, and a rising declared rate against a falling silent rate is the exact signature of the authority working. SILENT ABANDONMENT — obligations that left the live states without an authored transition — must be ZERO BY CONSTRUCTION, not by effort, because the Part V state machine has no unauthored exit and the Part XI ledger has no deletion; a non-zero reading here is therefore never a work item and never a target for improvement, it is evidence that the authority itself has a defect, and it is escalated as such. FALSE-CLOSURE RATE — closures re-opened by the Part VI probes — is the measure of closure honesty, and its Goodhart failure is that it is minimized by not probing, so its countermetric is PROBE COVERAGE, the fraction of the closed ledger actually re-derived in the period; a false-closure rate of zero against a probe coverage of four percent is not a clean estate, it is an unexamined one.

13.5 The boundary and authority metrics. OBLIGATION-SURVIVAL RATE across context resets is the Part XII gate, and it is BINARY, so it is not really a rate: it is a pass or a fail, and it is measured by the adversarial drill rather than inferred from a count, with the closeability clause enforced — an obligation surviving as a name with an empty resumption context counts as LOST. ESCALATION RATE is measured against the authority's observed decision throughput and never in the abstract, and it is bracketed on both sides: too low means the system is deciding things it has no standing to decide, which the zero-tolerance metric of Part X detects directly; too high means flooding, which is detected not by the escalation count but by DECISION LATENCY and the age of the open request queue. RECOMMENDATION-DIVERGENCE RATE — how often the authority chose an option other than the recommended one — is the direct measure of whether the Owner boundary is a real boundary or a rubber stamp, and an estate reporting zero divergence has not achieved perfect analysis; it has achieved an authority who has stopped reading.

13.6 The measured triple. Not one of these numbers is admissible as a claim without its triple, and the triple's third element is the one estates skip. The INSTRUMENT: which fold, over which segment of the ledger, with which state filter — because "closure rate" computed over a segment that excludes the cold terminal region is a different number wearing the same name, and two teams reporting it will disagree without ever discovering why. The OBSERVED VALUE, which is the number itself and the least interesting element. And the MOMENT: the period boundaries and the ledger's version at computation, because every metric here is a fold over an append-only sequence and therefore every metric here CHANGES when a correcting transition is appended — which means a metric quoted without its moment is unreproducible, and an unreproducible metric cannot be argued with, which is precisely why it is quoted. A dashboard that reports the numbers of 13.2 through 13.5 with no triples is not telemetry; it is a set of assertions with numerals in them.

13.7 The central gaming risk, stated bluntly. Every single metric in this Part improves if the system simply DETECTS FEWER OBLIGATIONS. Open count falls. Age distribution improves, because the obligations that would have aged were never born. Closure rate rises, because the denominator shrank faster than the numerator. Abandonment rate falls. Blocked duration falls. Escalation rate falls, and the authority is delighted. Survival rate rises trivially, because fewer obligations must survive. The estate reports the best quarter in its history, and the actual quantity of unfinished, unowned, unclosed work is precisely unchanged — every commitment that would have been detected and would have shown up as a number is instead exactly where it was before this dataset was written: latent in a transcript, silent, aging, and invisible. Detection starvation is not one failure mode among the others. It is the failure mode toward which every other metric in this Part actively pushes, and any estate that optimizes this dashboard without defending against it will arrive at it by gradient descent, with no bad actor anywhere in the loop.

13.8 The absolute, and the ratio law. The defense is that DETECTION COVERAGE is gated as an ABSOLUTE and never as a ratio, and this composes DAIF-03 Part XV's ratio law without amendment: a ratio is satisfied by shrinking its denominator, so a coverage ratio — obligations detected over obligations present — is satisfiable by detecting fewer and by counting fewer as present, and it is satisfied MOST easily by the system that has stopped looking. The gates are therefore stated in absolutes with both terms bounded. The count of sources compiled per period has a floor, and a fall in compiled sources is a defect of the intake path regardless of what the obligation numbers do. The count of obligations detected per source has a floor calibrated against the estate's own observed history, and a source that yields zero obligations is not a clean source, it is an UNEXAMINED one, and it is flagged. The secondary detectors of Part IV — scaffold-auditor, liveness, SQI — each carry an independent detection count, and a divergence between the compiler's yield and theirs is a signal about the compiler, because three instruments that disagree about how much unfinished work exists have told the estate something no one of them could. And the deepest gate is adversarial and absolute: a KNOWN-OBLIGATION SEEDING drill, in which obligations of known kind and phrasing — including the offhand asides of Part III, which are the hardest and the most consequential — are planted in real sources and the intake path is scored on how many it lifts. Recall against a known set is the only measurement of detection that cannot be gamed by detecting less, because the denominator is authored by the adversary rather than by the system under test, and a coverage number whose denominator the system itself supplies is not a measurement at all.

PART XIII FINAL LAW. The obligation authority measures open count by kind and age, closure rate, median and p95 age at closure, the age distribution of CLOSED obligations, declared abandonment against a silent-abandonment rate that must be ZERO BY CONSTRUCTION rather than by effort, false-closure rate against probe coverage, binary survival across context resets, escalation rate against decision latency, and blocked duration against blocked re-entry — each with its instrument, its observed value, and its moment, per CO-12, because a metric without its triple is an assertion with a numeral in it; and every one of these numbers improves if the system simply detects fewer obligations, which is the gradient every dashboard silently applies, so detection coverage may NEVER be gated as a ratio — a ratio is satisfied by shrinking its denominator, and it is satisfied most easily of all by the instrument that has stopped looking — but only as an absolute with both terms floored, proved by recall against a seeded set of known obligations whose denominator is authored by the adversary and not by the system under test.

PART XIV — FAILURE MODES AND ADVERSARIAL CASES

14.1 Mission. An authority built to prevent a failure will, unless it is designed against itself, reproduce that failure inside its own machinery — with better paperwork, and therefore with less chance of being caught. This Part enumerates the ways DAIF-07 fails, and for each one it names the detection and the countermeasure, every countermeasure having already been specified in an earlier Part, because a failure mode whose defense must be invented here is a failure mode the corpus did not actually design against. The organizing observation is that all seven failures below share one property: each of them makes the system's own metrics IMPROVE. Not one of them announces itself. That is not a coincidence — it is the same structural fact this dataset opened on, applied one level up, because a system whose purpose is to detect the absence of events is a system whose own failures are also absences.

14.2 Silent abandonment through the back door. The founding failure re-enters not by an obligation being dropped from the ledger — Part V's state machine and Part XI's append-only law make that impossible — but by an obligation NEVER BEING BORN. A commitment incurred in a source that the compiler did not process, or processed and did not lift, has no identity, no age, and no state, and therefore cannot be detected as missing by any instrument that reads the ledger, because the ledger is complete and consistent and simply does not contain it. This is the deepest failure in the dataset and it is the one the ledger is constitutionally blind to: every gate DAIF-07 owns operates on obligations that exist, and this obligation does not. Detection cannot come from inside the ledger and must come from outside it: the seeded-recall drill of Part XIII, whose denominator is authored adversarially; the cross-instrument divergence between the compiler's yield and the independent yields of scaffold-auditor, liveness, and SQI; and the source-coverage floor, which flags a source that yielded zero obligations as unexamined rather than clean. The countermeasure is Part IV's architecture — intake at t=0, from the source, by the compiler rather than by an actor who must first decide to file — and its limit is honestly stated: an obligation the compiler cannot recognize is an obligation that does not exist, and the estate's only defense is to keep measuring the compiler's recall against work it did not itself choose.

14.3 Closure theater. An obligation is marked verified-closed to clear a queue rather than because the work is done. It is the second-most-damaging failure in the fabric because it does not merely leave work undone — it converts undone work into a documented, timestamped, official claim that the work was done, and every future audit will find the obligation closed and stop looking. It is not principally a failure of honesty; it is the natural product of a system that rewards a closure count and accepts a claim as evidence. Detection is the Part VI probe triad, run continuously over the closed segment: resolve every evidence pointer and re-open what fails to resolve; verify that the done-gate actually RAN, since a closure recorded against a gate with no execution record was closed on a promise that the gate WOULD pass; and reconcile against liveness, because a closed obligation whose output is defined and never called is work that was completed and is not real. The countermeasures are the separation of powers of Part VI — the certifying identity may never equal the producing identity, not from distrust but because the producer's belief that the work is done is precisely the belief under test — and the closure-evidence-strength countermetric, which falls immediately when an estate begins closing on claims.

14.4 Detection starvation. Fewer obligations are detected, so every metric improves, so the system reports health while the work rots exactly where it always did. This is Part XIII's central risk and it deserves its place here because it is the failure that a well-run estate arrives at by DOING EVERYTHING RIGHT — optimizing a dashboard, following the gradient, celebrating the improvement. There is no adversary. There is no negligence. There is only a measurement whose easiest satisfaction is the abolition of the thing it measures. Detection is the seeded-recall drill and the multi-instrument divergence of 14.2; the countermeasure is the ratio law composed from DAIF-03 Part XV — coverage is an absolute with both terms floored, never a ratio, because a ratio is satisfied by shrinking its denominator — and the sharpest single indicator, worth naming on its own, is a ledger whose open count is FALLING while the estate's output is unchanged: the work did not get done, it stopped being counted.

14.5 Ledger bloat leading to disuse. The ledger grows, the queries slow, actors stop running them, and the metrics that would have caught every other failure in this Part are no longer computed. The estate now has a perfect, complete, append-only record of every obligation it ever incurred, and nobody reads it, which is a state indistinguishable from having no ledger at all except that it cost more. Detection is not size — size is not the disease — but QUERY LATENCY on the estate's actual working queries, and the leading indicator is a query-run count that falls: the aging register consulted weekly, then monthly, then not. The countermeasure is Part XI's compaction law, which permits compressing the representation and forbids removing a transition, plus the cold-segment boundary drawn so that the metrics of Part XIII — all of which fold over closed obligations — stay cheap. The trap is the obvious fix: an estate suffering bloat will be tempted to prune old terminal obligations, which improves latency, destroys the age distribution of closed obligations, and thereby blinds itself to the throughput capture of 14.8 at the exact moment it is most likely to be suffering from it.

14.6 Escalation flooding. Escalate everything and the authority becomes the bottleneck, which is a slower and more respectable way of not doing the work. The system's own numbers look excellent: escalation rate high, coverage complete, every boundary respected. The authority reads none of it. Detection is decision latency plus the age of the open request queue, never the escalation count, and the countermeasures are Part X's four-part economy: batching by common cause, deduplication against DAIF-00's ontology, thresholds calibrated to the authority's observed decision capacity rather than to an abstract notion of prudence, and a bounded per-period budget that forces the layer to rank its own requests. And the symmetric failure must be watched with equal care, because the budget creates it: an escalation suppressed and not RECORDED as a deferred-escalation transition is a new silent exit, invented by the very mechanism built to close the old one, which is why suppression increments the deferral count, feeds the super-linear term of the Part VII score, and makes the suppressed obligation the first candidate of the next period.

14.7 The obligation that outlived its purpose. It is open. It is real. It was correctly compiled, correctly owned, correctly aged. And it is no longer worth closing — the surface it targeted was replaced, the decision it hung from was reversed, the risk it mitigated no longer exists. This is not a defect of the obligation; it is the ordinary consequence of an estate that changes. The defect is what the estate does with it, which is nothing: it stays open, forever, because closing it would be false and abandoning it feels like an admission, and so it ages in the ledger, inflating every age metric, occupying a rank it does not deserve, and teaching every reader that the ledger contains work nobody intends to do — which is the precise mechanism by which a ledger becomes noise and a noisy ledger becomes an ignored one. The discipline is stated as a positive obligation on the estate: NO-LONGER-WORTH-DOING is an OUTCOME, and it must be DECLARED and RECORDED — an abandoned-with-record transition, carrying the authority, the date, the reason, and the notification to every unit in the consumers field per Part V — and never left open as a lie of omission. Detection is the Part VII abandonment-risk score, which is designed for exactly this: a high score is not a prediction that the obligation will be lost, it is an invitation to an authority to lose it ON PURPOSE. The countermeasure is Part X's escalation, and the metric that proves the estate has the courage to use it is the declared-abandonment rate, which the estate WANTS to be non-zero, because an estate that never declares an obligation dead is not a disciplined estate — it is an estate whose ledger is a fiction it has agreed not to examine.

14.8 Prioritization capture. Cheap wins forever. The queue divides by cost-to-close, the divisor is unbounded, and the fresh, small, well-understood obligations therefore rise indefinitely above the old, hard, consequential ones — closure rate up, mean age down, aggregate health excellent, and the migration nobody wants sitting untouched for a fourth quarter. Detection is exclusively over the CLOSED ledger, because every measure over the open ledger looks BETTER as the capture proceeds: the age distribution of closed obligations spikes at low age and shows nothing above it, and the p95 age at closure against the p95 age of the open ledger is a two-number indictment. The countermeasures are Part VIII's: a bounded divisor, the old-tail closure rate reported beside the aggregate and never merged into it, and a hard reserved-capacity floor for the top of the risk-times-impact ordering, because a queue with no floor will always be captured — the arithmetic of the divisor guarantees it, and no amount of intention survives arithmetic.

14.9 The meta-failure, and the one honest defense. Every failure above is an absence: an obligation not born, a gate not run, a probe not executed, a query not issued, a decision not read, an abandonment not declared, an old obligation not closed. The authority that detects absences is itself vulnerable to absence, and its own liveness is therefore not an assumption but a subject: is the compiler running, is the probe suite running, is the drill being run, is the escalation queue being read, is the ledger being queried, and is anyone looking at the age distribution of closed obligations. That question is asked of DAIF-07 by the same instrument DAIF-07 asks of everything else — the liveness ledger, which was never a rival and is here the last line of defense — and it is asked because a gate never observed to refuse is indistinguishable from an inert one. The whole dataset reduces to one uncomfortable admission: an obligation authority that is not itself watched will fail exactly as silently as the estate it was built to watch, and the only defense that has ever worked is to keep asking, of the authority as of everything else, not whether it exists but whether it has recently REFUSED anything.

PART XIV FINAL LAW. The obligation authority fails in seven ways and every one of them makes its own metrics improve: an obligation dropped during compilation and therefore never born, invisible to a ledger that is complete and consistent and simply does not contain it; a closure marked to clear a queue, which converts undone work into an official timestamped claim that every future audit will trust and stop looking behind; detection starvation, reached by a well-run estate following its dashboard's gradient with no adversary and no negligence anywhere in the loop; a ledger grown so large that nobody queries it, whose obvious remedy — pruning old terminal entries — destroys the closed-age distribution and blinds the estate at the exact moment it is most likely captured; escalation flooding, which makes the authority the bottleneck and calls that governance; the obligation that outlived its purpose and is left open because closing it would be false and abandoning it feels like an admission, when NO-LONGER-WORTH-DOING is a legitimate outcome that must be declared and recorded rather than sustained as a lie of omission; and prioritization capture, cheap wins forever, detectable only over the closed ledger because every measure over the open one improves as the capture proceeds — and above all of them the meta-failure, that an authority built to detect absences is itself defeated by absence, so DAIF-07's own liveness must be watched by the same instruments it points at everything else, since a gate never observed to refuse is indistinguishable from a gate that was never there.
