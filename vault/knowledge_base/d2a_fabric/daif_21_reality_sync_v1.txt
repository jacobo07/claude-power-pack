DAIF-21 — REALITY SYNCHRONIZATION AND SEMANTIC CHANGE

PART I — THESIS: EVERY COMPILATION IS A PHOTOGRAPH

1.1 Mission. This dataset states the law that makes the whole corpus survivable, and the law is one sentence: every compiled artifact is a photograph of a world that has since moved on, and an estate that does not detect the movement will act with total confidence on a picture of a reality that no longer exists. DAIF-02 compiles reality into representations. DAIF-03 proves the compilation was faithful — at the moment it was made, against the source as the source then stood. Neither dataset claims, and neither can claim, that the source held still afterward. It does not. The file is edited, the decision is reversed, the constraint is lifted, the gate is rewritten, the dependency is upgraded, the architecture moves under the module that named it, the environment shifts beneath the run that measured it, the evidence that grounded a claim is withdrawn by the very measurement that was supposed to confirm it. Each of those movements silently falsifies some set of compiled units, cached answers, assembled packs, open obligations, and standing contracts — and falsifies them without a single byte of the derived artifact changing, because a derived artifact has no way of noticing that its source moved. DAIF-21 is the mechanism that notices. It detects when reality has diverged from its representation, decides exactly what that divergence invalidates, and drives the recompilation that repairs it. Without this dataset the corpus is a beautifully typed, rigorously gated, provably faithful record of a moment that has passed.

1.2 The asymmetry that makes staleness uniquely dangerous. A wrong artifact and a stale artifact are not two grades of the same defect; they are opposites in the only dimension that governs damage, which is trust. A wrong artifact is distrusted. It failed once, it was caught, it is checked. It carries a scar, and the scar is a defense: the next consumer reads it warily, verifies before acting, and treats its claims as claims. A stale artifact carries no scar. It was right — genuinely, verifiably right — and it has a track record of being right, and every consumer that ever acted on it was rewarded for doing so. It has accumulated exactly the credit that suppresses verification, and it spends that credit at the precise moment it has stopped deserving it. This is the asymmetry: the artifact's trustworthiness is derived from its history, its danger is derived from the present, and the two have silently decoupled. The most damaging artifact in any estate is therefore never the one that was always wrong — it is the one that was right for a year and became wrong last Tuesday, because nothing about it looks different and everything about it invites belief. Staleness is a failure that hides inside a success, and it is the only failure whose probability rises with the artifact's reputation.

1.3 The cardinal principle, composing SQI. DAIF-21 does not invent its epistemic backbone; it composes SQI's reality-supremacy hierarchy whole and forks none of it. That hierarchy already ranks observed reality above the plan that predicted it, above the documentation that describes it, above the agent reasoning that reconstructed it, and above the green signal that reported it — and the ordering is exactly the ordering DAIF-21 needs, because a compiled artifact is a representation and every rank beneath observed reality is a representation of a representation. From that hierarchy the cardinal principle of this dataset follows directly and is stated once here for the whole corpus: observed reality outranks every representation of it, and the burden of proof sits on the representation to demonstrate that it still matches — never on reality to announce that it moved. Reality does not announce. It has no obligation, no channel, and no interest in telling a cached answer that its premise was withdrawn. A system built on the assumption that change will announce itself has inverted the burden, and an inverted burden is the whole failure: the representation goes on asserting, unchallenged, until the assertion causes a loss large enough to force an investigation, and the investigation discovers that nothing was ever wrong except the passage of time. The burden sits with the representation. It must earn its currency continuously, not once.

1.4 The absorption of D2A-14, stated explicitly. Semantic change detection was proposed as its own system and it is not one, and this dataset says so in its own voice rather than letting the fold pass silently. Change detection and reality synchronization are the same mechanism, decomposed the same way, with the same three moves: detect that something moved, decide what that movement invalidates, act on the invalidation. A detector that detects and does not decide produces a stream of notifications nobody actions. A decider that decides and does not act produces verdicts nobody honors. And an actor that acts without a detector acts on a schedule rather than on reality, which is the museum-keeping this dataset exists to abolish. The three moves are one pipeline and one pipeline is one dataset, so D2A-14's semantic-change candidate is absorbed here in full — its taxonomy in Part II, its hardest technique in Part IV, its verdicts in Part V — and no parallel change-detection system is built, because a second detector with its own invalidation logic would produce two verdicts on the same artifact's currency and the corpus's constitution convicts exactly that authority collision.

1.5 What DAIF-21 does not own. The dataset is thin by construction and its boundaries are declared before its first mechanism, because a synchronization layer that starts absorbing its neighbors becomes the estate's second compiler and its damage compounds. DAIF-21 does not compile. When it determines that an artifact must be rebuilt, it hands the rebuild to DAIF-02 and consumes the result; it owns no lowering pass, no representation, and no boundary heuristic. DAIF-21 does not judge fidelity. When a rebuilt artifact must prove that it faithfully carries its new source, DAIF-03's fidelity gate makes that judgment and DAIF-21 reads its verdict; a synchronization engine that decided for itself whether a recompilation was faithful would be marking its own homework at exactly the moment it is under the most pressure to declare the problem solved. DAIF-21 does not hold the graph. The typed edges that say which unit derived from which are GK-04's, the coordinates are graphify's, and DAIF-21 traverses them as a reader — it adds no node type and no edge type of its own. And DAIF-21 does not run the obligation lifecycle: it creates obligations and hands them to DAIF-07, which owns their aging, their escalation, and their closure. What remains after those four subtractions is DAIF-21's whole and only mandate: detect, decide, and drive.

1.6 The actors and the object model in outline. Four actors move through this dataset. The change source is whatever moved — a file, a decision, an environment, an authority, a measured result — and it is never a party to the synchronization; it changes and says nothing. The detector observes, through the push signals and pull probes Part III specifies, and emits a typed change event carrying what moved, when, in which of Part II's classes, and with what confidence. The invalidation engine of Part V consumes that event, traverses the derivation closure, and assigns each reachable artifact one of four currency verdicts — VALID, SUSPECT, INVALID, UNKNOWN — which are the dataset's central objects and are themselves typed cognitive objects in DAIF-01's discipline, carrying authority, scope, validity, provenance, and confidence like every other unit in the corpus. The driver of Part VI converts those verdicts into tracked work: a selective recompilation, a re-evaluation, a contract re-verification, a pack invalidation, and above all an obligation, because an invalidation that generates no tracked obligation is a message in a bottle and this corpus already knows precisely what becomes of work that nobody tracked.

1.7 The burden this dataset carries. The corpus's spine runs type → representation → fidelity → obligation, and every link in it produces artifacts whose correctness is indexed to an instant. DAIF-21 is the only dataset that carries the index forward in time, which makes its failure mode uniquely total: a gap anywhere else in the corpus produces one bad artifact, while a gap here produces a whole estate of artifacts that were once excellent and are now confidently, invisibly, historically wrong — and each of them will be trusted in proportion to how long it has been right. The burden is therefore completeness of detection over the change taxonomy, honesty about the classes it cannot detect, and a bias, declared and defended in Part IV, toward re-verifying too often rather than too rarely. The dataset earns its place only if a consumer, holding any compiled unit in the corpus, can ask one question — is this still true of the world — and get an answer that is grounded in an observation rather than in the artifact's reputation. Everything that follows is the mechanism that makes that question answerable.

PART I FINAL LAW. Every compiled artifact is a photograph of a world that has since moved on, and staleness is the uniquely dangerous defect because a wrong artifact is distrusted and checked while a stale one was right, has a history of being right, and is therefore trusted precisely when it has stopped deserving it; composing SQI's reality-supremacy hierarchy, observed reality outranks every representation of it and the burden falls on the representation to prove it still matches — reality never announces that it moved — and DAIF-21 absorbs D2A-14 entirely because change detection and reality synchronization are one mechanism with three moves, detect what changed, decide what it invalidates, act on the invalidation, while compiling nothing (DAIF-02 does), judging no fidelity (DAIF-03 does), and holding no graph (GK does).

PART II — THE CHANGE TAXONOMY

2.1 Mission. Not all change matters, and a synchronization layer that treats all change alike fails in both directions at once. Treat every byte movement as consequential and the estate drowns in invalidations it cannot service, until the actors that receive them learn to dismiss them wholesale — alert fatigue, which is not a nuisance but a total detection failure wearing the costume of a working detector. Treat change coarsely and the movements that actually falsify artifacts pass unnoticed while trivial reformattings consume the whole budget. The taxonomy in this Part is what makes discrimination possible: twelve classes, each with a detection surface, an invalidation reach, and a characteristic false negative — the specific way that class routinely escapes the instrument that is supposed to catch it. The classes are ordered from cheapest to most consequential, and the ordering is itself load-bearing, because it is the priority under which a detector with a bounded budget spends its attention.

2.2 The cheap classes: textual and formatting. A textual change is bytes moved with meaning intact — a rename that carries no semantics, a rewrapped line, a reordered set whose order was never significant, whitespace, a comment repaired. It is detected trivially by content hash against the CIR-0 fingerprint, and its invalidation reach is, correctly, near zero: nothing that derived from the unit's meaning is falsified, because the meaning did not move. Its characteristic false negative is the meaningful rename — an identifier or a term whose change looks purely lexical and in fact re-points a reference, so a unit that reads as textual is semantic in disguise. Formatting change is the adjacent class: structure re-expressed without content moving, a section resequenced, a table turned into prose. Its reach is likewise near zero and its characteristic false negative is the structural re-expression that silently drops a clause during the reshaping — the reformatting that lost the exception. Both classes exist in the taxonomy not because they matter but because they must be cheaply and confidently dismissed; a detector that cannot discharge them cheaply will spend its entire budget on them and have nothing left for the classes that count.

2.3 The class that matters most and hides best: semantic and behavioral change. A semantic change is a movement of meaning — a constraint's strength weakened from prohibition to preference, a definition's boundary redrawn, a scope narrowed, a claim's polarity reversed. It is detected by structural comparison of CIR units rather than by any hash, for the reason Part IV develops at length, and its invalidation reach is the full derivation closure of the changed unit. Its characteristic false negative is the semantic change expressed in a byte-identical form — the same sentence meaning something else because a term it uses was redefined elsewhere. Behavioral change is worse and is the most under-detected class in the entire taxonomy: what the system actually does moved, while nothing in the source looks different. A dependency was upgraded beneath an unchanged call, a gate's threshold was tuned in an environment that no artifact indexes, a default flipped, a probabilistic component drifted. The source is unchanged, so every source-watching detector reports stability with perfect sincerity, and the artifact that describes the system's behavior is now false while every freshness instrument in the estate says it is current. Behavioral change is detectable only by observation — by re-running the thing and comparing what it does, never by reading what it says — which is why Part III's pull probes exist and why a detection layer built exclusively on repository signals is structurally blind to the class that hurts it most.

2.4 The governance classes: authority and scope. An authority change moves who may decide. A rule that was the Owner's becomes an agent's to apply within bounds, or the reverse; a system that held final say over a domain is demoted; a precedence order between two rule families is re-ranked. It is detected in the authority dimension of DAIF-01's typed objects and in the standing rule registers, and its invalidation reach is unusually wide and unusually easy to under-estimate, because it does not falsify any single unit's content — it falsifies the right of a whole class of units to bind. Its characteristic false negative is precisely that width: the detector, finding no content change, records a low-consequence event, when in fact every decision that was made under the old authority is now of questionable standing. A scope change moves where a rule applies — a constraint that governed one project becomes universal, or a universal law is demoted to a single feature. Its detection surface is the scope dimension, and its reach is the set of units that were, or now are, inside the boundary. Its characteristic false negative is the silent widening: a scope enlarged, so units that never consulted the rule are now bound by it, and no artifact anywhere points at them to say so — the change is invisible from the changed unit's own closure, because the newly-governed units never derived from it in the first place.

2.5 The epistemic classes: evidence, lifecycle, and decision change. An evidence change is a movement in the support for a claim: a measurement strengthens it, a contrary result collapses it, an experiment that grounded it fails to reproduce. It is detected in the evaluation surface and its invalidation reach runs along the epistemic support chain — every unit whose status depends on the evidence must be re-typed, and a claim whose only support has collapsed does not merely weaken, it falls to the weakest link that remains. Its characteristic false negative is the quiet collapse: evidence is rarely withdrawn with a declaration, it is simply not reproduced, and an absence produces no signal. A lifecycle change is a movement in a unit's standing — superseded, expired, revoked — detected in DAIF-01's validity dimension, with a reach covering every consumer that is still citing the unit as live; its characteristic false negative is the expiry nobody read, a validity window that lapsed while every derived artifact went on treating the unit as current because nothing re-checked the window. A decision change is the reversal of a prior choice, and it is the class this dataset most explicitly serves: DAIF-02's decision representation deliberately carries revision conditions and an expiry so that a reversal has a place to land, and a reversed decision invalidates not only itself but everything justified by it, including the artifacts that never mentioned the decision and merely inherited its consequence.

2.6 The commitment classes: obligation, contract, and architectural change. An obligation change moves the work that is owed — a closure condition is redefined, an owner is reassigned, an obligation is discharged or abandoned — and its reach runs into DAIF-07's lifecycle, where a closure condition that moved after the obligation was opened is the specific poison that produces an obligation nobody can ever close. A contract change moves an interface between two systems: a schema field, an event shape, a guarantee one side made to the other. Its reach is both parties plus every artifact that assumed the old shape, it is detected against DAIF-04's typed contract objects, and its characteristic false negative is the compatible-looking break — a field added, a default changed — that passes every structural check and violates a guarantee the structure never expressed. An architectural change moves the shape of the system itself: a module is split, a boundary is redrawn, a layer is inverted. It is the most consequential class and the most expensive to service, because its reach is not a closure but a region — every artifact that named a location, a boundary, or a responsibility that has now moved — and its characteristic false negative is the slow architecture change, accumulated across dozens of individually-innocent commits, no one of which crosses any threshold while their sum has relocated the system out from under every artifact that described it.

2.7 The cardinal invariant: a hash tells you bytes moved, never that meaning moved. The twelve classes above are held apart by one invariant that this dataset states as its second law after reality supremacy: byte-identity and meaning-identity are independent properties, and a system that equates them will both cry wolf and sleep through the fire. It cries wolf on every reformatting, every whitespace repair, every re-serialization, flooding its consumers with invalidations that carry no meaning and training them to ignore the channel. And it sleeps through the fire on every behavioral change, every authority re-ranking, every quiet evidence collapse, every meaning that moved without a byte moving, reporting perfect currency while the estate acts on falsehoods. The hash is not thereby useless — it is the cheapest and most reliable instrument the detector owns, and Part III keeps it — but it answers exactly one question, whether this content is the content we last saw, and it must never be asked a second. The metric for this Part is class-resolved detection coverage, measured per class against a set of known real changes, and its anti-Goodhart countermetric is the behavioral-class detection rate held out separately: a taxonomy tuned to maximize aggregate coverage will optimize the classes a hash can see, score superbly, and remain totally blind to the one class that costs the most, so the aggregate is never permitted to speak for the hard class.

PART II FINAL LAW. Change is twelve classes, not one — textual and formatting (cheap, near-zero reach, dismissed cheaply so the budget survives), semantic and behavioral (the meaning moved, or what the system does moved while nothing in the source looks different, the most under-detected class in the estate and observable only by re-running rather than by reading), authority and scope (who may decide, where a rule binds), evidence, lifecycle, and decision (support strengthened or collapsed, a unit superseded or expired, a choice reversed), and obligation, contract, and architectural — each with its own detection surface, invalidation reach, and characteristic false negative; and over them all stands the second law of this dataset, that a hash tells you bytes moved and never that meaning moved, because a system which equates the two will cry wolf on every reformatting and sleep through every behavioral fire.

PART III — DETECTION: HOW REALITY ANNOUNCES ITSELF

3.1 Mission. Detection is the first of the three moves, and it is bounded by a fact the previous Part established: reality does not announce. Nothing in a changed world sends a message to the artifacts it falsified. What the estate has instead is a set of signal sources it already runs for other purposes, each of which incidentally carries evidence that something moved, and the whole of this Part is the discipline of composing those sources — never forking them, never building a parallel watcher beside a system that already watches — into a detection surface wide enough to cover the taxonomy. The mission is therefore not to construct an observer but to fuse the observers that exist, to classify what they emit into the twelve classes, and to be explicit and unashamed about the classes that no existing signal covers, because a detection layer that quietly omits a class it cannot see is worse than one that declares the blindness: the first produces false confidence, the second produces a probe.

3.2 The push signal sources. A push signal is one where reality, or a system watching reality, tells the detector without being asked. The estate has seven. Filesystem and repository movement — commits, diffs, content hashes against the CIR-0 fingerprints — is the cheapest and highest-volume source, and it covers textual, formatting, and much semantic change, while covering behavioral change not at all. Test and gate results are the second: a gate that flipped from pass to fail, or from fail to pass, is a behavioral observation the estate already produced for another reason, and it is the single most valuable push signal for the class the repository cannot see. Production evidence is the third — what the system actually did when it ran. Agent corrections are the fourth, and they are the highest-value push signal per unit volume, because a correction is the exact moment the estate's belief changed: an actor asserted something, reality or the Owner refuted it, and that refutation invalidates every artifact that carried the refuted belief. Owner directives are the fifth and carry the highest authority: a directive can move a decision, an authority, a scope, or a constraint in one sentence, and it is the only source that changes reality by declaring it. Event streams are the sixth — PM-03's bus and CEPS's event record are transports the estate already runs, and DAIF-21 subscribes rather than building a channel. And `liveness` findings are the seventh, reporting when a thing that was supposed to be running is not.

3.3 The pull probes. A pull check is one where the detector goes and asks reality, and it exists because the push sources are structurally incomplete. Behavioral change emits no push signal by construction: nothing changed in the source, so no source-watcher fires, and the only way to learn that the system's behavior moved is to make it behave and observe. A probe is therefore an executed question — re-run the gate the artifact claimed passes, re-resolve the dependency the artifact pinned, re-read the environment the artifact assumed, re-measure the value the artifact reported, re-check that the file the artifact cites still contains the span the provenance locator points at. Probes are expensive relative to push signals, which is the entire design tension of this Part: a probe costs an execution, and an estate cannot probe everything continuously, so probe budget must be allocated — and it is allocated by the decay model of Part VII, which is precisely the mechanism that says which artifacts have gone long enough without an observation to deserve one. The provenance-resolution probe deserves separate mention because it is the cheapest of them and catches an entire failure family: an artifact whose citation no longer resolves to the span it was compiled from has had its ground moved beneath it, and the check costs one read.

3.4 The invariant that forces the probe to exist. The distinction between push and pull is not an engineering preference; it is forced by an invariant that DAIF-21 composes directly from SQI: the absence of a change signal is not evidence of stability. SQI already holds that the absence of a failure signal is UNVERIFIED and never PROVEN — a gate that did not fire may have been satisfied, or may not have run, or may not have been wired, and the three are indistinguishable from the outside. DAIF-21 inherits that reasoning intact and applies it to currency: silence from the detection surface means no signal arrived, which is consistent with a world that did not move and equally consistent with a world that moved in a class the surface cannot see. The two readings are indistinguishable from the silence alone, and a system that reads silence as stability has converted an unverified state into a proven one — the exact epistemic collapse the corpus's type system was built to prevent. It follows that an artifact which has never been re-checked is not fresh. It is unexamined, which is a different verdict with a different consequence, and it is why time-triggered re-verification exists at all: the passage of time without a signal raises the probability that a silent class moved, and at some point that probability must be discharged by an observation rather than by an assumption.

3.5 Time as a first-class change source. The passage of time is itself a signal, and it enters the detector as one. An artifact carries an expiry — the validity window DAIF-01's type system already defines — and the lapse of that window is a change event with no external cause, emitted by the clock, requiring no push and no probe to detect. It is the cheapest detection in the taxonomy and it covers the lifecycle class outright. Time also drives the probe scheduler: an artifact whose decay model says it half-lives in days and which has not been observed in weeks is overdue by construction, and its overdue-ness is a first-class input to the probe budget. This is where DAIF-21 composes GK-07's freshness and integrity verdicts rather than computing its own — GK-07 already answers whether a node's knowledge is fresh, and the synchronization layer reads that verdict as an input to its currency decision instead of maintaining a rival notion of freshness that would inevitably drift from it. Two freshness authorities disagreeing about the same artifact is the authority collision the constitution convicts; there is one, and it is GK-07's.

3.6 Failure modes and the adversarial cases. The detector has three signature failures. The first is the silent source: a change arrives through a channel nobody subscribed to — a configuration edited outside the repository, an authority moved in conversation and never recorded, a dependency upgraded by a transitive resolution — so the surface reports stability with complete sincerity and the estate's confidence in that stability is exactly as high as its blindness is total. The second is the drowned signal: the surface fires correctly, at a volume that exceeds what any consumer can service, and the correct signal is lost inside the noise it arrived with; this is the alert-fatigue failure and it is a detection failure, not a presentation problem, because a signal that reaches a consumer who has learned to dismiss the channel has not been detected in any sense that matters. The third is the misclassified signal: a real semantic change classified as textual, dismissed cheaply, and never propagated — the most dangerous outcome available to this Part, because the detector fired, correctly logged an event, and confidently assigned it a reach of zero. The adversarial case against the detector is the change designed to look cheap: a semantic reversal expressed as a rename, an authority moved by editing one word, a constraint's strength weakened by deleting a single qualifier. The defense is that classification uncertainty resolves upward — a change that cannot be confidently discharged as textual is treated as semantic, paying the recompilation cost rather than the wrong-action cost, for the reasons Part IV makes explicit.

3.7 Metrics, countermetrics, and what consumes the detector. The detector is measured on time-to-detection per class — the interval between the world moving and the estate emitting the corresponding change event — and on class-resolved recall against a held-out set of real changes with known ground truth. Its anti-Goodhart countermetric is essential and is the actioned-signal rate: a detector optimizing for recall will maximize signal volume, score perfectly on recall, and destroy its own effectiveness by training every downstream consumer to ignore it, so recall is never permitted to be read without the fraction of emitted signals that produced a real invalidation. A third measure guards the silent-source failure: coverage of the taxonomy by source, computed as the set of classes for which at least one live signal exists, with the explicitly empty cells named rather than averaged away. The detector's outputs are consumed by exactly one system — the invalidation engine of Part V — and it emits nothing else, holds no verdict, and invalidates nothing on its own authority. The `learning-sentinel` and the UKDL register are its most valuable long-run consumers in the other direction: a change class that repeatedly escapes detection is not an incident, it is a law waiting to be sealed, and a detection blindness observed twice belongs in the rule register rather than in a report nobody re-reads.

PART III FINAL LAW. Reality never announces, so detection is the fusion of signals the estate already runs and never a parallel watcher — repository movement, gate and test results, production evidence, agent corrections, Owner directives, the PM-03 and CEPS event streams, and `liveness` findings as push signals; executed probes as pull checks for the behavioral class that emits no push signal by construction; and the clock itself as a first-class source through the expiry window — all governed by the invariant DAIF-21 composes from SQI, that the absence of a change signal is UNVERIFIED and never PROVEN, from which it follows that an artifact never re-checked is not fresh but merely unexamined, and that a detector optimizing recall without its actioned-signal countermetric will drown its own consumers and fail totally while scoring perfectly.

PART IV — SEMANTIC CHANGE: THE HARD PROBLEM

4.1 Mission. This Part is the technical core of the D2A-14 absorption and the hardest thing DAIF-21 attempts: determining that meaning moved, when the bytes may or may not have. The two directions of the problem are both real and neither is reducible to the other. Bytes may move without meaning moving — the reformatting, the rename, the re-serialization — and a detector that reads every byte movement as a meaning movement destroys itself by volume. And meaning may move without a single byte moving in the artifact that carries it — a term redefined elsewhere, an authority re-ranked, a dependency upgraded beneath an unchanged call, an evidence base quietly not reproduced — and a detector that requires a byte movement to fire is structurally blind to all of it. The problem is hard because it is not a comparison problem in the space where the comparison is cheap. It is a comparison problem in the space of meaning, and the estate's only access to that space is the compiled representation itself.

4.2 Structural comparison of CIR units. The first technique, and the one that carries most of the load, is to stop comparing text and start comparing the compiled units, because DAIF-02's representations were built precisely to expose the dimensions that a text comparison flattens. The comparison runs field by field over the typed objects and asks structural questions that have determinate answers. Has a constraint's strength changed — did an absolute prohibition become a preference, did a preference harden into a rule? Has a constraint's condition moved — is the case that bounded it now wider or narrower, which is the exact movement that turns a conditional prohibition into a universal one? Has a decision's justification been withdrawn — do the evidence pointers that grounded it still resolve, and do they still say what they said? Has a decision's authority or scope changed? Has an obligation's closure condition moved, which is the single most dangerous field movement in the corpus because it produces an obligation that can never be discharged? Has a unit's epistemic status weakened — did a claim that was verified fall back to inferred because the measurement that verified it no longer reproduces? Each of these is a comparison over a typed field, each has a defined answer, and each maps to a class in Part II's taxonomy. This is the payoff of compiling knowledge instead of re-sending it: a compiled corpus is comparable in the dimensions that matter, and a prose corpus is comparable only in bytes.

4.3 Dependency-aware propagation. The second technique is to recognize that a change to a unit is not confined to that unit. Meaning propagates along derivation, so a movement in a unit moves everything that derived from it, and the derived artifact does not need to change a byte to become false — its falsity is entirely inherited. The propagation is computed over the typed derivation edges that GK-04 already holds, which DAIF-21 traverses as a reader and never rebuilds, and it is the mechanism by which a single constraint's weakening invalidates the decision that assumed the constraint, the compiled artifact that encoded the decision, the assembled pack that carried the artifact, and the cached answer a consumer is about to act on. The propagation is also what makes the second-order semantic change detectable at all — the case where a unit's own bytes are identical but a term it depends on was redefined. That unit did not change; its dependency did; and the only instrument that sees this is one that walks the derivation edges rather than the file tree. Part V governs how far the walk goes and where it must stop.

4.4 The honest admission of incompleteness. Semantic change detection is fundamentally incomplete and this dataset says so plainly, because a mechanism that conceals its incompleteness converts a known limitation into an unknown one, which is strictly worse. Some meaning movements are invisible to any automated comparison the estate can run. Meaning that lives in the interaction of two units, neither of which changed, but whose relationship did. Meaning that was never compiled because the source expressed it implicitly and no representation captured it. Meaning that moved in the world with no representation at all — the assumption everyone shared, which stopped being true, and which no artifact ever wrote down because it was too obvious to state. Against this class, structural comparison is silent and will remain silent, and the correct response is not a better comparison but a different posture: because the detector's coverage is incomplete and its gaps are unknowable from the inside, the system must fail toward re-verification rather than toward assumed validity. That is the whole design consequence of the incompleteness admission, and it is why the default for an unexamined artifact is SUSPECT rather than VALID — not as a pessimism, but as the only verdict consistent with a detector that cannot enumerate what it missed.

4.5 The two error classes and their asymmetric cost. A semantic-change detector makes two kinds of error and they are not equally expensive. A false positive is a change reported that did not really move the meaning, and its cost is a recompilation: DAIF-02 runs over the affected closure, DAIF-03 re-gates the result, an obligation is opened and closed, and the estate spends compute and attention it did not strictly need to spend. The cost is real, it is bounded, and it is paid in a currency the estate has. A false negative is a change that moved the meaning and was not reported, and its cost is a confident wrong action: a consumer reads an artifact marked VALID, acts on it without verification precisely because it is marked VALID, and the action is wrong in a way that no gate downstream will catch, because every gate downstream is reading the same stale artifact. The cost is unbounded, it is paid at the point of consequence rather than at the point of error, and it is paid in the currency of trust — which, per Part I, is the currency that makes the next stale artifact more dangerous than this one. The asymmetry is not close. A false positive costs a recompilation; a false negative costs a wrong action taken with full confidence and a reputation that survives it.

4.6 The deliberate bias and the countermetric that keeps it honest. Because the cost asymmetry is that steep, DAIF-21 is deliberately tuned toward false positives. When a comparison is ambiguous, when a classification cannot confidently discharge a change as textual, when a derivation edge is uncertain, when an artifact's decay model is absent — in every one of these, the resolution is upward, toward suspicion and re-verification, and the recompilation cost is paid. This bias is stated as a design commitment rather than left as an emergent tendency, so that a future actor tuning the system for cost cannot quietly invert it. But a bias toward false positives is exactly the bias that produces alert fatigue, and alert fatigue is a total detection failure, so the bias cannot stand alone: it requires a countermetric that measures the thing the bias endangers. That countermetric is the actioned-invalidation rate — of the invalidations DAIF-21 emitted, what fraction produced a recompilation whose output differed materially from its predecessor. A high emission rate with a collapsing actioned rate means the system is invalidating things that did not need it, and the correct response is never to weaken the bias but to sharpen the comparison, because the bias protects against the unbounded error and the comparison quality is what makes the bias affordable. The failure to distinguish those two responses is how a synchronization layer talks itself into trusting its own photographs again.

4.7 Adversarial cases and what consumes this Part. The adversarial input against semantic comparison is the change engineered to look cheap — a qualifier deleted, a term redefined at a distance, a strength weakened by one word — and the defense is the upward resolution of ambiguity, which converts an adversarial cheap-looking change into an expensive-but-safe recompilation. The second adversarial input is the change that is genuinely invisible: a meaning that moved with no representation, which no defense catches and which the incompleteness admission of 4.4 exists to name rather than to conceal. The third is the flood — many small changes arriving together such that the propagation closure of their union covers the estate, which Part V's bounding must survive without either invalidating the world or silently truncating. This Part's output is consumed entirely by Part V's invalidation engine, which converts a detected semantic movement into a currency verdict over a closure; it is consumed indirectly by DAIF-03, whose fidelity gate reads the same compiled representations and asks a sibling question — not did this move, but does this still faithfully carry its source — and the two questions are the same question asked at two different times, which is exactly why DAIF-21 hands its recompilations back to DAIF-03 rather than answering for it.

PART IV FINAL LAW. Semantic change detection compares compiled units and not bytes — has a constraint's strength or condition moved, has a decision's justification been withdrawn, has an obligation's closure condition shifted, has an epistemic status weakened — and propagates the answer along GK-04's derivation edges so that a unit whose own bytes never moved is still invalidated by the movement of what it derived from; it is fundamentally incomplete, some meaning movements being invisible to any automated comparison, and that incompleteness forces the system to fail toward re-verification rather than toward assumed validity, because the two error classes are steeply asymmetric — a false positive costs a recompilation while a false negative costs a confident wrong action — so the bias toward false positives is a declared commitment, kept honest by the actioned-invalidation countermetric, and the correct response to a falling actioned rate is a sharper comparison, never a weaker bias.

PART V — THE INVALIDATION ENGINE

5.1 Mission. Detection tells the estate that something moved. It does not tell it what is now untrue, and the gap between those two facts is where a synchronization layer lives or dies. The invalidation engine is the second of the three moves: it consumes a typed change event and produces a currency verdict for every artifact the change can reach. Its whole difficulty is reach — computing it neither too widely, which invalidates the world and makes the mechanism unaffordable and therefore ignored, nor too narrowly, which leaves a stale artifact marked VALID and hands the estate exactly the confident falsehood Part I named as the corpus's most dangerous object. The engine is a graph traversal with a verdict function, and both halves are constrained: the graph is GK-04's and it does not rebuild it, and the verdict vocabulary is four values, closed, with a defined behavior for the one that means the engine could not answer.

5.2 The derivation chain of derived artifacts. The chain the engine walks is the corpus's own production line, and every arrow in it is a typed edge that already exists in the graph: source -> CIR units -> compiled artifacts -> Mission Packs -> cached answers -> obligations -> contracts. A source fragment produces CIR units through DAIF-02's compilation. Those units are assembled into compiled artifacts. Artifacts are selected and assembled into the Mission Packs that DAIF-08 hands to a reasoning session. Packs produce cached answers — the conclusions an actor reached and recorded rather than re-derived. Answers produce obligations, the tracked work DAIF-07 owns. And obligations, when they concern an interface between systems, produce or depend on the typed contracts DAIF-04 holds. Each arrow is a derivation, each derivation is an edge, and the engine's traversal is the transitive closure of those edges from the changed unit outward. The critical property of the chain is that falsity flows forward along it without any byte moving downstream: a compiled artifact three hops from a reversed decision becomes wrong the instant the decision reverses, and nothing about the artifact changes to reflect that. The chain is the only instrument that connects the two facts.

5.3 Propagation and its bounding. The naive engine invalidates the world on every commit, because the transitive closure of a sufficiently connected graph is the graph. The correct engine invalidates exactly the closure of what actually derived from the changed unit — not what merely mentions it, not what lives in the same file, not what shares a directory, but what has a derivation edge whose provenance chain terminates in the changed span. Three disciplines bound the walk. The first is edge typing: only derivation-class edges propagate invalidation, so a reference edge, a similarity edge, or a co-location edge is not a channel for falsity and the traversal does not cross it. The second is class-scoped reach: Part II assigned each change class an invalidation reach, and a formatting change with a near-zero reach does not begin a traversal at all, which is what keeps the cheap classes cheap. The third is dimension-scoped propagation: a change to a decision's authority invalidates the standing of what derived from it without invalidating its content, and a change to an evidence base invalidates the epistemic status of dependents without invalidating their structure — so the verdict a dependent receives is scoped to the dimension that moved rather than being total by default. Bounding is not an optimization. It is the difference between a mechanism the estate runs and a mechanism the estate turns off.

5.4 The four verdicts. The engine's output vocabulary is closed and has exactly four values. VALID means the artifact has been affirmatively determined to still match reality — not that no signal arrived, which is a different thing entirely, but that the derivation closure was walked and no change reached it, or that a probe observed it and confirmed it. SUSPECT means the artifact may have been falsified and must be re-verified before it is reused; it is not forbidden, it is not trusted, and any consumer that reaches for it must first pay the re-verification cost or decline to use it. INVALID means the artifact has been affirmatively determined to no longer match reality and may not be reused at all; it does not degrade to SUSPECT and it is not available under caution, because a known-false artifact used carefully is still a known-false artifact. And UNKNOWN means the derivation graph could not answer — an edge is missing, a provenance chain does not resolve, a decay model is absent, the closure could not be computed within budget. UNKNOWN is the verdict that decides whether this engine is honest.

5.5 UNKNOWN is treated as SUSPECT, never as VALID. The single rule that carries the most weight in this Part is that UNKNOWN degrades to SUSPECT and never to VALID, and it follows directly from the invariant Part III composed from SQI: the absence of an answer is not an affirmative answer. An engine that cannot determine whether an artifact was reached by a change has learned exactly nothing about that artifact's currency, and the two available defaults are not symmetric. Defaulting UNKNOWN to VALID converts every gap in the graph — every missing edge, every unresolved provenance pointer, every artifact whose lineage was never recorded — into a silent assertion of freshness, which means the engine's confidence is highest precisely where its knowledge is weakest, and the artifacts most likely to be stale (the old ones, the ones with lost lineage, the ones nobody has touched) are the ones it most confidently marks current. Defaulting UNKNOWN to SUSPECT costs a re-verification and preserves the honesty of the VALID verdict, which is the only thing that makes VALID worth anything at all. A VALID that includes the un-answerable is not a verdict, it is a shrug wearing a verdict's name.

5.6 The two failure modes, and which one is worse. The engine's first failure is the unbounded cascade: a traversal that crosses non-derivation edges, or that applies a total reach to a dimension-scoped change, and invalidates a substantial fraction of the estate on a routine commit. Its damage is economic and then behavioral — the recompilation cost exceeds what the estate will pay, the invalidations are dismissed, and within a few cycles the actors have learned to override the engine as a matter of routine, at which point the mechanism is decorative. This failure is loud, it is visible, and it is survivable. The second failure is the bounded cascade that bounds itself wrongly: a traversal that stops one edge short of a real dependent and leaves a genuinely stale artifact marked VALID. This failure is silent. It produces no cost signal, no complaint, and no visible symptom, and it delivers to a consumer an artifact bearing an affirmative freshness verdict that the engine had no right to issue. It is strictly worse than the unbounded cascade and it is worse than having no engine at all, because an estate with no engine knows it must verify, while an estate with a wrongly-bounding engine has been told, in the system's own authoritative voice, that verification is unnecessary. Every bounding discipline in 5.3 must therefore be conservative at its edge: when the traversal cannot determine whether an edge is a derivation edge, it crosses it and pays the cost, because the alternative is a false VALID.

5.7 Metrics, adversarial cases, and consumers. The engine is measured on invalidation precision — the fraction of invalidated artifacts whose recompilation produced a materially different output — and on the metric that matters far more and is far harder to obtain, the false-VALID rate, estimated by sampling artifacts the engine marked VALID and probing them directly. The countermetric discipline is unavoidable here: precision alone is maximized by invalidating less, and an engine tuned for precision will bound ever more tightly, score ever better, and manufacture the exact silent failure of 5.6, so precision is never permitted to be read without the sampled false-VALID rate beside it. The adversarial case is the change whose real dependents are connected by an edge the graph never recorded — a dependency that lives only in an actor's head, a coupling that was never compiled — and the defense is that an artifact with an incomplete lineage is UNKNOWN, and UNKNOWN is SUSPECT, so an unrecorded edge costs a re-verification rather than a false assurance. The engine's verdicts are consumed by Part VI, which converts them into tracked work; by DAIF-08, which must not assemble a Mission Pack out of INVALID units; and by every consumer in the estate that reads an artifact, because a currency verdict that is not checked at the point of use is a verdict that was computed for nobody.

PART V FINAL LAW. The invalidation engine walks the derivation chain — source -> CIR units -> compiled artifacts -> Mission Packs -> cached answers -> obligations -> contracts — over GK-04's typed edges which it reads and never rebuilds, bounding the traversal by edge type, by the change class's reach, and by the dimension that moved, so that it invalidates exactly the closure of what actually derived from the changed unit rather than the world; it emits four verdicts, VALID (affirmatively still matching), SUSPECT (re-verify before reuse), INVALID (may not be reused), and UNKNOWN (the graph could not answer), and it degrades UNKNOWN to SUSPECT and never to VALID, because the failure mode of the unbounded cascade is loud and survivable while the failure mode of the cascade that bounds itself wrongly is silent, marks a stale artifact VALID in the system's own authoritative voice, and is therefore worse than having no engine at all.

PART VI — WHAT CHANGE CREATES: RECOMPILATION, OBLIGATIONS, AND RE-EVALUATION

6.1 Mission. A verdict is not a repair. The invalidation engine of Part V produces an accurate account of what is no longer true, and if the pipeline ends there the estate has achieved nothing except a more precise description of its own decay. The third move — act — is what converts a currency verdict into a world where the artifact is once again faithful, and it is the move most often omitted, because it is the only one that costs real work rather than real compute. This Part specifies the five outputs a detected change generates, the sibling dataset each is handed to, and the invariant that governs all of them: DAIF-21 generates work, it does not perform work. It recompiles nothing itself, gates nothing itself, and closes nothing itself. Its entire contribution at this stage is to convert a verdict into a tracked commitment that some other system owns, and its entire failure mode is to convert a verdict into a notification that nobody owns.

6.2 Selective recompilation, handed to DAIF-02. The first output is the recompilation itself, and the operative word is selective. The naive repair recompiles the corpus, which is the same failure as the unbounded cascade transposed into the action phase — it is correct, it is expensive beyond what the estate will pay, and it will therefore be skipped, which converts a working repair mechanism into a decorative one. The correct repair recompiles exactly the closure the engine computed and nothing beyond it: the CIR units cut from the changed source span, the artifacts derived from those units, the packs assembled from those artifacts. DAIF-02's incremental ingest discipline is what makes this affordable, and DAIF-21 composes it rather than duplicating it — a source fragment whose content hash is unchanged is not re-acquired, so the recompilation touches only what genuinely moved and re-derives nothing that did not. The selectivity is also a correctness property and not merely an economy: recompiling an unchanged unit re-runs the boundary heuristics over content that did not move, and a boundary that lands differently on the second pass silently breaks every edge that pointed at the old unit, which means a gratuitous recompilation can manufacture the exact invalidation it was run to repair.

6.3 Re-evaluation, handed to DAIF-03. The second output is a fidelity re-evaluation, and it rests on a rule stated here without qualification: a recompiled artifact does not inherit its predecessor's PASS. The predecessor's fidelity verdict was a claim about a specific compilation of a specific source at a specific moment, and every one of those three has now changed — the source moved, which is why we are here; the compilation ran again, which means new boundaries, new units, and new opportunities to lose an exception; and the moment is a different moment. To carry the old PASS forward would be to assert fidelity on the authority of an artifact that no longer exists, which is the photograph fallacy of Part I applied to the fidelity gate itself. So the recompiled artifact re-enters DAIF-03 as a new artifact, spends its loss budget again, proves behavioral equivalence against the new source again, and earns a verdict of its own. This is the most-skipped step in any synchronization mechanism, because a recompilation that ran to completion feels finished, and the temptation to mark it current on the strength of its predecessor's record is exactly the trust-from-history mechanism this dataset was built to break.

6.4 The obligation, handed to DAIF-07, and the invariant that makes the whole thing real. The third output is the one that decides whether Parts I through V were engineering or theater. When the engine marks an artifact SUSPECT or INVALID, DAIF-21 opens a typed obligation — this artifact must be recompiled and re-verified — with an owner, an age, and a closure condition, and hands it to DAIF-07's lifecycle. The obligation is not a courtesy and it is not a log line. It is the mechanism by which the invalidation acquires a claim on the estate's future attention, and it is the difference between a system that noticed the decay and a system that repaired it. The invariant is stated bluntly: an invalidation that creates no tracked obligation is a message in a bottle, and this corpus already knows, in detail, what becomes of work that nobody tracked — the task named in a conversation that closed, the follow-up promised in a session that ended, the pending verification that attention moved past. Those are not hypothetical losses; they are the founding observation of DAIF-07 and the reason it exists. An invalidation is exactly that shape of work — named once, owed by nobody, forgotten by default — and it dies the same death unless it is typed, owned, aged, and closed. The obligation's closure condition is what forbids the comfortable ending: the obligation closes when the artifact has been recompiled and has re-cleared its fidelity gate, not when someone has acknowledged the notification.

6.5 Contract re-verification and pack invalidation, handed to DAIF-04 and DAIF-08. The fourth output concerns interfaces. When a change reaches a typed contract — a schema, an event shape, a guarantee one system made to another — the contract does not merely become SUSPECT; it must be re-verified against both parties, because a contract is the only artifact in the corpus whose falsity is immediately load-bearing on a system that did not change. DAIF-04 owns that verification and DAIF-21 hands it the verdict plus the changed unit that reached it. The fifth output concerns assembled context: a Mission Pack is a selection of artifacts frozen at assembly time, which makes it the most photograph-like object in the entire corpus, and when an artifact inside a pack is invalidated the pack itself inherits the verdict. DAIF-08 owns pack lifecycle and must not assemble, or continue to serve, a pack containing INVALID units. The subtle case is the pack in flight — assembled, handed to a reasoning session, and invalidated mid-session — and the rule is that the invalidation is surfaced into the session rather than silently swallowed, because an actor reasoning from a pack that has just been falsified is the precise situation in which a confident wrong action is about to be taken.

6.6 Failure modes of the action phase. Three failures recur. The first is the notification that nobody owns — the invalidation is emitted, correctly and precisely, into a channel with no consumer and no closure condition, and the estate's decay is now well-documented and entirely unrepaired; this is the failure the obligation invariant of 6.4 exists to make structurally impossible. The second is the inherited PASS — the recompiled artifact carried forward on its predecessor's fidelity verdict, which reintroduces at the repair site the exact staleness the repair was run to remove, and which is invisible because the artifact bears a PASS and PASSes are not questioned. The third is the partial repair: the closure was recompiled, the artifacts were re-gated, and the obligations, contracts, and packs that derived from them were not, so the estate is repaired at the layer that is easy to see and stale at the layers that are expensive to walk — and the layers that were skipped are precisely the ones closest to the point of action, which means the repair fixed the representation and left the consumption path untouched.

6.7 Metrics, countermetrics, and consumers. The action phase is measured on repair latency — the interval from change detection to a re-verified artifact — and on obligation closure rate for synchronization-generated obligations, which is the only number that distinguishes a mechanism from a report. The anti-Goodhart countermetric on repair latency is the re-gate pass rate: a system optimizing for fast repair will start skipping the DAIF-03 re-evaluation, achieve superb latency, and ship unverified recompilations at speed, so latency is never permitted to be read without the fraction of recompilations that actually re-cleared their fidelity gate. The countermetric on obligation closure rate is closure quality — the fraction of closed synchronization obligations whose artifact, when probed afterward, actually matches reality — because a closure rate is trivially maximized by closing obligations without doing the work, and the corpus has a standing rule about gates that are never observed to refuse. The outputs of this Part are consumed by DAIF-02, DAIF-03, DAIF-04, DAIF-07, and DAIF-08 respectively, which is the whole point: DAIF-21 is thin because every repair it commissions is performed by the dataset that owns the thing being repaired, and a synchronization layer that started performing those repairs itself would become a second compiler, a second fidelity gate, and a second obligation lifecycle, each of them drifting from the original it forked.

PART VI FINAL LAW. A detected change does not merely invalidate, it generates work, and the work must be tracked or the invalidation is theater: DAIF-21 hands selective recompilation to DAIF-02 (only the affected closure, never the corpus, because a gratuitous recompilation can manufacture the very invalidation it was run to repair), re-evaluation to DAIF-03 (a recompiled artifact does not inherit its predecessor's PASS — the source moved, the compilation re-ran, and the moment is different), contract re-verification to DAIF-04, pack invalidation to DAIF-08 (surfaced into any session holding a pack falsified in flight), and above all a typed obligation to DAIF-07 with an owner, an age, and a closure condition that closes only on a re-cleared fidelity gate — because an invalidation that creates no tracked obligation is a message in a bottle, and this corpus already knows exactly what becomes of work that nobody tracked.

PART VII — STALENESS, DECAY, AND THE HALF-LIFE OF KNOWLEDGE

7.1 Mission. Everything to this point has been reactive: something moved, the estate detected it, decided, and acted. This closing Part is the proactive half, and it exists because the reactive half is provably incomplete — Part IV admitted that some meaning movements are invisible to any comparison, and Part III established that silence from the detection surface is UNVERIFIED and never PROVEN. It follows that an artifact can be stale with no signal ever having been emitted about it, and the only instrument that reaches such an artifact is one that goes and asks. But the estate cannot ask about everything continuously, so the probe budget must be allocated, and allocating it requires knowing which artifacts are likely to have decayed. That is what a decay model is: a prediction of how fast a unit's currency erodes in the absence of any signal, and it is the mechanism that turns an unaffordable universal re-verification into an affordable targeted one. Knowledge does not decay at a uniform rate, and a system that treats it as though it does will either re-verify everything, which nobody will pay for, or re-verify on a flat schedule, which spends its budget uniformly across units whose decay rates differ by orders of magnitude.

7.2 The four decay classes. Model the decay explicitly and there are four kinds of unit. The near-permanent unit decays so slowly that its currency is effectively unconditioned: a physical constraint, a mathematical identity, a sealed law that the estate has promoted into its rule register precisely because it has stopped moving. Re-verifying it is nearly free of value and its probe budget is correspondingly near zero. The fast-decaying unit loses currency in hours or days: the state of a working tree, which process is alive, what a running session currently holds, the value of a measurement taken against a moving system. Its currency claim is almost always stale by the time it is read, and the correct treatment is often not to re-verify it but to refuse to compile it as a durable artifact at all — a unit that decays faster than the estate can observe it should be fetched live and never held. The scheduled-decay unit erodes on a clock the estate can predict: an environment description, a dependency version, a credential window, anything with a known refresh cadence, and it is the class the expiry field serves natively. And the event-decayed unit does not erode with time at all but falls the instant a specific event occurs: a decision stands until its revision condition fires, a constraint holds until its authority lifts it. Its half-life is meaningless and its probe schedule is a waste; what it needs is a watch on its triggering event, which is exactly the revision-condition slot DAIF-02's decision representation already carries.

7.3 The freshness verdict and the expiry field, composed and never forked. DAIF-21 does not compute freshness. GK-07 already issues freshness and integrity verdicts over the knowledge graph's nodes, and the synchronization layer reads those verdicts as an input to its currency decision rather than maintaining a rival notion that would inevitably drift from them — two freshness authorities disagreeing about the same node is the authority collision the constitution convicts, and the resolution is the one the corpus always applies: there is one authority, it is GK-07's, and DAIF-21 composes it. Nor does DAIF-21 define an expiry type. DAIF-01's Validity dimension already carries the window within which a typed cognitive object is current, and the decay model's output is written into that field rather than into a parallel structure, so a consumer that can read a DAIF-01 object can read its expiry without knowing DAIF-21 exists. The composition is what keeps this dataset thin: DAIF-21 contributes the decay model — the prediction of erosion rate — and contributes it into slots and verdicts that two sealed systems already own. It adds a rate to an existing field and an input to an existing verdict, and it adds nothing else.

7.4 The conservative default, and why permanence is the fatal assumption. A unit arrives with no decay model far more often than it arrives with one, and the rule for that unit is stated without exception: a unit with no decay model is treated as decaying. It is not treated as permanent, it is not treated as fresh, and its absence of a model is never read as a claim about its stability — it is read as what it is, an absence of information, which under the invariant Part III composed from SQI resolves to UNVERIFIED and therefore to SUSPECT. The reason the default runs this direction rather than the other is the whole argument of Part I compressed into a single design choice. Assuming permanence is comfortable, it is cheap, it is almost always locally correct, and it is the exact error that converts a knowledge base into a museum of confident falsehoods — a register full of beautifully typed, rigorously gated, fully provenanced units, every one of them describing a world that has moved, every one of them trusted in direct proportion to how long it has sat there untouched. The museum is not a hypothetical failure state. It is the default state of every knowledge system that does not model decay, and it is reached not by any single error but by the accumulation of thousands of individually-reasonable decisions to leave a currently-correct unit alone.

7.5 The half-life metric and its data source. The decay model is measurable and must be measured, because an unmeasured decay model is an assumption with a schedule attached. The metric is half-life per unit class: the elapsed time after which half the units of a given class, sampled and probed, are found to no longer match reality. Its data source is the estate's own repair history, which is a real and growing record — every synchronization obligation DAIF-07 closes carries the artifact's class, the interval since its last verification, and whether the recompilation produced a materially different output, and that last field is precisely a probe result. So the half-life of a class is not estimated from theory; it is computed from the observed rate at which units of that class were found stale, and the model improves as the estate repairs itself. Half-life then feeds directly back into the probe budget: a class with a short half-life gets frequent probes, a class with a long one gets rare ones, and a class whose half-life is unmeasured gets the conservative default of 7.4 until enough repairs have accumulated to measure it. The loop is closed and it is self-correcting, which is the property that distinguishes a decay model from a guess written into a configuration once and never revisited.

7.6 The anti-Goodhart countermetric, which is the sharpest one in this dataset. A decay model is a cost lever, and this makes it the most corruptible object in DAIF-21, because there is exactly one move that reduces recompilation cost to zero and it is available at any time: declare everything permanent. A model tuned to minimize recompilation cost will discover this move immediately. It will lengthen half-lives, extend expiry windows, promote units into the near-permanent class, and it will be rewarded on every cost metric the estate tracks while quietly rebuilding the museum. The countermetric that forbids this is the sampled staleness rate of units the model declared fresh: draw a sample from the units currently marked VALID with a live freshness verdict, probe them directly against reality, and measure what fraction is stale. That number is the model's honesty, it is orthogonal to cost by construction, and it must be read beside every cost improvement the model ever claims. A model that halved recompilation cost while its sampled staleness rate rose has not improved; it has moved the failure from a place where it was paid in compute to a place where it will be paid in a confident wrong action, which is the trade this entire dataset exists to refuse. Cost may never be read alone.

7.7 Failure modes, and the closing of the corpus. Three failures end this Part. The unmodeled unit, which is handled by the conservative default and would otherwise sit fresh forever. The optimistic model, which is the museum, guarded by the sampled staleness countermetric of 7.6. And the pessimistic model, which probes everything constantly, exhausts the budget on near-permanent units, and gets itself switched off — the same self-defeating over-firing that Part III named as alert fatigue and Part V named as the unbounded cascade, appearing here for the third and final time, because it is the recurring shape of every mechanism in this dataset that tries to be safe by being total. The corpus closes here for a reason. DAIF-01 typed the estate's knowledge, DAIF-02 compiled it, DAIF-03 proved the compilation faithful, DAIF-07 tracked the work it owed, DAIF-04 held the contracts between its systems, and DAIF-08 assembled it into missions — and every one of those artifacts is a photograph. DAIF-21 is the mechanism that keeps checking whether the world is still in the frame, and without it the corpus is a perfect, typed, gated, provenanced, and increasingly false account of a moment that has passed.

PART VII FINAL LAW. Knowledge decays at four rates — near-permanent, fast, scheduled, and event-triggered — and DAIF-21 contributes only the decay rate, writing it into DAIF-01's Validity window and feeding GK-07's freshness verdict rather than forking either; a unit with no decay model receives the conservative default and is treated as decaying, because assuming permanence is the single error that turns a knowledge base into a museum of confident falsehoods; and the half-life metric, computed from the estate's own repair history rather than from theory, is governed by the sharpest countermetric in this dataset — the sampled staleness rate of units the model declared fresh — because a decay model tuned to reduce recompilation cost will simply declare everything permanent, score perfectly on every cost measure, and quietly rebuild the museum it was created to prevent.

PART VIII — DRIFT: THE SLOW DIVERGENCE

8.1 Mission. Everything the dataset has built to this point assumes an event. Part III fuses signal sources that fire when something moves, Part V walks a closure from a changed unit outward, Part VI opens an obligation against a detected movement — and every one of those mechanisms is triggered by a discrete thing that happened at a nameable moment. Drift is the failure that has no such moment. It is accumulated, unannounced divergence: no single edit moved the meaning, and a hundred of them did. Each individual movement was too small to cross a threshold, too local to reach a closure, too defensible to argue with, and too innocent to log — and the sum of them has relocated the system out from under every artifact that described it. Drift is therefore not a class in Part II's taxonomy and must not be filed as one; it is a mode of change that is orthogonal to the taxonomy, capable of expressing itself through any of the twelve classes, and structurally invisible to every instrument the previous Parts built. A detector that fires on events cannot fire on drift, because drift is precisely the absence of an event large enough to fire on. This Part names the drift classes, explains why event-based detection is not merely weak against them but definitionally blind, and establishes the only mechanism that reaches them: periodic reconciliation, specified in Part IX.

8.2 Specification drift. The first class, and the one the estate notices last. A specification is a compiled representation of an intended system, and it is treated by every downstream consumer — an actor planning work, a gate deciding whether an output conforms, an obligation whose closure condition cites it — as an authoritative account of what the system is. Specification drift is the slow parting of that account from the system it describes, and it happens through a sequence of individually reasonable acts: a feature is shipped slightly wider than specified because the wider form was obviously better, a case the specification did not anticipate is handled in a way the specification would not have chosen, an edge is trimmed under time pressure and never written back. Each act leaves the specification technically wrong in a way too small to be worth a revision, and the estate's judgment on that smallness is almost always locally correct. The failure is that the smallness does not accumulate as smallness. Twenty such acts do not leave a specification twenty units wrong; they leave it describing a system that no longer exists, while every artifact derived from it inherits the falsehood, and every actor who reads it reasons from a plan of a thing that was never built. And the specification's authority does not decay with its accuracy — it retains its full standing as the governing account right up to the moment someone builds against it and discovers the gap by damage.

8.3 Documentation drift, implementation drift, and decision drift. Documentation drift is the same disease in a lower-authority organ, and it is the one every estate acknowledges and none repairs, because documentation's wrongness is diffuse and its correction is unrewarded. Its damage is not that a reader is misinformed once; it is that a documentation surface known to be unreliable is a surface that stops being read, at which point the estate has paid the full cost of maintaining it and receives none of the benefit. Implementation drift is sharper and more dangerous: two components that were once consistent no longer are. A validation rule enforced in two places, kept in step by nothing but the memory of the party who wrote both, and edited in one place last quarter. A default value duplicated across a runtime and its own test harness. A boundary condition that two modules agreed on and one of them has since narrowed. Nothing in either component is wrong on its own terms; the wrongness lives entirely in the relationship, which is exactly the meaning-in-the-interaction case Part IV named as invisible to any comparison that looks at one unit at a time. Decision drift is the governance instance: practice has quietly departed from the decision that governs it. The decision was never reversed, never revised, never argued with — it was simply not followed, once, then twice, then routinely, until the recorded decision and the actual practice are two different systems and only one of them is running.

8.4 Threshold drift, and why it is the most corrosive. A threshold is the numeric expression of a standard, and threshold drift is the erosion of that standard by a sequence of relaxations each of which is individually defensible on its own evidence. The gate demanded a coverage floor and the floor was lowered by two points because a legitimate refactor made the old floor unreachable this week. The loss budget was widened because one genuinely hard compilation needed the room. The staleness window was extended because a probe was expensive and the class turned out to be slower-moving than modelled. Not one of those moves was dishonest, and every one of them would survive a review on its own merits. The sum of them is a gate that constrains nothing — a gate that has never once refused, that costs its full operating expense every cycle, and that is cited by every done-claim in the estate as evidence of rigor. This is where DAIF-21 composes DAIF-03's governance-event rule and does not fork it: a threshold movement is a governance event, it requires the authority that set the threshold, and it must be recorded with the evidence that justified it, precisely so that the sequence is auditable as a sequence. The corrosion is not in any single relaxation; it is in the fact that the relaxations were never read together. A drift-aware estate reads them together, because the standard's true value is not the number in the configuration but the trajectory of that number over time, and a monotone downward trajectory is a defect no matter how good each step's paperwork was.

8.5 Why drift evades event-based detection entirely. It is tempting to answer drift with a more sensitive detector — lower the threshold, widen the signal set, fire on smaller movements — and the temptation must be refused, because the proposal misunderstands what drift is. Drift is not a small event that the detector missed. Drift is the absence of an event, and that absence is the definition rather than a symptom. Every individual movement in a drift sequence is genuinely below the consequence line; classifying each as consequential would not detect drift, it would abolish the taxonomy and re-create the alert fatigue of Part III at maximum volume, delivering a detector that fires constantly, is universally dismissed, and still misses drift because the sequence — the only thing that carries the meaning — is nowhere represented in the stream of individual firings. The deeper reason is a property of the derivation graph. Drift frequently produces no edge to traverse at all: the specification did not change, so nothing derived from the specification is invalidated by any signal, and the specification's falsity has no origin node from which a closure could be computed. The invalidation engine of Part V, walking outward from a changed unit, will never reach it, because there is no changed unit. Drift is invisible to the engine not because the engine is bad but because the engine's input does not exist.

8.6 The consequence: reconciliation, not notification. If the event surface cannot see drift, and it cannot, then the only remaining instrument is the one that does not wait for a signal: go and look. Take the representation, independently re-derive it from observed reality, and compare. This is the reconciliation posture that DAIF-21 composes whole from SQI's reality-supremacy hierarchy, and it is the same argument the hierarchy always makes, applied to a new failure: observed reality outranks every representation of it, and the burden sits on the representation to demonstrate that it still matches. Against a discrete change that burden can be discharged by a signal, because a signal is a cheap proxy for an observation. Against drift there is no signal, so the burden can be discharged only by the observation itself, which means the estate must periodically re-derive its representations from reality and compare them, on a cadence, forever, because nothing whatsoever will announce that they have parted. This is the structural reason Part IX exists as a Part rather than as a footnote to Part III. Notification and reconciliation are not two implementations of detection; they are two different mechanisms answering two different questions — did something move, and are we still right — and an estate that runs only the first has a detection layer that is complete against events and totally blind against time.

8.7 Metrics, failure modes, and what consumes this Part. Drift is measured by drift-detection lag: the interval between the moment a representation ceased to match reality and the moment the estate registered the mismatch. It is computable only retroactively, at the point a reconciliation cycle disagrees, by bisecting the change history to find where the parting actually began — which is expensive, is worth paying on a sample, and is the only number that reveals how long the estate has been reasoning from a false account. Its anti-Goodhart countermetric is the reconciliation disagreement rate held beside it: lag looks superb in an estate whose reconciliation never disagrees with anything, and a cycle that never disagrees is not a healthy estate, it is an instrument that is measuring nothing. The signature failure of this Part is the drift declared acceptable — each divergence noticed, judged minor, and left, with no register anywhere that accumulates the judgments, so the tenth minor divergence is assessed exactly as innocently as the first and the sequence is never seen as a sequence. The second is the drift attributed to noise: a reconciliation disagreement dismissed as measurement error because the alternative is expensive. This Part's output is consumed by Part IX, which specifies the mechanism, by Part X, which prices it, and by the UKDL register, because a drift class that has twice escaped an estate's reconciliation cadence is not an incident to write up but a law to seal.

PART VIII FINAL LAW. Drift is accumulated, unannounced divergence — specification drift, documentation drift, implementation drift between two components that were once consistent, decision drift where practice has quietly departed from the decision that still governs it, and threshold drift where every relaxation is individually defensible and the sum is a gate that constrains nothing, which is why a threshold movement is a governance event under DAIF-03's rule and must be read as a trajectory rather than as a number; drift evades event-based detection not because the detector is weak but because there is no event, and that absence is the definition, so no closure can be walked from a changed unit that never changed; therefore drift is reachable by exactly one mechanism, periodic reconciliation, composing SQI's posture that the representation must be re-derived from observed reality and compared on a cadence, forever, because nothing will ever announce that the two have parted.

PART IX — RECONCILIATION: THE PERIODIC RE-DERIVATION

9.1 Mission. Reconciliation is the mechanism drift requires and the only one that reaches it. Its statement is three clauses long and each is load-bearing: take the representation, independently re-derive it from observed reality, and compare — the two must agree, or one of them is wrong. It is not a re-read of the representation, which merely confirms that the representation says what it says. It is not a re-run of the compilation that produced it, which merely confirms that the same input yields the same output. It is a fresh derivation from the world, conducted by an instrument that does not depend on the representation being checked, whose output is then set beside the representation and compared field by field. The comparison has three possible outcomes, and all three are informative. The two agree, and the representation's currency is affirmatively established rather than merely unchallenged — this is the only path by which an artifact earns VALID under Part V's vocabulary rather than being defaulted into it. The two disagree, and one of them is wrong, which is the question Part XI answers and which this Part must not prejudge. Or the re-derivation cannot be performed at all, which is UNKNOWN and which degrades to SUSPECT by the rule Part V already sealed.

9.2 The cycle and its objects. A reconciliation cycle takes a scope, an instrument, and a budget, and produces a verdict per artifact in scope plus a disagreement record for every mismatch. The scope is a set of artifacts selected by the cadence policy of 9.3, never the whole estate, because a reconciliation that must be total is a reconciliation that will be skipped. The instrument is an executed derivation — the probe of Part III raised from a spot check to a systematic re-derivation — and its independence is the cardinal discipline of 9.4. The budget is the ceiling on observations the cycle may spend, and it is bounded in advance rather than discovered at the end, because an unbounded cycle competes with the estate's real work and loses. The output is not a repair. Reconciliation detects; it emits disagreement records into the invalidation engine of Part V exactly as a push signal would, and the engine assigns the currency verdicts, and Part VI's driver opens the obligations. The separation matters: a reconciliation cycle that repaired what it found would be a compiler, a fidelity gate, and an obligation lifecycle all at once, forked from three sealed systems, drifting from all of them — which would make the drift-detection mechanism the estate's largest new source of drift.

9.3 Cadence: risk-weighted, never uniform. A uniform cadence is the cheapest policy to specify and the worst one to run, because it spends the same attention on the near-permanent unit that nothing will ever falsify as on the load-bearing decision that half the estate derives from. Cadence is therefore weighted by expected damage, which is the product of three factors the estate can actually estimate. The first is centrality: how many artifacts derive from this one, read directly off GK-04's typed derivation edges, so that a unit with a wide closure is reconciled before a leaf. The second is decay rate: the half-life class from Part VII, so that a fast-decaying unit is checked on a short cycle and a near-permanent one is checked rarely and honestly rather than never and silently. The third is consequence: what it would cost to act on this artifact if it were wrong, which is a property of what the artifact governs rather than of the artifact itself — a constraint that gates a production action and a note that records a preference are not equivalently urgent even at identical centrality. Reconcile the load-bearing before the peripheral, and reconcile the peripheral honestly-rarely rather than pretending to a coverage the budget does not fund. An estate that claims a universal cadence it does not actually run has replaced a known gap with an unknown one.

9.4 The cardinal discipline: an independent instrument. This is the rule that makes reconciliation worth running, and it composes SQI directly. The re-derivation must be performed by an instrument that is independent of the one that produced the representation, because a measurement re-taken with the same broken ruler will confirm itself every time and produce a cycle of perfect, meaningless agreement. If the representation says a gate passes because the compilation read a configuration file, the reconciliation may not re-read that configuration file — it must run the gate. If the representation says a dependency resolves to a version because a manifest declares it, the reconciliation may not re-parse the manifest — it must resolve the dependency. If the representation records that a rule is being followed because the rule exists in the register, the reconciliation may not re-read the register — it must sample the practice. The estate's own repair history offers the sharpest instance: a claim grounded in a green signal is reconciled by observing the behavior, never by re-reading the signal, because a gate that was never wired emits exactly the silence of a gate that passed, and only the behavior distinguishes them. Independence is not a nicety of experimental hygiene. It is the entire difference between an observation and an echo, and a reconciliation layer that echoes is the most expensive way to learn nothing that any estate has ever built.

9.5 The failure mode of reconciliation theater. A cycle that runs, always passes, and has never once disagreed with the representation it checks is not a healthy estate — it is either measuring nothing or measuring itself, and those are the only two possibilities. The first case is the inert cycle: the re-derivation silently fails, or is scoped to nothing, or checks a field that cannot vary, and its unbroken record of agreement is an artifact of its own emptiness. The second case is the dependent cycle of 9.4: the instrument reads the same source the representation was compiled from, so agreement is guaranteed by construction and carries exactly zero information about reality. Both present identically from the outside — a green cycle, a clean dashboard, a rising confidence — and both are worse than no cycle at all, because an estate without reconciliation knows it is blind to drift, while an estate with theatrical reconciliation has been told in its own authoritative voice that it is not. The diagnostic is blunt and it is the corpus's standing rule about gates: a gate never observed to refuse is indistinguishable from an inert one. A reconciliation cycle must therefore be tested against a known-drifted artifact — an artifact deliberately allowed to part from reality, or one recovered from the repair history — and it must be observed to disagree, or it does not ship. Disagreement capability is proven, never assumed.

9.6 Adversarial cases and the honest gaps. The adversary against reconciliation is anything that makes agreement cheaper than truth. The first case is the instrument that quietly degrades to the dependent form: an expensive live probe replaced, under budget pressure, by a cached reading of the same value, which preserves the cycle's cost profile, preserves its green record, and destroys its meaning — and which is invisible unless the independence of the instrument is itself an audited property rather than a design intention. The second is the scope that shrinks toward the agreeable: a cadence policy tuned by disagreement rate will learn, if left to itself, that reconciling the peripheral produces fewer disagreements and lower cost, and will drift its scope away from exactly the load-bearing artifacts the policy of 9.3 exists to prioritize. The third is the disagreement dismissed as noise, which is Part VIII's failure appearing here in its most tempting form, because a disagreement is expensive and a measurement-error explanation is free. The honest gap this Part must name is that reconciliation cannot re-derive what was never derivable: the shared assumption nobody wrote down has no representation to compare against and no instrument to re-derive it with, and against that class reconciliation is as silent as the event surface. Naming the gap is the whole defense, because a named blindness invites a probe and an unnamed one invites confidence.

9.7 Metrics, countermetrics, and consumers. Reconciliation is measured on three numbers. The disagreement rate — the fraction of reconciled artifacts whose re-derivation differed from the representation — which is the cycle's proof of life and which must never be zero over any meaningful window. Coverage-weighted-by-consequence — the fraction of the estate's load-bearing artifacts reconciled within their cadence, weighted by what they govern rather than counted flat — because a raw coverage percentage is trivially maximized by reconciling ten thousand trivial units and no consequential ones. And instrument-independence, audited as a property of each probe rather than trusted as an intention. The anti-Goodhart countermetric on the disagreement rate is the false-VALID rate of Part XII, sampled independently: a cycle can raise its disagreement rate by becoming noisy, which looks like vigilance and is merely error, so disagreement must be read beside the fraction of disagreements that produced a real repair. And the countermetric on coverage is the disagreement rate within the covered set, because coverage rises fastest by reconciling exactly what cannot disagree. Reconciliation emits into Part V's invalidation engine and nowhere else; it is priced by Part X, which decides what the estate can afford to recompile once reconciliation has found what has parted.

PART IX FINAL LAW. Reconciliation is the periodic re-derivation drift requires — take the representation, independently re-derive it from observed reality, compare, and either the two agree, in which case the artifact has earned VALID rather than been defaulted into it, or they disagree, in which case one of them is wrong and Part XI decides which — run on a risk-weighted cadence that reconciles the load-bearing before the peripheral and the peripheral honestly-rarely rather than pretending to a coverage the budget does not fund; its cardinal discipline, composed from SQI, is that the instrument must be independent of the one that produced the representation, because a measurement re-taken with the same broken ruler will confirm itself every time; and its signature failure is reconciliation theater, a cycle that runs, always passes, and has never once disagreed, which is not a healthy estate but an instrument that is either measuring nothing or measuring itself, and which is worse than no cycle at all because it has told the estate, in the system's own voice, that it is not blind.

PART X — THE RECOMPILATION ECONOMY

10.1 Mission. Detection is cheap, invalidation is cheaper, and repair is where the estate actually pays. Recompilation is not free: it consumes compute, it consumes the fidelity gate's attention, it opens obligations that consume actors, and every unit of it is drawn from the same finite budget the estate uses to do its real work. A synchronization layer that recompiles everything on every change has not solved staleness — it has replaced staleness with paralysis, which is a worse trade, because a paralyzed estate will switch the layer off and be left with staleness anyway, plus a period of disruption and a durable institutional conviction that reality synchronization is a luxury. This Part therefore prices the repair. It states the expected-value calculation that governs what gets recompiled and when, specifies lazy invalidation as the mechanism that lets the estate be conservative about marking and economical about rebuilding, bounds the batch, and names the two symmetric ways an economy fails: the storm, which recompiles until nobody will pay, and the sedated economy, which recompiles nothing and turns the entire engine into decoration.

10.2 The expected-value calculation. The decision to recompile an artifact now is a comparison between two quantities, and both are estimable from data the estate already holds. On one side is the cost of recompiling: the compute of DAIF-02's incremental pass over the affected closure, the fidelity re-evaluation DAIF-03 charges, the obligation DAIF-07 must age and close, and the actor attention any of those consume. On the other side is the expected cost of not recompiling, which is the product of three terms — the probability the artifact is used before it would otherwise be repaired, the probability it is actually wrong given the change that reached it, and the cost of acting on it while wrong. The first term is measurable from access history and it is the term that varies most: most artifacts in any estate are never read, and recompiling an artifact nobody will consult is pure loss. The second is the decay model of Part VII combined with the change class's reach from Part II. The third is a property of what the artifact governs, which is the same consequence weighting the reconciliation cadence uses in Part IX. Recompile when the expected cost of staleness exceeds the cost of repair, and not before. This is DAIF-21 composing CO-01's work-per-cost discipline rather than inventing a rival economics: the question is always what work this expenditure buys, and an expenditure that buys the refresh of an artifact no consumer will ever read has bought nothing at all.

10.3 Selective recompilation and the priority order. The calculation yields an order rather than a binary, and the order is the whole of the policy. Recompile the load-bearing first: the artifacts with wide derivation closures, whose falsity propagates to many dependents, and whose repair therefore purchases the currency of everything beneath them. Recompile the frequently-used next: an artifact read weekly by an actor about to act is worth more repaired than an artifact read once a year, and the access record is the cheapest input this whole dataset consumes. Leave the cold and the peripheral SUSPECT. That last clause is the counterintuitive one and it is the load-bearing economic move of this Part: an artifact that is marked SUSPECT and never read has cost the estate exactly nothing and has lost it exactly nothing, because SUSPECT is not a defect, it is a truthful statement about what the estate knows. The verdict is cheap to assign, it is honest, and it carries its own protection — a consumer who reaches for a SUSPECT artifact is required by Part V's contract to re-verify before use. The estate does not need every artifact to be fresh. It needs every artifact to be either fresh or truthfully labelled as not-known-fresh, and the second is available at near-zero cost.

10.4 Lazy invalidation: mark now, recompile on demand. The mechanism that operationalizes 10.3 is lazy invalidation, and its rule is a single line: marking is eager, rebuilding is lazy. When a change is detected, the invalidation engine walks the full closure immediately and marks every reachable artifact — the marking is a graph traversal and a verdict write, it costs a fraction of a recompilation, and it must never be deferred, because a deferred mark leaves a stale artifact wearing a VALID verdict, which is the cardinal failure of the entire dataset. The recompilation, by contrast, is deferred until the artifact is actually requested, at which point the request itself proves the first term of 10.2's expected-value calculation — this artifact will be used, right now, by an actor who is about to act — and the estate pays for the rebuild exactly when the rebuild has demonstrable value. The consumer waits for a repair rather than acting on a photograph, which is the correct trade, and the artifacts nobody requests are never rebuilt at all, which is the correct saving. The discipline that makes lazy invalidation safe is the one that makes it different from procrastination: the eager mark is non-negotiable and the lazy rebuild is a policy. An estate that defers the mark to save the traversal has not built a lazy economy, it has built a slow lie.

10.5 The bounded batch. Even with selectivity and laziness, a single high-reach change can produce a closure larger than any cycle should attempt at once — an authority re-ranking, an architectural boundary redrawn, a widely-derived decision reversed. The batch must therefore be bounded in advance, and the bounding is subject to one invariant that separates it from the wrongly-bounded cascade Part V convicted: bounding the recompilation batch may never bound the invalidation. The marks are complete, always, over the entire closure; only the rebuilds are queued and rate-limited. An artifact whose rebuild is queued behind the batch limit is SUSPECT and is honestly labelled SUSPECT, and a consumer reaching for it either waits for its promotion in the queue or declines to use it — but it is never, under any budget pressure whatsoever, left marked VALID because the estate could not afford to rebuild it this cycle. The batch bound also protects the fidelity gate, which is a serial resource with a finite attention budget: a storm of recompilations arriving at DAIF-03 at once produces either a queue nobody drains or, far worse, pressure to wave them through, and a waved-through recompilation is an artifact carrying an inherited PASS, which is the failure Part VI already sealed against.

10.6 The two failures: the storm and the sedative. The recompilation storm is the loud failure. A change with a badly-bounded reach, or an economy with no expected-value gate, cascades into a rebuild of a substantial fraction of the estate; the compute bill spikes, the fidelity queue saturates, the obligation register fills with work nobody can service, and within two or three cycles the actors have learned to override the synchronization layer as routine. The engine is then decorative, and it is decorative in the most expensive possible way, because it is still running. The sedated economy is the quiet failure and it is strictly worse. Tune the expected-value gate conservatively enough — raise the probability thresholds, lengthen the decay half-lives, discount the cost-of-being-wrong term — and the calculation will conclude that nothing is worth recompiling, ever. Cost falls to zero. Every efficiency metric the estate tracks improves. The detector still detects, the engine still invalidates, the marks are still written, and not one artifact is ever repaired, so the estate accumulates a beautifully precise, fully typed, exhaustively provenanced register of its own falsehood and acts on it anyway. This is the museum of Part VII rebuilt through the cost lever instead of the decay lever, and it is reached by the same route: a sequence of individually reasonable economies. The countermetric is stated in 10.7 and it is not optional.

10.7 Metrics, countermetrics, and consumers. The economy is measured on recompilation cost per change, on the actioned-recompilation rate — the fraction of rebuilds whose output differed materially from its predecessor, which is the only evidence that the expenditure bought anything — and on the lazy-rebuild hit rate, the fraction of SUSPECT artifacts that were actually requested and therefore rebuilt, which validates the deferral policy against the possibility that the estate is deferring exactly what it needs. Every one of these is a cost metric and every cost metric is corruptible in the same direction, so the binding countermetric is the one this Part refuses to let go of: cost may never be read alone. Beside every cost improvement stands the false-VALID rate of Part XII, sampled independently, and the repair backlog — the count and age of artifacts marked SUSPECT that have never been rebuilt and are being read anyway. This is DAIF-21 composing CO-12's Telemetry-Before-Claims rather than asserting an efficiency it has not observed: a claim that the economy improved is a claim about measured burn and measured staleness together, and an economy that reports the first without the second has reported nothing. The outputs are consumed by DAIF-02, which performs the selective rebuild, by DAIF-07, which owns the queued obligations, and by every consumer at the point of use, because a lazy economy only works if the SUSPECT verdict is honored where the artifact is read.

PART X FINAL LAW. Recompilation is not free and an engine that rebuilds everything on every change has traded staleness for paralysis, which the estate will resolve by switching the engine off, so repair is governed by expected value — the cost of recompiling against the probability of use times the probability of being wrong times the cost of being wrong — composing CO-01's work-per-cost discipline: recompile the load-bearing and the frequently-used first, leave the cold and the peripheral honestly SUSPECT, and run lazy invalidation where marking is eager and complete over the full closure while rebuilding is deferred until the artifact is actually requested, because a deferred mark is not laziness but a slow lie; the batch is bounded and the bound may never bound the invalidation, only the rebuilds; and cost may never be read alone, because the storm is loud and survivable while the economy tuned so conservatively that nothing is ever recompiled scores perfectly on every efficiency measure and turns the entire engine into decoration.

PART XI — CONFLICT WITH REALITY: WHEN THE REPRESENTATION IS RIGHT AND REALITY IS WRONG

11.1 Mission. Every mechanism in this dataset to this point has carried an unexamined premise, and it is time to examine it. The premise is that when the representation and reality disagree, reality wins and the representation must be repaired. It follows straight from SQI's reality-supremacy hierarchy, it is correct in the overwhelming majority of cases, and it is exactly the inversion that makes an automated synchronization engine dangerous — because sometimes the divergence is not staleness at all. Sometimes it is a regression. The code drifted away from a decision that is still correct. The environment broke a contract that still binds. An actor violated a constraint that still holds. In every one of those cases the representation is right and reality is wrong, and a system that blindly synchronizes the representation to match reality has not repaired anything: it has laundered a violation into the new normal, over-written the record of what was decided with the record of what happened, and destroyed the only artifact that could have caught the regression. An unthinking reality-sync engine is an automated mechanism for ratifying every regression it finds, and it will do so silently, promptly, and with a clean fidelity gate. This is the most important Part in the dataset.

11.2 Reality supremacy is epistemic, not normative, and the corpus must not confuse the two. The confusion is subtle enough to be worth stating precisely. SQI's hierarchy is a claim about knowledge: when you want to know what the system does, the observation outranks the plan, the documentation, the reconstruction, and the green signal. It is not a claim about value. It does not say that whatever the system does is what the system ought to do. Observed reality is supreme as evidence of what is; it has no authority whatsoever over what should be. The representation, meanwhile, is often the only artifact in the estate that carries the ought — a decision is a record of what was chosen, a constraint is a record of what is forbidden, a contract is a record of what was promised, and none of those are descriptions of the world that can be falsified by the world declining to conform. A constraint that the system violates is not thereby a false constraint. It is a violated one. And the correct response to a violated constraint is not to relax the constraint until it becomes true; it is to fix the system until the constraint is true again. DAIF-21 therefore composes reality supremacy for one purpose only — determining what the world currently is — and refuses it categorically for the other, determining what the world should be.

11.3 The discriminator: authority and intent. If both readings of a divergence are available, the engine needs a discriminator, and it must be one that is computable from artifacts the estate already holds rather than one that requires a judgment call at every mismatch. The discriminator is authority and intent, composed from DAIF-01's authority ladder and CIR-3's Intent IR, and it asks one question of the change that produced the divergence: was there an authorizing decision behind it. A change made by a party with the authority to make it, carrying an intent that says what it was for, is an update — the world moved because someone with standing moved it, and the representation is now the stale artifact and must be recompiled. A divergence with no authorizing decision behind it — no party of sufficient authority, no recorded intent, no revision of the governing decision, nothing but a difference that appeared — is a violation. It generates an obligation to fix reality, not a recompilation to bless it. The two verdicts are not two grades of the same event; they run in opposite directions and produce opposite work, and the entire correctness of the synchronization layer at the point of divergence rests on the engine asking which one it is before it acts.

11.4 The mechanism, and what it demands of the estate. The discriminator is only computable if the estate records the two things it consults, which turns 11.3 into a requirement rather than a heuristic. Authority must be attached to changes, which is why DAIF-01's authority dimension is a field on the typed object rather than a convention. Intent must be attached to changes, which is why CIR-3 exists and why a change arriving without one is a change the engine cannot classify. When a divergence is found, the engine resolves the change that produced it, reads its authority against the authority of the representation it contradicts, and compares. If the change's authority equals or exceeds the representation's, and an intent is present, the verdict is UPDATE and the pipeline of Parts V through X runs as specified. If the change's authority is lower than the representation's, the verdict is VIOLATION and the pipeline runs backwards: the obligation opened is against reality, its closure condition is that the system again conforms to the representation, and the representation itself is not touched, not marked SUSPECT, and above all not recompiled. And if the authority cannot be resolved — no attributable party, no recorded intent, a change that simply appeared — the verdict is UNKNOWN, and UNKNOWN does not degrade to UPDATE. It degrades to VIOLATION-SUSPECT, which surfaces to a party with standing, because the failure of defaulting an unattributable change to UPDATE is precisely regression laundering with a plausible excuse.

11.5 Regression laundering: the failure, named and automated. Consider the sequence, because it is not hypothetical and every estate has run it. A constraint forbids a class of action. An actor, under pressure, performs the action anyway; the code now violates the constraint. A reconciliation cycle, running exactly as Part IX specifies, re-derives the practice from observed reality, finds that it does not match the recorded constraint, and reports a disagreement. The invalidation engine, treating disagreement as staleness, marks the constraint SUSPECT. The recompilation economy, finding the constraint load-bearing and frequently-read, prioritizes its repair. DAIF-02 recompiles it against the new observed practice. DAIF-03 gates the result and finds it faithful — because it is faithful, perfectly faithful, to a source that is now the violation. The obligation closes. Every gate in the estate is green, every metric improves, the repair latency is excellent, and the constraint that forbade the action now permits it, having been rewritten by an automated pipeline to describe the thing it existed to prevent. No party decided this. No authority approved it. The violation has been laundered into the register as law, and the only artifact that could have detected the regression was consumed in the process of ratifying it. Every mechanism in this dataset executed correctly. That is the point: correctness of mechanism is not correctness of direction, and the direction is set here or nowhere.

11.6 Adversarial cases and the hard boundaries. The adversary against the discriminator is the change dressed in authority it does not have. A commit message that asserts an intent nobody approved. A configuration edit made by an automated party whose authority was granted for a narrow purpose and is being spent on a wide one — which is a scope violation wearing an authority credential, and the defense is that authority is scoped in DAIF-01's type system and the scope is checked, not merely the identity. The second adversary is the slow violation, which is Part VIII's drift crossed with this Part's failure: no single act was unauthorized enough to flag, and the accumulated practice now contradicts the decision, with no changed unit anywhere carrying an authority that can be checked. Against this class the discriminator has nothing to resolve, and the correct verdict is VIOLATION-SUSPECT by default, because a practice that has parted from its governing decision with no authorizing revision anywhere in its history is, by the definition of 11.3, exactly a violation regardless of how gradually it arrived. The honest boundary is this: the engine can determine that a divergence is unauthorized, and it can determine what the representation says should be true; it cannot determine whether the representation was ever a good idea. That judgment belongs to a party with standing, and the engine's whole obligation is to put the choice in front of that party rather than to make it silently by rebuilding.

11.7 Metrics, countermetrics, and consumers. This Part is measured on the divergence-classification split — the fraction of divergences classified UPDATE, VIOLATION, and VIOLATION-SUSPECT — and the number that matters is the last one's trajectory, because a high or rising unattributable share means the estate is accumulating changes whose authority it cannot resolve, and every one of them is a candidate laundering. The primary metric is the laundering rate, estimated by sampling closed UPDATE verdicts and asking, of each, whether an authorizing decision genuinely existed; it is expensive, it is worth paying on a sample, and it is the only number that measures the failure of 11.5 directly. Its countermetric is the violation-obligation closure rate: an engine that classifies aggressively toward VIOLATION will score a beautiful laundering rate while generating a backlog of violation obligations that nobody ever closes, which converts a laundering problem into an ignored-alert problem and improves nothing. The two must be read together. This Part's verdicts are consumed by Part V, which must not invalidate a representation on a VIOLATION, by Part VI, which opens the obligation in the correct direction, by DAIF-07, which ages it, and by the UKDL register, because a violation class that recurs is not an incident but a law the estate has failed to enforce and must now seal.

PART XI FINAL LAW. Sometimes the representation is right and reality is wrong — the code drifted from a decision that still stands, the environment broke a contract that still binds, an actor violated a constraint that still holds — and SQI's reality supremacy is epistemic and never normative: the observation outranks every representation as evidence of what is, and has no authority whatsoever over what ought to be, so a violated constraint is not a false constraint but a violated one; the discriminator is authority and intent, composing DAIF-01's authority ladder and CIR-3's Intent IR — a change by an authorized party carrying an intent UPDATES the representation, a divergence with no authorizing decision behind it is a VIOLATION that creates an obligation to fix reality rather than a recompilation to bless it, and an unresolvable authority degrades to VIOLATION-SUSPECT and never to UPDATE — because an engine that synchronizes blindly will recompile a constraint against the very practice that broke it, close the obligation with a clean fidelity gate, and thereby launder every regression it finds into law.

PART XII — METRICS AND ANTI-GOODHART

12.1 Mission. A synchronization layer is a system whose entire output is a claim about its own reliability, which makes it the most self-serving object in the estate and the one whose measurement must be designed with the most suspicion. Every number this dataset can compute about itself is computed from its own detections, its own verdicts, and its own repairs, and every one of them is therefore an account written by the party under audit. This Part specifies the measures, names the data source for each, attaches a countermetric to each, and then states the central gaming risk bluntly, because it is not one risk among many — it is a single move that improves every metric in the set at once, it is always available, it requires no dishonesty, and it will be found by any optimization process left alone with the dashboard.

12.2 The measures and their data sources. Staleness rate: the fraction of artifacts currently marked VALID that do not in fact match reality, sampled and probed independently, never self-reported — a self-reported staleness rate is a tautology, since an artifact the system knows to be stale is by definition not marked VALID. Mean time-to-detect: the interval from a change occurring to the corresponding event being emitted, computed against a held-out set of changes with known timestamps. Mean time-to-invalidate: detection to verdict, read off the engine's own log, which is legitimate because the engine's timing is not the thing in dispute. Mean time-to-recompile: verdict to a re-gated artifact, read off DAIF-07's obligation closure records. The false-VALID rate: of artifacts the system affirmatively certified current, the fraction that were not — this is the number that matters most in the entire dataset, and it is the one number the system structurally cannot measure about itself, because if it could detect a false VALID it would not have issued it. Reconciliation disagreement rate, from Part IX's cycles. Drift-detection lag, from Part VIII's retroactive bisection. Invalidation-to-obligation-closure rate, from DAIF-07. And the recompilation-cost-to-error-prevented ratio, which is the economy's own justification and which is discussed in 12.5 because it is the most corruptible measure here.

12.3 The central gaming risk, stated bluntly. Every metric in 12.2 improves if the system simply detects fewer changes. A detector that sees nothing emits no false positives, so its precision is perfect. It invalidates nothing, so its invalidation precision is undefined and reads as clean. It recompiles nothing, so its cost is zero and its cost-to-error-prevented ratio is unbounded in the flattering direction. It opens no obligations, so its obligation closure rate is one hundred percent of a set with one member. Its mean time-to-invalidate is instantaneous across an empty queue, and its dashboard is beautiful. The only metric that degrades is the false-VALID rate, and that is exactly the metric the system cannot compute about itself — so an estate that measures its synchronization layer using the layer's own outputs will reward the layer, cycle after cycle, for going blind. This is not an exotic adversarial scenario. It is the default gradient. Any tuning process, any cost pressure, any well-meaning effort to reduce alert volume, pushes in this direction, and the only thing that stands against it is a measurement the layer does not control.

12.4 Detection coverage as an absolute, with both terms floored. The defense against 12.3 is structural and it composes DAIF-03's ratio law directly: never gate on a ratio, because a ratio is satisfied by shrinking its denominator. A detection layer measured on precision — the fraction of emitted signals that were real — improves monotonically by emitting fewer signals, and a layer measured on the actioned-invalidation rate improves by invalidating only the certainties. Both are ratios and both are gamed by silence. Detection coverage is therefore gated as an absolute with both terms floored: the count of change classes for which at least one live signal source exists must meet a floor, and the count of changes detected per unit of real change in the estate must meet a floor, and neither is permitted to be traded against the other. The class-resolved form is mandatory, since an aggregate coverage number is maximized by excelling at the classes a content hash can see while remaining totally blind to the behavioral class that costs the most — Part II's countermetric holds the behavioral class out separately for exactly this reason, and this Part re-states it as a gate rather than a report. A floor that both terms must clear cannot be satisfied by shrinking either one, which is the entire property that a ratio lacks.

12.5 The false-VALID rate and the independent sample. The false-VALID rate is the load-bearing number of this dataset and it must be obtained from outside. The method is a sample: draw artifacts at random from those the system currently marks VALID, weight the draw toward the load-bearing so that the estimate reflects consequence rather than count, probe each against reality with an instrument independent of the one that certified it — the Part IX discipline, applied here as a measurement rather than as a cycle — and compute the fraction found stale. The sample must be drawn by a process the synchronization layer does not control, and probed by an instrument the layer did not choose, or it degrades into exactly the reconciliation theater of Part IX with a statistic attached. The number is expensive. It is worth every unit of its cost, because it is the only measurement in the estate that can detect the failure mode of 12.3, and because it is the only evidence that the VALID verdict means anything at all. An estate that has never sampled its false-VALID rate does not know whether its synchronization layer is working; it knows only that the layer is running, and Part IX has already established what those two facts have to do with each other. Two properties of the sample are worth stating because both are routinely lost when the measurement is delegated back to the layer that is under audit. The first is that the draw must include the artifacts the layer is least equipped to reason about — the ones with incomplete lineage, the ones whose sources were acquired before the graph existed, the ones nobody has touched in a year — because those are precisely the artifacts a self-administered sample would exclude for being hard to probe, and they are precisely the population where the false VALID concentrates. The second is that the sample must be repeated on a cadence rather than taken once, because the false-VALID rate is not a property of the layer's design but of the layer's current condition, and a design that produced an excellent rate two quarters ago is exactly the kind of artifact this dataset exists to distrust. A single measurement establishes a point; only a series establishes a trajectory, and only a trajectory can show that the layer is going blind.

12.6 Countermetric pairings, stated explicitly. Each measure travels with the one that forbids its cheapest corruption, and none may be read alone. Detection recall travels with the actioned-signal rate, or a detector maximizes recall by emitting everything and training its consumers to dismiss the channel. Invalidation precision travels with the sampled false-VALID rate, or an engine maximizes precision by bounding ever more tightly until it certifies stale artifacts as current. Repair latency travels with the re-gate pass rate, or a repair pipeline achieves superb latency by skipping DAIF-03 and shipping unverified recompilations at speed. Obligation closure rate travels with closure quality — the fraction of closed obligations whose artifact, probed afterward, actually matches reality — or closure is maximized by closing without doing. Recompilation cost travels with the repair backlog and the false-VALID rate, or the economy is sedated to zero as Part X described. The reconciliation disagreement rate travels with the fraction of disagreements that produced a real repair, or a cycle raises its disagreement rate by becoming noisy, which looks like vigilance and is merely error. And the recompilation-cost-to-error-prevented ratio, which is a ratio and is therefore convicted by its own form, is admissible only as a report and never as a gate, because both of its terms are estimates the layer produces itself and their quotient can be driven anywhere.

12.7 Failure modes of the measurement layer and its consumers. The measurement layer has its own failures and they are worth naming because they are the ones that look most like health. The first is the dashboard that is entirely green because the estate is entirely blind, which 12.3 through 12.5 exist to catch and which no internal number will ever reveal. The second is the metric read without its countermetric, which is not a partial measurement but an actively misleading one, since every metric in 12.6 is optimized in a harmful direction when read alone — a half-instrumented dashboard is worse than an uninstrumented one, because it converts a known ignorance into a false confidence. The third is the sampled false-VALID rate that is measured once, found acceptable, and never repeated, which mistakes a snapshot for a trend and is the photograph fallacy of Part I applied to the instrument that exists to prevent it. The fourth is the metric whose data source is quietly re-pointed: a number that was computed from an independent probe is, after a cost review, computed from the layer's own verdict register instead, and the number's name, its chart, and its accumulated history all survive the substitution intact while its meaning is destroyed — which is exactly why the data source of every measure in 12.2 is named in this dataset rather than left to the instrument to choose, and why a change to a metric's data source is a governance event under the same rule that governs a threshold movement in Part VIII, requiring the authority that set the measure and the evidence that justified the move. The consumers of this Part are the Owner, who decides whether the layer is worth its cost; the recompilation economy of Part X, which may not claim an improvement without the staleness number beside it, composing CO-12's Telemetry-Before-Claims; DAIF-03, whose ratio law this Part composes and whose fidelity verdicts supply the re-gate pass rate that keeps repair latency honest; DAIF-07, whose obligation records are the data source for closure rate and closure quality alike; and the UKDL register, because a metric observed twice to have been gamed is not a tuning problem but a law waiting to be sealed.

PART XII FINAL LAW. The measures are staleness rate, time-to-detect, time-to-invalidate, time-to-recompile, false-VALID rate, reconciliation disagreement rate, drift-detection lag, invalidation-to-obligation-closure rate, and the cost-to-error-prevented ratio, and every one of them improves if the system simply detects fewer changes — a detector that sees nothing has a perfect false-positive rate, zero recompilation cost, an empty obligation queue, and a beautiful dashboard — so detection coverage is never gated as a ratio, which is satisfied by shrinking its denominator, but as an ABSOLUTE with both terms floored and resolved per change class, composing DAIF-03's ratio law; every measure travels with the countermetric that forbids its cheapest corruption and none may be read alone; and the false-VALID rate, the one number that matters most, must be estimated by independent sampling with an instrument the layer did not choose, because it is precisely the number the system cannot measure about itself — if it could see a false VALID it would not have issued one.

PART XIII — FAILURE MODES AND ADVERSARIAL CASES

13.1 Mission. Every preceding Part named the failures local to its own mechanism. This Part assembles them, ranks them by damage, and adds the ones that emerge only from the composition — the failures that no single mechanism produces and that the assembled engine produces reliably. The ordering principle is the one Part I established and every Part since has re-confirmed: a loud failure is survivable and a silent one is not, so the failures are ranked not by how bad they look but by how well they hide. The engine's most dangerous defects are the ones that improve its metrics.

13.2 The false VALID, and why it is the cardinal failure. A stale artifact certified current is the failure this entire dataset exists to prevent, and it is worse than every other failure in this Part combined, for a reason that is structural rather than rhetorical. An estate with no synchronization layer knows it must verify; its consumers carry an appropriate suspicion, they check before they act, and the suspicion is a defense. An estate whose synchronization layer issues a false VALID has replaced that suspicion with an affirmative certification, in the system's own authoritative voice, that verification is unnecessary — and the consumer, correctly trusting the estate's own instruments, acts without checking. The false VALID does not merely fail to protect; it actively disarms the defense that existed before it. This is why UNKNOWN degrades to SUSPECT and never to VALID, why the traversal crosses an edge it cannot classify, why the mark is eager even when the rebuild is lazy, and why the false-VALID rate is sampled from outside. Every conservative choice in this dataset is a payment against this one failure, and every one of those payments is a cost that a future actor optimizing for efficiency will be tempted to stop making. The false VALID also has a compounding property that no other failure here shares. A stale artifact certified current is consumed, and what it is consumed for is the production of further artifacts — a decision reasoned from it, a plan built on it, an obligation whose closure condition cites it — and each of those inherits the falsity while acquiring a fresh compilation timestamp, a clean fidelity verdict, and a currency verdict of its own. The estate does not merely hold one wrong artifact; it manufactures a lineage of confidently-wrong descendants, every one of them provably faithful to a source that was already false, and every one of them wearing the gates that were supposed to catch exactly this. Fidelity, as DAIF-03 defines it, is faithfulness to a source, and it is completely silent about whether the source was still true — which is the precise division of labor between that dataset and this one, and the precise reason a false VALID here defeats every gate downstream of it.

13.3 The cascade failures, in both directions. The unbounded cascade invalidates the world on a routine change, exceeds what the estate will pay, and is overridden into decoration within a few cycles — loud, visible, survivable, and self-limiting, because the actors will not tolerate it. The bounded cascade that bounds wrongly stops one edge short of a real dependent and leaves a stale artifact marked VALID, which is 13.2 arriving through the traversal, and it is silent: no cost signal, no complaint, no symptom, and an affirmative verdict the engine had no right to issue. The recompilation storm is the same asymmetry transposed into the economy — the storm is loud and the sedative is silent — and the sedated economy is the more dangerous by exactly the same logic. The general law recurs so often in this dataset that it should be stated once as a law: for every mechanism here, the over-firing failure is loud and the under-firing failure is silent, the loud one costs money and the silent one costs correctness, and an estate under cost pressure will always be tempted to trade the first for the second because the trade shows up immediately in the budget and never in any number the layer computes about itself.

13.4 Alert fatigue and the muted engine. A detector whose signals are ignored has the same value as no detector, at greater cost, and the mute happens gradually and rationally rather than by any decision anyone would defend. Signals arrive faster than they can be serviced. The first few are triaged. The next few are batched. Then the channel is filtered, then it is muted, then the filter is forgotten, and the estate has a fully functional detection layer whose output reaches nobody. Nothing in the layer's own metrics registers this: recall is unaffected, time-to-detect is unaffected, the emitted-signal count may even improve. The only instrument that sees it is the actioned-signal rate, which is why that countermetric is not an accessory but a vital sign. And the deeper point is that alert fatigue is a detection failure and not a presentation problem — a signal that reaches a consumer who has learned to dismiss the channel has not been detected in any sense the estate should count, so a layer with excellent recall and a collapsed actioned rate has not detected anything at all, and its recall number is a description of work it performed for nobody.

13.5 The self-confirming instruments: reconciliation theater and regression laundering. Two failures in this dataset are worse than wrong, because they are wrong in a way that manufactures confidence. Reconciliation theater is the cycle that measures itself: an instrument that re-derives the representation from the same source the representation was compiled from, producing agreement by construction, an unbroken green record, and a rising institutional certainty that the estate is synchronized. It is detected by one test and no other — the cycle must be run against a known-drifted artifact and observed to disagree, because a gate never observed to refuse is indistinguishable from an inert one. Regression laundering is Part XI's failure automated: the engine finds a divergence, does not ask whether an authorizing decision stood behind it, treats reality as supreme in the normative sense it never had, recompiles the representation against the violation, gates the result as faithful — and it is faithful, to a source that should never have existed — and closes the obligation with every light green. Both failures share a signature: the estate's instruments report health precisely because they have been co-opted by the thing they were built to catch, and neither can be detected by any measurement the instruments themselves produce.

13.6 The economy and the model, tuned to disappear. The decay model tuned to minimize cost declares everything permanent, because that is the one move that reduces recompilation to zero and it is available at any time and it is rewarded by every cost metric the estate tracks. The economy tuned to the same objective sets its expected-value thresholds high enough that no artifact ever clears them, and it rebuilds nothing while its detector detects, its engine invalidates, and its marks accumulate in a register of falsehood the estate reads and acts on anyway. And the invalidation that creates no obligation is the same disappearance in the action phase: the verdict is issued, correctly and precisely, into a channel with no owner and no closure condition, and the estate's decay becomes exquisitely well documented and entirely unrepaired. These three are one failure wearing three costumes — the engine that runs without acting — and they are the terminal state of every synchronization layer that is optimized without its countermetrics, because they are all reachable by a sequence of individually reasonable economies and none of them is visible in any number the layer produces about itself.

13.7 The deepest failure: the sync engine that itself goes stale. The engine is made of artifacts. Its dependency graph is an artifact, compiled once from a world that has since moved. Its decay models are artifacts, fitted from a repair history whose classes may no longer describe the estate. Its detection rules are artifacts, written against a set of signal sources that have themselves been reconfigured, deprecated, or silently disconnected. Its reconciliation instruments are artifacts, and an instrument that was independent when it was built becomes dependent the moment someone, under budget pressure, replaces its live probe with a cached reading. Every one of these is subject to exactly the decay this engine exists to catch, and the engine cannot catch its own, because it would be certifying its own currency with the instruments whose currency is in question — the marking-your-own-homework failure that this corpus convicts everywhere else and must convict here most of all. The consequence is not paradox but architecture: the watcher must be watched, and it must be watched from outside. The engine's own artifacts are reconciled by an instrument the engine does not own; its own liveness is monitored by `liveness`, which is not part of it; its own detection coverage is sampled by the independent process of Part XII, which it does not control; and its own blindnesses, once observed twice, are sealed into the UKDL register, which outlives any configuration the engine holds. A synchronization engine that is exempt from its own law is the last and largest photograph in the estate, and it will be the most trusted one.

PART XIII FINAL LAW. The sync engine fails in eight ways and they are ranked by how well they hide, not by how bad they look — the false VALID (a stale artifact certified current, which does not merely fail to protect but disarms the suspicion that protected the estate before the engine existed), the unbounded cascade (loud, survivable), the cascade that bounds wrongly (silent, and therefore worse), alert fatigue and the muted engine (a detector whose signals are ignored has the value of no detector at greater cost, and this is a detection failure and not a presentation problem), the reconciliation that measures itself, regression laundering automated, the decay model and the economy tuned until nothing is ever recompiled, and the invalidation that creates no obligation — and beneath all of them the deepest one, that the engine's own dependency graph, decay models, detection rules, and instruments are themselves artifacts subject to exactly the decay they exist to catch, so the watcher must be watched, its independence audited, and its liveness monitored by something outside it, because an engine exempt from its own law is the last and most trusted photograph in the estate.

PART XIV — THE MATURITY MODEL

14.1 Mission. This closing Part gives the estate a mirror. The five levels below are not a roadmap and not an aspiration; they are a diagnostic, and the diagnostic's only purpose is to let an estate locate itself honestly, because the characteristic error at every level is to believe one is at the next. Each level is specified by three things: an observable signature, which is what an outside auditor would actually see; a characteristic failure, which is the specific way that level's mechanism betrays the party who trusts it; and the gate to the next level, which is the one capability whose absence keeps the estate where it is. The levels are cumulative — a level-four estate runs everything a level-three estate runs — and they are not skippable, because each level's mechanism is the input to the next.

14.2 Level one: oblivious. The observable signature is that artifacts are used until someone notices they are wrong, and staleness is discovered by damage. There is no detection layer, no invalidation, no decay model, and no reconciliation. Compiled artifacts are produced, gated once at compilation time, and then trusted indefinitely on the strength of that single gate. The estate is not careless — a level-one estate can be rigorous, well-typed, exhaustively provenanced, and fully compliant with every gate DAIF-03 imposes — it simply has no mechanism that carries correctness forward in time. The characteristic failure is the discovery-by-damage cycle itself, and its cruelty is the asymmetry of Part I: the artifacts that cause the most damage are the ones that were right the longest, because they have accumulated the most trust and therefore the least verification. A level-one estate learns about staleness exactly once per incident, learns nothing transferable from it, and repairs the single artifact that hurt it while every sibling artifact compiled from the same moment remains untouched and equally false. The gate to level two is a detection surface: any signal source, wired to any consumer, that reports that something moved.

14.3 Level two: notified, which is where most estates sit. The observable signature is that change events are detected and reported — a change feed, a diff notification, a freshness warning surfaced somewhere — and that they are largely ignored, because nothing actions them. The estate has an event surface, the surface fires, and the signals arrive in a channel with no closure condition, no owner, and no consequence for being disregarded. This is the most common level in existence and it is worth being blunt about why: reaching it is cheap, it produces an immediately visible artifact that looks like progress, and it requires no change to how anyone works. The characteristic failure is stated without softening: a notification nobody actions is indistinguishable from no notification at all — except that it costs more and creates the feeling of safety. That last clause is the whole indictment. A level-two estate has paid for a detection layer, receives none of its protection, and has purchased in exchange an institutional belief that it is protected, which suppresses exactly the manual verification a level-one estate would have performed out of appropriate fear. It is therefore possible for level two to be worse than level one, and the sequence is not a fluke of implementation but the predictable outcome of detection without action. The gate to level three is propagation: the signals must reach the artifacts they falsify.

14.4 Level three: invalidating. The observable signature is that change propagates through the derivation graph and marks artifacts SUSPECT or INVALID — the invalidation engine of Part V is running, it walks GK-04's typed edges, it bounds its traversal, it degrades UNKNOWN to SUSPECT, and its verdicts are checked at the point of use rather than computed for nobody. This is a genuine and large step: the estate now knows, for any artifact it holds, whether a detected change has reached it, and a consumer reaching for a SUSPECT artifact is required to re-verify. The characteristic failure of level three is that it is complete against events and totally blind against time. Everything it catches, it catches because something announced itself; and Part VIII established that the most consequential divergences announce nothing. A level-three estate therefore has excellent hygiene against discrete change and an unexamined and steadily growing population of artifacts that no signal ever touched — which it marks VALID, honestly, by its own lights, because no change reached them, and which are stale for reasons its instruments cannot see. Its confidence in those artifacts is high and rising, which is the shape of the museum. The gate to level four is reconciliation: an independent re-derivation on a cadence.

14.5 Level four: reconciling. The observable signature is that periodic independent re-derivation runs, on a risk-weighted cadence, with instruments that do not depend on the artifacts they check, and that it disagrees — the disagreement rate is nonzero, the disagreements are investigated, and the cycle has been observed to refuse. Drift, which no event ever announces, is now reachable, and the estate's VALID verdict has begun to mean something it did not mean at level three: not merely that no signal arrived, but that an observation was taken and the artifact was found to match. The characteristic failure of level four is that detection has outrun repair. The estate now knows, in high resolution, what is wrong with it — and the knowledge lands in a channel with no obligation attached, or in an economy so conservatively tuned that nothing is rebuilt, or in a repair pipeline that closes obligations without re-clearing the fidelity gate. This is level two's failure recurring at a higher altitude and with better instruments, and it is more insidious here because the instruments are genuinely good: the estate is not deceived about its condition, it is merely unable to act on the diagnosis, and a precise account of one's own decay that generates no repair is a more elegant version of the same paralysis. The gate to level five is the tracked obligation and the governed economy.

14.6 Level five: self-correcting. The observable signature has three parts and all three must hold. Invalidation generates tracked obligations — every SUSPECT and INVALID verdict opens a typed obligation with an owner, an age, and a closure condition that closes only on a re-cleared fidelity gate, handed to DAIF-07, so that no invalidation is a message in a bottle. Recompilation is economically governed — the expected-value calculation of Part X runs, the load-bearing is rebuilt first, the cold is honestly left SUSPECT, the marks are eager and the rebuilds are lazy, and cost is never read without the staleness number beside it. And the false-VALID rate is independently sampled and falling — the one number the system cannot measure about itself is being measured from outside, on a cadence, with an instrument the layer did not choose, and its trajectory is the estate's honest report card. A level-five estate is not free of stale artifacts; that is not the claim and it is not achievable. It is an estate whose stale artifacts are labelled, whose labels are honored at the point of use, whose repairs are tracked and priced, and whose confidence in its own currency is calibrated against an observation rather than against a reputation. That is the whole ambition of this dataset, stated as a condition an estate can be audited against.

14.7 The honest diagnosis and the closing of DAIF-21. Most estates sit at level two, and the reason they stay there is not incompetence but the structure of the incentive: level two produces a visible artifact, satisfies an audit question, costs little, and demands nothing of anyone's working day. Levels three, four, and five each demand something real — a graph that must be maintained, a cadence that must be funded, an obligation that lands on a party's queue and stays there until the work is done — and each of them makes the estate's decay more visible rather than less, which is not a comfortable thing to buy. The countermetric on this entire model is therefore the level-claim audit: an estate's self-assessed level is a claim like any other, and it is checked the way this dataset checks every claim — by observation, with an independent instrument, against the signature rather than the intention. An estate that claims level four and whose reconciliation cycle has never disagreed is at level three with a decoration. An estate that claims level five and whose synchronization obligations close without a re-cleared gate is at level four with a closure rate. DAIF-21 closes here, and the corpus closes with it: DAIF-01 typed the estate's knowledge, DAIF-02 compiled it, DAIF-03 proved the compilation faithful, DAIF-04 held the contracts, DAIF-07 tracked the work owed, DAIF-08 assembled it into missions, and every one of those artifacts is a photograph of a world that has since moved on. This dataset is the mechanism that keeps checking whether the world is still in the frame — and the estate that runs it is not the one with the fewest stale artifacts, but the one that knows which of its artifacts it has actually looked at lately.

PART XIV FINAL LAW. Maturity runs in five levels — oblivious, where staleness is discovered by damage and the most damaging artifact is the one that was right the longest; notified, where change events are detected and reported and largely ignored, which is where most estates sit and where a notification nobody actions is indistinguishable from no notification at all except that it costs more and creates the feeling of safety, making level two capable of being worse than level one; invalidating, where change propagates through the derivation graph and marks artifacts SUSPECT or INVALID, complete against events and totally blind against time; reconciling, where periodic independent re-derivation catches the drift no event ever announces and the cycle has been observed to disagree; and self-correcting, where invalidation generates tracked obligations, recompilation is economically governed, and the false-VALID rate is independently sampled and falling — and an estate's claimed level is itself a claim, audited against its observable signature with an independent instrument, because a reconciliation that never disagrees is a decoration and a closure rate without a re-cleared gate is a number, not a repair.
