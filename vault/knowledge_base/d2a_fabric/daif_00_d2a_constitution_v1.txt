DAIF-00 — DUPLICATE-TO-ADVANTAGE CONSTITUTION AND DUPLICATE ONTOLOGY

PART I — THESIS, MISSION, AND THE CONSTITUTIONAL PROMOTION OF DUPLICATE-TO-ADVANTAGE

1.1 Mission. This dataset states the constitutional law that governs how the Claude Power Pack grows. The law is one sentence: an ecosystem must reinforce what it already has before it creates what it does not, and it may create a sovereign new system only for the irreducible remainder that no reinforcement, extension, composition, or shared-infrastructure extraction can absorb. The status this dataset changes is that Duplicate-to-Advantage already EXISTS in the Power Pack as a single engine — a six-stage prospective-intake pipeline sealed under the C85 milestone, with a fifteen-operation vocabulary and an anti-inflation gate — but it exists as one invokable tool that a session must remember to call. A tool that must be remembered is advisory. This dataset promotes the same discipline from an invokable engine to a constitutional default: the behavior that is applied by every builder on every candidate, whether or not the engine is called, because the law is internalized rather than dispatched. The engine is the executable; this constitution is the law the executable enforces, the ontology it reasons over, and the reason it exists at all. EXISTS: the engine and its root law. SPECIFIED: the constitutional layer built here.

1.2 The problem this solves. A capability ecosystem does not fail because it lacks systems. It fails because it accumulates them. Every new dataset, agent, hook, command, and engine that partly overlaps an existing one imposes a permanent tax: two places to read, two places to change, two places to drift, and two places for a future builder to choose wrongly between. Left ungoverned, the tax compounds until the cost of understanding the ecosystem exceeds the value of any single addition to it. The observed evidence for this is internal and precise: five prior families in this Power Pack each began as a large proposal and, on inspection, collapsed to a fraction of it — a frontier-distillation proposal of twenty-six candidates became eight, a quality-intelligence proposal of seventeen became fourteen, a session-resilience proposal of roughly thirty became six, a next-generation cost proposal of nine became two, and the Duplicate-to-Advantage engine's own proposal of six components became one. The pattern is not coincidence. It is the signature of an ecosystem dense enough that most new ideas are already partly present. In such an ecosystem the default of creation is wrong, and the default of reinforcement is right, and the difference between the two defaults, integrated over years, is the difference between a system that compounds and a system that congeals.

1.3 Why a constitution and not a checklist. A checklist is consulted; a constitution is inhabited. The distinction matters because the failure mode is not that builders disagree with anti-duplication — everyone agrees with it in the abstract — but that under the local pressure of a specific task, creating a fresh system feels faster than discovering, understanding, and extending an existing one. The constitution removes the choice from local discretion. It declares that the burden of proof runs against creation: a new sovereign system is guilty of redundancy until it proves, against fifteen explicit criteria, that it is irreducible. It declares that a detected duplicate is never an occasion for rejection but always an occasion for structured search — the duplicate marks the exact edge of the non-redundant capability space, and the builder's job at that edge is to map the adjacent gap and reinforce toward it. And it declares that the verdicts are typed, recorded, appealable, and measured, so that the discipline is auditable rather than aspirational. A constitution that cannot be audited is indistinguishable from a preference, and a preference does not survive contact with a deadline.

1.4 Scope and precedence. This constitution governs every artifact class the Power Pack produces: datasets, dataset Parts, agents, guardians, engines, sub-engines, commands, hooks, skills, plans, requirement documents, evaluators, registries, schemas, memories, workflows, and the architectural proposals that precede any of them. It does not govern the content of a domain — it governs the decision to add a system to the ecosystem at all. Where it conflicts with a local preference for a clean-slate build, the constitution wins, because the local preference optimizes one task and the constitution optimizes the estate across every task that will ever touch the overlapping surface. Where it conflicts with a higher constitutional law of the Power Pack — the reality-supremacy hierarchy that ranks observed disk above plan, the honesty rule that forbids claiming as built what is only specified, or an owner directive — those win, because Duplicate-to-Advantage is a growth discipline, not a truth discipline, and a growth discipline must never be permitted to suppress a fact.

1.5 The constitutional defaults, stated once. Reinforce before create. Compose before duplicate. Extend before replace. Extract shared infrastructure before spawning a new system. Generalize a repeated pattern before hard-coding it a second time. Formalize an informal convention before inventing a parallel one. And create a sovereign dataset only for the irreducible remainder. These seven clauses are the operative body of the law; every later Part of this dataset is their elaboration into an ontology, a pipeline, a test, a scanner, an economics, and a set of metrics. The clauses are ordered by preference, not by exclusivity — a single candidate may resolve into several of them at once, absorbing part of its surface into one existing system, extending a second, and composing a third, leaving a genuinely new core that is smaller than the original proposal by the sum of what the first three clauses absorbed.

1.6 What success looks like. The measure of this constitution is not how many systems it blocks — a law measured by its refusals invites the gaming of refusing good work to score points. The measure is the net institutional capability gained per unit of added structural complexity: capability that rises because reinforcement makes existing systems stronger, complexity that stays flat because duplication is converted rather than accumulated. A Power Pack governed by this law grows the way a well-run library grows — not by shelving a second copy of every book that resembles one already held, but by strengthening the catalog, cross-referencing the adjacent works, and acquiring only the volumes that genuinely extend the collection. The library that shelves every near-duplicate does not become richer; it becomes unusable, and its richness and its unusability are the same fact measured by different observers.

1.7 The relationship to the executable that already exists. This constitution does not replace the sealed Duplicate-to-Advantage engine; it is the law that engine was built to serve, written down so that the discipline survives when the engine is not invoked. The division of labor is exact. The engine is the mechanism: it takes a candidate, detects the nearest existing capability, maps the gap along a fixed set of dimensions, generates vertical and horizontal reinforcement options, scores them by value over cost, and reduces the winner to the smallest sufficient artifact under an anti-inflation gate. The constitution is the jurisprudence: it defines the ontology the engine's detection reasons over, the verdict set its recommendations draw from, the sovereignty test its final creation decisions must pass, the precedence rules that resolve its authority conflicts, and the metrics by which its outcomes are judged. When a builder runs the engine, the engine applies the constitution. When a builder does not run the engine — the common case, because a running mind is not a dispatched tool — the constitution still applies, because it has been internalized as the default posture toward every candidate: assume it already partly exists, and prove otherwise before creating. This is why the promotion from engine to constitution is not ceremonial. An engine improves the decisions of the sessions that remember to call it; a constitution improves the decisions of every session, including the ones that never call anything, which is where duplication is actually born.

PART I FINAL LAW. Duplicate-to-Advantage is not a tool the Power Pack owns but a law the Power Pack obeys: reinforce before create, and create sovereignly only for the irreducible remainder, because in a dense ecosystem the default of creation is the tax that congeals it and the default of reinforcement is the discipline that compounds it.

PART II — THE DUPLICATE ONTOLOGY

2.1 Mission of the ontology. A law against duplication is worthless if duplication is a single undifferentiated word, because the correct response to two systems that share a name is not the correct response to two systems that share a mechanism, and treating them alike produces either false alarms that block legitimate work or missed overlaps that let real redundancy through. This Part types duplication into its distinct kinds, so that the pipeline of Part VI can route each kind to its correct verdict. The ontology is the vocabulary the whole constitution reasons in: every detection the scanner produces is an instance of one or more of these types, and every verdict the engine issues is a response calibrated to the type detected. Without the ontology the law is a slogan; with it, the law is a decision procedure.

2.2 Nominal duplication. Two artifacts carry the same or confusingly similar name while denoting different things, or carry different names while denoting the same thing. This is the shallowest kind and the most common false signal. The alias that names one concept two ways — a coordinate system that appears as three different labels across three documents — is nominal duplication resolved by canonicalization, not by deletion. The homonym that names two concepts one way is nominal collision resolved by renaming, not by merger. Nominal duplication is dangerous precisely because it is easy to detect and easy to mis-handle: a builder who merges two systems because their names collide destroys a real distinction, and a builder who leaves two labels for one system lets the estate fracture into synonyms that no future search will reconcile. The ontology's first job is to strip the nominal layer away so the deeper kinds can be seen.

2.3 Semantic duplication. Two artifacts express the same meaning in different words — two datasets that both define the same invariant, two prompts that both encode the same constraint, two checklists that both enforce the same contract. Semantic duplication is not resolved by canonicalization because the surface forms genuinely differ; it is resolved by extraction of the shared meaning into one authoritative statement that both sites reference. The trap of semantic duplication is that light compression can erase a material difference: two statements that appear to mean the same thing may differ in an exception, a scope, or an authority that the careless merge discards. The ontology therefore pairs semantic-duplication detection with a mandatory difference-preservation check — the merge is permitted only after the differences between the two formulations are enumerated and shown to be immaterial, and any material difference converts the verdict from merge to specialize.

2.4 Conceptual duplication. Two artifacts model the same underlying concept at different levels of abstraction or from different vantage points — a general capability and a special case of it, a parent pattern and a repeated instance of that pattern. Conceptual duplication is the richest source of advantage in the whole ontology, because its correct resolution is rarely deletion and almost always promotion: the repeated instance is evidence that the concept deserves to be lifted into a shared abstraction, and the second occurrence is not waste but the second data point that justifies the generalization. A single instance of a pattern is a fact; a second is a trend; and the constitutional response to a confirmed trend is to extract the pattern into infrastructure that every future instance consumes, converting what looked like duplication into leverage.

2.5 Functional and mechanism duplication. Functional duplication is two artifacts that produce the same output from the same input regardless of how; mechanism duplication is two artifacts that use the same internal machinery regardless of what they produce. The two are distinct and must not be conflated. Two detectors that both flag stale context are functionally duplicate even if one uses timestamps and the other uses content hashes, and their resolution is to choose or compose the better mechanism behind one interface. Two systems that both implement a similarity comparison are mechanism-duplicate even if one ranks candidates and the other deduplicates records, and their resolution is to extract the shared machinery into a common component that both call. Functional duplication points toward interface consolidation; mechanism duplication points toward infrastructure extraction; and misreading one as the other consolidates interfaces that should stay separate or forks machinery that should be shared.

2.6 Data, evaluation, and authority duplication. Data duplication is the same fact stored in two places, resolved by naming one the source of truth and making the other a reference, never a copy, because two copies of a fact are two facts the moment one is updated and the other is not. Evaluation duplication is two artifacts that check the same property with two rubrics, resolved by unifying the rubric so a thing cannot pass one gate and fail the other. Authority duplication is two artifacts that both claim the right to decide the same question, resolved by the precedence ladder rather than by merger, because the two claimants may have legitimately different scopes and the conflict is jurisdictional, not redundant. Each of these three kinds is subtle because the duplication is invisible until the two sites disagree, and the disagreement surfaces at the worst possible time — when a fact has changed, a gate is being contested, or a decision is being overridden — which is exactly why the ontology insists they be resolved structurally in advance rather than reconciled reactively under pressure.

2.7 Lifecycle and temporal duplication. Lifecycle duplication is two artifacts at different maturity stages that will converge — a draft and the system it drafts, a prototype and the production form it anticipates — where the resolution is not to keep both indefinitely but to define the supersession that retires the earlier when the later matures. Temporal duplication is the same artifact preserved across versions, where the resolution is the opposite: the versions are legitimately retained for lineage, and destroying the history to save space destroys the ability to reconstruct why a decision was made. The two are mirror images — lifecycle duplication is convergence that should end in supersession, temporal duplication is history that should end in retention — and the ontology's final discrimination is to tell them apart, so that a supersession is not mistaken for history worth keeping and a history is not mistaken for a draft worth deleting.

2.8 The ontology as a routing table. These types are not academic categories; each routes to a distinct verdict in Part VII. Nominal routes to canonicalize or rename. Semantic routes to extract-with-difference-check. Conceptual routes to generalize. Functional routes to consolidate the interface. Mechanism routes to extract shared infrastructure. Data routes to source-of-truth. Evaluation routes to unify the rubric. Authority routes to the precedence ladder. Lifecycle routes to supersession. Temporal routes to retained history. A single detected overlap frequently carries several types at once, and the pipeline resolves it into the union of their verdicts, which is how a candidate that appeared to be one new system dissolves into an absorption here, an extension there, and a genuinely new remainder that is smaller than the whole by everything the ontology routed elsewhere.

PART II FINAL LAW. Duplication is not one thing but ten, and the constitutional response to each is a different structural act — canonicalize the name, extract the meaning, generalize the concept, consolidate the interface, share the mechanism, single-source the data, unify the rubric, ladder the authority, supersede the draft, retain the history — so that the same word that names a tax on a careless ecosystem names, once typed, the precise map of where that ecosystem's next advantage is hiding.
