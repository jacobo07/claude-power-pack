DAIF-00 — DUPLICATE-TO-ADVANTAGE CONSTITUTION AND DUPLICATE ONTOLOGY

PART I — THESIS, MISSION, AND THE CONSTITUTIONAL PROMOTION OF DUPLICATE-TO-ADVANTAGE

1.1 Mission. This dataset states the constitutional law that governs how the Claude Power Pack grows. The law is one sentence: an ecosystem must reinforce what it already has before it creates what it does not, and it may create a sovereign new system only for the irreducible remainder that no reinforcement, extension, composition, or shared-infrastructure extraction can absorb. The status this dataset changes is that Duplicate-to-Advantage already EXISTS in the Power Pack as a single engine — a six-stage prospective-intake pipeline sealed under the C85 milestone, with a fifteen-operation vocabulary and an anti-inflation gate — but it exists as one invokable tool that a session must remember to call. A tool that must be remembered is advisory. This dataset promotes the same discipline from an invokable engine to a constitutional default: the behavior that is applied by every builder on every candidate, whether or not the engine is called, because the law is internalized rather than dispatched. The engine is the executable; this constitution is the law the executable enforces, the ontology it reasons over, and the reason it exists at all. EXISTS: the engine and its root law. SPECIFIED: the constitutional layer built here.

1.2 The problem this solves. A capability ecosystem does not fail because it lacks systems. It fails because it accumulates them. Every new dataset, agent, hook, command, and engine that partly overlaps an existing one imposes a permanent tax: two places to read, two places to change, two places to drift, and two places for a future builder to choose wrongly between. Left ungoverned, the tax compounds until the cost of understanding the ecosystem exceeds the value of any single addition to it. The observed evidence for this is internal and precise: five prior families in this Power Pack each began as a large proposal and, on inspection, collapsed to a fraction of it — a frontier-distillation proposal of twenty-six candidates became eight, a quality-intelligence proposal of seventeen became fourteen, a session-resilience proposal of roughly thirty became six, a next-generation cost proposal of nine became two, and the Duplicate-to-Advantage engine's own proposal of six components became one. The pattern is not coincidence. It is the signature of an ecosystem dense enough that most new ideas are already partly present. In such an ecosystem the default of creation is wrong, and the default of reinforcement is right, and the difference between the two defaults, integrated over years, is the difference between a system that compounds and a system that congeals.

1.3 Why a constitution and not a checklist. A checklist is consulted; a constitution is inhabited. The distinction matters because the failure mode is not that builders disagree with anti-duplication — everyone agrees with it in the abstract — but that under the local pressure of a specific task, creating a fresh system feels faster than discovering, understanding, and extending an existing one. The constitution removes the choice from local discretion. It declares that the burden of proof runs against creation: a new sovereign system is guilty of redundancy until it proves, against fifteen explicit criteria, that it is irreducible. It declares that a detected duplicate is never an occasion for rejection but always an occasion for structured search — the duplicate marks the exact edge of the non-redundant capability space, and the builder's job at that edge is to map the adjacent gap and reinforce toward it. And it declares that the verdicts are typed, recorded, appealable, and measured, so that the discipline is auditable rather than aspirational. A constitution that cannot be audited is indistinguishable from a preference, and a preference does not survive contact with a deadline.

1.4 Scope and precedence. This constitution governs every artifact class the Power Pack produces: datasets, dataset Parts, agents, guardians, engines, sub-engines, commands, hooks, skills, plans, requirement documents, evaluators, registries, schemas, memories, workflows, and the architectural proposals that precede any of them. It does not govern the content of a domain — it governs the decision to add a system to the ecosystem at all. Where it conflicts with a local preference for a clean-slate build, the constitution wins, because the local preference optimizes one task and the constitution optimizes the estate across every task that will ever touch the overlapping surface. Where it conflicts with a higher constitutional law of the Power Pack — the reality-supremacy hierarchy that ranks observed disk above plan, the honesty rule that forbids claiming as built what is only specified, or an owner directive — those win, because Duplicate-to-Advantage is a growth discipline, not a truth discipline, and a growth discipline must never be permitted to suppress a fact.

1.5 The constitutional defaults, stated once. Reinforce before create. Compose before duplicate. Extend before replace. Extract shared infrastructure before spawning a new system. Generalize a repeated pattern before hard-coding it a second time. Formalize an informal convention before inventing a parallel one. And create a sovereign dataset only for the irreducible remainder. These seven clauses are the operative body of the law; every later Part of this dataset is their elaboration into an ontology, a pipeline, a test, a scanner, an economics, and a set of metrics. The clauses are ordered by preference, not by exclusivity — a single candidate may resolve into several of them at once, absorbing part of its surface into one existing system, extending a second, and composing a third, leaving a genuinely new core that is smaller than the original proposal by the sum of what the first three clauses absorbed.

1.6 What success looks like. The measure of this constitution is not how many systems it blocks — a law measured by its refusals invites the gaming of refusing good work to score points. The measure is the net institutional capability gained per unit of added structural complexity: capability that rises because reinforcement makes existing systems stronger, complexity that stays flat because duplication is converted rather than accumulated. A Power Pack governed by this law grows the way a well-run library grows — not by shelving a second copy of every book that resembles one already held, but by strengthening the catalog, cross-referencing the adjacent works, and acquiring only the volumes that genuinely extend the collection. The library that shelves every near-duplicate does not become richer; it becomes unusable, and its richness and its unusability are the same fact measured by different observers.

1.7 The relationship to the executable that already exists. This constitution does not replace the sealed Duplicate-to-Advantage engine; it is the law that engine was built to serve, written down so that the discipline survives when the engine is not invoked. The division of labor is exact. The engine is the mechanism: it takes a candidate, detects the nearest existing capability, maps the gap along a fixed set of dimensions, generates vertical and horizontal reinforcement options, scores them by value over cost, and reduces the winner to the smallest sufficient artifact under an anti-inflation gate. The constitution is the jurisprudence: it defines the ontology the engine's detection reasons over, the verdict set its recommendations draw from, the sovereignty test its final creation decisions must pass, the precedence rules that resolve its authority conflicts, and the metrics by which its outcomes are judged. When a builder runs the engine, the engine applies the constitution. When a builder does not run the engine — the common case, because a running mind is not a dispatched tool — the constitution still applies, because it has been internalized as the default posture toward every candidate: assume it already partly exists, and prove otherwise before creating. This is why the promotion from engine to constitution is not ceremonial. An engine improves the decisions of the sessions that remember to call it; a constitution improves the decisions of every session, including the ones that never call anything, which is where duplication is actually born.

PART I FINAL LAW. Duplicate-to-Advantage is not a tool the Power Pack owns but a law the Power Pack obeys: reinforce before create, and create sovereignly only for the irreducible remainder, because in a dense ecosystem the default of creation is the tax that congeals it and the default of reinforcement is the discipline that compounds it.

PART II — THE DUPLICATE ONTOLOGY

2.1 Mission of the ontology. A law against duplication is worthless if duplication is a single undifferentiated word, because the correct response to two systems that share a name is not the correct response to two systems that share a mechanism, and treating them alike produces either false alarms that block legitimate work or missed overlaps that let real redundancy through. This Part types duplication into its distinct kinds, so that the pipeline of Part VI can route each kind to its correct verdict. The ontology is the vocabulary the whole constitution reasons in: every detection the scanner produces is an instance of one or more of these types, and every verdict the engine issues is a response calibrated to the type detected. Without the ontology the law is a slogan; with it, the law is a decision procedure.

2.2 Nominal duplication. Two artifacts carry the same or confusingly similar name while denoting different things, or carry different names while denoting the same thing. This is the shallowest kind and the most common false signal. The alias that names one concept two ways — a coordinate system that appears as three different labels across three documents — is nominal duplication resolved by canonicalization, not by deletion. The homonym that names two concepts one way is nominal collision resolved by renaming, not by merger. Nominal duplication is dangerous precisely because it is easy to detect and easy to mis-handle: a builder who merges two systems because their names collide destroys a real distinction, and a builder who leaves two labels for one system lets the estate fracture into synonyms that no future search will reconcile. The ontology's first job is to strip the nominal layer away so the deeper kinds can be seen.

2.3 Semantic duplication. Two artifacts express the same meaning in different words — two datasets that both define the same invariant, two prompts that both encode the same constraint, two checklists that both enforce the same contract. Semantic duplication is not resolved by canonicalization because the surface forms genuinely differ; it is resolved by extraction of the shared meaning into one authoritative statement that both sites reference. The trap of semantic duplication is that light compression can erase a material difference: two statements that appear to mean the same thing may differ in an exception, a scope, or an authority that the careless merge discards. The ontology therefore pairs semantic-duplication detection with a mandatory difference-preservation check — the merge is permitted only after the differences between the two formulations are enumerated and shown to be immaterial, and any material difference converts the verdict from merge to specialize.

2.4 Conceptual duplication. Two artifacts model the same underlying concept at different levels of abstraction or from different vantage points — a general capability and a special case of it, a parent pattern and a repeated instance of that pattern. Conceptual duplication is the richest source of advantage in the whole ontology, because its correct resolution is rarely deletion and almost always promotion: the repeated instance is evidence that the concept deserves to be lifted into a shared abstraction, and the second occurrence is not waste but the second data point that justifies the generalization. A single instance of a pattern is a fact; a second is a trend; and the constitutional response to a confirmed trend is to extract the pattern into infrastructure that every future instance consumes, converting what looked like duplication into leverage.

2.5 Functional and mechanism duplication. Functional duplication is two artifacts that produce the same output from the same input regardless of how; mechanism duplication is two artifacts that use the same internal machinery regardless of what they produce. The two are distinct and must not be conflated. Two detectors that both flag stale context are functionally duplicate even if one uses timestamps and the other uses content hashes, and their resolution is to choose or compose the better mechanism behind one interface. Two systems that both implement a similarity comparison are mechanism-duplicate even if one ranks candidates and the other deduplicates records, and their resolution is to extract the shared machinery into a common component that both call. Functional duplication points toward interface consolidation; mechanism duplication points toward infrastructure extraction; and misreading one as the other consolidates interfaces that should stay separate or forks machinery that should be shared.

2.6 Data, evaluation, and authority duplication. Data duplication is the same fact stored in two places, resolved by naming one the source of truth and making the other a reference, never a copy, because two copies of a fact are two facts the moment one is updated and the other is not. Evaluation duplication is two artifacts that check the same property with two rubrics, resolved by unifying the rubric so a thing cannot pass one gate and fail the other. Authority duplication is two artifacts that both claim the right to decide the same question, resolved by the precedence ladder rather than by merger, because the two claimants may have legitimately different scopes and the conflict is jurisdictional, not redundant. Each of these three kinds is subtle because the duplication is invisible until the two sites disagree, and the disagreement surfaces at the worst possible time — when a fact has changed, a gate is being contested, or a decision is being overridden — which is exactly why the ontology insists they be resolved structurally in advance rather than reconciled reactively under pressure.

2.7 Lifecycle and temporal duplication. Lifecycle duplication is two artifacts at different maturity stages that will converge — a draft and the system it drafts, a prototype and the production form it anticipates — where the resolution is not to keep both indefinitely but to define the supersession that retires the earlier when the later matures. Temporal duplication is the same artifact preserved across versions, where the resolution is the opposite: the versions are legitimately retained for lineage, and destroying the history to save space destroys the ability to reconstruct why a decision was made. The two are mirror images — lifecycle duplication is convergence that should end in supersession, temporal duplication is history that should end in retention — and the ontology's final discrimination is to tell them apart, so that a supersession is not mistaken for history worth keeping and a history is not mistaken for a draft worth deleting.

2.8 The ontology as a routing table. These types are not academic categories; each routes to a distinct verdict in Part VII. Nominal routes to canonicalize or rename. Semantic routes to extract-with-difference-check. Conceptual routes to generalize. Functional routes to consolidate the interface. Mechanism routes to extract shared infrastructure. Data routes to source-of-truth. Evaluation routes to unify the rubric. Authority routes to the precedence ladder. Lifecycle routes to supersession. Temporal routes to retained history. A single detected overlap frequently carries several types at once, and the pipeline resolves it into the union of their verdicts, which is how a candidate that appeared to be one new system dissolves into an absorption here, an extension there, and a genuinely new remainder that is smaller than the whole by everything the ontology routed elsewhere.

PART II FINAL LAW. Duplication is not one thing but ten, and the constitutional response to each is a different structural act — canonicalize the name, extract the meaning, generalize the concept, consolidate the interface, share the mechanism, single-source the data, unify the rubric, ladder the authority, supersede the draft, retain the history — so that the same word that names a tax on a careless ecosystem names, once typed, the precise map of where that ecosystem's next advantage is hiding.

PART III — THE LEGITIMATE REDUNDANCY TAXONOMY

3.1 Mission of the taxonomy. A law that hunts duplication becomes dangerous the moment it cannot tell a tax from a safeguard, because most of the strongest structures a mature Power Pack owns are, on their surface, two things that do the same job. This Part types the redundancy that is CORRECT — the second copy that must be preserved, never converted — so that the anti-duplication engine of the sibling Parts does not amputate the very reinforcements that make the estate survivable. The core tension is stated once and governs everything below: a naive rule that says "two systems doing one job is waste" destroys defense-in-depth, fallbacks, specialization, compatibility, and history in a single stroke, and an ecosystem stripped of those is not leaner but more brittle. The discriminator this Part builds is the whole of its value: harmful duplication is a second copy that carries no independent failure mode, no genuine specialization, no real bridge, and no irreplaceable history; legitimate redundancy is a second copy that carries at least one of those four. Everything the taxonomy does is teach the scanner to look for the four before it recommends a merge.

3.2 Defense-in-depth. The first legitimate kind is two independent guards on one critical invariant, placed so that the failure of either still leaves the invariant held. A secret must never land in a commit: a pre-write firewall hook checks content, and an independent post-commit audit rescans history, and the two overlap almost entirely in what they forbid. A naive de-duplicator sees one redundant check and proposes deleting the cheaper. The constitution forbids it, because the two guards fail independently — the hook can be unwired by a mirror-sync drift, the audit can be skipped by a bypassed run — and independent failure modes are exactly the property that makes two guards worth more than one. The test is mechanical: if disabling copy A and copy B produces the same exposure window, they are one guard written twice and the redundancy is ornamental; if disabling A leaves B holding the line through a different substrate, they are defense-in-depth and both are load-bearing. The invariant's criticality sets the bar: the more catastrophic the breach, the more a second independent guard earns its keep.

3.3 Intentional fallback. The second kind is a cheaper or faster primary path plus a slower safe backstop that runs only when the primary is unavailable or untrusted. A context assembler reads a cached summary when a file's hash matches the index and re-reads the raw file only on a miss; a session recovery path trusts a live identity token and degrades to a disk-transcript scan when the token is absent. The primary and the backstop compute the same answer, which is precisely why a surface scan flags them as duplicate — and precisely why merging them is a defect, because the backstop exists for the case the primary cannot serve. The discriminator is the trigger asymmetry: a legitimate fallback is entered under a named condition (cache miss, token absent, network down) that the primary cannot handle, and it accepts a real cost (latency, tokens, coarser fidelity) in exchange for coverage the primary lacks. Delete the backstop and the system does not get simpler; it gets a hole shaped exactly like its worst day. The engine preserves the pair and, at most, extracts the shared answer-shape into one interface both paths satisfy, so the redundancy of the answer is consolidated while the redundancy of the paths — the part that earns its keep — remains.

3.4 Specialization. The third kind is a general capability plus a vertical form of it that genuinely differs — not a copy of the general with a new name, but an instance whose inputs, constraints, or failure surface are materially their own. A universal code-review doctrine and a repo-specific review agent that adds one substrate's ABI traps are specialization; a universal freshness check and a session-scoped freshness check tuned to a different clock are specialization. The trap is that specialization and conceptual duplication share a silhouette: both are a parent and a child. The discrimination is whether the child carries a difference that the parent cannot express without becoming wrong for its other consumers. If the vertical only re-states the general with cosmetic edits, it is harmful restatement and routes to absorption; if the vertical encodes a constraint that would break the general were it pushed upward, it is legitimate and both survive — the general stays general, the vertical stays sharp, and the seam between them is documented so a future builder does not "unify" them into a blunt middle that serves neither. Genuine specialization is measured by the material difference in the child, never by the mere fact of a second file, and the material difference is the same object the measurement layer will later score as an equivalence-lowering exception.

3.5 Compatibility layers. The fourth kind is a shim that bridges two contracts that legitimately differ and cannot be collapsed — an adapter that lets a component built against one interface serve a caller expecting another, a translation that carries a value across a boundary neither side will move. On its face a compatibility layer is pure duplication: it holds no capability of its own, it only re-expresses one side in the other's terms. But its worth is entirely in the boundary it spans, and the boundary is real when the two contracts have independent owners, independent release cadences, or independent authority. Deleting the shim to "remove the redundant translation" forces the two sides into a direct coupling that breaks the next time either moves. The discriminator is the existence of a genuine two-contract seam: a shim across a seam that will never close is legitimate and permanent; a shim across a seam that exists only because someone declined to update one caller is a migration debt masquerading as a bridge, and it routes to supersession, not preservation. The taxonomy keeps the bridge and dates the seam, so a real shim is not confused with a lazy one, and a seam recorded as permanent is never quietly reclassified as debt to justify a convenient deletion.

3.6 Retained historical versions. The fifth kind is lineage — prior versions preserved because the history is irreplaceable, not because it is still executed. The record of why a decision was made, the superseded formulation that a later gate still references to explain its own existence, the archived form that a lineage edge points to: these are duplicates of the current form only if history is worthless, and history is never worthless in an estate whose learnings compound. The discriminator is whether anything downstream reads the old copy as evidence rather than as a live path. If a lineage edge, an audit, or a rationale points at the historical version, it is retained knowledge and deleting it severs the ability to reconstruct intent; if nothing reads it and nothing derives from it, it is dead weight and routes to genuine retirement. The subtlety is that retention and lifecycle-supersession are mirror images from the ontology: the same two artifacts can be a draft to retire or a history to keep, and the reading of the downstream references is what tells them apart — a version with a live derivation is history, a version with none is a draft, and the constitution refuses to guess which without checking who reads it.

3.7 The four-property discriminator and false-positive control. The taxonomy collapses to one test the scanner runs before any merge recommendation: does the second copy carry an independent failure mode, a genuine specialization, a real bridge across a live seam, or an irreplaceable history that something downstream reads? A yes on any one converts the verdict from "duplication, absorb" to "redundancy, preserve." False-positive control lives here because this is where a careless anti-duplication law does its worst damage: the cost of a missed duplicate is a slow tax, but the cost of a destroyed safeguard is a fast breach at the worst possible moment — the day the primary fails, the day the guard is needed, the day the history must be read. The constitution therefore makes preservation the safe default under uncertainty: when the discriminator cannot confirm harm, the redundancy stands, and the burden runs against the merge exactly as, in the sibling law, the burden runs against creation. Two guards kept needlessly cost a little clarity; one guard deleted wrongly costs the invariant it protected.

PART III FINAL LAW. Not every second copy is a tax — defense-in-depth, an intentional fallback, a genuine specialization, a live-seam bridge, and an irreplaceable history are redundancy that must be preserved, and the constitution distinguishes them from harmful duplication by one mechanical test: a copy that carries an independent failure mode, real specialization, a real bridge, or read-downstream history is a safeguard, and under uncertainty the safeguard always stands.

PART IV — HARMFUL DUPLICATION AND ITS FAILURE MODES

4.1 Mission. This Part is the exact negative of its predecessor: the duplication that carries none of the four preserving properties and therefore always taxes. Its purpose is not to define harm abstractly but to trace each harmful kind to the concrete moment it detonates, because harmful duplication is invisible while nothing changes and injurious the instant something does. The taxonomy of Part III preserves; this taxonomy convicts. Five kinds recur, and each is dangerous precisely because at rest it looks harmless — two things that agree today, cost nothing today, and disagree only later, when the cost lands on whoever is least able to pay it. A conviction is trustworthy only when it can name that future moment, which is why every kind below is anchored to the specific event that turns its dormant contradiction into a live wound.

4.2 Two sources of truth that drift. The first and most corrosive harmful kind is one fact held authoritatively in two places. While the two copies agree, nothing signals a problem; the tax is entirely deferred to the update. The moment one copy is changed and the other is not, the estate holds two facts where it should hold one, and every reader downstream now depends on which copy it happened to consult. A count kept in both a manifest and a derived index, a threshold written into both a gate and its documentation, a path hard-coded in two hooks: each is silent until the day of the edit, and on that day the failure is not a crash but something worse — a system that runs, produces a confident answer, and is wrong. The reason it is the most corrosive kind is that nothing in normal operation ever consults both copies at once, so no test that merely exercises the system exercises the divergence; only an editor who happens to touch both, or a reader unlucky enough to hit the stale one, ever meets it, and by then the wrong answer has already propagated into everything that trusted it. The harm is maximal because it surfaces at the update, which is the one moment a builder believes they have finished a change, and the drift they introduced is discovered later by a victim who did not make it. The only structural cure is single-sourcing: one authoritative copy, every other site a reference that cannot hold an independent value.

4.3 Two rubrics that let a thing pass one gate and fail another. The second harmful kind is two evaluators that check the same property by different rules. The contradiction is dormant until an artifact sits in the narrow band where the rubrics disagree, and then the system enters a state it has no way to resolve: the thing is simultaneously approved and rejected, done and not-done, shipped and blocked. The harm lands at the contested gate — the highest-pressure moment in any pipeline, when a decision is being made and two authorities give opposite verdicts. A builder facing a pass-here-fail-there artifact cannot proceed and cannot stop; the pipeline stalls not on a hard error but on an unresolvable disagreement, and the resolution is usually the worst one available — the builder picks the rubric that unblocks them and the other quietly stops mattering, which means the estate did not have two checks, it had one check and one decoration that the first inconvenient case revealed. Two rubrics for one property are a coin flip disguised as a check, and the cure is to unify them so a property has exactly one definition of pass, enforced at exactly one gate that every path routes through.

4.4 Two authorities that collide under pressure. The third harmful kind is two artifacts that each claim the right to decide the same question. Unlike drift and rubric-conflict, this one can be legitimate — the ontology's authority type is often jurisdictional, two real scopes that never overlap — so the harmful case is specifically the one where the scopes are not separated and the claim is genuinely contested. The collision is dormant until the two authorities give opposite orders under load, and then the system has no ranking to appeal to. The harm lands at the override: the moment someone must be obeyed and it is unclear who, work either halts while the conflict is escalated or proceeds under whichever authority shouted last, and neither outcome is the one the estate designed for. The insidious part is that the override moment is by definition a high-stakes one — nobody contests a trivial decision — so the collision is guaranteed to surface exactly when the cost of stalling or of obeying the wrong voice is highest. The cure is not merger — merging two authorities can destroy a real jurisdictional boundary — but an explicit precedence ladder that ranks them in advance, so the contested moment resolves by a rule rather than by whoever holds the pen.

4.5 Ornamental restatement. The fourth harmful kind adds words without adding capability: a second document that re-says an existing one in fresh phrasing, a wrapper that forwards every call to the thing it wraps, a dataset Part that narrates a parent system as if it were new. Ornamental restatement is insidious because it feels like productivity — output was produced, pages exist — but the estate gained nothing it could not already do, and it now pays to read, to keep in sync, and to disambiguate the copy from the original. The harm is chronic rather than acute: every future reader spends attention deciding which of two equivalent statements is authoritative, and every future editor must remember to change both. The cure is extraction of the shared meaning into one authoritative statement that the second site references — and, for a wrapper that only forwards, deletion, because a layer that adds no transformation across no seam is exactly the compatibility shim of Part III with its one justifying property removed.

4.6 The system built because discovering the existing one felt slower. The fifth harmful kind is the origin of most of the others: a fresh system created not because no capability existed but because finding, reading, and understanding the existing one felt slower than starting clean. This is the local-pressure failure the constitution was written to defeat, and its harm is the compounding tax the thesis names — two systems where one would serve, forever, because a builder optimized one afternoon against the estate's whole future. The failure mode is not visible at creation; the new system works, ships, and looks like progress. It detonates later, at every update that must now touch two places, every gate that must now reconcile two verdicts, every search that now returns two candidates and forces a choice. The cure is the discipline of the whole constitution: assume the capability already partly exists, spend the discovery cost before the creation cost, and let the sovereignty test — not the felt speed of a blank file — decide whether a genuinely new core remains.

4.7 The unifying signature. Every harmful kind shares one property that inverts the Part III discriminator: the second copy carries no independent failure mode, no genuine specialization, no live-seam bridge, and no read-downstream history, so its only contribution is a divergence waiting to happen. And every harmful kind shares one detonation pattern: the cost is deferred from creation to the worst possible later moment — the update, the contested gate, the override — and paid by someone other than the person who introduced it. This deferral is what makes harmful duplication so hard to police by discipline alone, because the builder who introduces it is never the one who feels it, so the feedback that would teach restraint never reaches the hand that needs the lesson; the constitution supplies that feedback structurally, moving the cost forward from the future victim to the present builder by convicting the duplicate at intake. Naming these five is what lets the scanner convict with confidence, because a conviction that cannot point to the concrete future harm is indistinguishable from the false positive Part III exists to prevent, and a taxonomy that convicts without naming the wound is no better than the naive law that amputates safeguards.

PART IV FINAL LAW. Harmful duplication is the second copy that carries none of the four safeguards and defers its cost to the update, the contested gate, or the override — two sources of truth that drift, two rubrics that split a verdict, two authorities that collide, ornamental restatement, and the fresh build chosen because discovery felt slow — and each is convicted not by resemblance but by the concrete harm it will detonate at the moment its victim can least afford it.

PART V — THE MEASUREMENT LAYER: OVERLAP, EQUIVALENCE, AND REINFORCEMENT SCORING

5.1 Mission. A constitution whose verdicts rest on adjectives — "these are similar", "that seems redundant" — is a preference wearing a robe, and a preference does not survive a builder who feels otherwise under deadline. This Part states the law that turns the ontology's types and the taxonomies' discriminators into numbers, so a verdict can be defended by a measured value rather than asserted by a confident voice. The measurement layer is not new machinery: the sealed D2A engine already computes a three-axis overlap between a candidate and the nearest existing capability and already scores its reinforcement options across a sixteen-dimension candidate space. This Part does not re-implement those computations; it states the law those numbers must obey, so that the engine's arithmetic and a builder's internalized judgment converge on the same reading. The law has one spine: overlap is measured on independent axes that are never collapsed to a single scalar, because the moment three different questions are averaged into one number, the number stops meaning anything and starts hiding the disagreement that mattered.

5.2 The three overlap axes. Overlap is scored on semantic, functional, and mechanism axes, and the ontology's kinds map onto them directly: semantic overlap asks whether two artifacts mean the same thing, functional overlap asks whether they produce the same output from the same input, mechanism overlap asks whether they use the same internal machinery. The three are independent by construction and routinely disagree — two detectors can be functionally identical while mechanically opposite (timestamps versus content hashes), and two components can share a mechanism while producing unrelated outputs (a similarity comparison that ranks in one place and deduplicates in another). Collapsing them to one score destroys exactly the signal that selects the verdict: high functional and low mechanism overlap routes to interface consolidation, high mechanism and low functional overlap routes to shared-infrastructure extraction, and an average of the two points at neither. The law is that the three axes are reported as a vector, never a sum, and the verdict reads the vector's shape, not its magnitude — two candidates with the same average across the three axes can, and often do, route to opposite verdicts, and any procedure that cannot tell them apart has thrown away the only thing worth measuring.

5.3 Equivalence scoring. Above overlap sits a second question the axes cannot answer alone: are two things the SAME, or merely adjacent? Equivalence is not high overlap — two artifacts can overlap heavily and still differ in the one exception, scope, or authority that makes them non-substitutable, exactly the trap Part III names in semantic duplication. Equivalence scoring therefore measures substitutability under difference-preservation: two things are equivalent only if replacing either with the other changes no downstream behavior, no exception, and no jurisdiction. The output is deliberately conservative — a high equivalence claim requires that the enumerated differences all be shown immaterial, and any material difference caps equivalence below the merge threshold and reroutes to specialization. This is the numeric form of the four-property discriminator: an independent failure mode, a genuine specialization, a live bridge, or a read history each registers as a material difference that pulls equivalence down, so the safeguards of Part III cannot be scored away. The conservatism is deliberate and asymmetric: a false high-equivalence claim merges a safeguard and detonates later, while a false low-equivalence claim merely preserves a duplicate at the cost of some clarity, so the scorer is tuned to err toward adjacency and away from sameness whenever the difference ledger is not exhaustively cleared.

5.4 Reinforcement scoring. The third score is economic: the value of converting a duplicate into an extension, per unit of added complexity. Detecting overlap tells you two things are close; reinforcement scoring tells you whether closing the gap by extending the existing system is worth more than the structural cost of the extension. It is value-over-cost, and it is what makes the constitution a growth discipline rather than a purity crusade — reinforcement is preferred not because merging is virtuous but because, measured, the capability gained per unit of complexity added is higher for an extension than for a fresh system that re-pays the whole cost of existence. A low reinforcement score is itself informative: when extending the existing system would cost more complexity than it returns in capability, the number is telling the builder that the candidate is closer to an irreducible core than to a duplicate, and the verdict tilts toward a sovereign build the sovereignty test must then justify.

5.5 The anti-Goodhart countermetric for each axis. Every metric that governs a decision is a target, and every target is gamed the moment it is optimized directly, so each of the three scores ships with a countermetric that fails loudly when the primary is being satisfied hollowly. Overlap's countermetric is difference-coverage: a low overlap score is only trustworthy if the enumerated differences actually account for the divergence, so a claim of "not a duplicate" that cannot name where the two differ is flagged, not believed. Equivalence's countermetric is the material-difference ledger: an equivalence claim that lists zero differences is more suspect than one that lists several and dismisses them, because two real artifacts that differ in nothing is the signature of an ornamental restatement, not a genuine twin. Reinforcement's countermetric is complexity honesty: a reinforcement score is invalid if the added complexity is undercounted, so the denominator is measured against the actual structural delta — new fields, new edges, new authority — not the optimistic sketch. The countermetrics exist because a builder under pressure will always find the shortest path to the number that unblocks them, and the shortest path is usually the one that guts the check; a metric shipped without its countermetric is therefore not half a safeguard but a liability, because it offers the appearance of rigor to precisely the shortcut it was meant to catch.

5.6 Why the numbers are never collapsed. The single most consequential rule of the measurement layer restates itself here because it is the one most often violated: the scores are reported as a structured reading — a three-axis overlap vector, an equivalence value with its difference ledger, a reinforcement value with its complexity denominator — and they are never flattened into one composite verdict-number. The reason is empirical and lives in the estate's own scars: a composite score rises with length and vocabulary, so a longer candidate scores as "more overlapping" or "more novel" for reasons that have nothing to do with duplication, and whole verdict classes go silently unreachable when a hard decision is hung on a scalar that a candidate's mere size can move. The law forbids the single number precisely so that the shape of the disagreement between axes — the thing that selects the verdict — is never averaged into invisibility, and it further forbids gating any hard verdict on a composite score's magnitude, because a gate whose threshold a candidate's length alone can cross is a gate that has stopped measuring what it names.

5.7 The law the engine's arithmetic obeys. The sealed engine will keep computing its three-axis overlap and its sixteen-dimension candidate scores; this Part governs how those outputs are read. A verdict is defensible only if it cites the axis values that produced it, only if an equivalence claim carries its difference ledger, only if a reinforcement recommendation carries its complexity denominator, and only if each cited score's countermetric was checked and did not fire. A number offered without its countermetric is exactly the adjective this Part exists to abolish, wearing a more convincing disguise; the measurement layer is worthless the day its scores become slogans with decimals. The obligation runs both ways: the engine must expose the vector and the ledgers rather than a headline figure, and the builder must read them rather than the headline, because a measured value that no one inspects governs nothing, and a constitution whose numbers are produced but never consulted has merely automated the adjective it set out to replace.

PART V FINAL LAW. Duplication is quantified, never described — a three-axis overlap vector that is never summed, an equivalence score gated by a material-difference ledger, and a reinforcement score of value over honestly-counted complexity — and each number is trusted only alongside the countermetric that fails when it is gamed, because a verdict defended by a measured value survives the deadline that a verdict defended by an adjective does not.

PART VI — THE D2A+ PIPELINE

6.1 Mission. This Part is the operational spine: the ordered flow every candidate traverses from arrival to verdict, and then past the verdict to a check that the verdict was honored. Each stage has an input, an output, and a failure mode that is paid if the stage is skipped, and the ordering is not decorative — an earlier stage's output is a later stage's input, so skipping one does not merely omit a check, it starves everything downstream of the information it needed to decide. The pipeline composes the sealed engine's stages; it does not replace them. What this Part adds is the law of the sequence: why the order is the order, and what breaks when a builder, feeling the answer is obvious, jumps to the end. The recurring temptation is exactly that jump — a builder who is certain a candidate is new wants to skip to the build — and the pipeline's whole value is that it makes the certainty earn itself stage by stage rather than assert itself at the start.

6.2 Candidate intake -> duplicate scan. The pipeline begins when any artifact is proposed — a dataset, an agent, a hook, a plan, a schema. Intake's output is a normalized description of the candidate's intended capability, and its failure mode if skipped is that the whole pipeline reasons over a vague wish instead of a stated capability, so every later overlap measurement is noise measured against a moving target. The duplicate scanner then takes that description and returns the set of existing artifacts that share any of the ontology's ten types with it. Skipping the scan is the original sin the constitution exists to prevent: a builder who does not scan does not discover, and a capability built without discovery is the fresh-system-because-discovery-felt-slow failure of Part IV, detonating later at every update that must touch two places. The scan must run against the whole estate, not the corner the builder happens to remember, because the most expensive duplicates are the ones that share a mechanism with a system in a part of the ecosystem the builder never thought to look.

6.3 Existing-capability discovery -> three-axis overlap analysis. The scanner finds candidates; discovery reads them, mapping what each existing near-match actually does, so the overlap analysis has real machinery to compare against rather than names. Its failure mode when skipped is the false negative that flows from shallow matching: two systems that share no words but the same mechanism go unseen, and the estate forks machinery it already owned. Overlap analysis then scores the candidate against the nearest existing capability on the three independent axes of the measurement layer, emitting the vector — semantic, functional, mechanism — that selects the verdict's shape. Skipping the analysis, or collapsing its axes, is the measurement failure the sibling Part forbids: without the vector's shape the pipeline cannot tell an interface consolidation from an infrastructure extraction, and it guesses, which in a dense estate means it guesses toward a build, because a build is the verdict that requires no evidence about what already exists.

6.4 Capability-impact mapping -> reinforcement proposal. Impact mapping takes the overlap vector and locates, precisely, which parts of which existing systems the candidate touches and how a change would ripple — the input the economics needs. Its failure mode if skipped is a reinforcement that looks cheap and is not, because the true complexity of extending a system is invisible until its dependents are mapped, and an unmapped extension routinely costs several times its sketch when the ripple reaches a consumer nobody remembered. The reinforcement proposal then generates the concrete options: extend this system vertically, generalize that pattern, compose these two, each scored by value over honestly-counted complexity. Skipping the proposal jumps straight to a build and forfeits the entire point of the constitution — the pipeline is not a duplication detector that says no, it is a reinforcement generator that says how, and a pipeline that only rejects has abandoned its reason to exist and will be resented and bypassed by the builders it was meant to serve.

6.5 Shared-infrastructure extraction -> cross-system composition analysis. Extraction asks whether the candidate's mechanism-overlap with existing systems reveals machinery that should be lifted into a common component both call, rather than duplicated a second time. Its failure mode if skipped is the mechanism-duplication tax of the ontology: the same machinery forked, then maintained twice, then drifted, until a fix applied to one copy silently fails to reach the other. Composition analysis then asks the complementary question — can the candidate be assembled from existing systems wired together, rather than built at all? Its failure mode if skipped is the most expensive miss in the pipeline, because a candidate that could have been pure composition of sealed systems is instead constructed from scratch, paying the full cost of a new system for a capability the estate could already assemble from parts it had already paid for and already trusts.

6.6 Gap detection -> irreducible-core detection. Gap detection takes everything absorbed, extended, composed, and extracted so far and computes the remainder: the part of the candidate that no existing system covers and no reinforcement reaches. Its failure mode if skipped is that the builder cannot see the candidate has already dissolved — the remainder is empty and the verdict should be a pure absorption, but without the subtraction the builder still feels they are building something new and proceeds to build the thing the estate already had. Irreducible-core detection then tests whether that remainder is genuinely irreducible or merely not-yet-absorbed, pressing it against every reinforcement clause one final time. Skipping it is how a candidate that should have resolved to extend-plus-a-thin-core instead resolves to a full sovereign build, re-paying the whole cost of existence for a core that was never irreducible and that a single more careful pass would have folded into a system already present.

6.7 Sovereignty test -> typed verdict -> post-construction duplicate audit. The sovereignty test is the gate a genuinely new core must pass before CREATE_NEW is permitted; it is elaborated in the sibling Part on verdicts, and its role in the sequence is to be the last and highest barrier, positioned after every cheaper resolution has been tried and the remainder proven irreducible, so that nothing reaches it that a reinforcement could have absorbed. The verdict stage then emits the typed outcome — frequently several at once: absorb this surface, extend that system, compose those two, leaving a smaller new core the sovereignty test justified. The pipeline does not end at the verdict. The post-construction duplicate audit runs after the artifact is built and asks the founding question again against the now-larger estate: did construction, in the doing, re-create something that already existed? Its failure mode if skipped is the quiet reappearance of the exact duplication the whole pipeline was run to prevent, because a build always drifts from its plan and the drift is where an unplanned duplicate is born; the audit is the only stage positioned to catch a duplicate that did not exist at intake and came into being during construction, and without it the duplicate is discovered — if ever — only at the next update, by the next victim, which is the pattern this entire constitution was written to end.

PART VI FINAL LAW. Every candidate traverses one ordered spine — intake, scan, discovery, three-axis overlap, impact mapping, reinforcement proposal, infrastructure extraction, composition analysis, gap detection, irreducible-core detection, sovereignty test, typed verdict, and a post-construction audit that asks the founding question again — and each stage feeds the next, so skipping any stage does not omit one check but starves every stage downstream of the information it needed to refuse a duplicate before it was born.

PART VII — THE VERDICT SET AND ITS TRIGGERS

7.1 Mission. The pipeline resolves each candidate into typed outcomes, and this Part defines that set and the exact condition that triggers each, so a verdict is a named decision with a stated cause rather than a mood. The verdict set is the constitution's operative vocabulary: eleven typed outcomes, ordered by how much of an existing structure they reuse, from total absorption into what exists to the rare, heavily-burdened creation of something sovereign. The ordering encodes the preference the thesis states — reinforce before create — as a literal descent: a candidate is tested against the reuse-heavy verdicts first, and it falls through to a build only when every cheaper resolution has been proven insufficient. A single candidate routinely resolves to several verdicts at once, and that is the design working, not a fault: the whole point is that one proposal dissolves into an absorption here, an extension there, a composition of two more, leaving a core smaller than the original by everything the earlier verdicts carried away.

7.2 ABSORB, EXTEND, REINFORCE. ABSORB fires when the candidate's capability is already fully present in one existing system and the overlap vector is high on all three axes with an equivalence score above the merge threshold and an empty material-difference ledger — the candidate is the existing system, and it folds in with nothing left over. EXTEND fires when the candidate is mostly present but adds a genuine increment an existing system can hold without breaking its other consumers — high functional overlap, a small irreducible gap, and a reinforcement score whose value exceeds its complexity cost. REINFORCE fires when the candidate does not add a capability but strengthens an existing one — a second independent guard on a critical invariant, the defense-in-depth of Part III — where the trigger is precisely an independent failure mode that makes the redundancy legitimate rather than ornamental. These three are the constitution's common case, and a healthy estate resolves most candidates here; a run that rarely reaches them is a warning that the pipeline is being bypassed, not that the estate has suddenly become full of genuinely novel work.

7.3 COMPOSE, GENERALIZE, FORMALIZE. COMPOSE fires when the candidate's capability can be assembled by wiring existing systems together, none of which alone covers it but which jointly do — the composition analysis of the pipeline returns a wiring, and the verdict is to build the wiring, not the systems. GENERALIZE fires on conceptual duplication: a pattern has now appeared a second time, the second occurrence is the data point that justifies lifting it into a shared abstraction, and the trigger is exactly the confirmed repeat — one instance is a fact, two is the trend the constitution converts into leverage by extracting the pattern into infrastructure every future instance consumes. FORMALIZE fires when an informal convention exists — a prose practice, an unwritten rule enforced by habit — and the candidate would otherwise invent a parallel formal system; the verdict promotes the existing convention into the formal artifact rather than spawning a competitor beside it. These three convert latent structure into explicit structure without adding a sovereign system, and their shared discipline is that the raw material already exists in the estate — a wiring, a repeat, a habit — and the verdict merely gives it a form, never a second body.

7.4 EXTRACT_SHARED_INFRASTRUCTURE, REPLACE, DEPRECATE. EXTRACT_SHARED_INFRASTRUCTURE fires on mechanism duplication: two or more systems use the same machinery for different ends, the mechanism-overlap axis is high while the functional axis is low, and the verdict lifts the shared machinery into a common component both call — the fork of machinery is the failure it prevents. REPLACE fires when the candidate is genuinely superior to an existing system across the same surface and the existing one carries no independent failure mode, no specialization, no live bridge, and no read history worth keeping — the four-property discriminator of Part III returns all-clear, and only then may the new supersede the old. DEPRECATE fires on lifecycle duplication: a draft and the system it drafts, a prototype and its production form, where the verdict is not to keep both but to define the supersession that retires the earlier when the later matures. REPLACE and DEPRECATE are the only verdicts that remove structure, and both are gated by the taxonomy's preservation test so that a safeguard is never mistaken for obsolescence — the burden on a removal is symmetric to the burden on a creation, because deleting a guard and spawning a duplicate are the two ways an estate degrades, and neither is permitted on a hunch.

7.5 REJECT. REJECT fires when the candidate should not exist at all — it is pure ornamental restatement adding words without capability, or it duplicates a sealed family with no genuine delta, and no reinforcement verdict applies because there is nothing to reinforce and nothing to build. REJECT is distinct from ABSORB: absorption folds a real capability into an existing home, while rejection declines a candidate that carried no capability the estate lacked. The trigger is an empty irreducible core combined with a zero-value reinforcement score — there is neither a new capability to add nor an existing one to strengthen. REJECT is not a punishment; it is the pipeline correctly declining to pay for a copy whose only contribution would have been a future divergence. It is also the verdict most resented in the moment and most vindicated over time, because the work it declines already felt finished to whoever proposed it, and the cost it avoids is one that would only ever have been paid by someone else, later — which is exactly the deferral the constitution exists to intercept.

7.6 CREATE_NEW and its burden of proof. CREATE_NEW is the rarest verdict and carries the highest burden, because it is the one that adds a sovereign system to the estate and therefore the one that, misused, produces the compounding tax the whole constitution exists to prevent. It fires only when the irreducible-core detection returns a remainder that survived every reinforcement clause, and even then it does not fire on its own authority — it hands off to the sovereignty test, which the candidate must pass before a build is permitted. The trigger is a proven-irreducible core plus a passed sovereignty test; either alone is insufficient, and a core that looks irreducible but has not been pressed against the sovereignty test is a hypothesis, not a verdict. The frequency law is explicit and audited: CREATE_NEW must be the least frequent verdict the pipeline emits, and a run in which it is common is not evidence of a fertile estate but of a discipline that has failed, because in a dense ecosystem most candidates are already partly present and a pipeline that keeps concluding otherwise is measuring its own leniency, not the estate's gaps. The proof of the discipline is the same proof the thesis cites: proposals of twenty-six, seventeen, thirty, and nine each collapsed to a small remainder, and a CREATE_NEW rate that does not resemble that collapse is the signal to audit the pipeline, not to celebrate the yield.

7.7 Multiple verdicts and the type-to-verdict map. A candidate resolves to the union of its applicable verdicts, and the ontology's ten duplication types each carry a default verdict that the union is built from: nominal routes to canonicalize or rename, semantic to extract-with-difference-check under EXTEND or ABSORB, conceptual to GENERALIZE, functional to interface consolidation under COMPOSE, mechanism to EXTRACT_SHARED_INFRASTRUCTURE, data to single-source under ABSORB, evaluation to unify-the-rubric under FORMALIZE, authority to the precedence ladder rather than any merge, lifecycle to DEPRECATE, and temporal to retained history under REINFORCE-or-preserve. The map is a default, not a verdict — a detected type proposes its verdict and the pipeline's measurements confirm or override it — but the mapping is what makes the constitution a decision procedure rather than a judgment call: the type is detected, the default is proposed, the scores confirm, and the smaller-than-the-original new core, if any survives, is the only thing that reaches the sovereignty gate. This is the mechanism by which one proposal becomes many verdicts and a large candidate becomes a small build: each type present is routed to its own resolution, the resolutions execute in parallel, and what remains after all of them have carried their share away is the only part the estate actually pays a new system to hold.

PART VII FINAL LAW. A candidate resolves to a typed verdict with a named trigger — absorb, extend, reinforce, compose, generalize, formalize, extract shared infrastructure, replace, deprecate, reject, or the rarest and most heavily burdened, create new — and because one proposal routinely resolves to several verdicts at once, the constitution's success is measured by how small the surviving new core is after the reuse-heavy verdicts have carried the rest away, with CREATE_NEW forever the least frequent word the pipeline is permitted to say.
