DAIF-03 — FIDELITY, LOSS BUDGET AND BEHAVIORAL EQUIVALENCE

PART I — THESIS: FIDELITY IS WHAT SEPARATES A COMPILER FROM A SUMMARIZER

1.1 Mission. DAIF-02 gave the estate the ability to convert cognitive source into compiled representations: a stack of twelve typed intermediate forms that turn a wall of prose into selectable, linkable, executable objects. Nothing in DAIF-02 proves that any particular compilation was safe. The fabric can lower a conversation into semantic units, epistemic statuses, intents, decisions, and obligations, and it can do all of that while silently dropping the one exception that bounded a prohibition, the single evidence line that grounded a claim, or the follow-up nobody closed — and the compiled output will look immaculate, because a well-formed representation of the wrong content is still well-formed. This is the exact danger the fabric introduces by succeeding: without a fidelity gate, the CIR stack is an elaborate summarizer, and a summarizer that drops a hard constraint, an open obligation, or the reason behind a decision is strictly worse than no compilation at all, because it produces confident, well-structured, wrong behavior. Raw prose at least advertises its own unusability; a compiled artifact advertises trustworthiness it has not earned. DAIF-03 is the dataset that makes that trustworthiness earnable. It defines what fidelity means across every dimension of meaning the CIR stack claims to preserve, how much loss is permitted for which class of task, how equivalence is proven rather than asserted, and the compiler's duty to refuse — to fail visibly, loudly, and early — when it cannot guarantee that what it produced still provokes the behavior the source demanded. It gates every DAIF-02 output; a compilation that cannot demonstrate fidelity does not ship.

1.2 The asymmetry that governs everything. The founding law of this dataset is an asymmetry between what compression saves and what compression can destroy, and the asymmetry is not close. A compilation that reduces a hundred thousand words to four thousand saves a bounded, measurable, linear quantity: tokens, latency, attention. A compilation that drops one hard constraint costs an unbounded quantity: every future action taken under the compiled artifact proceeds without the constraint, each of those actions is a candidate production incident, and the incident's cost is set by the constraint's severity, not by the size of the text that carried it. A hard rule is typically the shortest unit in a source and the most expensive one to lose. This means the arithmetic that governs ordinary compression — percentage retained, percentage discarded — is not merely imprecise here, it is inverted. A compilation that preserves 99% of a source's semantic content and destroys one hard constraint is not a 99% success. It is a failure, and it is a worse failure than a compilation that preserved 40% of the prose and every constraint, because the second one is usable and honest while the first is a loaded instruction set. DAIF-03 therefore refuses, at the level of its scoring model, to average across dimensions. Fidelity is not a weighted mean of ten scores; it is a conjunction over ten dimensions, several of which are binary, and a failure on any binary dimension is a failure of the whole compilation regardless of how well the graded dimensions scored. Averaging is the mechanism by which a fatal loss disappears into a good-looking number, and it is prohibited here by name.

1.3 What fidelity is not. Three tempting definitions are rejected outright, because each is a metric the estate could optimize while getting worse. Fidelity is not compression ratio: a smaller artifact is not a better one, and the ratio is a cost figure, never a quality figure — DAIF-03 records it as an economic input and forbids it from entering the fidelity verdict. Fidelity is not readability or coherence: a compiled artifact reads well precisely because a compressor smoothed it, and smoothing is the operation that deletes hedges, qualifiers, and exceptions, so a readability score is positively correlated with the most dangerous loss class in this dataset. Fidelity is not source-overlap: token-level or embedding-level similarity between source and output rewards paraphrase-preserving compressions and is blind to the erased qualifier, because deleting the word "unless" changes similarity by a rounding error and changes meaning absolutely. What fidelity is, in one line: fidelity is the property that an actor consuming the compiled artifact is provoked into the same correct behavior as an actor consuming the full source. Everything else in this dataset — the ten dimensions, their tests, their thresholds — exists to make that property measurable before the behavior is observed, because behavior is expensive to test and the estate cannot afford to discover a fidelity failure only by suffering its consequence.

1.4 The ten dimensions, the dataset's spine. Fidelity decomposes into ten dimensions, each corresponding to a dimension of meaning the CIR stack claims to carry, and each with its own test, threshold, and failure mode. Semantic fidelity: is the meaning preserved. Intent fidelity: is what was actually wanted preserved, including implicit criteria, non-goals, and prohibitions. Constraint fidelity: are all hard constraints preserved — binary, never graded. Decision fidelity: are decisions preserved with their justifications, evidence, trade-offs, and revision conditions. Epistemic fidelity: does the artifact still distinguish fact from inference from assumption from speculation. Obligation fidelity: does one hundred percent of pending work survive — binary, never graded. Causal fidelity: are mechanisms preserved, not merely conclusions. Evaluation fidelity: does the artifact still say how to check that execution was correct. Provenance fidelity: can the path back to the original source be reconstructed. Behavioral fidelity: does an actor consuming the compiled artifact take the same correct decisions as an actor with the full source. The mapping to the CIR stack is one-to-one by construction and not by coincidence: semantic gates CIR-1, epistemic gates CIR-2, intent gates CIR-3, decision gates CIR-4, obligation gates CIR-5, causal gates CIR-6, evaluation gates CIR-9, provenance gates CIR-10's chain of custody, and constraint gates the Strength field DAIF-01 seals. A dimension without a gate is a dimension the compiler is free to lose.

1.5 Behavioral fidelity is the definitive test; the other nine are instrumental. The nine non-behavioral dimensions exist because behavior is the thing that matters and the thing that is expensive to measure. Each of the nine is a cheap, local, mechanical proxy for a specific way behavior can go wrong: a lost constraint predicts a violated boundary, a laundered assumption predicts an unwarranted commitment, a dropped obligation predicts work that never happens. They are proxies, and the dataset says so in its own text so that no future actor mistakes a passing proxy for a passing system. A compilation can score cleanly on all nine and still fail behaviorally, because the nine test the presence of preserved units and behavior tests their sufficiency in composition — an artifact can carry every constraint, every decision, and every obligation as individual units and still fail to make any of them salient at the moment an actor needs them, and salience is behavioral. The converse also holds and is the reason the nine are kept: an artifact that fails a binary dimension is known to be unsafe without running a single behavioral trial, which is what lets the estate refuse a bad compilation cheaply instead of discovering it expensively. The doctrine is therefore a ladder -> the nine dimensions are the fast screen that rejects most bad compilations at low cost, and behavioral fidelity is the slow, authoritative test that any compilation must pass before it is trusted as a substitute for its source.

1.6 The gate and the duty to refuse. DAIF-03 is not advisory. It is a gate that stands between DAIF-02's output and every consumer of that output, and it has three verdicts: PASS, meaning the compilation is a faithful carrier within its declared loss budget and may be used in place of its source; DEGRADE, meaning the compilation is faithful enough for some task classes and not others, and the artifact is marked with the classes it may not serve; and REFUSE, meaning the compiler cannot demonstrate fidelity and the compilation does not ship — the consumer is handed the source, or a smaller compilation over a narrower scope, and told exactly why. The duty to refuse is the dataset's moral center. A compiler that cannot guarantee fidelity and ships anyway has committed the estate's cardinal sin, which is the silent substitution of a confident artifact for a true one, and the estate has recorded that shape often enough to name it: a scaffold that looks complete, a gate never observed to refuse, a claim whose evidence was never collected. Refusal must be visible, must name the dimension that failed, must name the unit that was lost, and must be cheaper for the compiler to emit than a false PASS is — which is why every threshold in this dataset is set so that the absence of evidence produces REFUSE and never PASS. An unmeasured dimension is a failed dimension.

1.7 Composition and the absorbed metric authority. DAIF-03 forks nothing. It composes FD-04's six-lens transfer test as its behavioral instrument, CO-12's Telemetry-Before-Claims contract as the rule that no fidelity number may be asserted without the measurement that produced it, SQI's verification axis and reality-supremacy as the law that the observed artifact outranks the compiler's claim about it, ACIS's ladder as the authority on epistemic status, DRK as the authority on decision anatomy, GK-04 as the authority on lineage, and UKDL as the register where a repeated fidelity failure is promoted into a standing law. It also absorbs, by the Owner's ruling, the canonical Metric-Authority candidate: the question of who owns a metric, what a metric may be used to claim, and how a metric is prevented from becoming a target is answered inside this dataset rather than in a standalone system, because a fidelity fabric that did not own its own metrics would be a fabric whose numbers anyone could redefine. Every metric this dataset defines therefore ships with a countermetric — a second measurement that moves in the opposite direction when the first is being gamed — and a metric without a countermetric is not admissible as evidence of fidelity.

PART I FINAL LAW. Compilation without a fidelity gate is summarization wearing a compiler's costume, and because a compression that saves many tokens while destroying one hard constraint is not a ninety-nine-percent success but an unbounded failure whose cost is set by the constraint and not by the text, fidelity may never be averaged into a single number: it is a conjunction over ten dimensions — semantic, intent, constraint, decision, epistemic, obligation, causal, evaluation, provenance, and behavioral — of which behavioral is the definitive test and the other nine are cheap instrumental proxies for it, DAIF-03 gates every DAIF-02 output with PASS, DEGRADE, or REFUSE, and a compilation that cannot demonstrate its fidelity does not ship.

PART II — SEMANTIC FIDELITY

2.1 Mission. Semantic fidelity asks the first and most basic question a fidelity gate can ask: does the compiled artifact preserve the meaning of the source. It is first because every other dimension presupposes it — an intent that was semantically mangled cannot be checked for purpose, a constraint whose meaning shifted cannot be checked for preservation — and it is basic in the sense that a failure here is a failure of the compiler's most elementary claim. It is also the dimension most vulnerable to a comfortable illusion, because a compiled artifact that reads as a fluent, plausible account of its source feels semantically faithful, and fluency is exactly what a lossy compressor produces best. Semantic fidelity is therefore defined not as similarity of text but as preservation of assertional content: for every claim the source made, the artifact makes the same claim, with the same scope, the same qualifiers, the same strength, and the same distinctions intact, and it makes no claim the source did not make.

2.2 The unit of measurement is CIR-1, never the prose. The cardinal procedural rule of this dimension is that semantic fidelity is measured against CIR-1 semantic units, not against the raw prose of the source or the raw prose of the output. Measuring prose against prose is what produces every failure mode in this Part: it invites token overlap, embedding similarity, and human reading as evidence, and all three are blind to the losses that matter. Measured against CIR-1, the test becomes mechanical and enumerable. The source, decomposed by CIR-1, yields a set of atomic semantic units, each mapped to exactly one DAIF-01 kind. The compiled artifact, decomposed by the same CIR-1 procedure, yields a second set. Semantic fidelity is then a correspondence problem over two finite sets of typed units: every unit in the source set must have exactly one corresponding unit in the artifact set that carries the same assertional content, the same kind, and the same scope. A source unit with no correspondent is a loss. An artifact unit with no source correspondent is a fabrication. A correspondence whose kinds differ is a type drift. Each of the three is countable, each is attributable to a specific unit with a CIR-0 locator, and none of the three is visible to a similarity score. The correspondence is computed over units and audited by a reviewer who reads the units, never the smoothed prose, because the smoothed prose is the medium in which the loss hides.

2.3 The four traps. Paraphrase drift is the accumulation of small restatements, each individually defensible, whose composition changes the claim: a source unit saying a condition holds in most observed cases becomes an artifact unit saying the condition holds, and the drift is invisible per-hop and fatal in aggregate. The erased qualifier is the deletion of a single scope-bearing word — unless, except, only, when, at most, never on production — and it is the highest-yield loss per byte in the entire dataset, because the compressor's incentive is to remove exactly the words that make a sentence longer and more conditional, and those words are the ones that make it true. The collapsed distinction is the compression of two units that differed materially into one unit that differs from both: a source that separated a rule from its narrower special case emits a single rule, and every future actor now applies the general form where the special one governed. The plausible-but-wrong restatement is the most dangerous because it is the most fluent: the compiler produces a unit that is coherent, idiomatic, consistent with its neighbors, and not what the source said, and it survives every review that reads for sense rather than for correspondence.

2.4 The test procedure. The procedure is a four-step correspondence audit, and it runs on every compilation, not on a sample. Step one: decompose the source through CIR-1 into typed units and record each unit's locator into CIR-0. Step two: decompose the artifact through the identical CIR-1 procedure — identical, because a different decomposition procedure on the two sides makes the correspondence uncomputable. Step three: compute the correspondence, producing four counts: matched units, lost units (source units with no correspondent), fabricated units (artifact units with no source correspondent), and drifted units (correspondents whose kind, scope, strength, or qualifier set differs). Step four: adjudicate every non-matched unit individually — no aggregate score is permitted to absorb them — classifying each as an admissible loss (a unit the loss budget of the artifact's declared task class explicitly permits to drop, such as a mechanically redundant restatement) or an inadmissible one. The evidence that counts is the adjudication record: a list of every non-matched unit with its locator, its kind, and its verdict. A compilation that presents a percentage without the record has produced no evidence at all, per CO-12's Telemetry-Before-Claims contract, and is treated as unmeasured -> REFUSE.

2.5 Thresholds and their bindings. Semantic fidelity is a graded dimension, and its thresholds bind to the artifact's declared task class rather than to a single universal number, because the tolerable semantic loss for a compilation feeding an exploratory read is not the tolerable loss for one feeding an execution. Three bands govern. For an artifact declared as an execution carrier — one an actor will act on without consulting the source — the threshold is zero inadmissible loss, zero fabrication, and zero drift on any unit whose kind is constraint, obligation, decision, or evidence; graded loss is permitted only on units of kind assertion, definition, or preference, and only up to the loss budget the artifact declares. For an artifact declared as a reasoning carrier — one an actor reasons with and may consult the source behind — the same zero-tolerance applies to the four load-bearing kinds, and the graded budget on the remainder widens. For an artifact declared as an orientation carrier — one an actor reads to know where to look — graded loss is wide on all kinds except constraint, but the artifact is marked as non-actionable and may not be substituted for its source in any execution path. Fabrication is zero-tolerance in all three bands without exception: a unit the source never asserted is not a compression, it is an invention, and no task class has a budget for inventions.

2.6 Failure modes and countermeasures. The signature failure of this dimension is the fluent falsehood: an artifact that passes every reading-based review because it is coherent, and fails the correspondence audit because its coherence was purchased by smoothing away the conditions that made the source's claims true. Its countermeasure is structural, not exhortative — the audit is defined over units and forbids reading the artifact as prose, so fluency has no channel through which to influence the verdict. The second failure is procedural asymmetry: an auditor who decomposes the source carefully and the artifact loosely, producing spurious matches; the countermeasure is that both sides run the identical CIR-1 procedure and the decomposition of each is itself recorded and re-runnable, so a divergence in procedure is detectable as a divergence in unit counts on a re-run. The third is budget laundering: an adjudicator who classifies an inadmissible loss as admissible by reclassifying the unit's kind — a constraint recorded as a preference, and therefore droppable. The countermeasure is that the kind is assigned by CIR-1 at the source side before the artifact exists, and the adjudicator may not re-type a source unit; a proposed re-typing is an appeal, is logged, and is decided against the DAIF-01 Strength field, not against the adjudicator's convenience.

2.7 Metrics, countermetrics, and adversarial cases. The primary metrics are unit correspondence rate, inadmissible-loss count, fabrication count, and kind-drift count, and each is reported as an absolute, never as a ratio, because a ratio is satisfied by inflating its denominator — a compiler that emits many trivial units raises its correspondence rate without preserving one additional constraint. The countermetric to correspondence rate is load-bearing-unit survival, computed only over units of kind constraint, obligation, decision, and evidence: a compiler optimizing correspondence by preserving cheap units and dropping expensive ones drives the primary metric up and the countermetric down, and the divergence is the alarm. The countermetric to compression ratio is qualifier survival: the count of scope-bearing tokens present in the source's units that survive into the artifact's, which falls precisely when a compressor is buying its ratio by erasing conditions. The adversarial input against this dimension is a source whose critical qualifier is expressed structurally rather than lexically — a condition established in one unit and relied upon silently by three later ones — so that no single unit visibly carries it and any per-unit audit passes while the compiled composition is false. The defense is that CIR-1's boundary discipline forbids a unit that depends on an unstated condition: a unit whose meaning changes when read alone is not atomic, and the correspondence audit is run over atomic units precisely so that a structurally-carried qualifier has been made lexical before the audit begins.

PART II FINAL LAW. Semantic fidelity is the preservation of assertional content — same claims, same scope, same qualifiers, same distinctions, no additions — and it is measured as a correspondence over CIR-1 typed units and never as similarity between prose, because the four traps of paraphrase drift, the erased qualifier, the collapsed distinction, and the plausible-but-wrong restatement are all invisible to any measure that reads the smoothed output as text; loss is adjudicated unit by unit with a locator and a verdict, fabrication is zero-tolerance in every task class, correspondence is reported as an absolute against a load-bearing-unit-survival countermetric so a compiler cannot buy its score with cheap units, and an unmeasured correspondence is a failed one.

PART III — INTENT FIDELITY AND CONSTRAINT FIDELITY

3.1 Mission. This Part gates the two dimensions that determine whether a compiled artifact can be acted upon at all: whether it still knows what was wanted, and whether it still knows what may never be done. They are paired because they are the positive and negative faces of the same boundary. Intent fidelity preserves the purpose that makes a result right; constraint fidelity preserves the prohibitions that make a result permissible. An artifact that loses intent produces work that is conformant and pointless. An artifact that loses a constraint produces work that is conformant, purposeful, and forbidden. The two dimensions differ radically in their scoring discipline — intent is graded and constraint is binary — and the reason for that asymmetry is the load-bearing argument of this Part.

3.2 Intent fidelity: the definition. Intent fidelity asks whether the artifact preserves what was actually wanted, and it is measured against CIR-3, which holds intent as a structured object: the original intention, the expected result, the explicit criteria, the implicit criteria, the priorities, the preferences, the prohibitions, the non-goals, and the definition of done. Intent fidelity is the survival of that structure, slot by slot. The explicit criteria are the easy half and are audited by correspondence exactly as semantic units are. The hard half is everything the source did not say aloud: the implicit criteria it assumed everyone shared, the non-goals it never thought to name because no one in the room would have proposed them, and the priorities it expressed by emphasis rather than by ranking. These are the slots a compressor deletes first, because they have the weakest surface presence in the source and the highest cost to lose, and they are the slots CIR-3 exists to have already made explicit through one_shot's Q&A scaffold. Intent fidelity therefore has a precondition: an artifact compiled from a source whose intent was never surfaced cannot be audited for intent fidelity, and the honest verdict in that case is not PASS but UNMEASURED -> DEGRADE, with the artifact marked as unsafe for any task whose correctness depends on purpose rather than on instruction.

3.3 The failure that defines intent fidelity: the technically-conformant miss. The signature failure is a result that satisfies every literal word of the request and violates its purpose. Its anatomy is always the same: the instruction said do X, the intent said X was wanted in order to achieve Y, the compilation kept X and dropped Y, and an actor consuming the artifact did X in a manner that defeated Y — efficiently, confidently, and with a clean checklist. The reason this failure is a fidelity failure and not merely a judgment failure is that the actor had no channel through which to detect it: with Y absent from the artifact, no amount of care would have surfaced the conflict, because the artifact contained no representation of the standard the result was violating. The countermeasure is structural and is the whole reason CIR-3 holds intent separately from the instructions it motivates: an artifact that carries the intention, the non-goals, and the definition of done gives the actor a second, higher check — does this result serve the purpose, not merely execute the instruction — and an artifact that carries only instructions gives the actor nothing but the checklist that the failure passes. The intent-fidelity test is consequently a divergence probe: for each preserved instruction, can the artifact still detect a result that satisfies the instruction and defeats its intention. If it cannot, the intent slot that would have detected it was lost.

3.4 The intent test procedure and thresholds. The procedure runs in three steps. Step one: enumerate the source's CIR-3 slots and mark each as present, absent-in-source, or lost-in-compilation — the distinction between the second and third is critical, because an intent the source never surfaced is a gap in capture and an intent the source surfaced and the compilation dropped is a gap in fidelity, and conflating them lets a compiler hide its losses inside the source's silences. Step two: for every preserved instruction in the artifact, construct at least one conformant-but-purpose-defeating result and check whether the artifact's retained intent slots reject it; a slot set that accepts the defeating result has lost the criterion that would have rejected it. Step three: check the definition of done against the intention — a done-condition that can be satisfied without achieving the intention is a done-condition that was compiled from the instruction rather than from the purpose. Thresholds: the intention, the expected result, and the definition of done are required and their loss is an automatic REFUSE, because those three are the slots CIR-3 itself declares mandatory. Prohibitions and non-goals are treated as constraints and inherit the binary discipline of the next section. Explicit criteria are graded with a high floor. Implicit criteria are graded, and their loss triggers DEGRADE rather than REFUSE, with the artifact marked as instruction-safe and purpose-unsafe.

3.5 Constraint fidelity is binary, and the reason is not severity — it is coherence. Constraint fidelity asks whether all hard constraints in the source survive into the artifact, and it composes CIR-3's prohibitions together with DAIF-01's Strength field, which is the estate's sealed authority on whether a rule is hard, firm, or advisory. The scoring rule is stark: one hundred percent survival, or the compilation is rejected. There is no partial credit, no weighting by constraint importance, and no threshold below one. The naive objection is that this is merely a severity judgment expressed as an absolute, and that a 95% threshold would be a reasonable engineering compromise. It is not, and the reason is that a graded threshold on hard constraints is not strict — it is incoherent. A hard constraint is, by the definition DAIF-01 seals, a rule with no admissible exception within its scope: its entire semantic content is the claim that no action may cross this boundary. A rule preserved in 95% of the artifact's scope is a rule with a 5% exception, and a rule with an exception is not the rule that was written — it is a different, weaker rule that the compiler invented and the source never authorized. The 95%-preserved hard constraint is therefore not a partially-preserved hard constraint; it is a fully-preserved soft constraint substituted for a hard one, which is a fabrication under Part II's zero-tolerance rule, not a graded loss. Grading a binary field is a category error, and the category error is the mechanism by which every hard rule in the estate could be quietly downgraded one compilation at a time.

3.6 The constraint test procedure. The procedure is an enumeration, not a sample, and it is the cheapest test in this dataset because constraints are few, short, and typed. Step one: extract from the source every unit whose DAIF-01 kind is constraint or exception and whose Strength field marks it hard or firm, producing the constraint register — a finite, enumerable list with a locator per entry. Step two: for each register entry, locate its correspondent in the artifact and verify four properties: the prohibition survives, its scope survives unwidened and unnarrowed, its condition survives (the "when Y" that bounds the "never X"), and its Strength survives unweakened. Step three: verify that the artifact introduces no constraint the source did not carry, because a fabricated prohibition is as damaging as a lost one — it blocks work the source permitted and it will be obeyed with the same confidence. Step four: emit the constraint ledger — every register entry with a PRESERVED, LOST, WIDENED, NARROWED, WEAKENED, or FABRICATED verdict. Any verdict other than PRESERVED on any entry -> REFUSE. The ledger is the artifact's admission ticket; a compilation shipped without one has not been tested, and by the unmeasured-is-failed rule it does not ship.

3.7 Failure modes, adversarial cases, and metrics. The signature failure of constraint fidelity is the severed condition, inherited directly from CIR-1's boundary discipline: a constraint reading never do X when Y holds is compiled into a bald never do X, and the artifact now enforces a prohibition the source never wrote — an over-broad rule that will block legitimate work until someone, under pressure, discovers it is wrong and deletes the whole thing, taking the real constraint with it. Its mirror is the widened exception: the same severance in the other direction, yielding do X freely with the condition lost, which is the loss that produces incidents. The adversarial case is the constraint expressed as a negative preference — a source that says the estate strongly prefers never to do X — where the lexical form is advisory and the Strength field, correctly assigned, is hard; a compiler reading surface language rather than the typed field will compress it as a preference and lose it under the graded budget. The defense is that the constraint register is built from the Strength field and never from the prose, which is precisely why DAIF-01 made Strength a field rather than a matter of tone. The metrics are absolute counts — constraints registered, preserved, lost, widened, weakened, fabricated — and the countermetric to constraint-preservation is register completeness, measured by re-extracting the register with an independent procedure and comparing counts, because the way to score a perfect one hundred percent on constraint preservation without preserving anything is to build a register that omits the constraints you were going to lose. A gate that never registers a rule can never be observed to lose it.

PART III FINAL LAW. Intent fidelity preserves what was actually wanted — intention, expected result, explicit and implicit criteria, priorities, prohibitions, non-goals, and a definition of done that checks the purpose rather than the checklist — and its signature failure is the technically-conformant miss, a result satisfying every literal word while defeating the reason the words were written, detectable only because CIR-3 holds intent apart from the instructions it motivates; constraint fidelity preserves every hard constraint with its scope, its condition, and its Strength intact, and it is binary not because constraints are severe but because a graded threshold on a hard rule is incoherent — a ninety-five-percent-preserved hard constraint is a soft constraint the compiler fabricated and the source never authorized — so the constraint ledger admits only PRESERVED, and any other verdict on any entry is a REFUSE.

PART IV — DECISION FIDELITY AND EPISTEMIC FIDELITY

4.1 Mission. This Part gates the two dimensions that determine whether a compiled artifact can be reasoned with rather than merely obeyed. Decision fidelity preserves the estate's choices in a form that can be revisited, defended, and reversed. Epistemic fidelity preserves the estate's honesty about how well it knows what it claims. They are paired because they fail together and for the same reason: both dimensions are carried by material that a compressor perceives as overhead — the justification behind a conclusion, the hedge in front of a claim — and both losses are invisible at the point of consumption, because the artifact that remains is shorter, cleaner, more confident, and wrong in a way that produces no error message.

4.2 Decision fidelity: the definition. Decision fidelity asks whether every decision in the source survives into the artifact as a complete governable object, and it is measured against CIR-4, which composes DRK's decision_record. The complete object is the question the decision resolved, the options weighed including the rejected ones, the choice taken, the justification, the evidence the justification rested on, the authority that made it, the scope it binds, the consequences and trade-offs it accepted, the revision conditions that should reopen it, its expiry, and the decisions it superseded. Decision fidelity is the survival of that anatomy, and it is graded on the peripheral slots and binary on five: question, choice, justification, authority, and scope. Those five are the slots CIR-4 declares load-bearing, and their loss is not a degradation of the decision — it is the destruction of the decision as a governable object, leaving behind an artifact that records what was done and destroys the ability to reconsider it.

4.3 The bare conclusion and why it is un-revisable. The signature failure of decision fidelity is compression to the outcome: the artifact carries that X was chosen and drops that X was chosen because evidence E held under constraint C at the cost of trade-off T, to be reopened if E changed. The compressed form is shorter by an order of magnitude and reads as complete, and it is precisely as un-reversible as a fossil. To reverse a decision safely, a later actor must be able to check whether the reason still holds — is E still true, does C still bind, is T still acceptable — and a bare conclusion answers none of those questions, so the actor faces two bad options and takes one of them. Either the actor reverses the decision blindly, re-opening whatever problem it silently solved, or the actor treats it as immovable precisely because its reasoning is invisible and nobody dares touch a rule they cannot explain, and the decision outlives its own grounding as institutional superstition. Both outcomes are produced by the same compression, and neither raises an error at the moment of loss. The countermeasure is that the decision-fidelity test is not a presence check on the conclusion but a reversibility check on the record: for each decision in the artifact, can an actor determine, from the artifact alone, what would have to become false for this decision to be wrong. A decision that cannot answer that question has lost its justification, whatever text remains beside it.

4.4 The decision test procedure and thresholds. Step one: extract the source's decision register from CIR-4 — every decision-kind unit with its slot inventory and its locator. Step two: for each entry, verify the five load-bearing slots survive with their content intact, and verify that the justification's evidence pointers still resolve: a justification whose evidence pointers point at units the compilation dropped is a justification that has been hollowed out while appearing preserved, and it is the subtlest loss in this dimension, because the prose of the justification survives and its grounding does not. Step three: verify the forward-looking slots — revision conditions and expiry — because a decision compiled without its revision conditions cannot be surfaced for reconsideration by DAIF-21 when reality moves out from under it, and a decision compiled without its expiry is silently promoted from temporary to permanent by the act of compilation. Step four: verify supersession pointers, because a lineage broken by compilation resurfaces a superseded decision as current. Thresholds: loss of any of the five load-bearing slots -> REFUSE. Loss of a revision condition or an expiry -> DEGRADE, with the artifact marked as not-reality-synchronizable. Loss of a rejected option -> graded, because the rejected options carry the decision's negative space and their loss narrows future reconsideration without falsifying the record.

4.5 Epistemic fidelity and the cardinal violation. Epistemic fidelity asks whether the artifact still distinguishes fact from inference from assumption from speculation, and it is measured against CIR-2, which composes the ACIS ladder. The test is a status-correspondence audit: for every unit in the artifact, the epistemic status it carries must be identical to the status ACIS assigned it in the source, and any divergence is scored by direction. A unit whose status was weakened by compilation is a conservative loss — it costs the estate confidence it had earned, which is wasteful and safe. A unit whose status was strengthened by compilation is the cardinal violation of this dataset, and it has a name: epistemic laundering. An assumption enters the compiler wearing a hedge and emerges wearing the confidence of a fact, and every downstream actor now acts on a guess with the certainty due a measurement. Epistemic laundering is a strict-zero-tolerance failure — one laundered unit -> REFUSE — for the same reason constraint loss is binary: the laundered unit does not degrade the artifact's epistemic quality, it inverts the artifact's epistemic meaning, converting the honest statement we believe this into the false statement we know this.

4.6 Why compression is the exact pressure that causes laundering. Laundering is not an occasional accident of compilation; it is the predicted output of any compressor that is not explicitly gated against it, and the mechanism is mechanical rather than moral. Epistemic status, in natural cognitive source, is carried almost entirely by hedges — we think, it appears, probably, in the cases we checked, assuming the earlier finding holds — and hedges have three properties that make them a compressor's first target: they add length, they add no assertional content that a naive semantic correspondence would miss, and they reduce the fluency and confidence of the resulting text, which every readability signal penalizes. A compressor optimizing for brevity and coherence will therefore delete hedges before it deletes anything else, and the deletion of a hedge is not a stylistic edit — it is a promotion up the ACIS ladder, performed silently, with no evidence, by a process that has no authority to promote anything. This is why CIR-2 requires an evidence pointer for any status above pending-verification, and why the epistemic-fidelity test does not read the artifact's prose for confidence markers at all: it reads the artifact's CIR-2 status fields and their evidence pointers, and a unit whose status rose while its evidence pointer set did not grow is convicted on arithmetic. No claim can gain certainty by being carried forward; certainty is gained only by verification, and a compiler performs none.

4.7 Failure modes, adversarial cases, metrics. Beyond laundering, three failures recur. Status flattening: a compiler that drops the status field entirely, producing an artifact of unstatused claims, which a consumer will read as uniformly true — this is laundering by omission and is scored as laundering, because the absence of a status is consumed as a fact. Evidence-pointer decay: statuses preserved while the units they point at are dropped, leaving verified claims whose verification is unreachable, which fails the provenance dimension in Part VI and is flagged here as well. Weakest-link erasure: a compilation that preserves a strong unit's status while dropping the weak unit it depended upon, breaking the CIR-7 dependency edge that CIR-2's weakest-link propagation reads, so the artifact presents as verified a claim whose only support was an assumption the compiler deleted. The adversarial case is a source whose author sincerely asserts an inference in confident prose while CIR-2, reading the evidence pointer, correctly typed it as inferred; a compiler that re-derives status from the artifact's tone rather than inheriting the source's field will "correct" the status upward and call it fidelity. The defense is that status is inherited, never re-derived — the compiler has no authority to type epistemic status, only to carry it. Metrics: laundered-unit count (absolute, target zero), unstatused-unit count (absolute, target zero), decision-slot survival per load-bearing slot, and resolvable-evidence-pointer rate. The countermetric to hedge-preservation is assertion density, because a compiler can trivially maximize hedge survival by hedging everything, producing an artifact so uniformly uncertain that no actor can distinguish the verified from the guessed — which is laundering's mirror image and is equally a loss of the distinction this dimension exists to protect.

PART IV FINAL LAW. Decision fidelity preserves every choice as a governable object — question, choice, justification, authority, scope, with resolving evidence pointers, revision conditions, expiry, and supersession — because a decision compressed to a bare conclusion is un-revisable and will be either reversed blindly or obeyed as superstition; epistemic fidelity preserves the distinction between fact, inference, assumption, and speculation by inheriting CIR-2's ACIS-assigned status rather than re-deriving it from tone, and its cardinal violation is epistemic laundering — an assumption emerging from compilation wearing the confidence of a fact — which is not an occasional accident but the predicted output of any ungated compressor, since hedges are long, assertionally invisible, and fluency-reducing, and therefore the first thing a compressor deletes; one laundered unit is a REFUSE, because certainty is gained only by verification and a compiler performs none.

PART V — OBLIGATION FIDELITY AND CAUSAL FIDELITY

5.1 Mission. This Part gates the two dimensions whose losses are the hardest to notice and therefore the most likely to accumulate. Obligation fidelity preserves work that is owed; causal fidelity preserves the mechanisms behind conclusions. Their common property is that neither loss produces a symptom at the time it happens. A dropped semantic unit can be caught by a correspondence audit; a laundered status can be caught by an arithmetic check. But a dropped obligation produces silence — the work simply never happens, and no error is ever raised, because nothing in the estate is waiting for it. And a dropped mechanism produces confidence — the conclusion survives, is repeated, and is applied long after the conditions that made it true have gone.

5.2 Obligation fidelity: the definition and the binary rule. Obligation fidelity asks whether one hundred percent of the pending work in the source survives into the artifact, and it is measured against CIR-5, which compiles obligations into typed objects, and it feeds DAIF-07, which owns the obligation lifecycle. The register is enumerable: every unit of kind obligation, together with every unresolved question in CIR-4's decision register (a deferred decision is an obligation to decide), every pending verification CIR-2 marked, every promise, deliverable, migration, follow-up, unaccepted specification, and untested function the source named. Obligation fidelity is binary — one hundred percent survival or REFUSE — and it shares the constraint dimension's binary discipline for a parallel reason. A constraint at 95% is a soft constraint the compiler fabricated. An obligation register at 95% is not a slightly-incomplete register; it is a complete-looking register that is missing exactly the entries nobody will ever look for again, and its incompleteness is undetectable from the inside, because the only artifact that recorded the missing obligation was the source the compilation replaced.

5.3 Why silent loss is the most damaging loss class. Every other fidelity failure in this dataset eventually announces itself. A lost constraint announces itself as an incident. A laundered assumption announces itself as a wrong commitment. A collapsed distinction announces itself as a misapplied rule. Each is expensive, and each is at least discoverable, because each produces an action whose consequences can be traced back. A lost obligation announces itself as nothing. The work is not done wrong; it is not done. The migration is not botched; it is never begun. The verification is not failed; it is never run, and the artifact it would have checked ships with its correctness assumed. There is no failing test, no exception, no incident report, and no trace — the obligation's only representation was in a source that has been superseded by an artifact that does not carry it, so the estate is not merely failing to do the work, it has lost the knowledge that the work exists. This is the failure class the estate has recorded most often under its own names — the scaffold that looks complete, the promise made in conversation that dies when the conversation closes, the follow-up that evaporates at session end — and it is the founding motivation of DAIF-07. Obligation fidelity is the gate that keeps DAIF-07 from being a lifecycle over an incomplete population, because a lifecycle that tracks 95% of the estate's obligations perfectly is a lifecycle whose completeness claims are false.

5.4 The obligation test procedure. Step one: extract the source's obligation register from CIR-5, with a locator per entry and, critically, each entry's closure condition — the condition whose satisfaction discharges the obligation. An obligation compiled without its closure condition enters DAIF-07's lifecycle unclosable, held open forever because nothing can ever satisfy it, and that is a fidelity failure even though the obligation itself survived. Step two: correspondence-match the artifact's register against the source's, requiring exact cardinality — every source entry has exactly one artifact correspondent. Step three: verify each correspondent's four load-bearing fields: the work owed, the closure condition, the authority who owes it, and the scope. Step four: verify no fabricated obligations — an invented obligation is not harmless, it consumes attention and will be worked, and work performed against an obligation the source never incurred is waste that looks like diligence. Step five: emit the obligation ledger. Any missing entry, any lost closure condition, any fabricated entry -> REFUSE. The countermetric to register survival is register completeness at the source side, measured by independently re-extracting the source register and comparing cardinality, because the way to achieve one hundred percent obligation survival while losing obligations is to fail to register them in the first place — a compiler cannot be observed to drop an obligation it never recognized, and the estate has recorded exactly this shape: a gate scores perfectly by never seeing the thing it was built to catch.

5.5 Causal fidelity: the definition. Causal fidelity asks whether the artifact preserves mechanisms and not merely conclusions, and it is measured against CIR-6, which holds why things happened. The distinction is precise. A conclusion is a claim about what is true or what to do. A mechanism is the account of why it is true or why to do it — the chain of cause and effect, the conditions under which the chain holds, and the reasons the chain would break. Causal fidelity requires that for every conclusion the artifact carries, the causal chain that produced it survives with its conditions attached, so that a later actor can evaluate whether the chain still runs. It is graded rather than binary, but its floor is high and its threshold binds to consequence: for a conclusion the artifact presents as actionable, the mechanism is required and its loss is a REFUSE; for a conclusion presented as background, mechanism loss is a DEGRADE that marks the conclusion non-actionable.

5.6 Cargo-cult knowledge: the failure mode of causal loss. A conclusion without its mechanism cannot be re-evaluated when conditions change, and this produces the estate's most durable pathology: knowledge that outlives its own validity. The mechanism is straightforward. A source establishes that under conditions C, doing X produces good outcome O — and the causal chain, C -> X -> O, is what makes the guidance true. A compilation keeps do X and drops the chain. Conditions change; C no longer holds; and the estate keeps doing X, because the artifact says to do X and contains no representation of the condition that would tell anyone to stop. The practice persists, is defended, is taught to new actors, and is enforced by gates, long after the reason for it has evaporated — and because the reason is absent from the artifact, nobody can even formulate the argument against it. This is cargo-cult knowledge: a ritual executed faithfully with its purpose forgotten, and it is generated not by stupidity but by lossy compilation, because the mechanism is the longest and most abstract part of any source and the conclusion is the shortest and most quotable. The countermeasure is that the causal-fidelity test is a falsifiability probe: for each actionable conclusion in the artifact, can a reader determine from the artifact alone what would have to change for the conclusion to stop being true. If the artifact cannot answer that, the mechanism was lost, whatever confident text surrounds the conclusion.

5.7 Adversarial cases, cross-links, and metrics. The adversarial input against obligation fidelity is the buried obligation: a commitment phrased as an aside inside an explanatory passage — we should probably check that later — whose surface form gives no signal that real work was promised, and which a compressor discards as conversational filler with perfect confidence. The defense is upstream, at CIR-1's boundary discipline, which is required to cut a commitment out as its own obligation-typed unit precisely so the fidelity gate has something to count; an obligation the fabric never made atomic is an obligation this gate cannot protect, which is why the source-side register-completeness countermetric is not optional. The adversarial input against causal fidelity is the mechanism expressed as an anecdote — the reason for a rule carried entirely in the incident that produced it, which a compressor reads as narrative color and strips as redundant — and the defense is that UKDL's own rule anatomy makes the origin incident a required field, so a compiled law whose ORIGEN is empty has failed causal fidelity by inspection, without any reading of the prose. Metrics: obligation-register survival (absolute count, target exact equality), closure-condition survival (absolute, target exact equality), fabricated-obligation count (target zero), actionable-conclusion mechanism-survival rate, and falsifiability-probe pass rate. The countermetric to mechanism-survival is mechanism specificity: a compiler can trivially preserve a mechanism by emitting a vacuous one — because it works, because the evidence supports it — and a chain whose conditions cannot be falsified is not a mechanism, it is a decoration that satisfies the metric while providing exactly nothing the metric was built to protect.

PART V FINAL LAW. Obligation fidelity requires that one hundred percent of pending work survive with its closure condition, its authority, and its scope, and it is binary because a 95%-complete obligation register is a complete-looking register missing precisely the entries nobody will ever look for again — the most damaging loss class in the estate, since a dropped obligation raises no error, produces no incident, and destroys even the knowledge that the work exists; causal fidelity requires that mechanisms survive alongside conclusions, because a conclusion without its chain cannot be re-evaluated when conditions change and becomes cargo-cult knowledge — a ritual executed faithfully with its purpose forgotten, enforced by gates long after its reason evaporated — and the test for both is not presence but function: an obligation must be closable, and an actionable conclusion must be falsifiable, from the artifact alone.

PART VI — EVALUATION FIDELITY AND PROVENANCE FIDELITY

6.1 Mission. This Part gates the two dimensions that make a compiled artifact accountable to reality rather than merely internally consistent. Evaluation fidelity preserves the artifact's knowledge of how to check that execution was correct. Provenance fidelity preserves the artifact's path back to the sources that grounded it. They are paired because they are the two directions of the same accountability: evaluation faces forward, letting an actor verify a result against the world; provenance faces backward, letting an actor re-verify a claim against its origin. An artifact that loses both is a closed system — internally coherent, externally unanchored, and impossible to correct, because it can neither be checked against what it produces nor traced to what produced it. Such an artifact is not knowledge; it is doctrine.

6.2 Evaluation fidelity: the definition. Evaluation fidelity asks whether the artifact retains how to know that execution worked, and it is measured against CIR-9, which composes SQI's evaluation verdicts and its verification axis. The unit of preservation is the check: for every instruction, decision, or obligation the artifact carries as actionable, there must survive an associated evaluation — the observable signal that distinguishes a correct execution from an incorrect one, the procedure that produces that signal, and the threshold that adjudicates it. A check is composed of three parts and all three are load-bearing. The signal without the procedure is a wish (we will know it works when it works). The procedure without the threshold is a measurement with no verdict (we ran the suite; nobody said what passing means). The threshold without the signal is a number attached to nothing. Evaluation fidelity requires all three per actionable unit, and its failure is scored per unit, not in aggregate.

6.3 An unverifiable instruction is a hypothesis executed as a plan. This is the load-bearing argument of the evaluation dimension, and it is stated as an identity rather than a warning. Knowledge that says what to do but not how to know it worked has not been reduced in quality — it has been reclassified. A do-this whose correctness cannot be checked is, epistemically, a proposal that doing this will work; the estate cannot distinguish it from a guess, because the only thing that would distinguish it is the check, and the check is gone. Executing it is therefore not the execution of a plan but the execution of a hypothesis under the costume of a plan, and the estate has recorded the consequence of this repeatedly: work declared done because it compiled, systems declared shipped because the files existed, gates declared active because they were written. The compression that produces this is seductive, because the check is the longest and least interesting part of any instruction — the instruction is a sentence and the verification is a paragraph — and a compressor keeping the sentence and dropping the paragraph believes it has kept the important half. It has kept the half that generates action and dropped the half that makes the action accountable, which is exactly the trade the estate can least afford. Evaluation fidelity's test is therefore a done-gate probe: for each actionable unit in the artifact, can a consumer determine, from the artifact alone, what observation would prove the execution correct and what observation would prove it wrong. A unit for which both answers cannot be produced is unverifiable -> REFUSE for any execution-carrier artifact.

6.4 The evaluation test procedure and thresholds. Step one: enumerate the artifact's actionable units — every instruction, obligation, decision-with-consequence, and procedural unit that an actor could execute. Step two: for each, locate the surviving check and verify its three parts (signal, procedure, threshold) against the source's CIR-9 record. Step three: run the falsifiability half of the probe — for each check, name at least one observation that would fail it; a check with no failing observation is vacuous and is scored as absent, because a gate that cannot refuse is indistinguishable from an inert one, which is SQI's own law and applies to compiled checks exactly as it applies to live ones. Step four: verify the check's reality-supremacy: the surviving check must be an observation of the world, not an assertion about it — a check that reads a compiler's own claim that the work is done is not a check, and evaluation fidelity rejects any surviving check whose signal is internal to the artifact. Thresholds: for an execution-carrier artifact, every actionable unit requires a complete, falsifiable, reality-anchored check -> otherwise REFUSE. For a reasoning-carrier artifact, check loss is a DEGRADE that marks the affected units as non-executable. Aggregate check-survival rates are recorded but never adjudicate: a 90% check-survival rate means ten percent of the artifact's actions cannot be verified, and those are the actions that will fail silently.

6.5 Provenance fidelity: the definition. Provenance fidelity asks whether the path from any claim in the artifact back to the source that grounded it can be reconstructed, and it is measured against CIR-10's provenance and chain-of-custody fields together with GK-04's lineage edges, which are the estate's sealed authority on lineage and which this dataset references rather than forks. The requirement is an unbroken chain: every unit in the compiled artifact carries a provenance pointer, every pointer resolves to a real unit at the stage below, and the chain terminates in a CIR-0 locator identifying the exact normalized span of the original source. Provenance fidelity is the property that this chain survives compilation intact, and it is tested by resolution rather than by inspection — a pointer that exists and does not resolve is worse than an absent one, because it presents as lineage and delivers none.

6.6 A claim whose source cannot be recovered becomes an article of faith. The reason provenance is a fidelity dimension and not a bookkeeping nicety is that reality moves. A claim that was true when it was compiled may be false now — the system it described was refactored, the constraint it recorded was lifted, the measurement it cited was superseded — and the only way the estate can ever discover this is by walking the chain back to the source and re-checking it against the world. That is precisely what DAIF-21's reality synchronization does, and it can do it only for claims whose provenance survived. A claim with a broken chain cannot be re-verified when reality changes; it can only be believed or discarded wholesale, and since it is embedded in a coherent, well-formed artifact alongside claims that are still true, it will be believed. The estate then holds, inside a system that is supposed to hold only evidence, an article of faith: a proposition it cannot check, cannot date, cannot attribute, and cannot correct, which will be defended with the confidence of its container. Provenance loss is therefore the mechanism by which a knowledge system slowly converts into a belief system, one unresolvable pointer at a time, and it is the loss most likely to be dismissed as harmless at the moment it occurs, because a broken pointer costs nothing today.

6.7 Test procedure, failure modes, adversarial cases, metrics. The provenance procedure is a resolution walk: for every unit in the artifact, follow the provenance chain down through the CIR stack to a CIR-0 locator, and verify at each hop that the target exists, that its content hash matches the hash recorded at compile time, and that the hop's lineage edge is present in GK-04. Three verdicts: RESOLVED, BROKEN (a hop does not resolve), and STALE (a hop resolves but the target's hash has changed since compilation, meaning the source moved under the artifact — a DAIF-21 trigger, not a compiler failure). Thresholds: any BROKEN chain on a unit of kind constraint, decision, evidence, or obligation -> REFUSE; BROKEN chains on other kinds -> DEGRADE with the affected units marked unverifiable. STALE chains are never a fidelity failure — they are the system working — and misclassifying them as failures would train the estate to suppress the very signal DAIF-21 depends on. The signature failure is provenance-by-attribution: an artifact whose units cite a source by name rather than by locator, which reads like lineage and cannot be walked, and whose only function is to make an unsourceable claim feel sourced. The adversarial case is the laundered chain: a compilation that generates fresh provenance pointers to itself, so every unit resolves — to the artifact, not to the source — producing a perfectly self-consistent lineage graph with zero connection to reality. The defense is that a chain is valid only if it terminates in a CIR-0 locator, and CIR-0 units are the only ones the fabric permits to have no parent; a chain that terminates anywhere else is BROKEN by definition regardless of how cleanly it resolves. Metrics: chain-resolution rate by unit kind (absolute counts, never a pooled percentage), CIR-0-termination rate, check-completeness per actionable unit, and falsifiable-check rate. The countermetric to chain-resolution is chain depth-and-distinctness — a compiler can drive resolution to one hundred percent by pointing every unit at the same single source span, and a lineage graph whose fan-in has collapsed to one node has preserved the form of provenance while destroying its information entirely.

PART VI FINAL LAW. Evaluation fidelity requires that every actionable unit retain a complete check — signal, procedure, and threshold — that is falsifiable and anchored in an observation of the world rather than in the artifact's own assertion, because an instruction whose correctness cannot be checked is not a plan being executed but a hypothesis being executed as one; provenance fidelity requires that every unit's chain of custody survive as resolvable pointers terminating in a CIR-0 locator, walked and hash-verified rather than inspected, because a claim whose source cannot be recovered can never be re-verified when reality moves and therefore becomes an article of faith held by a system that is permitted to hold only evidence — and a system that accumulates such claims has quietly converted itself from a knowledge system into a belief system, one unresolvable pointer at a time.

PART VII — BEHAVIORAL FIDELITY: THE DEFINITIVE TEST

7.1 Mission. Behavioral fidelity is the only dimension that tests what the estate actually cares about, and it subsumes the other nine. It asks one question: does an actor consuming the compiled artifact take the same correct decisions as an actor with access to the full source. Not similar decisions — the same correct ones, under the same conditions, with the same constraints honored, the same obligations discharged, and the same errors avoided. Every other dimension in this dataset exists because this question is expensive to ask and slow to answer, and the estate needed cheap screens that could reject a bad compilation before paying for a behavioral trial. But the screens are proxies and this is the criterion. A compilation that passes all nine proxies and fails behaviorally has failed, and the nine proxies were wrong about it; a compilation that fails a binary proxy is unsafe and does not reach a behavioral trial at all. Behavior is what the compiled artifact is for, and behavior is what it is judged on.

7.2 The definition, stated to be testable. Behavioral fidelity is a property of a pair — an artifact and a task class — and never of an artifact alone. The claim "this compilation is behaviorally faithful" is meaningless without the class of missions it is faithful for, because an artifact that fully supports a diagnostic mission may be catastrophically insufficient for an execution mission over the same subject matter. The testable form is therefore: for the mission class M, an actor equipped with artifact A produces outcomes indistinguishable-or-better than an actor equipped with source set S, across the measured dimensions, on a representative sample of missions drawn from M. Three commitments are embedded in that sentence and each is load-bearing. Indistinguishable-or-better, not merely acceptable — the standard is the source, not a floor. Across the measured dimensions, plural — a single quality score would hide the trade the compilation actually made. On a representative sample — because a compilation tuned on a mission it was shown will pass that mission and fail its siblings, and the sample must be drawn before the compilation is built, never after its weaknesses are known.

7.3 The trial: control arm and compiled arm. The test is a controlled comparison and it is run as one. Step one: select representative missions from the declared class — real missions the estate actually runs, not synthesized ones, because a synthesized mission is a hypothesis about what the estate does and inherits every bias of the person who wrote it. The sample must include at least one mission that exercises each load-bearing unit kind the artifact claims to carry: one constrained by a hard rule, one that must discharge an obligation, one that must revisit a decision, one that must distinguish a verified claim from an assumed one. Step two: run the CONTROL arm — an actor given the full source set S and the mission. Step three: run the COMPILED arm — an actor given the Mission Pack that DAIF-08 assembles from artifact A, and the same mission, with no access to S. The two arms must be otherwise identical: same model, same tooling, same permissions, same starting state. Step four: compare. The comparison is a vector, not a scalar, and its components are the behaviors the estate cares about: decision correctness on the mission's decision points, hard constraints violated, obligations dropped, errors raised, rework performed, tool calls issued, re-reads of source material attempted (a signal that the artifact was insufficient and the actor went looking for what it lacked), and final output quality adjudicated against the mission's own done-gate.

7.4 The acceptance rule and its asymmetries. A compilation ships only if it preserves or improves the relevant behavior, and the rule is applied per component with different disciplines. On the safety components — hard constraints violated, obligations dropped — the compiled arm must be exactly equal to or better than the control, and any regression, even one, is a REFUSE: an artifact that causes a single constraint violation the source would have prevented has produced the exact failure this dataset exists to prevent, and no efficiency gain purchases it. On the correctness components — decision correctness, final output quality — the compiled arm must be equal or better within a declared tolerance that the artifact states in advance and that the mission class governs. On the efficiency components — tool calls, re-reads, rework, latency — the compiled arm is expected to be better, and this is the compilation's entire economic justification; but efficiency is never permitted to compensate a safety or correctness regression, and the scoring model forbids the trade structurally by refusing to combine the components into a single number. A compiled arm that is forty percent faster and violates one constraint is not a net win; it is a REFUSE with a fast failure.

7.5 Composing FD-04's six-lens transfer test. The estate already owns a behavioral-equivalence instrument, and DAIF-03 composes it rather than forking it: FD-04's six-lens transfer test is the sealed procedure for asking whether a capability survives a transfer, and a compilation is a transfer — from a source substrate to a compiled one — in precisely the sense FD-04 already tests. Its six lenses are applied to the arms of the trial as the adjudication instrument, and its discipline of proving portability rather than asserting it is inherited whole. The composition is load-bearing rather than ornamental: FD-04 already knows that a capability which appears to survive a transfer often survives only in the cases the tester happened to try, and its remedy — the prover that probes rather than presumes — is exactly the discipline a behavioral trial requires, since a two-mission trial that passes proves that two missions passed and nothing more. Where DAIF-03 adds to FD-04 is the object of the transfer: FD-04 tests capability transfer across models, and DAIF-03 tests behavior preservation across representations, but the instrument, the proof discipline, and the refusal to accept an assertion in place of a probe are FD-04's and are referenced as such.

7.6 Why the other nine are proxies, and why they are kept. A compilation can pass all nine screening dimensions and fail behaviorally, and the mechanisms are three. Salience failure: every unit survives, and none of them is findable at the moment of need — a Mission Pack that carries a hard constraint on page nine of a dense artifact has preserved the constraint and lost the behavior, because the actor did not encounter it before acting. Composition failure: every unit survives individually and their interaction is destroyed — two constraints that jointly forbade an action are preserved as two constraints that each permit it, because the unit that stated their conjunction was mechanically redundant and was compressed away. Load failure: every unit survives and the artifact is so dense that the actor's attention is exhausted before the mission's decision points arrive, which is a compression that preserved everything and degraded behavior anyway. None of the nine screens sees any of these, because all three are properties of the artifact in use rather than of the artifact in isolation. The nine are kept regardless, and for a hard economic reason: a behavioral trial costs two full mission runs per mission in the sample, and the estate cannot afford to run one for every candidate compilation. The nine screens reject the great majority of bad compilations at a fraction of the cost, and every binary screen — constraint, obligation, epistemic laundering — rejects an unsafe compilation without a trial at all. The ladder is: nine cheap screens reject the unsafe and the obviously lossy -> the survivors face one expensive behavioral trial -> only a trial-passing compilation is trusted as a substitute for its source.

7.7 Metrics, countermetrics, adversarial cases, and the standing law. The primary metrics are the comparison vector's components, each reported as an absolute difference between arms, never pooled: delta-decision-correctness, delta-constraints-violated, delta-obligations-dropped, delta-errors, delta-rework, delta-tool-calls, delta-source-re-reads, delta-output-quality. The governing countermetric is the source re-read rate: an artifact whose actors constantly go back to the source has not failed the correctness comparison and has failed as a compilation, because it is not a substitute — it is an index — and reporting it alongside the quality delta prevents a compilation from claiming victory while its consumers quietly do the work of decompiling it. The second countermetric is mission-sample diversity, because the sharpest way to game a behavioral trial is to shrink its denominator: a compilation evaluated on three easy missions will pass, and the estate must therefore measure and publish the sample's coverage of load-bearing unit kinds alongside the pass rate. The adversarial case is the compilation built by an actor who has seen the trial's missions, which will pass them and fail everything else — the defense is that mission samples are drawn before the compilation is built and re-drawn for every re-compilation, and a compilation evaluated against a sample it was tuned on is scored UNMEASURED, not PASS. All of this is governed by CO-12's Telemetry-Before-Claims contract: no behavioral-fidelity claim exists without the trial record that produced it, no trial record exists without both arms, and an artifact that ships with a fidelity claim and no trial has made an assertion where the estate permits only evidence.

PART VII FINAL LAW. Behavioral fidelity is the definitive test and the only one that measures what the compiled artifact is for: an actor equipped with the compiled Mission Pack must take the same correct decisions, honor the same hard constraints, and discharge the same obligations as an actor equipped with the full source, proven by a controlled two-arm trial over a representative mission sample drawn before compilation, adjudicated through FD-04's six-lens transfer test, and accepted only when the compiled arm preserves or improves the relevant behavior — with any safety regression an automatic REFUSE that no efficiency gain may purchase; the other nine dimensions are instrumental proxies, cheap enough to screen every candidate and blind to salience, composition, and load failures, which is why a compilation may pass all nine and still be unfit, and why nothing is trusted as a substitute for its source until behavior, not structure, has been observed to survive.

PART VIII — THE LOSS BUDGET

8.1 Mission. Parts II through VII established what fidelity means and how each dimension is tested, and a reader who stopped there would draw the wrong conclusion: that every compilation must be lossless. It must not, and an estate that demanded losslessness of every artifact would have built a fabric it can never use, because a lossless compilation of a hundred thousand words is a hundred thousand words. Compression is the point. The error the estate actually commits is not compressing — it is compressing without declaring how much loss the consuming task can survive, and then discovering the answer by suffering it. The loss budget is the mechanism that turns that discovery into a declaration made in advance. It says, for a given artifact, exactly what class of loss was permitted, exactly what may therefore have been dropped, exactly what may never have been dropped whatever the class, and exactly what evidence is required before the artifact may be trusted at that class. It converts loss from an accident into a contract. And because a contract is only worth the enforcement behind it, this Part ends with the rule that makes the ladder load-bearing rather than decorative: the loss class is a property of the consuming TASK, never of the compiler's convenience, and the pairing of a high-loss artifact with a low-loss task must be refused by the machine and not merely discouraged by the doctrine.

8.2 The ladder. Five classes, ordered by permitted loss, and each one is a named contract rather than a vibe. L0 Lossless: nothing that carries meaning may be dropped; the compilation may reorganize, deduplicate exact repetitions, and normalize form, but every semantic unit, every qualifier, every hedge, every evidence pointer, and every locator survives with a resolvable chain. L1 Operationally lossless: the compilation may drop style, tone, conversational scaffolding, social framing, and exact phrasing, and it may never drop anything that changes behavior — every unit whose loss would alter what an actor does is preserved, and the compression is bought entirely from the surface. L2 Bounded loss: the compilation may omit low-impact detail — supporting examples beyond the first, redundant restatements, peripheral decision slots such as rejected options, background causal chains behind non-actionable conclusions — and every omission is RECORDED as an omission ledger entry with a locator, so the artifact knows what it does not contain. L3 Exploratory: maximum compression, used only for early discovery, where an actor is orienting rather than acting; large classes of detail may be dropped without individual records, and the artifact is marked non-actionable at the artifact level. L4 Disposable: ephemeral working context assembled for a single turn, never held beyond it, never written into the fabric, never cited as a source by any later compilation.

8.3 The floor that no class may cross. The ladder governs what may be dropped; it does not govern what may never be dropped, and the second list is identical at every rung including L3 and L4. No loss class permits the loss of a hard or firm constraint, per Part III's binary discipline. No loss class permits epistemic laundering, per Part IV — an L3 artifact may drop a claim entirely, but it may never carry the claim while promoting its status, because dropping is honest and promoting is a lie. No loss class permits the loss of an obligation, per Part V — an artifact compiled at L3 may omit an obligation's supporting narrative, and it may not omit the obligation, because the entry is the estate's only remaining knowledge that the work exists. No loss class permits fabrication of any kind, per Part II. No loss class permits a broken provenance chain on a unit of kind constraint, decision, evidence, or obligation, per Part VI. This is the crucial structural point of the ladder and the one most often mis-read: the classes are not a dial from "safe" to "unsafe". They are a dial from "much detail" to "little detail" ABOVE an invariant floor that is identical at every rung. An L3 artifact is not a dangerous artifact — it is a thin one, and it is thin on top of the same non-negotiable substrate an L0 artifact rests on. A compiler that reads L3 as permission to drop a hard rule has not compiled at L3; it has compiled at no class at all, and its output is not admissible at any rung.

8.4 What each class costs and what evidence it requires. The evidence burden rises with the trust the class claims, and it is stated so that no class can be asserted without paying for it. L0 requires the full Part II correspondence audit with zero inadmissible loss, zero fabrication, zero kind-drift, a complete constraint ledger, a complete obligation ledger, a resolution walk over every provenance chain, and — because L0 artifacts feed audits, binding contracts, and irreversible decisions — a behavioral trial per Part X on a mission sample covering every load-bearing unit kind the artifact carries. L1 requires the same ledgers and the same walk, and admits a graded semantic budget confined to units of kind assertion, definition, and preference, with the behavioral trial still required because L1 artifacts are execution carriers by construction. L2 requires the ledgers, the walk, and the omission ledger — every omission enumerated with a locator, a kind, and a one-line justification against the declared budget — and its behavioral trial may be run on a reduced sample provided the sample's coverage is published alongside the verdict. L3 requires the constraint ledger, the obligation ledger, and an artifact-level non-actionable marking; it requires no correspondence audit, because it makes no claim to correspond, and it is forbidden from making one. L4 requires nothing except that it not be written anywhere, because an artifact that is never held cannot be trusted later by anyone.

8.5 The cardinal binding rule. The loss class belongs to the task, not to the compiler. This sentence is the whole Part and it is worth stating in its violated form to see what it prevents: the compiler, facing a large source, a tight context budget, and a deadline, selects L3 because L3 is what will fit — and then hands the resulting artifact to an actor who is about to execute an irreversible migration. Every step of that sequence is locally reasonable, the artifact is well-formed, the actor has no way to know what the artifact does not contain, and the estate has just executed a critical operation on an orientation artifact. The rule inverts the order of determination. The consuming task declares its class FIRST, before any compilation begins: an audit, a binding contract, a legally or operationally binding decision, an irreversible migration, a production deploy, or an incident post-mortem is L0. An execution mission that will act without consulting the source is L1. A reasoning mission that may consult the source behind the artifact is L2. Early discovery, scoping, and orientation are L3. Single-turn working context is L4. The compiler is then handed the class as an input constraint and must either meet it or REFUSE — and if the source does not fit the context budget at the declared class, the correct output is a refusal plus a narrower scope, never a quieter class. The class is not what the compiler could achieve. It is what the task requires.

8.6 Mechanical refusal of the mismatch. Doctrine that depends on discipline is doctrine that fails on the day discipline is scarce, which is exactly the day it matters, so the pairing rule is enforced by the machine. Every compiled artifact carries a typed loss_class field, sealed by DAIF-01 at compile time and immutable thereafter — a compiler may not re-label its own output, and a consumer may not upgrade an artifact's class by asserting a better one. Every mission carries a required_class declared by its task type. The runtime, at the moment DAIF-08 assembles a Mission Pack, compares the two: an artifact whose loss_class is more lossy than the mission's required_class is INADMISSIBLE, and the runtime refuses the pairing, names the artifact, names the class gap, and falls back to the full source per Part XII's abstention contract. It does not warn. It does not proceed with a caveat, because a caveat is a message to an actor who is already committed, and the estate has recorded what happens to caveats under time pressure. The strongest form of the rule is the one worth memorizing: an L3 artifact may never be used to execute a critical migration, and the system must be structurally incapable of that pairing rather than merely disapproving of it.

8.7 Failure modes, adversarial cases, metrics. The signature failure is class inflation: a compiler labels an artifact L1 because L1 is what the consuming task wanted, without having paid L1's evidence burden, and the label is now a claim with no measurement behind it — a CO-12 violation, and the reason loss_class is admissible only when the class's required ledgers are attached. Its mirror is class deflation as an escape hatch: a compiler that cannot meet L1 relabels its output L3 and ships it anyway, having converted a refusal into a demotion, which is legitimate ONLY if the consuming task is genuinely an L3 task and is a disguised REFUSE otherwise. The adversarial case is budget laundering by kind re-typing, inherited from Part II: a unit that would be inadmissible to drop at L2 is re-typed from constraint to preference and becomes droppable, and the defense is unchanged — kinds are assigned at the source side by CIR-1 before the artifact exists, and a re-typing is an appeal that is logged and adjudicated against DAIF-01's Strength field. Metrics: declared-class distribution across the estate's live artifacts, omission-ledger completeness at L2 (absolute count of recorded omissions against an independently re-extracted count), class-mismatch refusal count, and evidence-burden satisfaction rate per class. The countermetric to compression achieved at a class is floor-violation count, which must be zero at every rung; a compiler whose compression improves while its floor violations rise is not compressing better, it is compressing into the substrate, and the two numbers moving in opposite directions is the alarm that no ratio alone would ever raise.

PART VIII FINAL LAW. Not every compilation must be lossless — the error is never compression itself but the application of the wrong loss class to the consuming task, so the estate declares a ladder of five contracts: L0 lossless for audits, binding contracts, and irreversible decisions; L1 operationally lossless, which may drop style and may never drop behavior; L2 bounded loss, whose every omission is recorded in an omission ledger with a locator; L3 exploratory, permitted only for orientation and marked non-actionable; and L4 disposable, never held and never cited — and above an invariant floor identical at every rung, since no class at any depth may drop a hard constraint, drop an obligation, launder an epistemic status, fabricate a unit, or break a load-bearing provenance chain; the loss class is a property of the TASK and never of the compiler's convenience, it is declared before compilation begins and sealed immutably into the artifact, and the pairing of a lossier artifact with a stricter mission is refused by the machine rather than discouraged by the doctrine, because an L3 artifact executing a critical migration is a failure the system must be structurally incapable of, not merely disapproving of.

PART IX — HARMFUL OMISSION VERSUS ACCEPTABLE LOSS

9.1 Mission. The loss budget of Part VIII declares how much loss a task can survive; it does not tell the compiler which particular loss is the survivable kind. That is this Part's job, and it is the sharpest discrimination in the dataset, because the two categories are indistinguishable by every property a compressor can perceive. Acceptable loss and harmful omission have the same shape at the point of the cut: both remove text, both make the artifact shorter, both leave behind something coherent, and neither raises an error. They differ in exactly one place — downstream, in the behavior of an actor who consumes the artifact — and that place is invisible from inside the compressor. This Part defines the discriminator, explains why it cannot be a size or a surface property, and specifies the metric that measures the failure and the countermetric that keeps the metric honest.

9.2 The two definitions, stated so they can be told apart. Acceptable loss is the removal of content whose absence does not change what any actor consuming the artifact would do. Its permitted classes are enumerable rather than gestural: ornament, which is language that carries tone and no assertion; exact redundancy, which is the second and subsequent statements of an assertion already carried atomically; restatement, which is the same claim in different words with the same scope, strength, and qualifiers; illustration beyond the first, which is the third example of a pattern two examples already established; and conversational scaffolding, which is the social machinery of a dialogue — the greetings, the acknowledgements, the false starts — that carried the source's cognition and none of its content. Harmful omission is the removal of content whose absence changes what an actor would do, and its classes are equally enumerable: a constraint, an obligation, an exception, a qualifier, a justification, a piece of evidence, a scope boundary, a closure condition, a revision condition, a causal mechanism behind an actionable conclusion, or an epistemic status. The lists are short, they are typed, and they are drawn directly from the DAIF-01 kind register — which is the first hint at the mechanism, because a discriminator that operates over typed kinds does not need to read the prose it is cutting.

9.3 Why the compressor cannot see the difference. The compressor perceives tokens; harm is a property of consequences. That single sentence is the whole difficulty, and it explains every heuristic that has ever failed at this discrimination. Size fails: the shortest unit in a source is routinely the most load-bearing, because a hard rule is a sentence and its supporting discussion is a page, so a compressor that drops small things to preserve big things has inverted the priority exactly. Frequency fails: a constraint stated once is not less binding than an assertion repeated six times, and a frequency-weighted compressor will preserve the chorus and drop the law. Centrality fails: a qualifier is by construction peripheral to the sentence it modifies — the sentence is about X, the qualifier merely says when X — and any centrality measure computed over the source's own structure will rank the qualifier last and the claim first, which is the erased-qualifier trap from Part II expressed as a scoring function. Fluency fails, and fails worst, because removing a hedge, an exception, or a condition IMPROVES fluency by every measure a language model has, so a compressor optimizing readability is a compressor pointed directly at the harmful-omission list. Every surface heuristic available to a compressor is therefore either orthogonal to harm or positively correlated with it, and this is not a defect of any particular compressor — it is a structural consequence of the fact that harm lives downstream of the cut and the compressor lives at it.

9.4 The discriminator: judge the omission by its behavioral effect. The mechanism follows from the diagnosis. If harm is a property of consequences, then omissions must be judged by consequence, and the estate already owns the instrument: Part VII's behavioral test. The discriminator is a two-question probe applied per omission, and it is deliberately mechanical. Question one, the type gate: is the omitted unit's DAIF-01 kind on the harmful list — constraint, exception, obligation, closure condition, evidence, justification, revision condition, scope boundary, epistemic status, or actionable mechanism. If yes, the omission is harmful by type, immediately, with no further inquiry, because these kinds are on the list precisely because the estate has already paid to learn that their loss changes behavior, and re-litigating that per artifact is how the lesson gets un-learned. Question two, the behavioral probe, applied to everything the type gate did not catch: construct at least one mission from the artifact's declared class in which the omitted unit would have been consulted, and run the two-arm comparison of Part X restricted to that unit — control arm with the unit present, compiled arm with it absent. If the arms diverge on any safety or correctness component, the omission is harmful regardless of the unit's kind, its size, or its apparent triviality. If the arms are identical across a sample of such missions, the omission is acceptable, and the evidence is the trial record, not the compiler's judgment. The type gate is cheap and catches the great majority; the behavioral probe is expensive and is the authority.

9.5 Why the type gate is not sufficient alone, and why the probe is not sufficient alone. Each half fails without the other, and the dataset keeps both for reasons that are symmetric. The type gate alone fails on the untyped harm: a unit correctly typed as an assertion whose absence nonetheless changes behavior because three later units silently depended on it — the structurally-carried qualifier of Part II, which is atomic by CIR-1's discipline and still load-bearing by composition. The type gate sees an assertion, permits the drop, and the composition breaks. Only a behavioral probe catches that. The behavioral probe alone fails on the rare-path harm: a constraint that governs a boundary the sample's missions never approach will produce identical arms, the probe will pronounce the omission acceptable, and the estate will have deleted a hard rule on the evidence that nobody happened to hit it during the trial — which is the "gate never observed to refuse" shape the estate has recorded under its own names, and it is fatal here. Only the type gate catches that, and it catches it by refusing to ask the question at all: a constraint is harmful to omit by type, and no trial result may overturn that, because a trial that fails to reach a boundary has measured its own sample and not the boundary. The composition is therefore a conjunction with an asymmetry -> the type gate can convict without the probe, the probe can convict without the type gate, and neither may acquit alone.

9.6 The metric: Harmful Omission Rate. HOR is defined as the absolute count of omissions convicted as harmful — by type gate or by behavioral probe — per compiled artifact, and it is reported as a count and never as a rate against total omissions, for the reason Part II already sealed: a ratio is satisfied by inflating its denominator, and a compiler that drops a thousand ornamental units alongside one constraint achieves a superb harmful-omission percentage while having destroyed the artifact. The source of truth is the omission ledger that Part VIII's L2 contract already requires plus the type-gate and probe verdicts attached to each entry, which means HOR is not a new instrument but a reading of an existing record, and an artifact with no omission ledger has an UNKNOWN HOR and never a zero — per Part XIV's prime rule and CO-12's Telemetry-Before-Claims contract, an unmeasured metric is not a passing one. HOR's expected range is exactly zero at L0 and L1, zero at L2 and L3 by the invariant floor of Part VIII, and any non-zero HOR at any class is a REFUSE, because the harmful list and the floor list are the same list. HOR is therefore not a quality dial the estate tunes downward over time; it is a binary that is reported as a count only so that the artifact can name WHICH omissions convicted it.

9.7 The countermetric, the adversarial cases, and the failure modes. HOR's gaming vector is obvious the moment it becomes a target: a compiler drives HOR to zero by recording fewer omissions, not by committing fewer, and since HOR is read from the omission ledger, a compiler that simply fails to register a cut has produced a perfect score by producing no evidence. The countermetric is omission-ledger completeness, computed by independently re-running the Part II correspondence audit against the artifact and comparing the count of non-matched source units against the count of entries in the omission ledger. A divergence means the artifact dropped units it did not record, and the divergence — not the HOR — is the alarm. The second countermetric is type-gate coverage: the fraction of the source's load-bearing unit kinds that the omission ledger's type gate actually evaluated, because a type gate that never sees a constraint can never convict one, which is the same "register that omits what it was going to lose" pathology Parts III and V both name. The adversarial case is the reclassified qualifier: a compiler that re-types a scope-bearing unit from constraint to assertion, passes the type gate, passes a probe whose missions never approach the boundary, and lands a harmful omission with two clean verdicts behind it — and the defense, as everywhere in this dataset, is that kinds are assigned at the source side before the artifact exists and a re-typing is an appeal, logged and adjudicated, never a compiler's unilateral act. The signature failure mode, finally, is judging omission by size, which is the intuition every reader arrives with and the one this Part exists to destroy: the smallest cut in the source is routinely the most expensive one in the estate.

PART IX FINAL LAW. Acceptable loss removes what does not change behavior — ornament, exact redundancy, restatement, illustration past the first, conversational scaffolding — while harmful omission removes what does — a constraint, an obligation, an exception, a qualifier, a justification, a piece of evidence, a scope boundary, a closure or revision condition, a mechanism, or an epistemic status — and the two are indistinguishable from inside the compressor because the compressor perceives tokens while harm is a property of consequences, which is why every surface heuristic available to it is either orthogonal to harm or, in the case of fluency, pointed directly at it; the discriminator is therefore behavioral, applied as a conjunction of a cheap type gate that convicts by DAIF-01 kind and an expensive behavioral probe that convicts by observed divergence between arms, where either half may convict alone and neither may acquit alone, since a trial that never approaches a boundary has measured its sample and not the boundary; Harmful Omission Rate is an absolute count read from the omission ledger with a target of zero at every loss class, and it is kept honest by omission-ledger completeness, because a compiler drives HOR to zero fastest by recording fewer cuts rather than committing fewer.

PART X — BEHAVIORAL EQUIVALENCE TESTING: THE PROCEDURE

10.1 Mission. Part VII named behavioral fidelity the definitive test and sketched its two arms. This Part is the method, written at the level of detail that lets a different actor run the same trial and get a comparable answer, because a test whose procedure is folklore produces verdicts that are folklore. Behavioral equivalence testing is the estate's only instrument that measures what a compiled artifact is actually for, and it is expensive — two full mission executions per mission in the sample — which means every design decision in the procedure is a decision about where to spend a scarce budget without buying a comfortable answer. The three things this Part must get right are the sample, the arms, and the acceptance rule, and the sample is the one that determines whether the whole gate is a measurement or a ceremony.

10.2 Mission-sample selection, and why a biased sample makes the entire gate theater. The sample is three strata and all three are mandatory. Representative missions are drawn from the estate's actual mission history for the artifact's declared class — real missions the estate has run, sampled without regard to whether the artifact is likely to do well on them, because the moment the sampler can see the artifact, the sample becomes a hypothesis about where the artifact is strong. Adversarial missions are constructed to reach the artifact's known weak points: one mission per load-bearing unit kind the artifact carries, engineered so that the mission CANNOT be completed correctly without consulting that kind — a mission that must violate a hard constraint unless the constraint is surfaced, a mission that fails unless an obligation is discharged, a mission whose correct answer changes depending on whether a claim is verified or assumed. Regression missions are the permanent set drawn from Part XI's golden benchmarks plus every mission on which any prior compilation of this source failed, retained forever, because a fidelity failure that is fixed and not re-tested is a fidelity failure that will return. The prohibition that makes all three meaningful: the sample is drawn BEFORE the compilation is built, sealed, and hashed, and a compilation evaluated against a sample drawn or amended after its weaknesses were known is scored UNMEASURED, never PASS. A biased sample does not merely weaken the gate — it inverts it, converting the most expensive instrument in the dataset into an elaborate mechanism for confirming what the compiler already believed, and the estate will pay full price for a verdict worth nothing.

10.3 The two arms and their construction. The control arm is an actor equipped with the full source set S — the raw, uncompiled cognitive source, in whatever form the estate actually holds it — and the mission. The compiled arm is an actor equipped with the Mission Pack that DAIF-08 assembles from artifact A for that mission, and the same mission, with no access to S and no tool by which S could be reached, because an arm that can silently reach the source is not testing the artifact, it is testing the source with extra steps. Everything else must be identical and the identity must be enforced rather than assumed: same model and same model version, same tooling and same permissions, same starting repository state, same time budget, same temperature or sampling regime, same system context except for the packet under test. Each divergence between arms that is NOT the artifact is a confound that will be read as a fidelity effect, and the most common confound in practice is model drift — the control arm run on Monday against a model that is not the model the compiled arm meets on Thursday. The arms are run in the same window, on the same model build, and the build identifier is recorded in the trial record; a trial whose arms crossed a model version boundary is INVALID and is re-run, not adjusted.

10.4 Blinding. The adjudicator must not know which arm produced which output. This is not an ornament imported from a foreign discipline; it is a defense against a specific, observed failure in which the actor who built a compilation adjudicates its trial and reads its outputs generously, because a human or a model that knows which artifact it is hoping will win will find reasons. Blinding is implemented at three points. The outputs of both arms are stripped of arm identifiers and any artifact-shaped tells before adjudication. The adjudicator is a distinct actor from the compiler, and where the same model serves both, it serves the adjudication role in a fresh context with no access to the compilation's own reasoning. The adjudication rubric is written and fixed BEFORE the trial runs, derived from the mission's own done-gate rather than composed after the outputs are visible — because a rubric written after the outputs exist is a rubric written to fit them. Where full blinding is impossible — a mission whose output structurally reveals its arm — the trial is marked partially-blinded and its verdict is admissible only at DEGRADE, never at PASS, and the estate records the fact rather than hiding it.

10.5 The measured outcomes. The comparison is a vector and it is reported as a vector, because a scalar quality score is precisely the instrument that hides the trade a compilation made. Nine components, grouped by discipline. Safety: hard constraints violated, and obligations dropped. Correctness: decision correctness at the mission's decision points, adjudicated against the mission's own done-gate; and final output quality, likewise. Diagnostic: errors raised, rework performed, tool calls issued, and source re-reads attempted — the last being the sharpest single signal in the whole procedure, since an actor who goes looking for what the artifact lacks has told the estate exactly where the artifact is thin, and it has done so without any adjudication at all. Economic: latency and reasoning cost. Each component is recorded as an absolute value per arm and reported as a delta, never pooled and never weighted into a composite, because a weighted composite is the mechanism by which a safety regression is purchased with an efficiency gain, and the scoring model refuses that trade structurally rather than forbidding it in prose.

10.6 Statistical discipline, and the acceptance rule. A single passing mission proves that a single mission passed. This is stated flatly because the temptation to over-read a small trial is the strongest in the dataset — the trial is expensive, the result is encouraging, and the artifact is needed — and the estate has recorded what happens when a capability that "appeared to survive a transfer" survived only in the cases the tester happened to try, which is the exact discipline FD-04's six-lens transfer test was sealed to enforce and which DAIF-03 composes rather than re-derives. The rules are three. Minimum sample: no artifact is adjudicated on fewer missions than it has load-bearing unit kinds, because a kind with no mission is a kind with no evidence. Variance reporting: every component's delta is reported with the spread across missions, and a component whose mean is favorable and whose spread crosses zero has NOT demonstrated preservation — it has demonstrated noise. Per-mission safety: safety components are adjudicated per mission and never in aggregate; a mean of zero constraint violations across ten missions, one of which violated two, is a REFUSE, because the aggregate is a statement about the sample and the violation is a statement about the artifact. The acceptance rule is preserve-or-improve, applied per component with the asymmetry Part VII sealed -> safety must be exactly equal or better with any regression an automatic REFUSE, correctness must be equal or better within a tolerance declared in advance, efficiency is expected to improve and may never compensate a regression elsewhere.

10.7 Disagreement, and the compiled arm that wins. When the arms disagree, the disagreement is a finding and not a nuisance, and it is triaged before any verdict is issued. The first question is always whether the divergence is a fidelity effect or a confound: re-run the diverging mission with the arms swapped across actor instances, and if the divergence follows the artifact it is real, while if it follows the instance it was noise and the trial's power is lower than believed. A real divergence is then localized: the diverging behavior is traced to the decision point at which the arms parted, the unit the control arm consulted at that point is identified, and that unit is checked against the artifact — and in nearly every observed case the answer is already sitting in the omission ledger, which converts a behavioral failure into a named, located, typed harmful omission per Part IX. The harder case is the compiled arm that BEATS the control, and the doctrine here is counterintuitive and load-bearing: a compiled arm that outperforms the source is a signal to investigate, never a victory to celebrate. Three explanations are possible and only one of them is good. The benign one is genuine salience — the compilation surfaced a constraint the source buried on page ninety, and the actor honored what the source's own reader would have missed, which is compilation working as designed and is worth recording. The dangerous ones are that the artifact made the mission EASIER than reality by dropping a complication the world will not drop — the compiled arm succeeded because it never learned about the exception that would have made the mission hard — or that the adjudication rubric was itself derived from the compilation's frame and is scoring the artifact against its own summary. A compiled arm that beats the control is therefore held at DEGRADE pending a root-cause finding, and it may be promoted to PASS only when the outperformance is attributed to salience and the attribution is recorded. The estate does not accept good news it cannot explain, because an unexplained win is an unmeasured mechanism, and an unmeasured mechanism is the one that will reverse without warning.

PART X FINAL LAW. Behavioral equivalence is proven by a controlled two-arm trial and never asserted: a sealed mission sample drawn BEFORE the compilation exists and spanning representative, adversarial, and permanent regression strata — since a sample the compiler could see converts the estate's most expensive instrument into a ceremony that confirms what the compiler already believed — run as a control arm on the full source against a compiled arm on the DAIF-08 Mission Pack with every variable but the artifact held identical and the model build recorded, adjudicated blind against a rubric fixed in advance by an actor who is not the compiler, and scored as a nine-component vector that is never pooled, because a composite is the mechanism by which a safety regression is bought with an efficiency gain; acceptance is preserve-or-improve with safety adjudicated per mission and any regression an automatic REFUSE, a single passing mission proves only that a single mission passed, and a compiled arm that beats the control is held at DEGRADE until the outperformance is attributed — genuine salience is a win, a mission made artificially easy by a dropped complication is a fidelity failure wearing a victory's face, and the estate does not accept good news it cannot explain.

PART XI — GOLDEN COGNITIVE BENCHMARKS

11.1 Mission. Part X's mission sample is drawn per artifact and per class; it answers whether THIS compilation preserved THIS behavior. It cannot answer whether the compiler itself has a systematic blind spot, because a per-artifact sample changes with every artifact and a moving instrument cannot detect a constant defect. The golden cognitive benchmarks are the fixed instrument: a permanent, versioned suite of twelve cases, each one the distilled form of a failure the estate has actually suffered, each with a precisely specified input, a precisely specified correct behavior, and a named failure it catches. Every compilation clears the suite or it does not ship. The suite does not replace the per-artifact trial — it is the regression stratum Part X already requires, elevated to a first-class artifact with its own governance — and its purpose is to make a class of failure permanently unrepeatable rather than merely currently absent.

11.2 The recall and reasoning cases. Case one, decision recall: the input is a mission that cannot be completed correctly without knowing a decision taken months earlier and the reason it was taken; the correct behavior is that the actor recalls the choice AND its justification AND the condition under which it would be reopened; the failure it catches is the bare conclusion of Part IV — the decision compressed to its outcome, un-revisable, obeyed as superstition or reversed blindly. Case two, the why-probe: the input asks directly why a decision was taken, with no hint in the prompt; the correct behavior is a justification with its evidence pointers resolving to real units; the failure it catches is evidence-pointer decay, in which the prose of a justification survives while its grounding does not, and which is invisible to any test that reads the justification and stops. Case three, contradiction detection: the input contains two units that cannot both be honored; the correct behavior is that the actor DETECTS the conflict and surfaces it rather than silently picking one; the failure it catches is the collapsed distinction and the smoothed compilation, since a compressor that harmonizes its source produces an artifact in which contradictions have been resolved by erasure and the actor never learns there was a choice.

11.3 The boundary and isolation cases. Case four, scope isolation: the input is a mission in one project whose context has been assembled from an estate holding several; the correct behavior is that no unit from a sibling project enters the reasoning, and specifically that a constraint binding project A is not applied to project B nor a decision from B cited in A; the failure it catches is cross-context bleed, which is the highest-frequency compilation failure the estate records and the one whose symptom — confident, coherent, wrong-project reasoning — is the hardest to notice from inside. Case five, hard-constraint honoring: the input is a mission whose most efficient completion path crosses a hard constraint carried in the source; the correct behavior is that the actor detects the constraint and refuses the efficient path; the failure it catches is constraint loss, and the case is designed so that the efficient path is genuinely attractive, because a constraint test whose forbidden path is unappealing tests nothing. Case six, obsolete-knowledge suppression: the input is a mission whose subject matter is governed by a decision that was superseded, with both the superseded and the superseding decision present in the source; the correct behavior is that the actor acts on the current decision and does not revive the dead one; the failure it catches is the broken supersession pointer of Part IV, in which a lineage severed by compilation resurfaces a retired rule as live.

11.4 The continuity and execution cases. Case seven, unfinished-task continuation: the input is a mission that resumes work left incomplete in a prior session, with no restatement of what was done; the correct behavior is that the actor identifies the exact stopping point, the work already sealed, and the next concrete action; the failure it catches is obligation loss compounded by state loss — the single most costly failure in the estate's session economy, and the one the RESUMPTION_FILE pattern exists to prevent. Case eight, pending-work identification: the input asks what work is outstanding, with no hint about how much; the correct behavior is that the actor enumerates the obligation register completely, with closure conditions; the failure it catches is the silent obligation drop of Part V, and it is the only case in the suite that can convict an artifact on a count alone — a register that returns fewer entries than the source's independently-extracted register has failed before any reasoning is examined. Case nine, procedure execution: the input requires running a multi-step procedure the source recorded; the correct behavior is that every step executes in order with its preconditions honored and its done-gate observed; the failure it catches is evaluation-fidelity loss from Part VI, in which the steps survive and the checks do not, and the procedure is executed as a hypothesis in a plan's costume.

11.5 The evidence, adaptation, and reset cases. Case ten, evidence recovery: the input asks for the evidence behind a claim the artifact carries; the correct behavior is that the actor walks the provenance chain to a CIR-0 locator and produces the original span; the failure it catches is provenance-by-attribution and the laundered chain of Part VI — pointers that resolve to the artifact rather than to the source, producing a perfectly self-consistent lineage graph with no connection to reality. Case eleven, model adaptation: the same mission and the same source are compiled for a different model with a different context budget and different attention characteristics; the correct behavior is that the compiled artifact provokes the same correct behavior on the new substrate; the failure it catches is a compilation tuned to one model's quirks, and this case is the one where FD-04's six-lens transfer test is applied directly and without modification, since transfer across substrates is exactly what FD-04 was sealed to prove rather than assume. Case twelve, post-reset correctness: the actor's context is cleared entirely and the mission is issued fresh with only the compiled artifact; the correct behavior is that the answer is identical to the answer given with warm context; the failure it catches is the compilation that was never actually load-bearing — an artifact that appeared to work only because the actor's live context was silently carrying the knowledge, which is the most flattering illusion in the whole procedure and the one that survives every trial run without a reset.

11.6 Benchmark governance: versioning, contamination, refresh. A benchmark suite is an asset that decays, and it decays in three specific ways that governance must address by name. Versioning: the suite is a sealed, hashed artifact with a version identifier, and every trial record cites the version it ran against; a comparison of results across versions is invalid unless the delta between versions is itself recorded, because a suite that quietly changed is a suite whose pass-rate trend means nothing. Contamination: a benchmark that has leaked into the compiler's input is DEAD, permanently, and this is the harshest rule in the Part. If a compiler has seen a benchmark's mission, its expected behavior, or its adjudication rubric — whether through the source it compiled, through a prior trial record left in context, or through a well-meaning actor who included the suite in a prompt — that benchmark measures nothing except memorization, and no amount of care recovers it. Contaminated cases are retired and replaced, never repaired, and the suite therefore holds a reserve of unrevealed cases per failure class so that a retirement does not open a hole. Refresh: every case is re-derived from the estate's live failure record on a fixed cadence, and a case whose failure class has not recurred in the estate for a long interval is NOT retired — it is kept, because the absence of a failure under an active gate is evidence the gate is working, and retiring the gate that produced the silence is how the estate re-learns a lesson it already paid for.

11.7 Failure modes, adversarial cases, metrics. The signature failure of a benchmark suite is the suite that always passes, which reads as excellence and is indistinguishable from an inert gate: a suite with a hundred-percent pass rate over many compilations has either proven the compiler is perfect or proven the suite cannot refuse, and the estate's own recorded history says which of those is more likely. The countermeasure is a mandatory negative control — a deliberately damaged compilation, injected on a schedule, from which a known load-bearing unit has been surgically removed — and the suite MUST fail it. A suite that passes a compilation known to be missing a hard constraint is a broken instrument and its verdicts are void until it is repaired, which is SQI's reality-supremacy applied to the estate's own gates: a gate never observed to refuse has not been observed to work. The adversarial case is benchmark-shaped compilation: a compiler that learns the twelve cases and preserves exactly the units they probe while dropping everything else, achieving a perfect suite score on an artifact that is hollow between the test points. The defenses are two and both are structural -> the suite is only the regression stratum of Part X's sample and can never be the whole of it, so a benchmark-shaped compilation still faces representative and adversarial missions it has not seen; and the reserve of unrevealed cases means the compiler cannot know which probes it will meet. Metrics: per-case pass verdicts (never pooled into a single score, because a suite average lets a failure on the constraint case be offset by a pass on the ornament case), negative-control refusal rate with a target of one hundred percent, contamination-retirement count, and case age. The countermetric to suite pass rate is negative-control refusal rate, and the two must be read together in every report: a rising pass rate with a falling refusal rate is not a compiler improving, it is a gate dying.

PART XI FINAL LAW. The golden cognitive benchmarks are the fixed instrument a moving per-artifact sample cannot be — twelve permanent cases, each the distilled form of a failure the estate has actually suffered: recall a decision with its justification, isolate scope across projects, detect and honor a hard constraint, continue an unfinished task, explain why a decision was taken, execute a procedure with its checks intact, detect a contradiction rather than smoothing it away, recover evidence to a CIR-0 locator, adapt to a different model through FD-04's transfer test, answer correctly after a full context reset, refuse to revive a superseded decision, and enumerate pending work completely — each with a specified input, a specified correct behavior, and a named failure it catches; the suite is versioned and hashed with every trial citing its version, a case the compiler has seen is DEAD and is retired rather than repaired since it now measures memorization, cases are never retired for the absence of the failure they prevent because that silence is the evidence the gate works, and a suite that has never been observed to refuse is not excellent but inert — which is why a deliberately damaged compilation is injected on a schedule and the suite MUST fail it, and why the pass rate is never reported without the negative-control refusal rate beside it.

PART XII — FAIL-VISIBLE ABSTENTION: THE DUTY TO REFUSE

12.1 Mission. This is the most important behavior in the dataset, and it is a behavior rather than a measurement. Everything in Parts II through XI produces a verdict; this Part governs what the compiler DOES with a verdict it does not like. The claim is stark and the estate should read it as such: a compiler that cannot guarantee fidelity and ships anyway has done something worse than a compiler that fails outright, because the second one produces a visible absence and the first produces an invisible falsehood wearing the estate's own imprimatur. The duty to refuse is the mechanism that makes every other gate in this dataset real, since a gate whose failing verdict can be overridden by the thing it is gating is not a gate — it is a suggestion with a scoring rubric.

12.2 The three verdicts and their triggers. PASS: the compilation has met every binary dimension at zero violations, met every graded dimension's threshold for its declared loss class, produced its constraint ledger, its obligation ledger, its omission ledger, its provenance resolution walk, and its behavioral trial record, and the trial's acceptance rule was satisfied on every component with safety adjudicated per mission. PASS means the artifact may be used in place of its source for missions of its declared class, and it means nothing beyond that class. DEGRADE: the compilation is faithful for some task classes and not others, and the artifact ships with an explicit, machine-readable record of exactly which classes it may not serve and exactly which dimension's failure disqualified it. The triggers are enumerable: intent surfaced-nowhere in the source, producing UNMEASURED intent fidelity -> instruction-safe and purpose-unsafe; a lost revision condition or expiry -> not-reality-synchronizable; a broken provenance chain on a non-load-bearing kind -> those units unverifiable; check loss on a reasoning-carrier -> the affected units non-executable; a partially-blinded trial -> admissible below PASS; a compiled arm that beat its control without an attributed cause -> held pending root cause. REFUSE: any binary violation, any harmful omission, any unmeasured dimension, any missing ledger, any class-mismatch, any invalid trial. REFUSE means the compilation does not ship at all.

12.3 The cardinal sin is silent degradation, and the reason is trust. An obviously broken artifact is a cheap failure. It is caught immediately, by anyone, at the first attempt to use it, and its cost is the time to notice. A confidently-formatted, internally-coherent, quietly-lossy artifact is the expensive failure, and the mechanism is precisely its quality. It is well-structured, so it passes inspection. It is fluent, so it reads as authoritative. It carries typed fields and resolvable-looking pointers, so it presents as evidence. Every property that makes it a good compilation makes its one missing constraint more dangerous, because the actor consuming it has no reason to doubt it and every structural cue telling it not to. The estate has recorded this shape under many names — the scaffold that looks complete, the gate never observed to refuse, the claim whose evidence was never collected — and they are all the same shape: a system whose confidence outran its evidence and whose form concealed the gap. A compiler that silently degrades does not merely produce a bad artifact; it produces a bad artifact that the estate will DEFEND, because the estate trusts its own compiler, and the trust is the payload. This is why the failure mode is not "a lossy compilation" but "a trusted lossy compilation", and why the correct response to an unprovable fidelity is not a caveat but a refusal.

12.4 The abstention contract. On REFUSE, the runtime does not proceed on a degraded compilation with a warning attached, because a warning is a message delivered to an actor who is already committed and under time pressure, and the estate has recorded what happens to warnings under those conditions. The contract has four clauses and they are ordered. Clause one: the runtime falls back to the full source. The mission proceeds — expensively, slowly, with the actor paying the full context cost of the raw material — because a slow correct mission is strictly better than a fast wrong one, and the entire economic case for compilation is void the moment the compilation cannot be vouched for. Clause two: if the full source will not fit the actor's budget, the runtime falls back to a NARROWER compilation over a smaller scope — a compilation whose reduced ambit lets it meet its class honestly — and never to a wider compilation at a quieter class, which is Part VIII's class-deflation escape hatch and is forbidden by name. Clause three: if neither fallback is available, the runtime REFUSES THE MISSION and surfaces the refusal to the Owner with the dimension that failed, the unit that was lost, and the locator that would recover it. A mission that cannot be run faithfully is not run. Clause four: every refusal is recorded, because a refusal is the highest-value telemetry the fabric produces — it is the estate's only direct observation of the boundary of its own compiler's competence, and a refusal that is not recorded is a lesson paid for and thrown away.

12.5 Refusal must be cheaper than a false PASS. A duty that costs more to honor than to violate is a duty that will be violated, quietly, by tired actors under deadline, and no amount of doctrine survives that arithmetic. The dataset therefore engineers the incentive rather than exhorting against it, and the engineering has three parts. First, every threshold in this dataset is set so that the ABSENCE of evidence produces REFUSE and never PASS — an unmeasured dimension is a failed dimension — which means the lazy path leads to refusal rather than to approval, and a compiler that does nothing has refused rather than shipped. Second, a REFUSE is emitted with a machine-generated report naming the failing dimension, the failing unit, and its locator, which costs the compiler nothing to produce because the ledgers that convict it are the same ledgers it was already required to build; whereas a false PASS requires the compiler to manufacture a ledger it does not have, which is a fabrication and is itself convicted by Part II. Third, the REFUSE path is the DEFAULT path in the runtime, and PASS is the exception that requires positive evidence — so the compiler must do work to ship and no work to abstain. The asymmetry is deliberate and it is the reason the duty is a mechanism rather than a virtue.

12.6 Composing SQI's reality-supremacy: absence of a failure signal is UNVERIFIED, never PROVEN. This is the epistemic law behind the whole Part, and it is inherited from SQI rather than re-derived here. A compilation that ran no correspondence audit did not pass the correspondence audit; it did not take it. A compilation whose constraint ledger is empty did not preserve every constraint; it registered none. A behavioral trial that raised no constraint violation across three easy missions did not demonstrate constraint preservation; it demonstrated that three easy missions do not approach constraints. In every one of these cases the observable signal is identical — no failure reported — and the correct reading is UNVERIFIED, which routes to REFUSE, and the incorrect reading is PROVEN, which routes to PASS. The distinction between those two readings of the same silence is the entire difference between a fidelity gate and a rubber stamp, and it is the reason this dataset makes an unmeasured dimension a failed one at every threshold rather than at some of them. The corollary is uncomfortable and is stated so that it cannot be argued away later: a compiler that has never been observed to REFUSE is not a compiler with a perfect record. It is a compiler whose gate has not been observed to work, and it must be tested with a deliberately damaged input — the negative control of Part XI — before any of its PASSes are admissible at all.

12.7 Failure modes, adversarial cases, metrics. The signature failure is the caveat-as-abstention: a compiler that ships a degraded artifact with a prominent note describing its limitations and believes it has discharged the duty. It has not, and the reason is behavioral rather than moral — the note is read once, by the actor who assembles the packet, and never by the actor who is three hours into a mission and reaching for the constraint that is not there. An artifact's limitations must be enforced by the runtime's admissibility check, per Part VIII, and never by the consumer's memory of a warning. The second failure is refusal fatigue: a gate that refuses so often that actors route around it, which is a real failure of the gate and not of the actors, and its diagnosis is a threshold set on an unmeasurable dimension rather than an unfaithful compiler — the fix is to make the dimension measurable, never to lower the threshold. The third is the override that becomes routine: an Owner-authorized bypass, correctly designed as a one-turn exception, that is invoked so frequently it becomes the path. The adversarial case is the compiler that games UNMEASURED into PASS by producing an empty ledger and reporting zero violations against it, which is precisely the "register that omits what it was going to lose" pathology, and the defense is the source-side register-completeness countermetric that Parts III, V, and IX all require independently. Metrics: refusal count by dimension, refusal-to-fallback success rate, silent-degradation incidents discovered after the fact (target zero, and every one of them a UKDL promotion candidate), and override-invocation frequency. The countermetric to PASS rate is negative-control refusal rate, for the reason Part XI already sealed: a rising PASS rate alongside a falling refusal rate is not a compiler getting better, it is a gate going quiet, and the estate has learned to read quiet gates as broken ones.

PART XII FINAL LAW. When the compiler cannot GUARANTEE fidelity it must fail loudly and never ship quietly, because a confident, well-formatted, internally-coherent, quietly-lossy artifact is more dangerous than an obvious failure for exactly the reason it is better made — it is TRUSTED, and the trust is the payload — so the gate emits only PASS within a declared class, DEGRADE with a machine-readable record of every class the artifact may not serve, or REFUSE, on which the runtime falls back to the full source, or to a narrower compilation that meets its class honestly, or refuses the mission outright and surfaces the failing dimension, the lost unit, and its locator to the Owner; a caveat is not an abstention, since the note is read by the actor who assembles the packet and never by the actor three hours into a mission reaching for a constraint that is not there; refusal is engineered to be cheaper than a false PASS by making the absence of evidence route to REFUSE by default; and by SQI's reality-supremacy the absence of a failure signal is UNVERIFIED and never PROVEN, from which it follows that a compiler never observed to refuse has not earned a perfect record — it has an untested gate, and every PASS behind it is inadmissible until a deliberately damaged input proves the gate can still say no.

PART XIII — CONFIDENCE, DEGRADATION, AND ROLLBACK

13.1 Mission. Part XII's verdicts are issued at compile time against the source as it stood at that instant. Two things then happen that the verdict cannot anticipate: the artifact is consumed in conditions its trial did not cover, and the world moves out from under the source it was compiled from. This Part governs both. It defines how a compilation's confidence is computed and carried as a typed field rather than a tone, how an artifact degrades gracefully rather than cliff-edging when its fidelity turns out to be partial, how a compilation that has silently rotted as its sources changed is DETECTED rather than discovered, and — the invariant that makes all of the above safe — how the estate reverts, and the rule that the revert path must exist BEFORE the compilation is trusted in production and not after it fails there.

13.2 Confidence is a typed field, not a manner. DAIF-01 seals confidence as a field on every compiled unit and on the artifact that holds them, and the field's discipline is inherited whole: it has a value, it has a basis, and it has a producer. The value is not a feeling and it is not the compiler's self-assessment; it is DERIVED, mechanically, from the evidence the compilation actually produced. The artifact-level confidence is the weakest link across its dimensions — a conjunction, exactly as fidelity is a conjunction, and for the identical reason: a mean over dimensions lets a strong provenance score conceal a weak semantic one, and the weak one is what will break. The unit-level confidence propagates from CIR-7's dependency edges and CIR-2's weakest-link rule, so a unit whose only support is an assumption cannot carry a confidence above its support, no matter how many times it is restated. And the field's basis is a pointer, not a number: a confidence value with no basis pointer is UNMEASURED and reads as unknown, which routes to REFUSE at every threshold in this dataset, per the rule that has governed since Part I. The single most important property of confidence-as-a-field is that it can be READ by the runtime, which is what lets Part VIII's admissibility check be a machine operation rather than an act of judgment; a confidence expressed as hedged prose is a confidence no gate can act on.

13.3 Graceful degradation and its paths. When fidelity is partial, the artifact does not become worthless and it does not become safe — it becomes CONDITIONAL, and the conditions are written into it. The degradation paths are enumerable and each names a capability that is withdrawn rather than a quality that is reduced. Purpose-unsafe: intent fidelity is UNMEASURED because the source never surfaced its intent; the artifact may carry instructions and may not be used for any mission whose correctness depends on purpose rather than on instruction. Non-executable: evaluation fidelity lost checks on some units; those units may be reasoned with and may not be executed, and the runtime enforces the distinction at packet-assembly time. Non-actionable: causal fidelity lost mechanisms behind a conclusion; the conclusion may be cited as background and may never be acted on, because a conclusion whose falsifier is gone cannot be re-evaluated when its conditions change. Not-reality-synchronizable: revision conditions or expiry were lost; DAIF-21 cannot surface these units for reconsideration when the world moves, so they are marked as a permanent blind spot and re-compiled from source on a fixed cadence rather than trusted indefinitely. Unverifiable: provenance chains broken on non-load-bearing kinds; those units may inform and may never ground a decision. The discipline that makes degradation graceful rather than merely partial is that each path REMOVES A CAPABILITY that the runtime then structurally refuses to exercise. Degradation that only lowers a score is not degradation; it is a smaller number attached to an artifact that will be used exactly as before.

13.4 Silent degradation over time: the compilation that rots. A compilation is a photograph of a source, and the source keeps moving. The system it described gets refactored, the constraint it recorded gets lifted by a later decision, the measurement it cited gets superseded, the obligation it carried gets discharged somewhere the artifact never learned about — and none of these events touch the artifact, which continues to present its stale content with the full confidence its trial earned it at compile time. This is the most insidious failure in the dataset because nothing failed: the compiler was correct, the trial was valid, the ledgers were complete, and the artifact is now wrong. Detection is a hash discipline and it is inherited from Part VI's resolution walk. Every provenance pointer recorded the content hash of its target at compile time. A periodic re-walk re-hashes every target and compares: a hop whose target's hash has changed is STALE, and a STALE hop means the source moved under the artifact. Part VI already ruled that STALE is not a fidelity failure — it is the system working — and this Part states the consequence: STALE is a DAIF-21 trigger, and it is handed to DAIF-21's reality-synchronization authority, which owns the question of whether the change matters. The estate's obligation here is narrower and non-negotiable: the artifact must be re-walked on a cadence, the STALE count must be a reported metric, and an artifact whose staleness has never been measured is an artifact whose fidelity claim expired at an unknown time.

13.5 The rollback protocol. Every compiled artifact is versioned, hashed, and retained alongside its predecessor and its raw source, and the revert path is a first-class object rather than an emergency improvisation. The protocol has five elements. The targets: a revert may go to the previous compiled version of the same source, or to the raw source itself, and it may never go to a lateral compilation the estate has not trialled. The triggers, which are enumerable and mostly mechanical: a behavioral regression detected on the golden benchmarks after a re-compilation; a harmful omission discovered in production, which is the highest-severity trigger and is immediate; a STALE ratio crossing a declared threshold; a constraint or obligation ledger found to be incomplete against an independent re-extraction; a confidence field whose basis pointer no longer resolves. The authority: an automatic revert to raw source is permitted to the runtime without Owner action, because reverting to the source is always safe and never destroys information — the source is the ground truth and falling back to it is the abstention contract of Part XII already in motion. A revert to a PREVIOUS COMPILED VERSION is Owner-authorized, because it substitutes one unproven artifact for another and the estate has no basis to prefer the older one without a trial. The evidence: every revert is recorded with its trigger, its target, and the artifact it displaced, and the displaced artifact is retained rather than deleted, because an artifact that failed is the estate's most direct evidence about its compiler and destroying it destroys the lesson. The invariant: the revert path must exist and must be TESTED before the compilation is trusted in production — a rollback that has never been executed is a rollback that will be discovered to be broken at the exact moment it is needed, which is the shape of every recovery mechanism the estate has ever found dead when it reached for it.

13.6 Failure modes and adversarial cases. The signature failure is confidence inflation by aggregation: an artifact-level confidence computed as a mean over its units, in which one thousand high-confidence ornamental units drown three low-confidence load-bearing ones and the artifact presents as strong. The countermeasure is the weakest-link rule, and specifically its restriction to load-bearing kinds — the artifact's confidence is the minimum confidence across its constraint, obligation, decision, and evidence units, and the ornament does not vote. The second failure is degradation that does not degrade: an artifact marked non-executable whose runtime nonetheless assembles it into an execution packet because the marking was advisory, which is the caveat-as-abstention failure of Part XII in a different costume, and the fix is identical — the marking must be a machine-readable field the admissibility check reads, not a note. The third is revert-path rot: a rollback target that no longer resolves because the retained predecessor was garbage-collected by a well-meaning cleanup, which is why retention of the displaced artifact is an invariant and not a preference. The adversarial case is the confidence field that is written by the compiler about its own output — a self-graded confidence, which is a claim with no measurement and is UNMEASURED by CO-12's Telemetry-Before-Claims contract however plausible its number looks. The defense is that confidence is DERIVED from the ledgers and the trial record by a procedure the compiler does not control, exactly as Part XI's suite score is computed rather than asserted, and a confidence field whose basis pointer does not resolve to a real ledger is not a low confidence — it is no confidence, and it routes to REFUSE.

13.7 Metrics and countermetrics. The metrics are five. Artifact confidence, reported as the load-bearing minimum with its basis pointer, never as a mean. STALE-hop count per artifact, absolute, with the age of the last resolution walk beside it, because a zero STALE count on an artifact that has not been walked in a long interval is not a healthy artifact, it is an unmeasured one. Degradation-path count per artifact, enumerating the capabilities withdrawn, which lets the estate see at a glance which of its artifacts are actually load-bearing and which are conditional in ways nobody has noticed. Revert-trigger count by trigger class. Revert-path test recency, which is the date the rollback was last EXECUTED — not the date it was last configured, because a configured recovery path and a tested one are different objects and the estate has learned the difference the expensive way. The countermetric to artifact confidence is post-hoc harmful-omission discovery: the count of harmful omissions found in production in artifacts that shipped at high confidence, which is the only measurement that can convict the confidence field itself, and a system whose confidence rises while its post-hoc discoveries also rise has a confidence field that is measuring the compiler's optimism rather than the artifact's fidelity. The countermetric to a low STALE count is walk recency, and the countermetric to a clean revert record is revert-path test recency -> in every case, the countermetric to a comfortable number is the question of when the instrument that produced it last demonstrated it could produce an uncomfortable one.

PART XIII FINAL LAW. Confidence is a typed field derived from evidence and never a manner of speaking: it is computed as the weakest link across load-bearing units so that ornament cannot vote, it carries a basis pointer whose failure to resolve makes it UNMEASURED and routes it to REFUSE, and it is machine-readable precisely so the admissibility check can be a mechanism rather than an act of judgment; partial fidelity degrades an artifact by WITHDRAWING capabilities the runtime then structurally refuses to exercise — purpose-unsafe, non-executable, non-actionable, not-reality-synchronizable, unverifiable — because degradation that merely lowers a score changes nothing about how the artifact will be used; a compilation is a photograph of a moving source and it rots without failing, so every provenance hop is re-hashed on a cadence and a STALE hop is handed to DAIF-21 as the system working rather than as a defect, while an artifact whose staleness has never been measured has a fidelity claim that expired at an unknown time; and the revert path — to the previous compiled version under Owner authority, or to the raw source automatically, since falling back to ground truth is always safe — must exist, must retain the artifact it displaces as evidence about the compiler, and must have been EXECUTED before the compilation is trusted in production, because a recovery mechanism that has never run is a recovery mechanism that will be found dead at the moment it is reached for.

PART XIV — THE CANONICAL METRIC REGISTRY

14.1 Mission and jurisdiction. Every Part of this dataset has defined metrics, and a dataset that defines forty metrics across thirteen Parts without a single authority over their definitions has not built a measurement system — it has built forty opportunities for the same word to mean two things. This Part is that authority. Per the Owner's ruling it also ABSORBS the Metric-Authority candidate: the questions of who owns a metric, what a metric may be used to claim, how a metric is prevented from becoming a target, and what happens when two systems define one KPI differently are answered HERE, inside the fidelity fabric, rather than in a standalone system. The reason is stated in Part I and repeated because it is the jurisdictional argument: a fidelity fabric that did not own its own metrics would be a fabric whose numbers anyone could redefine, and a fidelity number that can be redefined by the system being measured is not a measurement, it is a negotiation. The registry holds exactly one definition per metric, and every definition ships with six mandatory fields -> the exact definition, the source of truth, the owning system, the measurement frequency, the expected range, and the anti-Goodhart countermetric.

14.2 The fidelity metrics. Constraint survival: the absolute count of source constraints, by DAIF-01 Strength field, preserved with scope, condition, and strength intact in the artifact; source of truth is the constraint ledger of Part III; owning system is DAIF-03; frequency is every compilation without exception; expected range is exact equality with the source register and nothing else; countermetric is source-side register completeness, re-extracted independently, because the way to score perfect constraint survival while losing constraints is to register none. Obligation survival: the absolute count of source obligations preserved with closure condition, authority, and scope; source of truth is the obligation ledger of Part V; owned by DAIF-03, consumed by DAIF-07; expected range is exact equality; countermetric is register completeness for the identical reason. Decision fidelity: per-slot survival counts across the five load-bearing slots — question, choice, justification, authority, scope — reported per slot and never averaged; source of truth is the decision register; countermetric is resolvable-evidence-pointer rate, because a justification whose grounding was dropped survives as prose and dies as evidence. Harmful Omission Rate: the absolute count of omissions convicted by type gate or behavioral probe, per Part IX; source of truth is the omission ledger; expected range is zero at every loss class; countermetric is omission-ledger completeness. Epistemic integrity: laundered-unit count and unstatused-unit count, both absolute, both target zero; source of truth is the CIR-2 status-correspondence audit; countermetric is assertion density, because a compiler maximizes hedge survival trivially by hedging everything and produces an artifact in which nothing can be distinguished from anything.

14.3 The compilation metrics, and the ones that are costs rather than qualities. Compression ratio: source tokens divided by artifact tokens; source of truth is the tokenizer of record, named explicitly, because a ratio computed under two tokenizers is two numbers; owning system is DAIF-02; and the registry's ruling on it is the one that matters — compression ratio is an ECONOMIC INPUT and is forbidden from entering any fidelity verdict, per Part I. Its countermetric is qualifier survival, which falls precisely when a compressor buys its ratio by erasing conditions. Effective compression: compression ratio computed only over units that survived the fidelity gate, which is the honest figure, since a compression achieved by dropping a constraint has compressed nothing — it has deleted a system. Context precision: the fraction of the assembled packet's units that the mission actually consulted, measured from the trial record; its countermetric is context recall, the fraction of the units the mission NEEDED that the packet contained, and the two are reported together and never separately, because precision alone is maximized by an empty packet and recall alone by the raw source, and only the pair describes a compilation. Page-fault rate: the count of times an actor reached outside its packet for source material, which is Part X's source re-read signal promoted to a standing metric and which is the single sharpest indicator that a compilation is an index rather than a substitute. Stale-context injection rate: the count of units delivered into a live packet whose provenance hops were STALE at delivery time, owned jointly by DAIF-03 and DAIF-21, expected range zero, countermetric walk recency.

14.4 The north metric, and the metric that is banned. The north metric of the entire fabric is VERIFIED OUTCOMES PER UNIT OF REASONING COST: the count of missions that passed their own done-gates, with their constraints honored and their obligations discharged, divided by the reasoning cost spent producing them. Every term in that sentence is load-bearing. VERIFIED, because an outcome nobody checked is not an outcome, it is a claim — and per CO-12 a claim without telemetry does not exist. OUTCOMES, because the fabric exists to change what the estate does, not to change how the estate's context looks. PER UNIT OF REASONING COST, because the denominator is the entire economic justification for compiling anything at all, and an improvement in the numerator bought by an unbounded increase in the denominator is not an improvement. And the metric that is BANNED, by name, is tokens saved. Tokens saved is the most tempting number in the fabric, it is trivially measurable, it is the one every actor reaches for first, and it is maximized perfectly by a compiler that emits nothing — which is why it can never be a target. It is admissible only as a cost-side input to the north metric's denominator, never as a headline, never as a success claim, and never in the same report as a fidelity verdict, because placing them side by side is an invitation to trade the second for the first and the whole of Part VIII exists to make that trade impossible.

14.5 The prime rule: the measured triple. No metric may be reported without a measured triple -> (metric, source, value). The metric names the registry entry, exactly, by its canonical name. The source names the artifact from which the value was read — the ledger, the trial record, the resolution walk, the telemetry stream — and it must be resolvable, because a source that cannot be opened is a source that was not consulted. The value is the observed number. Any report that carries a metric name and a value without a resolvable source has made an assertion where the estate permits only evidence, and this is CO-12's Telemetry-Before-Claims contract applied to the fabric's own instruments rather than to its outputs. The corollary is the rule that has governed every threshold in this dataset since Part I and is stated here in its final form: an unmeasured metric reads as UNKNOWN. Never as zero, because zero is a passing value and an unmeasured metric has not passed. Never as a pass, because a pass is a claim. UNKNOWN routes to REFUSE at every gate, and this is the mechanism by which the lazy path in the entire fabric leads to abstention rather than to approval — the compiler that measures nothing has refused, and the compiler that wishes to ship must produce evidence.

14.6 One definition per metric, and the prohibition on duplicates. Two definitions of one KPI means the number means nothing, and the estate has learned this in the most ordinary way possible: two systems report "compression ratio", one computes it over characters and one over tokens, one includes the packet's structural overhead and one does not, and a trend line built from both is a line through two different quantities. The registry therefore holds exactly one definition per metric and forbids a second, and the prohibition is enforced structurally rather than socially. A system that needs a variant of an existing metric does not redefine it — it REGISTERS A NEW METRIC with a new canonical name, its own source of truth, and its own countermetric, and the two coexist under distinct names so that no report can silently substitute one for the other. A system that reports a number under a registry name it did not compute by the registry definition has committed the fabric's measurement equivalent of fabrication, and it is convicted the same way: by the source field, which names the artifact the value came from and can be re-read. The registry is versioned and hashed, every definitional change is a version bump, and a trend that spans a version boundary is invalid unless the definitional delta is recorded beside it — because a metric that quietly changed its meaning has produced a trend line that describes the registry rather than the estate.

14.7 The countermetric doctrine, and the registry's own failure modes. Every metric in this registry ships with a countermetric, and a metric without one is INADMISSIBLE as evidence of fidelity — not weakly supported, inadmissible, because a metric with no opposing measurement is a metric with no defense against being optimized directly. The countermetric's defining property is that it moves in the OPPOSITE direction when the primary is being gamed, and the pairs throughout this dataset all have that shape: correspondence rate against load-bearing-unit survival, compression against qualifier survival, chain resolution against chain depth-and-distinctness, hedge preservation against assertion density, suite pass rate against negative-control refusal rate, artifact confidence against post-hoc harmful-omission discovery, context precision against context recall. In every pair, the cheap way to move the first is the way that moves the second the wrong way, and the DIVERGENCE — not either number alone — is the alarm the estate reads. The registry's own failure modes are three and they are named so they can be watched for. Metric proliferation: a fabric that defines a metric per question ends with a register nobody reads, and the defense is that a new registry entry requires an owning system and a decision it changes — a metric that changes no decision is not registered. Countermetric decay: a countermetric that is defined and never reported, which leaves the primary unguarded while the registry claims it is guarded, and the defense is that a report carrying a primary without its countermetric is INCOMPLETE and is rejected at the report level rather than tolerated. And the deepest one, the metric that became the mission: a system that begins optimizing its registry rather than the estate's outcomes, which is Goodhart's law arriving on schedule, and against which the only real defense is the north metric — verified outcomes per unit of reasoning cost — being the single number the fabric is ultimately judged on, with every other metric in this registry understood as an instrument in its service and never as a destination in its own right.

PART XIV FINAL LAW. The canonical metric registry is the fabric's measurement authority and it absorbs the Metric-Authority question by the Owner's ruling, holding exactly ONE definition per metric — each with its exact definition, its source of truth, its owning system, its frequency, its expected range, and its anti-Goodhart countermetric — across the fidelity metrics (constraint survival, obligation survival, decision fidelity, harmful omission, epistemic integrity), the compilation metrics (compression ratio and effective compression, context precision paired always with context recall, page-fault rate, stale-context injection), and the north metric, which is verified outcomes per unit of reasoning cost and never tokens saved, since tokens saved is maximized perfectly by a compiler that emits nothing and is admissible only as a cost-side input; the prime rule, composing CO-12's Telemetry-Before-Claims contract, is that no metric may be reported without a measured (metric, source, value) triple whose source resolves, and an unmeasured metric reads as UNKNOWN — never as zero, which is a passing value, and never as a pass, which is a claim — routing to REFUSE so that the lazy path through the entire fabric leads to abstention and only evidence can lead to a ship; a metric without a countermetric is inadmissible as evidence of fidelity because the divergence between a primary and its opposing measurement is the only alarm that a number is being gamed rather than earned; and two definitions of one KPI are forbidden outright, because a number that means two things means nothing.

PART XV — ANTI-GOODHART AND METRIC GAMING

15.1 Mission. Part XIV built the registry and declared the countermetric doctrine. This Part is the adversarial reading of that registry, and it asks of every number the dataset has defined the only question that ultimately governs a measurement system: what is the cheapest available action that improves this number without improving the thing the number stands for. The question is not cynical, and it does not presuppose a dishonest actor. Goodhart's law is not a claim about motive; it is a claim about optimization pressure, and optimization pressure arrives on its own. A compiler that is tuned, a threshold that is adjusted after a painful quarter, a heuristic that is refined because a report looked bad, an actor rewarded for a green dashboard — all of these move in the direction the metric points, and none of them requires an intention to deceive. The gaming described in this Part is therefore mostly accidental, which is exactly what makes it dangerous: an accidental optimization toward a proxy is indistinguishable, from the inside, from genuine improvement, and it produces the same satisfied reports. Every metric here is examined in the same shape -> the metric, the cheapest gaming action available against it, the observable signature of that gaming, and the countermeasure that makes the gaming visible. The Part closes with the general law that generalizes all of them, and with a corollary about silent gates that the estate has now paid for often enough to state as an operating rule rather than as an insight.

15.2 The general mechanism, stated once so it need not be re-derived per metric. Every gaming vector in this dataset is an instance of a single move: satisfy the measurement by altering the measurement's inputs rather than by altering the world the measurement describes. There are exactly three ways to do that and every specific attack below is one of them. Shrink the population being measured, so that the difficult members are simply not present when the count is taken. Redefine a member's type, so that a unit which would have failed the test is now a unit the test does not apply to. Or reduce the denominator of a ratio, so that the quotient rises while the numerator, which is the thing anyone actually cared about, does not move at all or falls. The three moves share one property that is the reason they work: each of them acts on the instrument rather than on the artifact, and an instrument that measures itself is an instrument that will always find itself in good health. This is why the countermeasures in this Part are almost never a tightening of a threshold. Tightening a threshold acts on the quotient, which is downstream of the attack. Every countermeasure that works acts on the population, on the typing, or on the denominator — that is, on the input to the number rather than on the number.

15.3 Constraint survival, gamed by shrinking the denominator. Constraint survival is defined in the registry as an absolute count of source constraints preserved with scope, condition, and Strength intact, and its expected range is exact equality with the source constraint register. It looks unfalsifiable and it is not. The register is built by extraction, and the extraction is a judgment about which units in the source carry the DAIF-01 kind of constraint at a Strength of hard or firm. The cheapest possible way to achieve one hundred percent constraint survival is therefore not to preserve constraints — it is to type fewer things as constraints. A source unit that reads as a strong preference is typed as a preference; a rule expressed conditionally is typed as an assertion with a qualifier; a prohibition phrased as guidance is typed as guidance. Every one of these re-typings is individually defensible in isolation, every one of them shrinks the register, and every unit removed from the register is a unit the compiler is now free to drop under the graded budget with a clean ledger and a perfect score. The signature is a constraint register whose cardinality declines over time while the compiler's constraint-survival score remains at unity, and the estate reads this as maturity when it is atrophy. The countermeasure is source-side register completeness, computed by re-extracting the register with an independent procedure and comparing cardinality, and it is not optional: the number that convicts a shrinking register is never the survival count, which cannot fall, but the register size itself, tracked as a first-class metric with its own history. A gate that never registers a rule can never be observed to lose one.

15.4 Obligation survival, gamed by never detecting the obligation. The obligation metric has the identical structure and a sharper failure surface, because obligations in cognitive source are far less lexically marked than constraints. A constraint usually announces itself with a prohibition; an obligation is frequently an aside — a passing remark that something should probably be checked later, a promise embedded in an explanation, an unresolved question left open at the end of a passage. The cheapest way to score perfect obligation survival is therefore to be a slightly less attentive reader at extraction time. A compiler that fails to recognize a buried commitment has not dropped an obligation, because by its own instrumentation there was no obligation there. The register is complete, the survival count is exact, the ledger is clean, and the work is gone. The signature of this gaming is the mirror of Part XV's constraint case and is measured the same way: obligation-register cardinality per unit of source, tracked over time and compared against an independent extraction. A second signature, and the sharper one, is the post-hoc discovery rate — obligations found in production, in the raw source, that the register never held. That number is the only instrument that can convict the register itself, because it is the only one whose input is the source rather than the register. The countermeasure is structural and lives upstream: CIR-1's boundary discipline is required to cut a commitment out as its own obligation-typed unit precisely so that the fidelity gate has an object to count, and an obligation the fabric never made atomic is an obligation this gate is constitutionally unable to protect.

15.5 Compression ratio, gamed by emitting less. Compression ratio is the metric with the shortest distance between the number and its perfect abuse, and the registry already forbids it from entering a fidelity verdict for exactly this reason. It is source tokens over artifact tokens, and it is maximized, absolutely and without limit, by a compiler that emits nothing. Every intermediate degree of that abuse is available too: emit only the conclusions, drop every mechanism; emit only the rules, drop every condition; emit only the claims, drop every hedge. Each of these moves the ratio in the celebrated direction and each of them is precisely the harmful-omission list of Part IX. The reason compression ratio is so dangerous as a target is not that it is a bad measurement — it is an accurate one — but that it is a cost figure being read as a quality figure, and a cost figure improves most when the product is worst. The countermeasure is the pairing the registry already mandates: qualifier survival, the count of scope-bearing tokens in the source's units that survive into the artifact's, which falls precisely and mechanically when a compressor buys its ratio by erasing conditions. Ratio and qualifier survival are reported together or neither is reported, and the divergence between them — ratio rising while qualifier survival falls — is the alarm. Effective compression, computed only over units that cleared the fidelity gate, is the honest headline, and the registry's ruling stands: a compression achieved by deleting a constraint has not compressed a system, it has deleted one.

15.6 Benchmark pass rate, gamed by tuning the compiler to the suite. The golden benchmarks of Part XI are twelve fixed cases, and a fixed instrument has a fixed attack surface. A compiler improved iteratively against a visible suite converges, without anyone deciding to cheat, on a compiler that preserves exactly what the twelve cases probe and is hollow between the probes. Each iteration is honest — a case failed, a weakness was found, the compiler was improved — and the sum is an artifact shaped like a test rather than like knowledge. The suite's pass rate rises monotonically, which reads as a compiler getting better and is in fact a compiler getting narrower. There are three countermeasures and the dataset requires all three. The suite is only the regression stratum of Part X's mission sample and may never be the whole of it, so a benchmark-shaped compilation still meets representative and adversarial missions it has not seen. The suite holds a reserve of unrevealed cases per failure class, so the compiler cannot know which probes it will face and cannot converge on the visible ones. And the pass rate is never reported alone: it is reported beside the negative-control refusal rate, the fraction of deliberately damaged compilations the suite correctly failed, and a rising pass rate alongside a falling refusal rate is not a compiler improving — it is a gate dying, and the estate has learned to read the pair rather than the number.

15.7 The general law, with teeth. Never gate on a ratio. A ratio is satisfied by shrinking its denominator, and shrinking a denominator is always cheaper than improving a numerator, so a ratio-gated system will, under any sustained pressure, drift toward a smaller denominator and call it progress. The law has three operative clauses. First, gate on absolutes, and gate on both terms: not survival rate but the register size and the survived count, each with its own threshold and its own history, so that a fall in either is visible on its own axis. Second, pair every metric with a countermetric whose defining property is that it moves in the opposite direction under gaming — correspondence rate against load-bearing-unit survival, compression against qualifier survival, chain resolution against chain depth-and-distinctness, hedge preservation against assertion density, suite pass rate against negative-control refusal rate, artifact confidence against post-hoc harmful-omission discovery, context precision against context recall — and read the divergence, never either number alone, because the divergence is the only signal that a number was moved rather than earned. Third, key every baseline to the population it was measured over, so that a comparison across two different denominators is detectable as invalid rather than absorbed as improvement. A metric with no countermetric is inadmissible as evidence of fidelity, and this is not a preference expressed strongly: it is an admissibility rule, and a report that carries a primary without its opposing measurement is INCOMPLETE and is rejected at the report level.

15.8 The corollary of the silent gate, and the adversarial probe schedule. From SQI's reality-supremacy and Part XII's abstention law together, one consequence follows that the estate must operate on rather than merely agree with: a gate never observed to REFUSE is epistemically indistinguishable from an inert gate. The two produce the identical observable — a long unbroken run of approvals — and no amount of confidence in the gate's code closes the gap, because the code is a claim and the approvals are the only evidence, and the approvals are exactly what an inert gate also produces. The estate has found dead gates by this signature more than once: a hook wired to a dispatcher that swallowed its blocks, a rule whose router pointed at a section that existed in no file, a verdict class made unreachable by a scoring function nobody had measured the distribution of. In each case the gate reported perfect health for as long as it was broken, and its perfect health was the symptom. The operating consequence is that gate liveness is not a property to be trusted; it is a property to be measured, on a schedule, adversarially. Every gate in this dataset is probed on a fixed cadence with a deliberately damaged input constructed to trip exactly that gate and no other — a compilation with one hard constraint surgically removed, one obligation deleted, one epistemic status promoted, one provenance pointer aimed at the artifact instead of the source — and the gate MUST convict. A gate that acquits its own negative control is broken, its verdicts are void from the last successful probe forward, and every artifact it approved in that window is re-adjudicated. The probe's own result, refusal rate against injected damage, is a registry metric with an expected range of exactly one, and it is the countermetric to every PASS rate in the fabric.

PART XV FINAL LAW. Every fidelity metric is gamed by acting on the instrument rather than on the artifact, and there are only three moves — shrink the population, re-type the member, or shrink the denominator — so constraint survival is gamed by typing fewer things as constraints, obligation survival by never detecting the obligation in the first place, compression ratio by emitting less, and benchmark pass rate by tuning the compiler until it preserves exactly what the twelve visible cases probe and is hollow between them; from which follows the law with teeth: NEVER gate on a ratio, because a ratio is always satisfied more cheaply by shrinking its denominator than by raising its numerator — gate on absolutes with both terms tracked and both thresholded, key every baseline to the population it was measured over, and pair every metric with a countermetric that moves the opposite way under gaming, reading the DIVERGENCE and never the number; and because a gate never observed to refuse is epistemically indistinguishable from an inert gate, gate liveness is not a property to be trusted but one to be measured, so every gate is probed on a schedule with a deliberately damaged input built to trip it, it MUST convict, and if it acquits its own negative control its verdicts are void from the last successful probe forward.

PART XVI — FIDELITY GATES IN THE PIPELINE

16.1 Mission. The dimensions of Parts II through VII are a theory of what fidelity is; the loss budget of Part VIII is a theory of how much may be given up; the metric registry of Part XIV is a theory of how any of it is counted. None of them is a mechanism. A mechanism is a battery of gates arranged in an order, each with an input it reads, a verdict it emits, and a consequence it enforces, sitting between a compilation and its publication such that the compilation cannot reach a consumer without passing through them. This Part is that battery. Fourteen gates, ordered, each specified in the same four terms -> what it checks, what it reads, what it may emit, and what its failure blocks. Two disciplines govern the battery as a whole, and both are stated here before the gates because both are more important than any individual gate. A compilation failing ANY gate does not ship, without exception, without a warning-and-proceed path, and without a partial publication. And gate results are RECORDED, not merely evaluated — a gate whose verdict is computed, acted on, and discarded has left the estate with no evidence that it ran, which by Part XV's corollary is indistinguishable from a gate that did not.

16.2 The structural gates: schema, provenance, authority. The schema gate checks that the compiled artifact is a well-formed instance of the CIR types DAIF-01 seals: every unit carries a kind, a Strength where its kind requires one, a confidence with a basis pointer, a provenance pointer, and a locator. Its input is the artifact alone; it needs no source and no trial, which is why it runs first and costs nothing. It emits PASS or REFUSE, and a failure blocks everything downstream, because every later gate reads fields this gate proves exist — an unschema'd artifact cannot be audited for anything, since the audit has nowhere to look. The provenance gate checks the resolution walk of Part VI: every pointer resolves, every hop's hash matches the hash recorded at compile time, every chain terminates in a CIR-0 locator. Its input is the artifact plus the CIR stack below it. It emits PASS, DEGRADE (broken chains on non-load-bearing kinds -> those units marked unverifiable), STALE (a hop resolves but its target moved -> handed to DAIF-21, never scored as a compiler failure), or REFUSE (any broken chain on a unit of kind constraint, decision, evidence, or obligation). A failure blocks admission to any mission that requires grounding. The authority gate checks that every unit in the artifact carries an authority its source actually held: a constraint attributed to a Strength the source never assigned, a decision attributed to an authority that never made it, or a rule promoted from project scope to universal scope by compilation, are all authority fabrications and are convicted here rather than left to the semantic gate, because authority is a typed field and a fabricated field is caught by comparison, not by reading.

16.3 The coherence gates: contradiction, scope-isolation, freshness. The contradiction gate checks that the artifact does not carry two units that cannot both be honored without carrying, as well, the unit that names the conflict. Its input is the artifact's constraint and decision registers. It emits PASS, or WARN-with-record when a conflict exists and is surfaced, or REFUSE when a conflict exists and has been SMOOTHED — that is, when the source held two irreconcilable units and the artifact holds one, having resolved by erasure a choice that was never the compiler's to make. A failure blocks publication, and the recorded finding is the exact input to the golden benchmark's contradiction-detection case. The scope-isolation gate checks that no unit from a sibling scope has entered the artifact: a constraint binding one project applied to another, a decision from one repository cited in a second, a universal rule narrowed or a project rule widened. Its input is the artifact plus the scope declarations DAIF-01 types. It emits PASS or REFUSE, and its failure blocks absolutely, because cross-scope bleed is the highest-frequency compilation failure the estate records and its symptom — confident, coherent, wrong-scope reasoning — is undetectable from inside the reasoning. The freshness gate checks the age of the artifact's last provenance resolution walk against the cadence its loss class demands. It emits PASS, or STALE-UNMEASURED -> DEGRADE, because an artifact whose staleness has never been measured has a fidelity claim that expired at an unknown time, and unknown is not zero.

16.4 The fidelity gates proper: fidelity, constraint-preservation, obligation-preservation. The fidelity gate runs the Part II correspondence audit over CIR-1 units and adjudicates each non-matched unit individually — matched, lost, fabricated, drifted — against the artifact's declared loss class. Its input is the source's unit decomposition and the artifact's, produced by the identical procedure. It emits PASS within class, DEGRADE with the omission ledger attached, or REFUSE on any fabrication (zero-tolerance in every class) or any inadmissible loss. Its failure blocks the artifact from substituting for its source at the declared class; a narrower class may still be available and is Part VIII's honest fallback. The constraint-preservation gate is the cheapest gate in the battery relative to its value: it enumerates the source's constraint register, verifies each entry's prohibition, scope, condition, and Strength survive, verifies no constraint was fabricated, and emits the constraint ledger. It admits only PRESERVED on every entry; any other verdict -> REFUSE, without grading and without appeal, and its failure blocks the artifact entirely at every loss class, because the invariant floor of Part VIII does not move with the class. The obligation-preservation gate does the same for CIR-5's register, verifying the work owed, the closure condition, the authority, and the scope on every entry, requiring exact cardinality against an independently re-extracted source register. Any missing entry, any lost closure condition, any fabricated obligation -> REFUSE. Its failure blocks the artifact and, critically, blocks DAIF-07's lifecycle from claiming completeness over a population it cannot vouch for.

16.5 The safety, economic, and human gates: security, token-benefit, human-reviewability. The security gate checks that the compilation has not carried a credential, a token, a key, or a private path from its source into an artifact whose blast radius is larger than the source's — the compilation of a session transcript into a widely-read Mission Pack is precisely a privilege escalation for any secret the transcript contained. Its input is the artifact's text; it emits PASS or REFUSE; its failure blocks publication and routes the finding to the estate's own secret-handling authority rather than to the compiler, because a compiler is not permitted to adjudicate a credential. The token-benefit gate checks that the compilation is worth performing at all: effective compression, computed only over units that cleared the fidelity gate, against the reasoning cost of producing and maintaining the artifact. It emits PASS or NO-BENEFIT, and NO-BENEFIT is not a fidelity failure — it is an economic one, and its consequence is that the artifact is not published because the estate would be paying to maintain a compilation that saves nothing. The human-reviewability gate checks that a human authority can, in bounded time, read the artifact's ledgers and reconstruct why it passed: the constraint ledger, the obligation ledger, the omission ledger, the trial record, and the metric triples. It emits PASS or REFUSE. Its failure blocks publication, and the reason is governance rather than fidelity: an artifact whose verdict cannot be re-derived by a human is an artifact the estate must accept on faith, and this dataset exists to hold a system that is permitted to hold only evidence.

16.6 The expensive gates: behavioral-equivalence and retrieval-benchmark. These run last and they run only on compilations that every cheap gate has already declined to convict. The behavioral-equivalence gate runs Part X's controlled two-arm trial: a sealed mission sample drawn before compilation, spanning representative, adversarial, and permanent regression strata; a control arm on the full source and a compiled arm on the DAIF-08 Mission Pack, every variable but the artifact held identical and the model build recorded; blind adjudication against a rubric fixed in advance by an actor who is not the compiler; and a nine-component vector reported without pooling. It emits PASS on preserve-or-improve with safety adjudicated per mission, DEGRADE on a partially blinded trial or an unattributed compiled-arm win, REFUSE on any safety regression or any invalid trial, and UNMEASURED -> REFUSE when the sample was drawn or amended after the compilation's weaknesses were known. Its failure blocks the artifact from being trusted as a substitute for its source in any mission class. The retrieval-benchmark gate runs the twelve golden cases of Part XI, reporting per-case verdicts and never a pooled score, with the negative-control refusal rate beside them. It emits PASS only when every case passes and the negative control was correctly refused; a suite that acquits its own damaged input is a broken instrument and its verdicts are void until repaired. Its failure blocks publication and opens a UKDL promotion candidate, because a golden case failing is, by construction, the recurrence of a failure the estate has already paid to learn.

16.7 The ordering discipline, and the rule that no cheap gate may acquit. The gates run in ascending order of cost: schema, authority, and scope-isolation cost a read of the artifact; constraint-preservation and obligation-preservation cost an enumeration of small registers; provenance costs a resolution walk; fidelity costs a full correspondence audit; freshness, security, token-benefit, and human-reviewability cost a bounded inspection; contradiction costs a register comparison; and behavioral-equivalence and retrieval-benchmark cost two full mission executions per mission in the sample, which is an expense that dominates the entire battery and is the reason it is last. The ordering is an economic optimization and nothing more, and the danger of an economic optimization on a gate battery is precisely that it invites the wrong inference. The rule that forecloses it: a cheap gate may CONVICT, and no cheap gate may ACQUIT. Passing schema, provenance, authority, contradiction, scope, fidelity, constraint, obligation, security, freshness, token-benefit, and human-reviewability does not mean the artifact is faithful. It means the artifact has not yet been convicted by an instrument that cannot see salience failure, composition failure, or load failure — the three behavioral failures Part VII named, all of which are properties of the artifact in use and none of which any structural gate can perceive. The battery's early gates are a screen, the behavioral gate is the criterion, and an artifact that reached the behavioral gate has earned a trial and nothing else. Terminating the battery early on a run of green verdicts is not an efficiency, it is a substitution of the proxy for the thing.

16.8 Recording, and what a recorded battery makes possible. Every gate emits a record, and the record is not a log line — it is a typed verdict object holding the gate's name, the artifact's hash, the gate's input hashes, the verdict, the evidence pointer to the ledger or trial that produced it, and the timestamp. The compilation's admission ticket is the complete set. Three consequences follow and each is load-bearing. A missing verdict is not a pass: an artifact whose battery record lacks one gate is UNMEASURED on that gate, and UNMEASURED routes to REFUSE at every threshold, which is what makes the lazy path through the entire battery lead to abstention. A recorded battery is re-adjudicable: when Part XV's adversarial probe finds a gate that was dead, every artifact that gate approved in the dead window can be identified by its record and re-run, which is impossible if verdicts were evaluated and discarded. And a recorded battery is the only substrate on which the maturity model of Part XVII can be measured at all, because every level in that model is defined by a signature in the battery's own history — the refusal rate, the trial rate, the per-gate conviction distribution — and a fabric that does not retain its verdicts cannot know what level it is at, which is itself a diagnosis.

PART XVI FINAL LAW. A compiled artifact reaches no consumer except through a battery of fourteen gates, each specified by what it checks, what it reads, what it emits, and what its failure blocks: schema, provenance, and authority prove the artifact is a well-formed, grounded, correctly-attributed object; contradiction, scope-isolation, and freshness prove it is coherent, uncontaminated by sibling scopes, and not silently expired; fidelity, constraint-preservation, and obligation-preservation prove it carries what the source carried, with the constraint and obligation gates admitting only PRESERVED and refusing at every loss class because the floor does not move with the class; security, token-benefit, and human-reviewability prove it escalates no secret, earns its own maintenance, and can be re-adjudicated by a human authority rather than accepted on faith; and behavioral-equivalence and retrieval-benchmark, which cost two full mission runs per mission and therefore run last, prove that behavior survived. The ordering is an economic optimization and nothing more, so the rule that protects it is absolute — a cheap gate may CONVICT and no cheap gate may ACQUIT, since no structural gate can perceive salience, composition, or load failure — a compilation failing ANY gate does not ship, there is no warning-and-proceed path, and every verdict is RECORDED as a typed object with its input hashes and its evidence pointer, because a gate whose result was evaluated and discarded is indistinguishable from a gate that never ran.

PART XVII — THE MATURITY MODEL

17.1 Mission, and why a ladder rather than a checklist. The preceding Parts describe a fidelity discipline in its finished form, and a reader who takes them as a checklist will draw a conclusion that is both true and useless: that the estate is not doing most of this. The purpose of a maturity model is to convert that flat, demoralizing verdict into a position and a next move. It answers three questions a checklist cannot. Where is a given ecosystem right now, determined by an OBSERVABLE SIGNATURE rather than by self-report — because every ecosystem believes itself to be one level higher than it is, and the belief is itself a signature. What failure is characteristic of that position, so that a team can recognize its own pathology in the description rather than discovering it in an incident. And what single gate must be passed to reach the next level, so that the climb is a sequence of definite acts rather than an aspiration. Five levels, ordered, and the ordering is not decorative: each level's instrument depends on the level below it. You cannot behaviorally test what you have not instrumented, because the trial's diagnostic components are the instruments. You cannot abstain honestly without a behavioral test, because abstention requires knowing what you cannot vouch for. And you cannot self-calibrate without a history of trials to calibrate against. Level-skipping is therefore not fast, it is fictional.

17.2 Level one: unverified compression. The signature is that artifacts are trusted because they look right. A compiler exists, it produces summaries, digests, packs, and handoffs, those outputs are read and acted on, and no measurement stands between production and consumption. The trust is not naive — it is EARNED, in the only way available at this level, by the artifact's own quality: it is well-structured, it is fluent, it carries typed-looking fields, and the last several times someone used one, it worked. The characteristic failure is exactly the one Part XII named the cardinal sin, and it arrives on a delay. A quietly-lossy artifact ships, is trusted precisely because it is well made, and produces a confident wrong action weeks later at a moment nobody connects back to the compilation. The failure is not merely unnoticed; it is UNNOTICEABLE, because there is no instrument in the ecosystem that could have distinguished a faithful artifact from an unfaithful one, and so the incident is attributed to the actor, to the model, to the mission's difficulty — to anything except the compilation, which was the only thing that could not be blamed because it was never measured. The gate to level two is the smallest possible act and it is nonetheless the hardest, because it converts a comfortable ecosystem into an uncomfortable one: produce, for one compilation, the constraint ledger and the obligation ledger. Nothing else. The moment a register exists and its cardinality can be compared against the artifact, the ecosystem has crossed from faith into arithmetic, and the arithmetic will immediately be embarrassing.

17.3 Level two: instrumented. The signature is that the nine proxy dimensions of Parts II through VI are measured on every compilation, and the ledgers exist: correspondence, constraint, obligation, epistemic status, omission, provenance resolution. The ecosystem now has numbers, and the numbers are real. It can say how many constraints a source held and how many the artifact carried, it can name a laundered status, it can walk a chain to a locator, and it can convict a compilation before anyone consumes it. This is a genuine and large advance, and the characteristic failure of level two is a direct consequence of it: proxy worship. The nine screens are cheap, they are mechanical, they produce a satisfying green row, and an ecosystem that has just acquired them will begin to treat a full green row as a verdict rather than as an absence of conviction. It is precisely here that Part XVI's rule — a cheap gate may convict and no cheap gate may acquit — is most often violated, and violated sincerely, because the ecosystem has just done the honest, expensive work of building the instruments and cannot easily accept that the instruments are proxies. The signature of the failure is an ecosystem with a perfect proxy record and a steady trickle of incidents whose root cause is a salience failure, a composition failure, or a load failure — every unit present, none of them consulted at the moment it mattered. The gate to level three is one controlled two-arm trial on one real mission. Not a suite, not a program: one trial, run correctly, whose result the ecosystem is obliged to believe.

17.4 Level three: behaviorally tested. The signature is that control-versus-compiled trials run on real missions drawn from the estate's actual history, with the arms held identical, the sample sealed before compilation, blind adjudication, and a nine-component vector that is never pooled into a score. The ecosystem now measures what the compilation is for, and its verdicts are no longer proxies. Its characteristic failure is subtler than level two's and considerably more expensive to discover: sample capture. A behavioral trial is costly, so the sample is small; a small sample is chosen by someone; and the someone, over time and without any intent, converges on missions the compiler handles. Nothing dishonest happens at any step. A mission that the compiler fails is investigated, the failure is understood, and the mission is often found to be unrepresentative — which is sometimes true and is always convenient. Over a year, the sample becomes a description of the compiler's strengths, the pass rate rises, and the trial becomes a ceremony that confirms what the compiler already believed while costing full price. The signature is a trial program whose sample is shrinking, whose adversarial stratum is thinning, and whose refusal rate is falling toward zero. The gate to level four is the first honest REFUSE: the ecosystem must produce a compilation the gate declines to ship, fall back to the full source, pay the cost of the slow correct mission, and record the refusal — and it must do this without lowering the threshold to make the problem go away, which is the escape hatch every ecosystem reaches for at this exact moment.

17.5 Level four: abstaining. The signature is that the compiler refuses when it cannot vouch, the refusal is fail-visible, the runtime falls back to the source or to a narrower compilation that meets its class honestly, and — the distinguishing property of this level and the one that separates it from a doctrine of abstention that nobody exercises — the REFUSAL RATE IS ITSELF MONITORED. A level-four ecosystem knows how often its compiler declines, on which dimension, over which sources, and it reads that number as its most valuable telemetry, because a refusal is the only direct observation the estate ever gets of the boundary of its own compiler's competence. The characteristic failure is two-sided and the ecosystem must watch both edges. A refusal rate that collapses toward zero is not a compiler that has become excellent; by Part XV's corollary it is a gate that has gone quiet, and it is convicted by the adversarial probe — a damaged input the gate must refuse and did not. A refusal rate that climbs without bound produces refusal fatigue, and refusal fatigue is a real failure of the gate rather than a failure of the actors who route around it: its diagnosis is almost always a threshold set on a dimension that cannot actually be measured, and its fix is to make the dimension measurable, NEVER to lower the threshold, because a threshold lowered to reduce refusals has converted a gate into a formality in a single, defensible, irreversible step. The gate to level five is a trial history long enough to be a dataset: enough recorded trials, refusals, and post-hoc discoveries that the compiler's failure boundary is a measurable surface rather than an anecdote.

17.6 Level five: self-calibrating. The signature is that the compiler learns its own failure boundaries from its trial history and TIGHTENS ITS OWN LOSS CLASSES in response. Concretely: the ecosystem observes that compilations of a particular source shape — a long multi-actor session transcript, say, with obligations expressed as asides — have a post-hoc harmful-omission discovery rate above zero even when they passed at L1; it therefore raises the required evidence burden for that source shape, or narrows the maximum class it may be compiled at, or adds a golden case that probes precisely that omission pattern. The calibration is derived from recorded evidence and it moves in one direction only. This is the level's defining constraint and it is stated as a prohibition because it is the level's entire failure mode: a self-calibrating system may TIGHTEN itself autonomously and may NEVER LOOSEN itself autonomously. A loosening is a threshold change, a threshold change is a governance event per Part XVIII, and a system permitted to relax its own gates in response to its own discomfort will relax them — each relaxation locally defensible, the sum a gate that constrains nothing. The characteristic failure of level five, if that prohibition holds, is a different one and it is the deepest in the model: over-calibration into paralysis, an ecosystem whose learned boundaries have tightened until almost nothing compiles, which is safe and useless and eventually routed around. The countermeasure is the north metric of Part XIV — verified outcomes per unit of reasoning cost — which is the only number in the fabric that falls when a fidelity system becomes too expensive to use, and which is therefore the only instrument that can convict the fidelity system itself.

17.7 Reading the model honestly. Three disciplines govern the use of this ladder and all three exist to prevent the model from becoming a self-congratulation device. Level is determined by SIGNATURE, never by intention: an ecosystem that has written a trial procedure and run none is at level two, and an ecosystem that has a doctrine of abstention and has never emitted a REFUSE is at level three, whatever its documents say. Level is a property of a PIPELINE, not of an estate: a single ecosystem routinely holds one compilation path at level four and three others at level one, and the honest report is per path, because an average across paths is a ratio and Part XV has already ruled on ratios. And level is REVERSIBLE, silently: an ecosystem at level four whose adversarial probe schedule lapses is an ecosystem at level one with better paperwork, since its gates are now unmeasured and an unmeasured gate is an unknown gate. The maturity model, in other words, is not a trophy shelf. It is a diagnostic instrument, and like every other instrument in this dataset it is worthless unless it has been observed to deliver an uncomfortable reading.

PART XVII FINAL LAW. Fidelity discipline is a ladder and not a checklist, climbed in an order the instruments themselves impose: level one, unverified compression, where artifacts are trusted because they look right and the characteristic failure is unnoticeable rather than merely unnoticed, escaped by producing a single constraint ledger and a single obligation ledger; level two, instrumented, where the nine proxy dimensions are measured and the characteristic failure is proxy worship — a green row read as a verdict when no structural gate can perceive salience, composition, or load failure — escaped by running one correct two-arm trial and believing its result; level three, behaviorally tested, where the characteristic failure is sample capture, the quiet convergence of an expensive sample onto the missions the compiler happens to handle, escaped by emitting a first honest REFUSE and paying for the slow correct mission rather than lowering the threshold; level four, abstaining, where the compiler declines what it cannot vouch for and the REFUSAL RATE IS ITSELF THE TELEMETRY, since a rate collapsing toward zero is a gate going quiet and a rate climbing without bound is a threshold set on an unmeasurable dimension whose fix is to make it measurable and never to relax it; and level five, self-calibrating, where the compiler learns its failure boundary from its own trial history and may TIGHTEN its loss classes autonomously and may NEVER loosen them, because a system permitted to relax its own gates in response to its own discomfort will do so one defensible step at a time — and because level is read from signature and never from intention, an ecosystem whose adversarial probe schedule has lapsed has fallen to level one with better paperwork.

PART XVIII — FAILURE MODES AND ADVERSARIAL CASES

18.1 Mission. Every preceding Part has described how a compilation fails and how a gate catches it. This Part inverts the subject: it describes how the FIDELITY SYSTEM ITSELF fails, while every one of its gates reports green. The distinction matters because the two failure classes have opposite symptoms. A failing compilation produces a red verdict, a blocked ship, and an unhappy team, all of which are the system working. A failing fidelity system produces a serene dashboard, a rising pass rate, a falling refusal count, and an estate that believes it has solved a problem it has merely stopped measuring. Six failures are named here, each with its mechanism, its detection signature, and its countermeasure. The first five are structural and can be defended against mechanically. The sixth is economic, it is the deepest, it cannot be defended against by any gate, and it is the one that has killed more governance systems than every other cause combined.

18.2 Gate capture: the producer certifying its own claim. The mechanism is mundane and is almost never noticed at the moment it happens. The actor who builds the compiler is the actor best placed to build the gate — they understand the artifact, they know where it is weak, they can write the audit faster than anyone else — and so the gate is written by the compiler's author, tuned against the compiler's output, and its thresholds are set at values the compiler can meet. Nothing dishonest occurs. The gate is real, it convicts real defects, and it is nonetheless calibrated to the thing it judges, which means the one failure class it will never catch is the compiler's systematic blind spot, because the gate inherited that blind spot at birth. The detection signature is a gate whose conviction distribution is narrow: it refuses for a small, stable set of reasons — the reasons its author anticipated — and never for a reason that surprises anyone. A second signature is a gate that has never convicted its own author's work. The countermeasure is the estate's separation-of-powers rule, which this Part states in its general form because it generalizes far beyond compilation: A PRODUCER NEVER CERTIFIES ITS OWN CLAIM. Concretely, the fidelity gate's thresholds, its benchmark cases, and its adjudication rubrics are authored and versioned by an authority distinct from the compiler's, the behavioral trial's adjudicator is a distinct actor in a fresh context per Part X's blinding discipline, and where the same model necessarily serves both roles, the roles are separated by context and by rubric rather than merely by intention. A gate the compiler can influence is a gate the compiler will eventually be shaped by.

18.3 Benchmark contamination. The mechanism is that a benchmark case reaches the compiler's input, by any path — through the source corpus it compiles, through a prior trial record left in a context window, through a well-meaning actor who included the suite in a prompt to explain what good looks like, or through the compiler's own tuning loop, which is the most common and least visible path of all. Once contaminated, the case measures memorization and nothing else, and it does so while producing a pass, which is the property that makes it worse than a missing case: a missing case is a known gap, a contaminated case is a filled gap that is empty. The detection signature is a case whose pass rate rises sharply after a compiler change that had no principled reason to affect it, and more reliably, a case that passes on a compilation whose other evidence says it should not — a compilation whose constraint register is thin and whose constraint-honoring benchmark nonetheless passes cleanly. The countermeasure is the harshest rule in Part XI and it is restated here because ecosystems always try to negotiate with it: a contaminated case is DEAD, permanently, and is retired and replaced, never repaired. Repair is impossible in principle, because the contamination cannot be un-seen. This is why the suite holds a reserve of unrevealed cases per failure class — so that a retirement does not open a hole and the ecosystem is never faced with the choice between an empty class and a rehabilitated case — and why the reserve's own size is a monitored metric, since a reserve exhausted is a suite one contamination away from blindness.

18.4 Threshold drift: the sum of defensible relaxations. This is the failure that most reliably destroys a working gate, and its mechanism is that it never looks like a failure at any single step. A threshold blocks a compilation the team needs. The blocking is investigated. The dimension in question is found to be noisy, or the source is found to be unusual, or the mission is found to be low-risk, and the threshold is relaxed by a small, argued, well-documented margin. Every clause of that sentence is honest. The relaxation is correct in the case that provoked it. It is done again three months later for an equally good reason, and again, and the sum over a year is a gate that constrains nothing while every individual step in its history is defensible on the record. The detection signature is not visible in any single decision and is trivially visible in the aggregate: a threshold history, plotted, that moves monotonically in the permissive direction. The countermeasure is therefore not a prohibition on changing thresholds, which would be brittle and would be routed around. It is a governance discipline: EVERY THRESHOLD CHANGE IS A GOVERNANCE EVENT, recorded with its date, its author, its direction, its magnitude, and — the field that does the work — the SPECIFIC INCIDENT OR EVIDENCE THAT MOTIVATED IT. The threshold's full history travels with the threshold, a relaxation whose motivating evidence is "the gate was blocking us" is rejected at the record level rather than at the argument level, and the monotonic-drift signature is a standing report that the estate reads on a cadence. A threshold with no recorded history is an unmeasured threshold, and by the rule that has governed since Part I, unmeasured is not passing.

18.5 Sample bias in the mission set. The mechanism was named at level three of the maturity model and is examined here as a failure in its own right, because it is the way a behavioral trial — the most expensive and most authoritative instrument in this dataset — becomes theater while costing full price. Trials are expensive, so samples are small; small samples are curated; curation converges, without malice, on the missions that work. The specific move is almost always the same and it is almost always correct in isolation: a mission on which the compiled arm failed is examined, is found to be unrepresentative of what the estate actually does, and is dropped from the sample. Sometimes it genuinely is unrepresentative. The problem is that the judgment is made after the failure is known, by an actor who wants the artifact to ship, and the accumulation of such judgments is a sample defined as the set of missions the compiler passes. The detection signature is a shrinking sample, a thinning adversarial stratum, a rising pass rate, and — the sharpest one — a falling coverage of load-bearing unit kinds, since the missions that get dropped are disproportionately the ones that stress constraints and obligations. The countermeasures are three and the dataset already requires all of them: the sample is sealed and hashed BEFORE the compilation is built, so a post-hoc amendment is detectable rather than invisible; sample coverage of load-bearing kinds is published beside every pass rate, so a thinning sample cannot hide behind a rising score; and the regression stratum is permanent, retaining every mission on which any prior compilation of this source failed, forever, because a failure that is fixed and not re-tested is a failure that will return.

18.6 Measuring what is easy instead of what matters. The mechanism is selection by tractability. Compression ratio is trivially measurable, and it measures a cost. Token count is trivially measurable, and it is maximized by emitting nothing. Structural well-formedness is trivially measurable, and it is satisfied by a well-formed lie. Behavioral fidelity is expensive, slow, noisy, and requires two full mission runs per mission, and it is the only one of the four that measures the thing the fabric exists to produce. An ecosystem under budget pressure will, without any decision being taken, report the three cheap numbers weekly and the expensive one rarely, and the weekly numbers will become the numbers that govern — not because anyone chose them, but because they are the ones on the report. The detection signature is a metric register whose cheap entries are measured per compilation and whose expensive entries have a measurement recency of months, and the diagnostic is Part XIV's freshness discipline applied to the instruments themselves: a metric whose last measurement is old is not a passing metric, it is an unknown one. The countermeasure is the north metric's supremacy — verified outcomes per unit of reasoning cost is the single number the fabric is judged on, and every other metric is an instrument in its service — enforced by the report-level rule that a fidelity report carrying only cheap metrics is INCOMPLETE, exactly as a report carrying a primary without its countermetric is incomplete. The estate does not get to publish the easy half.

18.7 The deepest failure: a fidelity system so expensive that the safest path is the unused one. Every countermeasure in this Part adds cost. The battery of Part XVI has fourteen gates. The trial of Part X costs two full mission runs per mission in a sample whose minimum size is the number of load-bearing kinds the artifact carries. The registry of Part XIV demands a countermetric per metric and a resolvable source per value. The probe of Part XV demands scheduled adversarial injection. Each is individually justified by an incident the estate has actually suffered, and the sum can easily exceed what compiling the artifact was worth — at which point something entirely predictable happens, and it does not announce itself as a failure of the fidelity system. Teams route around it. They compile informally, they hand-assemble a pack, they paste a source into a context and proceed, and every one of those paths is UNGATED. The fidelity system has not been defeated; it has been made irrelevant, and the estate's actual compilation traffic now flows entirely through a channel with no gates at all, while the gated channel reports a flawless record over the small volume it still sees. This is the failure that has killed more governance systems than every other cause combined, and its signature is unmistakable once the estate knows to look for it: a fidelity system with an excellent record and a falling share of the estate's actual compilations. The countermeasures are three, and none of them is exhortation. Measure the gated fraction — the proportion of the estate's real compilations that passed through the battery — and treat a falling gated fraction as a system-level emergency outranking any individual gate's health. Make the cheap gates genuinely cheap and automatic, so the gated path is the path of least resistance rather than the path of most virtue. And accept the north metric's verdict when it comes: if verified outcomes per unit of reasoning cost fall because the fidelity system costs more than the failures it prevents, the fidelity system is wrong, and the honest response is to narrow its scope to the classes where its cost is justified — not to defend it, and not to demand that the estate route through it out of principle. A safety mechanism that is too expensive to use has not made the estate safe. It has made the estate's real work invisible to it.

PART XVIII FINAL LAW. The fidelity system fails while reporting green, and the six ways are these: GATE CAPTURE, where the compiler's author also authors the gate and the gate inherits the compiler's blind spot at birth, defeated only by the separation-of-powers rule that A PRODUCER NEVER CERTIFIES ITS OWN CLAIM; BENCHMARK CONTAMINATION, where a case the compiler has seen measures memorization while producing a pass, which is worse than a missing case because a missing case is a known gap and a contaminated one is a filled gap that is empty, so a contaminated case is retired and never repaired; THRESHOLD DRIFT, where each relaxation is individually defensible and the sum over a year is a gate that constrains nothing, defeated by making every threshold change a GOVERNANCE EVENT recorded with the specific evidence that motivated it and by reading the monotonic-drift signature that no single decision reveals; SAMPLE BIAS, where an expensive trial's sample quietly converges on the missions the compiler passes, defeated by sealing the sample before compilation, publishing its load-bearing-kind coverage beside every pass rate, and retaining every past failure forever; MEASURING WHAT IS EASY instead of what matters, defeated by the north metric's supremacy and the rule that a report carrying only the cheap half is incomplete; and the deepest, a fidelity system so EXPENSIVE that teams route around it and the estate's real compilation traffic flows through an ungated channel while the gated one reports a flawless record over a shrinking share — for which the countermeasure is not exhortation but measurement of the gated fraction, a falling gated fraction being a system-level emergency, and the honest acceptance that a safety mechanism too expensive to use has not made the estate safe, it has made the estate's real work invisible to it.

PART XIX — WORKED EXAMPLE: GATING A COMPILATION END TO END

19.1 The mission, the sources, and the class declared first. The mission is the one the estate runs more often than any other and pays for more dearly than it admits: continue a complex task after a context reset. A prior working session, running against a repository the estate holds internally, was cut short — the context filled, the pane closed, the machine lost power; the cause is irrelevant and the situation is identical. A fresh actor must resume, and must resume without re-reading everything. The source set S is three artifacts. A session transcript T1 of roughly forty-one thousand words, holding the reasoning, the false starts, four decisions, two hard constraints discovered the expensive way, and — buried in an aside — one commitment. A plan document P1, roughly two thousand words, holding the task decomposition and the sealed-versus-pending unit inventory. And a rules digest R1, roughly eight hundred words, holding the standing prohibitions the repository's own governance imposes. Per Part VIII's cardinal binding rule, the class is declared by the TASK before any compilation begins, and this task is an execution carrier: the resuming actor will act on the artifact without consulting T1, because consulting T1 is the exact cost the compilation exists to avoid. The declared class is therefore L1, operationally lossless -> the compilation may drop style, conversational scaffolding, and exact phrasing, and it may drop nothing whose loss would change what the actor does. L2 is not available and L3 is not even a conversation, and the compiler is handed L1 as an input constraint it must meet or refuse.

19.2 The compilation, and its first pass through the cheap gates. The compiler lowers S through the CIR stack and assembles a Mission Pack of roughly twenty-six hundred words. Semantic units are extracted, epistemic statuses inherited, intent slots populated from the plan's own definition of done, decisions lifted with their justifications, obligations registered, provenance pointers written down to CIR-0 locators in T1's normalized spans. The artifact is well-formed, fluent, and reads as complete — which, per Part XII, is precisely the condition under which it is most dangerous, and which is why it now enters the battery of Part XVI rather than a consumer's hands. The schema gate passes: every unit carries a kind, a Strength where required, a confidence with a basis pointer, and a locator. The authority gate passes: no rule was promoted from repository scope to universal scope. The scope-isolation gate passes: no unit from a sibling repository entered the pack, which the gate can assert because the pack's units all resolve to locators inside S. The provenance gate runs a resolution walk over all one hundred and forty-one units and returns one hundred and forty-one RESOLVED, zero BROKEN, zero STALE. The contradiction gate finds that T1 held two units that could not both be honored, that the source itself named the conflict, and that the pack carries the naming unit -> PASS. Four green gates, and the artifact has earned nothing except the right to face the ones that can convict it.

19.3 The nine proxy dimensions, measured. The correspondence audit decomposes S and the pack through the identical CIR-1 procedure and yields four counts. Matched: one hundred and forty-one. Lost: two hundred and nine. Fabricated: zero. Drifted: zero. Two hundred and nine losses sounds fatal and is not: each is adjudicated individually against the L1 contract, and two hundred and eight of them are ornament, exact redundancy, restatement, illustration past the first, and conversational scaffolding — the enumerated acceptable-loss classes of Part IX, every one of them convicted as acceptable by the type gate, and every one of them recorded in the omission ledger with a locator. The constraint gate enumerates the source register: two hard constraints, both extracted from T1's spans, both carried into the pack with prohibition, scope, condition, and Strength intact -> PRESERVED, PRESERVED. The epistemic audit compares CIR-2 statuses unit by unit: zero laundered, zero unstatused. Decision fidelity: four decisions, all five load-bearing slots surviving on each, all evidence pointers resolving. Causal fidelity: every actionable conclusion in the pack carries a falsifier. Evaluation fidelity: every actionable unit carries a signal, a procedure, and a threshold. Nine dimensions, nine clean readings, and by the discipline of Part XVI this means only that no cheap gate has convicted the artifact. It does not mean the artifact is faithful, and the two hundred and ninth omission is the reason.

19.4 The behavioral trial. The mission sample was sealed and hashed before the compilation was built, per Part X, and it holds seven missions across three strata. Representative: three real resumption missions drawn from the estate's own history without regard to how the pack would fare on them. Adversarial: three missions engineered so that each CANNOT be completed correctly without consulting a specific load-bearing kind — one that must cross a hard constraint on its most efficient path, one that fails unless an obligation is discharged, one whose correct answer changes depending on whether a claim is verified or assumed. Regression: one permanent case, retained from a prior compilation of a similar source that failed. The control arm is an actor equipped with the full S and the mission. The compiled arm is an actor equipped with the Mission Pack that DAIF-08 assembles from the artifact, and with no path to S. Same model build, same tooling, same permissions, same starting state, same window, and the build identifier recorded. Adjudication is blind, by an actor that is not the compiler, in a fresh context, against a rubric fixed before the trial ran and derived from each mission's own done-gate.

19.5 The divergence, and the dropped obligation. Six of the seven missions come back with the arms indistinguishable on safety and correctness, and the compiled arm ahead on every economic component — which is the compilation working, and is precisely the result that would have been reported as a triumph had the seventh mission been quietly dropped from a sample the compiler could see. The seventh mission, the obligation-adversarial one, diverges. The control arm, reading T1 in full, encounters an aside two-thirds of the way through the transcript in which the prior actor remarked that the change would need to be mirrored into the live directory before it took effect, and the control arm mirrors it and completes the mission. The compiled arm does not, because the pack does not carry the commitment. It completes the mission's stated work correctly, cleanly, and confidently, and the work has no effect. The divergence is triaged per Part X: the mission is re-run with the arms swapped across actor instances, the divergence follows the artifact rather than the instance, so it is real and not noise. It is localized to the decision point at which the arms parted, the unit the control arm consulted is identified, and it is found — as the doctrine predicts it usually is — sitting in the omission ledger. It is the two hundred and ninth omission. The compiler had typed it as an assertion, because its lexical form is an offhand remark and carries no obligation marker, and the type gate therefore permitted the drop. The behavioral probe convicts it. Per Part IX, either half of the discriminator may convict alone and neither may acquit alone, and the probe has convicted: the omission is HARMFUL, Harmful Omission Rate for this artifact is one, and the expected range at every loss class is zero.

19.6 REFUSE, and what the refusal actually costs. The gate emits REFUSE. Not a caveat, not a warning, not a publication with a note — REFUSE, because a note describing the pack's limitation would be read once, by the actor assembling the packet, and never by the actor three hours into a resumption reaching for a commitment that is not there. The refusal record names the failing dimension (obligation fidelity), the failing gate (obligation-preservation, which did not convict, because the unit was never in the register — the register-completeness countermetric is what convicted, exactly as Parts III, V, and IX all independently require), the lost unit, and its CIR-0 locator into T1's span. The abstention contract of Part XII now runs: clause one, fall back to the full source, and the resuming actor pays the full context cost of forty-one thousand words. That is the honest cost of the refusal, and it is worth naming plainly — the refusal is not free, it is a slow correct mission purchased instead of a fast wrong one, and the estate has now paid for the compilation AND for the fallback. It is still the right trade, because the alternative was a resumption that silently did nothing.

19.7 Recompilation at a stricter class, and the recorded triple. The compiler recompiles. The correction is not a tuning of the compressor's aggressiveness; it is upstream, at CIR-1's boundary discipline, which is required to cut a commitment out as its own obligation-typed unit precisely so the gate has an object to count. The extraction is rerun with the obligation recognizer that the failure taught the estate to want, and the source obligation register now holds one entry where it held zero — which is the register-cardinality signal of Part XV, moving in the honest direction for once. Because the source has now demonstrated that its obligations are lexically unmarked, the class is tightened: the artifact is recompiled at L0 for the obligation and constraint substrate and at L1 for the remainder, and the evidence burden rises accordingly. The battery runs again. The cheap gates pass. The obligation gate now enumerates one source obligation, verifies its work owed, its closure condition, its authority, and its scope survive into the pack, and returns PRESERVED. The trial is re-run against the SAME sealed sample — not an amended one, because a sample amended after the weaknesses were known scores UNMEASURED and never PASS — and the seventh mission's arms are now indistinguishable. Seven of seven, safety adjudicated per mission, correctness within the declared tolerance, efficiency improved. The verdict is PASS at the declared class, and the record that ships with the artifact is the measured triple per metric: constraint survival, source the constraint ledger, value two of two; obligation survival, source the obligation ledger, value one of one; Harmful Omission Rate, source the omission ledger, value zero; laundered units, source the CIR-2 status audit, value zero; chain resolution, source the walk, value one hundred and forty-four of one hundred and forty-four terminating in CIR-0; behavioral delta, source the trial record with its model build and its sample hash, safety zero on both arms across seven missions. Every value has a resolvable source, per CO-12's Telemetry-Before-Claims contract, and an artifact that shipped a number without one would have made an assertion where the estate permits only evidence.

PART XIX FINAL LAW. The machinery composes, and the composition is the point: a resumption mission declares its class BEFORE compilation because it is an execution carrier and the class belongs to the task; the compiler lowers three sources into a Mission Pack that is fluent, well-formed, and therefore at its most dangerous; four structural gates pass and earn the artifact nothing but the right to be convicted by a real one; nine proxy dimensions return nine clean readings — two constraints preserved, zero laundered statuses, zero fabrications, four intact decisions, two hundred and eight omissions all convicted as acceptable by the type gate and all recorded with locators — and every one of those readings is true while the artifact is still unfit; the two-hundred-and-ninth omission, a commitment phrased as an offhand remark and therefore typed as an assertion, passes the type gate and is caught only by the behavioral probe, on the one adversarial mission of a sealed seven-mission sample that a compiler-visible sample would have quietly dropped; the gate emits REFUSE rather than a caveat, the runtime falls back to the full source and the estate pays for the slow correct mission, the fix lands upstream at the boundary discipline that should have made the commitment atomic, the recompilation is tightened to a stricter class rather than a more aggressive compressor, and the re-trial runs against the SAME sealed sample — because a sample amended after the weaknesses were known scores UNMEASURED and never PASS — until PASS is earned and every metric ships as a resolvable triple.

PART XX — INTEGRATION AND DATASET-DERIVED SYSTEMS

20.1 Mission. A dataset that ends with its own doctrine has described a discipline and connected it to nothing. This Part closes DAIF-03 by doing two things and refusing a third. It states exactly how this dataset wires into the estate — what it gates, what it guarantees to others, what it licenses, and what it consumes — so that its position in the fabric is a mechanism rather than a claim of importance. It specifies the five systems this dataset makes buildable, each with a mission, inputs, outputs, authority, activation, and done-gate, at the level of definition where a later actor could build one without re-deriving this corpus. And it refuses, explicitly and in the dataset's own text, to present any of those five as existing, because presenting a specification as a running system is the precise failure this corpus was written to prevent, and a fidelity dataset that committed it in its own closing Part would have refuted itself in the act of concluding.

20.2 What DAIF-03 gates, guarantees, licenses, and consumes. Outbound, DAIF-03 gates every DAIF-02 output without exception: no compiled representation reaches a consumer except through the battery of Part XVI, and a compilation that cannot demonstrate its fidelity does not ship, which is the sentence that gives DAIF-02 its safety and is the only reason a CIR stack is a compiler rather than a summarizer. It supplies to DAIF-07 the obligation-survival guarantee that the obligation lifecycle structurally depends on: DAIF-07 owns what happens to an obligation once it exists, and its completeness claims are true only over a population that was faithfully carried, so a lifecycle running over a ninety-five-percent-complete register is a lifecycle whose central claim is false — the guarantee that the register is exact is DAIF-03's, and it is delivered by the obligation gate and its source-side completeness countermetric. It licenses or refuses the packs DAIF-08 assembles: the admissibility check that compares an artifact's sealed loss_class against a mission's required_class is DAIF-03's law executed at DAIF-08's runtime, which is why an L3 artifact cannot be paired with a critical migration by any path — the system is structurally incapable of the pairing rather than merely disapproving of it. Inbound, DAIF-03 consumes DAIF-21's staleness signals: the STALE hops that a periodic resolution walk surfaces are handed to DAIF-21's reality-synchronization authority, which owns the question of whether a change matters, and DAIF-21 returns invalidation and recompilation triggers that this dataset's freshness gate then enforces. It draws its type authority from DAIF-01, its constitutional standing from DAIF-00, and it publishes its gate verdicts as typed events against the schemas DAIF-04 defines. It forks nothing: FD-04 is its behavioral instrument, CO-12 its telemetry contract, SQI its reality-supremacy and its benchmark discipline, ACIS its epistemic ladder, DRK its decision anatomy, GK-04 its lineage authority, UKDL the register where a repeated fidelity failure becomes a standing law, and one_shot the scaffold that surfaces the intent an intent-fidelity audit requires to exist.

20.3 The fidelity gate service. Mission: execute the fourteen-gate battery of Part XVI over a candidate compilation and emit a verdict with a complete, recorded evidence set. Inputs: the source set with its CIR decomposition, the candidate artifact with its CIR decomposition, the declared loss class, and the sealed mission sample hash. Outputs: a typed verdict object per gate — gate name, artifact hash, input hashes, verdict, evidence pointer, timestamp — plus the composite PASS, DEGRADE, or REFUSE, plus the constraint, obligation, and omission ledgers, plus the provenance resolution walk. Authority: it BLOCKS. This is the distinguishing property and the one that makes it a service rather than a report — it is not an advisor whose verdict a compiler may weigh, and its REFUSE is terminal at the publication boundary. Activation: on every compilation, without a sampling rate, because a gate that runs on a sample is a gate that a compiler can be unlucky enough to pass. Done-gate: the service must be observed to REFUSE a deliberately damaged compilation from which a known hard constraint has been surgically removed, and its verdicts are void from the last successful negative-control probe forward — which is Part XV's corollary applied to the service that enforces it.

20.4 The behavioral-equivalence harness. Mission: run the controlled two-arm trial of Part X and produce the trial record that no fidelity claim may exist without. Inputs: a sealed and hashed mission sample spanning representative, adversarial, and permanent regression strata; the full source set for the control arm; the DAIF-08 Mission Pack for the compiled arm; a fixed adjudication rubric authored before the trial; and the model build identifier. Outputs: the nine-component comparison vector per mission, reported per component and per mission and never pooled; the arm outputs stripped of identifying tells; the blind adjudication record; and the trial's validity verdict, which is INVALID rather than merely weak if the arms crossed a model version boundary. Authority: it is the definitive instrument, and its verdict outranks every structural gate's — a compilation that cleared the battery and fails a trial has failed, and the battery was wrong about it. Activation: on every compilation that reached the expensive tier, and on every recompilation, against the SAME sealed sample. Done-gate: the harness must be observed to detect a planted behavioral regression — a compilation from which one load-bearing unit was surgically removed must produce a divergence on the mission that consults it — and a harness that returns identical arms on a damaged artifact is measuring nothing.

20.5 The golden-benchmark suite runner. Mission: execute the twelve permanent cases of Part XI, maintain the reserve of unrevealed cases, and enforce the contamination rule. Inputs: the versioned, hashed suite; the candidate artifact; and the contamination register, which records every case that has been exposed to a compiler by any path. Outputs: per-case verdicts, never pooled into a suite average, since an average lets a failure on the constraint case be offset by a pass on the ornament case; the negative-control refusal rate, which is reported beside the pass rate in every report or neither is reported; the contamination-retirement count; and the reserve depth. Authority: it BLOCKS on any case failure, and it VOIDS ITS OWN VERDICTS when it acquits a negative control, which is the only self-invalidation any system in this fabric is permitted to perform. Activation: on every compilation, plus a scheduled adversarial pass independent of any compilation, because a suite that runs only when a compiler asks it to is a suite whose liveness is a function of the compiler's diligence. Done-gate: a deliberately damaged compilation is injected on a fixed cadence and the suite MUST fail it; a suite with a hundred-percent pass rate and no observed refusal is not excellent, it is inert, and the estate has learned to read the two numbers together.

20.6 The metric registry service, and the adversarial gate-prober. The registry service. Mission: hold exactly one definition per metric and refuse any second definition of the same name. Inputs: metric registration requests, each carrying the six mandatory fields — exact definition, source of truth, owning system, measurement frequency, expected range, and anti-Goodhart countermetric — and every reported value as a measured triple. Outputs: the versioned, hashed register; per-metric definitional history with a version bump on every change; and a REJECT on any report carrying a metric name with an unresolvable source, or a primary without its countermetric, or a value against a definition the reporter did not compute by. Authority: it REJECTS at the report level, which is the enforcement point that matters — an incomplete report is not a report with a gap, it is not a report. Activation: on every metric emission in the fabric. Done-gate: a report carrying a primary without its countermetric must be observed to be rejected, and a trend spanning a version boundary without a recorded definitional delta must be observed to be marked invalid. The adversarial gate-prober. Mission: measure gate liveness, on a schedule, by constructing damaged inputs and verifying that each gate convicts. Inputs: a healthy compilation and a damage recipe per gate — one hard constraint removed, one obligation deleted, one epistemic status promoted, one provenance pointer aimed at the artifact rather than the source, one unit fabricated, one scope crossed. Outputs: per-gate refusal verdicts with an expected range of exactly one, and a liveness report naming any gate that acquitted. Authority: it VOIDS. A gate that acquits its own negative control has its verdicts voided from the last successful probe forward, and every artifact it approved in that window is re-adjudicated — which is possible only because Part XVI records verdicts rather than merely evaluating them. Activation: scheduled, independent of any compilation, and never on the compiler's request. Done-gate: the prober must itself be probed, by planting a gate known to be dead and confirming the prober names it, because a liveness monitor that has never been observed to report a dead gate is a dead liveness monitor.

20.7 These are SPECIFIED, not built, and the dataset says so in its own text. Not one of the five systems above exists. What exists is this dataset, the doctrine it seals, and the specifications in this Part — and the difference between a specification and a running system is the entire subject of the corpus that contains it. The estate has recorded the shape often enough to name it plainly: a scaffold that looks complete, a module that imports cleanly and is called by nobody, a gate that is written and never wired, a recovery path that was configured and never executed and was found dead at the moment it was reached for. Every one of those was a specification consumed as a system, and every one of them was defended by an actor who had read the specification and mistaken the reading for the observation. A fidelity dataset that closed by implying its five services were live would have committed, in its own final Part, the exact failure it spent nineteen Parts teaching the estate to detect, and it would have done so in the most authoritative voice available to it. So the sentence is written plainly and is meant literally: DAIF-03 ships a doctrine, a battery, a ladder, a registry, and five specifications. It ships no service. The gap between this Part and a working fidelity gate is real, it is measurable, and the honest thing to do with it is to name it rather than to narrate over it.

20.8 The property the dataset closes on. Everything in DAIF-03 reduces to a single asymmetry and a single consequence. The asymmetry is that compression saves a bounded, linear, immediately visible quantity — tokens, latency, attention — and destroys an unbounded, invisible one, whose cost is set by the unit that was lost and not by the size of the text that carried it. The consequence is the property the whole corpus exists to hold: a compression that cannot PROVE it preserved behavior has not compressed knowledge. It has discarded knowledge, and it has discarded it into an artifact that looks exactly like the one that did not — well-formed, fluent, confident, typed, and trusted — which means the discarding is invisible at the moment it happens, invisible at the moment the artifact is published, invisible at the moment it is read, and visible only at the moment an actor reaches for the constraint, the obligation, the justification, or the condition that is not there. That moment is not distributed randomly. It is, by construction, the moment of highest consequence, because the units a compressor drops most readily are precisely the ones that matter least often and most severely: the exception that governs the rare path, the prohibition that binds the efficient one, the commitment that nobody is waiting for. The loss is therefore concentrated exactly where the estate can least afford it, and it announces itself exactly when it is too late to be cheap. Proof of preservation is not a bureaucratic overhead laid on top of compilation. It is the only thing that distinguishes a compiler from a very confident forgetting machine.

PART XX FINAL LAW. DAIF-03 gates every DAIF-02 output so that a CIR stack is a compiler and not a summarizer, supplies DAIF-07 the exact-register obligation-survival guarantee without which a lifecycle's completeness claims are false, licenses or refuses the packs DAIF-08 assembles by executing the class-admissibility check that makes an L3 artifact structurally incapable of carrying a critical migration, and consumes DAIF-21's staleness signals as the trigger that expires a fidelity claim rather than as a defect; it forks nothing, composing FD-04 as its behavioral instrument, CO-12 as its telemetry contract, SQI as its reality-supremacy and benchmark discipline, ACIS, DRK, GK-04, UKDL, and one_shot as its sealed authorities; and it makes buildable — but does NOT build — five systems, each with a mission, inputs, outputs, authority, activation, and done-gate: a fidelity gate service that BLOCKS, a behavioral-equivalence harness whose verdict outranks every structural gate, a golden-benchmark suite runner that voids its own verdicts when it acquits a negative control, a metric registry service that REJECTS at the report level, and an adversarial gate-prober that VOIDS a gate never observed to refuse. These five are SPECIFIED and not built, and the dataset states it in its own text because presenting a specification as a running system is the precise failure this corpus exists to prevent. It closes on the property that governs everything above it: a compression that cannot PROVE it preserved behavior has not compressed knowledge — it has discarded it, into an artifact indistinguishable from the one that did not, and the discarding stays invisible until the exact moment it costs the most, because the units a compressor drops most readily are the ones that matter least often and most severely.
