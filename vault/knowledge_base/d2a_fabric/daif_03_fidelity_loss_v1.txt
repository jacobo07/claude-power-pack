DAIF-03 — FIDELITY, LOSS BUDGET AND BEHAVIORAL EQUIVALENCE

PART I — THESIS: FIDELITY IS WHAT SEPARATES A COMPILER FROM A SUMMARIZER

1.1 Mission. DAIF-02 gave the estate the ability to convert cognitive source into compiled representations: a stack of twelve typed intermediate forms that turn a wall of prose into selectable, linkable, executable objects. Nothing in DAIF-02 proves that any particular compilation was safe. The fabric can lower a conversation into semantic units, epistemic statuses, intents, decisions, and obligations, and it can do all of that while silently dropping the one exception that bounded a prohibition, the single evidence line that grounded a claim, or the follow-up nobody closed — and the compiled output will look immaculate, because a well-formed representation of the wrong content is still well-formed. This is the exact danger the fabric introduces by succeeding: without a fidelity gate, the CIR stack is an elaborate summarizer, and a summarizer that drops a hard constraint, an open obligation, or the reason behind a decision is strictly worse than no compilation at all, because it produces confident, well-structured, wrong behavior. Raw prose at least advertises its own unusability; a compiled artifact advertises trustworthiness it has not earned. DAIF-03 is the dataset that makes that trustworthiness earnable. It defines what fidelity means across every dimension of meaning the CIR stack claims to preserve, how much loss is permitted for which class of task, how equivalence is proven rather than asserted, and the compiler's duty to refuse — to fail visibly, loudly, and early — when it cannot guarantee that what it produced still provokes the behavior the source demanded. It gates every DAIF-02 output; a compilation that cannot demonstrate fidelity does not ship.

1.2 The asymmetry that governs everything. The founding law of this dataset is an asymmetry between what compression saves and what compression can destroy, and the asymmetry is not close. A compilation that reduces a hundred thousand words to four thousand saves a bounded, measurable, linear quantity: tokens, latency, attention. A compilation that drops one hard constraint costs an unbounded quantity: every future action taken under the compiled artifact proceeds without the constraint, each of those actions is a candidate production incident, and the incident's cost is set by the constraint's severity, not by the size of the text that carried it. A hard rule is typically the shortest unit in a source and the most expensive one to lose. This means the arithmetic that governs ordinary compression — percentage retained, percentage discarded — is not merely imprecise here, it is inverted. A compilation that preserves 99% of a source's semantic content and destroys one hard constraint is not a 99% success. It is a failure, and it is a worse failure than a compilation that preserved 40% of the prose and every constraint, because the second one is usable and honest while the first is a loaded instruction set. DAIF-03 therefore refuses, at the level of its scoring model, to average across dimensions. Fidelity is not a weighted mean of ten scores; it is a conjunction over ten dimensions, several of which are binary, and a failure on any binary dimension is a failure of the whole compilation regardless of how well the graded dimensions scored. Averaging is the mechanism by which a fatal loss disappears into a good-looking number, and it is prohibited here by name.

1.3 What fidelity is not. Three tempting definitions are rejected outright, because each is a metric the estate could optimize while getting worse. Fidelity is not compression ratio: a smaller artifact is not a better one, and the ratio is a cost figure, never a quality figure — DAIF-03 records it as an economic input and forbids it from entering the fidelity verdict. Fidelity is not readability or coherence: a compiled artifact reads well precisely because a compressor smoothed it, and smoothing is the operation that deletes hedges, qualifiers, and exceptions, so a readability score is positively correlated with the most dangerous loss class in this dataset. Fidelity is not source-overlap: token-level or embedding-level similarity between source and output rewards paraphrase-preserving compressions and is blind to the erased qualifier, because deleting the word "unless" changes similarity by a rounding error and changes meaning absolutely. What fidelity is, in one line: fidelity is the property that an actor consuming the compiled artifact is provoked into the same correct behavior as an actor consuming the full source. Everything else in this dataset — the ten dimensions, their tests, their thresholds — exists to make that property measurable before the behavior is observed, because behavior is expensive to test and the estate cannot afford to discover a fidelity failure only by suffering its consequence.

1.4 The ten dimensions, the dataset's spine. Fidelity decomposes into ten dimensions, each corresponding to a dimension of meaning the CIR stack claims to carry, and each with its own test, threshold, and failure mode. Semantic fidelity: is the meaning preserved. Intent fidelity: is what was actually wanted preserved, including implicit criteria, non-goals, and prohibitions. Constraint fidelity: are all hard constraints preserved — binary, never graded. Decision fidelity: are decisions preserved with their justifications, evidence, trade-offs, and revision conditions. Epistemic fidelity: does the artifact still distinguish fact from inference from assumption from speculation. Obligation fidelity: does one hundred percent of pending work survive — binary, never graded. Causal fidelity: are mechanisms preserved, not merely conclusions. Evaluation fidelity: does the artifact still say how to check that execution was correct. Provenance fidelity: can the path back to the original source be reconstructed. Behavioral fidelity: does an actor consuming the compiled artifact take the same correct decisions as an actor with the full source. The mapping to the CIR stack is one-to-one by construction and not by coincidence: semantic gates CIR-1, epistemic gates CIR-2, intent gates CIR-3, decision gates CIR-4, obligation gates CIR-5, causal gates CIR-6, evaluation gates CIR-9, provenance gates CIR-10's chain of custody, and constraint gates the Strength field DAIF-01 seals. A dimension without a gate is a dimension the compiler is free to lose.

1.5 Behavioral fidelity is the definitive test; the other nine are instrumental. The nine non-behavioral dimensions exist because behavior is the thing that matters and the thing that is expensive to measure. Each of the nine is a cheap, local, mechanical proxy for a specific way behavior can go wrong: a lost constraint predicts a violated boundary, a laundered assumption predicts an unwarranted commitment, a dropped obligation predicts work that never happens. They are proxies, and the dataset says so in its own text so that no future actor mistakes a passing proxy for a passing system. A compilation can score cleanly on all nine and still fail behaviorally, because the nine test the presence of preserved units and behavior tests their sufficiency in composition — an artifact can carry every constraint, every decision, and every obligation as individual units and still fail to make any of them salient at the moment an actor needs them, and salience is behavioral. The converse also holds and is the reason the nine are kept: an artifact that fails a binary dimension is known to be unsafe without running a single behavioral trial, which is what lets the estate refuse a bad compilation cheaply instead of discovering it expensively. The doctrine is therefore a ladder -> the nine dimensions are the fast screen that rejects most bad compilations at low cost, and behavioral fidelity is the slow, authoritative test that any compilation must pass before it is trusted as a substitute for its source.

1.6 The gate and the duty to refuse. DAIF-03 is not advisory. It is a gate that stands between DAIF-02's output and every consumer of that output, and it has three verdicts: PASS, meaning the compilation is a faithful carrier within its declared loss budget and may be used in place of its source; DEGRADE, meaning the compilation is faithful enough for some task classes and not others, and the artifact is marked with the classes it may not serve; and REFUSE, meaning the compiler cannot demonstrate fidelity and the compilation does not ship — the consumer is handed the source, or a smaller compilation over a narrower scope, and told exactly why. The duty to refuse is the dataset's moral center. A compiler that cannot guarantee fidelity and ships anyway has committed the estate's cardinal sin, which is the silent substitution of a confident artifact for a true one, and the estate has recorded that shape often enough to name it: a scaffold that looks complete, a gate never observed to refuse, a claim whose evidence was never collected. Refusal must be visible, must name the dimension that failed, must name the unit that was lost, and must be cheaper for the compiler to emit than a false PASS is — which is why every threshold in this dataset is set so that the absence of evidence produces REFUSE and never PASS. An unmeasured dimension is a failed dimension.

1.7 Composition and the absorbed metric authority. DAIF-03 forks nothing. It composes FD-04's six-lens transfer test as its behavioral instrument, CO-12's Telemetry-Before-Claims contract as the rule that no fidelity number may be asserted without the measurement that produced it, SQI's verification axis and reality-supremacy as the law that the observed artifact outranks the compiler's claim about it, ACIS's ladder as the authority on epistemic status, DRK as the authority on decision anatomy, GK-04 as the authority on lineage, and UKDL as the register where a repeated fidelity failure is promoted into a standing law. It also absorbs, by the Owner's ruling, the canonical Metric-Authority candidate: the question of who owns a metric, what a metric may be used to claim, and how a metric is prevented from becoming a target is answered inside this dataset rather than in a standalone system, because a fidelity fabric that did not own its own metrics would be a fabric whose numbers anyone could redefine. Every metric this dataset defines therefore ships with a countermetric — a second measurement that moves in the opposite direction when the first is being gamed — and a metric without a countermetric is not admissible as evidence of fidelity.

PART I FINAL LAW. Compilation without a fidelity gate is summarization wearing a compiler's costume, and because a compression that saves many tokens while destroying one hard constraint is not a ninety-nine-percent success but an unbounded failure whose cost is set by the constraint and not by the text, fidelity may never be averaged into a single number: it is a conjunction over ten dimensions — semantic, intent, constraint, decision, epistemic, obligation, causal, evaluation, provenance, and behavioral — of which behavioral is the definitive test and the other nine are cheap instrumental proxies for it, DAIF-03 gates every DAIF-02 output with PASS, DEGRADE, or REFUSE, and a compilation that cannot demonstrate its fidelity does not ship.

PART II — SEMANTIC FIDELITY

2.1 Mission. Semantic fidelity asks the first and most basic question a fidelity gate can ask: does the compiled artifact preserve the meaning of the source. It is first because every other dimension presupposes it — an intent that was semantically mangled cannot be checked for purpose, a constraint whose meaning shifted cannot be checked for preservation — and it is basic in the sense that a failure here is a failure of the compiler's most elementary claim. It is also the dimension most vulnerable to a comfortable illusion, because a compiled artifact that reads as a fluent, plausible account of its source feels semantically faithful, and fluency is exactly what a lossy compressor produces best. Semantic fidelity is therefore defined not as similarity of text but as preservation of assertional content: for every claim the source made, the artifact makes the same claim, with the same scope, the same qualifiers, the same strength, and the same distinctions intact, and it makes no claim the source did not make.

2.2 The unit of measurement is CIR-1, never the prose. The cardinal procedural rule of this dimension is that semantic fidelity is measured against CIR-1 semantic units, not against the raw prose of the source or the raw prose of the output. Measuring prose against prose is what produces every failure mode in this Part: it invites token overlap, embedding similarity, and human reading as evidence, and all three are blind to the losses that matter. Measured against CIR-1, the test becomes mechanical and enumerable. The source, decomposed by CIR-1, yields a set of atomic semantic units, each mapped to exactly one DAIF-01 kind. The compiled artifact, decomposed by the same CIR-1 procedure, yields a second set. Semantic fidelity is then a correspondence problem over two finite sets of typed units: every unit in the source set must have exactly one corresponding unit in the artifact set that carries the same assertional content, the same kind, and the same scope. A source unit with no correspondent is a loss. An artifact unit with no source correspondent is a fabrication. A correspondence whose kinds differ is a type drift. Each of the three is countable, each is attributable to a specific unit with a CIR-0 locator, and none of the three is visible to a similarity score. The correspondence is computed over units and audited by a reviewer who reads the units, never the smoothed prose, because the smoothed prose is the medium in which the loss hides.

2.3 The four traps. Paraphrase drift is the accumulation of small restatements, each individually defensible, whose composition changes the claim: a source unit saying a condition holds in most observed cases becomes an artifact unit saying the condition holds, and the drift is invisible per-hop and fatal in aggregate. The erased qualifier is the deletion of a single scope-bearing word — unless, except, only, when, at most, never on production — and it is the highest-yield loss per byte in the entire dataset, because the compressor's incentive is to remove exactly the words that make a sentence longer and more conditional, and those words are the ones that make it true. The collapsed distinction is the compression of two units that differed materially into one unit that differs from both: a source that separated a rule from its narrower special case emits a single rule, and every future actor now applies the general form where the special one governed. The plausible-but-wrong restatement is the most dangerous because it is the most fluent: the compiler produces a unit that is coherent, idiomatic, consistent with its neighbors, and not what the source said, and it survives every review that reads for sense rather than for correspondence.

2.4 The test procedure. The procedure is a four-step correspondence audit, and it runs on every compilation, not on a sample. Step one: decompose the source through CIR-1 into typed units and record each unit's locator into CIR-0. Step two: decompose the artifact through the identical CIR-1 procedure — identical, because a different decomposition procedure on the two sides makes the correspondence uncomputable. Step three: compute the correspondence, producing four counts: matched units, lost units (source units with no correspondent), fabricated units (artifact units with no source correspondent), and drifted units (correspondents whose kind, scope, strength, or qualifier set differs). Step four: adjudicate every non-matched unit individually — no aggregate score is permitted to absorb them — classifying each as an admissible loss (a unit the loss budget of the artifact's declared task class explicitly permits to drop, such as a mechanically redundant restatement) or an inadmissible one. The evidence that counts is the adjudication record: a list of every non-matched unit with its locator, its kind, and its verdict. A compilation that presents a percentage without the record has produced no evidence at all, per CO-12's Telemetry-Before-Claims contract, and is treated as unmeasured -> REFUSE.

2.5 Thresholds and their bindings. Semantic fidelity is a graded dimension, and its thresholds bind to the artifact's declared task class rather than to a single universal number, because the tolerable semantic loss for a compilation feeding an exploratory read is not the tolerable loss for one feeding an execution. Three bands govern. For an artifact declared as an execution carrier — one an actor will act on without consulting the source — the threshold is zero inadmissible loss, zero fabrication, and zero drift on any unit whose kind is constraint, obligation, decision, or evidence; graded loss is permitted only on units of kind assertion, definition, or preference, and only up to the loss budget the artifact declares. For an artifact declared as a reasoning carrier — one an actor reasons with and may consult the source behind — the same zero-tolerance applies to the four load-bearing kinds, and the graded budget on the remainder widens. For an artifact declared as an orientation carrier — one an actor reads to know where to look — graded loss is wide on all kinds except constraint, but the artifact is marked as non-actionable and may not be substituted for its source in any execution path. Fabrication is zero-tolerance in all three bands without exception: a unit the source never asserted is not a compression, it is an invention, and no task class has a budget for inventions.

2.6 Failure modes and countermeasures. The signature failure of this dimension is the fluent falsehood: an artifact that passes every reading-based review because it is coherent, and fails the correspondence audit because its coherence was purchased by smoothing away the conditions that made the source's claims true. Its countermeasure is structural, not exhortative — the audit is defined over units and forbids reading the artifact as prose, so fluency has no channel through which to influence the verdict. The second failure is procedural asymmetry: an auditor who decomposes the source carefully and the artifact loosely, producing spurious matches; the countermeasure is that both sides run the identical CIR-1 procedure and the decomposition of each is itself recorded and re-runnable, so a divergence in procedure is detectable as a divergence in unit counts on a re-run. The third is budget laundering: an adjudicator who classifies an inadmissible loss as admissible by reclassifying the unit's kind — a constraint recorded as a preference, and therefore droppable. The countermeasure is that the kind is assigned by CIR-1 at the source side before the artifact exists, and the adjudicator may not re-type a source unit; a proposed re-typing is an appeal, is logged, and is decided against the DAIF-01 Strength field, not against the adjudicator's convenience.

2.7 Metrics, countermetrics, and adversarial cases. The primary metrics are unit correspondence rate, inadmissible-loss count, fabrication count, and kind-drift count, and each is reported as an absolute, never as a ratio, because a ratio is satisfied by inflating its denominator — a compiler that emits many trivial units raises its correspondence rate without preserving one additional constraint. The countermetric to correspondence rate is load-bearing-unit survival, computed only over units of kind constraint, obligation, decision, and evidence: a compiler optimizing correspondence by preserving cheap units and dropping expensive ones drives the primary metric up and the countermetric down, and the divergence is the alarm. The countermetric to compression ratio is qualifier survival: the count of scope-bearing tokens present in the source's units that survive into the artifact's, which falls precisely when a compressor is buying its ratio by erasing conditions. The adversarial input against this dimension is a source whose critical qualifier is expressed structurally rather than lexically — a condition established in one unit and relied upon silently by three later ones — so that no single unit visibly carries it and any per-unit audit passes while the compiled composition is false. The defense is that CIR-1's boundary discipline forbids a unit that depends on an unstated condition: a unit whose meaning changes when read alone is not atomic, and the correspondence audit is run over atomic units precisely so that a structurally-carried qualifier has been made lexical before the audit begins.

PART II FINAL LAW. Semantic fidelity is the preservation of assertional content — same claims, same scope, same qualifiers, same distinctions, no additions — and it is measured as a correspondence over CIR-1 typed units and never as similarity between prose, because the four traps of paraphrase drift, the erased qualifier, the collapsed distinction, and the plausible-but-wrong restatement are all invisible to any measure that reads the smoothed output as text; loss is adjudicated unit by unit with a locator and a verdict, fabrication is zero-tolerance in every task class, correspondence is reported as an absolute against a load-bearing-unit-survival countermetric so a compiler cannot buy its score with cheap units, and an unmeasured correspondence is a failed one.

PART III — INTENT FIDELITY AND CONSTRAINT FIDELITY

3.1 Mission. This Part gates the two dimensions that determine whether a compiled artifact can be acted upon at all: whether it still knows what was wanted, and whether it still knows what may never be done. They are paired because they are the positive and negative faces of the same boundary. Intent fidelity preserves the purpose that makes a result right; constraint fidelity preserves the prohibitions that make a result permissible. An artifact that loses intent produces work that is conformant and pointless. An artifact that loses a constraint produces work that is conformant, purposeful, and forbidden. The two dimensions differ radically in their scoring discipline — intent is graded and constraint is binary — and the reason for that asymmetry is the load-bearing argument of this Part.

3.2 Intent fidelity: the definition. Intent fidelity asks whether the artifact preserves what was actually wanted, and it is measured against CIR-3, which holds intent as a structured object: the original intention, the expected result, the explicit criteria, the implicit criteria, the priorities, the preferences, the prohibitions, the non-goals, and the definition of done. Intent fidelity is the survival of that structure, slot by slot. The explicit criteria are the easy half and are audited by correspondence exactly as semantic units are. The hard half is everything the source did not say aloud: the implicit criteria it assumed everyone shared, the non-goals it never thought to name because no one in the room would have proposed them, and the priorities it expressed by emphasis rather than by ranking. These are the slots a compressor deletes first, because they have the weakest surface presence in the source and the highest cost to lose, and they are the slots CIR-3 exists to have already made explicit through one_shot's Q&A scaffold. Intent fidelity therefore has a precondition: an artifact compiled from a source whose intent was never surfaced cannot be audited for intent fidelity, and the honest verdict in that case is not PASS but UNMEASURED -> DEGRADE, with the artifact marked as unsafe for any task whose correctness depends on purpose rather than on instruction.

3.3 The failure that defines intent fidelity: the technically-conformant miss. The signature failure is a result that satisfies every literal word of the request and violates its purpose. Its anatomy is always the same: the instruction said do X, the intent said X was wanted in order to achieve Y, the compilation kept X and dropped Y, and an actor consuming the artifact did X in a manner that defeated Y — efficiently, confidently, and with a clean checklist. The reason this failure is a fidelity failure and not merely a judgment failure is that the actor had no channel through which to detect it: with Y absent from the artifact, no amount of care would have surfaced the conflict, because the artifact contained no representation of the standard the result was violating. The countermeasure is structural and is the whole reason CIR-3 holds intent separately from the instructions it motivates: an artifact that carries the intention, the non-goals, and the definition of done gives the actor a second, higher check — does this result serve the purpose, not merely execute the instruction — and an artifact that carries only instructions gives the actor nothing but the checklist that the failure passes. The intent-fidelity test is consequently a divergence probe: for each preserved instruction, can the artifact still detect a result that satisfies the instruction and defeats its intention. If it cannot, the intent slot that would have detected it was lost.

3.4 The intent test procedure and thresholds. The procedure runs in three steps. Step one: enumerate the source's CIR-3 slots and mark each as present, absent-in-source, or lost-in-compilation — the distinction between the second and third is critical, because an intent the source never surfaced is a gap in capture and an intent the source surfaced and the compilation dropped is a gap in fidelity, and conflating them lets a compiler hide its losses inside the source's silences. Step two: for every preserved instruction in the artifact, construct at least one conformant-but-purpose-defeating result and check whether the artifact's retained intent slots reject it; a slot set that accepts the defeating result has lost the criterion that would have rejected it. Step three: check the definition of done against the intention — a done-condition that can be satisfied without achieving the intention is a done-condition that was compiled from the instruction rather than from the purpose. Thresholds: the intention, the expected result, and the definition of done are required and their loss is an automatic REFUSE, because those three are the slots CIR-3 itself declares mandatory. Prohibitions and non-goals are treated as constraints and inherit the binary discipline of the next section. Explicit criteria are graded with a high floor. Implicit criteria are graded, and their loss triggers DEGRADE rather than REFUSE, with the artifact marked as instruction-safe and purpose-unsafe.

3.5 Constraint fidelity is binary, and the reason is not severity — it is coherence. Constraint fidelity asks whether all hard constraints in the source survive into the artifact, and it composes CIR-3's prohibitions together with DAIF-01's Strength field, which is the estate's sealed authority on whether a rule is hard, firm, or advisory. The scoring rule is stark: one hundred percent survival, or the compilation is rejected. There is no partial credit, no weighting by constraint importance, and no threshold below one. The naive objection is that this is merely a severity judgment expressed as an absolute, and that a 95% threshold would be a reasonable engineering compromise. It is not, and the reason is that a graded threshold on hard constraints is not strict — it is incoherent. A hard constraint is, by the definition DAIF-01 seals, a rule with no admissible exception within its scope: its entire semantic content is the claim that no action may cross this boundary. A rule preserved in 95% of the artifact's scope is a rule with a 5% exception, and a rule with an exception is not the rule that was written — it is a different, weaker rule that the compiler invented and the source never authorized. The 95%-preserved hard constraint is therefore not a partially-preserved hard constraint; it is a fully-preserved soft constraint substituted for a hard one, which is a fabrication under Part II's zero-tolerance rule, not a graded loss. Grading a binary field is a category error, and the category error is the mechanism by which every hard rule in the estate could be quietly downgraded one compilation at a time.

3.6 The constraint test procedure. The procedure is an enumeration, not a sample, and it is the cheapest test in this dataset because constraints are few, short, and typed. Step one: extract from the source every unit whose DAIF-01 kind is constraint or exception and whose Strength field marks it hard or firm, producing the constraint register — a finite, enumerable list with a locator per entry. Step two: for each register entry, locate its correspondent in the artifact and verify four properties: the prohibition survives, its scope survives unwidened and unnarrowed, its condition survives (the "when Y" that bounds the "never X"), and its Strength survives unweakened. Step three: verify that the artifact introduces no constraint the source did not carry, because a fabricated prohibition is as damaging as a lost one — it blocks work the source permitted and it will be obeyed with the same confidence. Step four: emit the constraint ledger — every register entry with a PRESERVED, LOST, WIDENED, NARROWED, WEAKENED, or FABRICATED verdict. Any verdict other than PRESERVED on any entry -> REFUSE. The ledger is the artifact's admission ticket; a compilation shipped without one has not been tested, and by the unmeasured-is-failed rule it does not ship.

3.7 Failure modes, adversarial cases, and metrics. The signature failure of constraint fidelity is the severed condition, inherited directly from CIR-1's boundary discipline: a constraint reading never do X when Y holds is compiled into a bald never do X, and the artifact now enforces a prohibition the source never wrote — an over-broad rule that will block legitimate work until someone, under pressure, discovers it is wrong and deletes the whole thing, taking the real constraint with it. Its mirror is the widened exception: the same severance in the other direction, yielding do X freely with the condition lost, which is the loss that produces incidents. The adversarial case is the constraint expressed as a negative preference — a source that says the estate strongly prefers never to do X — where the lexical form is advisory and the Strength field, correctly assigned, is hard; a compiler reading surface language rather than the typed field will compress it as a preference and lose it under the graded budget. The defense is that the constraint register is built from the Strength field and never from the prose, which is precisely why DAIF-01 made Strength a field rather than a matter of tone. The metrics are absolute counts — constraints registered, preserved, lost, widened, weakened, fabricated — and the countermetric to constraint-preservation is register completeness, measured by re-extracting the register with an independent procedure and comparing counts, because the way to score a perfect one hundred percent on constraint preservation without preserving anything is to build a register that omits the constraints you were going to lose. A gate that never registers a rule can never be observed to lose it.

PART III FINAL LAW. Intent fidelity preserves what was actually wanted — intention, expected result, explicit and implicit criteria, priorities, prohibitions, non-goals, and a definition of done that checks the purpose rather than the checklist — and its signature failure is the technically-conformant miss, a result satisfying every literal word while defeating the reason the words were written, detectable only because CIR-3 holds intent apart from the instructions it motivates; constraint fidelity preserves every hard constraint with its scope, its condition, and its Strength intact, and it is binary not because constraints are severe but because a graded threshold on a hard rule is incoherent — a ninety-five-percent-preserved hard constraint is a soft constraint the compiler fabricated and the source never authorized — so the constraint ledger admits only PRESERVED, and any other verdict on any entry is a REFUSE.

PART IV — DECISION FIDELITY AND EPISTEMIC FIDELITY

4.1 Mission. This Part gates the two dimensions that determine whether a compiled artifact can be reasoned with rather than merely obeyed. Decision fidelity preserves the estate's choices in a form that can be revisited, defended, and reversed. Epistemic fidelity preserves the estate's honesty about how well it knows what it claims. They are paired because they fail together and for the same reason: both dimensions are carried by material that a compressor perceives as overhead — the justification behind a conclusion, the hedge in front of a claim — and both losses are invisible at the point of consumption, because the artifact that remains is shorter, cleaner, more confident, and wrong in a way that produces no error message.

4.2 Decision fidelity: the definition. Decision fidelity asks whether every decision in the source survives into the artifact as a complete governable object, and it is measured against CIR-4, which composes DRK's decision_record. The complete object is the question the decision resolved, the options weighed including the rejected ones, the choice taken, the justification, the evidence the justification rested on, the authority that made it, the scope it binds, the consequences and trade-offs it accepted, the revision conditions that should reopen it, its expiry, and the decisions it superseded. Decision fidelity is the survival of that anatomy, and it is graded on the peripheral slots and binary on five: question, choice, justification, authority, and scope. Those five are the slots CIR-4 declares load-bearing, and their loss is not a degradation of the decision — it is the destruction of the decision as a governable object, leaving behind an artifact that records what was done and destroys the ability to reconsider it.

4.3 The bare conclusion and why it is un-revisable. The signature failure of decision fidelity is compression to the outcome: the artifact carries that X was chosen and drops that X was chosen because evidence E held under constraint C at the cost of trade-off T, to be reopened if E changed. The compressed form is shorter by an order of magnitude and reads as complete, and it is precisely as un-reversible as a fossil. To reverse a decision safely, a later actor must be able to check whether the reason still holds — is E still true, does C still bind, is T still acceptable — and a bare conclusion answers none of those questions, so the actor faces two bad options and takes one of them. Either the actor reverses the decision blindly, re-opening whatever problem it silently solved, or the actor treats it as immovable precisely because its reasoning is invisible and nobody dares touch a rule they cannot explain, and the decision outlives its own grounding as institutional superstition. Both outcomes are produced by the same compression, and neither raises an error at the moment of loss. The countermeasure is that the decision-fidelity test is not a presence check on the conclusion but a reversibility check on the record: for each decision in the artifact, can an actor determine, from the artifact alone, what would have to become false for this decision to be wrong. A decision that cannot answer that question has lost its justification, whatever text remains beside it.

4.4 The decision test procedure and thresholds. Step one: extract the source's decision register from CIR-4 — every decision-kind unit with its slot inventory and its locator. Step two: for each entry, verify the five load-bearing slots survive with their content intact, and verify that the justification's evidence pointers still resolve: a justification whose evidence pointers point at units the compilation dropped is a justification that has been hollowed out while appearing preserved, and it is the subtlest loss in this dimension, because the prose of the justification survives and its grounding does not. Step three: verify the forward-looking slots — revision conditions and expiry — because a decision compiled without its revision conditions cannot be surfaced for reconsideration by DAIF-21 when reality moves out from under it, and a decision compiled without its expiry is silently promoted from temporary to permanent by the act of compilation. Step four: verify supersession pointers, because a lineage broken by compilation resurfaces a superseded decision as current. Thresholds: loss of any of the five load-bearing slots -> REFUSE. Loss of a revision condition or an expiry -> DEGRADE, with the artifact marked as not-reality-synchronizable. Loss of a rejected option -> graded, because the rejected options carry the decision's negative space and their loss narrows future reconsideration without falsifying the record.

4.5 Epistemic fidelity and the cardinal violation. Epistemic fidelity asks whether the artifact still distinguishes fact from inference from assumption from speculation, and it is measured against CIR-2, which composes the ACIS ladder. The test is a status-correspondence audit: for every unit in the artifact, the epistemic status it carries must be identical to the status ACIS assigned it in the source, and any divergence is scored by direction. A unit whose status was weakened by compilation is a conservative loss — it costs the estate confidence it had earned, which is wasteful and safe. A unit whose status was strengthened by compilation is the cardinal violation of this dataset, and it has a name: epistemic laundering. An assumption enters the compiler wearing a hedge and emerges wearing the confidence of a fact, and every downstream actor now acts on a guess with the certainty due a measurement. Epistemic laundering is a strict-zero-tolerance failure — one laundered unit -> REFUSE — for the same reason constraint loss is binary: the laundered unit does not degrade the artifact's epistemic quality, it inverts the artifact's epistemic meaning, converting the honest statement we believe this into the false statement we know this.

4.6 Why compression is the exact pressure that causes laundering. Laundering is not an occasional accident of compilation; it is the predicted output of any compressor that is not explicitly gated against it, and the mechanism is mechanical rather than moral. Epistemic status, in natural cognitive source, is carried almost entirely by hedges — we think, it appears, probably, in the cases we checked, assuming the earlier finding holds — and hedges have three properties that make them a compressor's first target: they add length, they add no assertional content that a naive semantic correspondence would miss, and they reduce the fluency and confidence of the resulting text, which every readability signal penalizes. A compressor optimizing for brevity and coherence will therefore delete hedges before it deletes anything else, and the deletion of a hedge is not a stylistic edit — it is a promotion up the ACIS ladder, performed silently, with no evidence, by a process that has no authority to promote anything. This is why CIR-2 requires an evidence pointer for any status above pending-verification, and why the epistemic-fidelity test does not read the artifact's prose for confidence markers at all: it reads the artifact's CIR-2 status fields and their evidence pointers, and a unit whose status rose while its evidence pointer set did not grow is convicted on arithmetic. No claim can gain certainty by being carried forward; certainty is gained only by verification, and a compiler performs none.

4.7 Failure modes, adversarial cases, metrics. Beyond laundering, three failures recur. Status flattening: a compiler that drops the status field entirely, producing an artifact of unstatused claims, which a consumer will read as uniformly true — this is laundering by omission and is scored as laundering, because the absence of a status is consumed as a fact. Evidence-pointer decay: statuses preserved while the units they point at are dropped, leaving verified claims whose verification is unreachable, which fails the provenance dimension in Part VI and is flagged here as well. Weakest-link erasure: a compilation that preserves a strong unit's status while dropping the weak unit it depended upon, breaking the CIR-7 dependency edge that CIR-2's weakest-link propagation reads, so the artifact presents as verified a claim whose only support was an assumption the compiler deleted. The adversarial case is a source whose author sincerely asserts an inference in confident prose while CIR-2, reading the evidence pointer, correctly typed it as inferred; a compiler that re-derives status from the artifact's tone rather than inheriting the source's field will "correct" the status upward and call it fidelity. The defense is that status is inherited, never re-derived — the compiler has no authority to type epistemic status, only to carry it. Metrics: laundered-unit count (absolute, target zero), unstatused-unit count (absolute, target zero), decision-slot survival per load-bearing slot, and resolvable-evidence-pointer rate. The countermetric to hedge-preservation is assertion density, because a compiler can trivially maximize hedge survival by hedging everything, producing an artifact so uniformly uncertain that no actor can distinguish the verified from the guessed — which is laundering's mirror image and is equally a loss of the distinction this dimension exists to protect.

PART IV FINAL LAW. Decision fidelity preserves every choice as a governable object — question, choice, justification, authority, scope, with resolving evidence pointers, revision conditions, expiry, and supersession — because a decision compressed to a bare conclusion is un-revisable and will be either reversed blindly or obeyed as superstition; epistemic fidelity preserves the distinction between fact, inference, assumption, and speculation by inheriting CIR-2's ACIS-assigned status rather than re-deriving it from tone, and its cardinal violation is epistemic laundering — an assumption emerging from compilation wearing the confidence of a fact — which is not an occasional accident but the predicted output of any ungated compressor, since hedges are long, assertionally invisible, and fluency-reducing, and therefore the first thing a compressor deletes; one laundered unit is a REFUSE, because certainty is gained only by verification and a compiler performs none.

PART V — OBLIGATION FIDELITY AND CAUSAL FIDELITY

5.1 Mission. This Part gates the two dimensions whose losses are the hardest to notice and therefore the most likely to accumulate. Obligation fidelity preserves work that is owed; causal fidelity preserves the mechanisms behind conclusions. Their common property is that neither loss produces a symptom at the time it happens. A dropped semantic unit can be caught by a correspondence audit; a laundered status can be caught by an arithmetic check. But a dropped obligation produces silence — the work simply never happens, and no error is ever raised, because nothing in the estate is waiting for it. And a dropped mechanism produces confidence — the conclusion survives, is repeated, and is applied long after the conditions that made it true have gone.

5.2 Obligation fidelity: the definition and the binary rule. Obligation fidelity asks whether one hundred percent of the pending work in the source survives into the artifact, and it is measured against CIR-5, which compiles obligations into typed objects, and it feeds DAIF-07, which owns the obligation lifecycle. The register is enumerable: every unit of kind obligation, together with every unresolved question in CIR-4's decision register (a deferred decision is an obligation to decide), every pending verification CIR-2 marked, every promise, deliverable, migration, follow-up, unaccepted specification, and untested function the source named. Obligation fidelity is binary — one hundred percent survival or REFUSE — and it shares the constraint dimension's binary discipline for a parallel reason. A constraint at 95% is a soft constraint the compiler fabricated. An obligation register at 95% is not a slightly-incomplete register; it is a complete-looking register that is missing exactly the entries nobody will ever look for again, and its incompleteness is undetectable from the inside, because the only artifact that recorded the missing obligation was the source the compilation replaced.

5.3 Why silent loss is the most damaging loss class. Every other fidelity failure in this dataset eventually announces itself. A lost constraint announces itself as an incident. A laundered assumption announces itself as a wrong commitment. A collapsed distinction announces itself as a misapplied rule. Each is expensive, and each is at least discoverable, because each produces an action whose consequences can be traced back. A lost obligation announces itself as nothing. The work is not done wrong; it is not done. The migration is not botched; it is never begun. The verification is not failed; it is never run, and the artifact it would have checked ships with its correctness assumed. There is no failing test, no exception, no incident report, and no trace — the obligation's only representation was in a source that has been superseded by an artifact that does not carry it, so the estate is not merely failing to do the work, it has lost the knowledge that the work exists. This is the failure class the estate has recorded most often under its own names — the scaffold that looks complete, the promise made in conversation that dies when the conversation closes, the follow-up that evaporates at session end — and it is the founding motivation of DAIF-07. Obligation fidelity is the gate that keeps DAIF-07 from being a lifecycle over an incomplete population, because a lifecycle that tracks 95% of the estate's obligations perfectly is a lifecycle whose completeness claims are false.

5.4 The obligation test procedure. Step one: extract the source's obligation register from CIR-5, with a locator per entry and, critically, each entry's closure condition — the condition whose satisfaction discharges the obligation. An obligation compiled without its closure condition enters DAIF-07's lifecycle unclosable, held open forever because nothing can ever satisfy it, and that is a fidelity failure even though the obligation itself survived. Step two: correspondence-match the artifact's register against the source's, requiring exact cardinality — every source entry has exactly one artifact correspondent. Step three: verify each correspondent's four load-bearing fields: the work owed, the closure condition, the authority who owes it, and the scope. Step four: verify no fabricated obligations — an invented obligation is not harmless, it consumes attention and will be worked, and work performed against an obligation the source never incurred is waste that looks like diligence. Step five: emit the obligation ledger. Any missing entry, any lost closure condition, any fabricated entry -> REFUSE. The countermetric to register survival is register completeness at the source side, measured by independently re-extracting the source register and comparing cardinality, because the way to achieve one hundred percent obligation survival while losing obligations is to fail to register them in the first place — a compiler cannot be observed to drop an obligation it never recognized, and the estate has recorded exactly this shape: a gate scores perfectly by never seeing the thing it was built to catch.

5.5 Causal fidelity: the definition. Causal fidelity asks whether the artifact preserves mechanisms and not merely conclusions, and it is measured against CIR-6, which holds why things happened. The distinction is precise. A conclusion is a claim about what is true or what to do. A mechanism is the account of why it is true or why to do it — the chain of cause and effect, the conditions under which the chain holds, and the reasons the chain would break. Causal fidelity requires that for every conclusion the artifact carries, the causal chain that produced it survives with its conditions attached, so that a later actor can evaluate whether the chain still runs. It is graded rather than binary, but its floor is high and its threshold binds to consequence: for a conclusion the artifact presents as actionable, the mechanism is required and its loss is a REFUSE; for a conclusion presented as background, mechanism loss is a DEGRADE that marks the conclusion non-actionable.

5.6 Cargo-cult knowledge: the failure mode of causal loss. A conclusion without its mechanism cannot be re-evaluated when conditions change, and this produces the estate's most durable pathology: knowledge that outlives its own validity. The mechanism is straightforward. A source establishes that under conditions C, doing X produces good outcome O — and the causal chain, C -> X -> O, is what makes the guidance true. A compilation keeps do X and drops the chain. Conditions change; C no longer holds; and the estate keeps doing X, because the artifact says to do X and contains no representation of the condition that would tell anyone to stop. The practice persists, is defended, is taught to new actors, and is enforced by gates, long after the reason for it has evaporated — and because the reason is absent from the artifact, nobody can even formulate the argument against it. This is cargo-cult knowledge: a ritual executed faithfully with its purpose forgotten, and it is generated not by stupidity but by lossy compilation, because the mechanism is the longest and most abstract part of any source and the conclusion is the shortest and most quotable. The countermeasure is that the causal-fidelity test is a falsifiability probe: for each actionable conclusion in the artifact, can a reader determine from the artifact alone what would have to change for the conclusion to stop being true. If the artifact cannot answer that, the mechanism was lost, whatever confident text surrounds the conclusion.

5.7 Adversarial cases, cross-links, and metrics. The adversarial input against obligation fidelity is the buried obligation: a commitment phrased as an aside inside an explanatory passage — we should probably check that later — whose surface form gives no signal that real work was promised, and which a compressor discards as conversational filler with perfect confidence. The defense is upstream, at CIR-1's boundary discipline, which is required to cut a commitment out as its own obligation-typed unit precisely so the fidelity gate has something to count; an obligation the fabric never made atomic is an obligation this gate cannot protect, which is why the source-side register-completeness countermetric is not optional. The adversarial input against causal fidelity is the mechanism expressed as an anecdote — the reason for a rule carried entirely in the incident that produced it, which a compressor reads as narrative color and strips as redundant — and the defense is that UKDL's own rule anatomy makes the origin incident a required field, so a compiled law whose ORIGEN is empty has failed causal fidelity by inspection, without any reading of the prose. Metrics: obligation-register survival (absolute count, target exact equality), closure-condition survival (absolute, target exact equality), fabricated-obligation count (target zero), actionable-conclusion mechanism-survival rate, and falsifiability-probe pass rate. The countermetric to mechanism-survival is mechanism specificity: a compiler can trivially preserve a mechanism by emitting a vacuous one — because it works, because the evidence supports it — and a chain whose conditions cannot be falsified is not a mechanism, it is a decoration that satisfies the metric while providing exactly nothing the metric was built to protect.

PART V FINAL LAW. Obligation fidelity requires that one hundred percent of pending work survive with its closure condition, its authority, and its scope, and it is binary because a 95%-complete obligation register is a complete-looking register missing precisely the entries nobody will ever look for again — the most damaging loss class in the estate, since a dropped obligation raises no error, produces no incident, and destroys even the knowledge that the work exists; causal fidelity requires that mechanisms survive alongside conclusions, because a conclusion without its chain cannot be re-evaluated when conditions change and becomes cargo-cult knowledge — a ritual executed faithfully with its purpose forgotten, enforced by gates long after its reason evaporated — and the test for both is not presence but function: an obligation must be closable, and an actionable conclusion must be falsifiable, from the artifact alone.

PART VI — EVALUATION FIDELITY AND PROVENANCE FIDELITY

6.1 Mission. This Part gates the two dimensions that make a compiled artifact accountable to reality rather than merely internally consistent. Evaluation fidelity preserves the artifact's knowledge of how to check that execution was correct. Provenance fidelity preserves the artifact's path back to the sources that grounded it. They are paired because they are the two directions of the same accountability: evaluation faces forward, letting an actor verify a result against the world; provenance faces backward, letting an actor re-verify a claim against its origin. An artifact that loses both is a closed system — internally coherent, externally unanchored, and impossible to correct, because it can neither be checked against what it produces nor traced to what produced it. Such an artifact is not knowledge; it is doctrine.

6.2 Evaluation fidelity: the definition. Evaluation fidelity asks whether the artifact retains how to know that execution worked, and it is measured against CIR-9, which composes SQI's evaluation verdicts and its verification axis. The unit of preservation is the check: for every instruction, decision, or obligation the artifact carries as actionable, there must survive an associated evaluation — the observable signal that distinguishes a correct execution from an incorrect one, the procedure that produces that signal, and the threshold that adjudicates it. A check is composed of three parts and all three are load-bearing. The signal without the procedure is a wish (we will know it works when it works). The procedure without the threshold is a measurement with no verdict (we ran the suite; nobody said what passing means). The threshold without the signal is a number attached to nothing. Evaluation fidelity requires all three per actionable unit, and its failure is scored per unit, not in aggregate.

6.3 An unverifiable instruction is a hypothesis executed as a plan. This is the load-bearing argument of the evaluation dimension, and it is stated as an identity rather than a warning. Knowledge that says what to do but not how to know it worked has not been reduced in quality — it has been reclassified. A do-this whose correctness cannot be checked is, epistemically, a proposal that doing this will work; the estate cannot distinguish it from a guess, because the only thing that would distinguish it is the check, and the check is gone. Executing it is therefore not the execution of a plan but the execution of a hypothesis under the costume of a plan, and the estate has recorded the consequence of this repeatedly: work declared done because it compiled, systems declared shipped because the files existed, gates declared active because they were written. The compression that produces this is seductive, because the check is the longest and least interesting part of any instruction — the instruction is a sentence and the verification is a paragraph — and a compressor keeping the sentence and dropping the paragraph believes it has kept the important half. It has kept the half that generates action and dropped the half that makes the action accountable, which is exactly the trade the estate can least afford. Evaluation fidelity's test is therefore a done-gate probe: for each actionable unit in the artifact, can a consumer determine, from the artifact alone, what observation would prove the execution correct and what observation would prove it wrong. A unit for which both answers cannot be produced is unverifiable -> REFUSE for any execution-carrier artifact.

6.4 The evaluation test procedure and thresholds. Step one: enumerate the artifact's actionable units — every instruction, obligation, decision-with-consequence, and procedural unit that an actor could execute. Step two: for each, locate the surviving check and verify its three parts (signal, procedure, threshold) against the source's CIR-9 record. Step three: run the falsifiability half of the probe — for each check, name at least one observation that would fail it; a check with no failing observation is vacuous and is scored as absent, because a gate that cannot refuse is indistinguishable from an inert one, which is SQI's own law and applies to compiled checks exactly as it applies to live ones. Step four: verify the check's reality-supremacy: the surviving check must be an observation of the world, not an assertion about it — a check that reads a compiler's own claim that the work is done is not a check, and evaluation fidelity rejects any surviving check whose signal is internal to the artifact. Thresholds: for an execution-carrier artifact, every actionable unit requires a complete, falsifiable, reality-anchored check -> otherwise REFUSE. For a reasoning-carrier artifact, check loss is a DEGRADE that marks the affected units as non-executable. Aggregate check-survival rates are recorded but never adjudicate: a 90% check-survival rate means ten percent of the artifact's actions cannot be verified, and those are the actions that will fail silently.

6.5 Provenance fidelity: the definition. Provenance fidelity asks whether the path from any claim in the artifact back to the source that grounded it can be reconstructed, and it is measured against CIR-10's provenance and chain-of-custody fields together with GK-04's lineage edges, which are the estate's sealed authority on lineage and which this dataset references rather than forks. The requirement is an unbroken chain: every unit in the compiled artifact carries a provenance pointer, every pointer resolves to a real unit at the stage below, and the chain terminates in a CIR-0 locator identifying the exact normalized span of the original source. Provenance fidelity is the property that this chain survives compilation intact, and it is tested by resolution rather than by inspection — a pointer that exists and does not resolve is worse than an absent one, because it presents as lineage and delivers none.

6.6 A claim whose source cannot be recovered becomes an article of faith. The reason provenance is a fidelity dimension and not a bookkeeping nicety is that reality moves. A claim that was true when it was compiled may be false now — the system it described was refactored, the constraint it recorded was lifted, the measurement it cited was superseded — and the only way the estate can ever discover this is by walking the chain back to the source and re-checking it against the world. That is precisely what DAIF-21's reality synchronization does, and it can do it only for claims whose provenance survived. A claim with a broken chain cannot be re-verified when reality changes; it can only be believed or discarded wholesale, and since it is embedded in a coherent, well-formed artifact alongside claims that are still true, it will be believed. The estate then holds, inside a system that is supposed to hold only evidence, an article of faith: a proposition it cannot check, cannot date, cannot attribute, and cannot correct, which will be defended with the confidence of its container. Provenance loss is therefore the mechanism by which a knowledge system slowly converts into a belief system, one unresolvable pointer at a time, and it is the loss most likely to be dismissed as harmless at the moment it occurs, because a broken pointer costs nothing today.

6.7 Test procedure, failure modes, adversarial cases, metrics. The provenance procedure is a resolution walk: for every unit in the artifact, follow the provenance chain down through the CIR stack to a CIR-0 locator, and verify at each hop that the target exists, that its content hash matches the hash recorded at compile time, and that the hop's lineage edge is present in GK-04. Three verdicts: RESOLVED, BROKEN (a hop does not resolve), and STALE (a hop resolves but the target's hash has changed since compilation, meaning the source moved under the artifact — a DAIF-21 trigger, not a compiler failure). Thresholds: any BROKEN chain on a unit of kind constraint, decision, evidence, or obligation -> REFUSE; BROKEN chains on other kinds -> DEGRADE with the affected units marked unverifiable. STALE chains are never a fidelity failure — they are the system working — and misclassifying them as failures would train the estate to suppress the very signal DAIF-21 depends on. The signature failure is provenance-by-attribution: an artifact whose units cite a source by name rather than by locator, which reads like lineage and cannot be walked, and whose only function is to make an unsourceable claim feel sourced. The adversarial case is the laundered chain: a compilation that generates fresh provenance pointers to itself, so every unit resolves — to the artifact, not to the source — producing a perfectly self-consistent lineage graph with zero connection to reality. The defense is that a chain is valid only if it terminates in a CIR-0 locator, and CIR-0 units are the only ones the fabric permits to have no parent; a chain that terminates anywhere else is BROKEN by definition regardless of how cleanly it resolves. Metrics: chain-resolution rate by unit kind (absolute counts, never a pooled percentage), CIR-0-termination rate, check-completeness per actionable unit, and falsifiable-check rate. The countermetric to chain-resolution is chain depth-and-distinctness — a compiler can drive resolution to one hundred percent by pointing every unit at the same single source span, and a lineage graph whose fan-in has collapsed to one node has preserved the form of provenance while destroying its information entirely.

PART VI FINAL LAW. Evaluation fidelity requires that every actionable unit retain a complete check — signal, procedure, and threshold — that is falsifiable and anchored in an observation of the world rather than in the artifact's own assertion, because an instruction whose correctness cannot be checked is not a plan being executed but a hypothesis being executed as one; provenance fidelity requires that every unit's chain of custody survive as resolvable pointers terminating in a CIR-0 locator, walked and hash-verified rather than inspected, because a claim whose source cannot be recovered can never be re-verified when reality moves and therefore becomes an article of faith held by a system that is permitted to hold only evidence — and a system that accumulates such claims has quietly converted itself from a knowledge system into a belief system, one unresolvable pointer at a time.

PART VII — BEHAVIORAL FIDELITY: THE DEFINITIVE TEST

7.1 Mission. Behavioral fidelity is the only dimension that tests what the estate actually cares about, and it subsumes the other nine. It asks one question: does an actor consuming the compiled artifact take the same correct decisions as an actor with access to the full source. Not similar decisions — the same correct ones, under the same conditions, with the same constraints honored, the same obligations discharged, and the same errors avoided. Every other dimension in this dataset exists because this question is expensive to ask and slow to answer, and the estate needed cheap screens that could reject a bad compilation before paying for a behavioral trial. But the screens are proxies and this is the criterion. A compilation that passes all nine proxies and fails behaviorally has failed, and the nine proxies were wrong about it; a compilation that fails a binary proxy is unsafe and does not reach a behavioral trial at all. Behavior is what the compiled artifact is for, and behavior is what it is judged on.

7.2 The definition, stated to be testable. Behavioral fidelity is a property of a pair — an artifact and a task class — and never of an artifact alone. The claim "this compilation is behaviorally faithful" is meaningless without the class of missions it is faithful for, because an artifact that fully supports a diagnostic mission may be catastrophically insufficient for an execution mission over the same subject matter. The testable form is therefore: for the mission class M, an actor equipped with artifact A produces outcomes indistinguishable-or-better than an actor equipped with source set S, across the measured dimensions, on a representative sample of missions drawn from M. Three commitments are embedded in that sentence and each is load-bearing. Indistinguishable-or-better, not merely acceptable — the standard is the source, not a floor. Across the measured dimensions, plural — a single quality score would hide the trade the compilation actually made. On a representative sample — because a compilation tuned on a mission it was shown will pass that mission and fail its siblings, and the sample must be drawn before the compilation is built, never after its weaknesses are known.

7.3 The trial: control arm and compiled arm. The test is a controlled comparison and it is run as one. Step one: select representative missions from the declared class — real missions the estate actually runs, not synthesized ones, because a synthesized mission is a hypothesis about what the estate does and inherits every bias of the person who wrote it. The sample must include at least one mission that exercises each load-bearing unit kind the artifact claims to carry: one constrained by a hard rule, one that must discharge an obligation, one that must revisit a decision, one that must distinguish a verified claim from an assumed one. Step two: run the CONTROL arm — an actor given the full source set S and the mission. Step three: run the COMPILED arm — an actor given the Mission Pack that DAIF-08 assembles from artifact A, and the same mission, with no access to S. The two arms must be otherwise identical: same model, same tooling, same permissions, same starting state. Step four: compare. The comparison is a vector, not a scalar, and its components are the behaviors the estate cares about: decision correctness on the mission's decision points, hard constraints violated, obligations dropped, errors raised, rework performed, tool calls issued, re-reads of source material attempted (a signal that the artifact was insufficient and the actor went looking for what it lacked), and final output quality adjudicated against the mission's own done-gate.

7.4 The acceptance rule and its asymmetries. A compilation ships only if it preserves or improves the relevant behavior, and the rule is applied per component with different disciplines. On the safety components — hard constraints violated, obligations dropped — the compiled arm must be exactly equal to or better than the control, and any regression, even one, is a REFUSE: an artifact that causes a single constraint violation the source would have prevented has produced the exact failure this dataset exists to prevent, and no efficiency gain purchases it. On the correctness components — decision correctness, final output quality — the compiled arm must be equal or better within a declared tolerance that the artifact states in advance and that the mission class governs. On the efficiency components — tool calls, re-reads, rework, latency — the compiled arm is expected to be better, and this is the compilation's entire economic justification; but efficiency is never permitted to compensate a safety or correctness regression, and the scoring model forbids the trade structurally by refusing to combine the components into a single number. A compiled arm that is forty percent faster and violates one constraint is not a net win; it is a REFUSE with a fast failure.

7.5 Composing FD-04's six-lens transfer test. The estate already owns a behavioral-equivalence instrument, and DAIF-03 composes it rather than forking it: FD-04's six-lens transfer test is the sealed procedure for asking whether a capability survives a transfer, and a compilation is a transfer — from a source substrate to a compiled one — in precisely the sense FD-04 already tests. Its six lenses are applied to the arms of the trial as the adjudication instrument, and its discipline of proving portability rather than asserting it is inherited whole. The composition is load-bearing rather than ornamental: FD-04 already knows that a capability which appears to survive a transfer often survives only in the cases the tester happened to try, and its remedy — the prover that probes rather than presumes — is exactly the discipline a behavioral trial requires, since a two-mission trial that passes proves that two missions passed and nothing more. Where DAIF-03 adds to FD-04 is the object of the transfer: FD-04 tests capability transfer across models, and DAIF-03 tests behavior preservation across representations, but the instrument, the proof discipline, and the refusal to accept an assertion in place of a probe are FD-04's and are referenced as such.

7.6 Why the other nine are proxies, and why they are kept. A compilation can pass all nine screening dimensions and fail behaviorally, and the mechanisms are three. Salience failure: every unit survives, and none of them is findable at the moment of need — a Mission Pack that carries a hard constraint on page nine of a dense artifact has preserved the constraint and lost the behavior, because the actor did not encounter it before acting. Composition failure: every unit survives individually and their interaction is destroyed — two constraints that jointly forbade an action are preserved as two constraints that each permit it, because the unit that stated their conjunction was mechanically redundant and was compressed away. Load failure: every unit survives and the artifact is so dense that the actor's attention is exhausted before the mission's decision points arrive, which is a compression that preserved everything and degraded behavior anyway. None of the nine screens sees any of these, because all three are properties of the artifact in use rather than of the artifact in isolation. The nine are kept regardless, and for a hard economic reason: a behavioral trial costs two full mission runs per mission in the sample, and the estate cannot afford to run one for every candidate compilation. The nine screens reject the great majority of bad compilations at a fraction of the cost, and every binary screen — constraint, obligation, epistemic laundering — rejects an unsafe compilation without a trial at all. The ladder is: nine cheap screens reject the unsafe and the obviously lossy -> the survivors face one expensive behavioral trial -> only a trial-passing compilation is trusted as a substitute for its source.

7.7 Metrics, countermetrics, adversarial cases, and the standing law. The primary metrics are the comparison vector's components, each reported as an absolute difference between arms, never pooled: delta-decision-correctness, delta-constraints-violated, delta-obligations-dropped, delta-errors, delta-rework, delta-tool-calls, delta-source-re-reads, delta-output-quality. The governing countermetric is the source re-read rate: an artifact whose actors constantly go back to the source has not failed the correctness comparison and has failed as a compilation, because it is not a substitute — it is an index — and reporting it alongside the quality delta prevents a compilation from claiming victory while its consumers quietly do the work of decompiling it. The second countermetric is mission-sample diversity, because the sharpest way to game a behavioral trial is to shrink its denominator: a compilation evaluated on three easy missions will pass, and the estate must therefore measure and publish the sample's coverage of load-bearing unit kinds alongside the pass rate. The adversarial case is the compilation built by an actor who has seen the trial's missions, which will pass them and fail everything else — the defense is that mission samples are drawn before the compilation is built and re-drawn for every re-compilation, and a compilation evaluated against a sample it was tuned on is scored UNMEASURED, not PASS. All of this is governed by CO-12's Telemetry-Before-Claims contract: no behavioral-fidelity claim exists without the trial record that produced it, no trial record exists without both arms, and an artifact that ships with a fidelity claim and no trial has made an assertion where the estate permits only evidence.

PART VII FINAL LAW. Behavioral fidelity is the definitive test and the only one that measures what the compiled artifact is for: an actor equipped with the compiled Mission Pack must take the same correct decisions, honor the same hard constraints, and discharge the same obligations as an actor equipped with the full source, proven by a controlled two-arm trial over a representative mission sample drawn before compilation, adjudicated through FD-04's six-lens transfer test, and accepted only when the compiled arm preserves or improves the relevant behavior — with any safety regression an automatic REFUSE that no efficiency gain may purchase; the other nine dimensions are instrumental proxies, cheap enough to screen every candidate and blind to salience, composition, and load failures, which is why a compilation may pass all nine and still be unfit, and why nothing is trusted as a substitute for its source until behavior, not structure, has been observed to survive.
