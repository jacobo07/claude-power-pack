===============================================================================================
PART I — THE FOUNDING GAP: A TRIBUNAL WITHOUT A LEGISLATURE
===============================================================================================

1.1 The gap, stated precisely against the estate as it stands. The Claude Power Pack estate, as
OBSERVED against the repatriated corpus on 2026-07-21, is exceptionally well-equipped to JUDGE a law
and entirely unequipped to WRITE one. SQI and ACIS form a court with rules of standing and an E0-E7
admissibility ladder; DRK routes a decision under whatever rule governs it; UKDL is the statute book
where an adopted rule is stored; CEPS is the docket recording the events a law governs. (OBSERVED: four
distinct systems, each acting on a law that already exists.) What no system holds is the drafting: the
induction, from the estate's accumulated evidence, of a general rule the estate did not previously
have. The Tribunal's grep made the absence exact — one corpus-wide hit for the generation vocabulary,
and that hit was the proposal naming itself. The estate is a courthouse with no legislature.

1.2 Why the gap has stayed invisible. Candidate laws have always been written — the estate has 156+
Hard Rules, a UKDL full of adopted rules, a doctrine that grows every session. But every one of them
was drafted by the Owner or a frontier session, by hand, from intuition sharpened by a bug or a
pattern noticed. (OBSERVED: the Bug->Hard-Rule protocol is a human noticing a recurrence and writing a
stop.) That drafting is a real cognitive act, and like the education gap of A1's Part I, it has stayed
invisible because a competent author absorbed its cost silently every time. The gap becomes visible
only at scale: when the estate's evidence ledgers hold thousands of deposits, proofs, rules, and
events, the laws they collectively support outrun what any hand-drafting session can notice, and the
un-induced laws — the general rules the evidence supports but no one has articulated — accumulate as
latent, unwritten legislation.

1.3 The asymmetry that makes this the interesting kind of gap. A missing judge lets a bad law stand; a
missing legislature lets a good law go unwritten. The first failure is loud — a bad law causes visible
harm. The second is silent — the estate simply never benefits from a rule its own evidence would have
supported, and no one can point to the absence because you cannot see a law that was never proposed.
(DERIVED: this is the generative twin of the enrollment defect — an un-induced law is invisible for
the same reason an unenrolled registry entry is: absence leaves no error, only a benefit never
collected.) A2's value is therefore not correcting bad laws — ACIS does that — but surfacing the good
laws latent in evidence that would otherwise stay unwritten, which is a value the estate cannot measure
by what breaks, only by what it was missing.

1.4 What A2 is, in one sentence, and what it is deliberately not. A2 is the estate's legislature: the
system that induces candidate laws from accumulated evidence, attaches a falsifier and a boundary to
each, and offers them for judgment and adoption — never adopting them itself. (DEFINED.) It is not a
judge — leveling and falsifying an adopted theorem is ACIS's object. It is not a router, a store, or a
recorder. And it is emphatically not an adopter: the propose-never-apply invariant (Part XII) binds A2
absolutely, because a legislature that enacted its own bills without a separate adoption act would be
the self-modifying capture risk the estate's doctrine names as its deepest governance danger.

1.5 The three evidence sources A2 legislates from, named now and formalized in Part IV. A2 induces
candidate laws from the estate's own accumulated evidence: FD-07 distillation deposits (what the estate
has learned), fd_04 portability proofs and the UKDL rule corpus (what it has already adopted, mined for
the meta-rules beneath them), and CEPS event history plus IAS-D2 incidents (what has happened,
repeatedly). (DEFINED, three evidence families.) The generative act reads across these for a RECURRENCE
that no adopted rule yet governs — a pattern in the evidence that, generalized, is a candidate law. A2
adds to intuition-drafting exactly what intuition cannot scale to: an exhaustive, evidence-grounded
sweep for the laws latent in a corpus too large for any session to hold at once.

1.6 The standard A2 inherits and does not rebuild. A candidate law, like any claim, is void if it
cannot be refuted. A2 inherits ACIS's falsifier standard wholesale (cited, not restated): a falsifier
names a measurement, a signal, and a window, and "the law is false if reasoning doesn't compress" is
not a falsifier. (NORMATIVE, inherited.) A2 attaches this falsifier at the moment of PROPOSAL — a
candidate law with no concrete refutation condition is not proposed at all — where ACIS enforces the
same standard at the moment of JUDGMENT. The two systems apply one standard at two different points in
a law's life, which is exactly why they are distinct rather than redundant: the falsifier at proposal
is A2's gate on what may be offered; the falsifier at judgment is ACIS's gate on what may be adopted.

1.7 The founding law. An estate that can judge a law but not write one waits for its legislation to
arrive from a human's intuition, and the general rules its own evidence supports but no session has
noticed stay unwritten forever. The purpose of A2 is to induce those rules from evidence and offer them
for judgment, so that the estate's statute book grows from what its evidence supports and not only from
what a session happened to notice.

1.8 A latent law traced through real evidence, so the gap is concrete. Consider the estate's own
accumulated evidence about Windows execution: dozens of CEPS events and NEVER_AGAIN entries recording a
Bash-bridge hang, a spread of Hard Rules each stopping one instance (git-via-Bash, python-via-Bash,
tail-pipe-via-Bash), and FD deposits on the MSYS2 transport. (OBSERVED: the evidence is abundant and
distributed across three ledgers.) Each Hard Rule is a law about ONE trigger; no adopted rule states
the GENERAL law the evidence collectively supports — "on a Windows host, any Bash invocation of an
external toolchain risks the transport hang, so the default execution path for external tools is
PowerShell." That general rule is latent in the evidence, supported by every instance, and was written
only piecemeal, one trigger at a time, as each instance surfaced. (DERIVED: A2's induction over the
CEPS-plus-HardRule-plus-FD evidence would surface the general law from the recurrence — not a new
observation, but the generalization the scattered instances already support — and offer it for
judgment, where today it exists only as a pattern a human eventually noticed rule-by-rule.) The trace
makes the gap concrete: the estate had all the evidence for the general law and no mechanism to induce
it, so the law arrived slowly, in fragments, from intuition, instead of at once from its own evidence.

FINAL LAW — PART I. The estate is a courthouse without a legislature — it can level, falsify, route,
store, and record a law, and it cannot draft one, so its legislation waits on a human's intuition while
the rules its own evidence supports go unwritten. A2 is the object that induces candidate laws from
evidence and offers them for judgment, never adopting them itself — because a good law unwritten is a
silent loss no error announces, and a legislature that enacted its own bills would be the capture the
estate's deepest doctrine forbids.

===============================================================================================
PART II — WHY "GENERATION" AND NOT JUDGMENT, ROUTING, STORAGE, OR RECORDING
===============================================================================================

2.1 The boundary discipline this Part enforces. A2 acts on the same object — a law — that four estate
systems already act on, and under the CPCSC audit's governing rule (PR-CORPUS-AUDIT-CONTENT-TIER-001,
no ownership claimed or ceded on vocabulary matching), A2 must name, for each, the sibling that owns
the law in its other relation and the specific act that separates A2's generation from the sibling's.
(NORMATIVE: this Part is the Article II test applied four times, pre-emptively — and the sharpest
boundary in the dataset, JUDGE-versus-PROPOSE, is drawn here and restated wherever a law's lifecycle
recurs, because it is the one a careless reader will collapse.)

2.2 Judgment — ACIS's relation to a law versus A2's. ACIS takes a law that EXISTS and asks whether it
is admissible: it levels the theorem on the E0-E7 ladder and enforces the falsifier gate — "a theorem
without a concrete, observable refutation condition is not a weak theorem, it is not a theorem at all"
(cited from acis_00). (OBSERVED: ACIS's input is a written theorem; its act is adjudication.) A2's
input is EVIDENCE and its act is drafting — it produces the theorem ACIS will later judge. The
distinction is exact and directional: A2 runs BEFORE ACIS in a law's life, turning evidence into a
candidate; ACIS runs after, turning a candidate into an adopted or rejected law. (DERIVED: a legislature
and a court are not redundant because both handle laws — one drafts, one adjudicates, and the estate
had the court and lacked the legislature.) A2 that leveled its own candidate on the ladder would be
doing ACIS's work; ACIS that induced a law from evidence would be doing A2's. Neither does the other's.

2.3 Routing — DRK's relation versus A2's. DRK takes a rule that governs a decision and routes the
decision under it: it owns decision burden, the typed evidence record, and the application of a general
rule to a specific case. (OBSERVED, from DRK-00/03: "Level is an ACIS concept; burden is a DRK
concept.") A2 induces the general rule; DRK applies it. The subjects are orthogonal — A2's object is
the general rule itself (does the evidence support this law across cases), DRK's is a single decision
under an existing rule (given this rule, what does this case require). (DERIVED: A2 works at the
granularity of a law; DRK at the granularity of a decision — the same law/instance distinction that
separates a statute from a court case.) A2 supplies DRK with better-grounded general rules to route
under; it never routes a decision itself.

2.4 Storage — UKDL's relation versus A2's, and the graduation seam. UKDL is the statute book: the store
of ADOPTED rules the estate obeys. A2 owns a different store — the Hypothesis Registry (Part V), the
store of CANDIDATE laws not yet judged. (Boundary, Article II: UKDL holds law that IS; A2 holds law
that is PROPOSED.) The seam between them is a graduation: when a candidate A2 induced is judged and
adopted, it graduates OUT of A2's registry and INTO UKDL, and A2's registry marks it ADOPTED-elsewhere
rather than retaining a competing copy. (DERIVED: two stores, two states of a law's life — candidate
and adopted — and the boundary is the adoption act that moves a rule from one to the other. A2 that
kept adopted rules would be shadowing UKDL; UKDL that held un-adopted candidates would be storing
statutes that were never enacted.) The registries are complementary halves of a law's lifecycle, joined
at adoption.

2.5 Recording — CEPS's relation versus A2's. CEPS records the events the estate's laws govern — the
error events, the cascades, the incidents — as a docket of what happened. (OBSERVED: CEPS's object is
the event log.) A2 READS that log as one of its richest evidence sources (a recurrence in CEPS events
is exactly the pattern a candidate law might generalize) and WRITES nothing to it. (Boundary: CEPS
owns the record of events; A2 owns the induction of a law FROM events. The event log is CEPS's output
and A2's input, the same consumer relation A2 has to FD deposits and DAIF proofs.) A2 that wrote events
would be doing CEPS's work; A2 only reads events to legislate from them.

2.6 The general test for a future collision, and the asymmetric danger. For any word A2 shares with a
sibling, ask what act each system performs on the law — draft, adjudicate, route, store, record — not
what the word connotes. (DEFINED test, the same content-tier discipline that corrected the Tribunal.)
The asymmetry that makes the discipline non-optional is specific to a legislature: a false absorption
in which A2 claimed judgment, routing, storage, or recording would not merely duplicate a sibling — it
would let a self-adopting legislature emerge, the capture risk of Part XII, because a system that both
drafts AND judges its own drafts has removed the separation that keeps legislation honest. (DERIVED:
for A2 the boundary is not only an anti-duplication discipline but a safety property — the separation
of drafting from judging is what the propose-never-apply invariant depends on, and collapsing the
JUDGE-PROPOSE boundary would collapse the invariant with it.)

2.7 The one-sentence separators, collected. ACIS adjudicates a law that exists; A2 drafts one that did
not. DRK routes a decision under a rule; A2 induces the rule. UKDL stores an adopted statute; A2 holds
a candidate. CEPS records the events a law governs; A2 legislates from them. (DEFINED: four relations
to a law, four distinct objects, zero absorption.) Each sibling keeps its relation to the law; A2 holds
the one relation none of them does — authorship from evidence.

2.8 Why this Part precedes any mechanism. A2 is authored immediately after the audit that proved a
title-tier reading mis-assigns ownership, and drafting-versus-judging is precisely the kind of boundary
a title-tier reading collapses — "meta-science" sounds like one thing, and ACIS's falsification plus
A2's generation are two. (DERIVED: to specify A2's mechanisms before drawing this boundary would risk
building, inside a new dataset, the collapse the audit exists to prevent — and here the collapse is not
merely a duplication but a safety hazard, since a legislature that absorbed the judge's role is a
self-adopting one.) A capability that cannot say precisely what it is not has not earned the right to
say what it is — and for a legislature, the thing it most is not is a judge of its own bills.

2.9 The seam with FD, drawn because a distillate looks like a law. The most seductive collision is not
with the four judges but with FD, because a distilled insight and a candidate law can look alike — both
are compressed general statements. (CONTESTED, resolved by object.) FD-03 takes a FINISHED insight
someone already had and routes it to its home and form; its input is a conclusion, and its act is
placement. A2's input is EVIDENCE — many deposits, proofs, events — and its act is INDUCTION: it does
not place a conclusion, it generalizes a rule from a corpus of instances none of which is itself the
rule. (DERIVED: a distillate is one insight compressed; a candidate law is a generalization over many,
carrying a falsifier and a boundary the distillate need not have. FD asks "where does this finished
insight belong"; A2 asks "what general rule do these many pieces of evidence collectively support that
no one has stated." The operations differ even where the outputs resemble each other — one compresses
a known conclusion, the other induces an unknown one.) A2 is one of FD's largest CONSUMERS — the
deposits are premier evidence for induction — but consuming deposits to induce a law is not the act of
producing a deposit, and a candidate law is not a deposit: it is a proposal about the estate's general
structure, offered for judgment, which no FD deposit ever is.

FINAL LAW — PART II. A2 shares the word "law" with four siblings and owns none of their relations to
it: ACIS adjudicates, DRK routes, UKDL stores, CEPS records — A2 alone drafts, from evidence. The
boundary is not only anti-duplication but a safety property, because the one relation a legislature
must never absorb is the judge's: a system that drafts and adjudicates its own drafts has removed the
separation the propose-never-apply invariant stands on, and a legislature that enacts its own bills is
the capture the estate's deepest doctrine was written to forbid.

===============================================================================================
PART III — WHAT A CANDIDATE LAW IS: THE PROPOSED-RULE OBJECT
===============================================================================================

3.1 The candidate law as a first-class object. The central object A2 produces is the CANDIDATE LAW,
and like A1's learner or curriculum it must be first-class — versioned, provenanced, inspectable — not
an informal assertion. (DEFINED: a candidate law is a proposed general rule about the estate's own
structure or behaviour, carrying four mandatory parts — the rule statement, a concrete falsifier, an
evidence trail, and a boundary condition — offered for judgment and never self-adopted.) A candidate
missing any of the four is malformed and is not proposed: a rule with no falsifier is a slogan (Part
VIII), with no evidence is an intuition (invariant 3), with no boundary is a false universal (Part
XIV). The four-part structure is what makes a candidate JUDGEABLE — ACIS cannot adjudicate a rule it
cannot try to refute, so A2's output is shaped from the start to be admissible input to the court.

3.2 The rule statement, and the granularity of a law. The rule statement is a general claim over a
class of instances — "for all X of kind K, property P holds" — not a statement about a single case.
(DEFINED: a law's granularity is the general rule; a single decision under it is DRK's granularity, a
single event is CEPS's.) The generality is what distinguishes a candidate law from an observation: "this
Bash call hung" is an observation (a CEPS event); "Bash calls of external toolchains on Windows hosts
hang" is a candidate law. (DERIVED: A2's induction is precisely the move from the instance to the
class — from many observations to the general rule they collectively support — and the rule statement
names the class and the property, so that its falsifier can be a test over the class rather than a
check of one case.)

3.3 The falsifier, attached at proposal. Every candidate carries a falsifier meeting ACIS's standard —
a named measurement, a signal, and a window — stating the concrete condition under which the law would
be shown false. (DEFINED, inherited from acis_00's standard, cited.) A2 attaches the falsifier at
proposal because a rule whose refutation condition cannot be stated is not a law A2 is willing to
offer: the discipline of naming how the rule could be broken is what separates an inducible law from a
pattern that merely happens to hold in the sample. (DERIVED: the falsifier is also A2's own guard
against the false universal — forcing the author to name the breaking condition surfaces, at proposal
time, whether the rule is genuinely general or an artifact of a biased evidence sample.)

3.4 The evidence trail. Every candidate names the evidence it was induced from — the specific deposits,
proofs, events, and incidents whose recurrence the rule generalizes. (DEFINED, invariant 3: induction
over evidence, never over intuition.) The trail serves three purposes: it lets ACIS and the Owner
inspect the basis before adopting; it lets a later audit check whether the evidence was
representative or cherry-picked (the anti-p-hacking check, Part XIII); and it is the substrate for
recomputing the candidate's confidence as new evidence arrives. (DERIVED: a candidate law with no
evidence trail is exactly the intuition-drafted law A2 exists to supplement — traceless, unauditable,
and indistinguishable from a guess; the trail is what makes A2's legislation evidence-grounded rather
than merely a machine's intuition substituted for a human's.)

3.5 The boundary condition. Every candidate states where it holds and where it does not — the domain of
its validity. (DEFINED, invariant 5.) A law claimed to hold everywhere is almost always a false
universal (Part XIV, XX); a well-formed candidate names its boundary explicitly — "on Windows hosts"
bounds the Bash law, and a candidate that omitted the qualifier would falsely claim the hang occurs on
Linux too. (DERIVED: the boundary is induced alongside the rule, from the same evidence — the cases
where the pattern held and, critically, the cases where it did not, which the boundary must exclude.
A candidate law is a rule PLUS the fence around where it applies, and the fence is as load-bearing as
the rule.)

3.6 The candidate's status field, and its lifecycle. A candidate carries a status through its life in
A2: PROPOSED (drafted, falsifier attached, awaiting judgment), UNDER-JUDGMENT (handed to ACIS/Owner),
ADOPTED-ELSEWHERE (accepted and graduated to UKDL, Part II §2.4), REFUTED (killed by A2's own
counterexample search, Part XV, or by ACIS), or WITHDRAWN (the author retracted it). (DEFINED, five
states.) The status is a field with a real transition producer at every edge — a candidate never sits
in a status nothing can move it out of, because a status field with no transition producer is
decoration and a registry that carried one would lie about its own state. (DERIVED: this is the
estate's own lesson about a status field nobody can transition, applied at authoring — every A2 status
has a named event that produces the next.)

3.7 What a candidate law is NOT — the boundary with an ACIS theorem. A candidate law and an ACIS
theorem are the same content at two points in its life, and confusing them collapses the JUDGE-PROPOSE
boundary. (Boundary, Article II: an ACIS theorem is a law ON the ladder, carrying a level; a candidate
law is a law BEFORE the ladder, carrying a proposal. A2 produces the pre-ladder object; ACIS assigns it
a level.) A candidate has no ACIS level — assigning one is the court's act, and a candidate that
arrived pre-leveled would be A2 usurping the judgment. (DERIVED: the clean handoff, Part XVII — A2
hands a level-less candidate with its falsifier; ACIS assigns the level; the candidate becomes a
theorem. A2 never writes a level, and ACIS never writes a candidate.)

3.8 The candidate as a read-only-derivable proposal. A candidate law is, like A1's certification, best
held as a projection over its evidence trail rather than a free-floating assertion — recomputable from
the evidence it cites, so its confidence tracks the evidence and never drifts from it. (DERIVED: a
candidate whose confidence was a stored number could drift from its evidence; a candidate whose
confidence is derived from its trail recomputes as evidence accrues or is refuted, and a candidate
whose evidence has been undercut falls in confidence automatically. The pattern is deliberately the
same read-only-projection shape ACIS gives its ladder and A1 gives its certification — a projection
that invents nothing and recomputes from its inputs, so all the integrity work lives on the evidence.)

3.9 Why the four-part structure is the whole discipline. The candidate law's four mandatory parts —
rule, falsifier, evidence, boundary — are not bureaucratic fields; each closes one of A2's named
failure modes at the object level. (NORMATIVE: no rule without a falsifier closes the unfalsifiable-law
failure; no rule without evidence closes the intuition-substituted-for-evidence failure; no rule
without a boundary closes the false-universal failure; the status field with real transitions closes
the orphan-candidate failure.) A candidate that carries all four is a proposal the court can actually
judge; a candidate missing any is a defect A2 refuses to propose. The object's shape IS the discipline
— A2's quality is enforced not by an after-the-fact review but by what a well-formed candidate is
required to contain.

FINAL LAW — PART III. A candidate law is a first-class object with four mandatory parts — a general
rule, a concrete falsifier, an evidence trail, and a boundary — and missing any one it is not a law but
a slogan, a guess, or a false universal A2 refuses to propose. It carries no ACIS level, because
leveling is the court's act and a pre-leveled candidate would usurp the judgment; it is a projection
over its evidence, recomputing as the evidence moves. The object's four-part shape is the whole
discipline: A2's rigor is what a well-formed candidate is required to contain, not a review applied
after.

===============================================================================================
PART IV — THE EVIDENCE SUBSTRATE: WHAT A2 LEGISLATES FROM
===============================================================================================

4.1 Induction requires a substrate, and A2's is the estate's own memory. A2 does not legislate from the
world at large; it legislates from the estate's accumulated record of itself — the deposits, proofs,
rules, events, and incidents the estate has already produced. (DEFINED: the evidence substrate is the
union of the estate's evidence ledgers, read as one corpus over which a candidate law can be induced.)
This scoping is deliberate: A2's laws are about the ESTATE — how it behaves, what fails, what holds —
and the only admissible evidence for a law about the estate is the estate's own record, not an external
prior. A2 induces the estate's self-knowledge from the estate's self-record.

4.2 The five evidence families, named. A2 reads five families. FD-07 DEPOSITS — what the estate has
learned and distilled. FD_04 PROOFS and the UKDL RULE CORPUS — what it has adopted, mined for the
meta-rules beneath the object-rules. CEPS EVENTS — what has happened, with recurrence. IAS-D2 INCIDENTS
and the HARD RULES — what has broken and been stopped. A1 CROSS-DOMAIN ASSUMPTION EVIDENCE — which
assumptions held across domains and which failed (the A1->A2 loop, Part XIV). (DEFINED, five families.)
Each is a system's OUTPUT, read by A2 as INPUT — A2 writes to none of them, consuming their records to
legislate, the same consumer relation A1 has to FD and D2.

4.3 The one-algebra requirement, and the B2 dependency. The five families speak different evidence
vocabularies — DRK-00's ten typed evidence records, DAIF-01's ten-status lattice, ACIS levels, CEPS
event types, FD deposit classes — and A2 cannot induce over incommensurable vocabularies. (OBSERVED:
these overlap without a mapping, which is exactly the Tier-B2 gap — epistemic algebra unification.)
A2's induction requires ONE algebra over which evidence of any family is comparable, and B2 supplies
it. (DERIVED, and stated as an honest dependency: the DATASET specifies A2's induction assuming a
unified evidence algebra; the BUILD needs B2 wired first, or A2 induces over evidence it cannot
commensurate. This is recorded here and in Part XXIV's residual, not hidden — A2's spec is complete,
but its build has B2 as a precondition, and claiming A2 runs before B2 exists would be the Scaffold
Illusion.)

4.4 Recurrence as the raw signal of a candidate law. The raw signal A2 mines is RECURRENCE — a pattern
that appears across many pieces of evidence, in more than one family, that no adopted rule yet governs.
(DEFINED: a recurrence spanning families is stronger evidence for a general law than one confined to a
single family — a pattern in CEPS events AND FD deposits AND Hard Rules is more likely a genuine
general rule than a pattern in one ledger, which might be an artifact of that ledger's collection
bias.) A2 ranks candidate recurrences by cross-family breadth and instance count, the same hit-density
discipline the Tribunal used to find true owners, applied to finding true laws. (DERIVED: breadth
across families is A2's primary defense against the single-ledger false universal — a law induced from
one biased ledger is exactly the p-hacking failure of Part XX, and cross-family corroboration is the
structural guard against it.)

4.5 The evidence must be READ-ONLY and provenance-preserving. A2 reads the ledgers without mutating
them, and it preserves, in each candidate's evidence trail (Part III §3.4), the exact provenance of
every piece it induced from. (NORMATIVE: A2's read-only relation to the ledgers is a boundary — the
ledgers are their owners' objects, and A2 writing to CEPS or UKDL would be doing a sibling's work — and
the provenance preservation is what makes a candidate auditable. A candidate that cited "the evidence"
without naming the specific deposits and events could not be checked for cherry-picking.) The
combination — read-only plus provenance-preserving — is what lets A2 legislate from the estate's memory
without corrupting it and without producing unauditable laws.

4.6 Redaction of evidence before it enters a candidate. Evidence drawn from real ledgers may contain
credential material — a CEPS event's captured command, an incident's transcript — and A2 routes every
piece through the estate's redaction bus before it enters a candidate's evidence trail. (NORMATIVE,
HR-SECRET-006 cited: a candidate law is a proposal that will be read by ACIS, the Owner, and eventually
A1's curricula, so a credential surviving into its evidence trail would be a leak with a wide blast
radius.) The redaction is a precondition of the evidence entering the trail, not a post-hoc scrub — the
same discipline A1 applies to synthetic cases, applied to a candidate's citations.

4.7 The substrate grows, and A2's induction is continuous. The evidence ledgers grow every session, so
the set of inducible laws grows with them, and A2's induction is not a one-time sweep but a standing
process re-run as evidence accrues. (DERIVED: a recurrence that was too thin to support a candidate law
last month may cross the threshold this month as more instances land, so A2 re-induces continuously,
and a candidate's confidence (Part XI) recomputes as its evidence trail grows or is refuted. The
substrate is a living corpus, and A2 legislates from it continuously, not once.) This continuity is why
the Hypothesis Registry (Part V) is a standing store rather than a batch output — candidates
accumulate, strengthen, weaken, and graduate over time as the evidence beneath them moves.

4.8 The honest limit: A2 can only induce what the evidence contains. A2 legislates from the estate's
record, so a law the record does not support — a rule about a situation the estate has never
encountered — A2 cannot induce. (CONTESTED-honest: this is A2's fundamental epistemic bound, and it is
why B1's unknown-unknown surface (Part X) is a dependency — the richest genuinely-new laws concern the
unmapped space the current evidence does NOT cover, and A2 needs B1 to point at that space.) A2 induces
the laws latent in what the estate has recorded; the laws about what it has never recorded require
first mapping the unrepresented surface, which is B1's object. A2 is honest that its reach is bounded by
its substrate, and names the dependency that would extend it rather than claiming a reach it does not
have.

4.9 The evidence substrate is discovered, not curated. The set of ledgers A2 induces over is enumerated
by discovery — a scan of the estate's evidence surfaces — not by a hand-maintained list of "sources A2
reads." (NORMATIVE, PR-COVERAGE-BY-CONSTRUCTION-001 applied to A2's inputs: a hand-curated source list
cannot fail A2 for the ledger it forgot to include, and a ledger absent from A2's denominator is a body
of evidence A2 silently never legislates from — the enrollment defect relocated to the legislature's
inputs.) A2 that read only a curated five-source list would miss a sixth evidence surface the moment
one was added, and no error would announce the omission — only a class of inducible laws never
proposed. (DERIVED: the discovery discipline is the same one A1's registry and the D2A family registry
obey, and for the same reason — the completeness of A2's legislation is bounded by the completeness of
its evidence enumeration, and only a discovered enumeration resists the silent gap. A new evidence
ledger the estate adds must appear in A2's substrate by discovery, or A2's laws are induced over a
denominator that quietly excludes it.)

FINAL LAW — PART IV. A2 legislates from the estate's own memory — deposits, proofs, rules, events,
incidents, and A1's cross-domain evidence — read read-only, provenance-preserved, and redacted, over
the single evidence algebra B2 must supply. Its raw signal is recurrence across families, because a
pattern spanning ledgers is a law and a pattern in one ledger is often that ledger's bias. A2 can
induce only what the evidence contains, and it names that bound honestly: the genuinely-new laws about
the unmapped surface need B1, and a legislature that claimed to induce what its record never held would
be substituting a machine's intuition for the evidence it exists to legislate from.

===============================================================================================
PART V — THE HYPOTHESIS REGISTRY: THE STORE OF CANDIDATE LAWS
===============================================================================================

5.1 The registry as A2's standing store. A2's candidate laws live in the HYPOTHESIS REGISTRY — the
first-class, standing store of every candidate A2 has induced, with its status, evidence trail, and
confidence. (DEFINED: the registry is to A2 what the statute book UKDL is to the adopted corpus — but
holding law that is PROPOSED, not law that IS.) It is standing rather than a batch output because
induction is continuous (Part IV §4.7): candidates accumulate, strengthen as evidence corroborates
them, weaken as counterexamples surface, graduate when adopted, and die when refuted, and the registry
holds that whole living population.

5.2 What a registry entry contains. Each entry is a candidate law (Part III's four-part object) plus
its lifecycle metadata: current status (Part III §3.6), confidence (Part XI), the induction date, the
evidence trail with provenance, and — once handed to judgment — the judgment outcome. (DEFINED.) The
entry is the complete auditable record of a candidate's life, from the recurrence that induced it to
its adoption, refutation, or withdrawal. An entry with a status but no evidence trail is malformed (the
intuition-drafted candidate), and the registry's write gate refuses it, the same shape as A1's
malformed-learner-record catalogue.

5.3 The registry is discovered, not curated. Like every other estate registry authored after the audit,
the Hypothesis Registry is DERIVED from the candidate-law artifacts on disk, not hand-maintained.
(NORMATIVE, PR-COVERAGE-BY-CONSTRUCTION-001: a candidate law induced but absent from the registry is
invisible to every downstream consumer — it will never be handed to judgment, never graduate, never be
counted — the orphan-candidate failure caused by an enrollment gap.) A candidate artifact on disk with
no registry entry fails the registry-completeness gate by name, never a count. (DERIVED: the discovery
discipline is what stops A2 growing a shadow of un-enrolled candidates that were induced and then
silently lost — an induced law nothing can find is an induced law that never happened.)

5.4 The candidate versus the adopted, and the graduation. The registry holds candidates; UKDL holds
adopted rules; a candidate graduates from the first to the second at adoption (Part II §2.4). (DEFINED:
on adoption, the registry entry transitions to ADOPTED-ELSEWHERE and points to the UKDL rule it became,
rather than retaining a competing copy.) This is the load-bearing boundary that keeps A2 from shadowing
UKDL: the registry never holds an adopted rule as if it were still a candidate, and UKDL never holds a
candidate as if it were adopted. (DERIVED: the pointer-not-copy design means there is exactly one
authoritative record of an adopted rule — UKDL's — and the registry retains only a historical note of
its origin, so a reader is never confused about which store owns a rule at which stage.)

5.5 Deduplication against the estate's existing rules. Before a candidate is registered, A2 checks it
against the adopted corpus (UKDL, Hard Rules) and against the existing registry, so it does not propose
a law the estate already holds. (NORMATIVE: a candidate that duplicates an adopted rule is not a new
law — it is a rediscovery, and proposing it wastes judgment attention. A2 checks the candidate's rule
statement against existing rules by subject, not by vocabulary, the content-tier discipline applied to
its own output.) (DERIVED: this is A2's application of the D2A duplicate-to-advantage logic to laws — a
candidate that overlaps an existing rule is either a duplicate to fold or a genuine refinement to
propose as an amendment, and A2 distinguishes them by whether the candidate says something the existing
rule does not.)

5.6 The registry's status distribution as a health signal. Reading the distribution of candidates
across statuses is itself a signal about A2's health, the same way IAS-C1 reads its position lifecycle
distribution. (DEFINED: a healthy registry shows a steady flow of PROPOSED candidates, a reasonable
adoption rate, and an occasional REFUTED — a registry with NO refutations is suspicious, because it
suggests A2's counterexample search (Part XIII) is not actually trying to break its own candidates.)
(DERIVED: a registry that only ever proposed and adopted, never refuting, would be a p-hacking
legislature — inducing laws that confirm and never testing them to destruction, exactly the failure
Part XX names. The refutation flow is evidence that A2 is stress-testing its own output, not merely
generating confirmations.)

5.7 The registry does not rank laws by importance. A2's registry stores candidates and their evidence;
it does NOT rank which laws matter most to adopt — that ranking weighs a law's value against the
estate's priorities, which is a portfolio/governance judgment, not A2's. (Boundary, Article II: A2
owns the induction and the candidate store; the PRIORITIZATION of which candidates the Owner should
judge first is a governance decision A2 supplies inputs to — a candidate's confidence and evidence
breadth — without making.) (DERIVED: A2 that ranked laws by importance would be legislating AND setting
the legislative agenda, and the agenda is the Owner's; A2 hands a well-evidenced candidate set and lets
governance choose what to judge, the same restraint A1 shows in handing C1 cost without making the
allocation.)

5.8 The orphan candidate, and the registry's obligation to surface it. A candidate that is induced,
registered, and then never handed to judgment is an ORPHAN — the generative twin of A1's orphan learner
— and the registry surfaces long-PROPOSED candidates rather than letting them accumulate silently.
(NORMATIVE: an induced law that is never judged is teaching capital spent for nothing — the induction
cost was paid and no adoption decision was ever made. The registry flags candidates that have sat
PROPOSED beyond a window for the Owner's attention.) (DERIVED: the orphan candidate is a real failure
mode because induction is cheap and judgment is scarce — A2 can induce faster than the Owner can judge,
so without the surfacing obligation the registry would fill with un-judged candidates, and A2's value
would leak away as induced-but-never-adopted laws.)

5.9 The registry as the substrate for theory composition. Individual candidate laws are the registry's
entries, but the registry is also the substrate from which A2 composes THEORIES (Part VII) — structures
of related laws that together explain more than any one alone. (DERIVED: a cluster of candidate laws
about one estate subject, held together in the registry, is the raw material for a theory, and the
registry's cross-references between related candidates are what let A2 see the cluster. The registry is
therefore not only a store of individual candidates but the graph over which theory composition
operates — the same way A1's transfer graph emerges from its certifications.)

5.10 The registry's provenance closes the loop to A1. Each registry entry, once its candidate is
adopted, retains the provenance chain from the recurrence that induced it through the judgment that
adopted it — and that chain is exactly what A1 reads when it turns the adopted law into curriculum
(Part XVIII). (DERIVED: a law A1 teaches is more teachable when its origin — the evidence it was
induced from, the failure it generalizes — travels with it, because A1's failure simulations (its own
Part VIII) draw on precisely that origin evidence. The registry's provenance is therefore not only A2's
audit substrate but A1's teaching input, and preserving it is what makes the A2→A1 seam carry not just
the law but the reason for it — a rule taught with its founding evidence is learned as understanding,
not as an arbitrary stricture.)

FINAL LAW — PART V. The Hypothesis Registry is A2's standing store of candidate laws — proposed, not
adopted — discovered from disk not curated, so no induced law is silently lost. It holds candidates;
UKDL holds adopted rules; a candidate graduates by pointer, never by copy, so exactly one store owns a
rule at each stage. Its refutation flow is the evidence that A2 tests its own output rather than only
confirming it, and its orphan-surfacing obligation is what stops A2's value leaking away as laws induced
but never judged.

===============================================================================================
PART VI — THE LAW EXTRACTION ENGINE: INDUCING A CANDIDATE FROM RECURRING EVIDENCE
===============================================================================================

6.1 The engine's single job. The Law Extraction Engine is the mechanism that turns a recurrence in the
evidence substrate into a candidate law's four-part object. (DEFINED: given a recurrence — a pattern
across evidence that no adopted rule governs — the engine induces the general rule it supports, attaches
a falsifier, assembles the evidence trail, and extracts the boundary condition, producing a registrable
candidate.) It is the core generative act of the whole dataset: everything before it prepares the
substrate, everything after it judges or handles the output, and the engine is where evidence becomes a
proposed law.

6.2 From recurrence to rule statement — the induction step. The engine's first step is generalization:
from many instances sharing a pattern, state the general rule the instances collectively support.
(DEFINED: the induction moves from "these N observations share property P under condition C" to the
candidate rule "under condition C, property P holds." The condition C becomes the boundary (Part III
§3.5); the property P becomes the rule's claim.) (DERIVED: the quality of the induction is bounded by
the representativeness of the instances — an induction over a biased sample produces a false universal,
which is why the engine's next steps are counterexample search and boundary extraction, not immediate
proposal. The rule statement is a hypothesis the engine then tries to break.)

6.3 The falsifier-extraction step. Having stated a candidate rule, the engine extracts its falsifier —
the concrete condition that would show the rule false. (DEFINED: for "under condition C, property P
holds," the falsifier is "an instance of condition C where P does NOT hold," specified as a measurement,
a signal, and a window per ACIS's standard.) (DERIVED: the engine cannot propose a rule whose falsifier
it cannot state — a rule where "P fails under C" is unobservable is unfalsifiable, and the engine
discards it rather than registering a slogan. The falsifier-extraction step is where the unfalsifiable
candidate dies before it is ever proposed, which is why Part VIII's falsifier-first discipline is
enforced in the engine, not merely stated as a policy.)

6.4 The evidence-assembly step. The engine assembles the candidate's evidence trail — the specific
instances the induction generalized from, with provenance, redacted. (DEFINED, Part IV §4.5-4.6 applied
in the engine.) (DERIVED: the trail must be the ACTUAL instances the induction used, not a
post-hoc-selected supporting set — an engine that induced from a broad sample but cited only the
confirming instances would be building the p-hacking failure into its own output. The trail is the
honest record of what the rule was induced from, including the breadth across families that is A2's
corroboration signal.)

6.5 The boundary-extraction step. The engine extracts the boundary from the same evidence — the cases
where the pattern held and, critically, the nearest cases where it did NOT. (DEFINED: the boundary is
the fence between the two — the condition C that separates instances where P held from instances where
it failed.) (DERIVED: boundary extraction requires the engine to have looked at DISconfirming cases, not
only confirming ones — the boundary is defined by where the pattern stops, and a candidate with no
boundary is one whose engine never looked past its confirmations. This is why boundary extraction and
counterexample search (Part XIII) are the same discipline seen twice: both require the engine to seek
the case the rule does not cover.)

6.6 The meta-rule extraction — inducing over the adopted corpus. A special and powerful mode of the
engine induces not over events but over the estate's ADOPTED RULES — mining the Hard Rules and UKDL for
the meta-rule beneath many object-rules. (DEFINED: many Hard Rules about specific Windows execution
failures support one meta-rule about the class; many secret-firewall rules support one meta-rule about
credential egress. The engine induces the meta-rule the object-rules collectively instantiate.)
(DERIVED: this is A2's highest-value mode — the estate's adopted corpus is its most refined evidence,
and the meta-rules latent in it are laws about the estate's own laws, which no object-rule states and
which only an induction over the corpus can surface. It is also where A2 most clearly complements the
hand-drafting that produced the object-rules one at a time.)

6.7 The engine proposes; it never adopts. The engine's output is a REGISTERED CANDIDATE — PROPOSED
status, awaiting judgment — never an adopted rule. (NORMATIVE, invariant 2, propose-never-apply,
enforced at the engine's output: the engine writes to the Hypothesis Registry, never to UKDL or the
Hard Rules.) (DERIVED: the engine's terminal act is registration, and the boundary between registration
and adoption is the propose-never-apply invariant made mechanical — the engine has no code path that
adopts, only one that proposes, so even a compromised or drifting engine cannot enact a law, it can only
offer one for judgment. The invariant is enforced by the absence of an adoption path in the engine, not
by a policy the engine is trusted to follow.)

6.8 The engine's honesty about induction strength. The engine attaches to each candidate a confidence
(Part XI) reflecting how strongly the evidence supports the rule — instance count, cross-family breadth,
and the absence of counterexamples — and it is honest about weak inductions rather than proposing them
at false confidence. (DERIVED: an engine that proposed every recurrence at high confidence would flood
the registry with weak candidates and waste judgment; an engine honest about induction strength lets the
Owner judge the well-supported first. Confidence is a projection over the evidence trail, recomputed as
evidence moves, never a stored assertion.)

6.9 The engine is bounded by the substrate and the algebra. The engine can induce only over the evidence
the substrate contains (Part IV §4.8) and only across the algebra B2 supplies (Part IV §4.3) — its reach
is exactly the reach of its inputs. (CONTESTED-honest: the engine is specified completely here, but its
BUILD needs the unified algebra and the discovered substrate, and an engine run over un-unified evidence
would produce candidates induced across incommensurable vocabularies — worse than no candidate. A2's
spec names this bound; A2's build must satisfy it.) (DERIVED: the engine is the dataset's core mechanism
and also the one most dependent on B1 and B2 — which is why the honest residual of Part XXIV foregrounds
them, and why claiming A2 induces well before B2 unifies its evidence would be the exact overreach the
whole corpus is disciplined against.)

6.10 The engine as a read-only projection, like every load-bearing mechanism in the corpus. The engine
writes exactly one thing — a registered candidate — and computes everything else as a projection over
its inputs: the rule from the recurrence, the confidence from the evidence trail, the boundary from the
disconfirming cases. (DERIVED: this is the same read-only-projection discipline ACIS gives its ladder,
A1 its certification, and A2 its candidate object — a mechanism that invents nothing and derives
everything from inputs, so all the integrity work concentrates on the inputs where it can be audited.
The engine's single write, its lack of an adoption path, and its projection-not-assertion design are
three expressions of one principle: A2's most powerful mechanism is also its most constrained, because
a legislature's drafting engine is exactly the component that must never be able to enact.)

FINAL LAW — PART VI. The Law Extraction Engine turns a recurrence into a candidate's four parts —
generalizing the rule, extracting its falsifier, assembling its honest evidence trail, fencing its
boundary — and its highest mode induces the meta-rules latent in the estate's own adopted corpus, the
laws about its laws no object-rule states. It proposes and never adopts, because it has no adoption path
at all, only registration — the propose-never-apply invariant enforced by construction. Its reach is
exactly its inputs' reach, bounded by the substrate it reads and the one algebra B2 must supply, and A2
names that bound rather than claiming an induction its inputs cannot yet support.

===============================================================================================
PART VII — THE THEORY GENERATOR: COMPOSING LAWS INTO AN EXPLANATORY STRUCTURE
===============================================================================================

7.1 From individual laws to a theory. The Law Extraction Engine (Part VI) produces individual candidate
laws; the Theory Generator composes related laws into a THEORY — a structure of laws that together
explain more than any one alone. (DEFINED: a theory is a set of candidate laws plus the explanatory
relationships among them — one law explaining why another holds, several laws sharing a common cause, a
hierarchy from specific rules to the general principle beneath them.) A theory is the estate's account
of WHY a family of its behaviours holds, not merely THAT each holds — the difference between a list of
Windows-execution rules and the theory of why the MSYS2 bridge fails that explains them all.

7.2 The composition operation. The generator composes by finding explanatory relationships among
registry candidates: subsumption (law A is a special case of law B), common-cause (laws A and B both
follow from principle C), and sequence (law A's condition produces law B's condition). (DEFINED, three
relationship types.) (DERIVED: composition is a graph operation over the Hypothesis Registry (Part V
§5.9) — the candidates are nodes, the explanatory relationships are edges, and a theory is a connected
subgraph with an explanatory structure. The generator does not induce new object-laws in composition;
it induces the STRUCTURE relating laws already induced, which is a second-order induction over the
first-order candidates.)

7.3 The explanatory principle at a theory's root. A well-formed theory has a root — the most general
principle from which the others follow — and that root is itself a candidate law, subject to the same
four-part discipline (rule, falsifier, evidence, boundary). (DEFINED: the theory of Windows-execution
failure has, at its root, the principle "the MSYS2 transport corrupts external-toolchain invocations
under condition X," and the specific rules (git-via-Bash hangs, python-via-Bash hangs) follow from it.)
(DERIVED: the root principle is more valuable and more dangerous than any specific rule — more valuable
because it explains many, more dangerous because if false it invalidates the whole theory — which is
why the root carries the strictest falsifier and the broadest evidence requirement. A theory is only as
sound as its root, and A2 holds the root to the highest evidentiary bar in the composition.)

7.4 The theory as a compression of the estate's self-knowledge. A theory compresses many object-laws
into one explanatory structure, and that compression is itself a form of the estate understanding
itself better. (DERIVED: before the theory, the estate held N separate rules; after, it holds one
principle and the derivations — fewer independent things to know, more understanding of how they
relate. This is not FD's distillation — FD compresses a finished insight for storage; A2's theory
composition induces the explanatory structure that was never stated, producing understanding the
individual laws did not contain. The theory is genuinely new knowledge: the WHY that the collection of
WHATs never articulated.) The compression is A2's highest-order product — a theory is worth more than
the sum of its laws because it explains them.

7.5 Theory composition is still proposal, never adoption. A composed theory, like an individual
candidate, is PROPOSED — offered for judgment, never self-adopted. (NORMATIVE, invariant 2: the
generator writes a theory to the registry as a structured candidate, and its adoption is a separate
governance act, just as an individual law's is.) (DERIVED: a self-adopting theory generator would be
even more dangerous than a self-adopting law engine, because a theory reshapes how the estate
understands a whole family of its behaviours — adopting a wrong theory would propagate a wrong
explanation across every rule it claims to ground. The propose-never-apply invariant is therefore most
critical exactly where A2 is most powerful, at the theory level, and the generator has no adoption path
for a theory any more than the engine has for a law.)

7.6 The theory's falsifier is compositional. A theory's falsifier is not merely the conjunction of its
laws' falsifiers — it includes the falsifier of its explanatory STRUCTURE: the condition under which
the laws are all true but the claimed explanatory relationship among them is false. (DEFINED: two laws
can both hold while the claim that one explains the other is wrong — correlation without the claimed
causation — and the theory's falsifier must name how that structural error would show. A theory falsely
claiming a common cause is refuted by finding the cause absent where the effects are present.)
(DERIVED: this is why theory composition is harder than law induction and carries a distinct failure
mode, paradigm-thrash (Part XVI, XX) — a theory can be wrong in a way none of its laws are, by claiming
a false structure over true laws, and the compositional falsifier is what tests the structure itself,
not just its parts.)

7.7 The boundary with ACIS's meta-science. ACIS owns the JUDGMENT of a theory — leveling it, enforcing
its falsifier, retiring it — and the estate's meta-science includes paradigm retirement as "a designed
operation rather than an embarrassment." (Boundary, Article II: ACIS adjudicates and retires a theory;
A2 composes and proposes one. The generative act — building the explanatory structure from evidence —
is A2's; the judgmental act — deciding whether the structure is admissible and when to retire it — is
ACIS's.) (DERIVED: this is the JUDGE-PROPOSE boundary at the theory level, and it is the exact
white-space Science XII named — the estate had paradigm RETIREMENT (ACIS) and lacked paradigm
PROPOSAL (A2). A2 proposes the paradigm; ACIS retires the old one and adjudicates the new; neither does
the other's act.)

7.8 Theories organize the registry and guide induction. A composed theory feeds back into A2's own
process: it organizes the registry (candidates cluster under the theories that explain them) and it
guides further induction (a theory predicts laws not yet induced — if the root principle holds, certain
specific rules should too, and A2 can look for their evidence). (DERIVED: a theory is not only an output
but a hypothesis-generator — it predicts where new laws should be found, directing the engine's
attention to the evidence that would confirm or refute the predicted rules. This is how A2 becomes more
than a pattern-miner: a theory lets it predict laws from structure and then seek their evidence, which
is the difference between induction and genuine theorizing.)

7.9 The honest limit: theory composition is the most evidence-hungry mode. Composing a sound theory
requires enough evidence to establish not only the laws but their relationships, and the estate's record
may support the laws while under-supporting the structure. (CONTESTED-honest: A2 proposes a theory at a
confidence reflecting the evidence for its STRUCTURE, which is often weaker than the evidence for its
individual laws, and it is honest about a theory whose laws are well-supported but whose explanatory
claim is thin. A speculative theory is proposed as speculative, or not proposed at all.) (DERIVED: the
theory generator is A2's highest-value and highest-risk mode, and its discipline is to be most cautious
exactly where it is most powerful — a confidently-proposed wrong theory is the paradigm-thrash failure,
and honesty about structural evidence strength is the guard against it.)

FINAL LAW — PART VII. The Theory Generator composes individual laws into an explanatory structure — the
WHY the collection of WHATs never stated — rooted in a most-general principle held to the strictest
evidentiary bar, because a theory is only as sound as its root and worth more than the sum of its laws.
Its falsifier is compositional: a theory can be wrong by claiming a false structure over true laws, and
that structural error is what must be testable. It proposes and never adopts, most critically here where
a wrong theory would propagate a wrong explanation across every rule it grounds — the propose-never-apply
invariant is strongest exactly where A2 is.

===============================================================================================
PART VIII — FALSIFIER-FIRST DISCIPLINE: NO CANDIDATE WITHOUT A REFUTATION CONDITION
===============================================================================================

8.1 The discipline stated. A2's central quality gate is that no candidate law is proposed without a
concrete falsifier — a named measurement, a signal, and a window under which the law would be shown
false. (DEFINED, invariant 1, inherited from ACIS's standard and enforced in the Law Extraction Engine,
Part VI §6.3.) The falsifier is attached at PROPOSAL, before the candidate enters the registry, so that
an unfalsifiable pattern never becomes a candidate at all — the discipline is a gate on what may be
proposed, not a review of what was.

8.2 Why the falsifier comes first, not last. A2 attaches the falsifier before assembling evidence or
computing confidence, because the falsifier determines whether the candidate is a LAW at all. (NORMATIVE:
"a theorem without a concrete, observable refutation condition is not a weak theorem, it is not a
theorem at all" — acis_00, cited. A pattern with no statable refutation condition is not a law with low
confidence; it is not a law, and no amount of confirming evidence makes it one.) (DERIVED: putting the
falsifier first means the engine discards the unfalsifiable candidate before spending effort on its
evidence trail — the cheapest point to kill a slogan is before it is dressed as a law, and the
falsifier-first order is what makes the discipline efficient as well as sound.)

8.3 The difference between a falsifier and a slogan, made operational. ACIS's own example is the
standard: "the law is false if reasoning doesn't compress" is NOT a falsifier — it names no measurement,
no signal, no window. (DEFINED, cited.) A2 operationalizes the distinction: a falsifier must name WHAT
would be measured (a specific observable), WHAT value of it refutes the law (the signal), and OVER WHAT
scope the measurement applies (the window). (DERIVED: "the Bash-hang law is false if, on a Windows host,
an external-toolchain Bash invocation completes normally across 30 consecutive trials" is a falsifier —
observable (completion), signal (normal across 30), window (Windows host, external toolchain). The
three-part requirement is what separates a testable law from a rhetorical one, and A2 enforces all
three.)

8.4 The falsifier as the boundary between A2 and ACIS, seen precisely. A2 attaches the falsifier; ACIS
enforces it. (Boundary, Article II: the SAME falsifier standard is applied at two moments — A2's gate on
proposal, ACIS's gate on adoption. A2 will not propose a law it cannot state a falsifier for; ACIS will
not adopt a law whose falsifier does not survive scrutiny.) (DERIVED: this is why the two systems apply
one standard without redundancy — the falsifier at proposal filters what reaches the court, the
falsifier at judgment filters what leaves it adopted, and a candidate can pass A2's gate (a statable
falsifier) yet fail ACIS's (the falsifier, tested, reveals the law false). The two gates are sequential
filters on one standard, not two copies of one filter.)

8.5 Falsifier-first prevents the unfalsifiable-law failure at its source. The unfalsifiable law (Part
XX) is a candidate that cannot be refuted by any evidence — it "explains" everything and predicts
nothing. (NORMATIVE: because the falsifier is attached first and is a gate, the unfalsifiable candidate
never enters the registry — it is killed at the engine's falsifier-extraction step. The failure mode is
closed by construction, not by a later audit that might miss it.) (DERIVED: this is the object-level
enforcement of Part III §3.9 — the candidate's mandatory falsifier part is what makes "no unfalsifiable
law" a structural property rather than an aspiration, since a candidate literally cannot be well-formed
without one.)

8.6 The falsifier must be CHEAP enough to actually run. A falsifier that names a measurement no one can
afford to make is technically a falsifier but practically useless — the law can never actually be
tested. (CONTESTED-honest: A2 prefers falsifiers whose measurement the estate can realistically perform,
and flags a candidate whose only falsifier is prohibitively expensive as weakly-testable.) (DERIVED: a
law testable only in principle is barely better than an unfalsifiable one, because the estate will never
run the test — so A2's falsifier-extraction seeks the cheapest sufficient refutation condition, the one
the estate could actually check, the same practicality discipline A1 applies to its assessment cases. A
falsifier the estate can run is a law the estate can actually maintain.)

8.7 Falsifier and boundary are jointly extracted. The falsifier and the boundary condition (Part III
§3.5) are extracted together, because they are two views of the same thing — the boundary names where
the law claims to hold, and the falsifier names the observation that would show it fails within that
boundary. (DERIVED: a law's boundary and its falsifier are dual — the boundary excludes the cases where
the law does not apply, and the falsifier tests the cases where it should. Extracting them jointly means
A2 never proposes a law whose falsifier tests outside its own boundary, which would be an incoherent
candidate testable only where it never claimed to hold.)

8.8 The falsifier as A2's honesty instrument. The requirement to state a falsifier is A2's primary
defense against its own confirmation bias — forcing the engine to name how the law could be WRONG,
before proposing it, surfaces at proposal time whether the induction is genuine or a pattern the engine
wanted to find. (DERIVED: an engine that only sought confirmations would propose false universals; the
falsifier-first discipline forces it to name the disconfirmation it should look for, which is the
counterexample search of Part XIII made mandatory at the object level. The falsifier is where A2's
intellectual honesty is enforced by structure rather than trusted to disposition.)

8.9 Falsifier-first is inherited, not invented, and A2 says so. A2 did not invent the falsifier
standard — it is ACIS's, and before ACIS it is Popper's, and A2 inherits it wholesale rather than
claiming it. (NORMATIVE, boundary honesty: A2's contribution is applying the standard at the PROPOSAL
point — attaching a falsifier to an induced candidate — not authoring the standard itself. A2 that
claimed to have originated falsifiability would be inflating its own novelty, the credential-inflation
failure applied to a dataset's self-account.) (DERIVED: naming the inheritance is itself the corpus's
discipline — A2 cites ACIS's standard, applies it at its own distinct point in a law's life, and claims
only the application, which is the same citation-not-restatement rule every Part of both datasets obeys.)

8.10 Falsifier-first is what makes A2 safe to run at scale. Because every candidate must carry a runnable
refutation condition before it is proposed, A2 can induce aggressively — flooding the registry with
candidates costs nothing dangerous, since each is falsifiable and none is adopted without judgment.
(DERIVED: the discipline is what lets A2 be a prolific legislature rather than a cautious one — the
volume of proposals is bounded by the Owner's judgment capacity, not by any risk in proposing, because a
falsifiable candidate offered for judgment can do no harm until adopted. The falsifier-first gate plus
propose-never-apply together mean A2's generative volume is safe by construction, however much it
induces.)

FINAL LAW — PART VIII. No candidate law is proposed without a concrete falsifier — a named measurement,
a signal, and a window — because a rule with no statable refutation condition is not a weak law but no
law at all, and A2 kills the slogan at the engine before it is ever dressed as a candidate. The
falsifier is inherited from ACIS's standard and applied at A2's distinct point, proposal, where ACIS
applies it at judgment: two sequential filters on one standard. It is A2's honesty instrument — forcing
the engine to name how its law could be wrong is what keeps induction from being confirmation the engine
merely wanted to find.

===============================================================================================
PART IX — THE INSTITUTIONAL EXPERIMENT DESIGNER: DESIGNING THE TEST
===============================================================================================

9.1 From falsifier to experiment. A candidate's falsifier states the condition that would refute it; the
Institutional Experiment Designer turns that condition into a concrete, runnable EXPERIMENT — the actual
procedure the estate would perform to test the law. (DEFINED: a falsifier is a specification of what
would refute a law; an experiment is the procedure that produces that observation. The designer bridges
"the law is false if X" to "here is how the estate would observe X.") This is what makes A2's laws not
merely falsifiable in principle but testable in practice — a legislature that proposed only untested
laws would be proposing hypotheses; A2 designs the test that would confirm or refute each.

9.2 The experiment operates on the estate, not the world. An institutional experiment tests a law ABOUT
the estate by manipulating the estate and observing the result — running an external-toolchain Bash call
on a Windows host and observing whether it hangs, to test the Bash-hang law. (DEFINED: the experiment's
subject is the estate's own behaviour, and its manipulations are estate operations. This is why it is an
INSTITUTIONAL experiment — it experiments on the institution to learn the institution's laws.) (DERIVED:
the scoping matters — A2's laws are about the estate, so their experiments must be run on the estate, and
the designer's job is to specify an estate-manipulation whose outcome discriminates the law from its
negation.)

9.3 The experiment must be safe, and reuses the estate's sandboxes. An experiment that tests a law about
a destructive or irreversible behaviour must itself be safe — it runs in a sandbox (IAS-G1's, the same
substrate A1 uses for synthetic experience), never on production. (NORMATIVE: testing the law "destructive
command without backup loses data" must not itself lose real data — the experiment runs against synthetic
state, exactly as A1's failure simulations do. A2 reuses G1's sandbox and builds no isolation machinery.)
(DERIVED: this is a boundary — the sandbox is G1's object, and A2 configures it as an experiment
environment, the same reuse-not-rebuild relation A1 has to G1. A2's contribution is the experiment
DESIGN, not the sandbox it runs in.)

9.4 The experiment as the bridge from candidate to evidence. An experiment's result is new evidence —
it either corroborates the candidate (the predicted outcome occurred) or refutes it (the falsifying
outcome occurred), and either way it updates the candidate's confidence and evidence trail. (DEFINED:
the experiment is how A2 goes beyond PASSIVE induction over existing evidence to ACTIVE generation of new
evidence targeted at a specific candidate.) (DERIVED: this is the difference between mining and
experimenting — mining finds laws latent in existing evidence; experimenting produces new evidence to
test a specific law. The designer is what lets A2 do the second, and it is most valuable exactly where
the existing evidence is thin — a candidate the record barely supports can be tested by an experiment
designed to produce the missing evidence.)

9.5 The designer proposes experiments; it does not run them autonomously. Like every A2 mechanism, the
designer PROPOSES an experiment — it does not execute estate manipulations autonomously. (NORMATIVE,
propose-never-apply extended to experiments: an experiment manipulates the estate, and autonomous
estate-manipulation by A2 would breach the same invariant that forbids autonomous adoption. The designer
specifies the experiment; running it is a separate, authorized act.) (DERIVED: an A2 that ran its own
experiments on the estate without authorization would be acting on the estate, not merely proposing to —
and an experiment on a destructive-behaviour law, run autonomously, could cause the very harm it tests
for. The designer's output is a runnable specification the Owner or an authorized process executes, never
an execution A2 performs itself.)

9.6 The experiment reuses the estate's evidence gates as its instruments. An experiment's observation is
made by the estate's existing verification gates — the tests, the reality probes, the CEPS event log —
read as impartial instruments, exactly as A1's assessment reuses them. (DERIVED: A2 does not build a new
observation apparatus; it specifies which existing gate observes the experiment's outcome, because the
estate's gates are already impartial instruments hardened for their own purposes. An experiment testing
the Bash-hang law observes via the same completion/timeout signals the estate already records, which are
impartial because they were not built to flatter any law.) This is the same reach-for-an-existing-gate
discipline that keeps A1's assessment cheap and impartial, applied to A2's experiments.

9.7 The cost of an experiment, handed to governance. An experiment consumes estate resources — a sandbox,
compute, attention — and A2 attaches an estimated cost to each proposed experiment, so the Owner can
decide whether testing a given law is worth it. (Boundary: the PRIORITIZATION of which experiments to run
weighs test cost against a law's value, which is a governance judgment; A2 supplies the cost and the
candidate's confidence, and governance chooses. A2 does not run experiments on its own budget.) (DERIVED:
this is the same input-not-decision restraint A2 shows on law prioritization (Part V §5.7) — A2 designs
and costs the experiment; the estate decides whether to spend on it, most readily for a high-value law
whose existing evidence is thin.)

9.8 The experiment closes the loop back to induction. An experiment's result feeds back into the evidence
substrate as new evidence, which A2 then induces over — a refuted candidate is killed (Part XV), a
corroborated one strengthens, and either result may shift a theory built on the law. (DERIVED: the
designer completes A2's cycle from passive miner to active theorizer — induce a candidate from existing
evidence, design an experiment to test it, run it (with authorization), fold the result back into the
substrate, re-induce. This is the scientific method instantiated as an estate mechanism, with the
critical constraint that A2 proposes each step and an authorized process executes the manipulations.)

9.9 The honest limit: many institutional laws are hard to experiment on. Some laws about the estate
concern rare or slow phenomena — a law about long-session degradation, or about cross-session knowledge
decay — whose experiments would take a long time or a rare condition to run. (CONTESTED-honest: A2 is
honest that not every law is cheaply testable, and for a law whose experiment is impractical it relies on
passive evidence accumulation instead, flagging the candidate as observation-dependent rather than
experiment-testable.) (DERIVED: the designer is powerful for laws about fast, repeatable phenomena and
weak for laws about slow, rare ones — and A2 says which a given candidate is, rather than pretending every
law can be put to a quick experiment. The honest bound is that A2's active-evidence generation reaches
only as far as its experiments are practical to run.)

FINAL LAW — PART IX. The Institutional Experiment Designer turns a falsifier into a runnable test —
bridging "the law is false if X" to "here is how the estate would observe X" — and it is what makes A2 an
experimenter and not only a miner, producing new evidence targeted at a candidate the record barely
supports. It experiments on the estate to learn the estate's laws, safely in G1's sandbox, observed by the
estate's own impartial gates. And it proposes the experiment, never runs it — because an experiment
manipulates the estate, and autonomous manipulation is the same breach as autonomous adoption.

===============================================================================================
PART X — NOVELTY AND THE UNKNOWN-UNKNOWN SURFACE
===============================================================================================

10.1 The two kinds of law A2 can induce. A2 induces laws of two kinds. KNOWN-SPACE laws generalize a
recurrence in what the estate has already recorded — the Bash-hang law, induced from many logged
instances. UNKNOWN-SPACE laws concern the unmapped surface — the assumptions no artifact ever wrote down,
the situations the estate has never encountered. (DEFINED, two kinds.) The first, A2 induces from its
substrate; the second, it cannot induce without first being pointed at the unmapped space, which is B1's
object. Novelty — the genuinely new law — lives disproportionately in the second kind, because the estate
has already hand-drafted most of the laws its recorded evidence obviously supports.

10.2 Why the richest laws are in the unknown space. The estate's competent sessions have already noticed
and hand-drafted the laws that its recorded evidence makes obvious — the Hard Rules exist. What remains
un-induced is disproportionately the non-obvious: the meta-rules beneath many object-rules (Part VI §6.6),
and the laws about the unmapped surface the evidence does NOT yet cover. (DERIVED: A2's marginal value
over hand-drafting is highest exactly where hand-drafting is weakest — the non-obvious generalization and
the unrepresented space. A2 that only induced the obvious known-space laws would mostly rediscover what
sessions already drafted; its genuine novelty is in the meta-rules and the unknown space.)

10.3 The B1 dependency, stated honestly. The unknown-unknown surface — "the assumption everyone shared,
which stopped being true, and which no artifact ever wrote down because it was too obvious to state"
(cited from daif_21, the estate's own account of the class) — is Tier-B1's object, carried by FIOS as a
🟡 EXTEND, not built. (OBSERVED, from A1's Part I §1 and the Tribunal.) A2 needs B1 to point at the
unmapped surface, because A2 cannot induce a law about a space its evidence does not represent. (DERIVED,
honest dependency: the DATASET specifies how A2 would legislate over the unknown space once B1 maps it;
the BUILD needs B1. A2's known-space induction works without B1; its unknown-space induction — its
richest mode — depends on B1, and claiming A2 covers the unknown space before B1 exists would be the
overreach the corpus forbids.)

10.4 Novelty as a first-class property of a candidate. A2 attaches a NOVELTY assessment to each candidate
— whether it generalizes known evidence (lower novelty, still valuable), states a meta-rule beneath
existing rules (higher novelty), or concerns the unmapped surface (highest novelty, B1-dependent).
(DEFINED: novelty is orthogonal to confidence — a well-evidenced known-space law is high-confidence,
low-novelty; a thin unknown-space candidate is low-confidence, high-novelty. Both are worth proposing,
and the Owner judges them differently.) (DERIVED: separating novelty from confidence lets governance
weigh them — a high-novelty, low-confidence candidate is a speculative but valuable proposal worth an
experiment; a low-novelty, high-confidence one is a safe codification. Collapsing the two would lose
exactly the distinction that guides which candidates deserve testing versus adoption.)

10.5 The unknown-unknown is where A2 and A1's cross-domain loop meet. A1's cross-domain education surfaces
assumptions that held natively and failed in a foreign domain (A1 Part XV §15.8) — and an assumption that
failed where it was silently assumed is a signal about the unmapped surface. (DERIVED: A1's cross-domain
failures are one of A2's richest sources of unknown-space evidence, because a native assumption that broke
in a new domain is exactly "the assumption no one wrote down because it was too obvious" — surfaced by
A1's teaching. The A1→A2 evidence loop (Part IV §4.2) is therefore not only about known laws but a primary
feed for the unknown-space induction, and it partially substitutes for B1 where A1's cross-domain teaching
has already surfaced an unwritten assumption.)

10.6 The distinction from SQI-01's reality scanning. SQI-01 detects DIVERGENCE in what is already
represented — believed-versus-observed state for things the estate has modeled. (Boundary, Article II:
SQI-01's object is drift in the represented; the unknown-unknown is the UNrepresented, which SQI-01
itself concedes it is silent against — "against this class, structural comparison is silent and will
remain silent," cited.) (DERIVED: A2's unknown-space induction is not SQI-01's reality scanning — SQI-01
finds where the map disagrees with the territory; A2, via B1, seeks the territory the map never drew.
Neither owns the other's object: SQI-01 checks represented reality, A2 legislates about the unrepresented,
and the unknown-unknown is precisely the gap SQI-01 names as beyond its own reach.)

10.7 Novelty must not become novelty-for-its-own-sake. A generator rewarded for novelty would drift
toward proposing exotic laws over sound ones — the paradigm-thrash failure (Part XX) driven by a novelty
incentive. (CONTESTED, resolved by the evidence discipline: A2 proposes a high-novelty candidate only
with an honest confidence reflecting its (often thin) evidence, and never inflates a speculative law's
confidence because it is exciting.) (DERIVED: novelty is a property A2 reports, not a target it maximizes
— the target is sound laws the evidence supports, and novelty is one dimension governance weighs. An A2
that chased novelty would flood the registry with exotic, poorly-evidenced candidates, which is why
novelty and confidence are kept separate and confidence is never sacrificed to novelty.)

10.8 The unknown space is where a legislature earns its keep. The deepest justification for A2 is that a
legislature which only codified the obvious would barely beat hand-drafting — its value is inducing the
laws no session noticed, and those cluster in the meta-rules and the unmapped surface. (DERIVED: this is
why the B1 dependency is worth stating as prominently as the B2 one — A2's known-space induction is
useful, but its transformative value is the unknown-space legislation B1 enables, and an A2 built without
B1 is a useful codifier of the obvious rather than the discoverer of the non-obvious it was proposed to
be. A2 names this so the build is not mistaken for complete when only the known-space half runs.)

10.9 The honest residual, foregrounded. A2's spec is complete; A2's transformative value — the
unknown-space induction — depends on B1, and its whole induction depends on B2's algebra. (CONTESTED-
honest, and repeated deliberately: the two Tier-B dependencies are not incidental to A2, they are the
difference between A2-that-codifies-the-obvious and A2-that-discovers-the-non-obvious. The dataset
specifies the full system; the build has an ordering — B2 then B1 then A2's richest modes — and A2 states
it rather than letting a reader assume the sealed spec means a running legislature.) (DERIVED: naming the
residual here, in the novelty Part, is deliberate — novelty is exactly what B1 unlocks, so the Part about
A2's highest value is the honest place to foreground the dependency that value rests on.)

10.10 The known-space half is a real, shippable first increment. Because the unknown-space induction
depends on B1, a reader might conclude A2 is not worth building until B1 lands — and that conclusion is
wrong. (NORMATIVE: A2's known-space induction and its meta-rule mode (Part VI §6.6) run over the existing
substrate with only B2's algebra, and the meta-rules latent in the estate's own adopted corpus are
already high-value laws no object-rule states. A2 is worth building as a known-space legislature first,
and extended to the unknown space when B1 is ready.) (DERIVED: this is the honest build sequencing — B2
then A2-known-space then B1 then A2-unknown-space — and it means A2 delivers value in increments rather
than waiting on its full dependency set, the same incremental-delivery discipline the estate applies to
any large build.)

FINAL LAW — PART X. A2 induces two kinds of law — the known-space generalization of recorded evidence and
the unknown-space law about the unmapped surface — and its genuine novelty lives disproportionately in the
second, because sessions have already hand-drafted the obvious. The unknown space is B1's object, and A2
names the dependency honestly: its known-space induction runs without B1, its transformative value does
not. Novelty is reported, never chased — a legislature that pursued exotic laws over sound ones is
paradigm-thrash — and A2's confidence is never sacrificed to how new a law feels.

===============================================================================================
PART XI — CONFIDENCE WITHOUT ADOPTION
===============================================================================================

11.1 Confidence is a property of a candidate, decoupled from adoption. Every candidate carries a
CONFIDENCE — how strongly the evidence supports the rule — and confidence is strictly decoupled from
adoption: a high-confidence candidate is not thereby adopted, and adoption is not thereby a confidence
claim. (DEFINED: confidence is A2's assessment of evidential support; adoption is the Owner's decision to
make the rule binding. The two are different judgments by different parties about different questions —
"how well does the evidence support this" versus "should the estate obey this.") This decoupling is
load-bearing: collapsing it would let a high-confidence candidate self-adopt, which is precisely the
propose-never-apply breach Part XII forbids.

11.2 Confidence is a projection over the evidence trail. A candidate's confidence is computed from its
evidence — instance count, cross-family breadth, the presence or absence of counterexamples, experiment
results — not stored as a free number. (DEFINED, the read-only-projection shape again: confidence
recomputes as the evidence trail moves, so it always reflects the current evidence and never drifts from
it.) (DERIVED: a candidate whose confidence was a stored assertion could remain "high" after its evidence
was undercut; a candidate whose confidence is derived falls automatically when a counterexample lands or
an experiment refutes it. Confidence-as-projection is what makes A2's confidence honest over time rather
than a snapshot that ages into a lie.)

11.3 Confidence is inherited from ACIS's decoupling of confidence and level. A2 adopts ACIS's own rule
that confidence is "subjective, and decoupled from level... confidence is not evidence; the ladder
ignores it for promotion" (cited from acis_00). (NORMATIVE: A2 extends the same decoupling — A2's
confidence informs the Owner's judgment but does not itself promote a candidate to adoption, exactly as
ACIS's confidence does not promote a theorem up the ladder. Confidence is an input to judgment, never a
substitute for it.) (DERIVED: this is why A2 can report confidence freely without endangering the
propose-never-apply invariant — because confidence, however high, is decoupled from adoption by
construction, the same way ACIS keeps it decoupled from level. A2 that let confidence drive adoption
would be re-coupling what the estate's doctrine deliberately separates.)

11.4 Why confidence must never trigger adoption. The most dangerous possible A2 design would let a
sufficiently-high-confidence candidate auto-adopt — "if the evidence is overwhelming, why wait for
judgment." (NORMATIVE, and A2 forbids it absolutely: no confidence threshold triggers adoption, because
adoption is a governance act reserved to the Owner, and a confidence-triggered adoption is a self-adopting
legislature by another name.) (DERIVED: the temptation is real — a candidate at overwhelming confidence
FEELS like it should just be adopted — but the whole point of the separation is that even overwhelming
evidence does not authorize a legislature to enact its own bill. The confidence-triggers-adoption design
is the capture risk dressed as efficiency, and A2's refusal of any adoption threshold is what keeps the
separation absolute.)

11.5 Confidence and novelty are orthogonal dimensions the Owner weighs. A candidate carries both
confidence (Part XI) and novelty (Part X §10.4), and they are independent — the four quadrants
(high/low confidence × high/low novelty) each warrant different handling. (DEFINED: high-confidence
high-novelty is a valuable discovery worth adopting; high-confidence low-novelty is a safe codification;
low-confidence high-novelty is a speculative proposal worth an experiment; low-confidence low-novelty is
usually not worth proposing.) (DERIVED: keeping the two dimensions separate is what lets governance make
a nuanced decision — an A2 that collapsed them into one "score" would lose exactly the distinction
between "well-evidenced" and "surprising," which are different reasons to care about a law and call for
different actions.)

11.6 Confidence must be honest about weak evidence, not optimistic. A2 reports a candidate's confidence
at the level its evidence actually supports, including LOW where the evidence is thin — it does not round
up a speculative candidate to make it look adoptable. (NORMATIVE: an A2 optimistic about weak evidence
would flood judgment with candidates that look stronger than they are, wasting the Owner's scarce
judgment attention on laws the evidence does not really support.) (DERIVED: honest low confidence is
what makes A2's high-confidence candidates trustworthy — if A2 inflated weak candidates, the Owner could
not trust any confidence, so the discipline of honest low confidence is what preserves the meaning of
high confidence. This is the same calibration honesty A1 demands of its assessment, applied to A2's
confidence.)

11.7 Confidence decays, like every claim in the estate. A candidate's confidence is not permanent — as
its supporting evidence ages, or as the estate changes such that old instances are less relevant,
confidence decays, the same way A1's certifications and the estate's other evidence-based claims decay.
(DERIVED: a candidate proposed at high confidence years ago, whose evidence is now stale and whose domain
has shifted, should not still read as high-confidence — its confidence recomputes downward as its
evidence ages, per the projection design. A candidate whose confidence never decayed would be the
permanent-credential failure relocated to laws, and A2 inherits the estate's decay discipline to prevent
it.)

11.8 Confidence is A2's input to the judgment handoff. When a candidate is handed to ACIS/the Owner for
judgment (Part XVII), its confidence and evidence trail travel with it as the primary inputs to the
adoption decision — but the decision remains the judge's. (DERIVED: A2's confidence is the legislature's
honest assessment of how well-evidenced its own bill is, handed to the body that decides whether to enact
it. The judge may adopt a lower-confidence candidate it deems important, or decline a high-confidence one
it deems unwise — confidence informs but never binds the judgment, which is the decoupling made
operational at the handoff.)

11.9 The whole Part is one guard around the invariant. Every subsection here protects the same thing —
the propose-never-apply invariant (Part XII) — by ensuring confidence, however computed, however high,
never becomes adoption. (NORMATIVE: confidence is decoupled from adoption, no threshold triggers adoption,
confidence informs but never binds the judge — three statements of one guard.) (DERIVED: A2 reports
confidence richly precisely because the decoupling makes rich confidence-reporting safe — a legislature
can be fully transparent about how strongly it believes its own bills exactly because that belief has no
power to enact them. Confidence without adoption is what lets A2 be both honest and safe.)

11.10 Confidence is reported per-candidate and never aggregated into a single A2 "score." A2 does not
collapse its many candidates' confidences into one number describing A2's own quality, because such an
aggregate would be gameable and misleading — a legislature is judged by whether its adopted laws hold,
not by the average confidence of everything it proposed. (NORMATIVE: A2's effectiveness, like an
educator's (A1 Part XVIII), is measured on the downstream reality of its ADOPTED laws — do the laws the
Owner adopted on A2's proposals actually hold — never on A2's self-reported confidence in its own output.)
(DERIVED: an A2 measured on its own average confidence would be incentivized to inflate confidence or to
propose only safe, high-confidence codifications; an A2 measured on whether its adopted laws hold is
aligned with the estate's actual interest — sound legislation — the same countermetric discipline that
aligns the educator, applied to the legislature.)

FINAL LAW — PART XI. A2 reports how strongly the evidence supports each candidate, and that confidence —
however overwhelming — never adopts it, because adoption is the Owner's act and a confidence threshold
that enacted would be a self-adopting legislature by another name. Confidence is a projection over the
evidence, decoupled from adoption exactly as ACIS decouples it from level, honest about weak evidence so
that strong confidence stays meaningful, and decaying as its evidence ages. A2 can be fully transparent
about its beliefs precisely because its beliefs have no power to enact.

===============================================================================================
PART XII — THE PROPOSE-NEVER-APPLY INVARIANT
===============================================================================================

12.1 The invariant, stated as the dataset's spine. A2 proposes candidate laws; it never adopts or applies
one. (DEFINED, invariant 2, and the load-bearing safety property of the entire dataset: adoption is a
separate act by the Owner or a governance process, and A2 has no code path that enacts a law it induced.)
Every mechanism in A2 — the engine, the theory generator, the experiment designer — terminates at a
PROPOSAL, and none has an adoption path. The invariant is what makes A2 safe to build and run: a
legislature that could enact its own bills is the estate's deepest governance danger, and A2 is
constructed so it structurally cannot.

12.2 Why a self-adopting legislature is the capture risk. dfp_05 names the mechanism: "the first thing a
self-modifying governance system learns to modify is the mechanism by which it could be found wrong"
(cited). (NORMATIVE: a legislature that both drafts AND enacts its own laws could, over time, enact laws
that weaken the checks on its own drafting — the capture spiral. The separation of proposing from
adopting is the structural firewall against it, and A2's propose-never-apply is that firewall made
absolute.) (DERIVED: this is why the JUDGE-PROPOSE boundary (Part II) is a safety property and not merely
anti-duplication — a legislature that absorbed the judge would be a self-adopting one, and the whole
danger dfp_05 names would materialize. Keeping A2 unable to adopt is keeping the estate's governance
un-capturable by its own law-generator.)

12.3 The invariant is enforced by construction, not by policy. A2 does not "follow a policy" of not
adopting — it has no adoption path at all. (DEFINED: the engine writes to the Hypothesis Registry; the
registry has a graduation transition to UKDL that is triggered ONLY by an external adoption act, never by
A2 itself. There is no function in A2 that writes an adopted rule.) (DERIVED: enforcement-by-construction
is stronger than enforcement-by-policy because a policy can be violated by a bug or a drift, while a
missing code path cannot be traversed. A2 cannot adopt a law for the same reason a read-only file cannot
be written — the capability is absent, not merely forbidden, which is the only enforcement robust against
a compromised or drifting A2.)

12.4 The No-Autopromotion Invariant, inherited and extended. The estate already holds a No-Autopromotion
Invariant — no capability promotes itself. (OBSERVED, estate doctrine.) A2 inherits it and extends it to
laws: no law A2 induced promotes itself to adopted. (DERIVED: A2's propose-never-apply is the
No-Autopromotion Invariant applied to the legislature — the same principle that stops a capability
self-promoting stops a law self-adopting, because both are a system granting itself authority it should
receive only from the Owner. A2 does not invent the invariant; it applies the estate's existing one to
its own object, and names the inheritance rather than claiming it.)

12.5 Propose-never-apply extends to experiments and theories. The invariant covers not only law adoption
but experiment execution (Part IX §9.5) and theory adoption (Part VII §7.5) — A2 proposes an experiment
but does not run it, proposes a theory but does not enact it. (NORMATIVE: any A2 act that would CHANGE
the estate — adopting a law, running an estate-manipulating experiment, enacting a theory — is reserved
to an authorized external act. A2's writes are confined to its own registry.) (DERIVED: the invariant is
really one principle — A2 changes nothing about the estate except its own candidate store — applied to
every surface where A2 might otherwise act. The uniform rule is what makes A2's safety total rather than
patchy: there is no A2 surface that mutates the estate, only surfaces that propose mutations for
authorized parties to perform.)

12.6 The invariant is what lets A2 be prolific. Because A2 cannot enact, it can propose freely — the
volume of A2's induction is bounded only by the Owner's judgment capacity, not by any risk, since an
un-adopted proposal is inert. (DERIVED: the propose-never-apply invariant and the falsifier-first
discipline (Part VIII §8.10) together make A2's generative volume safe by construction — a prolific
legislature is dangerous only if it can enact, and A2 cannot, so it can induce as many falsifiable
candidates as the evidence supports without any of them binding the estate until judged. Safety-by-
construction is what unlocks generative volume.)

12.7 The invariant preserves the human's authority over the estate's laws. Adoption reserved to the Owner
means the estate's binding laws are always the ones a human chose to enact, informed by A2's proposals but
never dictated by them. (NORMATIVE: A2 shifts WHERE candidate laws come from — from a human's intuition to
the estate's evidence — but not WHO decides which bind. The Owner's authority over the estate's law is
preserved absolutely; A2 makes the Owner a better-informed legislator, not a bypassed one.) (DERIVED:
this is the deepest justification for the invariant — the estate's laws govern the estate, and the
authority over what governs must remain with the Owner, not migrate to a machine that induces well. A2
augments the legislator; it does not replace the legislature's authority.)

12.8 The invariant's honest tension with autonomy, acknowledged. There is a real tension: A2 can induce
laws faster than the Owner can judge them, so a strict propose-never-apply could bottleneck on human
judgment. (CONTESTED-honest: A2 does not resolve this by relaxing the invariant — the invariant is
absolute — but by prioritizing its proposals (confidence + evidence breadth) so the Owner's scarce
judgment goes to the best candidates first, and by surfacing orphan candidates (Part V §5.8) rather than
letting the bottleneck hide them.) (DERIVED: the tension is real and A2 names it rather than resolving it
by the forbidden shortcut of auto-adoption — the answer to "A2 out-produces judgment" is better
prioritization of what to judge, never a relaxation of who judges. The bottleneck is a feature: human
judgment over the estate's laws is exactly what must not be bypassed.)

12.9 Why this invariant is A2's most important sentence. If a single property of A2 had to survive, it is
this one — propose-never-apply — because every other capability of A2 is safe only under it. (NORMATIVE:
a powerful law-generator without the invariant is the capture risk; the same generator with the invariant
is a safe augmentation of the Owner's legislative authority. The invariant is not one feature among many;
it is the condition under which all the others are safe to have.) (DERIVED: this is why the invariant is
restated in nearly every Part — the candidate object refuses adoption, the engine has no adoption path,
confidence never triggers adoption, experiments are proposed not run — because a safety property that
matters this much cannot be stated once and trusted; it must be built into every surface, and named at
each, so no future extension of A2 can quietly add the adoption path the whole dataset was designed to
exclude.)

FINAL LAW — PART XII. A2 proposes and never adopts — enforced by construction, not policy: there is no
adoption path in A2 to traverse, the way a read-only file cannot be written. A self-adopting legislature
is the capture dfp_05 names, where a system learns first to modify how it could be found wrong, so the
separation of proposing from adopting is the estate's firewall and A2's is absolute. The invariant is
what lets A2 be prolific and safe at once, and what keeps the authority over the estate's binding laws
with the Owner — A2 makes the legislator better-informed, never bypassed.

===============================================================================================
PART XIII — COUNTEREXAMPLE SEARCH: SEEKING THE CASE THAT BREAKS THE LAW
===============================================================================================

13.1 The discipline stated. Before proposing a candidate, A2 actively seeks the case that would REFUTE
it — a counterexample search, not a confirmation search. (DEFINED: for a candidate rule "under condition
C, property P holds," A2 searches the evidence for an instance of C where P does NOT hold, and it does so
BEFORE proposing, so a candidate reaches the registry already stress-tested against the evidence that
would break it.) This is the operational form of intellectual honesty — a legislature that only sought
confirmations would propose false universals, and the counterexample search is what forces A2 to try to
break its own laws before offering them.

13.2 Why confirmation is cheap and counterexample is dear. Confirming evidence for almost any pattern is
easy to find — the human and machine tendency is to notice instances that fit and overlook those that do
not. (OBSERVED, the estate's own p-hacking failure and the confirmation bias it guards against.) A
counterexample search inverts the default: it spends effort specifically looking for the disconfirming
case, which is exactly the case the confirmation-seeking engine would miss. (DERIVED: the search is dear
precisely because it goes against the grain — finding the instance that breaks a satisfying pattern is
cognitively and computationally the opposite of finding instances that confirm it, and A2's discipline is
to pay that cost before proposing, because a false universal that survives to adoption is far more
expensive than the search that would have caught it.)

13.3 The search operates over the same substrate as induction. The counterexample search mines the same
five evidence families (Part IV §4.2) A2 induced from — but asking the opposite question: not "where does
this pattern hold" but "where, in all the evidence, does condition C occur WITHOUT property P." (DEFINED:
a found counterexample either refutes the candidate outright or reveals a boundary — condition C holds P
except under sub-condition C', which becomes part of the boundary, Part XIV.) (DERIVED: this is why
counterexample search and boundary extraction are two faces of one operation — a counterexample that
refutes the whole law kills the candidate; a counterexample that holds only in a sub-region fences the
boundary. The search's output is either a dead candidate or a sharpened one, never an unchanged one.)

13.4 The search and the experiment designer are complementary. Counterexample search looks for the
breaking case in EXISTING evidence; the experiment designer (Part IX) produces NEW evidence to test the
law. (DERIVED: for a well-recorded phenomenon, counterexample search over existing evidence may suffice;
for a thinly-recorded one, the experiment designer produces the disconfirming test the existing evidence
cannot. Together they are A2's two ways of trying to break a candidate — mine the record for the
counterexample, or design the experiment that would produce it — and a candidate that survives both is
one A2 tried hard to refute and could not, which is exactly the candidate worth proposing.)

13.5 The anti-p-hacking property. P-hacking is mining evidence until a pattern appears, then proposing
it as if the mining had not shaped it. (OBSERVED, the estate's named failure.) The counterexample search
is A2's structural defense: because A2 must actively seek the disconfirming case before proposing, a
pattern that only survives because A2 stopped looking as soon as it found confirmations cannot pass — the
search would surface the counterexamples the confirmation-mining ignored. (DERIVED: p-hacking is defeated
not by forbidding mining but by requiring the disconfirmation search alongside it — A2 mines for patterns
AND mines for their counterexamples, and only a pattern that survives the second mining is proposed. The
symmetry of the two searches is what makes the induction honest.)

13.6 The search must be recorded, not just performed. A2 records, in the candidate's evidence trail, that
the counterexample search was performed and what it found — the counterexamples that were considered and
either refuted the candidate or defined its boundary. (NORMATIVE: a candidate that claimed to be
stress-tested but recorded no search is unauditable — the search's honesty depends on its being
inspectable, so a later audit can check that A2 actually looked for the breaking case rather than
asserting it did.) (DERIVED: recording the search is what makes the anti-p-hacking property auditable —
without a record, "we searched for counterexamples" is an unverifiable claim, and A2's discipline is to
make the search a documented part of the candidate's provenance, not an asserted virtue.)

13.7 A found counterexample is valuable, not a failure. When the search finds a counterexample that kills
a candidate, that is A2 working, not A2 failing. (CONTESTED-honest: a killed candidate saved the estate
from adopting a false law, which is more valuable than the candidate would have been — the counterexample
is a positive output.) (DERIVED: an A2 that treated killed candidates as failures would be incentivized
to search less hard, which is the p-hacking incentive; an A2 that values a found counterexample as a
saved false-adoption searches honestly. This is the same framing A1 gives a non-graduating learner — the
mechanism catching a problem before production is the mechanism succeeding, and A2's counterexample that
kills a false universal before adoption is the search succeeding.)

13.8 The search bounds A2's confidence honestly. The counterexample search directly informs a candidate's
confidence — a candidate that survived a hard search with no counterexample earns higher confidence than
one whose search was cursory or which had near-misses. (DERIVED: confidence (Part XI) is partly a function
of how hard A2 tried and failed to break the candidate — a law that resisted a determined counterexample
search is better-evidenced than one that merely accumulated confirmations. The search is therefore not
only a gate (kill the false ones) but a calibrator (the survivors' confidence reflects the search's
rigor), and an honest confidence requires an honest search behind it.)

13.9 Why counterexample search is A2's scientific conscience. The willingness to seek the evidence that
would prove oneself wrong is the core of scientific honesty, and the counterexample search is where A2
enforces it structurally rather than trusting it. (DERIVED: A2 cannot be trusted to WANT to break its own
satisfying patterns — no generator can — so the discipline is built in as a required, recorded step, the
same way falsifier-first builds in the requirement to state how the law could be wrong. Between them,
falsifier-first (name the refutation condition) and counterexample search (actively seek it), A2's
intellectual honesty is a matter of construction, not disposition — which is the only way it survives an
engine that would rather be right.)

FINAL LAW — PART XIII. A2 seeks the case that breaks its own law before proposing it — a counterexample
search, not a confirmation search — because confirming evidence is cheap and the disconfirming case is
exactly the one a satisfied engine overlooks. The search defeats p-hacking by construction: a pattern
that survived only because A2 stopped looking cannot pass a mining that specifically hunts its
counterexamples. A found counterexample is the search succeeding, not failing, and the willingness to
seek one's own refutation — built in, recorded, auditable — is A2's scientific conscience, enforced by
structure because no generator can be trusted to want it.

===============================================================================================
PART XIV — BOUNDARY-CONDITION EXTRACTION: WHERE THE LAW HOLDS AND WHERE IT DOES NOT
===============================================================================================

14.1 Every law has a boundary, and stating it is mandatory. A candidate law without a boundary claims to
hold everywhere, and a law claimed to hold everywhere is almost always a false universal (Part XX).
(DEFINED, invariant 5: every candidate states its boundary — the domain of conditions under which it
holds — and a candidate with no boundary is malformed and not proposed.) The boundary is as load-bearing
as the rule: "Bash calls hang" is a false universal; "Bash calls of external toolchains on Windows hosts
hang" is a bounded, true law. The qualifier is not a caveat appended to the rule — it is part of what the
rule IS.

14.2 The boundary is extracted from the disconfirming evidence. A2 extracts the boundary from the cases
where the pattern did NOT hold — the counterexamples (Part XIII) that did not kill the candidate but
fenced it. (DEFINED: the boundary is the condition separating instances where property P held from
instances where it failed; it is found by examining the near-misses, not by asserting a scope.) (DERIVED:
this is why boundary extraction requires the counterexample search — a boundary asserted without looking
at where the pattern fails is a guess, and a boundary extracted from the actual failing cases is
evidence-grounded. The boundary is the fence the disconfirming evidence draws, and A2 draws it from that
evidence rather than from intuition about where the law "should" apply.)

14.3 The boundary and the A1 cross-domain loop. A1's cross-domain education (A1 Part XV) surfaces exactly
the boundary information A2 needs — a competence's native assumption that held in one domain and failed in
another is evidence of where a law's boundary lies. (DERIVED: when A1 discovers that "every path returns a
value" held for Python and failed for shell, that is boundary evidence for any law involving return-value
assumptions — the boundary is "languages with mandatory returns." The A1→A2 loop (Part IV §4.2) feeds A2
boundary evidence A2 could not get from its own ledgers, because A1's cross-domain teaching actively probes
where assumptions break, which is precisely where boundaries lie.) A1 surfaces the assumption failure; A2
extracts the boundary it implies.

14.4 A boundary too narrow and a boundary too wide are both failures. A boundary drawn too WIDE claims the
law holds where it does not (a false universal in miniature); a boundary too NARROW claims the law holds
only in a sub-region when it actually holds more broadly (an under-general law). (DEFINED, two boundary
errors.) (DERIVED: A2 extracts the boundary at exactly the extent the evidence supports — no wider (which
the counterexamples would refute) and no narrower (which would waste the law's generality). The evidence
sets the boundary, and both over-claiming and under-claiming the scope are errors the extraction must
avoid — the boundary is a claim about extent, and like any claim it must match its evidence.)

14.5 The boundary is what makes a law composable into a theory. A theory (Part VII) composes laws, and
composition requires knowing each law's boundary — a theory that combined laws without respecting their
boundaries would claim explanatory reach beyond where its component laws hold. (DERIVED: the boundary is
the interface at which laws compose — a theory's explanatory structure is valid only within the
intersection of its laws' boundaries, and knowing each boundary is what lets A2 compose a theory whose
reach is honestly bounded. A composition that ignored boundaries would be a theory over-claiming its
scope, the paradigm-thrash failure at the composition level.)

14.6 The boundary decays and shifts as the estate changes. A law's boundary is not permanent — as the
estate changes, a boundary can shift (a law that held on one platform may extend to a new one, or cease
to hold on an old one). (DERIVED: the boundary, like the rule and the confidence, is a projection over
current evidence, and it recomputes as evidence accrues — a candidate whose boundary was "Windows hosts"
might extend if new evidence shows the pattern on another host, or contract if the estate's tooling
changes. A2's boundary is a living claim, and a boundary asserted once and never revisited would be the
stale-law failure relocated to the law's scope.)

14.7 An unbounded candidate is the false universal caught at the object level. Because the boundary is a
mandatory part of a well-formed candidate (Part III §3.5), an unbounded candidate literally cannot be
proposed — the false-universal failure is closed by construction, the same way falsifier-first closes the
unfalsifiable-law failure. (NORMATIVE: A2 does not rely on an audit to catch false universals; it makes a
boundary mandatory so an unbounded universal is malformed at the object level and never reaches the
registry.) (DERIVED: this is the object-shape-as-discipline principle again — A2's quality is enforced by
what a well-formed candidate must contain, and a boundary is one of the four mandatory parts precisely
because its absence is a named failure mode.)

14.8 The boundary is a claim the falsifier must respect. A candidate's falsifier tests the law WITHIN its
boundary, never outside it — a falsifier that tested the law where it never claimed to hold would be
incoherent. (DERIVED, the falsifier-boundary duality of Part VIII §8.7: the boundary says where the law
applies, the falsifier says what would refute it there, and the two must be consistent. A2 extracts them
jointly so a candidate never carries a falsifier testing outside its boundary — the boundary and the
falsifier are two views of the law's extent, and a coherent candidate keeps them aligned.)

14.9 The boundary is where A2's honesty about generality lives. The temptation in any generalization is
to over-claim its scope — a broad law feels more valuable than a narrow one. (CONTESTED, resolved by the
evidence: A2 draws the boundary at the evidence's extent, resisting the pull toward a grander universal,
because an over-broad law is a false universal and a false universal that reaches adoption is a law the
estate will obey where it does not hold.) (DERIVED: the boundary is where A2 resists its own incentive
toward grandeur — the same way falsifier-first resists confirmation bias — and draws the honest fence the
evidence supports rather than the impressive universal it might prefer. A2's generality is exactly what
its evidence licenses, no more.)

14.10 The boundary is what a law's adopter and teacher both need. When a candidate is adopted (Part XVII)
and later taught (Part XVIII, the A2→A1 seam), its boundary travels with it — the adopter needs to know
where the law binds, and A1 needs to teach where it applies and where it does not. (DERIVED: a law taught
without its boundary would produce the assumption-carry failure A1's cross-domain education (A1 Part XV)
exists to prevent — a learner taught "Bash calls hang" without "on Windows hosts" would wrongly avoid Bash
on Linux. The boundary is therefore not only A2's honesty about generality but a mandatory input to
teaching the law correctly, and preserving it through adoption and into the curriculum is what keeps the
taught law as bounded as the induced one.)

FINAL LAW — PART XIV. Every candidate law states its boundary — the fence between where it holds and where
it fails — extracted from the disconfirming cases, not asserted, because a law claimed to hold everywhere
is a false universal and the qualifier is part of what the rule is. A boundary too wide over-claims and
too narrow wastes generality; A2 draws it at exactly the evidence's extent. It is the interface at which
laws compose into theories, it decays and shifts as the estate changes, and it is where A2 resists its own
pull toward an impressive universal in favor of the honest fence the evidence supports.
