## 1. Ownership — the institutional object A1 holds

**The estate's education system: the mechanism that takes a capability, agent, or model
instance from *cannot-be-trusted-yet* to *certified estate member*, and keeps it there
through decay.** Not the building of a capability, not the valuation of it, not the proof
that it will not regress — the *teaching* of it: curriculum, apprenticeship, synthetic
experience, failure simulation, assessment, certification, recertification, and the
graduation ladder from supervised to bounded to full autonomy.

The founding gap, stated as the Non-Duplication Article II test: the estate on 2026-07-20
holds 70+ systems and 20+ sealed dataset domains, and every one of them presumes its
operators already know how to use it. No system OWNS the transition from ignorance to
competence for a new participant. The router (IAS-A1) routes to capabilities assumed
already trustworthy; the portfolio (IAS-C1) values capabilities assumed already built; the
immune system (IAS-D2) proves a fix will not recur *after* someone competent wrote it.
None teaches. A capability arrives certified by a human session's implicit judgment, every
time. A1 is the object that makes that judgment explicit, evidenced, and repeatable.

## 2. Non-ownership — what A1 must NOT absorb (anti-inflation boundary)

Each line names a sibling owner A1 defers to. Crossing any of these is the misattribution
this whole audit exists to prevent.

- **Does NOT build capabilities.** The foundry step (certified corpus → running capability)
  is the Science IV / Tier-B4 gap, seam DFP FREEZE → IAS-C1 FUNDED. A1 educates a
  capability that already exists; it never authors one.
- **Does NOT propose laws.** That is A2 (Theory Generator). A1 *teaches* laws already
  written; A2 writes them.
- **Does NOT value or allocate capital.** IAS-C1 owns portfolio valuation. Education *cost*
  is one input A1 hands to C1; A1 never ranks holdings.
- **Does NOT prove non-recurrence.** IAS-D2 owns the antibody record and immunity decay.
  A1 consumes D2's decay signal as a recertification trigger; it never authors the proof.
- **Does NOT distill answers.** FD distills a finished insight into a deposit. A1 consumes
  distillations as *teaching material*; distilling for transfer is FD's object, teaching
  from the distillate is A1's.
- **Does NOT certify a corpus.** DFP certifies that a governing corpus exists and is frozen.
  A1 certifies a *learner*. Different subject, same word.
- **Does NOT judge on the epistemic ladder.** ACIS levels a claim E0–E7. A1 assesses a
  learner's competence, a distinct object from a theorem's evidential standing.

## 3. Consumers (who reads A1's output)

- **IAS-A1 capability router** — reads A1's certification state to decide whether a
  capability may be routed to unsupervised.
- **Bounded-autonomy graduation (PART XVII)** feeds the router a trust tier, not a boolean.
- **IAS-C1 portfolio** — education cost per capability is a capital input to valuation.
- **Model-onboarding pipeline (PART XVI)** — the entry gate for a new model instance.
- **IAS-D2 immune system** — supplies the decay signal that triggers recertification.

## 4. Dependencies (upstream)

**None.** Confirmed 0%-owned by targeted search across `vault/knowledge_base/`, `modules/`,
`agents/`, `commands/`; every hit was a vocabulary collision (human onboarding, SaaS

## 5. Invariants

1. **No certification without observed assessment.** A credential asserted from a proxy
   (passed-the-curriculum) and not from behaviour is void — the SQI evidence-physics rule,
   cited not rebuilt.
2. **No permanent certification.** Every credential carries a decay clock; competence
   unexercised or its substrate changed drops the learner to recertify. (Cites IAS-D2 §13,
   FD-04 decay — A1 owns the *educational* consequence, not the decay proof.)
3. **The educator may not grade its own graduate on its own activity log** (anti-gaming,
   parallels IAS-C1 §14.5 governance-yield countermetric).
4. **Graduation is a ladder, never a switch** — supervised → bounded → full, each rung an
   evidenced gate, no skipping.
5. **Teaching-to-the-test is a failure, not a pass** (PART XX diploma-mill taxonomy).

## 6. Failure taxonomy (what breaks, enumerated in PART XXIV)

Credential inflation · teaching-to-the-test · the diploma mill (curriculum with no
practicum) · stale curriculum teaching a moved-on substrate · educator conflict of interest
· graduation-by-elapsed-time · orphan learner (educated, never routed) · cross-domain
transfer that carries a native assumption into a foreign domain.
