## 1. Ownership — the institutional object A2 holds

**The estate's legislature: the generative half of meta-science.** Every existing owner *judges* a
law a human or a frontier session already wrote — ACIS falsifies and levels it on the E0–E7 ladder,
DRK routes decisions under it, UKDL stores it, CEPS records the events it governs. **Nothing proposes
one.** A2 is the object that mines the estate's accumulated evidence — FD-07 deposits, fd_04 proofs,
UKDL rules, CEPS events, and the cross-domain assumption evidence A1 surfaces — to INDUCE a candidate
law: a proposed general rule the estate did not have, carrying a concrete falsifier, offered for
judgment and adoption.

The founding gap, stated as the Non-Duplication Article II test: **ACIS is a tribunal without a
legislature.** The estate has a court (SQI/ACIS), a case-router (DRK), a statute book (UKDL), and a
docket (CEPS) — and no body that drafts a bill. Candidate laws today are written by the Owner or a
frontier session, by hand, from intuition. A2 is the object that induces them from the estate's own
evidence, so a general rule the evidence supports but no one has articulated is proposed rather than
waited-for.

## 2. Non-ownership — what A2 must NOT absorb (anti-inflation boundary)

- **Does NOT judge or level a law.** ACIS owns the E0–E7 ladder and the falsifier gate. A2 attaches a
  falsifier at PROPOSAL; ACIS enforces it at JUDGMENT. A2 proposes; ACIS adjudicates.
- **Does NOT adopt a law autonomously.** The No-Autopromotion Invariant and DFP-05's propose-never-
  apply bind A2 absolutely: A2 proposes, the Owner/governance adopts. A2 that self-adopted a law it
  induced would be the self-modifying-governance capture risk DFP-05 names.
- **Does NOT route decisions.** DRK owns decision burden and routing. A2 induces the general rule;
  DRK applies it to a specific decision.
- **Does NOT store the statute.** UKDL owns the adopted-rule store. A2 owns the CANDIDATE store
  (Hypothesis Registry, PART V); an adopted candidate graduates OUT of A2 into UKDL.
- **Does NOT record events.** CEPS owns the event log. A2 READS CEPS events as evidence; it writes
  none.
- **Does NOT teach the law.** A1 owns education. An adopted law becomes A1 curriculum material — the
  A2→A1 seam (PART XVIII), one-way.
- **Does NOT distill.** FD compresses a finished insight. A2 INDUCES a general rule from many
  deposits — a different operation on a different object (a law is not a deposit).

## 3. Consumers (who reads A2's output)

- **ACIS / the Owner** — receive a candidate law with its falsifier for judgment and adoption (PART XVII).
- **UKDL** — receives an adopted candidate as a new stored rule.
- **A1 Cognitive Education** — turns an adopted law into curriculum (PART XVIII).
- **DRK** — applies an adopted general rule to specific decisions.
- **IAS-C1** — receives the cost of theory generation as one capital input (PART XXI).

## 4. Dependencies (upstream)

- **B2 · epistemic algebra unification** — A2 induces over evidence of many types (DRK-00 evidence
  records, DAIF-01 statuses, ACIS levels, CEPS events); it needs ONE algebra to induce over, which
  B2 supplies. Without B2, A2 induces over incommensurable evidence vocabularies.
- **B1 · unknown-unknown surface** — a generator needs the unmapped space; B1 supplies the surface of
  what is NOT yet represented, the richest source of genuinely new laws.
- The DATASET (this spec) is authorable now; the two dependencies are wiring the BUILD needs, recorded
  honestly in-dataset (PART IV §evidence, PART X §novelty, PART XXIV §residual).

## 5. Invariants

1. **No candidate law without a concrete falsifier** — a named measurement, signal, and window
   (cites ACIS's falsifier standard; A2 attaches it at proposal). A law that cannot be refuted is
   not a law, it is a slogan.
2. **Propose-never-apply** — A2 never adopts or applies a law it induced; adoption is a separate,
   Owner/governance act (No-Autopromotion + DFP-05).
3. **Induction over evidence, never over intuition** — every candidate law names the evidence it was
   induced from; a law with no evidence trail is void.
4. **Counterexample-seeking, not confirmation-seeking** — a candidate law is stress-tested against the
   case that would break it before it is proposed (anti-p-hacking, PART XIII).
5. **Boundary-conditioned** — every candidate law states where it holds and where it does not; an
   unbounded universal is a false universal (PART XIV, XX).

## 6. Failure taxonomy (PART XXIV)

The false universal (a law induced from a biased sample) · p-hacking the ledgers (mining until a
pattern appears) · the unfalsifiable law · paradigm-thrash (replacing frameworks faster than evidence
warrants) · the self-adopted law (invariant 2 violation) · the evidence-planted law (adversarial,
PART XXII) · the orphan candidate (induced, never judged).
