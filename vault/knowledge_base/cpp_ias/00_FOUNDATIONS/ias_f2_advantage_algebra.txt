PART I — THE UNIT OF INSTITUTIONAL ADVANTAGE
===============================================================================================

1.1 The missing currency. The estate, as OBSERVED across the Non-Duplication Ledger and the
System-Strengthening Matrix, already produces numbers. D2A-5 scores a proposed duplication's
disposition. CO-01 prices a session's cognitive cost. token_irr computes a return on token spend
across a dataset's lifetime. recall_roi measures how often a stored capability is reused before it
decays. GK-09 scores the health of a knowledge-navigation path. Each of these numbers is correct
within its own domain and incommensurable outside it: a token_irr of 1.8 and a GK-09 health score of
0.65 cannot be added, ranked against each other, or fed into a single allocation decision, because
they are denominated in different currencies that happen to share the visual form of a number.
(OBSERVED: five parent systems, five score domains, zero shared unit — this is the precise gap
IAS-F2 exists to close, per the System-Strengthening Matrix row for F2.) A federation that cannot
compare a token_irr improvement against a GK-09 improvement cannot allocate its cognitive capital
rationally between them; it can only act on whichever number was computed most recently or shouted
loudest. This is the founding observation that makes F2 necessary and, per Article II of the
Constitution, necessary at the ensemble level rather than as a sixth resource-level scorer.

1.2 What a shared unit must do, and must not do. A shared unit of institutional advantage must
satisfy three properties or it is not a unit, only a relabeling. First, COMMENSURABILITY: any two
quantities expressed in the unit must be meaningfully addable, comparable, or ratio-able, which rules
out a unit defined as "whatever each parent already outputs, renamed." Second, TRACEABILITY: a value
in the unit must be decomposable back to the OBSERVED or DERIVED facts that produced it, so a
disputed score can be audited to its origin rather than merely asserted. Third, NON-INFLATION: the
unit must resist the temptation to expand its own scope to claim credit for value it did not cause —
this is the anti-Goodhart discipline formalized in Part X and is a NORMATIVE constraint on the unit's
design, not an afterthought bolted onto a finished formula. (DERIVED: a unit failing traceability
cannot be audited by IAS-E1's Observability Fabric; a unit failing non-inflation poisons IAS-C1's
Portfolio the moment it is used to rank allocation choices — the unit's correctness is therefore a
precondition for two sibling datasets, not a cosmetic detail.)

1.3 Defining the Institutional Advantage Unit (IAU). The INSTITUTIONAL ADVANTAGE UNIT, as DEFINED in
this corpus, is a composite, dimensionless, bounded quantity in the closed interval [-1, +1] that
expresses the net contribution a system, capability, or intervention makes to the ensemble's capacity
to accomplish missions, relative to the cost of maintaining that contribution. Zero IAU means the
item is exactly self-funding at the estate level: it returns as much capability as it consumes in
complexity, overlap, and maintenance burden. Positive IAU means the item is a net amplifier — it makes
the rest of the ensemble more capable than its own footprint would predict. Negative IAU means the
item is a net drag: a parasite in the vocabulary of IAS-D1, a candidate for the extinction protocol
that dataset owns. The IAU is deliberately NOT a token count, a dollar figure, or a line-count metric,
because those measure the resource consumed, not the advantage produced; the IAU is a ratio-derived
score whose numerator and denominator are specified fully in Parts III-IV. (DEFINED, with the bound
[-1,+1] itself NORMATIVE — a design choice explained in 1.5.)

1.4 Why the unit is composite and bounded, not raw. An unbounded score invites false precision (Part
XVI) and cannot be meaningfully averaged across a portfolio without one outlier dominating the mean.
Bounding the IAU to [-1, +1] forces every scorer to express advantage as a RATIO against the item's
own footprint rather than an absolute magnitude, which has two consequences the family depends on.
First, a small utility script and a large architectural system can both score near +1 if each is
efficient relative to its own cost — the unit measures efficiency of contribution, not scale of
contribution, and scale is tracked separately as the systems-strengthened count (Part V) precisely so
the two are never confused. Second, the bound makes the IAU compatible with the compounding formula of
Part VI, which multiplies advantage scores across a chain of systems; an unbounded score would let the
product explode or collapse in ways that have nothing to do with real compounding and everything to do
with arithmetic artifact. (DERIVED: boundedness is a precondition for a well-behaved compounding
product, established formally in Part VI.)

1.5 Boundary conditions: what the IAU does not measure. The IAU is not a measure of correctness (a
system can be architecturally sound and still score near zero if it has no consumers — DRK owns
decision correctness, not F2). It is not a measure of urgency (owner_queue and backlog_autopilot own
prioritization by time-sensitivity; the IAU is a standing property of a system's contribution, not a
schedule). It is not a measure of popularity or usage frequency alone (recall_roi owns reuse frequency
as one local metric that maps INTO the IAU via Part XIII, but frequency without amplification effect
would double-count reuse as if it were advantage, which is exactly the Goodhart failure Part X exists
to prevent). Drawing these boundaries precisely is what keeps F2 from re-narrating D2A, CO-01,
token_irr, recall_roi, or GK-09 rather than strengthening them: F2 does not compute a competing
version of any of their numbers; it defines the shared unit their numbers convert into, and the
conversion is a mapping (Part XIII), not a replacement. (NORMATIVE.)

1.6 The unit as the family's Rosetta artifact. Every later Part of this dataset — the decomposition,
the Fusion Score, the compounding formula, the two named metrics (NICG and DAR) — is built on the
IAU as its atomic quantity. Every sibling IAS dataset that consumes a "score" (IAS-C1's Portfolio,
IAS-B1's Cognitive Multiplier, IAS-D1's Ecology, IAS-G1's Architecture Intelligence, per the
Compounding Graph's row for F2) consumes it as an IAU or an aggregate of IAUs, never as a parent's
raw local number. This is the Rosetta property: the estate keeps its five existing scorers exactly as
they are, unmodified, and gains one small, auditable translation layer that lets their outputs speak
to each other for the first time. (DERIVED from Article III of the Constitution: strengthen, never
replace — F2 strengthens five parents by giving their numbers a common tongue, and replaces none of
their computation.)

1.7 A worked micro-example of the zero point, stated before the full apparatus is introduced. Consider
two synthetic, illustrative systems, neither real, offered only to make the zero-point definition of
1.3 concrete before Part XVIII's fuller worked ensemble arrives. System P is a small utility that
closes a genuine gap, gets invoked by two other systems a handful of times, and requires no ongoing
attention — its footprint is tiny and its contribution, while modest in absolute terms, is roughly
proportional to its cost: P is close to IAU zero, self-funding, neither an amplifier nor a drag. System
Q is larger, closes the same size of gap, gets invoked more often in absolute terms than P, but also
requires substantial ongoing correction, duplicates roughly a third of an existing system's coverage,
and is difficult for anyone but its original author to extend safely — Q's absolute usage is higher than
P's, but its IAU may still land BELOW P's, because the unit measures the ratio of amplification to
footprint, not the raw magnitude of either. (DEFINED, illustrative.) This is the precise, concrete
sense in which 1.4's claim — "a small utility script and a large architectural system can both score
near +1 if each is efficient relative to its own cost" — cuts both ways: largeness is not rewarded and
largeness is not punished; only the ratio is scored, and a reviewer's intuition that "Q does more, so Q
must be worth more" is exactly the intuition the IAU is built to correct, because Q's extra usage was
purchased at a cost P did not pay, and until that cost is netted out, "does more" is a claim about
Q's footprint, not about Q's advantage. (NORMATIVE: this is the founding intuition every later factor
and formula in this dataset operationalizes precisely.)

FINAL LAW — PART I. An institution with five scorers and no shared unit can rank nothing against
anything; the Institutional Advantage Unit is not a sixth score but the currency the other five were
missing, bounded and ratio-derived so it can be compared, audited, and compounded without inventing a
seventh, competing measurement of value.

PART II — OBJECTS AND RELATIONS: WHAT F2 ADDS TO THE F1 ONTOLOGY
===============================================================================================

2.1 F2 is a thin object layer, not a new ontology. Per Article II of the Constitution, F2 must not
re-narrate F1's primitives; it introduces only the objects a measurement substrate needs and defines
each strictly in terms of F1's SYSTEM, CAPABILITY, and ENSEMBLE STATE. Four new objects are DEFINED
here: the SCORE, the CLAIM, the EVIDENCE LEVEL, and the FUSION SCORE. A SCORE is a numeric property —
typically an IAU or a component of one — attached to exactly one F1 object (a system, a subsystem, a
capability, or the ensemble state) at a stated point in the ensemble's history. A CLAIM is the
proposition a score asserts, stated in natural language alongside the number ("system X contributed
0.34 IAU to the ensemble as of mission M"), because a bare number without its claim is unauditable. An
EVIDENCE LEVEL is the epistemic status of a claim, drawn from the Constitution's Article V vocabulary
and specialized for scores in Part XII. A FUSION SCORE is an aggregate over a named set of component
scores, defined fully in Part V. (DEFINED, all four.)

2.2 The relation SCORES. F2 introduces one relation type into F1's relation model: SCORES(measurer,
target, score, evidence_level, timestamp), read as "measurer attaches score to target with the stated
evidence level at the stated time." The measurer is always a system (D2A-5, CO-01, token_irr,
recall_roi, GK-09, or an IAS dataset itself); the target is always an F1 object; the score is an IAU or
IAU-component; the evidence level is one of the Article V terms. This relation is what lets IAS-E1's
Observability Fabric later render a complete score history for any system in the ensemble, and what
lets a disputed score be traced to exactly one measurer, one moment, and one evidence level rather than
floating as an anonymous assertion. (DERIVED: without a typed SCORES relation, a score is just a number
printed somewhere, which is precisely the state of the estate before F2 — five parents printing numbers
nobody can trace, compare, or audit collectively.)

2.3 Scores are not properties of a system in the ontological sense; they are claims about a system. This
distinction, easy to blur, is load-bearing. F1's Part III established that the ensemble state is
"explicitly a claimed state until observed" — F2 extends that discipline to every score: a score is
never treated as an intrinsic property of the system it targets (the way "distinct object" is an
intrinsic property in F1's systemhood test); it is always treated as a dated assertion made by a named
measurer with a stated evidence level, subject to revision when new evidence arrives. (NORMATIVE: this
prevents the single most common failure mode of institutional scoring — a number computed once and then
treated as permanent fact, which Part XV names as evidence decay and Part XVI names as false precision's
close cousin.)

2.4 The DECOMPOSES-INTO relation. A Fusion Score DECOMPOSES-INTO a named, ordered set of component
scores (Part III-IV specify the eight canonical components of an Advantage Score; Part V specifies the
five canonical components of a Fusion Score). This relation is what makes a Fusion Score auditable
rather than a black box: given a Fusion Score of 0.41 for some system, the DECOMPOSES-INTO relation
lets any consumer — a human Owner, IAS-E1's fabric, IAS-C1's portfolio — retrieve the five (or eight)
numbers that produced it and inspect which components are strong and which are weak, rather than
accepting or rejecting the aggregate blind. (DERIVED from the traceability property required of the
unit in Part I.)

2.5 The AMPLIFIES and DRAGS-ON relations, imported not invented. F1's relation model already carries a
STRENGTHENS relation (used throughout the System-Strengthening Matrix) and the Compounding Graph's
PRODUCES/CONSUMES edges. F2 does not invent parallel relations; it computes its scores FROM the
strength and direction of those existing relations. A system's white-space and systems-strengthened
components (Part III) are read directly off the count and quality of its outbound STRENGTHENS edges in
F1's relation graph; its overlap and complexity components (Part IV) are read off contested or
redundant edges. This is a second instance of the Rosetta property from Part I: F2 measures the
ensemble's existing relation graph, it does not overlay a second, competing graph on top of it.
(DERIVED: measuring an existing graph rather than building a parallel one is what keeps F2 an
ensemble-level measurement layer rather than a seventh coordination system — the exact trap F1 Part 1.2
warned against.)

2.6 Object cardinality and lifecycle preview. Each F1 object may carry many scores over its lifetime
(one per measurer, per mission, per evidence-level update) but exactly one CURRENT Fusion Score at any
given ensemble state — the most recent aggregate that has not been superseded. The lifecycle by which a
score moves from freshly computed to current to superseded to stale is specified fully in Part XV; this
Part establishes only that the object model supports the lifecycle (scores are dated and ordered, never
overwritten in place) so that a stale score can always be distinguished from a fresh one rather than
silently masquerading as current. (DEFINED.)

2.7 A worked instantiation of the SCORES relation. Concretely, a single SCORES tuple reads: measurer =
"recall_roi"; target = synthetic system SYN-A (introduced fully in Part XVIII); score = "Reuse factor,
0.70"; evidence_level = "OBSERVED"; timestamp = "ensemble state E_3." This tuple is what lets a
downstream consumer — say, IAS-C1's Portfolio, once built — ask two separate, answerable questions
that an untyped number could never support: "what is the CURRENT Reuse score for SYN-A" (a lookup
filtered to the most recent, non-superseded tuple per target) and "who asserted this, and when, and how
confident were they" (a direct read of the tuple's remaining three fields). Without the relation typed
this way, a score is a bare figure sitting in a report, and a reviewer wanting to check it must locate
the report, locate the paragraph, and manually extract the context — exactly the friction that made the
estate's five pre-F2 scorers mutually unusable in the first place (Part I.1). (DERIVED, worked.)

2.8 The multiple-simultaneous-measurer edge case, previewed. Nothing in the object model of 2.1-2.6
prevents two different measurers from independently computing a SCORES tuple for the same target at
overlapping timestamps — D2A-5 and a human reviewer might both assess the same system's WhiteSpace in
the same ensemble-state window. The object model does not resolve this case itself; it only ensures the
case is representable rather than silently overwritten (per the append-only cardinality rule of 2.6): both
tuples are retained, both are visible, and the question of which one is CURRENT, or whether the pair
constitutes a CONTESTED disagreement requiring the conflicting-scores protocol, is answered by Part
XIV.3's decision model, not by the ontology layer defined here. (NORMATIVE: this Part's job is to make
the disagreement representable; Part XIV's job is to decide what happens once it is represented — a
deliberate separation of concerns between "what can be said" and "what is done about what is said" that
keeps this Part's object model stable even as the decision protocols built on top of it evolve.)

FINAL LAW — PART II. F2 adds exactly four objects and two relations to the federation ontology — score,
claim, evidence level, Fusion Score, SCORES, DECOMPOSES-INTO — and derives every one of them from F1's
existing systems and relation graph rather than building a parallel structure; a measurement layer that
needs its own private ontology to measure the ensemble has already stopped measuring it.

PART III — THE ADVANTAGE SCORE DECOMPOSITION, PART ONE: THE NUMERATOR FACTORS
===============================================================================================

3.1 The decomposition as a ratio of forces. The Institutional Advantage Score for a system S, as
DEFINED, is not a single measured quantity; it is a ratio of eight independently measurable factors,
three in a rough "amplification" grouping that principally drive the numerator, three in a "cost"
grouping that principally drive the denominator, and two — cross-project reach and model-independence —
that modulate both sides because they change how durable the amplification is, not merely how large it
is. Stated as a form (the precise arithmetic is fixed in Part IV once all eight factors are defined):

  Advantage(S) = [ WhiteSpace(S) x SystemsStrengthened(S) x Reuse(S) x CrossProject(S) x
                    Compounding(S) x ModelIndependence(S) ]
                  divided by
                  [ Complexity(S) x Overlap(S) x Maintenance(S) ]

This Part defines the six numerator-side factors; Part IV defines the three denominator-side factors
and states the full normalized formula. Each factor below is itself a small measurement protocol, not a
single number pulled from thin air — a discipline the Constitution's Article V demands and Part XI
formalizes as "measurement discipline." (DEFINED, structure; each factor's derivation, DERIVED per
sub-section.)

3.2 WHITE-SPACE — the gap-closure factor. WhiteSpace(S), DEFINED, measures the degree to which S closes
a genuine capability gap the ensemble previously lacked, scaled zero (S duplicates fully covered
territory) to one (S is the sole provider of a capability the ensemble had zero coverage of before S
existed). WhiteSpace is computed by consulting the Non-Duplication Ledger's verdict for S's founding
candidate: a REJECT or MERGE-INTO-FAMILY verdict caps WhiteSpace near zero (S adds no new capability,
by the ledger's own finding); a BUILD verdict with zero prior candidates in the same family scores
WhiteSpace near one; a SUBENGINE or EXTEND verdict scores in between, proportional to how much of the
capability was genuinely absent versus inherited from a parent. (DERIVED: WhiteSpace is the F2
expression of the D2A verdict, imported by reference exactly as F1 Part 2.2 imports D2A's verdict
vocabulary — this is the first of the five mapped-parent relationships fully specified in Part XIII.)

3.3 SYSTEMS-STRENGTHENED — the breadth factor. SystemsStrengthened(S), DEFINED, is a normalized count
of the distinct parent systems whose value, decision quality, or cost improves because S exists, per
the System-Strengthening Matrix's own definition of "strengthen" (Article III: produces more value,
makes better decisions, or costs less because the IAS system exists — never replaces). This factor is
given its own full metric treatment in Part V because it is also the primary input to two of the
family's named indices (the Fusion Score and Net Institutional Capability Gain); here it is stated only
as a component: the raw count of strengthened parents, normalized against the ensemble's current system
count so that a large ensemble does not automatically inflate every system's score merely by having more
potential parents to strengthen. (DERIVED, normalization NORMATIVE.)

3.4 REUSE — the redeployment factor. Reuse(S), DEFINED, measures how often S's capability is invoked by
a mission, system, or agent OTHER than the one that first built or requested it, over a trailing window
of ensemble history. This is the factor into which recall_roi's local metric maps most directly (Part
XIII specifies the mapping precisely): a capability reused ten times across four distinct consuming
systems scores higher than one invoked once by its own creator, because reuse without redeployment is
not yet institutional advantage — it is merely a working feature. (OBSERVED premise: recall_roi already
tracks reuse-before-decay for stored capabilities; DERIVED: Reuse(S) is that same observation renormalized
into the [0,1] band F2's formula requires.)

3.5 CROSS-PROJECT — the portability factor. CrossProject(S), DEFINED, measures whether S's advantage is
confined to the single project or repository in which it was built, or portable to other projects the
estate governs. A capability that only functions inside one KobiiCraft-shaped repository scores low
here even if its Reuse factor within that repository is high, because the ensemble the estate governs
spans multiple projects (per F1 Part 3.4's boundary: "its systems, its datasets, its modules, and the
projects the estate governs" — plural). CrossProject is deliberately kept separate from Reuse because
the two can diverge sharply: a capability can be reused constantly within one project (high Reuse, low
CrossProject) or invoked rarely but in every project that has needed it (moderate Reuse, high
CrossProject), and the two failure modes require different remediation — the first needs promotion
outward, the second needs discovery improvement. (DERIVED, distinction NORMATIVE.)

3.6 COMPOUNDING and MODEL-INDEPENDENCE — the durability factors. Compounding(S), DEFINED, measures
whether S's advantage grows, holds steady, or decays as the ensemble's other systems evolve around it —
formalized fully as its own named formula in Part VI, and included here as a numerator factor because a
system whose advantage compounds over missions is worth more, at any given instant, than one whose
advantage is a one-time gain that will not recur. ModelIndependence(S), DEFINED, measures whether S's
advantage depends on the properties of one specific underlying model (a prompt tuned to one model's
quirks, a capability that only functions with one context-window size) or survives a model swap intact
— importing, by REFERENCE and not by re-derivation per Article II, the anti-model-dependence doctrine
the Ledger folds into IAS-G1 from Family M (Optionality). A high-WhiteSpace, high-Reuse system that
breaks on every model upgrade is fragile advantage, and ModelIndependence is the factor that prevents
the algebra from rewarding fragility as if it were durability. (DERIVED, both factors; HYPOTHESIZED that
the two are the complete set of durability modulators — Part XX opens this as a question.)

3.7 Interaction and independence among the six numerator factors. The six factors are defined
separately, but they are not statistically independent in practice, and this Part closes by naming the
correlations a reviewer should expect rather than be surprised by. WhiteSpace and SystemsStrengthened
tend to move together, because a system that closes a genuine gap (per the Ledger's BUILD verdict)
typically does so by serving multiple parents that previously had no coverage for that gap — a
correlation that is EXPECTED and not itself evidence of double-counting, since the two factors measure
different things (novelty of the gap versus breadth of the resulting relationships) even when driven by
the same underlying cause. Reuse and CrossProject, by contrast, are DELIBERATELY decorrelated by design
(Part III.5) precisely because the family needed a factor that could catch the specific case where they
diverge — a system reused constantly in one project but never exported is a distinct institutional
condition from one that travels widely but is invoked rarely, and collapsing the two into a single
"reuse" factor would erase that distinction. Compounding and ModelIndependence are the least correlated
pair in the numerator: a system can show strong Compounding while being fragile to a model swap it has
not yet experienced (CONTESTED until the swap occurs and either confirms or refutes the fragility), which
is precisely why both are carried as separate factors rather than folded into one "durability" score — a
single durability factor would hide exactly this divergence, the same objection Part V.3 raises against
collapsing the Fusion Score's five dimensions into one number. (DERIVED, and this sub-section is the
numerator-side analogue of Part IV's forthcoming denominator-side sensitivity discussion.)

FINAL LAW — PART III. Advantage is not one number pulled from a system's reputation; it is the product
of six independently falsifiable factors — how much genuine gap it closes, how many parents it
strengthens, how often it is redeployed, how far it travels across projects, whether its advantage
compounds, and whether it survives a model swap — and a system strong on one factor and empty on the
rest has not earned a high score, only a plausible story.

===============================================================================================
PART IV — THE ADVANTAGE SCORE DECOMPOSITION, PART TWO: THE DENOMINATOR AND THE FULL FORMULA
===============================================================================================

4.1 Why advantage needs a denominator at all. A numerator-only score rewards every system that closes
gaps, strengthens parents, gets reused, travels, compounds, and survives model swaps — which is to say,
it rewards scale and activity, exactly the failure mode F1 Part 2.1 named as clone-family metric
inflation and the failure mode this whole family exists to avoid (Constitution Article I: a duplication
converted to advantage, never merely counted as more). A system that achieves all six numerator factors
by being enormous, tangled, and expensive to keep alive has not produced net institutional advantage; it
has produced gross capability at a cost the ensemble may not be able to sustain. The denominator is what
converts gross capability into net advantage, and it is exactly as load-bearing as the numerator.
(NORMATIVE, DERIVED from Article I.)

4.2 COMPLEXITY — the comprehension-cost factor. Complexity(S), DEFINED, measures the cognitive and
structural cost of understanding, modifying, and safely extending S, independent of how valuable S's
output is. A system with a small, legible interface and a short dependency chain scores low Complexity
(cheap in the denominator, good); a system whose internals require tracing through a dozen subengines
and undocumented coupling scores high Complexity (expensive, bad). Complexity is deliberately not a line
count — the estate has already learned, per the token-austerity and DNA-3000 protocols referenced in the
project's own governance, that raw size is a poor proxy for cost — it is instead read from the
dependency depth and interface surface recorded in IAS-G1's forthcoming topology map, imported by
reference rather than recomputed here. (DERIVED; the topology dependency is HYPOTHESIZED to be
available at F2's consumption time, since G1 is a later-priority dataset — Part XIII notes this
ordering risk explicitly.)

4.3 OVERLAP — the redundancy-cost factor. Overlap(S), DEFINED, measures the degree to which S's
capability is already covered, wholly or partially, by another system in the ensemble, drawn directly
from the Non-Duplication Ledger's own overlap column for S's founding candidate. A system with a REJECT
or MERGE verdict against a well-covered parent carries high Overlap even after conversion to a
second-order capability, because the underlying redundancy the ledger identified does not vanish merely
because the artifact was renamed into a subengine — it persists as an ongoing maintenance and confusion
cost that the denominator must continue to charge against the system's score. Overlap is thus the one
factor computed identically to WhiteSpace's source data (the ledger) but read as the opposite pole: where
WhiteSpace asks "how much of this was genuinely new," Overlap asks "how much of this still duplicates
something else, having been converted rather than eliminated." (DERIVED, and CONTESTED in one respect:
whether a fully-converted second-order capability should still carry any Overlap charge at all is not
settled — Part XX opens this as a question, since the D2A conversion doctrine implies the overlap is
resolved by conversion while the measurement discipline implies residual coupling cost never reaches
zero.)

4.4 MAINTENANCE — the standing-cost factor. Maintenance(S), DEFINED, measures the ongoing attention S
requires to remain correct as the ensemble around it changes: how often its interface breaks when a
consumer or provider it depends on is modified, how frequently its outputs must be manually re-verified,
and how much of the estate's governance overhead (hard-rule checks, scaffold audits, secret-firewall
scans) it triggers relative to systems of comparable size. A system that was built once and has required
zero corrective attention across many ensemble-state transitions scores low Maintenance; a system that
regenerates BLOCKED_DELIVERY conditions, trips false-positive gates, or requires a human to intervene
on a predictable cadence scores high Maintenance regardless of how valuable its output is when it does
work. (OBSERVED premise: the estate's own governance layer already logs the signals Maintenance draws
from — hook trip counts, gate-failure counts, BLOCKED_DELIVERY incidence — per the project's Reality
Contract and Anti-Antipattern Protocol; DERIVED: Maintenance is a read over existing governance
telemetry, not a new logging system F2 must build.)

4.5 The full normalized formula. Combining Parts III and IV, the Advantage Score for system S over
ensemble state E is DEFINED as:

  Advantage(S, E) = clip( [WhiteSpace x SystemsStrengthened x Reuse x CrossProject x Compounding x
                            ModelIndependence]^(1/6)
                           divided by
                           [Complexity x Overlap x Maintenance]^(1/3) x k ,   -1, +1 )

The geometric mean on the numerator (sixth root of the six-factor product) and on the denominator (cube
root of the three-factor product) is a deliberate NORMATIVE choice, not an arbitrary one: an arithmetic
mean would let one very high factor mask five weak ones (a system with perfect WhiteSpace and near-zero
everything else would still average respectably), while the geometric mean forces every factor to be
genuinely present — a single factor near zero collapses the whole numerator toward zero, which is the
correct behavior for a claim like "this system is institutionally advantageous," a conjunctive claim
that should fail if any one of its supporting legs fails. The scaling constant k and the clip to [-1,+1]
exist to keep the score inside the bounded interval Part I requires; k is CALIBRATED per ensemble
generation against the synthetic worked example of Part XVIII rather than fixed by theory, and is
therefore marked EXPERIMENTAL — a value the algebra proposes and the estate's real measurement history
must eventually confirm or revise. (DEFINED formula; EXPERIMENTAL calibration constant.)

4.6 Degenerate cases the formula must handle honestly. Three degenerate inputs deserve explicit
treatment because a formula that silently mishandles them produces false precision (Part XVI) rather
than an honest null. If any denominator factor is measured as exactly zero (a system with no
detectable maintenance cost is not credible; it means the signal is unmeasured, not that the cost is
truly zero), the formula does not divide by zero — it returns UNRESOLVED for that system's score rather
than an artificially inflated infinite advantage, and the score record carries an evidence level of
UNRESOLVED rather than a number. If a numerator factor is zero because the system has never been
invoked by anything (Reuse = 0, CrossProject = 0), the geometric mean correctly collapses the score
toward zero — this is the intended behavior, not a bug, and is what lets IAS-E1's dead-capability list
be populated from F2 scores directly. If a system is too newly built to have any mission history at all,
its score is not computed as zero; it is marked NOT-YET-MEASURABLE, distinct from a computed zero,
because "unmeasured" and "measured-and-worthless" are different institutional facts that a downstream
allocation decision (IAS-C1) must be able to tell apart. (NORMATIVE, all three.)

4.7 Sensitivity of the formula to the denominator's three factors, and why none may be dropped for
convenience. A natural simplification, if the estate ever finds computing all three denominator factors
burdensome, would be to drop the hardest-to-measure of the three (typically Complexity, since it depends
on IAS-G1's not-yet-built topology map per 4.2) and compute a two-factor denominator instead. This Part
states explicitly why that simplification is rejected: because the denominator is a geometric mean, not
an arithmetic one, dropping a factor does not merely lose one-third of the cost signal — it changes the
formula's very shape, since a two-factor geometric mean responds differently to extreme values than a
three-factor one, and any historical score computed under the three-factor formula becomes
incommensurable with any score computed under a two-factor one, breaking the trailing series Compounding
and NICG both depend on (Parts VI, VIII). If Complexity is genuinely unmeasurable at a given moment
because IAS-G1's topology map does not yet cover a given system, the correct response, per the
degenerate-case discipline of 4.6, is to mark the WHOLE Advantage Score UNRESOLVED for that system until
Complexity becomes measurable — not to silently recompute the formula with fewer terms and report a
number that looks like every other system's Advantage Score but was produced by a structurally different
calculation. (NORMATIVE, DERIVED: formula-shape consistency across the trailing series is a harder
requirement than any single measurement's convenience, and this is the concrete instance of Part XI's
general precision-matching discipline applied to the denominator specifically.)

FINAL LAW — PART IV. Advantage is a ratio, and a ratio without an honest denominator is propaganda; the
geometric-mean formula is built to fail loudly — toward zero, toward UNRESOLVED, toward NOT-YET-
MEASURABLE — rather than to flatter a system that is large, tangled, or merely old.

===============================================================================================
PART V — THE FUSION SCORE
===============================================================================================

5.1 Why a second, qualitative aggregate above the Advantage Score. The Advantage Score of Parts III-IV
is a single system's ratio at a single ensemble state — precise, but narrow: it says nothing about how
WELL that system's strengthening effect is integrated into the systems it touches, nor about the risk
that its apparent advantage is a measurement artifact rather than a durable fact. The FUSION SCORE, as
DEFINED, is the family's second, deliberately qualitative composite, required by this dataset's own
brief and by the Constitution's demand (implicit in Article V) that not every institutional judgment be
forced into a single spuriously precise number. The Fusion Score is reported as a qualitative profile
across five named dimensions — never collapsed to one digit — because the five dimensions answer
different questions a single scalar cannot honestly hold at once.

5.2 The five dimensions, defined individually. SYSTEMS STRENGTHENED restates, at the qualitative level,
the count and identity of parents strengthened (Part III.3), reported as a named list, not merely a
number, so a reviewer can see WHICH systems benefit and judge whether the list is plausible.
INTEGRATION QUALITY, DEFINED, assesses whether S's strengthening effect on its parents is a clean,
documented interface (per F1's SCORES-relation-style traceability) or an implicit, undocumented
coupling that happens to work today — high integration quality means the strengthening survives a
parent's internal refactor; low integration quality means it is fragile to exactly the kind of change
the estate makes constantly. REUSE restates Part III.4's factor qualitatively, annotated with the
CONTEXTS of reuse (which missions, which projects) rather than only the count. COMPLEXITY COST
restates Part IV.2's factor as a qualitative burden assessment: "trivial to extend," "requires the
original author's context," "requires reading three other systems first." REDUNDANCY RISK restates
Part IV.3's Overlap factor as a forward-looking risk statement: not "how much does this duplicate today"
but "how likely is a future proposal to re-duplicate this territory because its coverage is not
discoverable" — a distinct question from static overlap, because a system can have zero current overlap
and still carry high redundancy risk if it is poorly indexed and therefore invisible to the next
proposer. (DEFINED, all five; REDUNDANCY RISK's forward-looking framing DERIVED from the observed
pattern, per the Ledger's own 150-candidate exercise, that most duplication proposals arise from
poor discoverability rather than deliberate re-invention.)

5.3 Why the Fusion Score resists collapse to a single number, on purpose. Averaging the five dimensions
into one digit would recreate the exact false-precision failure Part XVI catalogs: a system with
excellent Reuse and terrible Integration Quality would average to "medium," which is not an honest
description of either its strength or its risk — it is a number that hides both. The Fusion Score is
therefore reported, NORMATIVE, as a five-slot profile (each slot qualitative: strong / adequate / weak /
unmeasured) accompanied by the Advantage Score number from Part IV for the one purpose that genuinely
needs a scalar — ranking within IAS-C1's Portfolio (Part VII specifies that ranking use). The profile and
the scalar are never presented as substitutes for each other; a consumer wanting "is this system good"
reads the profile, a consumer wanting "which of these forty systems ranks higher" reads the Advantage
Score, and conflating the two questions is the single most common misuse this dataset anticipates (Part
XVII, adversarial case 5.4).

5.4 Constructing a Fusion Score from Advantage Score components, worked in brief. Because the Fusion
Score's five dimensions are drawn from the same measurement protocols as the Advantage Score's eight
factors (Systems Strengthened and Reuse are shared verbatim; Complexity Cost and Redundancy Risk map
from Complexity and Overlap; Integration Quality is the one genuinely new measurement, assessed via the
SCORES and DECOMPOSES-INTO relations' documentation completeness), constructing a system's Fusion Score
requires no new data collection beyond what the Advantage Score already gathers — it requires only a
second, qualitative READING of the same evidence. This is intentional: a measurement substrate that
needed two independent data-gathering passes to produce its scalar and its profile would double the
estate's measurement burden for marginal benefit, which is precisely the Complexity and Maintenance
cost this dataset itself penalizes in Part IV. (DERIVED, and self-referential — Part XIX returns to
this point when evaluating F2 by its own algebra.)

5.5 The Fusion Score's role in the Compounding Graph. Per the Compounding Graph's cross-dataset edge
"IAS-F2 → fusion/advantage scores → IAS-C1, IAS-B1, IAS-D1 → portfolio ranking, leverage ranking,
fitness," the Fusion Score's profile form is what IAS-D1's Ecology reads to distinguish a healthy
symbiosis (strong Integration Quality, strong Systems Strengthened) from a parasitic relationship
(strong Reuse but weak Integration Quality — a system other systems lean on without a clean, documented
seam, which is exactly the pattern IAS-D1's Parasitic Complexity Detector is built to find). The
Advantage Score's scalar is what IAS-C1's Portfolio and IAS-B1's Cognitive Multiplier read for ranking.
The two consumers reading two different facets of the same underlying evidence, rather than each
re-deriving its own score from raw data, is the concrete mechanism by which F2 strengthens both siblings
without either duplicating F2's measurement work. (DERIVED, evidence required per the Matrix: "each
parent's metric expressible in F2 units" — satisfied when C1 and D1 can be shown consuming the Advantage
Score and Fusion Score respectively without independent re-measurement.)

5.6 Producing and consuming the Fusion Score: cadence and audience. The Fusion Score is not recomputed
at every ensemble-state transition automatically the way the Advantage Score's numeric factors are;
because it is a qualitative READING (5.4) rather than a fresh data-gathering pass, it is produced on a
slower, NORMATIVE cadence — at each dataset seal, each major mission close, or on demand when a human
Owner or a consuming IAS dataset requests a fresh read of a system's profile ahead of an allocation
decision. This cadence distinction matters because a Fusion Score computed once and left stale for many
ensemble-state transitions is subject to the same lifecycle discipline as any other score (Part XV): its
evidence level decays toward HYPOTHESIZED if it is not refreshed, and a downstream consumer reading an
old profile as if it described the system's CURRENT integration quality risks exactly the false-confidence
failure Part XII.5 names. The intended audience split is also worth stating plainly: the profile form is
written for a human reviewer or a qualitative-reasoning system (IAS-D1's ecology judgment, an Owner's
allocation review) to READ, while the accompanying scalar is written for a sorting or ranking ALGORITHM
to CONSUME — the two forms exist side by side because they serve two different kinds of consumer, not
because one is a summary of the other.

5.7 The Fusion Score as a dispute-surfacing instrument, not only a health report. Beyond describing a
single system's condition, a Fusion Score profile with one or more WEAK slots alongside otherwise
STRONG slots is itself a signal worth routing, per Part XIV's decision model, to the specific sibling
dataset best positioned to act on that particular weakness: a WEAK Integration Quality slot routes to
IAS-D1's ecology map as a candidate parasitic-pattern flag; a WEAK Redundancy Risk slot routes to a
Ledger-Independence re-audit per Part X.2; a WEAK Complexity Cost slot routes to IAS-G1's topology
review once that dataset exists. This routing behavior is what makes the profile format more than a
report card — it is a triage instrument, and it is the concrete mechanism by which the Fusion Score
"strengthens" its downstream siblings (per the Matrix's language) rather than merely describing them: a
weak slot is not a passive observation, it is an actionable pointer to the specific sibling system whose
authority covers that weakness. (NORMATIVE, DERIVED from Part XIV.5's Owner-override protocol — the
routing surfaces a recommendation, it never triggers an automatic remediation.)

FINAL LAW — PART V. A single number can rank; it cannot explain. The Fusion Score is deliberately a
profile, not a digit, because the question "is this system healthy" and the question "which system
ranks higher" are different questions, and an algebra that answers both with the same scalar has quietly
chosen to answer neither one honestly.

===============================================================================================
PART VI — THE COMPOUNDING FORMULA
===============================================================================================

6.1 Compounding as a factor and as a formula. Part III.6 introduced Compounding(S) as one of the six
numerator factors of the Advantage Score — a single-system property answering "does this system's
advantage grow over time." This Part specifies the formula that produces that factor, because
compounding is not directly observable at a single ensemble-state snapshot; it must be DERIVED from a
trailing series of Advantage Score measurements for the same system across multiple ensemble states, and
the shape of that derivation is itself a load-bearing design decision for the whole algebra, since it is
the mechanism that lets the Compounding Graph's "the estate does not just run; it gets better at getting
better" claim (its closing sentence) be measured rather than merely asserted.

6.2 The trailing-ratio definition. Given a system S with Advantage Score measurements A(S, E_1), A(S,
E_2), ..., A(S, E_n) at successive ensemble states E_1 through E_n (each state corresponding to a
mission, a sealed dataset, or another discrete estate transition against which F2 can be recomputed),
Compounding(S) is DEFINED as the geometric growth rate of A(S, E_i) over the trailing window, clipped to
a [0,1] band via a saturating transform (a system whose advantage triples between two states scores no
higher than a system whose advantage merely doubles and continues doubling, because the factor measures
DIRECTION and PERSISTENCE of growth, not a single window's raw magnitude, which would reward one lucky
measurement interval over sustained structural improvement). A system with only one measurement (a
NOT-YET-MEASURABLE system, per Part IV.6) has no trailing series and therefore no Compounding factor;
its Advantage Score computation substitutes a NORMATIVE prior of 0.5 (neither rewarding nor penalizing
newness) until a second measurement exists. (DEFINED, DERIVED transform, NORMATIVE prior.)

6.6 Edge cases: negative-advantage trajectories and the meaning of Compounding for a declining system.
The trailing-ratio definition of 6.2 is stated for a system whose Advantage Score is positive across its
measured history, but the formula must also behave sensibly for a system whose score has been negative
or crossed zero during the trailing window. Three sub-cases deserve explicit treatment. A system moving
from negative toward zero (a recovering parasite, per IAS-D1's vocabulary, perhaps after a remediation)
shows POSITIVE Compounding under the growth-rate definition even though its Advantage Score itself
remains below zero — this is correct behavior, not an artifact, because Compounding measures the
DIRECTION of change, and a system actively improving deserves credit for that trajectory distinct from
its still-negative absolute standing, which the Advantage Score's own numerator/denominator ratio
continues to report honestly in parallel. A system moving from positive toward negative (a system
decaying into parasitism) shows NEGATIVE Compounding, clipped at the [0,1] band's floor per 6.2's
saturating transform, and this negative reading is precisely the early-warning signal IAS-D1's ecology
map is designed to consume before the system's Advantage Score itself crosses zero — Compounding, in
this sense, is a leading indicator, not merely a lagging descriptor. A system oscillating around zero
across successive measurements (neither reliably improving nor reliably declining) produces a
Compounding factor that the growth-rate transform correctly renders near the neutral prior of 0.5, since
a true oscillation has no stable direction to reward or penalize — and this outcome is itself diagnostic:
an oscillating Compounding reading across several ensemble-state transitions for the same system is
flagged, per Part X.5's countermetric, as a candidate CONTESTED case warranting a closer look at whether
the oscillation reflects genuine instability or a measurement-consistency problem in how the system's
underlying factors are being assessed. (DERIVED, all three sub-cases, and this Part is the concrete
demonstration that the trailing-ratio formula degrades gracefully rather than producing nonsense at its
boundary conditions — the same discipline Part IV.6 demands of the Advantage Score formula, applied here
to its Compounding sub-formula.)

6.3 Why compounding must be measured relative to the ensemble, not in isolation. A system's raw output
could grow simply because the ensemble grew around it (more consumers exist, so more consume it) without
the system itself becoming more advantageous per consumer. The trailing-ratio formula therefore
normalizes A(S, E_i) against the ensemble's total system count at E_i before computing growth, so that
Compounding(S) answers "is S becoming disproportionately more valuable as the ensemble grows" rather
than "did S's raw usage grow alongside everything else's" — the latter would falsely credit every system
with compounding merely because the estate as a whole is expanding, which would make the factor useless
for distinguishing genuine leverage points (IAS-B1's object) from systems riding the estate's general
growth. (DERIVED, and this is precisely the discipline the vault's own recorded lesson — never gate on a
ratio whose denominator can be shrunk or whose growth can be inflated by an unrelated numerator rise —
requires; F2 applies that lesson to its own central formula rather than merely citing it.)

6.4 The ensemble-level compounding formula, distinct from the per-system factor. Separately from any
single system's Compounding factor, the ENSEMBLE itself carries a compounding property, DEFINED as the
trailing growth rate of the SUM of all current Advantage Scores across the ensemble's systems, read at
successive ensemble states. This ensemble-level formula is what operationalizes Net Institutional
Capability Gain (Part VII) as a time series rather than a single snapshot, and it is the quantity the
Compounding Graph's four named loops (circulatory, amplification, defense, foresight) each predict should
rise over time if the loops are genuinely closing as designed. A federation whose ensemble-level
compounding rate is flat or negative despite all four loops reporting local improvements has a
measurement or an integration problem — either the loops are not actually connected as diagrammed, or
individual gains are being offset by rising Complexity/Overlap/Maintenance costs elsewhere in the
ensemble, and the ensemble-level formula is the instrument that would catch either failure. (DERIVED,
and this is the primary self-check mechanism specified fully in Part XIX.)

6.5 Compounding's interaction with the anti-Goodhart discipline. Compounding is, of the eight Advantage
Score factors, the one most vulnerable to gaming, because a measurer under pressure to show growth can
select a favorable trailing window, or can inflate early measurements low so later ones show more
dramatic "improvement." Part X assigns Compounding a specific countermetric — window-consistency
auditing — precisely because this factor's incentive structure is the sharpest in the whole algebra: it
is the one factor whose formula literally rewards showing improvement over time, which is also the one
formula an adversarial or merely enthusiastic measurer is likeliest to reverse-engineer and satisfy
without producing real improvement. (NORMATIVE, cross-referenced fully in Part X and Part XVII's
adversarial case 6 and case 7.)

6.7 Interaction between the per-system and ensemble-level Compounding formulas: a consistency check. The
two formulas (per-system, 6.2; ensemble-level, 6.4) are computed independently but are not independent
facts about the world, and their relationship yields a useful DERIVED consistency check: the ensemble-
level compounding rate should approximate a weighted aggregate of the per-system Compounding factors,
weighted by each system's contribution to the ensemble's total Advantage Score sum. A material, persistent
divergence between the two — the ensemble-level rate rising sharply while most per-system factors read
flat or declining — would indicate that the ensemble's growth is concentrated in a small number of
systems rather than broadly distributed, a finding of direct interest to IAS-D1's biodiversity-governance
concern (per the Ledger's Family D disposition) even though F2 itself makes no biodiversity judgment; F2
merely surfaces the divergence as a checkable fact for IAS-D1 to interpret. Conversely, a persistent
divergence in the OTHER direction — most per-system factors reading positive while the ensemble-level rate
stays flat — would suggest the NewComplexityCost and NewMaintenanceCost terms NICG already charges (Part
VIII.2) are absorbing gains that individual systems' Compounding factors do not themselves account for,
since those cost terms are computed at the ensemble level and have no per-system Compounding analogue.
(DERIVED, and this cross-check is the concrete reason both formulas are specified separately rather than
one being treated as a mere aggregate of the other — the DIVERGENCE between them, when it occurs, carries
more diagnostic information than either formula computed alone.)

FINAL LAW — PART VI. Compounding is not a compliment paid to a system that has been useful for a while;
it is a measured, ensemble-normalized growth rate over a trailing series, and a federation that cannot
show its total Advantage Score rising over successive states has not proven the compounding loops of its
own architecture — it has only diagrammed them.

===============================================================================================
PART VII — THE SYSTEMS-STRENGTHENED METRIC
===============================================================================================

7.1 Elevating a factor to a named metric. Part III.3 introduced SystemsStrengthened(S) as one component
of the Advantage Score; this Part gives it the fuller treatment promised there, because it is also,
independently, one of the family's two headline consumable indices (alongside Net Institutional
Capability Gain, Part VIII), and every IAS dataset's row in the System-Strengthening Matrix is itself an
instance of this metric applied to that dataset. The SYSTEMS-STRENGTHENED METRIC, as DEFINED, is the
count and identified set of parent systems P = {p_1, ..., p_k} for which a STRENGTHENS relation from S to
p_i is both asserted and evidenced, together with, for each p_i, the specific mechanism and expected
gain the Matrix format requires (mechanism of strengthening, expected gain, evidence required — the
exact three columns the Matrix already uses for every IAS dataset's own row).

7.2 Counting rules: what qualifies as a strengthened system. Not every mention of a parent in a
dataset's text counts as strengthening it. A parent p_i is counted in S's Systems-Strengthened set only
if three conditions hold: (a) a concrete mechanism is stated (not "helps X" but the specific way S
changes what p_i can do, decide, or cost); (b) the mechanism is falsifiable — a reviewer could in
principle find evidence the claimed effect did NOT occur; (c) the direction is strengthening, not merely
coexistence — p_i produces more value, decides better, or costs less BECAUSE S exists, per Article III's
own definition, repeated here because it is the operative test. A dataset that lists five parents in
prose but satisfies these three conditions for only two has a Systems-Strengthened count of two, not
five, regardless of how many names appear in its narrative. (NORMATIVE, DERIVED directly from Article
III and from the Matrix's own format.)

7.3 Avoiding double-count across the compounding loops. Because the Compounding Graph's loops route
signal through multiple IAS datasets before it reaches a final consumer (e.g., IAS-E1's usage data
reaches IAS-C1 both directly and indirectly via IAS-D1's fitness updates), a naive count risks crediting
S with strengthening a parent it only strengthens THROUGH an intermediate IAS dataset, which would let
every dataset early in a loop claim credit for every downstream effect the loop eventually produces. The
counting rule therefore distinguishes DIRECT strengthening (S changes p_i's own behavior or output
without an intermediate dataset's participation) from MEDIATED strengthening (S improves p_i only via
another IAS dataset's transformation of S's signal), and the Systems-Strengthened metric reports the two
separately: a direct count and a mediated count, never summed into one number, because summing them
would double-count the same underlying improvement once at its source and again at every hop along the
loop that carried it. (DERIVED, NORMATIVE separation.)

7.4 The metric's role in the Matrix's coverage check. The System-Strengthening Matrix's own "coverage
check" section — the list of every parent strengthened at least once across all fourteen IAS datasets —
is, formally, the union of each dataset's direct Systems-Strengthened set, computed by this metric. This
gives the Matrix's coverage claim a checkable procedure rather than an assertion: "every IAS dataset
strengthens at least four systems, no orphan dataset" (the Matrix's own coverage law) is verifiable by
recomputing each dataset's Systems-Strengthened count against the three-condition test of 7.2 and
confirming the minimum holds, rather than trusting the Matrix's prose to have counted correctly the
first time it was written. (DERIVED: F2 gives the Matrix's own self-audit a formula, which is itself an
instance of F2 strengthening a document that predates it — see the D2A Decision Record's sovereignty
test in the dataset's closing sections.)

7.5 Systems-Strengthened as a portfolio-allocation input, previewed. IAS-C1's Portfolio, per the
Compounding Graph, consumes "fusion/advantage scores" to inform "portfolio ranking." The
Systems-Strengthened count is the single factor within that score with the clearest intuitive reading
for a human Owner deciding where to invest attention: a system strengthening six parents, evidenced, is
self-evidently a higher-leverage investment than one strengthening two, all else equal — which is
precisely why the raw count, not only its normalized contribution to the Advantage Score, is preserved
and reported separately in every score record, so a human reviewing an allocation decision can see the
count directly rather than reverse-engineering it from a composite ratio. (NORMATIVE: preserve
intermediate, human-legible quantities alongside composite scores — a discipline Part XI generalizes.)

7.6 A worked mini-example of the counting rule, and the edge case of a strengthened DOCUMENT rather than
a SYSTEM. Suppose synthetic system SYN-H's prose (Part XVIII builds SYN-H fully) claims it strengthens
six parents. Applying 7.2's three-condition test: two claims name a concrete, falsifiable mechanism and
a stated direction and survive; two claims name a mechanism but describe only coexistence ("SYN-H's
output is consumed alongside parent P's") without a stated improvement in P's value, decisions, or cost,
and are excluded; one claim is MEDIATED (SYN-H strengthens a parent only via an intermediate dataset's
transformation, per 7.3) and is counted separately in the mediated column, not the direct column; one
claim targets an artifact that fails F1's systemhood test entirely — a governance document, not a SYSTEM
— and is excluded from the count altogether, though noted qualitatively, because the Systems-Strengthened
metric is defined over F1 SYSTEM objects specifically (per 7.1's cross-reference to the Matrix format)
and a document-level effect, however real, is not commensurable with a system-level STRENGTHENS relation
without first establishing what F1 object the document maps to. SYN-H's honest, reported
Systems-Strengthened count is therefore "2 direct, 1 mediated," not the "6" its prose narrative
suggested — the precise gap between a narrative count and a disciplined count this Part exists to close.
(DERIVED, worked; this is the general procedure Part IX.4 applies when discussing DAR's relationship to a
non-system document, the Ledger, using the identical reasoning.)

7.7 The metric's lower bound and the case of a system that strengthens nothing directly. A system with a
direct Systems-Strengthened count of zero is not automatically a failed system — it may be a genuinely
new capability whose consumers have not yet been built (a NOT-YET-MEASURABLE condition per Part IV.6,
not a negative judgment), or it may be a leaf-node utility whose value is fully captured by its own Reuse
factor rather than by strengthening other SYSTEMS in F1's strict sense (a distinction worth naming: a
capability can be genuinely useful to missions directly, per Reuse, without strengthening any other
SYSTEM'S own value, decisions, or cost, since not every useful thing operates by improving a peer). The
counting rule of 7.2 is deliberately strict about what counts as a STRENGTHENS relation precisely so that
a zero count is informative rather than noisy: a system with zero direct and zero mediated
Systems-Strengthened relations, after a genuine review against the three-condition test, is either too
new to have accumulated relations yet or is a standalone capability whose institutional value routes
entirely through its own direct mission-reuse rather than through peer amplification — and the Advantage
Score formula (Part IV.5) correctly reflects this by letting a low SystemsStrengthened factor be offset,
in the geometric mean, by strong Reuse, rather than treating breadth as the only path to a healthy score.
(DERIVED, and this closing distinction is what keeps the metric from implicitly penalizing every
standalone, single-purpose utility in the ensemble merely for not being a hub.)

FINAL LAW — PART VII. A system's breadth of strengthening is not a number of names in a paragraph; it is
a count of falsifiable, directional, evidenced relations, kept honest by separating what a system
strengthens directly from what it only relays, and preserved as a legible number a human can check
without decoding a formula.

===============================================================================================
PART VIII — NET INSTITUTIONAL CAPABILITY GAIN
===============================================================================================

8.1 From per-system scores to an estate-wide index. Every formula so far in this dataset — the Advantage
Score, the Fusion Score, the Compounding factor, the Systems-Strengthened metric — is computed per
system. NET INSTITUTIONAL CAPABILITY GAIN (NICG), as DEFINED, is the family's first ensemble-wide index:
the change in the ensemble's total realized capability between two ensemble states, net of the
complexity, overlap, and maintenance cost that capability required to sustain. NICG answers the
question no per-system score can answer alone: "is the estate, taken as a whole, better off than it was
at the last measured state, once the full cost of getting there is subtracted?" — the question every
allocation decision ultimately serves.

8.2 The formula. NICG(E_i, E_{i-1}), DEFINED, is:

  NICG = SUM_over_systems[ Advantage(S, E_i) - Advantage(S, E_{i-1}) ]
         - NewComplexityCost(E_i, E_{i-1})
         - NewMaintenanceCost(E_i, E_{i-1})

The first term sums each system's Advantage Score delta across the two states — the raw capability
movement. The two subtracted terms charge, separately, the complexity and maintenance cost the ensemble
took on to produce that movement (new systems built, new coupling introduced, new governance overhead
triggered), so that NICG cannot be inflated merely by adding more systems whose individual Advantage
Scores are each modestly positive but whose aggregate cost to the ensemble's comprehensibility and
upkeep outpaces their aggregate gain. A quarter in which the estate builds five new marginally-useful
systems can show a positive raw sum and a negative NICG once the cost terms are charged, which is the
intended, NORMATIVE behavior: NICG is designed to be harder to satisfy by mere activity than the raw
sum of individual scores would be.

8.3 NICG as the compounding loop's scoreboard. Per Part VI.4, the ensemble-level compounding rate is
the trailing growth rate of the sum of Advantage Scores; NICG is the discrete, state-to-state version of
the same underlying quantity, reported per transition rather than as a smoothed rate, and is therefore
the number a human Owner reads after each sealed mission or dataset to answer "did that just make the
estate better off, net of what it cost." A negative NICG at a given transition is not necessarily a
failure signal on its own — a transition that pays down technical debt (retiring a parasite per IAS-D1,
simplifying a tangled system per IAS-G1) can show negative raw score movement in the short term while
setting up strongly positive NICG in subsequent transitions, which is why NICG is read as a trailing
series (per Part VI's discipline) and never as a single isolated reading treated as final verdict.
(DERIVED, NORMATIVE reading discipline.)

8.4 NICG's relationship to the Portfolio and the Digital Twin. IAS-C1's Portfolio uses NICG, alongside
the per-system Advantage Scores, as the objective function its allocation decisions are meant to
maximize over time — the Portfolio does not merely rank systems by individual score; it selects the
allocation of future attention and resources that maximizes PROJECTED NICG across upcoming transitions,
which is the precise sense in which "the portfolio uses usage+ROI data to allocate cognitive capital" (the
Compounding Graph's own phrasing) becomes a computable objective rather than a qualitative intention.
IAS-F3's Digital Twin, correspondingly, simulates a proposed mission's likely NICG before the mission is
committed, giving the Owner a projected number to weigh against the mission's cost before resources are
spent — the exact mechanism the Matrix's row for IAS-F3 calls "simulates the federation before spending
real resources," made concrete via the NICG projection this dataset defines. (DERIVED, both
relationships; the Digital Twin's projection accuracy is itself EXPERIMENTAL until IAS-F3 exists to be
measured against.)

8.5 Reading a run of negative NICG transitions, and the point at which a trend becomes a signal rather
than noise. Part 8.3 already established that a SINGLE negative NICG transition is not, on its own, a
failure signal. This sub-section specifies the NORMATIVE threshold at which a run of negative or
flat transitions stops being tolerable debt-paydown and starts being a genuine ensemble-health concern:
three or more consecutive transitions with NICG at or below zero, absent a stated and evidenced
debt-paydown rationale (a named parasite retirement, a named topology simplification) for each of those
transitions, is escalated per Part XIV.2's threshold protocol as an amber advisory to the Owner and to
IAS-C1's Portfolio review — not because three negative readings prove decline, but because three
readings are the minimum needed to distinguish a genuine downward trend from the kind of single-transition
noise a trailing series is expected to contain even in a healthy ensemble. This threshold is itself
CALIBRATED and EXPERIMENTAL, stated here as a starting discipline rather than a proven optimum, and is
one of the concrete parameters Part XIX.5's falsification program is expected to test once real
ensemble-state history accumulates. A related edge case: a run of negative NICG each accompanied by a
DIFFERENT stated debt-paydown rationale is treated differently from a run each accompanied by the SAME
rationale repeated — the former is diversified debt clearance across the ensemble; the latter suggests
either a single large remediation spanning several transitions (legitimate) or a rationale being reused
as a blanket excuse for otherwise-unexplained decline (the exact pattern Part XVII's adversarial catalog
would flag if formalized as a tenth case), and a reviewer encountering the latter pattern should apply
Part X.6's Cross-Evaluator Consensus countermetric to the debt-paydown claim itself before accepting the
NICG reading as benign. (NORMATIVE, DERIVED, EXPERIMENTAL threshold.)

8.6 Failure mode: NICG computed without state discipline. NICG requires two well-defined ensemble
states, E_i and E_{i-1}, each with its own complete set of Advantage Score measurements. A common failure
mode — CONTESTED as to whether it is avoidable in practice given real measurement cadence — is computing
NICG against a "previous state" that is itself a mixture of measurements taken at different times for
different systems (some fresh, some stale per Part XV), which silently corrupts the delta: a system
measured stale at E_{i-1} and fresh at E_i can show a large apparent gain that is partly or wholly a
measurement-timing artifact rather than real capability change. The mitigation, NORMATIVE, is that NICG
may only be computed between two states where every system's measurement evidence level is at least
OBSERVED as of each state's timestamp; a state with unresolved or stale measurements for a material
fraction of the ensemble yields a NICG marked UNRESOLVED rather than a number, mirroring the degenerate-
case discipline of Part IV.6.

8.7 NICG's relationship to a human Owner's intuitive sense of "did this quarter go well." NICG is
deliberately built as the single number closest to answering the plain-language question an Owner asks
after a stretch of work: was this worth it. This sub-section names, explicitly, the ways NICG can diverge
from that intuitive sense and why the divergence is a feature rather than a bug to be tuned away. NICG can
read strongly positive in a transition an Owner FEELS was chaotic or stressful, because NICG measures
realized capability gain net of logged cost, not subjective experience of the work — a genuinely
productive but exhausting transition and a genuinely productive, calm one can post the same NICG, and the
metric is not designed to distinguish them, since operator wellbeing is outside F2's ensemble-object
boundary (per Article II, F2 governs the ensemble's systems, not the humans operating them). NICG can also
read flat or negative in a transition that FEELS productive because a large amount of exploratory,
not-yet-converted work occurred — research, prototyping, discussion — that has not yet crystallized into
a scored system with a measurable Advantage Score; this is the concrete instance of Part IV.6's NOT-YET-
MEASURABLE discipline applied at the ensemble level, and the correct read is that NICG lags genuinely
valuable groundwork until that groundwork produces a measurable artifact, not that the groundwork was
worthless. An Owner reading NICG against their own intuition should therefore treat a persistent, large
divergence between the two as worth investigating — not because NICG is wrong, and not because the
intuition is wrong, but because the divergence usually reveals which of the two is measuring something the
other cannot see. (NORMATIVE, DERIVED.)

FINAL LAW — PART VIII. The sum of everyone's individual improvement is not the estate's improvement; Net
Institutional Capability Gain charges the complexity and maintenance cost of getting there against the
raw gain, and an estate that cannot show this net number rising has not shown progress — it has shown
motion.

PART IX — DUPLICATE AVOIDANCE RATE
===============================================================================================

9.1 The second named index: measuring the family's own founding law. DUPLICATE AVOIDANCE RATE (DAR), as
DEFINED, is the ensemble-wide fraction of proposed new capabilities, across a trailing window of
proposals, that are converted to a second-order capability (per Article I) rather than shipped as a
naming-only duplicate that the D2A engine would later have to catch and unwind. DAR is the family's
operationalization of its own supreme law: Article I states the duplicate-to-advantage principle as a
NORMATIVE rule; DAR is the OBSERVED rate at which the estate actually follows it, and a family that
proposes a rule without a metric measuring adherence to that rule has, per this dataset's own measurement
discipline (Part XI), stated a hope rather than a governed practice.

9.2 The formula and its two failure directions. DAR(window) = (proposals with a D2A verdict of BUILD,
MERGE-INTO-FAMILY, SUBENGINE, EXTEND, or COMPOSITION recorded BEFORE implementation began) divided by
(total proposals in the window). DAR can fail low in two structurally different ways that a single
number cannot distinguish without its accompanying evidence trail: PROCESS failure (proposals are
implemented without ever being checked against the Ledger, so no verdict exists to count) and JUDGMENT
failure (proposals are checked, receive a verdict, but the verdict is ignored and a naming-only duplicate
ships anyway). The two failures call for different remediation — the first needs the spec-gate-style
discipline reinforced (HR-SPEC-001's own trigger, already codified in the estate's hard rules), the
second needs the D2A engine's authority strengthened relative to the proposer's — so DAR is reported,
NORMATIVE, with a breakdown of unchecked-versus-overridden proposals whenever the rate falls below a
CALIBRATED healthy threshold, rather than as a bare percentage.

9.3 DAR's relationship to WhiteSpace and Overlap. DAR is measured at the ensemble level and at the moment
of proposal; WhiteSpace and Overlap (Parts III.2 and IV.3) are measured per system and persist across the
system's lifetime. The two are related but not identical: a high DAR over a trailing window means the
ESTATE is currently disciplined about checking new proposals against the Ledger before building, while a
given system's WhiteSpace/Overlap pair describes whether THAT system, once built, actually turned out to
close genuine gap or duplicate coverage — a system can be proposed under a high-DAR regime (properly
checked) and still receive a verdict of MERGE (genuinely overlapping, correctly caught and converted),
which is DAR working as intended, not a DAR failure. Conflating "DAR is low" with "many systems duplicate
each other" is a specific instance of the metric-gaming and misreading risk Part XVII catalogs as
adversarial case 9. (DERIVED, distinction NORMATIVE.)

9.4 DAR as the Non-Duplication Ledger's living successor metric. The Ledger, as it stands, is a static
disposition of 150 candidates into 14 datasets — a point-in-time artifact. DAR is what keeps the Ledger's
discipline alive after its founding 150-candidate exercise is sealed: every future proposal the estate
considers, whether a new IAS sub-capability, a new PP module, or a new project-level tool, is a candidate
DAR's window can measure. (DERIVED: this is the mechanism by which F2 strengthens the Ledger itself,
which the Matrix does not list as a separate strengthened parent because the Ledger is a document rather
than a system per F1's systemhood test — but the Ledger's DISCIPLINE, embodied procedurally, is exactly
what DAR extends past the document's own text. This is noted here as a HYPOTHESIZED extension of the
Matrix's coverage, since the Ledger is not itself an F1 system and DAR's relationship to it is therefore
a governance effect rather than a formal STRENGTHENS relation.)

9.5 DAR's interaction with cost. A perfect DAR of 1.0 achieved by rejecting every proposal outright is
not a healthy signal; it would mean the estate has stopped building, which is a different failure than
duplication. DAR is therefore always read jointly with the estate's raw proposal volume and with NICG:
a high DAR alongside healthy proposal volume and positive NICG indicates genuine disciplined growth; a
high DAR alongside collapsing proposal volume indicates the discipline has curdled into paralysis, which
is not what Article I intends and not what this metric is built to reward. (NORMATIVE, and this joint-
reading requirement is itself an anti-Goodhart countermetric, formalized as DAR's specific entry in Part
X.)

9.7 DAR's relationship to the estate's spec-gate discipline, named explicitly rather than left implicit.
Part 9.2 already names HR-SPEC-001 as the remediation for PROCESS-failure DAR misses; this sub-section
states the relationship in the other direction — what DAR gives BACK to the spec-gate discipline that
D2A-5's own verdict process and HR-SPEC-001's trigger already enforce. Where HR-SPEC-001 is a per-proposal
gate (does THIS specific L/XL task have a spec before coding begins), DAR is the estate-wide, TRAILING
measurement of how consistently that gate is actually honored across many proposals over time — the gate
enforces the rule at the moment of one decision; DAR reveals whether the rule is holding up in aggregate,
across many decisions, many proposers, and potentially many sessions where the same discipline was
supposed to apply. A gate that fires correctly on every individual proposal but whose cumulative DAR still
reads low over a trailing window would be a contradiction worth investigating — it would suggest either
that proposals are being routed around the gate entirely (a PROCESS failure the gate itself cannot see,
since the gate only fires on proposals that reach it) or that the gate's own verdict is being systematically
overridden after the fact (a JUDGMENT failure the gate also cannot see, since the gate's job ends once it
returns a verdict). DAR is therefore the closest thing this algebra provides to an audit of the estate's
gates themselves, not merely of the proposals the gates review — a second-order check the individual gates
cannot perform on themselves, precisely because they operate proposal-by-proposal while DAR operates
across the whole trailing population. (DERIVED, NORMATIVE, and this is the concrete sense in which DAR
strengthens HR-SPEC-001 and its sibling hard rules, an unlisted beneficiary alongside the Matrix's
formally named parents.)

9.6 DAR applied retrodictively to the family's own founding exercise, and what that retrodiction shows
about window selection. Part IX.2's formula is stated prospectively (measuring future proposals against
the Ledger going forward), but nothing in the definition prevents a RETRODICTIVE application to the
Ledger's own founding disposition of one hundred fifty candidates into fourteen datasets — indeed, this
retrodiction is a natural first test of the metric, since the founding exercise is the one body of
proposal history that already exists in full. Applying DAR's formula to that one-time, large-batch
disposition requires one explicit caveat this sub-section states rather than leaves implicit: a single
massive batch processed under concentrated, careful review (the Ledger's own founding audit) is not
statistically comparable to DAR's intended STEADY-STATE reading of ongoing, day-to-day proposal flow —
a founding-batch DAR reading near 1.0 (nearly every one of the hundred fifty candidates received a
verdict before any implementation) would be an artifact of the founding exercise's unusually thorough,
one-time review discipline, not evidence that the estate's ONGOING proposal discipline will sustain the
same rate under normal operating cadence. This is precisely the case Part IX.2's PROCESS-versus-JUDGMENT
distinction anticipates: the founding batch's high DAR reflects an absence of PROCESS failure (every
candidate WAS checked) under conditions unlikely to recur exactly, and a reviewer using the founding
batch as DAR's calibration baseline must discount for that one-time-review effect rather than treating it
as the steady-state target future windows should be held to. (DERIVED, NORMATIVE caveat; this is the
concrete instance, specific to DAR, of the general founding-finding-is-a-hypothesis discipline the
estate's own recorded lesson requires — a number computed once, even honestly, should not be silently
promoted to a permanent baseline without checking whether the conditions that produced it will recur.)

FINAL LAW — PART IX. A supreme law without a measured adherence rate is a slogan; Duplicate Avoidance
Rate is what turns "convert every duplication to advantage" from Article I's sentence into a number the
estate can watch rise or fall, catch itself failing, and correct — provided it is never read as a bare
percentage divorced from the proposal volume and net gain it must be judged alongside.

PART X — ANTI-GOODHART COUNTERMETRICS PER SCORE
===============================================================================================

10.1 Goodhart's law as the algebra's central operational risk. Every formula defined in Parts III through
IX becomes, the moment it is used to allocate attention or resources, a target — and per Goodhart's law,
imported here by REFERENCE as an established measurement-theory principle rather than re-derived, any
measure that becomes a target ceases to be a good measure, because the systems and agents whose behavior
it scores will optimize the measurable proxy rather than the underlying quality it was meant to track.
This is not a hypothetical risk for F2; it is the single most predictable failure mode of exactly the
kind of ratio-and-count algebra this dataset defines, and Part I's non-inflation requirement is
insufficient on its own to prevent it — a formula can resist naive inflation while still being gameable
by a sophisticated actor who understands its structure. Every score this dataset defines is therefore
paired with a NAMED countermetric: a second, independently-sourced signal that a measurer or reviewer
checks specifically to detect gaming of the first.

10.2 Countermetric for WhiteSpace: LEDGER-INDEPENDENCE CHECK. Because WhiteSpace is read from the
Non-Duplication Ledger's own verdict (Part III.2), the gaming risk is a proposer describing a candidate
to the Ledger in terms engineered to earn a BUILD verdict rather than the verdict the candidate's true
overlap merits — essentially, gaming the input the Ledger consumes rather than gaming F2's formula
directly. The countermetric is a periodic, INDEPENDENT re-audit of a sample of Ledger verdicts by a
reviewer (or an oneshot-architect-auditor-class subagent) who was not the original proposer, checking
whether the candidate's actual overlap matches the verdict's implied overlap — a mismatch flags the
WhiteSpace score for re-evaluation rather than trusting the original verdict permanently. (NORMATIVE.)

10.3 Countermetric for SystemsStrengthened: DIRECT-EVIDENCE SAMPLING. The gaming risk here is listing
plausible-sounding parents with vague mechanisms that satisfy a lenient reading of Part VII.2's three
conditions without genuinely producing measurable effect. The countermetric is direct-evidence sampling:
for a random subset of claimed STRENGTHENS relations each measurement cycle, a reviewer demands the
concrete "evidence required" artifact the Matrix format specifies (a mission trace, a matched failure
fingerprint, a before/after cost) and downgrades the relation to HYPOTHESIZED if the artifact cannot be
produced on demand — converting an unauditable claim into an honestly-marked, lower-confidence one rather
than either accepting it uncritically or rejecting the whole dataset. (NORMATIVE, DERIVED from Article
V.)

10.4 Countermetric for Reuse and CrossProject: CONSUMER-IDENTITY VERIFICATION. The gaming risk is a
single consuming process invoking a capability many times in a tight loop to inflate an invocation
count, or a proposer instrumenting a capability's own test suite as if test invocations were genuine
missions. The countermetric requires that Reuse and CrossProject counts distinguish invocations by
DISTINCT consuming system identity and DISTINCT mission identity, not raw call count — ten invocations
from one test harness in one afternoon count as one weak reuse signal, not ten strong ones, and this
distinction is enforced by requiring the SCORES relation (Part II.2) to log consumer identity alongside
every reuse event it contributes to the factor. (NORMATIVE.) A related, subtler variant of the same gaming
risk is a genuine consumer that invokes a capability only because ANOTHER system routes to it by default,
without the invoking mission actually depending on the result — a case Consumer-Identity Verification
alone cannot fully catch, since the identity is genuinely distinct, and which is why the countermetric is
paired, in practice, with a spot-check of whether the invoking mission's own output actually changed as a
result of the invocation, not merely whether the call occurred.

10.5 Countermetric for Compounding: WINDOW-CONSISTENCY AUDITING. As flagged in Part VI.5, Compounding is
the sharpest gaming target because its formula literally rewards showing improvement across a trailing
window, which invites a measurer to choose the window that shows the best trend. The countermetric,
NORMATIVE, fixes the window definition at the ensemble-state granularity (every sealed mission or
dataset, not a hand-picked subset of them) and requires that a reported Compounding factor be
reproducible by recomputing it from the full, ordered ensemble-state history rather than a curated
subset — any Compounding value that cannot be reproduced from the complete history is marked CONTESTED
until the discrepancy is resolved, and a pattern of discrepancies for one measurer or one system is
itself a signal fed to IAS-D2's Immune System as a possible measurement-integrity fingerprint.

10.6 Countermetric for ModelIndependence, Complexity, Overlap, Maintenance, and NICG/DAR jointly:
CROSS-EVALUATOR CONSENSUS. The remaining factors share a common gaming risk — self-assessment bias, where
the system's own author or advocate is also the one estimating its Complexity, Maintenance burden, or
model-portability, three quantities that are each easier to underestimate when the estimator built the
thing being estimated. The countermetric, NORMATIVE, requires that these four factors, and the two
headline indices they feed (NICG and DAR), never be finalized from a single measurer's estimate alone:
at least one independent evaluator (a different IAS dataset's consuming system, a governance-overlay
pass, or the Owner directly) must concur within a stated tolerance before the score exits HYPOTHESIZED
evidence level, which is the general mechanism Part XII formalizes for every score in the algebra, stated
here specifically for the four factors most exposed to self-assessment bias.

10.7 Governing the countermetrics themselves: the meta-Goodhart risk and its containment. A countermetric
that becomes as well-known and as routinely checked as the primary metric it defends is, itself, subject
to the same Goodhart dynamic one level up — a sufficiently sophisticated actor who knows that
Direct-Evidence Sampling (10.3) checks a RANDOM subset each cycle could, in principle, prepare polished
evidence artifacts only for the specific relations most likely to be sampled, or could learn the sampling
cadence and time submissions to avoid an active sampling window. This meta-risk is not hypothetical
paranoia; it is the logical consequence of the same actor-incentive reasoning that motivated Part X in
the first place, applied recursively. The containment, NORMATIVE, is threefold and deliberately modest
rather than infinitely regressive: sampling parameters (which relations, how many, on what cadence) are
never published as a fixed, predictable schedule, so a gaming strategy tuned to today's sampling pattern
does not reliably transfer to tomorrow's; the Cross-Evaluator Consensus countermetric (10.6) is applied
periodically to the countermetric-application PROCESS itself, not only to the underlying scores, meaning
an evaluator occasionally checks whether Direct-Evidence Sampling is actually catching what it claims to
catch, closing the loop one level up without opening a third, infinite level; and any countermetric whose
own catch rate (the fraction of known-gamed cases it successfully flags, measured retrospectively once a
gaming case is discovered by other means) falls below a stated floor is itself marked CONTESTED and
scheduled for redesign, following the same evidence-decay discipline Part XV applies to ordinary scores.
This Part therefore does not claim its nine countermetrics are permanently gaming-proof — no measurement
system can honestly claim that — it claims only that the countermetrics are themselves measured,
auditable, and revisable under the identical discipline they impose on everything else, which is the one
property that keeps the regress from being infinite: the meta-level uses the SAME machinery as the object
level, rather than requiring an ever-taller stack of new, unaudited watchers. (NORMATIVE, DERIVED, and
EXPERIMENTAL as to the catch-rate floor's exact value, which — like the calibration constant k of Part
IV.5 — awaits real measurement history to set honestly.)

FINAL LAW — PART X. A formula unaccompanied by a countermetric is an invitation to be gamed; every score
this algebra produces carries a second, independently-sourced check specifically designed to catch the
way a rational actor would satisfy the first number without producing the value it claims to represent.

PART XI — MEASUREMENT DISCIPLINE: NUMBERS NOT ADJECTIVES
===============================================================================================

11.1 Importing the D2A numeric-benchmark rule, by reference and extension. The Duplicate-to-Advantage
family's own measurement doctrine — referenced here rather than re-derived, per Article II's boundary
discipline — establishes that a claim of advantage must be stated as a checkable number against a stated
benchmark, never as an unquantified adjective ("significantly better," "much more reusable"). F2 imports
this rule as its own PRIMARY measurement discipline and extends it across all eight Advantage Score
factors, the Fusion Score's five dimensions, and both headline indices: every score this dataset's
formulas produce must be accompanied by (a) the number, (b) the benchmark or baseline it is measured
against, and (c) the evidence level (Part XII) licensing the claim. A score reported as "strong" without
a number and a baseline is not a score under this discipline; it is an adjective wearing a score's
clothing, and F2's own Fusion Score profile (Part V) is the one deliberate, NAMED exception — its
qualitative slots are explicitly adjectives, but each slot is itself DERIVED from an underlying numeric
factor a reviewer can retrieve via the DECOMPOSES-INTO relation, so the adjective is always one hop from
a number rather than a substitute for one. (NORMATIVE, DERIVED, exception stated explicitly.)

11.2 The baseline requirement. A number without a comparison point is nearly as uninformative as an
adjective: "Reuse = 6" means nothing until compared against the ensemble's median Reuse, the system's own
prior-state Reuse, or a stated design target. Every Part III-IX factor and index is therefore reported,
NORMATIVE, with its comparison baseline named explicitly — cross-sectional (against the current
ensemble's distribution of the same factor across all systems), longitudinal (against the same system's
own prior measurement), or normative (against a target value stated in advance of measurement, which is
the only baseline type immune to the false-precision risk of retrofitting a target to match whatever was
observed). Retrofitting a normative baseline after the fact — declaring "0.3 was always the target" once
a measurement of 0.3 is in hand — is itself an adversarial case cataloged in Part XVII. A NORMATIVE
baseline is therefore never valid unless its declaration timestamp, logged as part of the SCORES relation
record per Part II.2, demonstrably precedes the measurement it is compared against; a baseline discovered
only after the fact is, by definition, not a target the system was aiming for, and reporting it as one
misrepresents the measurement's own honest history.

11.3 Precision-matching: reporting no more precision than the underlying evidence supports. A factor
computed from a single, weakly-evidenced observation must not be reported to three decimal places, because
the extra digits assert a precision the underlying evidence cannot support — this is the discipline Part
XVI names as false precision's proximate cause, stated here as the positive rule it violates by omission.
The NORMATIVE convention adopted across this dataset: a score derived from OBSERVED evidence at
sufficient sample size (per-factor thresholds set per measurement protocol) may report two decimal
places; a score derived from a single observation or DERIVED reasoning reports one decimal place or a
qualitative band; a HYPOTHESIZED score reports no decimal precision at all — a band (low/medium/high) or
an explicit UNRESOLVED marker — because reporting "0.47" for a guess dresses a guess as a measurement.

11.4 Legibility over compression: preserving intermediate quantities. Extending Part VII.5's principle
generally, every composite score in this algebra (the Advantage Score, the Fusion Score, NICG, DAR)
preserves and reports its full set of contributing factors alongside the composite, not merely the final
number — a discipline that costs storage and reporting length but buys auditability, because a composite
score that arrives without its components cannot be checked, only trusted, and this whole dataset exists
because the estate's five existing scorers were each individually trustworthy but collectively
incommensurable; a sixth trustworthy-but-opaque scorer would not fix that problem, it would compound it.
(NORMATIVE, DERIVED from Part I's traceability requirement.)

11.5 The discipline's own cost, acknowledged. Measurement discipline is not free: computing eight
factors, five Fusion Score dimensions, and two headline indices per system per ensemble-state transition,
each with baseline comparisons and preserved intermediates, is itself a Complexity and Maintenance cost
against the very denominator F2's own formula charges (Part IV.2-4.4) — and F2, measured by its own
algebra, must therefore be honest that its own Maintenance factor is non-trivial. This is not a flaw to
hide but a fact to price: Part XIX's self-evaluation and the Fusion Score row in this dataset's own
closing section report F2's Complexity Cost honestly rather than exempting the measurement substrate from
the measurement it imposes on everyone else. (NORMATIVE, self-referential, previewed for Part XIX.)

11.6 A worked application of the discipline to a single factor, showing what a properly formed report
looks like versus what an improperly formed one looks like. IMPROPERLY FORMED: "SYN-C has strong Reuse."
This is an adjective with no number, no baseline, and no evidence level — it fails 11.1's PRIMARY
discipline outright. IMPROPERLY FORMED, SECOND FAILURE MODE: "SYN-C's Reuse is 0.847." This has a number
but no baseline (0.847 relative to what?) and asserts three-decimal precision from what turns out, on
inspection, to be six logged invocations — a sample size too small to support that precision, violating
11.3. PROPERLY FORMED: "SYN-C's Reuse factor reads 0.8 (one decimal, per 11.3's small-sample convention),
cross-sectional baseline: ensemble median Reuse at this state is 0.5, so SYN-C sits materially above
median; sample basis: six invocations from four distinct consuming systems across three ensemble-state
transitions; evidence level: OBSERVED (Consumer-Identity Verification per Part X.4 has been run and
concurs)." The properly formed version is longer, and that length is the point: every claim a downstream
reviewer would need to independently verify or challenge — the number, what it is compared to, how much
evidence stands behind it, and who checked it — is present in the report itself rather than requiring a
follow-up investigation. (DERIVED, worked; this sub-section is the concrete template the Dataset-to-
Runtime Translation's schema candidate, in this dataset's closing sections, is built to enforce
structurally rather than rely on a measurer's memory of the discipline.)

11.7 Relationship to the estate's existing token-austerity and DNA-3000 protocols. The estate's own
governance already imposes a "circuit breaker" discipline against reading more raw data than a task
requires, favoring cached summaries over repeated re-reads. F2's measurement discipline is the scoring-
layer analogue of that same austerity principle, applied in the opposite direction: where token-austerity
governs how much RAW EVIDENCE F2's measurers should read before computing a factor (favoring cached,
already-logged telemetry — recall_roi's existing tracking, CO-01's existing accounting — over fresh,
expensive re-instrumentation), the precision-matching and baseline-preservation rules of 11.2-11.4 govern
how much of that evidence's PROVENANCE must be preserved in the OUTPUT report. The two disciplines are
complementary, not in tension: austerity on the input side keeps measurement cheap; legibility on the
output side keeps measurement trustworthy, and a formula that saved cost by reading less evidence but
then reported its result without preserving what little evidence it did read would have optimized the
wrong half of the cost equation, cheapening the compute while destroying the auditability that
justifies the algebra's existence in the first place. (DERIVED, NORMATIVE synthesis.)

FINAL LAW — PART XI. A number without a baseline is decoration, an adjective in numeral's clothing is
worse than an honest adjective, and a composite that hides its components asks to be trusted rather than
checked; this algebra reports numbers, states their baselines, matches precision to evidence, and shows
its work every time, including its own.

===============================================================================================
PART XII — EVIDENCE LEVELS FOR A SCORE: A SCORE IS A CLAIM
===============================================================================================

12.1 A score is not a fact; it is an assertion with a stated confidence. Part II.3 established that a
score is ontologically a claim, not an intrinsic property. This Part specifies exactly how the
Constitution's Article V epistemic vocabulary applies to a score, because "mark every load-bearing claim"
is a general rule and a score is the single most common load-bearing claim this whole family produces —
if the epistemic marking discipline fails anywhere in the corpus, it is most consequential here, since a
mis-marked score propagates its false confidence into every downstream allocation, ranking, and
extinction decision that consumes it.

12.2 The score-specific reading of each Article V term. HYPOTHESIZED: the formula has been computed from
available data, but the underlying factors rest on self-assessment, a single data point, or an
unverified STRENGTHENS relation — the default level for any newly-computed score per Article V's own
default rule (a mechanism without a level defaults to HYPOTHESIZED). OBSERVED: at least the Part X
countermetric for the dominant factor has been checked and concurs, and the raw invocation/relation data
the score is computed from is traceable to logged events rather than assertion. DERIVED: the score itself
was not directly measured but follows by stated reasoning from OBSERVED component factors — for instance,
an Advantage Score is always at most DERIVED even when every one of its eight factors is individually
OBSERVED, because the formula's combination step (the geometric means, the calibrated constant k) is
itself a modeling choice, not a measurement, and the composite inherits the lower of "all factors
OBSERVED" and "the combination step is DERIVED." NORMATIVE: a score expressing a target or threshold
(the healthy DAR threshold of Part IX.2, the calibration constant k) rather than a measured value.
CONTESTED: two independent measurers or countermetric checks disagree and the disagreement has not been
resolved. UNRESOLVED: explicitly named as the outcome of Part IV.6's degenerate cases and Part VIII.5's
state-discipline failure — a score that could not be computed honestly is reported as UNRESOLVED, never
silently omitted or silently defaulted to a neutral number, because a silent omission looks identical to
"this system scored acceptably" to a downstream consumer scanning for red flags, while UNRESOLVED is
visibly a gap demanding attention.

12.3 The composite ceiling rule. Generalizing the Advantage Score example of 12.2: any composite score's
evidence level is capped at the LOWEST evidence level among its required inputs, never averaged and never
inherited from its strongest input. A Fusion Score with four dimensions at OBSERVED and one at
HYPOTHESIZED (Integration Quality, the hardest of the five to verify directly, per Part V.2) is reported
at HYPOTHESIZED overall, with the profile format (Part V.3) additionally allowing the individual
dimension's evidence level to be shown alongside its qualitative band — a reviewer therefore sees both
"this profile's weakest link is Integration Quality" and "that weak link is why the whole profile is not
yet OBSERVED," rather than a single ambiguous overall label. (NORMATIVE, DERIVED: this is the score-level
instance of the geometric-mean discipline from Part IV.5 — a conjunctive claim is only as strong as its
weakest support, applied here to epistemic status rather than to the numeric formula, but the same logic.)

12.4 Evidence-level transitions and who may raise them. A score may move from HYPOTHESIZED toward
OBSERVED only when its stated countermetric (Part X) has been independently checked and concurred, never
by the original measurer's own re-assertion of confidence, and never by the mere passage of time — a
HYPOTHESIZED score that sits unchecked for many ensemble-state transitions does not become more credible
by persisting; per Part XV, it becomes STALE instead, a distinct and lower-trust state addressed fully
there. This asymmetry — evidence level can only be raised by new, independent verification, and can be
lowered by staleness, contradiction, or a failed countermetric check at any time — is the mechanism that
keeps the algebra's epistemic marking honest over the estate's real operating history rather than merely
at the moment of first computation. (NORMATIVE.)

12.5 A worked evidence-level timeline for a single factor, tracing every transition named in 12.4 and
12.2 end to end. At ensemble state E_1, SYN-G's ModelIndependence factor is first computed from the
system owner's self-assessment: evidence level HYPOTHESIZED, per 12.2's default rule. At E_2, an
independent evaluator (per Part X.6's Cross-Evaluator Consensus) runs an actual synthetic model-swap test
and confirms the self-assessment was roughly accurate: evidence level rises to OBSERVED, the only
permitted direction of a manual rise, and only because independent verification — not the passage of
time and not the original measurer's confidence — produced it. At E_3, no new verification occurs, but
E_3 is within ModelIndependence's factor-specific freshness window (Part XV.2), so the score remains
CURRENT at OBSERVED. At E_4, a real model upgrade occurs across the estate and a second independent
evaluator reruns the swap test, this time finding SYN-G's advantage substantially degraded — the new
measurement CONTRADICTS the OBSERVED reading from E_2, and per Part XIV.3's conflicting-scores protocol,
neither the old nor the new number is silently averaged; both are preserved, the discrepancy is logged to
IAS-A2's bus, and the evidence level for SYN-G's ModelIndependence is downgraded to CONTESTED pending
reconciliation — reconciliation resolves the CONTESTED state by determining whether the E_2 test was
flawed, the E_4 test is flawed, or SYN-G's true portability simply changed between the two model
generations (the most likely explanation, and itself a finding worth recording rather than treating as
an error). At E_5, with no reconciliation yet performed and two full ensemble-state transitions having
elapsed since the CONTESTED flag was raised, the factor additionally crosses its staleness window (Part
XV.3), and its evidence level is capped at HYPOTHESIZED by the staleness rule even though it is
simultaneously CONTESTED by the disagreement rule — the two downgrade mechanisms are independent and
either alone is sufficient to suppress a factor's trust level, which is the deliberately conservative,
NORMATIVE design this whole lifecycle exists to enforce: a score's confidence never survives on inertia,
whether the inertia is silence (staleness) or an unresolved fight (contestation). (DERIVED, worked, and
this timeline is the concrete demonstration that Parts XII and XV are not two independent rulebooks but
one coherent epistemic lifecycle viewed from two angles — evidence-level semantics here, temporal
mechanics there.)

12.6 Why this matters more here than anywhere else in the corpus. Every other IAS dataset produces
findings, maps, and mechanisms whose epistemic status a reader can usually assess by reading the
surrounding prose. A score is different: it is a single number that will be extracted from this dataset's
prose, stored in a registry, and consumed by a downstream system (IAS-C1's allocation, IAS-D1's
extinction candidate list) that may never read the prose that produced it — only the number and, if the
discipline holds, the evidence level attached to it. An evidence level that is dropped somewhere in that
extraction pipeline turns a HYPOTHESIZED guess into an apparently-authoritative fact by the time it
reaches an allocation decision, which is the single most consequential failure mode this whole dataset
exists to prevent, and it is precisely why the SCORES relation (Part II.2) makes the evidence level a
mandatory field of the relation itself, not an optional annotation a downstream consumer can silently
drop. (NORMATIVE, DERIVED, and the direct justification for treating Part XII as load-bearing rather than
procedural boilerplate.)

FINAL LAW — PART XII. A score that has shed its evidence level in transit is a lie wearing a number's
credibility; the evidence level is not metadata attached to a score, it is part of what the score IS, and
any system that stores, forwards, or consumes a score without its evidence level has broken the claim
into an assertion no one can any longer audit.

PART XIV — DECISION MODELS AND PROTOCOLS: WHAT A SCORE TRIGGERS
===============================================================================================

14.1 Scores exist to inform decisions, not to accumulate. Every formula in Parts III-IX would be inert
scholarship if no protocol specified what happens once a score crosses a threshold, conflicts with
another score, or reaches a consumer. This Part specifies the DECISION MODEL layer the Constitution's
depth contract requires (architecture; decision models; protocols; lifecycle) — the rules by which a
computed score becomes an action, always mediated through the appropriate sibling system per Article III
(F2 never acts directly on a system; it informs the system that has authority to act).

14.2 The threshold-and-escalation protocol. Each of the eight Advantage Score factors and both headline
indices carries a CALIBRATED, EXPERIMENTAL threshold band (green / amber / red) rather than a single
trip-wire, because a single hard threshold invites exactly the boundary-gaming Part X exists to prevent
(a score engineered to land at 0.501 rather than 0.499 is a Goodhart artifact, not a genuine improvement).
Crossing from green into amber for any factor triggers a NORMATIVE advisory surfaced to the relevant
consuming system (a low-Reuse advisory to IAS-C1's allocation review; a rising-Overlap advisory to
IAS-D1's ecology map) rather than an automatic action; crossing into red for the composite Advantage
Score or for NICG at the ensemble level triggers the same class of advisory-only escalation this
dataset's parent Constitution requires throughout the estate (per the recorded doctrine that stalled or
unbounded conditions are surfaced, never auto-acted on) — F2 proposes, the receiving system with
authority decides, exactly as Article IX requires for the estate's self-evolution more broadly.

14.3 The conflicting-scores protocol. When two independent measurers produce materially different scores
for the same target (a CONTESTED evidence level per Part XII.2), the protocol is not simple averaging —
averaging two disputed numbers manufactures a third number with no more claim to truth than either input,
and buries the disagreement rather than surfacing it. The protocol instead requires: (a) both scores and
both measurers' identities are preserved and shown side by side, never merged into one figure; (b) the
disagreement is itself logged as a signal on IAS-A2's Control Bus (the mechanism the Compounding Graph
names for cross-system event exchange), since a persistent disagreement pattern for one factor or one
measurer is diagnostic information in its own right — possibly indicating a genuinely ambiguous system, a
flawed measurement protocol for that factor, or an adversarial measurer, three different underlying
conditions the Immune System (IAS-D2) is positioned to distinguish over time; (c) the CONTESTED score is
excluded from any composite (Advantage Score, Fusion Score, NICG, DAR) until resolved, per the
composite-ceiling rule of Part XII.3, rather than silently included at a discounted weight that would
still let a disputed number influence an allocation decision.

14.4 The mission-scoped versus standing-score distinction. A score may be computed in two protocol
contexts that must never be conflated: a STANDING score, attached to a system and updated at each
ensemble-state transition per this dataset's normal cadence, and a MISSION-SCOPED score, computed
on-demand by IAS-F3's Digital Twin as part of a simulated mission's projected outcome before the mission
is committed. A mission-scoped score is always marked EXPERIMENTAL (it is a projection, not a
measurement) and is never written back into a system's standing score history until the real mission
executes and produces an OBSERVED result to compare the projection against — conflating the two would let
a favorable simulation masquerade as a confirmed measurement, corrupting NICG's state-discipline
requirement from Part VIII.5. (NORMATIVE, DERIVED, and the precise seam at which F2 and IAS-F3 interlock
per the Compounding Graph's foresight loop.)

14.5 The Owner-override protocol. Per Article IX (governed self-evolution: IAS proposes, the Owner
promotes) and the estate's own recorded doctrine that a stalled-session advisory is never auto-acted on,
every decision this Part's protocols surface — an amber advisory, a red escalation, a CONTESTED exclusion
— remains a RECOMMENDATION to the consuming system or the human Owner, never a directive the algebra
enforces unilaterally. F2 has no authority of its own (per F1's systemhood test, F2's distinct object is
the measurement substrate, not decision authority over any resource); the algebra's outputs are inputs to
decisions made by systems that DO hold authority — IAS-C1 over allocation, IAS-D1 over
retirement/promotion, the Owner over anything the algebra flags as high-stakes or CONTESTED. (NORMATIVE,
DERIVED directly from Article IX and from F1's own object model.)

14.6 The competing-escalations protocol: what happens when several advisories reach the Owner at once.
14.2's threshold protocol and 14.3's conflicting-scores protocol each specify how a SINGLE advisory or a
SINGLE disagreement is surfaced; neither specifies what happens when an ensemble-state transition
produces several amber advisories, one red escalation, and two CONTESTED exclusions simultaneously — a
realistic outcome once F2 is operating across dozens of systems, and a case this dataset must not leave
implicit given the estate's own recorded lesson that an unbounded, un-prioritized backlog of signals
reaching a human Owner degrades into the exact "invisible OWNER_QUEUE" failure the estate has already
catalogued once. The protocol, NORMATIVE, borrows its prioritization logic by REFERENCE from owner_queue
rather than inventing a competing one: every advisory this dataset's decision model produces is submitted
to owner_queue as a typed item (severity, source factor, affected system, evidence level) rather than
delivered as an ad hoc alert, and owner_queue's own existing priority ordering — not a second, F2-private
ordering — determines what the Owner sees first. This is a deliberate ensemble-object-boundary decision:
F2 could have built its own escalation-ranking logic, but doing so would duplicate owner_queue's
distinct object (the ordering of Owner-facing items across the whole estate, not merely F2's slice of
them) in exactly the way Article II forbids, so F2 instead FEEDS owner_queue rather than competing with
it. A red escalation for the ensemble-level NICG (per 14.2) is submitted at the highest available
severity tier, since a whole-ensemble signal outranks any single system's amber advisory by construction;
two or more CONTESTED exclusions affecting the SAME downstream allocation decision (per 14.3) are
submitted as a single bundled item rather than two separate ones, so the Owner sees the compound
uncertainty in one place rather than resolving each in isolation and missing that they jointly block the
same decision. (NORMATIVE, DERIVED, and this sub-section is the concrete mechanism by which F2
strengthens owner_queue — an unlisted sixth beneficiary beyond the Matrix's five named parents, feeding
it typed, severity-ranked items it would not otherwise have visibility into — while carefully avoiding
building a duplicate prioritization engine of its own.)

14.7 A worked micro-trace of one score's path through the full decision model, tying 14.2 through 14.6
together. Suppose SYN-E's (Part XVIII) Overlap factor crosses from green into amber at ensemble state
E_4. Per 14.2, this triggers a NORMATIVE advisory, not an automatic action, routed to IAS-D1's ecology
map as the receiving system with authority over redundancy judgments. At the same transition, a second,
independent measurement of SYN-E's SystemsStrengthened relation disagrees with the first (per 18.4's
CONTESTED flag), triggering 14.3's conflicting-scores protocol: both readings are preserved, the
disagreement is logged to IAS-A2's bus, and the CONTESTED score is excluded from SYN-E's Advantage Score
composite until resolved. Because these two events — the amber Overlap advisory and the CONTESTED
SystemsStrengthened exclusion — both concern SYN-E and both surface in the same ensemble-state transition,
14.6's bundling rule applies: rather than reaching the Owner as two unrelated items, they are submitted to
owner_queue as a single bundled entry naming SYN-E as the common subject, so the Owner sees at a glance
that one system carries two open concerns rather than discovering the connection by cross-referencing two
separate queue items. Throughout this entire trace, per 14.5's Owner-override protocol, F2 itself takes no
action on SYN-E — it does not retire it, does not exclude it from a mission, does not adjust its score
downward beyond the honest CONTESTED exclusion the evidence itself requires; every actionable step (should
SYN-E be retired, should its Overlap be remediated, should the disagreement be investigated) waits on
IAS-D1's ecology judgment or the Owner's direct decision, exactly as Article IX requires. (DERIVED, worked,
and this trace is the concrete demonstration that the four protocols of 14.2-14.6 are not four separate
mechanisms operating in isolation but one coherent decision model that a single real event exercises
end to end.)

FINAL LAW — PART XIV. A score that triggers nothing is trivia and a score that triggers an unmediated
action is a coup; this algebra's decision model routes every threshold crossing, every disagreement, and
every projection to the system with actual authority over the resource at stake, and proposes — it never
decides.

PART XV — THE LIFECYCLE OF A SCORE
===============================================================================================

15.1 Why a score needs a lifecycle at all. Part II.6 previewed that the object model supports a lifecycle
by dating and ordering scores rather than overwriting them in place; this Part specifies that lifecycle
fully, because the Constitution's depth contract explicitly requires a lifecycle treatment and because
the estate's own recorded lesson — a described state is a hypothesis until reconciled against the disk,
and staleness is a distinct failure mode from absence — applies with particular force to a measurement
substrate whose entire purpose is to be trusted by downstream allocation decisions. A score that is
silently trusted past its useful life is as dangerous as a score that was wrong on arrival.

15.2 The five lifecycle states. A score, DEFINED, passes through up to five states: PROPOSED (a
measurement has been computed but not yet checked against its countermetric — evidence level
HYPOTHESIZED by default per Part XII.2); CURRENT (the countermetric has concurred, or sufficient
independent evidence otherwise supports the score, and it is the most recent score for its target —
evidence level at least OBSERVED, per Part II.6's cardinality rule of exactly one CURRENT score per
target at a time); SUPERSEDED (a newer measurement for the same target has become CURRENT, demoting this
one; a superseded score is retained in the SCORES relation history for auditability and for the trailing
series Compounding and NICG require, but is never consulted as if it were current); STALE (a CURRENT
score has aged past a factor-specific freshness window — set NORMATIVE per factor, since WhiteSpace,
sourced from a largely-static Ledger verdict, ages slowly, while Maintenance, sourced from ongoing
governance telemetry, ages within a small number of ensemble-state transitions — without a newer
measurement replacing it, and is downgraded automatically, without requiring a countermetric failure, on
the principle that confidence decays with time even absent contradicting evidence); RETIRED (the target
system itself has been retired per IAS-D1's extinction protocol, and its score history is archived rather
than actively maintained, though never deleted, since a retired system's history remains evidence for
future ecology and portfolio decisions about similar systems).

15.3 The staleness transition as an automatic, time-triggered downgrade. Staleness is the one lifecycle
transition that fires without any new evidence arriving — purely from elapsed ensemble-state transitions
— and it is deliberately automatic rather than requiring a reviewer to notice, because the estate's own
observed failure pattern (a session hung undetected for hours, per the recorded stalled-session incident)
demonstrates that relying on a human or a downstream consumer to notice staleness is not a safe default.
A STALE score's evidence level is capped, NORMATIVE, at HYPOTHESIZED regardless of what evidence level it
held while CURRENT, and any composite consuming a STALE component inherits that cap per Part XII.3's
composite-ceiling rule — a Fusion Score built on three fresh dimensions and two STALE ones is capped at
HYPOTHESIZED overall, which is the intended, conservative behavior: staleness is not merely a footnote, it
actively degrades the trustworthiness of anything built on top of it.

15.4 Refreshing a stale or superseded score: the re-measurement protocol. A STALE score is refreshed by
recomputing the full factor from current data and re-running its Part X countermetric, exactly as if it
were a first-time PROPOSED score — there is no shortcut that lets a stale score "re-validate" itself
without new evidence, because the whole point of the staleness transition is that time alone is not
evidence. A SUPERSEDED score is never refreshed; it is historical record, and any question about "what
did we believe at ensemble state E_i" is answered by retrieving the score that was CURRENT at that state
from the SCORES relation's timestamped history, never by asking what the CURRENT score is today and
assuming it applied retroactively — this distinction is precisely what NICG's state-discipline
requirement (Part VIII.5) depends on.

15.5 Lifecycle interaction with the ensemble state and with Compounding. Because Compounding (Part VI)
and NICG (Part VIII) both require a well-formed trailing series of scores at successive ensemble states,
the lifecycle's STALE and SUPERSEDED states are what make that series well-formed rather than a jumble
of measurements taken at arbitrary, uncoordinated times: the trailing series is built exclusively from
the sequence of scores that were CURRENT at each successive ensemble state, retrieved from history, never
from whatever happens to be CURRENT at the moment the series is assembled. A trailing series assembled
carelessly from "whatever score exists now for each historical state" would silently substitute later
knowledge for earlier belief, corrupting the growth-rate calculation Compounding depends on — the exact
timing-artifact risk Part VIII.5 already named for NICG, generalized here as a lifecycle discipline that
protects every formula depending on a historical series, not NICG alone. (DERIVED.)

15.6 The tension between rapid ensemble-state churn and per-factor freshness windows, and how it is
resolved. 15.2 states that freshness windows are set per factor because different factors age at
different rates — WhiteSpace slowly, Maintenance quickly. This creates a genuine tension under a
condition this sub-section names explicitly: if the estate enters a period of unusually rapid
ensemble-state churn (many missions sealing in quick succession, each triggering a fresh measurement
opportunity), a factor with a SHORT freshness window (Maintenance) may go STALE and be refreshed several
times within the span that a factor with a LONG freshness window (WhiteSpace) has not yet required a
single refresh — meaning a single system's Fusion Score, at any given instant during a churn period, may
be built from components refreshed at wildly different recency, even though none of them is individually
STALE by its own window's standard. This is not a defect to patch; it is the correct, DERIVED consequence
of factors that genuinely age at different real-world rates, and the NORMATIVE handling is to report each
factor's own last-refresh timestamp alongside its value (per 11.4's legibility principle) rather than
attempt to force all eight factors onto one artificial, uniform refresh cadence — a uniform cadence would
either over-refresh WhiteSpace (wasting measurement effort on a factor that has not meaningfully changed)
or under-refresh Maintenance (letting a genuinely fast-moving cost signal go stale under a slower factor's
window). The opposite condition — an unusually QUIET period with few ensemble-state transitions — produces
the mirror risk: even a short-window factor like Maintenance may go STALE simply because no new transition
occurred to trigger a refresh opportunity, not because anything about the system changed, and a reviewer
encountering a STALE Maintenance reading during a quiet period should not read staleness as a signal of
DEGRADATION, only as a signal of UNMEASUREDNESS — a distinction Part IV.6's NOT-YET-MEASURABLE category
was designed to make for a system's first measurement, and which applies with equal force to a system's
SUBSEQUENT stale readings during an unusually quiet operating period. (DERIVED, NORMATIVE, and this
sub-section closes the lifecycle Part by confirming that "aged" and "worsened" are never the same claim
in this algebra — a distinction Part XV shares with, and reinforces, Part XII's evidence-level discipline
throughout.)

FINAL LAW — PART XV. A score does not live forever on the strength of the evidence that first produced
it; it is proposed, becomes current, ages toward stale whether or not anyone notices, and is superseded or
retired — and an algebra that lets a score's confidence outlive its evidence is building every downstream
decision on a foundation that looks solid only because no one checked the date.

PART XVI — THE FALSE-PRECISION FAILURE MODE
===============================================================================================

16.1 Naming the failure mode precisely. FALSE PRECISION, as DEFINED for this dataset, is the failure of
reporting a score with more numeric specificity than its underlying evidence can support, such that the
reported precision itself becomes a false claim independent of whether the score's central tendency is
roughly correct. A score of "0.473" reported from a single self-assessed observation is false-precise even
if 0.473 happens to be a reasonable estimate of the true value, because the three significant figures
assert a measurement resolution — confidence to within roughly one part in a thousand — that a single
self-assessment cannot possibly have earned. This is a distinct failure from being simply wrong: a
false-precise score can be centrally accurate and still misleading, because its FORM communicates a
confidence its CONTENT does not possess, and a downstream consumer reading the number without reading the
evidence trail behind it inherits the false confidence along with the number. The failure is therefore
independent of correctness: a false-precise but centrally accurate score and a false-precise, badly wrong
score look IDENTICAL to a downstream consumer who has no way to distinguish the two without re-deriving
the original evidence, which is precisely the auditability gap Part XI's discipline exists to close.

16.2 Why this algebra is unusually exposed to the failure mode. An eight-factor geometric-mean formula
composing five headline indices is, by its own structure, an attractive target for false precision,
because a formula this articulated LOOKS rigorous regardless of whether its inputs are — the sophistication
of the arithmetic can be mistaken for the sophistication of the evidence feeding it. This is precisely
the failure the vault's own recorded lesson warns against under the name "founding finding is a
hypothesis": a composite number, once computed, tends to be treated as more solid than the individual,
weaker inputs that produced it, because the act of computation feels like an act of verification even
when it is not. F2 is a formula-heavy dataset by design (Part I through Part IX are substantially
arithmetic), which makes this Part not an optional caution but a necessary counterweight built into the
same document, not appended as an afterthought.

16.3 The three concrete triggers, worked. TRIGGER ONE: reporting a composite (Advantage Score, Fusion
Score, NICG, DAR) to full decimal precision when one or more of its inputs is HYPOTHESIZED or STALE —
prevented by the precision-matching convention of Part XI.3 and the composite-ceiling rule of Part XII.3,
which together force the composite's reported precision down to match its weakest input rather than its
strongest. TRIGGER TWO: reporting a CALIBRATED constant (the scaling factor k of Part IV.5, the healthy
DAR threshold of Part IX.2) as if it were a measured fact rather than a design choice awaiting empirical
confirmation — prevented by marking every such constant EXPERIMENTAL explicitly at first mention and
requiring the mark to travel with the constant wherever it is cited, never dropped after its first
appearance. TRIGGER THREE: reporting a small-sample statistic (a Reuse count of 3 invocations, a
Compounding trend from only two ensemble-state measurements) with the same formatting as a large-sample
statistic, letting a reader's eye treat "Reuse = 0.60" from three data points as equivalent in confidence
to "Reuse = 0.60" from three hundred — prevented by the NORMATIVE requirement that every reported factor
carry its underlying sample size (or state NOT-YET-MEASURABLE per Part IV.6 for genuinely thin samples)
alongside the number, never the number alone.

16.4 False precision compounding across the family. Because the Compounding Graph routes F2's scores into
IAS-C1, IAS-B1, and IAS-D1, a false-precise score does not stay contained to F2's own output — it
propagates: IAS-C1's Portfolio ranking, IAS-B1's leverage ranking, and IAS-D1's fitness map all inherit
whatever false confidence F2 exports, and each of those consumers may in turn treat the number as settled
input to ITS own formula, compounding the false confidence a second time before a human ever sees the
result. This is the concrete mechanism by which a single sloppy score at the foundation layer becomes
several confidently-wrong downstream decisions, and it is the reason Part XII's evidence-level discipline
is treated in this dataset as more important than the arithmetic elegance of any individual formula — an
inelegant formula with honest error bars is safer for the ensemble than an elegant one with none.

16.5 The remedy is discipline, not abandonment of precision. The response to false-precision risk is not
to strip the algebra of numbers and retreat to pure adjectives — Part XI.1 already rejected that
alternative as its own failure mode (adjectives in numeral's clothing, or numeral-less vagueness, are
both worse than disciplined numbers). The remedy, consistently applied across Parts XI, XII, and this
Part, is that every number in this algebra travels with three companions it may never be separated from
in any report, registry entry, or downstream consumption: its evidence level, its sample size or basis,
and its comparison baseline. A number missing any of the three is, by this dataset's own definition,
not yet a properly formed F2 score — it is a raw figure that has not completed the discipline this
dataset exists to impose.

16.6 A worked micro-example of false precision caught before it propagates. Suppose a newly-onboarded
measurer computes SYN-F's (Part XVIII's REJECTED-then-abandoned synthetic system) Complexity factor from
a single, informal read of its file structure and reports "Complexity = 0.612." Applying this Part's
three triggers in sequence: TRIGGER THREE fires first, since the report carries no sample-size or basis
annotation — a single informal read is not a sample in any defensible sense, and the omission alone is
disqualifying under 11.3's precision-matching convention. Even setting that aside, TRIGGER ONE would
independently fire, because SYN-F's overall Advantage Score composite (per Part XVIII.2's worked
computation for a REJECT-verdict system) is already capped at low confidence by its high Overlap and its
UNRESOLVED Compounding — a composite this uncertain reporting one component to three decimal places is
an internal inconsistency any reviewer applying the composite-ceiling rule of Part XII.3 should catch on
sight. The corrected, properly formed report reads: "Complexity: qualitative band HIGH (per 11.3's
HYPOTHESIZED-level convention, no decimal precision); basis: single informal structural read, not yet
independently evaluated per Part X.6; comparison baseline: cross-sectional, roughly in line with other
REJECT-verdict systems' typical Complexity band; evidence level: HYPOTHESIZED." Nothing about SYN-F's
true complexity changed between the two reports — only the FORM of the claim changed, from a number that
falsely implied precision to a band that honestly discloses its own thinness, and this is the entire
content of what this Part asks every measurer in the ensemble to do differently. (DERIVED, worked, and
this closing example is deliberately the algebra's plainest one — a false-precision failure caught is not
a dramatic event, it is a small, procedural correction repeated consistently, which is precisely why the
discipline must be structural (per the schema candidate in this dataset's Dataset-to-Runtime Translation
section) rather than left to a measurer's individual diligence.)
dataset exists to impose. (NORMATIVE, and the synthesis of Parts XI-XII-XVI's three-part discipline.)

FINAL LAW — PART XVI. An elegant formula computing over thin or self-assessed evidence produces a
number that is precise and wrong in the same breath; false precision is not solved by fewer decimal
places, it is solved by never letting a number travel without the evidence level, sample size, and
baseline that earned it — the arithmetic's sophistication is never allowed to stand in for the evidence's.

PART XVII — METRIC-GAMING ADVERSARIAL CASES
===============================================================================================

17.1 Purpose: adversarial cases as the algebra's own stress test. Part X paired each factor with a
countermetric in the abstract; this Part works nine concrete adversarial scenarios end to end — an
actor's incentive, the exact gaming move, why the naive formula would fall for it, and why the stated
countermetric catches it — because a countermetric asserted without being run against a worked attack is
itself an instance of the false-precision failure Part XVI just catalogued: a defense that has never been
tried against an attacker is a claim, not a demonstrated property. All nine cases are synthetic,
constructed against the eight-system ensemble Part XVIII builds, and none references any real Claude
Power Pack system by an accusation of actual gaming — these are stress tests of the formula, not findings
about any real actor.

17.2 Case 1 — the WhiteSpace-shopping proposer. INCENTIVE: a proposer wants a new system's WhiteSpace
factor scored near 1 to maximize its Advantage Score. MOVE: the proposer describes the candidate to the
Ledger using language that emphasizes its novel surface (a new name, a new file location, a new entry
point) while omitting that its core mechanism duplicates an existing system's already-covered territory,
angling for a BUILD verdict the true overlap does not merit. WHY NAIVE SCORING FALLS FOR IT: WhiteSpace
is read directly from the recorded verdict (Part III.2, Part XIII.2), and if the verdict itself was
earned by an under-described candidate, WhiteSpace inherits the error faithfully. WHY THE COUNTERMETRIC
CATCHES IT: Part X.2's Ledger-Independence Check requires periodic re-audit by a reviewer who was not the
original proposer, specifically checking the candidate's ACTUAL mechanism (not its self-description)
against the Ledger's existing entries — a re-audit that reads the candidate's code path rather than its
prose catches the mismatch the original verdict missed.

17.3 Case 2 — the reciprocal-strengthening ring. INCENTIVE: two or more system owners want high
SystemsStrengthened counts for each of their systems. MOVE: system A and system B each claim to
"strengthen" the other via a vague, mutually-reinforcing mechanism ("A's output improves B's context, B's
output improves A's routing") stated just concretely enough to pass a superficial reading of Part VII.2's
three conditions, without either producing a checkable artifact. WHY NAIVE SCORING FALLS FOR IT: a
reviewer checking each claim in isolation, rather than checking the pair together, may accept each side of
the ring independently on the strength of the other's matching claim, each lending the other false
plausibility. WHY THE COUNTERMETRIC CATCHES IT: Part X.3's Direct-Evidence Sampling demands the concrete
"evidence required" artifact on demand, per-relation, regardless of how mutually consistent the pair's
narrative sounds — a ring collapses the moment either side is asked to produce its artifact independently
of the other's claim.

17.4 Case 3 — the reuse-inflation test-harness. Already introduced structurally in Part X.4; worked here
in full. INCENTIVE: a system owner wants a high Reuse factor. MOVE: the owner's own CI or test suite
invokes the capability hundreds of times, each invocation logged as a "use." WHY NAIVE SCORING FALLS FOR
IT: a raw invocation count cannot distinguish a genuine mission consumer from an automated test loop.
WHY THE COUNTERMETRIC CATCHES IT: Consumer-Identity Verification requires DISTINCT consuming-system and
DISTINCT-mission identity, logged via the SCORES relation at the moment of each reuse event — a test
harness invoking itself repeatedly registers as one weak signal from one identity, not hundreds of strong
ones.

17.5 Case 4 — the window-cherry-picking compounder. Already flagged structurally in Part VI.5 and Part
X.5; worked here. INCENTIVE: a measurer wants to show strong Compounding for a system whose true
trajectory is flat or declining. MOVE: the measurer selects a trailing window that begins at an
artificially low early measurement (perhaps itself under-measured or measured before the system matured)
and ends at a favorable recent one, presenting the resulting slope as sustained growth. WHY NAIVE SCORING
FALLS FOR IT: without a fixed window definition, any measurer with access to the full history can select
the sub-window that tells the best story. WHY THE COUNTERMETRIC CATCHES IT: Window-Consistency Auditing
fixes the window to the complete, ordered ensemble-state history and requires reproducibility from that
full history — a cherry-picked sub-window trend that vanishes when recomputed over the complete series is
caught immediately and the discrepancy is logged to IAS-A2's bus per Part XIV.3's conflicting-scores
protocol.

17.6 Case 5 — the profile-scalar conflation. INCENTIVE: an allocator wants a simple "which is better"
answer and does not want to read a five-slot profile. MOVE: the allocator, or a downstream tool built
carelessly, collapses a system's Fusion Score profile into an informal average ("three strong, one
adequate, one weak — call it strong overall") and uses that informal collapse for ranking, silently
recreating the single-number failure Part V.3 explicitly rejected. WHY NAIVE SCORING FALLS FOR IT: nothing
in the arithmetic prevents a downstream consumer from performing an unauthorized collapse; the profile
format is a NORMATIVE convention, not a technical enforcement. WHY THE COUNTERMETRIC CATCHES IT: this is
the one case whose defense is procedural rather than computational — the Dataset-to-Runtime Translation
section specifies that any tool consuming a Fusion Score must be built to reject a request for "the Fusion
Score as one number," structurally refusing the conflation at the interface level rather than trusting
every future consumer to remember the NORMATIVE convention.

17.7 Case 6 — the self-assessed Complexity discount. INCENTIVE: a system's own author wants a low
Complexity score (cheap in the denominator, per Part IV.2) for a system they know is, in practice, hard
for anyone else to extend. MOVE: the author estimates Complexity based on how legible the system is TO
THEM, having built it and holding its full context, rather than how legible it would be to an independent
reader. WHY NAIVE SCORING FALLS FOR IT: self-assessment is systematically biased toward the assessor's own
familiarity, and nothing in a single-source estimate corrects for that bias. WHY THE COUNTERMETRIC CATCHES
IT: Part X.6's Cross-Evaluator Consensus requires an independent evaluator's concurrence before the score
exits HYPOTHESIZED — an evaluator without the author's context will, almost by construction, produce a
higher Complexity estimate if the true legibility gap exists, and the discrepancy itself becomes evidence.

17.8 Case 7 — the DAR-by-paralysis gambit. Already flagged structurally in Part IX.5; worked here.
INCENTIVE: a reviewer wants to show a strong Duplicate Avoidance Rate. MOVE: the reviewer rejects nearly
every proposal outright regardless of merit, driving DAR toward 1.0 by minimizing the denominator's risk
rather than by genuinely improving the estate's proposal-checking discipline. WHY NAIVE SCORING FALLS FOR
IT: DAR's bare formula (Part IX.2) cannot distinguish "proposals are checked and correctly converted" from
"proposals are refused wholesale." WHY THE COUNTERMETRIC CATCHES IT: Part IX.5's joint-reading requirement
mandates DAR always be read alongside raw proposal volume and NICG — a DAR near 1.0 accompanied by
collapsing proposal volume and flat-to-negative NICG is flagged, per Part XIV.2's threshold protocol, as
an amber advisory for paralysis rather than accepted as a green signal for discipline.

17.9 Case 8 — the documentation-only Integration Quality lift. Directly extending Part XIII.6's flagged
weak mapping. INCENTIVE: a system owner wants a higher Fusion Score Integration Quality dimension without
doing the harder work of actually cleaning up undocumented coupling. MOVE: the owner substantially improves
the system's GK-09-indexed documentation — clearer descriptions, better cross-links — without touching the
underlying interface's actual fragility to a parent system's refactor. WHY NAIVE SCORING FALLS FOR IT:
because the GK-09-to-Integration-Quality mapping (Part XIII.6) is HYPOTHESIZED and imperfectly correlated,
a documentation-only improvement can move the proxy without moving the underlying property. WHY THE
COUNTERMETRIC CATCHES IT: this case is the reason Part XIII.6 marks the mapping HYPOTHESIZED rather than
DERIVED in the first place — a HYPOTHESIZED mapping's score never exits that evidence level (per Part
12.4's transition rule) without independent verification, meaning a documentation-only lift caps at
HYPOTHESIZED and cannot alone push Integration Quality into a trusted "strong" reading regardless of how
much the GK-09 proxy moves.

17.10 Case 9 — the retroactive normative baseline. Already flagged structurally in Part XI.2; worked here
in full. INCENTIVE: a measurer wants a factor to read as meeting its target. MOVE: after observing a
factor's value, the measurer states, or implies, that the observed value "was always" the intended
normative target, retroactively defining success to match whatever occurred. WHY NAIVE SCORING FALLS FOR
IT: nothing in a bare number distinguishes a genuine advance-stated target from a retroactively-declared
one unless the target's declaration timestamp is checked against the measurement's timestamp. WHY THE
COUNTERMETRIC CATCHES IT: Part XI.2's normative-baseline discipline requires every normative target to
carry its own declaration timestamp as part of the SCORES relation record, and any normative baseline
whose declaration timestamp postdates the measurement it is being compared against is automatically
invalid and excluded, per the same evidence-discipline mechanism that governs every other claim in this
algebra.

FINAL LAW — PART XVII. Nine incentives, nine moves, nine countermetrics that were actually tried against
them rather than merely stated — an anti-Goodhart design that has not been run against its own worked
attacks has only asserted its own resistance, and this Part is the difference between asserting and
demonstrating that the algebra survives contact with a rational adversary.

PART XIX — EVALUATION OF THE ALGEBRA ITSELF

19.1 Turning the formula on its own author. Per Part V.4's self-referential note and Part XI.5's

19.2 F2's own factor estimates, honestly capped at HYPOTHESIZED. WhiteSpace: per the Ledger's own verdict
for F2's founding candidates (Family C's Capability ROI and Opportunity Cost candidates, folded BUILD/MERGE

DECISION LOG
===============================================================================================

D-1. DECISION: Bound the Institutional Advantage Unit to a closed [-1, +1] interval rather than leave it
unbounded. ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED: an unbounded ratio (raw amplification-over-cost, no ceiling); a
[0,100] percentile-style score matching common dashboard conventions. WHY THIS: an unbounded score
invites false precision (Part XVI) and cannot be composed via a geometric-mean product (Part IV.5) without
arithmetic blow-up; the [-1,+1] band also gives the algebra a natural, meaningful zero (self-funding) and a
natural negative region (net drag, feeding IAS-D1's parasite detection directly). TRADE-OFFS: bounding
compresses information at the extremes — two systems that are both "very good" may both clip near +1 and
become hard to distinguish without reading their component factors. REVERSAL CONDITION: if real ensemble
history shows the majority of systems clustering within a narrow sub-band (say, 0.6-0.9) such that the
bound is rarely tested, an unbounded or wider-banded alternative should be reconsidered — a decision for
Part XIX.5's falsification program to surface.

D-2. DECISION: Use geometric means (not arithmetic means) for both the numerator and denominator of the
Advantage Score formula. ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED: weighted arithmetic mean with tunable per-factor weights;
a minimum-of-factors ("weakest link only") approach. WHY THIS: geometric mean forces every factor to be
genuinely present, matching the conjunctive nature of the claim "this system is institutionally
advantageous" (Part IV.5); a weighted arithmetic mean would require calibrating eight more free parameters
(the weights) on top of the single constant k, multiplying the false-precision risk this dataset spends
three Parts (XI, XII, XVI) guarding against. TRADE-OFFS: the geometric mean is harsher than an arithmetic
mean on systems with one weak factor and five strong ones, which may undervalue a genuinely excellent but
narrowly-focused system. REVERSAL CONDITION: if Part XIX.5's falsification program finds real systems
scoring implausibly low due to one thin-but-improving factor (a young system's low Reuse dragging down an
otherwise excellent profile), a hybrid — geometric mean with a floor substitution for NOT-YET-MEASURABLE
factors, rather than a zero-adjacent value — should be reconsidered.

D-3. DECISION: Report the Fusion Score as a five-slot qualitative profile, never collapsed to a scalar.
ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED: a single weighted-average Fusion scalar; a simple pass/fail gate per dimension.
WHY THIS: Part V.3 demonstrates that averaging hides exactly the information (a strong-Reuse,
weak-Integration-Quality system) that a portfolio or ecology decision most needs to see; a pass/fail gate
loses the gradation between "weak" and "unmeasured" that Part XII's evidence-level discipline requires.
TRADE-OFFS: a profile is harder to sort a long list by than a single number, which is why the Advantage
Score scalar is preserved alongside it specifically for ranking use (Part V.3). REVERSAL CONDITION: none
identified within this dataset's scope — Part XVII Case 5 shows this decision is actively defended against
downstream conflation rather than merely stated, which is evidence the decision is load-bearing, not that
it should be reversed.

D-4. DECISION: Cap a composite score's evidence level at the LOWEST evidence level of its required inputs
(Part XII.3), never an average or the highest input's level. ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED: majority-rule (a
composite is OBSERVED if most of its inputs are OBSERVED); weighted-confidence blending. WHY THIS: a
conjunctive claim is only as strong as its weakest support (the same logic underlying the geometric-mean
decision, D-2, applied to epistemics rather than arithmetic); majority-rule would let a composite with one
CONTESTED input and four OBSERVED inputs read as confidently OBSERVED, precisely the false-confidence
failure Part XII.5 identifies as this dataset's most consequential risk. TRADE-OFFS: the ceiling rule is
conservative — it can hold an otherwise-strong composite at HYPOTHESIZED because of a single thin
component, which may undercount genuinely well-evidenced systems that happen to have one newly-added
factor. REVERSAL CONDITION: if Part XIX.5's program shows the ceiling rule systematically suppresses
legitimate CURRENT scores across a majority of ensemble systems (rather than the intended minority of
genuinely thin cases), the rule's strictness should be revisited — but not before real measurement history
exists to make that judgment on evidence rather than convenience.

D-5. DECISION: Defer NICG's complexity/maintenance cost terms to per-transition telemetry (hook trips,
gate failures, new-system counts) rather than a hand-estimated "cost of change" figure. ALTERNATIVES
CONSIDERED: a fixed per-new-system cost constant; an Owner-estimated cost figure per transition. WHY THIS:
per Part IV.4's Maintenance-factor design, the estate already logs the signals needed (governance hook
trips, BLOCKED_DELIVERY incidence) — reusing existing telemetry avoids building a parallel cost-tracking
system, honoring the Rosetta principle of Part I.6 and Part 2.5's "minimum vocabulary" discipline.
TRADE-OFFS: telemetry-derived cost terms are only as good as the telemetry's own coverage, and a
transition involving a system class the governance layer does not yet instrument (a brand-new capability
type) could under-charge its true cost. REVERSAL CONDITION: if a transition is later found to have
under-charged NICG due to an instrumentation gap, the gap should be closed at the telemetry layer (a new
governance hook) rather than patched with a hand-estimated NICG adjustment, preserving the principle that
NICG measures what is actually logged, not what is believed.

five-slot profile in a form that structurally resists numeric collapse (named qualitative bands, not a 1-5
integer scale that an implementer could average without noticing the violation).

NORMATIVE one without ever running the falsification program Part XIX.5 specifies. PREVENTION: k's
EXPERIMENTAL status must be carried in any registry or schema that stores it (Part XXIII), with a mandatory
review trigger tied to ensemble-state count (per Part XIX.5's condition (b): at least two real transitions
before any recalibration is even attempted) rather than a one-time value frozen at first deployment.
