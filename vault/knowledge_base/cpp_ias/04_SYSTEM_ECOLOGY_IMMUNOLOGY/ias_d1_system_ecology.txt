===============================================================================================
PART I — THE ECOLOGY OBJECT: WHY NEITHER D2A NOR EVOLUTION_ENGINE ALREADY OWNS IT
===============================================================================================

1.1 The founding observation. The Claude Power Pack estate, as OBSERVED across the Non-
Duplication Ledger and the Compounding Graph, already contains two systems that touch the
territory this dataset claims. The Duplicate-to-Advantage engine (D2A) renders a verdict —
BUILD, MERGE-INTO-FAMILY, SUBENGINE, EXTEND, COMPOSITION, REFERENCE, or REJECT — every time one
proposed capability overlaps one existing system; it is, by the object model IAS-F1 supplies,
a system whose object is a single DUPLICATES relation between two named systems, resolved once,
at the moment of proposal. The evolution_engine executes a single MERGE operation between two
systems once a merger decision has already been made; its object is one fusion event, bounded in
time, bounded to the two systems named in the operation. Both are real systems, both are
strengthened by this dataset, and neither is duplicated by it, because both are EVENT-SCOPED:
each fires once, resolves one pair, and returns to quiescence until the next proposal or the
next merger decision arrives. (OBSERVED: the ledger's own row for candidate 31 states the
overlap precisely — "D2A (per event), evolution_engine" — and states the unique object as
"cooperation/dependency/competition of all systems," which is the ensemble-level, standing
object this Part formalizes.)

1.2 What "ongoing, population-level" means, made precise. An ECOLOGY, as DEFINED in this
dataset, is the standing model of how the ensemble's systems, taken as a POPULATION rather than
as a sequence of pairwise events, cooperate, compete, depend on one another, occupy niches, rise,
fall, and replace one another OVER TIME. The defining contrast with D2A and evolution_engine is
not depth or sophistication; it is TEMPORAL AND POPULATION SCOPE. D2A asks "does proposed system
X duplicate existing system Y?" — a question about two systems, answered once. The ecology asks
"given the full population of systems and their typed relations (feeds, depends-on, competes-
with, duplicates, strengthens, consumes, per IAS-F1 Part VI), which regions of the relation graph
are thriving, which are parasitic, which niches sit vacant, and how does this configuration
change as missions, environments, and the systems themselves evolve?" — a question about the
WHOLE population, answered continuously, never once and done. (DERIVED: this is exactly the
resource-level-versus-ensemble-level distinction of IAS-F1 Part I §1.4 and the formal level test
of IAS-F1 Part VIII, applied a second time — not to a single system's object, but to the
TEMPORAL GRAIN at which an object is evaluated. D2A and evolution_engine are ensemble-level in
IAS-F1's sense — they act on systems, not on resources — but they are EVENT-GRAIN within the
ensemble level, while the ecology is POPULATION-GRAIN. The federation ontology did not
distinguish grain within the ensemble level because F1's job was the resource/ensemble boundary,
not the finer distinction this dataset now draws.)

1.3 The formal boundary test, stated as a question any future proposal can be run through. A
candidate mechanism is ECOLOGY-LEVEL, and therefore this dataset's object rather than D2A's or
evolution_engine's, if and only if its honest answer to "what does this act on?" names a
PATTERN ACROSS THE POPULATION persisting or changing across MULTIPLE evaluation cycles — a
fitness trend, a symbiotic cluster, a niche vacancy, a biodiversity index — rather than a single
named pair of systems resolved at a single decision point. A mechanism that decides "should
System A merge into System B, right now, given today's evidence" is event-grain and belongs to
evolution_engine's execution or D2A's verdict layer. A mechanism that decides "which regions of
the ensemble are exhibiting parasitic dynamics THIS QUARTER, as a standing, recomputed model" is
population-grain and belongs to the ecology. (NORMATIVE: this test is the ecology's own
non-duplication gate, applied inward to every sub-mechanism this dataset itself proposes across
Parts V through XXII — a symbiosis DETECTOR is ecology-level because it maintains a standing
model across the population; a symbiosis ACTIVATION, once detected, that wires two specific
systems together is event-grain and is, correctly, handed to evolution_engine or D2A to execute,
never re-implemented here.)

1.4 Why the ecology needs D2A and evolution_engine to remain event-scoped, not the reverse. It
would be a category error, and a violation of Article III's "strengthen, never replace," for the
ecology to attempt to absorb D2A's verdict logic or evolution_engine's merge mechanics into a
single population-level system. The ecology's standing models — fitness landscapes, symbiosis
maps, niche vacancies, biodiversity indices — are precisely what makes D2A's per-event verdicts
and evolution_engine's per-merge executions BETTER INFORMED without replacing either: a D2A
verdict rendered with the ecology's fitness-landscape context in hand is a better verdict than
one rendered blind to the population; an evolution_engine merge executed after the ecology's
merger-intelligence sub-mechanism (Part XIII) has confirmed the candidate pair are genuinely
complementary rather than merely adjacent is a safer merge than one triggered by proposal alone.
(DERIVED: this is the STRENGTHENS relation of IAS-F1 Part VI §6.6 applied twice — the ecology
strengthens D2A by supplying population context to a per-event decision, and strengthens
evolution_engine by supplying a standing recommendation queue a per-event executor consumes
rather than generates. Neither existing system's authority is touched; D2A retains final
authority over the duplicates verdict, per Invariant Three of IAS-F1 Part V, and evolution_engine
retains sole execution rights over the merge operation itself.)

1.5 The ecology's own scope boundary: what it does NOT model. Symmetrically, the ecology does
not model a single mission's routing (IAS-A1's object), a single decision's provenance (DRK's
object), or the ensemble's cost allocation across a budget cycle (IAS-C1's object) — each of
those is a distinct facet of the ensemble already claimed by a named sibling or parent, and the
ecology's population-dynamics lens reads their outputs as INPUTS to fitness (Part III) rather
than recomputing them. (NORMATIVE: the ecology is a lens over the SAME typed relation graph
IAS-F1 Part VI defines, not a new graph; it adds no new relation types, only a set of PATTERNS
computed over the existing six — feeds, depends-on, competes-with, duplicates, strengthens,
consumes — read across the population and across time. This is the specific, minimal sense in
which the ecology is a second-order capability under IAS-F1 Part VII §7.2: its direct object is
not a resource and not a single system, but the RELATIONAL BEHAVIOR of the whole population of
systems, which is itself a set of capabilities considered together.)

1.6 Why this dataset must exist even though its raw material — the relation graph — already
exists in F1. A graph with edges is not the same object as an understanding of that graph's
population dynamics, precisely as a census with names and addresses is not the same object as
an epidemiologist's model of how a population's health changes over time. F1 supplies the
census; this dataset supplies the epidemiology. Without it, the estate can, at best, enumerate
its systems and their pairwise relations (F1's own scope, honestly bounded at Part III §3.4);
it cannot answer "is the ensemble getting healthier or sicker," "which capability is quietly
starving three consumers to feed one," or "where has a niche sat vacant for six evaluation
cycles because nothing has been built to fill it." Those questions require a standing,
recomputed, population-scoped model — exactly what D2A and evolution_engine, by design, do not
maintain, because maintaining one is not their object. (OBSERVED: the ledger's evidence
requirement for IAS-D1 is explicit and checkable — "an ecology map naming ≥1 parasite + ≥1
symbiosis with evidence" — a requirement that presupposes a standing map exists to be queried,
not a one-off verdict to be re-derived each time the question is asked.)

FINAL LAW — PART I. D2A resolves one duplication and evolution_engine executes one merger; the
ecology is neither — it is the standing, population-grain model of how the whole federation's
systems cooperate, compete, and change over time, and that temporal-population grain, not any
new relation type or any new authority, is the entire distinct object this dataset claims.

===============================================================================================
PART II — ECOLOGICAL PRIMITIVES: POPULATION, ENVIRONMENT, NICHE, FITNESS
===============================================================================================

2.1 Why a second primitive layer, built on top of F1's. IAS-F1 Part II fixed four primitives —
system, subsystem, engine, capability — deliberately minimal, because a larger primitive set
would pre-judge the mechanisms later datasets were meant to design. This dataset is one of the
datasets F1 anticipated: it needs four further primitives, each DERIVED from F1's own objects
rather than invented independently, because an ecology cannot be reasoned about using only
"system" and "capability" any more than a biologist can reason about population health using
only "organism" and "trait." F1 explicitly reserves this expansion (Part II §2.5: "the four
primitives compose upward into the ensemble object and outward into the relation model;
everything else the family needs is built from them") — this Part is that composition, performed
once, for the ecology family specifically.

2.2 POPULATION, defined. A POPULATION, DEFINED, is the complete set of systems (IAS-F1 Part II
§2.1) that are ADMITTED (a lifecycle state IAS-F1 Part XI defines) at a given evaluation cycle,
together with the typed relation graph (IAS-F1 Part VI) among them, restricted to a chosen
SCOPE — the whole estate, or a named sub-federation such as "every system that touches mission
routing," or "every system admitted in the last two evolutionary cycles." A population is, in
other words, the ensemble object of IAS-F1 Part III, but explicitly PARAMETERIZED by scope and
by evaluation cycle, because the ecology never reasons about "the ensemble" as a timeless
totality — it reasons about a specific population, at a specific moment, because fitness,
symbiosis, and niche vacancy are all properties that only make sense relative to a bounded,
dated population. (DERIVED: without this parameterization, every ecological claim would be
implicitly about "all systems, forever," which is precisely the kind of unfalsifiable,
un-dated claim Article V's evidence discipline forbids.)

2.3 ENVIRONMENT, defined. An ENVIRONMENT, DEFINED, is a named context of demand and constraint
under which systems operate and are evaluated — most commonly a MISSION-TYPE (the recurring
shape of work a system is asked to do: a research mission, a build mission, a governance-audit
mission) but also, where relevant, a resource regime (low-budget versus high-budget cycles) or
an operational mode (autonomous versus Owner-supervised execution). The environment is the
independent variable against which fitness (§2.5) is measured, and its introduction is what
prevents the single most common ecological measurement error: scoring a system's fitness as one
global number that silently averages its performance across incompatible contexts, hiding the
fact that a system may be excellent in one environment and near-useless in another. (NORMATIVE:
IAS-F1's property model, Part IV, already warns against a single blended evidence level for a
multi-field record; this dataset applies the identical caution to fitness, refusing a single
blended fitness score in favor of the per-environment landscape formalized in Part III.)

2.4 NICHE, defined. A NICHE, DEFINED, is the specific combination of environment and capability-
demand that a system's capability signature (IAS-F1 Part IX §9.2) is suited to fill — the
narrowest honest description of "what does this system do, for whom, under what conditions, that
justifies its continued existence." Two systems occupy the SAME niche if their capability
signatures address the same environment-demand combination with substitutable outputs; two
systems occupy OVERLAPPING niches if they partially address the same combination; and a niche is
VACANT if an environment's demand has no system whose capability signature addresses it at all.
Niche is the concept that makes "duplication" (IAS-F1 Invariant Two) and "healthy redundancy"
(a competes-with relation the ecology may judge favorably, Part XVIII) distinguishable without
re-litigating D2A's verdict machinery: two systems in the SAME niche are candidates for D2A's
duplicates disposition; two systems in OVERLAPPING but not identical niches are candidates for
the ecology's own speciation or merger judgment (Parts XI-XIV), a distinction D2A's binary
duplicates test does not itself draw, because D2A's object is a single pairwise verdict, not a
population-wide niche map.

2.5 FITNESS, defined provisionally (full treatment, Part III). FITNESS, DEFINED here at the
primitive level and elaborated operationally in Part III, is a system's demonstrated capacity to
fill its niche relative to the cost the ensemble pays to sustain it, measured PER ENVIRONMENT
rather than as a single global figure. Fitness is not a synonym for "quality" in the abstract; a
system can be exceptionally well-built and still be UNFIT if its niche has gone vacant of demand,
or FIT-BUT-EXPENSIVE if it fills real demand at a cost the portfolio (IAS-C1) cannot sustain
relative to alternatives. Fitness is, in this sense, always a RELATION between a system, an
environment, and the population's other occupants of adjacent niches — never a property a system
holds in isolation, which is the precise reason fitness belongs to the ecology rather than to any
single system's own self-report.

2.6 The four primitives composed: a worked illustration. Consider a synthetic, illustrative
population: three systems, named generically as System M, System N, and System P, evaluated
across two environments, "routine governance missions" and "novel architecture missions." System
M's capability signature addresses routine governance missions narrowly and well; its niche in
that environment is well-defined and mostly vacant of competitors, so its fitness there is high.
The same System M, asked to serve a novel architecture mission, has no capability signature
suited to that environment at all — not because it performs poorly, but because the environment
lies outside its niche entirely, and its fitness figure for that environment is UNDEFINED rather
than low, a distinction Part III §3.6 treats as load-bearing. System N's capability signature
overlaps System M's in the governance environment (a competes-with relation, IAS-F1 Part VI
§6.4) while also extending into the architecture environment where System M has no presence at
all — System N therefore occupies a BROADER niche than M, and its fitness landscape (Part III)
shows two populated points where M's shows one. System P duplicates System N's governance-
environment niche exactly, with no differentiation and no extension into the architecture
environment — P and N are, under §2.4's same-niche test, candidates for D2A's duplicates
disposition, a judgment the ecology surfaces as evidence (Part V §5.4's worked symbiosis-versus-
duplication triage) but does not itself render, preserving D2A's Invariant-Three-protected
authority.

2.7 Why niche and environment together prevent the single most damaging ecological error. A
population model that scored fitness without reference to environment would inevitably rank
generalist systems above specialist systems whenever the generalist's aggregate footprint looked
larger, even when the specialist is the ONLY system serving a narrow, critical niche no
generalist reaches as well. This is the precise failure mode the biodiversity governor (Parts
XVIII-XIX) exists to prevent at the population-policy level, but the failure is prevented at the
DEFINITIONAL level, here, by refusing to define fitness except as a per-environment quantity.
(HYPOTHESIZED: a global, environment-blind fitness score would systematically undervalue narrow
specialists relative to broad generalists in direct proportion to how many environments the
generalist's footprint touches, independent of the generalist's actual per-environment quality —
this specific claim is HYPOTHESIZED because it has not yet been checked against the real estate's
population, and Part XXV names the metric that would confirm or refute it.)

2.8 A fifth, load-bearing distinction: EVALUATION CYCLE as the population's own clock. Every
primitive defined above is implicitly parameterized by an EVALUATION CYCLE, DEFINED here
explicitly because Parts III onward depend on it without further ceremony: a bounded interval —
never fixed numerically by this dataset, per Article VIII's restraint on operational parameters —
across which the population's fitness landscape, relation graph, and ledger are recomputed as one
consistent snapshot. The cycle is the ecology's own unit of time, distinct from a mission's
duration (many missions execute within one cycle) and distinct from a system's own lifecycle
state transitions (IAS-F1 Part XI), which can occur mid-cycle and are simply picked up at the next
recomputation. Without a named cycle concept, every "sustained trend," "multiple consecutive
cycles," and "rolling window" claim this dataset makes from Part III forward would be referencing
an undefined unit — the same discipline IAS-F1 Part III §3.3 applied to ensemble STATE, now applied
to the ecology's own temporal grain specifically.

2.9 Why population, environment, and niche must be evaluated at the SAME cycle boundary, never
skewed. A subtle but consequential requirement: a population snapshot, an environment's demand
reading, and a niche's occupancy determination must all be computed from data as of the SAME
evaluation cycle, never from a population snapshot at cycle N compared against an environment
demand reading collected at cycle N-1. (DERIVED: a skewed comparison — the specific failure this
sub-section exists to forbid — would silently misattribute a system admitted between the two
readings as already having served demand it never had the chance to serve, or would misattribute a
retired system's absence as a niche vacancy when the vacancy is an artifact of the skew rather
than a real gap. Every mechanism this dataset specifies from Part III forward inherits this
same-cycle-boundary requirement implicitly; this sub-section names it once, here, rather than
re-deriving it at each mechanism's own interface section.)

FINAL LAW — PART II. Population, environment, niche, and fitness are the four concepts an
ecology needs beyond F1's system and capability, and none is invented independently — each is
derived by composing F1's existing objects with the one dimension F1 deliberately left open:
standing behavior across a bounded population, over time, per environment, all read from one
consistent evaluation-cycle boundary.

===============================================================================================
PART III — THE SYSTEM FITNESS LANDSCAPE: FITNESS PER ENVIRONMENT, NEVER ONE GLOBAL NUMBER
===============================================================================================

3.1 The landscape, defined. A FITNESS LANDSCAPE, DEFINED, is the complete mapping, for a given
population and evaluation cycle, from (system, environment) pairs to a fitness value, together
with the confidence and evidence level of each value per Article V. The landscape is called a
"landscape" rather than a "table" deliberately: read across environments for one system, it
shows where that system is strong, weak, or absent, exactly as a topographic landscape shows
peaks and valleys; read across systems for one environment, it shows which systems compete for
that niche and by how much. Neither reading alone is sufficient for the decisions the later Parts
build on it — Part XI's speciation judgment needs the per-system reading (is this system's
landscape bimodal, suggesting it is really two systems wearing one capability signature?); Part
XVIII's biodiversity judgment needs the per-environment reading (does this environment have one
dominant peak or several comparable ones?).

3.2 The fitness formula's components, named without over-specifying arithmetic. F1 Article VIII
forbids production code and implementation-level specification this mission; this dataset
therefore names fitness's INPUT COMPONENTS as a specification rather than a formula with fixed
weights, because fixed weights would be a design decision this dataset is not positioned to make
without empirical calibration against the real estate (Part XVI names this the calibration gap
explicitly). Fitness, per (system, environment), is DERIVED from four components already present
in IAS-F1's property model (Part IV) and relation graph (Part VI), read at environment-scoped
granularity: DEMAND SATISFACTION (the observed rate at which the system's output, when consumed
within this environment, is accepted rather than overridden or escalated — a signal the Control
Bus, IAS-A2, already carries as event data); COST-PER-UNIT-VALUE (the system's cost property,
IAS-F1 Part IV §4.2, apportioned to this environment's share of the system's total consumption,
divided by the demand-satisfaction figure); RELATIONAL POSITION (the system's count of inbound
feeds and strengthens edges from other systems ACTIVE in this environment, as a proxy for how
load-bearing the system has become within this niche); and EVIDENCE LEVEL (per Article V, a
fitness value resting on OBSERVED inputs is a different institutional bet than one resting on
HYPOTHESIZED inputs, and the landscape must carry this distinction forward rather than collapsing
it into a bare number).

3.3 Why fitness is not simply "usage count." The most tempting, and most wrong, simplification
of fitness is to equate it with raw invocation frequency — how often a system is consumed. A
system consumed constantly because it sits on the only path to a mandatory governance gate (a
depends-on relation, IAS-F1 Part VI §6.3, not a chosen one) can show enormous usage while
providing mediocre demand satisfaction, because consumers have no alternative, not because the
system serves them well. (DERIVED: raw usage confuses CONSUMPTION with SATISFACTION, exactly the
distinction demand-satisfaction in §3.2 is built to preserve; a landscape that used usage-count
alone would systematically overrate mandatory-path systems and underrate optional, elective
systems that consumers seek out specifically because they perform well — an inversion of the
signal fitness is meant to carry.) This is also why RELATIONAL POSITION is measured by feeds and
strengthens edges specifically, not by depends-on edges: a system many others structurally must
route through is not thereby fit; a system many others voluntarily build value on top of is
closer to the signal fitness needs.

3.4 The landscape's shape as diagnostic information, independent of any single value. Beyond the
individual (system, environment) fitness values, the SHAPE of a system's row across the
landscape — how many environments it registers a defined value in, how uniform or bimodal those
values are — is itself diagnostic, and three shapes recur often enough to name. A NARROW-PEAK
shape (one environment with high fitness, all others UNDEFINED) describes a well-fitted
specialist; this shape is not a defect, and the biodiversity governor (Part XVIII) exists partly
to protect it from being penalized for narrowness alone. A BROAD-PLATEAU shape (moderate,
comparable fitness across many environments, no standout peak) describes a generalist whose
value is breadth rather than depth, and whose extinction risk (Part XV) is different in kind from
a specialist's — a generalist's loss degrades many environments a little, while a specialist's
loss eliminates one environment's coverage entirely. A BIMODAL shape (high fitness in two
disjoint environment clusters, near-zero fitness in between) is the landscape's own signal that
a system may, in fact, be two systems' worth of capability wearing one capability signature — the
precise pattern Part XI's speciation judgment is built to detect and act on, using the landscape
as its primary evidence source rather than inventing a separate detector.

3.5 Worked example: three landscape shapes read side by side. Returning to the synthetic Systems
M, N, and P of Part II §2.6, their landscapes illustrate the three shapes directly. System M's
landscape is a clean narrow peak: high, well-evidenced fitness in the governance environment,
UNDEFINED (not zero — genuinely absent capability signature, per §3.6) in the architecture
environment. System N's landscape is broad-plateau-leaning-bimodal: moderate fitness in
governance (suppressed somewhat by competition with M and P in the same niche, per §3.6's
competition effect) and moderate, independently-evidenced fitness in architecture, where N has no
competitor at all — a shape that, on inspection, is not truly bimodal in the speciation sense,
because the two peaks are not disjoint in provenance (the same underlying capability handles
both, just at different demand levels), a distinction Part XI §11.4 elaborates as the difference
between a genuinely divergent capability and a merely uneven one. System P's landscape duplicates
M's shape almost exactly, in the same environment, at a comparable fitness value — the landscape
itself is the primary evidence a duplicates relation (IAS-F1 Invariant Two) may be live between
M and P, surfaced here as an ecological observation for D2A to adjudicate, not as an ecology-
rendered verdict.

3.6 Competition effects and the UNDEFINED-versus-zero distinction. Two refinements matter for
the landscape's honesty. First, fitness in an environment where a system holds a competes-with
relation (IAS-F1 Part VI §6.4) to another system active in the same niche is measured
NET of that competition — demand satisfaction is computed against the share of the environment's
total demand this system actually captured, not against a hypothetical world where it served the
environment alone — so that a landscape correctly shows two comparably fit competitors as two
moderate peaks rather than one artificially depressed peak and one artificially inflated one.
Second, and critically, a system with no presence in an environment at all is recorded as
UNDEFINED, never as zero fitness: zero would imply the system was tried and failed, while
UNDEFINED honestly states the system was never evaluated there at all, and conflating the two —
the exact error Part II §2.6 flagged for System M in the architecture environment — would corrupt
every downstream niche-vacancy judgment (Part IX) by making every unattempted environment look
like a graveyard of failed attempts rather than the honestly unexplored territory it is.

3.7 The landscape's own staleness discipline. Following IAS-F1 Part IV §4.3's precedent exactly,
every (system, environment) fitness value carries a last-reconciled marker; a landscape is not
computed once and cached indefinitely, because environments themselves drift (a mission-type's
demand shape changes as the estate's own priorities change) and a stale landscape misleads every
mechanism built on it with the same silent confidence a stale property record misleads IAS-C1's
portfolio math. (NORMATIVE: the ecology does not itself mandate a reconciliation cadence — that
operational decision, per F1's own precedent, belongs to whichever mechanism implements this
specification — but the landscape's schema must be able to represent "this fitness value has not
been reconciled in N cycles" as a first-class, visible fact.)

FINAL LAW — PART III. Fitness is never one number; it is a landscape of values, one per
(system, environment) pair, each carrying its own evidence level and staleness, and the
landscape's SHAPE — narrow peak, broad plateau, or bimodal — is diagnostic information the later
ecological mechanisms read directly, rather than a decoration on top of a single fitness score.

===============================================================================================
PART IV — FITNESS COMPUTATION IN PRACTICE: INPUTS, AGGREGATION, AND THE CALIBRATION GAP
===============================================================================================

4.1 From specification to computable procedure, without crossing into implementation. Part III
named fitness's four input components; this Part specifies how those components combine into a
usable landscape value, at the level of a computable PROCEDURE rather than an implemented
formula, honoring Article VIII's boundary while still giving a future implementer enough to build
from without further design decisions of consequence. The procedure, DEFINED, has three stages:
COLLECTION (gather demand-satisfaction, cost-per-unit-value, and relational-position readings for
the (system, environment) pair from their respective source systems — IAS-A2's event log for
satisfaction, IAS-F1's property record for cost, the typed relation graph for position), NORMAL-
IZATION (each of the three readings is expressed on a comparable scale relative to the OTHER
systems active in the same environment, never on an absolute scale, because fitness is
inherently a relative, within-environment concept per Part III §3.1), and COMPOSITION (the three
normalized readings are combined into a single per-environment fitness value, carrying forward
the LOWEST evidence level among its three inputs per Article V's discipline that a claim is only
as strong as its weakest supporting fact).

4.2 Why normalization is within-environment, never cross-environment. A cost-per-unit-value
reading of "moderate" in a low-budget governance environment and a cost-per-unit-value reading
of "moderate" in a high-budget architecture environment are not the same fact, because the
environments themselves define different baselines for what "moderate" cost means. Normalizing
within-environment — comparing System M's governance-environment cost only against System N's
and System P's governance-environment cost, never against costs incurred in a different
environment — is what prevents the landscape from silently importing one environment's cost
structure into another's fitness judgment, a specific version of the cross-environment
contamination Part III §3.6 already warned against for competition effects, now applied to the
cost component directly.

4.3 The calibration gap, named honestly. The composition stage's most consequential open
question — how much weight demand-satisfaction should carry relative to cost-per-unit-value and
relational-position — is NOT resolved by this dataset, and that non-resolution is deliberate
rather than an oversight. A fixed weighting scheme asserted without empirical calibration against
the real estate's actual fitness outcomes would be a HYPOTHESIZED formula dressed as a DEFINED
one, the exact category error Article V exists to prevent. (EXPERIMENTAL: the composition
weighting is EXPERIMENTAL, carrying its own test — Part XVI's Protocol Two specifies a
retrodiction check against the estate's own history of systems that were later confirmed fit or
unfit by independent evidence, such as a D2A REJECT verdict against a system the landscape had
already flagged as bimodal-and-shrinking, or a successful merger, Part XIII, between two systems
the landscape had flagged as complementary. Until that retrodiction check runs, any specific
weighting is a proposal, not a finding.)

4.4 A worked composition, illustrative only, showing the procedure without asserting fixed
weights. Consider System N from the running example, evaluated in the architecture environment
where it holds no direct competitor. Collection yields: demand-satisfaction HIGH (its output is
rarely overridden when consumed in this environment, per the Control Bus event log), cost-per-
unit-value MODERATE (it consumes a non-trivial token share to serve this environment, but no
comparably-fit alternative exists to normalize against, so its within-environment cost rank is
effectively first-and-only), relational-position LOW (few other systems yet feed from or
strengthen because of N's architecture-environment output, because the environment itself is
young). Composition, applying the lowest-evidence-level discipline, yields a fitness value that
is directionally HIGH but whose evidence level is capped at DERIVED rather than OBSERVED, because
the relational-position input — LOW, but based on a young, thin sample — has not yet accumulated
enough independent confirmation to license an OBSERVED-level composite. This worked example
demonstrates the procedure's key discipline directly: a fitness value can be directionally
confident while still being evidentially cautious, and the landscape is required to carry both
facts rather than collapsing them into a single reassuring number.

4.5 Aggregate ensemble fitness: a derived, not primitive, quantity. Some downstream mechanisms
— most notably IAS-C1's portfolio math and IAS-E1's observability sweep — will want a single
figure summarizing "how fit is the ensemble overall," and this dataset supplies that figure as an
explicitly DERIVED aggregate, never as an independently measured primitive: ensemble fitness is
the population-weighted composition of the full landscape, read across all (system, environment)
pairs with defined values, EXCLUDING UNDEFINED cells (per §3.6's discipline against treating
absence as failure) and explicitly flagging the count of UNDEFINED cells as a companion figure,
because a rising UNDEFINED count across the population is itself a signal — either of niche
vacancy (Part IX, healthy, expected) or of measurement failure (Part XXIII's failure-mode
catalogue, unhealthy, requiring attention) — that a single blended aggregate would hide entirely.

4.6 Interfaces: what the landscape reads and what it exposes. Consuming from elsewhere in the
estate, the landscape reads: IAS-F1's property records (cost, per Part IV §4.2), IAS-F1's typed
relation graph (feeds and strengthens edges, per Part VI), and IAS-A2's Control Bus event stream
(demand-satisfaction signals, per the Compounding Graph's own "IAS-A2 carries failure events...
IAS-E1 observes... IAS-C2 demand signal" pattern, generalized here to satisfaction events rather
than only failure events). Exposing outward, the landscape produces one artifact — the per-
(system, environment) fitness table with evidence levels and staleness markers — consumed
directly by Part IX (niche vacancy reads UNDEFINED cells), Part XI (speciation reads bimodal
shapes), Part XV (extinction reads sustained low-fitness trends), Part XVIII (biodiversity reads
per-environment peak concentration), and, per the Strengthening Matrix and the Compounding Graph,
by IAS-G1 (Architecture Intelligence, which converts fitness into topology decisions, per Part
XXVI's integration treatment) and IAS-C1 (Capability Portfolio, which converts fitness into
retire-versus-reinvest allocation, also Part XXVI).

4.7 Why the ecology does not itself decide anything from the landscape. Consistent with the
event-grain-versus-population-grain boundary of Part I, the landscape is the ecology's core
PRODUCT, not its final action: the landscape does not, on its own, retire a system, trigger a
merger, or reallocate a budget. Those actions belong to D2A, evolution_engine, and IAS-C1
respectively, each consuming the landscape as an input to a decision that remains theirs to make
under their own authority (IAS-F1 Part V Invariant Three). The ecology's discipline here mirrors
F1's own restraint at Part I §1.2 exactly: a foundation that supplies a shared fact base without
seizing the authority that fact base informs is what "strengthens, never replaces" means in
concrete mechanism rather than slogan.

4.8 A fourth input candidate considered and explicitly excluded: SELF-REPORTED CONFIDENCE. During
specification, a fourth component was considered for the composition procedure — a system's own
self-reported confidence in its fitness for a given environment, analogous to a maintainer's
subjective assessment. This dataset excludes it deliberately: self-reported confidence is neither
OBSERVED (it is not measured against outcomes) nor DERIVED (it does not follow from other
OBSERVED facts) — it is, at best, the reporting system's own HYPOTHESIS about itself, and
Article V's discipline forbids treating a system's self-assessment as evidence of its own fitness
without independent corroboration, the same discipline that makes a maintaining agent's narrative
summary inadmissible as the calibration record's source of truth (Part XXIV §24.5). Self-reported
confidence remains permissible as METADATA attached to a landscape cell — useful context for a
human reviewer — but is explicitly barred from the composition procedure itself.

4.9 Interface with the succession tracker: fitness deltas as the tracker's raw material. Although
Part XVI specifies the succession tracker as its own mechanism, its dependency on this Part's
output deserves naming here, at the fitness-computation layer, because the tracker consumes not
the landscape's raw values but their CYCLE-OVER-CYCLE DELTA — the signed change in a
(system, environment) fitness value between consecutive evaluation cycles (Part II §2.8's cycle
concept). A delta computation inherits the composition procedure's own lowest-evidence-level
discipline doubly: a delta between two DERIVED-level readings is itself, at best, DERIVED, and a
delta spanning a cycle boundary where one reading is UNDEFINED (Part III §3.6) is itself UNDEFINED
rather than computable as a numeric change — a system entering or leaving a niche produces an
undefined delta, not a delta of arbitrarily large magnitude, which is the specific guard that
prevents a newly-admitted system's first cycle from registering as a spuriously enormous fitness
gain in the succession tracker's own trend calculation.

FINAL LAW — PART IV. Fitness is computed from three within-environment-normalized components —
demand satisfaction, cost-per-unit-value, relational position, explicitly excluding self-reported
confidence — carried at the lowest evidence level any component supports; the composition
weighting is EXPERIMENTAL pending retrodiction, and the landscape it produces is a shared fact base
other systems decide from, never a decision the ecology renders itself.

===============================================================================================
PART V — SYMBIOSIS: THE TAXONOMY OF MUTUALLY-AMPLIFYING COMBINATIONS
===============================================================================================

5.1 Symbiosis, defined against the strengthens relation it specializes. IAS-F1 Part VI §6.6
defines STRENGTHENS as the relation type where System A makes System B produce more value,
decide better, or cost less because A exists. SYMBIOSIS, DEFINED in this dataset, is the
POPULATION-LEVEL PATTERN that emerges when strengthens edges form a mutually-reinforcing
cluster — most simply, a pair or small group of systems that strengthen ONE ANOTHER, such that
the cluster's combined fitness (Part III) across its shared environments exceeds the sum of what
each member's fitness would be in that environment absent the others. Symbiosis is therefore not
a new relation type — F1's six types remain closed, per IAS-F1 Part VI §6.1's explicit design
intent — it is a PATTERN over existing strengthens (and, in weaker forms, feeds) edges, detected
and named at the population level, which is precisely the kind of object the ecology exists to
add without inflating the relation graph itself.

5.2 The three symbiotic shapes, and why only two are load-bearing for this dataset's mechanisms.
Borrowing the ecology's own vocabulary deliberately, three shapes recur: MUTUALISM (bidirectional
strengthens edges — A strengthens B and B strengthens A, both gaining), COMMENSALISM
(unidirectional strengthens or feeds — A strengthens B, B does not reciprocally strengthen A, but
B's gain costs A nothing), and the negative case, PARASITISM, which Part VII treats as its own
Part because it requires a distinct detection mechanism rather than a mere relation-graph
pattern. Of the two positive shapes, MUTUALISM is the higher-value pattern this dataset's
detection mechanism (§5.4) prioritizes, because a mutualistic cluster is, by IAS-F1's Strengthening
Matrix logic, evidence that BOTH members are strengthened simultaneously — a two-for-one
compounding effect the Compounding Graph's "amplification spine" section already gestures at in
the abstract ("strengthens the whole estate's knowledge... which raises the baseline every other
IAS system reasons from") without naming the detection mechanism this dataset now supplies.
COMMENSALISM is real and worth recording, but carries lower priority for active intervention,
because a commensal relationship, by definition, has no cost side requiring correction — it is
already healthy and stable without governance attention.

5.3 Dormant versus active symbiosis: the detection mechanism's actual job. The single most
valuable output of this Part is not naming ALREADY-ACTIVE mutualistic clusters — those are
visible directly in the strengthens subgraph and require no special detection — but surfacing
DORMANT symbiosis: pairs of systems whose capability signatures (IAS-F1 Part IX §9.2) are
COMPLEMENTARY (System A's outputs are of a type System B's inputs could consume, and vice versa)
but between which NO strengthens or feeds edge currently exists, meaning the mutualistic gain is
POSSIBLE but not yet realized. Dormant symbiosis detection, DEFINED, is a search over the
population for (system, system) pairs meeting three conditions: complementary capability
signatures per the above, no existing edge of any type between them, and non-overlapping or only
partially-overlapping niches (per Part II §2.4 — two systems in the exact same niche are
duplication candidates for D2A, not symbiosis candidates, a distinction the triage in §5.4 makes
explicit).

5.4 Worked example: dormant symbiosis surfaced and triaged against the duplication boundary. Two
synthetic systems, illustrative only: System Q, whose capability signature is "detect a recurring
failure pattern across sessions," and System R, whose capability signature is "convert a
recurring pattern into a standing governance rule." Q's output (a detected pattern) is exactly
the type of input R's capability consumes; R's output (a governance rule) is, in turn, exactly
the type of signal that would let Q refine its own detection thresholds — a genuinely
bidirectional complementarity. At the moment of evaluation, no feeds or strengthens edge exists
between Q and R; each operates independently, Q surfacing patterns nowhere they are consumed by
rule-formation, R waiting on manually-surfaced patterns from elsewhere in the estate. The
ecology's detection mechanism flags this pair as DORMANT MUTUALISM, distinct from a duplication
flag, precisely because Q and R's niches are complementary rather than identical — Q detects, R
formalizes, and neither substitutes for the other. This flag is the ecology's product; the
ACTUAL WIRING of a feeds edge between Q and R — the event-grain action of connecting them — is
handed off to whichever mechanism implements the wiring (a specification-level candidate named
in the Dataset-to-Runtime Translation section), never executed by the ecology itself, consistent
with Part I §1.4's restraint.

5.5 Why symbiosis detection requires the complementarity test, not merely proximity. A naive
detector might flag any two systems that CONSUME similar upstream data as symbiosis candidates,
on the theory that "they're already adjacent." This is wrong, and is, in fact, the COMPETES-WITH
pattern (IAS-F1 Part VI §6.4) misread as symbiosis: two systems drawing on the same upstream
resource are more likely to be RIVALS for that resource than complements to one another, and
wiring a feeds edge between rivals does not create mutualism, it creates a new dependency without
new value. (DERIVED: the complementarity test — output-type of A matches input-type of B, AND
output-type of B matches input-type of A for mutualism, or the weaker one-directional match for
commensalism — is what distinguishes genuine dormant symbiosis from mere topological proximity,
and any detection mechanism that skips this test in favor of a cheaper proximity heuristic will
systematically over-recommend wiring between systems that would produce redundancy, not
amplification, an error Part XXIV's adversarial catalogue names explicitly as the false-
symbiosis gaming vector.)

5.6 Evidence levels for a symbiosis claim. A dormant symbiosis flag is, at the moment of
detection, HYPOTHESIZED by construction — it asserts a POTENTIAL gain from a wiring that has not
yet been made, and no amount of capability-signature analysis alone can promote it past
HYPOTHESIZED, because the actual gain can only be OBSERVED after the wiring exists and the
cluster's combined fitness has been measured across at least one evaluation cycle. This is a
DELIBERATE ceiling, not a limitation to be engineered away: the ecology's honest epistemic
posture is that it can point at PROMISING pairs with high confidence in the complementarity test
itself (which is DERIVED from OBSERVED capability signatures) while remaining HYPOTHESIZED about
the outcome of acting on the pointer, exactly mirroring the general Article V discipline that a
mechanism, however well-reasoned, does not earn OBSERVED status for its own predictions until
those predictions are checked against reality.

5.7 Symbiosis at the cluster level, beyond pairs. The mechanisms above are stated for pairs
because pairs are the minimal and clearest illustration, but symbiosis clusters of three or more
systems are equally within scope, and in fact more common at scale: a cluster where A strengthens
B, B strengthens C, and C feeds back to A in a way that raises A's own fitness (a closed loop) is
a higher-order mutualism the Compounding Graph's own "primary loop" and "amplification spine"
sections describe at the level of specific named IAS datasets — this Part generalizes that same
loop-detection logic to ANY cluster of systems in the estate, not only the fourteen IAS datasets
themselves, which is the concrete sense in which the ecology strengthens the Compounding Graph's
own methodology rather than merely reusing its conclusions.

5.8 Why the complementarity test must be run in BOTH directions independently, not assumed
symmetric. A subtlety worth naming explicitly: System A's output type matching System B's input
type does not, by itself, guarantee the reverse holds — capability signatures are not
automatically symmetric, and a detector that tested only one direction and inferred the other
would systematically misclassify one-directional COMMENSALISM (§5.2) as bidirectional MUTUALISM.
The complementarity test, correctly specified, runs TWICE per candidate pair — once for A-to-B
compatibility, once for B-to-A — and the pair's classification (mutualism, commensalism, or no
relation at all) follows directly and only from how many of the two directions independently pass,
never from a single test result generalized to both directions by assumption.

5.9 A worked near-miss: complementary TYPES with incompatible SCALES. A pair can pass the
complementarity test on type alone while still failing to produce genuine symbiotic gain if the
two systems' operating scales are mismatched — System Q (Part V §5.4), producing a detected
pattern once per several sessions, wired to a hypothetical high-throughput consumer expecting a
pattern-stream at a much higher cadence would pass the type-level complementarity test while
still under-delivering relative to the consumer's actual capacity, a mismatch the type test alone
cannot see because type and throughput are independent dimensions of a capability signature. This
dataset does not specify a throughput-matching sub-test as part of the core complementarity check
(Article VIII's restraint against over-specifying operational parameters applies here directly),
but names the gap explicitly: a dormant-symbiosis flag's HYPOTHESIZED status (§5.6) is precisely
what allows this class of near-miss to be caught at the MEASURED stage (Part VI) — a scale-
mismatched pair, once activated, will show a demand-satisfaction reading below what the
complementarity evidence alone predicted, and the REFUTED outcome (Part VI §6.4) is the honest,
structurally-guaranteed catch for exactly this failure mode, even though the type-level detector
that produced the original flag could not have caught it earlier.

FINAL LAW — PART V. Symbiosis is not a new relation type but a population-level pattern read over
the existing strengthens and feeds subgraph, its complementarity test run independently in both
directions; its highest-value product is not naming clusters already visible in the graph but
surfacing DORMANT, complementarity-tested pairs whose wiring is HYPOTHESIZED to help and whose
actual gain — including scale mismatches the type test alone cannot see — can only be OBSERVED
after the wiring is made and measured.

===============================================================================================
PART VI — SYMBIOSIS ACTIVATION: FROM DORMANT FLAG TO MEASURED OUTCOME
===============================================================================================

6.1 The activation lifecycle, as a specification for a handoff, not an execution. Following the
event-grain restraint of Part I §1.4, this Part specifies the LIFECYCLE a dormant symbiosis flag
passes through on its way to becoming an active, measured relation — without the ecology itself
performing the wiring. The lifecycle, DEFINED, has four stages: FLAGGED (Part V's output — a
complementarity-tested, HYPOTHESIZED pair), PROPOSED (the flag is surfaced to whichever
governance surface handles cross-system wiring proposals — a specification-level candidate this
dataset does not build, named in the translation section), ACTIVATED (the wiring exists — a
feeds or strengthens edge now appears in IAS-F1's relation graph, an action performed by
evolution_engine or an equivalent event-grain mechanism, not by the ecology), and MEASURED (at
least one full evaluation cycle has elapsed since activation, and the landscape (Part III) now
carries an OBSERVED-level fitness reading for the cluster, promoting the original HYPOTHESIZED
claim to either CONFIRMED or REFUTED).

6.2 Why the ecology tracks the lifecycle even though it does not execute any stage past FLAGGED.
Tracking, as distinct from executing, is itself a legitimate ecology-level function: without a
standing record of which flags were proposed, which were activated, and which were subsequently
measured, the ecology has no way to CALIBRATE its own detection mechanism (the Part IV §4.3
calibration gap applies here with equal force — a symbiosis detector whose hit rate is never
checked against outcomes is a detector operating on faith). The lifecycle record is therefore the
ecology's own feedback loop, closing the amplification spine of the Compounding Graph at the
mechanism level: IAS-E1's usage data feeds the landscape (Part IV §4.6), the landscape feeds
symbiosis detection (Part V), detection feeds the lifecycle record, and the lifecycle's measured
outcomes feed BACK into recalibrating the detection mechanism's complementarity test — a genuine
closed loop, not a one-way pipeline.

6.3 Worked continuation: System Q and System R, from FLAGGED to MEASURED. Continuing the Part V
§5.4 example, suppose the Q-R dormant mutualism flag is PROPOSED and, following a governance
surface's independent judgment (an event-grain decision the ecology neither makes nor overrides),
ACTIVATED: a feeds edge now carries Q's detected patterns to R's rule-formation input, and a
second feeds edge carries R's formalized rules back to Q as a refinement signal. One evaluation
cycle later, the landscape (Part III) computes fitness for the Q-R cluster in the environment
both systems share — "recurring-failure governance" — and finds demand-satisfaction has risen
for BOTH Q and R relative to their pre-activation baselines, cost-per-unit-value has fallen for R
specifically (fewer manually-surfaced patterns needed, lowering R's maintenance burden), and
relational position has risen for both (each now holds an inbound edge from the other it lacked
before). The MEASURED stage promotes the original HYPOTHESIZED mutualism claim to CONFIRMED, and
this confirmed instance becomes one data point in the calibration record §6.2 describes — a
single instance, explicitly not yet sufficient to validate the complementarity test in general
(Part XVI's Protocol Two requires a larger, independently-sourced sample before the test itself
can be marked OBSERVED rather than EXPERIMENTAL).

6.4 The REFUTED case, equally important to track. Not every activated flag confirms. Consider a
second synthetic pair, System S and System T, flagged as dormant commensalism (S's output type
matching T's input type, no reciprocal match required for commensalism per §5.2) and activated
via a feeds edge. One evaluation cycle later, the landscape shows NO measurable change in T's
fitness — T was already sourcing equivalent input from elsewhere, and the new edge from S turned
out to be redundant capacity, not a genuine gain. This REFUTED outcome is not a failure of the
ecology's mechanism to be hidden or explained away; it is exactly the kind of falsifiable
prediction Article V's EXPERIMENTAL category exists to license, and the lifecycle record's
honest accounting of REFUTED alongside CONFIRMED outcomes is what prevents the symbiosis
detector from silently becoming a mechanism that only ever reports its own successes — the
precise failure mode Part XXIV's adversarial catalogue names as SELECTIVE REPORTING.

6.5 Interaction with the biodiversity governor: symbiosis activation is not unconditionally good.
A subtlety the lifecycle must respect: activating every flagged dormant symbiosis, without
restraint, risks OVER-COUPLING the population — turning a healthy, loosely-connected ensemble
into a tightly-wired mesh where a failure in one system cascades through many strengthens edges
at once, an outcome Part XVIII's biodiversity governor and Part XIX's anti-proliferation pole
both have standing to object to, and IAS-E2 (Cognitive Reliability)'s failure-budget logic,
referenced via the Compounding Graph's defense loop, would need to account for. (NORMATIVE: this
dataset does NOT resolve how many dormant flags should be activated per cycle, or under what
coupling-density ceiling — that is an allocation judgment belonging to whichever governance
surface consumes the PROPOSED-stage flags, informed by, but not decided by, the ecology's own
symbiosis and biodiversity outputs together.)

6.6 The activation record as genome material. Looking ahead to Part XX, every CONFIRMED
symbiosis activation becomes part of the eventual GENOME record of both participating systems —
a heritable fact ("this system's fitness gain in this environment was measurably attributable to
a specific activated symbiosis") that matters if either system later undergoes speciation (Part
XI) or merger (Part XIII), because a successor or merged system's own expected fitness can be
partially DERIVED from its ancestors' confirmed symbiotic history rather than measured from
zero. This is the first of several forward references this dataset makes deliberately, because
the ecology's mechanisms are not independent modules but a single lifecycle whose outputs feed
one another in the exact way the Compounding Graph's four loops describe at the corpus level.

6.7 The lifecycle's minimum dwell time at each stage, and why rushing FLAGGED-to-PROPOSED is a
distinct risk from rushing PROPOSED-to-ACTIVATED. Two transitions in the four-stage lifecycle carry
different risk profiles and, correctly specified, different minimum evidence-gathering windows.
The FLAGGED-to-PROPOSED transition risks surfacing a complementarity match that later analysis
would have refined or rejected — a comparatively low-cost error, since PROPOSED status alone
commits no population resources and any governance surface reviewing the proposal can still
decline it. The PROPOSED-to-ACTIVATED transition, by contrast, is where Part XXII's governed-
evolution discipline (evidence threshold, canary) actually engages, because ACTIVATED means a real
edge now exists in the population's relation graph, with the coupling-density costs §6.5 already
named. A specification that treated both transitions as requiring the SAME evidentiary weight
would either under-protect activation (treating it as cheaply as a flag) or over-burden flagging
(treating every candidate as though it were already committing population resources) — this Part's
four-stage structure exists specifically to let the two transitions carry asymmetric weight without
requiring a fifth or sixth intermediate stage to express the distinction.

6.8 Interface with the merger pathway: a CONFIRMED symbiosis as one of merger intelligence's own
trigger conditions. Closing a forward reference already made at Part XIII §13.2's second signature
(CONFIRMED-SYMBIOSIS SATURATION): this Part's MEASURED-stage CONFIRMED outcome is not merely an
endpoint for the symbiosis lifecycle itself, it is simultaneously an INPUT EVENT the merger-
candidate detector (Part XIII) watches for, specifically tracking whether a CONFIRMED cluster's own
coordination overhead (cross-system latency, duplicated context-loading) grows, across subsequent
cycles, to the point where the cluster's own maintained-as-two-systems cost exceeds what fusing the
pair would cost. This is the concrete mechanism by which the symbiosis lifecycle and the merger
lifecycle share data without duplicating detection logic: symbiosis produces the CONFIRMED
relationship; merger intelligence watches CONFIRMED relationships specifically for the point at
which their own success becomes the argument for their consolidation, a genuinely different signal
than the CONVERGENT EVOLUTION signature (Part XIII §13.2's first shape) that arises without any
prior symbiotic activation at all.

FINAL LAW — PART VI. A dormant symbiosis flag is not the ecology's final word; it is the first
stage of a four-stage lifecycle — flagged, proposed, activated, measured — whose two central
transitions carry asymmetric evidentiary weight, whose CONFIRMED and REFUTED outcomes both feed
back into calibrating the detector itself and, for CONFIRMED clusters specifically, into the
merger-candidate detector's own watch list, and whose activation decisions remain bounded by the
coupling-density concerns the biodiversity governor holds standing to raise.

===============================================================================================
PART VII — PARASITIC COMPLEXITY: DEFINITION AND SIGNAL TAXONOMY
===============================================================================================

7.1 Parasitism, defined against symbiosis's negative pole. Where symbiosis (Part V) is the
population-level pattern of mutually- or one-sidedly-amplifying strengthens and feeds edges,
PARASITIC COMPLEXITY, DEFINED, is the population-level pattern in which a system CONSUMES more
ensemble value — cost (IAS-F1 Part IV §4.2), consumer attention, maintenance burden, cognitive
overhead on the humans and agents who must reason about it — than it RETURNS, measured against
its own fitness landscape (Part III) across the environments it claims to serve. A parasitic
system is not necessarily a badly-built one; it is one whose COST-TO-RETURN RATIO has drifted
unfavorable, whether through the system's own decay, through its environment's demand shrinking
while its cost footprint did not, or through the emergence of a fitter alternative that has
absorbed the demand the parasitic system once served without the parasitic system's own cost
footprint shrinking to match.

7.2 The three parasitic signal shapes. Consistent with the estate's existing candidate 33 (CO-06
GC — Cognitive OS garbage collection, "systems consuming more value than they create"), this
dataset names three distinguishable signal shapes rather than treating parasitism as one
undifferentiated condition, because each shape implies a different corrective action at the
event-grain level (D2A's or CO-06's, never the ecology's own). SUSTAINED LOW FITNESS, HIGH COST
(the system's landscape shows persistently low or UNDEFINED fitness across most of its claimed
environments, while its cost property remains HIGH or MODERATE — the clearest parasitic signal,
because it fails on both the return side and the cost side simultaneously). CONSUMER STARVATION
(the system's RELATIONAL POSITION, Part III §3.2, shows a declining trend in inbound feeds and
strengthens edges over successive evaluation cycles, even as its cost holds steady — a signal
that the system's actual value to the population is eroding even where its raw fitness figure has
not yet caught up, an EARLY WARNING shape rather than a confirmed one). MANDATORY-PATH INFLATION
(the system holds one or more depends-on edges, IAS-F1 Part VI §6.3, from other systems — meaning
consumers have no alternative — while its demand-satisfaction component, Part III §3.2, is low;
this shape is the specific case Part III §3.3 warned about, where high consumption is confused
with high value, and it is the MOST DANGEROUS parasitic shape precisely because raw usage looks
healthy while the underlying signal is not).

7.3 Why parasitism requires a detector distinct from a simple cost threshold. A naive detector —
"flag any system whose cost exceeds threshold X" — would systematically misclassify expensive-
but-fit systems (a high-cost system serving a high-value, well-satisfied niche is not parasitic;
it is simply expensive, and the portfolio math of IAS-C1, not this dataset, judges whether the
expense is worth it) as parasitic, while missing genuinely parasitic LOW-cost systems whose
return is even lower than their modest cost. (DERIVED: parasitism is a RATIO condition — cost
relative to return, measured per environment, per Part III's landscape — never an absolute cost
condition, and any implementation collapsing it to an absolute threshold has built a cost gate,
not a parasite detector, a distinction this dataset insists on precisely because CO-06's existing
garbage-collection mechanism, the parent this dataset strengthens, is understood by the ledger
itself to already operate at the single-session grain — Candidate 33's overlap note reads "one
session" — while this dataset's detector operates at the population, multi-cycle grain, again the
Part I temporal-population distinction applied to a specific mechanism.)

7.4 The confounding case: a system parasitic in ONE environment, symbiotic in another. Because
fitness is landscape-shaped (Part III), a system can register parasitic signals in one
environment while registering healthy or even symbiotic signals in another, and the detector
must resist the temptation to render a single global "is this system a parasite" verdict. A
system whose governance-environment presence has decayed into consumer starvation (§7.2's second
shape) while its architecture-environment presence remains a confirmed mutualism (Part VI §6.3)
is not, taken as a whole, simply "a parasite" — it is a system whose NICHE has partially shifted,
and the correct ecological reading is PARTIAL, environment-scoped parasitism, a signal for
niche-narrowing or capability-signature revision (a form of partial speciation, Part XI) rather
than wholesale extinction (Part XV), which is the exact distinction Part VIII's adjudication
protocol formalizes as the difference between TOTAL and PARTIAL parasitic classification.

7.5 Distinguishing decay-driven from demand-driven parasitism. Two root causes produce the same
observable signal shapes and require the detector to trace back to CAUSE, not merely SHAPE,
before a parasitism flag is treated as actionable. DECAY-DRIVEN parasitism arises when the
system's own quality has degraded — perhaps through drift, unmaintained assumptions, or a
toolchain dependency that has quietly rotted — while environmental demand for its niche remains
real; the corrective action here is repair or succession (Part XVII), not extinction, because the
niche itself is still worth filling. DEMAND-DRIVEN parasitism arises when the system's quality is
unchanged but the environment's demand for its niche has genuinely shrunk or vanished — the
system has become a well-built answer to a question the estate no longer asks; the corrective
action here IS extinction-with-fossilization (Part XV), because no amount of repair restores
demand that no longer exists. (NORMATIVE: the detector's output must carry this cause
distinction, not merely the shape classification of §7.2, because routing a demand-driven case to
repair wastes effort restoring a system nobody needs, while routing a decay-driven case to
extinction discards a genuinely-needed niche along with its unfit occupant.)

7.6 Interfaces: what feeds the parasitism detector and what it produces. The detector reads the
same landscape (Part III), relational-position trend data (a time-series extension of Part III
§3.2's single-cycle reading), and cost properties (IAS-F1 Part IV §4.2) as the symbiosis detector,
confirming the two are SIBLING MECHANISMS reading one shared substrate rather than independent
systems each maintaining their own duplicate model of the population — the same non-duplication
discipline this dataset asks of the wider estate, applied reflexively to its own internal
architecture. It produces a PARASITISM RECORD per (system, environment) pair showing signal
shape (§7.2), cause classification (§7.5), evidence level, and a recommended disposition category
— REPAIR, NARROW (partial speciation), or RETIRE (extinction candidate) — surfaced to D2A and
CO-06 as input to their own event-grain decisions, never rendered as the ecology's own final
verdict, per the Part I restraint carried consistently through every mechanism this dataset
specifies.

7.7 A fourth, rarer signal shape worth naming for completeness: INHERITED PARASITISM. Distinct
from the three shapes of §7.2, a system can register parasitic signals not because of its own
operation but because it inherited, via a merger or speciation event (Parts XI-XIII, formalized in
Part XX's genome mechanism), a genome component from an ancestor whose own fitness was already in
decline at the moment of transfer. INHERITED PARASITISM, DEFINED, is a signal shape where a
NEWLY-PRODUCED system (within its young-system exemption window, Part VIII §8.2) already shows
low fitness in an environment its genome claims coverage of, distinguishable from ordinary young-
system noise specifically because the low reading correlates with a DOCUMENTED ancestral decline
(traceable via the lineage graph, Part XX §20.3) rather than with the absence of accumulated
evidence young systems generically show. This shape requires its own adjudication branch, distinct
from Part VIII's four checks: rather than asking whether the system is too young to trust, the
question is whether the INHERITED genome component itself was already stale or declining at
transfer time — a genome-provenance question, not a fitness-measurement question, and one this
Part hands to Part XX §20.6's contamination-check discipline rather than resolving independently
here, since a stale, already-declining inheritance is structurally the same category of problem as
an inherited already-REFUTED claim.

7.8 Interfaces with the biodiversity governor: a parasitism record's relevance to dependency
concentration. A final interface worth making explicit: a system flagged MANDATORY-PATH INFLATION
(§7.2's third shape) is, by construction, a strong candidate for the anti-monoculture pole's own
dependency-concentration check (Part XVIII §18.4), because a system many others structurally must
route through, while itself showing low demand-satisfaction, is simultaneously a parasitism risk
AND a structural fragility risk — two independently-motivated flags that, in practice, frequently
co-occur on the same system for related but distinct reasons (the parasitism flag concerns the
system's OWN cost-to-return ratio; the monoculture flag concerns the POPULATION's resilience to
that system's failure). This dataset does not merge the two checks into one, consistent with the
governor's own separate-labeled-outputs discipline (Part XIX §19.6), but names the expected
co-occurrence here so an implementer is not surprised to see both flags land on the same system
simultaneously and does not mistake the redundancy for a specification error.

FINAL LAW — PART VII. Parasitic complexity is a cost-to-return RATIO condition read per
environment from the same landscape symbiosis is read from, never an absolute cost threshold; it
carries three primary signal shapes plus a rarer inherited-genome shape, two distinguishable root
causes, and only the combination of shape and cause — never shape alone — licenses a recommended
disposition.

===============================================================================================
PART VIII — PARASITE ADJUDICATION: FALSE POSITIVES, PROTECTED PARASITES, AND THE TRIAGE PROTOCOL
===============================================================================================

8.1 Why detection alone is insufficient and adjudication must be a separate stage. A parasitism
record (Part VII §7.6) is a FLAG, not a verdict, and treating a flag as sufficient grounds for
action would repeat, at the ecology's own mechanism level, the exact error F1's invariants exist
to prevent at the ensemble level: a claim asserted without the evidence its own severity demands.
This Part specifies the ADJUDICATION stage a parasitism flag must pass through before it is
surfaced to D2A or CO-06 as an actionable recommendation, distinguishing genuine parasitism from
three specific false-positive shapes that recur predictably enough to name.

8.2 False positive one: the YOUNG SYSTEM MISREAD. A system recently admitted (a lifecycle state
IAS-F1 Part XI defines) will, almost by construction, show low relational position (few inbound
feeds and strengthens edges have had time to form) and possibly low demand-satisfaction (few
evaluation cycles have elapsed to build a reliable satisfaction trend), producing a signal shape
resembling consumer starvation (Part VII §7.2) despite the system being perfectly healthy for its
age. The adjudication protocol's first check is therefore an AGE GATE: a system within its first
N evaluation cycles (a threshold this dataset does not fix numerically, per Article VIII, but
specifies as a required gate any implementation must include) is EXEMPT from a parasitism flag
regardless of raw signal shape, because the shape is expected and temporary, not diagnostic.

8.3 False positive two: the FOUNDATION MISREAD. A system whose object is, like IAS-F1 itself,
deliberately WIDE-AND-SHALLOW (Part I §1.6 of the F1 dataset names this pattern explicitly:
"width without depth here is what allows every other IAS dataset to supply the depth against a
common base") will show a relational-position reading that looks unusually broad relative to any
single environment's demand-satisfaction figure, because its value is structural — enabling other
systems — rather than directly consumed within any one environment the landscape currently
tracks. The adjudication protocol's second check is a FOUNDATION EXEMPTION: a system whose
strengthens-edge fan-out (Part VI §6.6) exceeds a population-relative threshold, even where its
own environment-scoped demand-satisfaction reads low, is presumptively a foundation pattern, not
a parasite, and requires a DIRECT REVIEW of its strengthens edges (not merely its landscape cells)
before any parasitism disposition is finalized.

8.4 False positive three: the SEASONAL DEMAND MISREAD. An environment whose demand is
genuinely cyclical — recurring but not constant, such as a mission-type that surges during a
specific project phase and goes quiet otherwise — will produce a system whose landscape cell
alternates between high and UNDEFINED-or-low fitness across cycles, resembling demand-driven
parasitism (Part VII §7.5) during its quiet phase despite the system being the correct, fit
occupant of a genuinely cyclical niche. The adjudication protocol's third check requires
TREND-LENGTH sufficiency: a parasitism flag requires a sustained signal across MULTIPLE
consecutive cycles, per Part VII §7.2's own "sustained" and "declining trend" language, and a
single-cycle low reading, however severe, is INSUFFICIENT EVIDENCE on its own, precisely to
protect the seasonal case from a premature disposition.

8.5 The harder problem: PROTECTED PARASITES. The three false positives above are detection
errors, correctable by better checks. A PROTECTED PARASITE, DEFINED, is a harder and more
consequential case: a system that GENUINELY meets the parasitism criteria of Part VII — its
signal shape and root cause both confirm a real, sustained, cost-exceeds-return condition — but
which nonetheless continues to be renewed, maintained, or defended, typically because it is
politically, historically, or habitually entrenched (it was an early, foundational build; it is
associated with a decision-maker's prior investment; its removal is deferred because "it might
still be needed") rather than because any evidence supports its continued cost. Protected
parasitism is a FAILURE MODE this dataset names and Part XXIII elaborates in full, but the
adjudication protocol's job is narrower and more mechanical: to ensure the DETECTION step itself
is not the point of failure — a protected parasite must still be correctly FLAGGED by this
mechanism, even if what happens to the flag afterward (whether it is acted upon) is a governance
question outside the ecology's own authority.

8.6 The triage protocol, stated as a sequence. Bringing the checks together: a parasitism record
(Part VII §7.6) passes to adjudication only after (1) the age gate (§8.2) confirms the system is
not a young-system misread, (2) the foundation exemption check (§8.3) confirms the system's
strengthens-edge fan-out does not explain the apparent low return, (3) the trend-length check
(§8.4) confirms the signal is sustained across multiple cycles rather than a single seasonal dip,
and (4) the cause classification (Part VII §7.5) has been assigned, distinguishing decay-driven
from demand-driven. Only a record surviving all four steps is promoted from FLAGGED to
ADJUDICATED, and only an ADJUDICATED record is surfaced to D2A or CO-06 as an actionable
recommendation — a discipline directly analogous to the symbiosis lifecycle's FLAGGED-to-
PROPOSED distinction (Part VI §6.1), applied to the negative case.

8.7 Worked adjudication: a synthetic system surviving three checks and failing the fourth. System
U, illustrative only, shows sustained low demand-satisfaction and moderate cost across four
consecutive evaluation cycles in its sole claimed environment — surviving the age gate (it has
operated for many cycles), the foundation exemption (its strengthens fan-out is minimal, ruling
out a foundation pattern), and the trend-length check (four consecutive cycles is well beyond a
single seasonal dip). The cause classification, however, resolves the case ambiguously: System
U's environment shows genuinely declining aggregate demand across the SAME four cycles (a
demand-driven signature), while System U's own output quality, independently checked against a
held-out sample, has ALSO measurably degraded over the same window (a decay-driven signature) —
both causes are present simultaneously, a MIXED-CAUSE case this dataset's binary decay/demand
taxonomy (Part VII §7.5) does not cleanly resolve. The adjudication protocol's honest output
here is not a forced single-cause classification but a MIXED-CAUSE flag, carried forward to D2A
and CO-06 as CONTESTED per Article V, with both candidate dispositions (repair-worth-attempting
versus retire-with-fossilization) named rather than one asserted — an explicit acknowledgment
that the ecology's taxonomy, however useful in the clean cases of §7.5's worked shapes, does not
always resolve reality's messier instances, and forcing a clean answer where the evidence is
mixed would be a worse error than naming the ambiguity honestly.

8.8 Ordering the four checks, and why the sequence itself matters. The four checks of §8.6 are
specified in a deliberate order — age gate, then foundation exemption, then trend-length, then
cause classification — and the order is not arbitrary: each earlier check is CHEAPER to evaluate
and FILTERS OUT a larger fraction of false positives than the check that follows it, so running
them in this sequence lets an implementation short-circuit on the first failing check rather than
computing all four unconditionally. The age gate requires only a single lookup (admission
timestamp versus current cycle); the foundation exemption requires a strengthens-edge fan-out
count, a modest graph traversal; the trend-length check requires a multi-cycle landscape history
read; and the cause classification, the most expensive, requires cross-referencing environment-
level demand trends against the system's own independently-checked output-quality history. A
specification that ran cause classification FIRST, before the cheaper checks, would waste the most
expensive computation on candidates the age gate alone would have excluded — a genuine efficiency
argument, not merely a stylistic one, for the ordering this Part specifies.

8.9 The adjudicated-but-stale record: a fifth, procedural concern distinct from the four
substantive checks. An ADJUDICATED record (one that has passed all four checks) is not
permanently valid — per Part III §3.7's staleness discipline, applied here to adjudication records
specifically, an ADJUDICATED record that sits unactioned for many subsequent cycles (the exact
PROTECTED-PARASITE-CANDIDATE condition Part XXIII §23.5 names) must be RE-VALIDATED against fresh
landscape data before being surfaced again, because the population may have shifted enough in the
interim — a repair may have already occurred through some unrelated maintenance action, or the
environment's demand may have shifted further — that the original adjudication, though correctly
performed at the time, no longer reflects current reality. This re-validation requirement is what
keeps the protected-parasite cost tracker (Part XXIII §23.5) honest: the accumulating cost figure
it reports must be computed against CURRENT, re-validated adjudication data, never against a
stale, one-time adjudication snapshot that may have silently gone out of date.

FINAL LAW — PART VIII. A parasitism flag earns action only after surviving four checks, run in
cost-ascending order — age, foundation exemption, trend length, and cause classification — kept
fresh by periodic re-validation rather than trusted indefinitely once adjudicated, and where the
evidence genuinely splits between decay and demand, the honest output is a CONTESTED mixed-cause
record naming both candidate dispositions, not a forced single verdict the evidence does not
support.

PART IX — NICHE DISCOVERY: DOMAINS MISSING A SPECIALIZED CAPABILITY
===============================================================================================

9.1 Niche vacancy, formalized from Part II's primitive. Part II §2.4 defined a niche as VACANT
when an environment's demand has no system whose capability signature addresses it at all. NICHE
DISCOVERY, DEFINED as this dataset's fifth mechanism, is the systematic search over the
landscape (Part III) for UNDEFINED cells that represent genuine vacancy rather than the honest
absence Part III §3.6 protects — that is, cells where an environment demonstrably GENERATES
demand (missions of that type recur, are attempted by ill-fitting systems, or are escalated to
the Owner for lack of a fitting system) while no system's capability signature covers it. Niche
discovery is therefore the landscape's UNDEFINED cells read through a second lens: not merely
"unmeasured" but "unmeasured AND demanded," a distinction that separates a genuine gap from an
environment nobody has ever actually needed served.

9.2 The relationship to CO-12's gap radar, and the boundary between them. The estate already
contains, as OBSERVED via the Non-Duplication Ledger's candidate 35 ("Niche Discovery | CO-12 gap
radar | one gap | domains missing a specialized capability | SUBENGINE"), a gap-radar mechanism
under the Cognitive OS's ownership. CO-12's object, per the ledger's own overlap note, is "one
gap" — a single, event-scoped identification of a missing capability, surfaced presumably at the
moment a mission fails to find a fitting system. This dataset's niche discovery mechanism is the
POPULATION-GRAIN generalization: rather than surfacing one gap reactively when a mission hits it,
it maintains a STANDING MAP of vacancy across the FULL landscape, proactively, whether or not any
mission has yet failed against a given vacant niche. (DERIVED, consistent with Part I's temporal-
population distinction: CO-12 is event-grain gap surfacing; this dataset's niche discovery is
population-grain vacancy mapping; the ledger's own SUBENGINE verdict confirms CO-12's per-event
mechanism is correctly subsumed as a signal INPUT to this dataset's standing map, not replaced by
it — every CO-12 gap event becomes one data point confirming a vacancy this dataset's landscape
analysis may have already flagged proactively, or, where the landscape had not yet flagged it, a
correction to the landscape's own coverage.)

9.3 Distinguishing genuine vacancy from three non-vacant UNDEFINED shapes. Following the
adjudication discipline of Part VIII, niche discovery requires its own triage before an UNDEFINED
cell is promoted to a VACANCY CANDIDATE. The NEVER-DEMANDED shape (the environment itself has
never generated real demand — a hypothetical mission-type nobody has proposed or attempted) is
not a vacancy; it is correctly UNDEFINED and stays that way until real demand appears. The
MISROUTED-DEMAND shape (demand exists, but IAS-A1's routing has been sending it to an ill-fitting
system that produces low-satisfaction output rather than to no system at all — the landscape cell
is not UNDEFINED, it is DEFINED-BUT-LOW, a parasitism-adjacent signal, Part VII, rather than a
niche-discovery signal) must be excluded from vacancy candidates precisely because a fix here is
better routing, not a new system. The GENUINELY-VACANT shape (demand recurs, escalates, or is
manually handled outside any system's capability signature at all) is the only shape niche
discovery promotes forward.

9.4 Vacancy severity: breadth, recurrence, and escalation cost. Not every genuine vacancy carries
equal weight, and niche discovery's output must rank vacancies rather than list them flatly,
because a governance surface consuming this output (per the Backlog Autopilot and Demand
Forecasting parents named in the Compounding Graph's foresight loop) needs to prioritize. Three
factors compose a vacancy's severity, each DERIVED from data the ecology already reads: BREADTH
(how many distinct sub-environments or mission variants fall within the vacant niche — a narrow
vacancy affecting one rare mission-type is lower severity than a broad vacancy affecting many
recurring variants), RECURRENCE RATE (how often, per evaluation cycle, the vacancy is actually hit
by real missions, read from the Control Bus event log per Part IV §4.6's interface), and
ESCALATION COST (what happens when the vacancy is hit — a mission that silently proceeds with a
degraded substitute carries lower escalation cost than one that halts and requires Owner
intervention, per the Owner-decide precedent IAS-F1 Part X §10.5 references).

9.5 Worked example: a vacancy surfaced, ranked, and distinguished from a misrouted-demand
near-miss. Consider a synthetic environment, "cross-project failure-fingerprint correlation" —
illustrative only — where missions recurringly need to compare a failure signature against
patterns observed in OTHER projects, not merely the current one. No system's capability signature,
in this synthetic trace, covers cross-project correlation specifically; the nearest system, System
V, correlates failure signatures WITHIN one project only. Two readings are possible on first
inspection: either System V is a MISROUTED-DEMAND near-miss (missions are being sent to V and
receiving a within-project-only answer that partially, unsatisfyingly serves the demand) or the
cross-project niche is GENUINELY VACANT (no system, including V, actually covers the
cross-project case, and V's involvement is a best-effort substitute rather than evidence of
routing failure). The distinguishing test, per §9.3, is whether V's own capability signature
CLAIMS cross-project coverage (in which case any shortfall is V's fitness problem, Part VII) or
explicitly EXCLUDES it (in which case the shortfall is a niche vacancy, this Part). In this
synthetic trace, V's signature is scoped to "within one project" explicitly, so the case resolves
as GENUINE VACANCY, ranked at moderate-to-high severity given a recurrence rate the Control Bus
log shows rising over three consecutive cycles and an escalation cost that, in two of the last
five hits, required Owner intervention.

9.6 Niche discovery's output and its consumers. The mechanism produces a ranked VACANCY REGISTRY:
one entry per genuine, triaged, severity-scored vacancy, each carrying its defining environment-
demand combination, severity components (§9.4), and evidence level (a vacancy's existence is
OBSERVED from real recurrence data; its FUTURE severity trend is, at best, DERIVED or
HYPOTHESIZED). This registry feeds IAS-C2 (Demand Forecasting) directly, per the Compounding
Graph's foresight loop ("IAS-E1 + IAS-D1 history → IAS-C2 Demand Forecasting predicts next-needed
capabilities"), and feeds the Capability Portfolio (IAS-C1) as a candidate list for new-system
investment, distinct from — and upstream of — IAS-C1's own allocation judgment over which
candidate vacancies actually merit building a new system to fill.

9.7 Why niche discovery does not itself propose the filling system. Consistent with the ecology's
restraint throughout, the vacancy registry stops at NAMING the gap; it does not design, propose,
or build the system that would fill it, because doing so would cross from the ecology's
population-observation object into IAS-A1's routing-and-capability-design territory and, more
directly, into a BUILD-class D2A verdict the ecology has no standing to render on its own — a
vacancy is evidence a BUILD verdict MIGHT be warranted, never a substitute for D2A's own
adjudication of whether the vacancy is best filled by a new system, an EXTEND of an existing
near-neighbor's capability signature, or left vacant because the escalation cost, however real,
does not yet justify the ongoing maintenance cost a new system would add to the population
(itself a portfolio judgment, IAS-C1's object, not this dataset's).

9.8 Vacancy DECAY: why a registry entry must expire if recurrence stops. Symmetric to the
staleness discipline applied to fitness (Part III §3.7) and to adjudication records (Part VIII
§8.9), a vacancy registry entry (§9.6) is not permanent once created — if the recurrence rate
(§9.4) that justified the entry falls to zero across several subsequent cycles (the environment
that once generated recurring demand has itself gone quiet, an environment-level analogue of the
DEMAND-DRIVEN parasitism cause, Part VII §7.5, applied here to a vacancy rather than to an occupied
niche), the entry is marked DECAYED rather than deleted outright — decayed entries remain in the
registry's historical record (feeding IAS-C2's demand-forecasting retrodiction, per Part IX §9.6's
interface) but are excluded from the ACTIVE severity ranking a governance surface consumes for
present-day prioritization. Without this decay discipline, a vacancy registry would only ever
grow, accumulating entries for demand patterns that were real once but are no longer, degrading
the registry's own signal-to-noise ratio over the estate's lifetime exactly as an unfossilized,
never-pruned hard-rules list would.

9.9 The relationship between niche vacancy and the biodiversity governor's WELL-COVERED region,
made explicit as a closure property. A niche that transitions from VACANT to WELL-COVERED (Part
X §10.4) — because a new system was built to fill it, following a BUILD verdict D2A rendered on
this Part's registry evidence — is the single cleanest success signature this dataset's whole
apparatus can produce: a gap named (this Part), a system built (D2A, outside this dataset's
authority), and a landscape cell that was UNDEFINED now showing a DEFINED, OBSERVED fitness value
(Part III) confirming the gap is genuinely closed rather than merely nominally addressed by a
system that, once measured, turns out not to actually satisfy the demand that motivated it. This
closure loop — vacancy flagged, system built, fitness measured, ledger region reclassified — is
this dataset's own miniature instance of the Compounding Graph's foresight loop, compressed to a
single niche rather than the estate-wide cycle Part XXVI's integration treatment describes.

FINAL LAW — PART IX. Niche discovery generalizes CO-12's one-gap radar into a standing,
population-grain vacancy map that decays its own stale entries rather than accumulating them
indefinitely; a vacancy is genuine only when demand recurs against no capability signature at all
— never when demand is merely misrouted to an ill-fitting system — and the registry it produces
names gaps for others to fill, closing the loop only once a built system's own measured fitness
confirms the gap is genuinely gone.

PART X — NICHE BREADTH, OVERLAP, AND THE ECOLOGY'S OWN COMPETITION-VERSUS-VACANCY LEDGER
===============================================================================================

10.1 Why niche discovery needs a companion analysis of OCCUPIED niches, not only vacant ones. Part
IX's vacancy registry answers "where is nothing?" A complete niche model also needs to answer
"where is TOO MUCH, and where is exactly enough?" — the occupied side of the same map, which
this Part supplies as niche discovery's necessary companion, because a population that fixes
every vacancy while ignoring its occupied-niche imbalances has only solved half the allocation
problem the Compounding Graph's primary loop depends on.

10.2 Niche breadth, defined and measured. NICHE BREADTH, DEFINED, is the count and diversity of
distinct environment-demand combinations a single system's capability signature genuinely
addresses at DEFINED, non-trivial fitness (Part III), as distinct from the raw count of
environments where the system merely holds SOME presence. A system with narrow breadth
(one or two well-fitted environments) is a specialist; a system with broad breadth (many
environments at moderate, comparable fitness) is a generalist, echoing the BROAD-PLATEAU shape
Part III §3.4 already named — breadth and landscape shape are, in fact, the same underlying
signal read from two angles, breadth counting the shape's populated cells, shape describing their
relative heights.

10.3 Niche overlap, defined and distinguished from duplication. NICHE OVERLAP, DEFINED, is the
degree to which two or more systems' niches intersect — measured as the proportion of
environment-demand combinations both systems address at comparable, non-trivial fitness. Overlap
exists on a spectrum from ZERO (fully disjoint niches, no competition) through PARTIAL (systems
share some environments, diverge in others — the System M/System N relationship of Part II §2.6)
to COMPLETE (identical niche coverage, the System M/System P relationship, a duplication
candidate). The critical distinction this Part insists on: overlap is a MEASURED, continuous
quantity the ecology computes directly from the landscape, while duplication is a BINARY VERDICT
D2A renders using overlap as one input among others (capability-signature textual similarity,
provenance, intent) — the ecology supplies the overlap MEASUREMENT; D2A supplies the duplication
JUDGMENT, and conflating the two would have the ecology quietly usurping D2A's Invariant-Three-
protected authority by another route.

10.4 The competition-versus-vacancy ledger: reading breadth and overlap together across the whole
population. Bringing niche breadth (§10.2) and niche overlap (§10.3) together with the vacancy
registry (Part IX §9.6) produces a single, population-wide LEDGER with three regions: VACANT
(Part IX's registry — no coverage), CONTESTED (high overlap among two or more systems — multiple
occupants competing for the same demand, a competes-with cluster IAS-F1 Part VI §6.4 already
types), and WELL-COVERED (moderate-to-zero overlap, at least one system with defined, non-trivial
fitness — the healthy default state a niche should occupy). This three-region ledger is the
single artifact this Part exists to produce, and it is the primary input to the biodiversity
governor's two poles (Parts XVIII-XIX): CONTESTED regions with excessive occupant count feed the
anti-proliferation pole, VACANT and thinly-covered regions feed the anti-monoculture pole's
concern that too few systems means too little resilience.

10.5 Worked example: reading the ledger across the running synthetic population. Applying the
ledger to Systems M, N, P (Part II §2.6), Q, R (Part V §5.4), S, T (Part VI §6.4), and U (Part
VIII §8.7), a synthetic snapshot emerges: the governance environment is CONTESTED (M, N, and P
all hold defined fitness there, with M and P at COMPLETE overlap per §10.3 — a duplication
candidate already flagged to D2A per Part II §2.6 — and N at PARTIAL overlap with both,
contributing genuine breadth rather than pure redundancy); the architecture environment is
WELL-COVERED by N alone, with no contesting occupant; the recurring-failure-governance
environment, post-activation (Part VI §6.3), is WELL-COVERED by the confirmed Q-R symbiotic
cluster; System U's environment, given its adjudicated mixed-cause parasitism (Part VIII §8.7),
reads as CONTESTED-BUT-UNHEALTHY — occupied, but by a system whose own fitness is failing,
which the ledger flags distinctly from a healthy CONTESTED region precisely because the
corrective action differs (repair or retire U, not merely "add competition").

10.6 Why the ledger, not breadth or overlap alone, is the correct unit for the biodiversity
governor to consume. A governor reading breadth alone would see System N's broad plateau and
might (incorrectly) recommend narrowing it to "reduce complexity," missing that N's breadth is
precisely what makes the architecture environment WELL-COVERED rather than VACANT. A governor
reading overlap alone would see M-P's complete overlap and correctly flag duplication, but would
miss that the SAME overlap measurement, applied to Q-R's post-activation symbiotic cluster (which
shares an environment but through COMPLEMENTARY rather than SUBSTITUTABLE capability, per Part V
§5.5's complementarity test), must NOT be read as unhealthy overlap at all — Q and R occupy
adjacent, complementary sub-niches within one environment, not one contested niche. The three-
region ledger, by construction, keeps these cases distinguishable because it is computed from the
same underlying complementarity and substitutability tests the symbiosis and parasitism
detectors already apply, rather than from overlap percentage alone.

10.7 Interfaces and evidence discipline. The ledger inherits the evidence level of its weakest
input per environment (an established discipline, consistent with Part III §3.7 and Part IV
§4.1), and it is recomputed every evaluation cycle rather than cached, because niche occupancy is
exactly as time-sensitive as the fitness landscape it is derived from — a niche WELL-COVERED this
cycle can become CONTESTED next cycle if a new system is admitted, or VACANT if its sole occupant
is retired (Part XV), and a stale ledger would misinform the biodiversity governor in the same way
a stale property record misinforms IAS-C1's portfolio math.

10.8 A fourth region considered and rejected: DORMANT. During specification, a fourth ledger
region was considered — DORMANT, for niches with a system whose capability signature claims
coverage but whose landscape cell has gone UNDEFINED through prolonged non-use rather than through
genuine absence. This dataset rejects a separate DORMANT region as unnecessary: a system claiming
coverage it has not exercised in many cycles is more precisely captured as an instance of the
CONSUMER STARVATION parasitic signal (Part VII §7.2's second shape) than as a distinct occupancy
state, because "claims coverage, unused" is a claim about the SYSTEM's own fitness trend, not a
property of the NICHE's occupancy — the niche itself, from the population's perspective, is either
genuinely served (WELL-COVERED, if the claim still holds under scrutiny) or genuinely vacant (if
the claim has decayed to the point of being false), and introducing a fourth region would blur this
already-established distinction between niche-level occupancy (Part X's object) and system-level
fitness (Part VII's object) rather than sharpening it.

10.9 Worked closure: the CC-DD proliferation pair (Part XIX §19.4) read through this Part's ledger
before the anti-proliferation pole ever engages. Anticipating Part XIX's own worked example, it is
worth showing here how the SAME ledger this Part specifies is the shared substrate both the
biodiversity governor's poles read from a single computation: the "governance-audit reporting"
environment housing Systems CC, DD, EE, and FF (Part XIX §19.4) registers, under this Part's own
three-region test, as CONTESTED — four occupants, all showing DEFINED, non-trivial fitness, no
single dominant occupant crossing the anti-monoculture pole's dependency-concentration threshold
(Part XVIII §18.4). This Part's own output stops there: it reports CONTESTED and moves on, exactly
as specified in §10.4-§10.6. It does NOT, and structurally cannot, distinguish CC-DD's
substitutability from EE-FF's complementarity on its own — that finer-grained read is the anti-
proliferation pole's own added value (Part XIX §19.3), layered on top of this Part's coarser
three-region classification rather than duplicating it, which is the precise, checkable sense in
which Part X and Part XIX are sibling mechanisms at different resolutions over the identical
underlying ledger rather than two independent detectors that happen to examine the same data.

FINAL LAW — PART X. Niche breadth and niche overlap are measured, continuous quantities the
ecology computes directly; duplication remains D2A's binary verdict built partly on overlap as
evidence, and the three-region ledger — vacant, contested, well-covered, deliberately without a
fourth dormant region that would blur niche-level occupancy with system-level fitness — recomputed
every cycle, is the single artifact that lets the biodiversity governor read the whole population's
occupancy at once rather than one niche at a time.

===============================================================================================
PART XI — SPECIATION: SPLITTING A SYSTEM WHEN ITS USES DIVERGE
===============================================================================================

11.1 Speciation, defined against the bimodal landscape signal. Part III §3.4 named the BIMODAL
landscape shape — high fitness in two disjoint environment clusters, near-zero fitness between —
as the primary evidence for speciation. SPECIATION, DEFINED, is the ecological judgment that a
single system's capability signature has, over time, come to serve two (or more) genuinely
divergent uses whose combined maintenance under one capability signature costs the population
more (in confusion, in coupled-change risk, in blocked independent evolution of either use) than
splitting the system into two would cost, each successor free to specialize toward its own
cluster of demand without carrying the other's unrelated constraints.

11.2 The divergence threshold: what makes a bimodal shape TRULY bimodal, not merely uneven. Part
III §3.5 already distinguished System N's broad-plateau-leaning-bimodal shape (genuinely uneven,
not truly divergent, because "the same underlying capability handles both, just at different
demand levels") from a genuine speciation candidate. This Part formalizes that distinction: a
landscape is TRULY BIMODAL, and a speciation candidate, only when the two high-fitness clusters
are served by DEMONSTRABLY DIFFERENT INTERNAL LOGIC PATHS within the system — evidenced,
practically, by the two clusters requiring INDEPENDENT changes to serve well (a change that
improves cluster-one fitness does not improve, and sometimes degrades, cluster-two fitness, a
signal read from the system's own change history where available) — as opposed to a single
logic path that simply performs better under some demand levels than others, which is unevenness,
not divergence. (DERIVED: this is the same complementarity-versus-substitutability distinction
Part V §5.5 and Part X §10.6 both apply, now applied to a single system's INTERNAL coherence
rather than to a relation between two systems — the speciation test asks whether a system is,
functionally, already two systems wearing one capability signature.)

11.3 Why premature speciation is worse than delayed speciation, and the asymmetric caution this
implies. Splitting a system that was merely uneven, not divergent, produces the FRAGMENTATION
anti-pattern named in Part XXIII's failure catalogue: two successor systems, each narrower and
each now individually more exposed to the young-system misread (Part VIII §8.2) and to reduced
relational position (fewer consumers naturally address a newly-narrowed capability signature
than addressed the original broad one), for a coupling-cost saving that was never real, because
the original system's single logic path was never actually straining under divergent, mutually-
interfering demands. (NORMATIVE: speciation is therefore held to a HIGHER evidence bar than
symbiosis activation, Part VI, precisely because its downside — fragmenting a healthy generalist
into two weaker specialists — is more costly and harder to reverse than activating a wiring that
turns out unhelpful; a speciation candidate requires the internal-change-history evidence of
§11.2, not merely a bimodal landscape shape observed in a single cycle.)

11.4 The speciation procedure, specified. Following the same lifecycle discipline as symbiosis
(Part VI §6.1), speciation proceeds through stages: CANDIDATE (a landscape shows a bimodal shape
surviving multiple cycles, per Part VII §7.2's "sustained" discipline applied here by analogy),
DIVERGENCE-CONFIRMED (the §11.2 internal-change-history test independently corroborates the
bimodal shape is genuine divergence, not unevenness), PROPOSED (surfaced to D2A, whose SUBENGINE
disposition for candidate 36 — "Speciation | D2A specialize | one split | SUBENGINE → IAS-D1" —
confirms the ecology performs the DETECTION and evidence-gathering while D2A's own "specialize"
operation, per the ledger, remains the mechanism that actually executes a split, consistent with
the D2A-authority-preserved pattern established for every other mechanism in this dataset), and
MEASURED (post-split, both successor systems' independent landscapes are tracked across at least
one full cycle to confirm the predicted specialization gain materializes — a CONFIRMED or
REFUTED outcome exactly analogous to Part VI §6.3-6.4's symbiosis measurement stage).

11.5 Worked example: a synthetic speciation candidate, evidenced and proposed. Consider System W,
illustrative only, whose capability signature reads "validate a proposed change against the
estate's governance rules." Its landscape shows two disjoint high-fitness clusters: one in a
"pre-commit validation" environment (fast, narrow, syntactic checks against a small rule subset,
invoked automatically and frequently) and one in a "pre-deployment validation" environment (slow,
broad, semantic checks against the full rule corpus, invoked rarely and requiring more context).
Change-history evidence corroborates genuine divergence: three of the system's last five
modifications improved pre-commit validation speed while measurably slowing pre-deployment
validation's thoroughness (a resource-contention signature — the same internal logic path was
being tuned in opposite directions by two different consumer groups), and two modifications did
the reverse. This CANDIDATE clears DIVERGENCE-CONFIRMED and is PROPOSED to D2A as a speciation
recommendation: System W-fast (pre-commit) and System W-deep (pre-deployment), each free to
specialize its own logic path without the tuning conflict the shared signature has been absorbing.

11.6 The genome inheritance obligation at speciation. Anticipating Part XX, a speciation event is
one of the two primary GENOME-transfer events this dataset tracks (the other is merger, Part
XIII). Both successor systems inherit the parent's confirmed fitness history, confirmed symbiotic
relationships (apportioned to whichever successor's environment the relationship actually served,
per the divergence evidence itself), and any hard rules or ACIS-level principles the parent
accumulated — a LINEAGE record, not a fresh start, which matters because a successor treated as a
brand-new system would incorrectly re-trigger the young-system exemption (Part VIII §8.2) despite
inheriting a genuinely mature capability core.

11.7 Speciation's interaction with the biodiversity governor. A successful speciation event
increases population count (one system becomes two) while, correctly read, INCREASING rather
than decreasing biodiversity in the sense Part XVIII cares about, because the two successors now
occupy genuinely distinct niches (per §11.2's divergence test) rather than the single, internally-
strained niche the parent occupied — the anti-proliferation pole (Part XIX) must therefore be
specified carefully enough to distinguish a genuine, divergence-evidenced speciation from mere
system-count inflation, a distinction Part XIX addresses directly rather than leaving speciation
vulnerable to being penalized by the very governor it is meant to serve.

11.8 The minimum cluster size below which speciation is not a candidate at all. A landscape
showing high fitness in exactly ONE environment plus a second, barely-above-UNDEFINED reading in a
second environment is not yet a genuine bimodal candidate under §11.2's test — a true bimodal
shape requires BOTH clusters to independently clear the same non-trivial fitness bar the rest of
this dataset applies (Part III §3.1's "defined, non-trivial fitness" standard), because a landscape
with one strong peak and one weak, marginal signal is better read as a narrow-peak specialist
(Part III §3.4) with an emerging, not-yet-established secondary presence than as two coherent,
already-divergent capability clusters. PREVENTION, stated as a required gate: a speciation
candidate requires BOTH clusters to have accumulated the SAME multi-cycle sustained-evidence
standard (Part VII §7.2's discipline, applied here) independently, not one cluster established and
one merely emerging — a system with one clear niche and one nascent, unproven secondary presence
is a candidate for ordinary growth monitoring, never for a split.

11.9 Why the divergence-confirmed test (§11.2) is asymmetric with the merger overlap-confirmed
test (Part XIII §13.4) in what it corroborates against. Speciation's evidence bar corroborates
against the SYSTEM's own internal change history — did modifications benefiting one cluster harm
the other — while merger's evidence bar (Part XIII §13.4) corroborates against MULTIPLE
ENVIRONMENTS held by TWO DIFFERENT systems. This asymmetry is not an oversight but a direct
consequence of the two mechanisms' different objects: speciation asks whether ONE system's
internal logic is coherent; merger asks whether TWO systems' external niches are convergent — and
a specification that tried to use the same corroboration method for both would either apply an
internal-coherence test to a question about two systems' external relationship (a category error)
or apply a cross-system overlap test to a question about one system's own internal logic (an
equally poor fit), confirming that the two mechanisms, though structural inverses of each other at
the outcome level, require genuinely different evidence at the corroboration level.

FINAL LAW — PART XI. Speciation splits one system into two only when a bimodal landscape shape,
with BOTH clusters independently clearing the sustained-evidence bar, is corroborated by
independent internal-change-history evidence of genuine divergence, never on landscape shape alone
and never on one established cluster paired with one merely emerging; the ecology detects and
evidences the candidate, D2A's specialize operation executes the split, and both successors
inherit the parent's lineage rather than starting from zero.

PART XII — SPECIATION'S FAILURE MODES AND THE ANTI-FRAGMENTATION DISCIPLINE
===============================================================================================

12.1 Why speciation earns its own failure-mode Part, distinct from Part XXIII's general
catalogue. Speciation's downside (Part XI §11.3) is severe enough, and specific enough, to merit
dedicated treatment before this dataset moves on to merger — the mechanism's structural inverse —
because a reader evaluating merger (Part XIII) without first internalizing exactly how speciation
can go wrong will be poorly positioned to judge when the OPPOSITE correction (fusing two systems
back together) is warranted.

12.2 The FRAGMENTATION anti-pattern, elaborated. Building on Part XI §11.3's naming, fragmentation
is what results when a speciation candidate clears the CANDIDATE stage on landscape shape alone
without genuine DIVERGENCE-CONFIRMED evidence — two successor systems are created, each narrower,
each individually weaker on relational position (Part III §3.2) than the parent was, and, most
damagingly, each now REQUIRES the ecology's own coordination mechanisms (symbiosis detection,
Part V, to potentially re-discover the dormant complementarity the split just destroyed) to
recover value the unsplit parent already provided for free by virtue of being one system. A
fragmented pair is, in the worst case, WORSE than the parasitic pattern it may have been intended
to cure, because the parent's confused-but-functional single logic path is replaced by two clean-
but-under-consumed logic paths, and consumer traffic that once went to one well-known system must
now be correctly routed (IAS-A1's object) to the right successor — a routing burden that did not
exist before the split.

12.3 Detecting fragmentation after the fact: the MEASURED-stage reversal signal. Because
speciation, like symbiosis activation, carries a MEASURED stage (Part XI §11.4), a fragmentation
error is not permanently silent — it is detectable, one to several cycles after the split, as a
specific pattern: BOTH successors show DECLINING relational position relative to a
population-weighted baseline (rather than one or both showing the predicted fitness GAIN the
speciation proposal justified itself on), and the ecology's own symbiosis detector (Part V)
independently flags a DORMANT MUTUALISM between the two successors within one to two cycles of
the split — a strong secondary signal, because a genuine divergence-based split should not
immediately re-generate a complementarity flag between its own successors; if it does, the split
likely separated two halves of ONE coherent capability rather than two genuinely distinct ones.

12.4 The reversal mechanism: RE-MERGER as a distinct case from ordinary merger intelligence.
Where the MEASURED-stage evidence of §12.3 confirms fragmentation, the corrective action is a
RE-MERGER — structurally identical to the merger mechanism Part XIII specifies for two previously-
unrelated systems, but carrying a distinct provenance tag ("re-merger, reversing speciation event
ID such-and-such") that feeds directly into the calibration record (analogous to Part VI §6.2's
symbiosis-detector calibration loop) used to recalibrate the DIVERGENCE-CONFIRMED test of Part XI
§11.2 itself — a re-merger is not merely a correction of one bad split, it is EVIDENCE that the
divergence test's evidentiary bar, as currently calibrated, let a false positive through, and
that evidence must feed back into tightening the test, not simply be filed away as a one-off
mistake.

12.5 Worked example: a hypothetical fragmentation, detected and reversed. Continuing the System
W-fast / System W-deep example from Part XI §11.5, suppose — illustrative only, presented as a
counterfactual to demonstrate the failure mode rather than as a claim about the actual outcome of
that worked example — the split had instead been approved on landscape shape alone, without the
change-history corroboration Part XI §11.2 actually required in the real worked case. Two cycles
post-split, W-fast shows declining relational position (consumers who once reached "validate my
change" as a single, well-known entry point now frequently invoke W-deep by mistake for
pre-commit needs, a routing-confusion cost), W-deep shows a similar decline from the opposite
direction, and the symbiosis detector flags a dormant mutualism between W-fast and W-deep within
one cycle — both share near-identical capability-signature roots and their outputs are, in fact,
mutually informative in exactly the way Part V §5.5 would expect from two halves of one coherent
system. This pattern triggers a RE-MERGER recommendation, and the calibration record notes the
divergence test, as applied in this counterfactual, was insufficiently strict — a finding that
would, in the real estate, tighten the change-history corroboration threshold for future
speciation candidates.

12.6 Why the ecology treats re-merger as a LEARNING event, not merely a rollback. Consistent with
Article IX's governed-self-evolution discipline (which this dataset's Part XXII treats fully),
the re-merger case is valuable specifically because it is a FALSIFIED prediction the ecology made
about its own detection mechanism — the speciation candidate's DIVERGENCE-CONFIRMED claim was
EXPERIMENTAL (Article V) and has now been REFUTED by the MEASURED-stage evidence, exactly the
epistemic lifecycle Part VI §6.4 already modeled for symbiosis's REFUTED case. A dataset that
hid or under-reported its own re-merger rate would be committing the SELECTIVE REPORTING failure
Part VI §6.4 named, now at the more consequential speciation grain, and this dataset's honest
position is that a non-zero re-merger rate is EXPECTED and HEALTHY evidence the calibration loop
is functioning, while a re-merger rate that never falls despite repeated calibration adjustments
would itself be the signal that the divergence test (Part XI §11.2) requires a more fundamental
redesign, not merely a threshold tightening.

12.7 A second, subtler fragmentation signature: ASYMMETRIC DECLINE. Beyond the symmetric pattern
of §12.3 (both successors declining, a dormant-mutualism flag re-emerging), a rarer but real
fragmentation signature is ASYMMETRIC DECLINE — one successor thrives while the other withers,
neither showing the classic symmetric decline pattern, because the original system's demand was
never actually balanced between its two apparent clusters to begin with; the "bimodal" landscape
that triggered the original speciation was itself an artifact of one cluster's demand being
chronically under-measured relative to the other, not two genuinely comparable, divergent
sub-capabilities. PREVENTION: the MEASURED-stage check (Part XII §12.3) must compare each
successor's post-split trajectory against a PROJECTED baseline derived from its pre-split cluster's
own fitness reading, not merely against the OTHER successor's trajectory — a successor that
independently underperforms its own pre-split projection is evidence of a problem regardless of how
its sibling successor is doing, and requires this Part's re-merger consideration even in the
absence of the symmetric-decline pattern §12.3 primarily describes.

12.8 Why a THIRD consecutive fragmentation on the same underlying niche escalates beyond automated
calibration. If a given niche has undergone speciation, re-merger (per this Part), and then a
SECOND speciation attempt that also fragments, this dataset's own calibration loop (§12.4) has now
failed to prevent the identical error twice on the same underlying capability — a pattern this
dataset treats as exceeding the ordinary calibration-feedback mechanism's remit, mirroring the
estate's own general 2-CONSECUTIVE-FAILURES discipline (a documented governance pattern this
dataset imports by reference rather than re-deriving) applied here at the speciation-mechanism
grain: a third attempt at the same split is not a candidate for a third round of automated
threshold tightening, it is a candidate for manual, Owner-level review of whether the underlying
niche is genuinely, irreducibly ambiguous in a way no divergence-test calibration can resolve — the
same escalation this dataset already names generally at Part XXIII §23.6 under SPECIATION CHURN,
now given its specific triggering condition.

12.9 Interfaces: what a confirmed fragmentation event hands to the anti-proliferation pole. A
re-merged pair, once fused back into a single system, becomes, by definition, a fresh instance of
whatever the ORIGINAL pre-speciation system's niche occupancy was — and the anti-proliferation
pole's substitutability check (Part XIX §19.3) should, in principle, find NO substitutable
duplicate for the re-merged system in the same niche, precisely because the split-and-rejoin
cycle leaves the population's occupancy count unchanged from before the original, mistaken
speciation. Confirming this null result — that a re-merger genuinely restores the pre-speciation
occupancy state rather than leaving some residual trace of the failed split behind (an orphaned
partial edge, a stale capability-signature fragment) — is itself a useful, low-cost cross-check an
implementation can run automatically at the moment a re-merger closes, catching any incomplete
rollback before it has a chance to compound into a second, unrelated anomaly.

FINAL LAW — PART XII. Fragmentation — a speciation approved on landscape shape without genuine
divergence evidence — is detectable within one to two cycles as either symmetric decline with a
re-emerging dormant-mutualism flag or asymmetric decline against each successor's own pre-split
projection, and the correct response is a provenance-tagged re-merger, cross-checked against a
clean return to the pre-speciation occupancy state, that feeds back into tightening the divergence
test itself, escalating to manual review rather than a third automated attempt if the same niche
fragments twice.

PART XIII — MERGER INTELLIGENCE: WHEN TO FUSE SYSTEMS
===============================================================================================

13.1 Merger intelligence as speciation's structural inverse, formalized against evolution_engine.
Where speciation (Part XI) splits one system whose internal logic path is straining under
genuinely divergent demand, MERGER INTELLIGENCE, DEFINED, is the ecological judgment that two (or
more) DISTINCT systems have come to occupy niches so tightly complementary, or so nearly
identical without full duplication, that maintaining them as separate systems costs the
population more — in coordination overhead, in duplicated maintenance burden, in the routing
confusion two near-identical entry points create for consumers — than fusing them into one system
would cost in the transition itself. Per the Non-Duplication Ledger's disposition for candidate
37 ("Merger Intelligence | evolution_engine.merge | one merge | when to fuse systems | SUBENGINE →
IAS-D1"), this dataset supplies the DETECTION and evidence-gathering that decides WHEN a merger is
warranted; evolution_engine's existing merge operation remains the EXECUTION mechanism, exactly
mirroring the D2A-authority-preserved pattern this dataset has applied consistently since Part I.

13.2 The three merger-candidate signatures. Merger candidates arise from three distinguishable
population states, each requiring a different confirmatory test before a merger recommendation is
warranted. CONVERGENT EVOLUTION (two systems, built independently for what were once distinct
niches, have each grown capability signatures that now substantially overlap — the COMPLETE or
near-COMPLETE overlap reading of Part X §10.3 — without either having been built as a duplicate of
the other; their overlap emerged over time as both systems' scope organically expanded toward the
same demand). CONFIRMED-SYMBIOSIS SATURATION (a symbiotic cluster, Part VI, has been CONFIRMED and
measured across enough cycles that the coordination cost of maintaining two systems wired together
via feeds and strengthens edges has itself become a measurable overhead — cross-system latency,
duplicated context-loading, or maintenance burden falling on two codebases instead of one — that
would be eliminated, not merely reduced, by fusing the pair). SUCCESSION-DRIVEN CONSOLIDATION (a
newer, fitter system has been absorbing an older system's niche via succession, Part XVII, to the
point that the older system's remaining capability signature is a thin residual shell better
absorbed into the successor outright than maintained as a separate, nearly-empty system).

13.3 Why merger intelligence requires a HIGHER evidence bar than the overlap measurement alone,
symmetric to speciation's asymmetric caution. Just as Part XI §11.3 held speciation to a higher
bar than symbiosis activation because fragmentation is costly and hard to reverse, merger carries
a symmetric risk this Part names FORCED CONSOLIDATION: fusing two systems whose overlap is real
but whose underlying capability logic remains genuinely distinct (a PARTIAL overlap misread as
COMPLETE, or a convergent-evolution candidate whose apparent overlap is measured only in the ONE
environment both currently serve, while each retains distinct, non-overlapping capability in
OTHER environments the current measurement window did not capture) produces a merged system that
re-triggers, almost immediately, the exact BIMODAL landscape signature Part XI's speciation
detector is built to catch — a merger that should never have happened becomes, within a cycle or
two, a speciation candidate to undo it, a wasted round trip through two costly event-grain
operations (D2A's specialize and evolution_engine's merge) that a stricter merger evidence bar
would have avoided entirely.

13.4 The merger evidence protocol. Following the same lifecycle-and-evidence discipline as
speciation, a merger candidate proceeds through CANDIDATE (one of the three §13.2 signatures is
observed), OVERLAP-CONFIRMED (the overlap or coordination-cost signal is corroborated across
MULTIPLE environments, not merely the one where it was first observed — the direct structural
inverse of speciation's multiple-cycle sustain requirement, applied here across environments
rather than across time, because a merger's core risk is under-sampling BREADTH while speciation's
core risk is under-sampling DURATION), PROPOSED (surfaced to evolution_engine with the full
evidence package — overlap measurements per environment, coordination-cost trend, and cause
signature per §13.2), and MEASURED (the merged system's post-fusion landscape is tracked across at
least one full cycle to confirm the predicted consolidation gain — reduced aggregate cost,
maintained or improved aggregate demand-satisfaction — actually materializes, with a REFUTED
outcome here signaling, symmetrically to Part XII's re-merger case, a candidate for RE-SPECIATION
rather than a permanent, unexamined fusion).

13.5 Worked example: convergent evolution detected and evidenced across multiple environments.
Consider Systems N and a new synthetic system, System X, illustrative only, where X was built
originally to serve a narrow "architecture-scenario cost estimation" niche and, over several
build cycles unrelated to any deliberate consolidation plan, has organically absorbed enough
general architecture-environment capability that its landscape now shows COMPLETE overlap with
System N's architecture-environment presence (recall Part II §2.6: N alone was WELL-COVERED there
originally) — a convergent-evolution signature. The overlap-confirmation stage checks a SECOND
environment neither system was originally built for but where both have, independently, begun
registering low-but-defined fitness (a "cross-environment cost modeling" niche): if BOTH systems
show comparable, substitutable presence there too, the merger evidence strengthens (true
convergence, not a single-environment coincidence); if only one shows presence there, the case
weakens toward PARTIAL overlap, and the merger candidate is downgraded, correctly, to a
competes-with relation the ecology may simply record (Part X's WELL-COVERED-with-mild-contest
reading) rather than propose fusing. In this worked trace, both N and X register presence in the
second environment, corroborating genuine convergence, and the case is PROPOSED to
evolution_engine with both environments' overlap measurements attached.

13.6 The residual-shell case, worked briefly. Returning to the succession-driven signature
(§13.2's third shape): where an older system's fitness has declined across a sustained trend not
because of the decay- or demand-driven causes Part VII §7.5 names for straightforward parasitism,
but because a NEWER system has been organically absorbing its consumers via succession (Part
XVII), the older system's residual capability signature — the sliver of demand its successor has
not yet fully absorbed — is a merger candidate distinct from a pure extinction candidate (Part XV):
extinction with fossilization is warranted when the older system's niche itself has gone vacant
of demand; merger-as-absorption is warranted when the demand persists but has functionally moved
to the successor, and formally fusing the residual shell into the successor (rather than a slow,
indefinite fade) closes the transition cleanly and removes the older system's continuing
maintenance burden and its lingering presence in the relation graph as a near-orphan.

13.7 Interfaces: what merger intelligence hands to succession and to genome. A CONFIRMED merger,
like a CONFIRMED speciation, is a genome-transfer event (Part XX): the fused system inherits BOTH
parents' confirmed fitness histories, confirmed symbiotic relationships (union, with duplicate
edges collapsed), and accumulated hard rules or principles from both — and, distinctly from
speciation's one-parent-two-children lineage shape, merger produces a two-parents-one-child
lineage shape, a distinction Part XXI's lineage-tracing worked example addresses directly because
the two shapes require different graph-traversal logic to reconstruct history from.

13.8 A boundary case: two systems eligible under MULTIPLE signatures simultaneously. A candidate
pair can, in principle, satisfy more than one of the three §13.2 signatures at once — a pair
showing both convergent-evolution overlap AND confirmed-symbiosis-saturation coordination cost,
for instance. This dataset's position is that MULTIPLE simultaneous signatures STRENGTHEN, rather
than complicate, the merger case: each signature is an independent line of evidence for the same
underlying conclusion (these two systems are better fused than separate), and the evidence
protocol's OVERLAP-CONFIRMED stage (§13.4) is satisfied at least as easily, never with additional
difficulty, when corroborated from more than one angle — the multi-environment requirement is a
floor, not a ceiling, and a pair clearing it via two independent signatures simultaneously is
strictly stronger evidence than a pair clearing it via one signature alone, a straightforward
consequence of Article V's evidence-accumulation discipline applied to this specific mechanism.

13.9 Why a THREE-way merger candidate is deliberately out of this dataset's initial scope. Every
worked example and every stage of the evidence protocol in this Part is specified for PAIRWISE
merger candidates. A population could, in principle, present a genuine three-or-more-system
merger candidate — three systems whose niches have all converged toward the same demand
simultaneously. This dataset does not extend the merger protocol to the n-ary case explicitly,
NORMATIVELY recommending instead that an n-ary candidate be resolved as a SEQUENCE of pairwise
merger events (merge the two most-overlapping first, then evaluate the resulting fused system
against the third as a fresh pairwise candidate), because sequencing preserves every safeguard this
Part already specifies — the MEASURED stage between each pairwise step confirms the fusion is
actually working before the next step compounds it — while a single, simultaneous three-way fusion
event would have no intermediate MEASURED checkpoint at all, precisely the kind of un-canaried,
high-blast-radius action Part XXII's governed-evolution discipline exists to prevent.

FINAL LAW — PART XIII. Merger intelligence detects convergent evolution, symbiosis-saturation
coordination overhead, and succession-driven residual shells as its three candidate signatures —
strengthened, never complicated, when more than one applies to the same pair simultaneously —
requires overlap confirmation across MULTIPLE environments before proposing fusion to
evolution_engine, resolves any apparent n-ary candidate as a sequence of canaried pairwise steps
rather than one simultaneous fusion, and treats a REFUTED post-merger measurement as evidence for
re-speciation, not as a fusion to be quietly left in place regardless of outcome.

PART XIV — MERGER FAILURE MODES: FORCED CONSOLIDATION AND THE ANTI-PATTERN OF SCOPE CREEP
===============================================================================================

14.1 FORCED CONSOLIDATION, elaborated beyond Part XIII §13.3's naming. A forced consolidation is
the merger-side mirror of speciation's fragmentation: two systems fused on single-environment
overlap evidence, absent the multi-environment corroboration Part XIII §13.4 requires, producing
one system whose capability signature now spans what were genuinely two distinct logic paths — the
identical structural condition speciation exists to correct, arrived at from the opposite
direction. The resulting merged system typically shows, within one to two post-merger cycles, the
BIMODAL landscape signature (Part III §3.4) directly, because the fusion did not eliminate the
underlying divergence, it merely relabeled two systems as one without resolving the logic-path
tension that made them distinct to begin with.

14.2 SCOPE CREEP, a distinct merger anti-pattern from forced consolidation. Where forced
consolidation is a single bad merger decision, SCOPE CREEP, DEFINED, is a SLOW-ACCUMULATING
failure mode in which a system that survives repeated, individually-reasonable partial mergers or
residual-shell absorptions (Part XIII §13.6) over many cycles gradually accretes capability
signature breadth far beyond what any single merger decision, examined alone, would have
appeared to risk — each individual merger passed the overlap-confirmation bar, yet the CUMULATIVE
effect is a system whose capability signature has become so broad that it is, in practice,
indistinguishable from the FOUNDATION MISREAD pattern (Part VIII §8.3) despite having none of a
genuine foundation's structural width-without-depth justification — it is simply large, not
architecturally wide-by-design.

14.3 Why scope creep evades the per-merger evidence protocol and requires a separate, population-
level detector. Part XIII §13.4's overlap-confirmation stage is, by construction, LOCAL to each
individual merger candidate — it correctly evaluates whether THIS proposed fusion clears the bar,
but it has no visibility into how many PRIOR fusions the receiving system has already absorbed.
A system that has undergone four residual-shell absorptions over eight cycles, each individually
well-evidenced, requires a distinct, POPULATION-GRAIN check: a CUMULATIVE ABSORPTION COUNT tracked
per system across its full lineage (Part XX), flagged when it crosses a population-relative
threshold this dataset does not fix numerically (per Article VIII) but specifies as a required
gate — at minimum, any system whose absorption count places it in the top decile of the current
population triggers a SCOPE-CREEP REVIEW, distinct from and in addition to the ordinary landscape-
based speciation candidacy check of Part XI, because a scope-crept system may not yet show a
clean bimodal shape (its many small absorptions may blend into a smeared, multi-modal rather than
cleanly bimodal landscape) even though it has, in aggregate, become exactly the kind of
overloaded, internally-conflicted system speciation exists to correct.

14.4 The relationship between scope creep and parasitic complexity's mandatory-path-inflation
shape. A scope-crept system frequently, though not necessarily, co-occurs with the MANDATORY-PATH
INFLATION parasitic signature (Part VII §7.2): as a system accretes more of the population's
absorbed niches, more OTHER systems come to depend on it (depends-on edges accumulate, IAS-F1
Part VI §6.3) simply because there are fewer alternative paths left in the population, not because
the accreting system's demand-satisfaction has genuinely earned that dependency. This co-
occurrence is not coincidental: both patterns are downstream consequences of a population allowed
to consolidate without a standing scope-creep check, which is precisely why this Part's detector
(§14.3) and Part VII's parasitism detector are specified as SIBLING mechanisms sharing the
cumulative-absorption and relational-position data streams, rather than independent checks that
might each individually pass a system the other would have flagged.

14.5 Worked example: scope creep detected via cumulative absorption count, distinct from a clean
bimodal reading. Consider a synthetic System Y, illustrative only, that has absorbed three
residual shells over six cycles (per Part XIII §13.6's mechanism, each individually well-
evidenced against a genuinely declining predecessor), plus one convergent-evolution merger (Part
XIII §13.2's first signature). System Y's landscape, examined cycle-by-cycle in isolation, shows
no single, clean BIMODAL split — its fitness across its now-eight served environments is uneven
but not sharply two-clustered, which would let it pass Part XI §11.2's divergence test as
"merely uneven" rather than "genuinely divergent" if evaluated by landscape shape alone. The
cumulative absorption count, however, places System Y in the population's top decile, triggering
the SCOPE-CREEP REVIEW independently of the speciation pathway — a review that, in this worked
trace, recommends a STAGED SPECIATION (splitting Y not into two but into three successors, each
absorbing a coherent cluster of its eight environments, an operation this dataset specifies as a
generalization of Part XI's pairwise split, executed as multiple sequential D2A specialize
operations rather than one).

14.6 Why scope creep is named as its own anti-pattern rather than folded entirely into speciation.
The distinction matters operationally: speciation's trigger (Part XI) is a LANDSCAPE-SHAPE signal,
readable in a single evaluation cycle once sustained; scope creep's trigger (§14.3) is a LINEAGE-
DEPTH signal, readable only by tracking absorption history across many cycles, and a population
that checked ONLY landscape shape would systematically miss scope-crept systems whose accretion
was gradual enough that no single cycle's shape ever crossed the bimodal threshold — the classic
boiling-frog failure of incremental, individually-justified decisions compounding into an
unjustified aggregate state, a pattern this dataset's Part XXIII failure catalogue names generally
and this Part instantiates specifically for the merger pathway.

14.7 Why the cumulative-absorption count must be measured against the POPULATION'S current
distribution, never against a fixed historical baseline. A population that has, over its whole
history, grown substantially will naturally show higher absorption counts across the board than a
young, small population — an implementation that compared every system's absorption count against
a FIXED number carried forward from an early evaluation cycle would drift toward flagging an
ever-larger share of the population as the estate matures, a spurious result with no relationship
to genuine scope creep. §14.3's "top decile of the current population" language is deliberate on
this point: the threshold is always relative to the population's OWN current distribution,
recomputed every evaluation cycle exactly as the fitness landscape's within-environment
normalization (Part IV §4.2) is, ensuring the scope-creep detector scales correctly as the
estate's own system count grows or shrinks over the corpus's lifetime.

14.8 The relationship between scope creep and the biodiversity governor's anti-monoculture pole,
made explicit. A scope-crept system, by definition accumulating more of the population's absorbed
niches over time, is a NATURAL PRECURSOR to the anti-monoculture pole's own dependency-
concentration flag (Part XVIII §18.4) — as System Y (§14.5) accretes more served environments, more
downstream systems come to hold depends-on edges to it, simply because fewer alternative paths
remain in the population. This dataset positions the SCOPE-CREEP REVIEW (§14.3) as the EARLIER-
STAGE detector — catching accretion via lineage depth before it necessarily manifests as a
structural dependency-concentration problem — and the anti-monoculture check (Part XVIII) as the
LATER-STAGE detector catching the same underlying accretion once it has actually produced
population-wide structural risk, giving the estate two independent chances to catch the same
underlying pathology at two different points in its development, a deliberately redundant defense-
in-depth this dataset treats as a feature rather than an inefficiency, consistent with this
dataset's own anti-monoculture reasoning that redundancy against a genuine structural risk is worth
its coordination cost.

14.9 Why a STAGED SPECIATION (§14.5's recommended remedy) is specified as a SEQUENCE of pairwise
operations rather than a single n-way split, mirroring Part XIII §13.9's identical restraint on the
merger side. A scope-crept system split into three or more successors in one simultaneous
operation would have no intermediate MEASURED checkpoint between successors — exactly the
un-canaried, high-blast-radius pattern Part XIII §13.9 already rules out for n-ary mergers. This
dataset applies the identical restraint here: a staged speciation proceeds as a sequence of
PAIRWISE splits, each carrying its own full evidence protocol (Part XI §11.2's divergence
corroboration, independently satisfied at each stage) and its own MEASURED checkpoint before the
next split proceeds — splitting the most clearly divergent cluster off first, remeasuring, and only
then evaluating whether the REMAINING system still shows a genuine bimodal (or now trimodal-minus-
one) signature warranting a further split, rather than committing to a fixed final successor count
up front. This sequencing is what lets a staged speciation inherit every one of this dataset's
existing safeguards — the divergence test, the MEASURED-stage falsifiability, the re-merger
rollback — without requiring a parallel, n-ary-specific evidence protocol this dataset would
otherwise have to specify and maintain separately.

FINAL LAW — PART XIV. Forced consolidation is a single bad merger surfacing as a bimodal landscape
within a cycle or two; scope creep is many individually-justified mergers compounding, over many
cycles, into an overloaded system no single landscape check would catch — measured always against
the population's own current distribution, never a fixed historical baseline, and corrected, where
warranted, by a staged sequence of pairwise splits rather than one simultaneous n-way operation —
and this Part's early lineage-grain detector, together with the anti-monoculture pole's later
structural detector, gives the estate two independent chances to catch the identical underlying
accretion pathology before it becomes indistinguishable from a genuine, deliberately-built
foundation.

PART XV — EXTINCTION AND FOSSILIZATION: RETIRING A SYSTEM WHILE PRESERVING ITS USEFUL LAW
===============================================================================================

15.1 Extinction, defined against D2A's RETIRE disposition and CO-06's cleanup mechanism. Per the
Non-Duplication Ledger's candidate 38 ("Extinction | D2A RETIRE, CO-06 | one retire | retire
preserving useful law | SUBENGINE → IAS-D1"), the estate already possesses the EXECUTION
mechanisms for removing a system — D2A's RETIRE verdict and CO-06's garbage-collection cleanup.
EXTINCTION, as this dataset's object, DEFINED, is the ecological judgment of WHEN a system's
continued existence no longer justifies its ensemble cost (the demand-driven parasitism case of
Part VII §7.5, most directly, though not exclusively — a system whose niche has been fully
absorbed by succession, Part XVII, is equally an extinction candidate even absent a parasitism
flag) together with the FOSSILIZATION mechanism that ensures the system's validated, reusable law
survives its own removal — the specific, named addition this dataset makes beyond what RETIRE and
CO-06's cleanup, alone, would otherwise silently discard.

15.2 Why fossilization is the load-bearing addition, not extinction judgment itself. D2A's RETIRE
disposition and CO-06's cleanup are, on their own, sufficient to REMOVE a system's bytes, its
relation-graph edges, and its consumption of ensemble resources. Neither, as OBSERVED from the
ledger's own overlap note ("one retire"), carries a standing obligation to ask: of everything this
system learned, validated, or established as a working principle over its lifetime, what should
SURVIVE its removal, in a form some OTHER system or the estate's own knowledge base can still use?
Without an explicit fossilization step, a retired system's accumulated, hard-won law — a
validated heuristic, a confirmed hard rule, a proven capability boundary — is lost by default,
not by decision, the moment its bytes are removed, which is precisely the silent knowledge-loss
failure this dataset names FOSSIL LOSS in Part XXIII's catalogue and prevents by making
fossilization a MANDATORY, not optional, companion to every extinction event this dataset
recommends.

15.3 The fossil record's content: what is extracted before removal. FOSSILIZATION, DEFINED, is
the extraction, at the moment a system is confirmed for extinction, of three categories of
durable content from the system's own lifetime record. VALIDATED PRINCIPLES (any HARD RULE, per
the estate's existing hard-rules mechanism, or ACIS-level LAW the retiring system's own operation
established or confirmed, per Article V's OBSERVED or DERIVED evidence levels — these transfer
directly into the estate's hard_rules and ACIS registries, unchanged in status, with a provenance
tag noting their origin system is now retired). CONFIRMED RELATIONS (any symbiosis the retiring
system participated in as a CONFIRMED member, Part VI, is recorded in the fossil record as
evidence for the CALIBRATION LOOP even though the relation itself necessarily lapses with the
system's removal — a confirmed mutualism between a retiring system and a survivor is data about
the survivor's OWN validated capability-signature complementarity, useful to future symbiosis
detection even after one half of the original pair is gone). CAPABILITY-SIGNATURE BOUNDARY
KNOWLEDGE (the specific, OBSERVED shape of what the retiring system's niche did and did NOT cover
— a negative fact, but a load-bearing one, because it prevents a future system, built to fill the
now-vacant niche, from re-discovering the same boundary the retiring system already learned
through its own operational history, an efficiency the Cognitive Multiplier, IAS-B1, would
otherwise have to rediscover from scratch).

15.4 The extinction decision protocol, respecting D2A's authority throughout. Following the
established pattern, the ecology's extinction mechanism produces an EXTINCTION CANDIDATE record —
carrying the parasitism adjudication (Part VIII, where applicable), the demand-driven cause
classification (Part VII §7.5), and, distinctly, a PRE-FOSSILIZED CONTENT PACKAGE (§15.3's three
categories, already extracted and ready for handoff) — and surfaces this complete package to D2A
for the actual RETIRE verdict. This ordering matters: fossilization is specified to occur BEFORE
D2A's verdict is finalized, not after, because a system whose retire verdict has already executed
may have its bytes and, more importantly, its OWN internal change-history record already removed
by the time a post-hoc fossilization attempt would run, risking the fossil record itself being
built from incomplete or already-purged source material — the specific sequencing hazard this
Part's protocol exists to prevent.

15.5 Worked example: a demand-driven extinction with a full fossil record. Returning to System U
(Part VIII §8.7), suppose its MIXED-CAUSE adjudication, upon further evidence gathering across two
additional cycles, resolves cleanly toward demand-driven (the decay-side signal, on closer
inspection, turns out to have been a downstream effect of the SAME shrinking demand — fewer real
missions meant fewer opportunities to exercise and thereby maintain quality, not an independent
decay process). System U is proposed as an extinction candidate. Fossilization extracts: one hard
rule U had established regarding a specific failure-handling edge case (transferred to hard_rules
with provenance noting U's now-retired status), the record of U's earlier CONFIRMED commensal
relationship with a since-adjacent system (useful calibration data even though the relationship
itself lapses), and the precise boundary of U's niche — specifically, which sub-variant of its
environment's demand U did and did not cover, preventing a future replacement system from having
to re-learn that boundary through its own trial and error. This complete package, not merely a
bare RETIRE recommendation, is what the ecology hands to D2A.

15.6 Why extinction is asymmetric with speciation and merger in its reversibility posture. Unlike
speciation (reversible via re-merger, Part XII) and merger (reversible via re-speciation, Part
XIII §13.4), extinction, once executed, is NOT symmetrically reversible by an equivalent "re-
admission" operation, because the system's bytes and operational context are, by CO-06's cleanup
mechanism, actually removed rather than merely relabeled. (NORMATIVE: this asymmetry is precisely
why fossilization is MANDATORY rather than optional for every extinction this dataset recommends —
a mistaken speciation or merger can be corrected by the population itself, through the ecology's
own detection mechanisms, within a few cycles; a mistaken extinction without a fossil record can
only be corrected by rebuilding the lost knowledge from scratch, which is a strictly worse outcome
than either fragmentation or forced consolidation, and the asymmetry is the specific justification
for holding extinction to a fossilization requirement neither speciation nor merger carries.)

15.7 Fossilization's own failure mode: INCOMPLETE EXTRACTION, and why it requires a completeness
check distinct from the extraction procedure itself. Because fossilization's three categories
(§15.3) draw on different source records — hard_rules and ACIS for validated principles, the
symbiosis lifecycle log (Part VI §6.1) for confirmed relations, and the system's own operational
history for boundary knowledge — an extraction process that successfully pulls from two of the
three sources while silently failing on the third (a source record that is itself stale, corrupted,
or already partially purged by an over-eager cleanup step running concurrently) produces an
INCOMPLETE fossil package that LOOKS complete unless explicitly checked. PREVENTION: this dataset
specifies that a fossil package must carry an explicit completeness marker per category — VALIDATED-
PRINCIPLES-EXTRACTED, CONFIRMED-RELATIONS-EXTRACTED, BOUNDARY-KNOWLEDGE-EXTRACTED, each
independently TRUE, FALSE, or UNRESOLVED — rather than a single boolean "fossilized: yes," because a
single boolean would let a two-out-of-three extraction silently pass as complete, exactly the kind
of collapsed, falsely-reassuring signal Part IV §4.1's per-component evidence-level discipline was
built to prevent, applied here to the fossilization procedure specifically.

15.8 Why the fossil record's provenance tag must survive even a subsequent SCOPE-CREEP-triggered
re-absorption of the successor that received it. If a fossilized principle is transferred into a
successor system (via the residual-shell merger pathway, Part XIII §13.6) and that successor is
later itself absorbed into a THIRD system through further consolidation (the scope-creep pathway,
Part XIV), the ORIGINAL provenance tag — naming the FIRST, now long-retired ancestor system the
principle actually originated from — must be carried forward through every subsequent transfer,
never overwritten with only the most recent intermediate holder's identity. This is the specific
mechanism by which the lineage graph (Part XX §20.3) and the fossil record together prevent a
multi-hop chain of consolidations from erasing a principle's true origin, which matters concretely
for the continuity-of-law cross-check (Part XVII §17.2): auditing whether a given principle's
inheritance is complete requires knowing its ORIGINAL source, not merely its most recent one.

FINAL LAW — PART XV. D2A's RETIRE and CO-06's cleanup remove a system; this dataset adds the one
thing neither does on its own — a mandatory, pre-verdict fossilization extracting validated
principles, confirmed-relation calibration data, and niche-boundary knowledge, each tracked to
independent completeness and each carrying its ORIGINAL provenance through any number of
subsequent transfers — because extinction, unlike speciation or merger, has no equivalent reversal
operation, and unfossilized or incompletely-fossilized law lost at retirement is lost for good.

PART XVI — SUCCESSION: GRADUAL SYSTEM REPLACEMENT WITHOUT A GOVERNANCE GAP
===============================================================================================

16.1 Succession, defined against the pp_dataset model-succession precedent. Per the Non-
Duplication Ledger's candidate 39 ("Succession | pp_dataset model succession | model swap | MERGE
→ IAS-D1"), the estate already recognizes a narrower form of succession scoped to model or
toolchain swaps for a single dataset's own implementation. SUCCESSION, as this dataset's broader
object, DEFINED, is the population-level pattern in which a NEWER system gradually absorbs an
OLDER system's niche — consumer by consumer, environment by environment — over multiple
evaluation cycles, such that the older system's relevance fades not through a single decision but
through an accumulating shift in the landscape itself, and the ecology's job is to TRACK this
gradual shift explicitly, so that the transition is GOVERNED (visible, measured, and eventually
resolved via merger or extinction) rather than left as an invisible, ungoverned drift.

16.2 Why succession requires standing tracking, not a one-time detection. Unlike speciation and
merger, which trigger on a landscape shape or overlap measurement CROSSING a threshold at a
particular cycle, succession is, by its nature, a TREND spanning many cycles before it resolves
into an actionable recommendation — the older system's fitness declines gradually, the newer
system's fitness in the SAME environment rises gradually, and no single cycle's reading, examined
alone, would justify action. The ecology therefore maintains a SUCCESSION TRACKER: for every
(older-system, newer-system, shared-environment) triple where both systems show DEFINED,
non-trivial landscape presence and a directionally opposite fitness trend over a rolling window of
cycles, the tracker records the trend's slope, its consistency (how many consecutive cycles
confirm the direction, following Part VII §7.2's "sustained" discipline), and a PROJECTED
CROSSOVER cycle — the point at which, if the trend continues, the newer system's fitness would
exceed the older system's in this environment.

16.3 The three succession outcomes, and why the tracker does not force a single resolution path.
A tracked succession trend can resolve three ways, and the tracker's job is to surface the trend,
not to predetermine which resolution applies. FULL ABSORPTION (the older system's presence in the
shared environment eventually reaches UNDEFINED — not merely low, but genuinely absent, following
Part III §3.6's distinction — while its OTHER environments, if any, remain served; this resolves
as a residual-shell merger candidate, Part XIII §13.6, if the older system retains other served
environments worth preserving, or an extinction candidate, Part XV, if the shared environment was
its only one). PLATEAU (the trend slows and stabilizes before reaching full absorption — both
systems settle into a stable, moderate coexistence, at which point the tracker reclassifies the
pair from "succession in progress" to an ordinary CONTESTED niche entry in Part X's ledger, because
the succession pattern, in this outcome, was better read as the population finding a genuine
two-occupant equilibrium than a true handoff). REVERSAL (the trend's direction flips — the
presumed "older" system's fitness recovers, typically because it was repaired or received a fitness-
improving symbiosis activation, Part VI, mid-trend — at which point the succession label is
retracted entirely, and the tracker's own honest handling of reversal is what distinguishes it
from a naive extrapolation that would have prematurely recommended retiring a system that was, in
fact, about to recover).

16.4 Worked example: a succession trend tracked across five cycles to a projected crossover. A
synthetic pair — System Z (older, established) and System AA (newer, admitted three cycles after
Z) — both register defined presence in a shared "estate-wide log correlation" environment. Cycle-
by-cycle, Z's fitness there declines from HIGH toward MODERATE while AA's rises from LOW-just-
admitted toward MODERATE-approaching-HIGH, a consistent, five-cycle trend the tracker records with
HIGH confidence (five consecutive cycles exceeds the sustained-trend bar) and projects a crossover
within two further cycles at the observed slope. The tracker does not, at this stage, recommend
extinguishing Z or merging AA into it — it surfaces the tracked trend, with its projected
crossover and confidence level, to whichever governance surface consumes succession data (the
Compounding Graph's foresight loop, via IAS-C2, which the Strengthening Matrix names as a
consumer of exactly this kind of forward-looking signal), leaving the RESOLUTION judgment — full
absorption, plateau, or reversal — to unfold across the actual subsequent cycles rather than being
forced prematurely by the projection alone.

16.5 Why succession is distinct from ordinary competition (Part X) despite superficial similarity.
Two systems sharing an environment with a competes-with relation (IAS-F1 Part VI §6.4) are, by
default, simply CONTESTED occupants (Part X §10.4) unless a directional trend emerges — the
distinguishing feature succession requires is DIRECTIONALITY over TIME, not merely coexistence at
one cycle. A pair that has coexisted at stable, comparable fitness across many cycles with no
consistent trend either way is correctly read as healthy competition (Part X's WELL-COVERED-
via-diversity reading, feeding the biodiversity governor's anti-monoculture pole, Part XVIII,
favorably) and must NOT be mislabeled as a succession-in-progress merely because two systems share
a niche — a mislabeling this dataset's tracker avoids by requiring the multi-cycle directional-
consistency test of §16.2 before a pair enters the succession tracker at all.

16.6 The governance-gap this Part exists to close. Absent standing succession tracking, the
estate's default failure mode, OBSERVED as a general pattern across institutions that lack such
tracking, is that an older system's decline is noticed only reactively — when a mission fails
against it, when its cost becomes visibly wasteful, or when someone happens to compare it against
its newer rival by accident — producing a governance gap during which the older system continues
consuming ensemble resources for a niche it has, in substance, already lost, while the newer
system's own rise goes correspondingly unrecognized and under-resourced. The succession tracker's
entire value is closing this gap PROACTIVELY, converting an invisible drift into a visible,
dated, confidence-scored trend that other systems (Part XXVI's integration with IAS-G1 and IAS-
C1) can act on before the gap's cost accumulates unmanaged.

16.7 Multi-way succession: when THREE OR MORE systems compete in a directional pattern
simultaneously. Real populations, over enough cycles, can present a pattern where an OLDER system
declines while TWO OR MORE newer systems simultaneously rise in the same shared environment,
rather than the clean pairwise pattern §16.2-16.4 describe. This dataset extends the tracker to
this case by recording MULTIPLE (older, newer, environment) triples sharing the same older system
and environment, rather than forcing an artificial pairwise reduction — each newer system's own
trend is tracked independently against the same declining baseline, and the eventual FULL
ABSORPTION outcome (§16.3), if it occurs, may resolve as either a single newer system absorbing the
full niche or as a NICHE-SPLIT absorption where two or more newer systems each absorb a genuinely
different sub-slice of the older system's former demand — a pattern this dataset treats as evidence
the older system's original niche was, in fact, broader or more internally heterogeneous than any
single successor's capability signature, an insight that itself feeds the speciation detector
(Part XI) as corroborating evidence should the older system's own landscape, prior to its full
absorption, have shown early bimodal signs that a pairwise succession-only reading would have
missed.

16.8 The tracker's relationship to the niche-vacancy registry during an in-progress succession. A
tracked succession trend, while still in progress (before reaching FULL ABSORPTION, PLATEAU, or
REVERSAL), must NOT be misread by the niche-discovery mechanism (Part IX) as evidence of a vacancy
forming — the older system's declining-but-still-DEFINED presence means the niche remains, by Part
III §3.6's own discipline, occupied rather than UNDEFINED, and Part IX's own genuine-vacancy triage
(§9.3) already excludes this case by requiring UNDEFINED rather than merely low-and-declining
fitness before a cell is even eligible for vacancy candidacy — this cross-reference is named here
explicitly so an implementer building both mechanisms understands they are already structurally
protected from double-counting an in-progress succession as a false vacancy signal, without either
mechanism needing to query the other's internal state directly.

FINAL LAW — PART XVI. Succession is a multi-cycle, directional fitness trend between two or more
systems sharing an environment, tracked explicitly — including the niche-split case where several
newer systems absorb genuinely different sub-slices of one declining older system's demand — so
the transition is governed rather than invisible; it resolves to full absorption, a stable plateau,
or an honest reversal, and the tracker's job is to surface the trend with its confidence and
projected crossover, never to force the resolution before the evidence actually settles it.

PART XVII — SUCCESSION'S INTERACTION WITH GENOME, AND THE CONTINUITY-OF-LAW REQUIREMENT
===============================================================================================

17.1 Why succession needs a dedicated genome treatment distinct from Part XV's fossilization. A
system fully absorbed via succession (Part XVI §16.3's FULL ABSORPTION outcome) is not always
routed through the extinction pathway's mandatory fossilization (Part XV) — where the succession
resolves as a residual-shell MERGER (Part XIII §13.6) rather than an outright retirement, the
older system's law transfers via the MERGER genome-inheritance mechanism (Part XIII §13.7)
instead of the extinction fossil record, and this Part exists to confirm the two transfer paths
are EQUIVALENT in their completeness — that no validated principle, confirmed relation, or
boundary knowledge is lost merely because succession happened to resolve via merger rather than
via extinction.

17.2 The continuity-of-law requirement, stated as an invariant on the ecology's own mechanisms.
NORMATIVE: whichever of the two resolution paths a given succession trend takes — merger-as-
absorption (Part XIII §13.6) or extinction-with-fossilization (Part XV) — the THREE categories of
durable content Part XV §15.3 names (validated principles, confirmed-relation calibration data,
capability-boundary knowledge) must be preserved with equivalent completeness in either path. This
is not a new mechanism this Part builds; it is a CROSS-CHECK this Part imposes on the two
mechanisms Parts XIII and XV already specify, closing a gap that would otherwise exist if merger's
genome inheritance (Part XIII §13.7, focused on fitness history and symbiotic relations) were read
as a lighter-weight substitute for extinction's fossilization (Part XV §15.3, additionally
extracting capability-boundary knowledge) rather than as an equally complete transfer.

17.3 Worked example: verifying continuity-of-law across a succession that resolves via merger. Return
to System Z and System AA (Part XVI §16.4), and suppose the tracked trend reaches its projected
crossover, with Z's remaining presence in the shared environment now UNDEFINED while Z retains one
other served environment — the residual-shell merger condition of Part XIII §13.6. The merger
genome-inheritance mechanism (Part XIII §13.7) transfers Z's confirmed fitness history and
symbiotic relations into AA directly. This Part's continuity-of-law cross-check additionally
verifies: did Z accumulate any validated hard rule or ACIS-level principle during its operation
that the merger mechanism, as specified, would not automatically capture? In this worked trace,
Z had, in fact, established one such principle — a boundary condition under which the shared
environment's demand should escalate rather than proceed automatically — and the cross-check
confirms this principle is EXPLICITLY carried into AA's own governing record as part of the
merger's completion, not silently dropped because the transfer happened to occur via merger's
fitness-and-relations-focused mechanism rather than extinction's principles-focused one.

17.4 Why this cross-check matters more for succession specifically than for ordinary merger or
extinction in isolation. An ORDINARY merger candidate (Part XIII, arising from convergent
evolution rather than succession) fuses two systems that were, by construction, built somewhat
independently and may never have accumulated much system-specific validated law worth
transferring beyond fitness history. A succession-driven merger, by contrast, involves an OLDER
system that has typically operated longer, and an older system has had correspondingly more
opportunity to accumulate exactly the kind of validated, hard-won principle this dataset treats as
its highest-value fossilizable content — which means the continuity-of-law risk is SYSTEMATICALLY
HIGHER for succession-resolved mergers than for convergent-evolution-resolved mergers, and this
Part's dedicated cross-check exists specifically because that risk differential would otherwise go
unaddressed by Part XIII's general merger mechanism alone.

17.5 The succession tracker's own retirement. A final, minor but necessary specification: once a
tracked succession trend resolves — via any of the three outcomes in Part XVI §16.3 — the tracker
entry itself is closed and archived, not left open indefinitely. A closed tracker entry becomes
part of the CALIBRATION RECORD (mirroring Part VI §6.2 and Part XII §12.4's calibration loops):
did the PROJECTED CROSSOVER cycle (Part XVI §16.4) match the actual resolution cycle, and did the
resolution match the outcome the trend's directionality would have predicted? A tracker whose
projections consistently overshoot or undershoot the actual crossover is evidence the tracking
mechanism's own trend-slope calculation requires recalibration, exactly the same EXPERIMENTAL-
until-checked discipline Part IV §4.3 applied to the fitness composition weighting, now applied to
the succession tracker's projection accuracy.

17.6 Interfaces: what succession, closed and archived, hands to the rest of the estate. A closed
succession record, regardless of resolution path, becomes part of the LINEAGE this dataset
formalizes fully in Part XX — the successor system's genome now carries not only its own
operational history but a documented, evidenced account of the specific older system it absorbed,
the trend that predicted the absorption, and the calibration verdict on how accurately that trend
was tracked. This lineage entry is what lets a future observer, or a future IAS-B2 (Insight
Synthesis) cross-dataset read, trace WHY a given system's capability signature includes a
particular sub-capability — not merely THAT it does — closing a provenance gap that a system's
bare capability signature (IAS-F1 Part IX) alone cannot answer.

17.7 Why succession's continuity-of-law obligation extends to the NICHE-SPLIT case named at Part
XVI §16.7, and what changes when it does. Where a succession trend resolves as a niche-split
absorption — several newer systems each absorbing a different sub-slice of one declining older
system's former demand — the continuity-of-law cross-check (§17.2) cannot simply hand the OLDER
system's full genome to any single successor, because no single successor's divergence-evidenced
sub-slice (mirroring the apportionment discipline Part XI §11.6 already established for
speciation) corresponds to the whole of what the older system validated. This dataset resolves the
case by treating a niche-split succession as functionally equivalent, for genome-transfer purposes,
to a SPECIATION event performed on the OLDER system's own historical record rather than on its
live landscape — each successor inherits the APPORTIONED subset of the older system's validated
principles that corresponds to the specific sub-slice of demand it absorbed, using the identical
apportionment logic Part XI §11.6 already specifies, rather than requiring this Part to invent a
second, parallel apportionment mechanism for the succession pathway specifically.

17.8 The auditability requirement: why a continuity-of-law verification must be independently
re-runnable, not a one-time check performed and discarded. Because the risk this Part identifies
(§17.4's systematically-higher risk for succession-resolved mergers) is a STRUCTURAL property of
the succession pathway rather than a one-off concern for any single resolution event, the
continuity-of-law cross-check is specified as a PROCEDURE any auditor can re-run against a closed
succession record at any later time, reading the same lifecycle log (Part VI §6.1's append-only
discipline, applied here to the succession tracker's own closed entries per Part XVII §17.5) rather
than trusting a one-time PASS/FAIL verdict recorded at closure time and never revisited. This
matters concretely because a later discovery — a previously-unknown validated principle surfacing
in some other part of the estate's records, attributable to the retired older system — must be
checkable against the successor's actual inherited genome years after the fact, not merely at the
moment of transfer, the same forward-compatibility discipline IAS-F1 Part XII's provenance model
already establishes for the estate's property records generally, applied here to the succession-
specific genome-transfer obligation this Part supplies.

17.9 Why the continuity-of-law cross-check's cost is bounded, not open-ended, despite requiring
re-runnability. A reasonable concern about §17.8's re-runnable-audit requirement is unbounded
storage or computation cost accumulating as the estate's history grows. This dataset addresses the
concern directly: the audit's cost is bounded by the SAME lifecycle-log structure every other
mechanism already relies on (Part VI §6.1's append-only discipline) — re-running the check means
re-reading a FINITE, already-stored log slice bounded by the specific succession event's own start
and close markers (Part XVI §16.4's tracker-open and Part XVII §17.5's tracker-close), never a scan
of the estate's entire history. The audit's cost therefore scales with the SIZE of one succession
event's own record, not with the estate's cumulative age, which is the specific design property
that makes re-runnability a genuinely cheap guarantee rather than a theoretical one this dataset
asserts without regard for its own implementation cost.

FINAL LAW — PART XVII. Succession's resolution — whether via merger, extinction, or a niche-split
absorption apportioned using the same logic speciation already specifies — must preserve validated
principles, confirmed-relation data, and boundary knowledge with EQUAL completeness regardless of
path, checkable by an independently re-runnable audit bounded in cost to one event's own log slice
rather than a one-time verdict; a closed tracker entry becomes a calibration data point testing the
tracker's own projection accuracy, and, together with the resolution's genome transfer, becomes the
successor's documented lineage.

===============================================================================================
PART XVIII — THE BIODIVERSITY GOVERNOR, POLE ONE: ANTI-MONOCULTURE
===============================================================================================

18.1 Biodiversity, defined as a population-level property distinct from raw system count. Per the
Non-Duplication Ledger's candidate 40 ("Biodiversity Governor | (none) | avoid monoculture and
chaotic proliferation | MERGE → IAS-D1"), this mechanism has no direct estate parent and is a
genuine BUILD-class addition within the ecology family. BIODIVERSITY, DEFINED, is a measure of how
many GENUINELY DISTINCT capability-signature clusters (not raw system count, which Part XIV
§14.3's scope-creep detector already shows can inflate without adding real distinctness) the
population maintains across its niches, together with how EVENLY distributed the population's
relational weight (inbound feeds and strengthens edges, and — most critically for this Part —
depends-on edges, IAS-F1 Part VI §6.3) is across those clusters rather than concentrated in one.

18.2 MONOCULTURE, defined as this Part's specific target condition. A MONOCULTURE, DEFINED, is a
population state in which a SINGLE system, or a small, tightly-coupled cluster of systems sharing
near-identical failure modes, holds a disproportionate share of the population's depends-on edges
across MULTIPLE environments — meaning a large fraction of the estate's missions would fail
simultaneously, not merely degrade, if that one system or cluster were compromised, decayed, or
became unavailable. Monoculture is a DIFFERENT condition from the MANDATORY-PATH INFLATION
parasitic signature (Part VII §7.2's third shape): mandatory-path inflation is about ONE system's
demand-satisfaction failing while consumers have no alternative; monoculture is about the
POPULATION's structural fragility to a single point of failure REGARDLESS of that system's current
demand-satisfaction quality — a system can be excellent AND still constitute a monoculture risk,
because excellence today does not protect against a future decay, compromise, or unavailability
event the population has, structurally, no redundancy to absorb.

18.3 Why anti-monoculture is a distinct pole from mere diversity-counting. A population could show
a high RAW count of distinct systems while still exhibiting monoculture at the STRUCTURAL level —
many systems, but nearly all of them holding a depends-on edge to the SAME single upstream system,
such as a shared knowledge-lookup or shared routing primitive, means the population's apparent
diversity is a facade over a single structural chokepoint. The anti-monoculture check, therefore,
is computed over the DEPENDS-ON SUBGRAPH specifically (IAS-F1 Part VI §6.3), not over raw system
count or even over the competes-with clusters Part X's ledger already tracks, because competition
diversity (many systems competing for one niche) and structural resilience diversity (few or no
systems representing a single point of catastrophic failure) are measuring genuinely different
things, and a governor that conflated them would celebrate competitive redundancy while missing a
structural chokepoint sitting one layer beneath it.

18.4 The monoculture index and its threshold discipline. The anti-monoculture check computes, for
every system, its DEPENDENCY CONCENTRATION — the count of distinct downstream systems and
environments that hold a depends-on edge to it, weighted by how many of those downstream systems
have NO alternative depends-on path to an equivalent capability (a system with a redundant
fallback is a lower monoculture risk than one whose downstream consumers have exactly one
structural path to the capability it provides). A system whose dependency concentration exceeds a
population-relative threshold (again, per Article VIII, specified as a required gate rather than a
fixed number this dataset asserts without calibration) is flagged as a MONOCULTURE RISK,
regardless of its own fitness or evidence level, because the risk being flagged is STRUCTURAL
fragility, not quality — a genuinely excellent system can still be, correctly, a monoculture risk.

18.5 Worked example: a monoculture risk detected independent of the flagged system's own fitness.
Consider a synthetic System BB, illustrative only, whose capability — "resolve a canonical
identifier for a knowledge object" — has, over many cycles, become the depends-on target for a
large and growing share of the population, because it is simply the best available resolver and
every new system that needs identifier resolution has, reasonably, chosen to depend on it rather
than build a redundant alternative. System BB's own landscape shows uniformly HIGH fitness across
every environment it touches — by every quality measure this dataset has specified elsewhere, BB
is a model system. The monoculture check, applied independently of fitness, flags BB anyway: its
dependency concentration has crossed the population-relative threshold, and a significant fraction
of the estate's missions would be simultaneously degraded if BB's own capability were ever
compromised, decayed through an unmaintained assumption (Part VII §7.5's decay-driven cause,
applicable here even though BB currently shows no such decay), or unavailable during a maintenance
window. The recommended action is NOT to degrade or retire BB — that would be a category error,
discarding a genuinely excellent system to chase a structural metric — but to flag the niche BB
occupies as a candidate for DELIBERATE REDUNDANCY: a second, independently-built system serving
the same identifier-resolution capability, accepted as a deliberate INCREASE in apparent
duplication (a controlled exception the anti-proliferation pole, Part XIX, must be specified to
permit rather than automatically flag as waste) in exchange for structural resilience.

18.6 The anti-monoculture pole's tension with D2A's Invariant Two, resolved explicitly. Building a
deliberate redundant system to resolve BB's monoculture risk appears, on its face, to CREATE a
duplicates relation (IAS-F1 Part V Invariant Two) the estate's own invariants require to be
resolved via a D2A disposition. This Part resolves the apparent tension directly: a
DELIBERATE-REDUNDANCY recommendation is surfaced to D2A not as an unresolved duplicate requiring
elimination, but as a candidate for D2A's COMPOSITION or EXTEND disposition — building the second
resolver as a genuinely independent implementation (different failure modes, different underlying
assumptions) rather than a naive copy, so that the resulting pair satisfies BOTH the anti-
monoculture pole's structural-resilience goal AND Invariant Two's requirement that any residual
overlap be a NAMED, deliberate disposition rather than an unresolved defect — the specific,
concrete mechanism by which this Part's recommendation strengthens D2A's own decision quality
rather than creating a new class of invariant violation for D2A to clean up after the fact.

18.7 Why the dependency-concentration threshold must be computed per-environment as well as
population-wide, and what the two readings each catch that the other misses. A system could show
LOW population-wide dependency concentration (its depends-on edges are spread across many
environments, none individually dominant) while still constituting a SEVERE monoculture risk
WITHIN one specific, critical environment where it happens to be the sole structural path — a
governance-audit environment, say, where every audit-adjacent system depends on it exclusively,
even though the same system's presence in other environments is comparatively light. Conversely, a
system could show HIGH population-wide concentration purely as an artifact of the population's own
size and structure (a young, small population where most systems depend on a handful of early-
built foundational systems by necessity, not by genuine fragility) without any single environment
showing the acute, mission-critical exposure a narrower per-environment reading would reveal. This
Part therefore specifies BOTH readings as required outputs, never one substituting for the other:
population-wide concentration catches broad, estate-level structural risk; per-environment
concentration catches acute, narrow, potentially higher-consequence risk the broader reading
averages away.

18.8 The monoculture check's own false-positive risk, and why it inherits Part VIII's adjudication
discipline by structural analogy rather than by direct reuse. A newly-admitted system that happens
to be the FIRST to serve a newly-emerged environment will, by construction, show maximal dependency
concentration in that environment (it is the sole occupant, trivially) without this representing a
genuine structural risk worth flagging — the environment is simply too young to have attracted a
second occupant yet, the same YOUNG-SYSTEM logic Part VIII §8.2 applies to parasitism, now applied
to an ENVIRONMENT'S age rather than to a SYSTEM's age. PREVENTION: the monoculture check requires
an ENVIRONMENT-AGE GATE analogous to, but distinct from, Part VIII's system-age gate — an
environment within its own first several evaluation cycles is exempt from generating a monoculture
flag for its sole occupant, regardless of that occupant's dependency-concentration reading, because
a first-mover advantage in a young environment is not yet evidence of unhealthy structural
consolidation; it becomes a genuine monoculture candidate only once the environment itself has
matured enough that a second, redundant occupant's absence reflects a real gap rather than simple
youth.

FINAL LAW — PART XVIII. Monoculture is a structural fragility measured over the depends-on
subgraph both population-wide and per-environment — because each reading catches a risk the other
averages away — independent of any flagged system's own fitness or quality and exempted for young
environments exactly as young systems are exempted from parasitism; the correct response to a
genuine monoculture risk is deliberate, D2A-adjudicated redundancy, never the retirement of an
excellent system, because the risk this pole exists to catch is the population's resilience to
loss, not the flagged system's present-day performance.

PART XIX — THE BIODIVERSITY GOVERNOR, POLE TWO: ANTI-PROLIFERATION
===============================================================================================

19.1 Proliferation, defined as monoculture's structural opposite. Where Part XVIII's anti-
monoculture pole guards against TOO FEW genuinely distinct occupants of the population's
structural core, PROLIFERATION, DEFINED, is the opposite failure: a population that has grown too
many NEAR-IDENTICAL, thinly-differentiated systems, each individually plausible (none obviously a
COMPLETE-overlap duplicate D2A's Invariant Two would catch outright) but collectively imposing a
maintenance, coordination, and cognitive-load burden on the ensemble that exceeds the marginal
value their near-identical variation provides. Proliferation is CHAOTIC in the specific sense the
ledger's own candidate-40 language uses ("avoid monoculture and chaotic proliferation"): it is not
a single bad BUILD decision, but an emergent, population-level pattern of many individually-
defensible BUILD decisions compounding into an ensemble too fragmented to reason about coherently
— the mirror-image, at the population scale, of Part XIV's scope-creep failure at the single-
system scale.

19.2 Why proliferation evades D2A's per-proposal duplicates check, structurally. D2A's Invariant
Two fires when two systems' distinct-object tests resolve to the SAME honest answer (IAS-F1 Part
V §5.3) — a strict, pairwise test. A population of, say, five systems each addressing a SLIGHTLY
different slice of one broad niche, none of which pairwise resolves to an IDENTICAL distinct
object with any other single system, can pass every pairwise D2A check while still constituting,
in aggregate, a proliferation the population would be better served by consolidating into two or
three genuinely distinct systems. (DERIVED: this is the same LOCAL-versus-POPULATION-GRAIN gap
Part XIV §14.3 named for scope creep, now applied to BUILD-time proliferation rather than merger-
time accretion — a pairwise check, however correctly implemented, structurally cannot see an
n-way pattern spread across many pairs each individually below its own detection threshold.)

19.3 The proliferation index: measuring fragmentation within a niche cluster. The anti-
proliferation check operates over Part X's three-region ledger, specifically its CONTESTED
regions, and computes, per contested niche cluster, an OCCUPANT COUNT weighted against the
cluster's DEMAND BREADTH (Part X §10.2's breadth concept, applied here to the ENVIRONMENT rather
than to a single system) — a cluster with many occupants relative to how much genuinely
differentiated demand the environment generates is a proliferation candidate; a cluster with many
occupants matched by comparably broad, genuinely differentiated demand (the healthy competition
reading Part XVIII favors) is not. The distinguishing test, precisely mirroring the
complementarity-versus-substitutability discipline this dataset has applied since Part V §5.5, is
whether the cluster's occupants' capability signatures are SUBSTITUTABLE for one another (true
proliferation — a consumer could switch between them with no loss) or genuinely COMPLEMENTARY
(healthy diversity — different occupants serve different sub-slices a single generalist could not
serve as well).

19.4 Worked example: a proliferation candidate distinguished from healthy diversity in the same
niche cluster. Consider a synthetic contested cluster in the "governance-audit reporting"
environment holding four systems, illustrative only: Systems CC, DD, EE, and FF. CC and DD, on
inspection, produce SUBSTITUTABLE output — either could serve the same audit-report request with
no meaningful difference a consumer would notice, and their capability signatures differ mainly in
implementation history (CC was built first; DD was built later by a different contributor
addressing what turned out to be the identical need) rather than in served demand — a genuine
proliferation pair, distinct from a D2A-catchable COMPLETE-overlap duplicate only because CC and
DD's textual capability-signature descriptions happen to use different enough language that a
naive pairwise text-similarity check (were D2A to rely on one) might not flag them automatically.
EE and FF, by contrast, each serve genuinely different audit sub-types (EE handles
governance-rule-compliance audits; FF handles cost-governance audits) that happen to share the
same broad "governance-audit reporting" environment label without being substitutable at all — a
healthy diversity pair the anti-proliferation check must NOT flag. The proliferation index,
applying the substitutability test cluster-wide, correctly isolates the CC-DD pair as the
proliferation candidate while leaving EE and FF untouched.

19.5 The recommended action for a confirmed proliferation candidate, and its handoff to D2A. A
confirmed proliferation pair (or cluster) is surfaced to D2A not as an ordinary duplicates flag
(which the pairwise mechanism may already have missed, per §19.2) but as an ECOLOGY-SOURCED
duplicates candidate carrying the substitutability evidence (§19.3-19.4) as its supporting
package — functionally extending D2A's own detection reach by supplying population-grain evidence
a purely pairwise, proposal-time check cannot generate on its own. This is the anti-proliferation
pole's primary strengthening mechanism for D2A, distinct from and complementary to the ordinary
proposal-time duplicates check D2A already performs: D2A catches duplication AT THE MOMENT OF
PROPOSAL when the overlap is textually or structurally obvious; the anti-proliferation pole
catches duplication AFTER THE FACT, once a population has drifted into substitutability that
individual proposals, each considered in isolation against the estate at ITS OWN moment of
proposal, could not have anticipated.

19.6 The two poles read together: a single governor, not two competing mandates. The anti-
monoculture pole (Part XVIII) and the anti-proliferation pole (this Part) are frequently, and
correctly, understood as being in TENSION — reducing monoculture risk (§18.5's deliberate-
redundancy recommendation) literally increases apparent system count and overlap in the short
term, exactly the pattern the anti-proliferation pole is built to flag. The BIODIVERSITY
GOVERNOR, DEFINED as the composed mechanism reading both poles together, resolves this tension not
by averaging the two signals into one blended score (the same anti-blending discipline Part III
§3.1 applied to fitness, applied here to biodiversity) but by keeping them SEPARATE, LABELED
outputs — a monoculture-risk flag and a proliferation-risk flag can coexist for the SAME
environment simultaneously (a cluster can be both too concentrated at its structural core AND
carrying substitutable, redundant occupants at its periphery), and the governor's honest output is
both flags side by side, with the substitutability evidence (§19.3) distinguishing WHICH occupants
a proliferation correction should target — never the near-duplicate substitutable pair that a
deliberate-redundancy recommendation just, correctly, asked to be built.

19.7 Why the governor does not itself set a target biodiversity number. Consistent with this
dataset's restraint throughout, the biodiversity governor does not assert a target system count,
a target occupant-per-niche ratio, or any other fixed numerical goal, because a fixed target would
be gameable in both directions — a population could hit a numeric diversity target by building
trivially-differentiated systems (BIODIVERSITY-WASHING, named explicitly in Part XXIV's
adversarial catalogue) or could hit a numeric concentration target by artificially merging
genuinely distinct systems to reduce count. (NORMATIVE: the governor's output is always the two
labeled flag types — monoculture risk with dependency-concentration evidence, proliferation risk
with substitutability evidence — surfaced for D2A's, IAS-C1's, and IAS-G1's own judgment, never a
scalar biodiversity score asserted as a target to be optimized directly, which is the specific
anti-Goodhart discipline Part XXV's metrics treatment elaborates for this mechanism precisely.)

19.8 Why deliberate redundancy (Part XVIII §18.5) and proliferation are distinguished by
PROVENANCE, not by appearance, and what that means operationally. A deliberately-built redundant
resolver (Part XVIII §18.5's worked example) and a genuinely proliferative near-duplicate (§19.4's
CC-DD pair) can, at the moment of inspection, look STRUCTURALLY IDENTICAL on the substitutability
test alone — both are substitutable occupants of the same niche. The distinction this dataset
draws is PROVENANCE: a deliberate-redundancy system carries, from its own admission record, a
D2A COMPOSITION or EXTEND disposition explicitly citing a monoculture-risk finding as its
justification (Part XVIII §18.6), while a proliferative system carries no such citation — it simply
arose independently. PREVENTION for an implementer: the anti-proliferation check must read this
provenance tag BEFORE flagging a substitutable pair, exempting any pair where one member's
admission record traces to an explicit deliberate-redundancy disposition, because flagging a
deliberately-built redundancy as unwanted proliferation would directly contradict the very
recommendation the anti-monoculture pole made to justify building it in the first place — an
internal consistency requirement this dataset states explicitly rather than leaving as an implicit
assumption an implementer might miss.

19.9 The governor's aggregate output: a single population-wide biodiversity REPORT distinct from a
biodiversity SCORE. Consistent with §19.7's refusal to assert a target number, this dataset
specifies the governor's top-level output as a REPORT — the full set of per-niche monoculture and
proliferation flags, each carrying its own evidence and provenance per §19.8 — rather than any
single aggregate figure, even a non-target descriptive one. A human or governance-surface consumer
wanting a quick population-wide READ can compute a simple COUNT of open flags by type (monoculture
flags open, proliferation flags open, deliberate-redundancy exemptions applied) as a DERIVED,
presentation-layer view over the report, exactly as Decision D6 in this dataset's own Decision Log
permits for the metrics generally — but that count is explicitly a secondary, non-authoritative
convenience, never the object any of this dataset's own mechanisms reason from directly.

FINAL LAW — PART XIX. Proliferation is many individually-defensible BUILD decisions compounding
into substitutable, near-identical occupants no pairwise D2A check can see; the anti-proliferation
pole detects it via a substitutability test distinguishing true redundancy from healthy
complementary diversity, exempting substitutable pairs whose provenance traces to an explicit
deliberate-redundancy disposition, and the two poles — monoculture and proliferation — are read
side by side as separate labeled flags in one report, never blended into one score a population
could game from either direction.

PART XX — THE DATASET GENOME AND LINEAGE: HERITABLE PROPERTIES ACROSS SPECIATION, MERGER, SUCCESSION
===============================================================================================

20.1 Genome and lineage, folded from Family H per the Non-Duplication Ledger. Per the ledger's own
disposition ("Family H Dataset Genetics (71-80) | REFERENCE + fold | D2A (15 ops), DFP,
evolution_engine, SQI_CONTAMINATION | Genome/lineage folded into IAS-D1; contamination into global
QA"), this Part is the ecology's formal absorption of the genome/lineage concept, distinct from a
fresh BUILD, because the ledger's own routing already establishes that genome and lineage are not
a standalone system's object — they are properties OF the ecological events this dataset already
specifies (speciation, Part XI; merger, Part XIII; succession, Part XVI) and belong, by the
ledger's explicit instruction, folded INTO this dataset rather than built as a fifteenth sibling.

20.2 GENOME, defined. A system's GENOME, DEFINED, is the heritable subset of its property record
(IAS-F1 Part IV) and its accumulated validated law (validated principles, per Part XV §15.3) that
transfers, with evidenced completeness (Part XVII §17.2's continuity-of-law requirement), to
whatever successor system a speciation, merger, or succession-resolution event produces. A
genome is explicitly NOT the system's entire property record — properties tied to the system's
own specific implementation history (a stale cost figure, a specific current locator per IAS-F1
Part IX §9.2) do not transfer meaningfully, while properties tied to VALIDATED, evidence-backed
capability (a confirmed fitness pattern in a given environment, a hard rule the system's own
operation established, a confirmed symbiotic relationship) do. The genome concept, in other words,
answers "what about this system is worth passing on" as distinct from "what is currently true of
this system" — a narrower, curated subset by design.

20.3 LINEAGE, defined. A system's LINEAGE, DEFINED, is the directed graph of ancestor-descendant
relationships a system accumulates across its full history of speciation, merger, and succession-
resolution events, distinct from IAS-F1's ordinary relation graph (Part VI) because lineage edges
are TEMPORAL and IRREVERSIBLE in a way feeds, depends-on, and the other five relation types are
not — a lineage edge records a HISTORICAL FACT ("this system's genome descends, in part, from that
now-retired or now-fused system") rather than a CURRENT structural relationship, and it persists
even after the ancestor system itself has undergone extinction (Part XV) and its bytes have been
removed, precisely because the fossil record (Part XV §15.3) and the lineage graph are the two
mechanisms this dataset specifies for ensuring a system's disappearance does not erase the fact of
its historical contribution.

20.4 Lineage shapes: the two topologies speciation and merger produce, and how succession composes
with them. As named at Part XI §11.6 and Part XIII §13.7, speciation produces a ONE-PARENT-TWO-
CHILDREN lineage shape (a fan-out), while merger produces a TWO-PARENTS-ONE-CHILD shape (a
fan-in). A population's full lineage graph, over enough evaluation cycles, is therefore neither a
simple tree (which would require every node to have exactly one parent, ruled out by merger's
fan-in) nor a simple DAG of unbounded branching (which would understate the specific, bounded
fan-out speciation produces) — it is, DEFINED, a BIPARTITE-EVENT LINEAGE GRAPH, where every non-
root node connects to its predecessors through exactly one NAMED EVENT (a specific speciation ID,
merger ID, or succession-resolution ID per Part XVII §17.5's closed-tracker-entry mechanism),
which is the structural property that makes lineage traversal well-defined despite the mixed fan-
in/fan-out topology: an implementer traversing the graph always knows, at every edge, WHICH kind
of event produced it and can apply the correct genome-transfer rule (Part XI §11.6's speciation
rule or Part XIII §13.7's merger rule) rather than a single undifferentiated inheritance rule.

20.5 Worked example: tracing lineage across a combined merger-then-speciation history. Consider a
synthetic system, System GG, illustrative only, whose lineage graph shows: System GG was produced
by a MERGER of two earlier systems, System HH and System II (a fan-in event, per §20.4), three
cycles later. Six cycles after that, GG's own landscape develops a genuine, divergence-confirmed
bimodal shape (Part XI), and GG undergoes SPECIATION into two successors, System GG-alpha and
System GG-beta (a fan-out event). Tracing GG-alpha's full lineage backward: GG-alpha's immediate
parent is GG (via the speciation event), and GG's own parents are HH and II (via the merger
event) — a two-hop traversal crossing one fan-out edge and one fan-in edge, and GG-alpha's genome,
correctly assembled, carries the SUBSET of HH's and II's original validated principles that
remained relevant to whichever of GG's two divergent environment clusters GG-alpha inherited
(per Part XI §11.6's apportionment-by-divergence-evidence rule), not the full, undifferentiated
union of everything HH, II, and GG ever validated — a critical distinction, because a naive
lineage traversal that simply unioned every ancestor's full genome at every hop would produce an
ever-growing, increasingly undifferentiated inheritance that defeats the entire purpose of
speciation's divergence-based apportionment.

20.6 Contamination handling: the fold from SQI_CONTAMINATION, and why it is a GLOBAL QA concern
routed THROUGH, not owned BY, this dataset. The ledger's own fold instruction routes genome
CONTAMINATION checking to "the global QA" baseline (SQI_CONTAMINATION) rather than building a
contamination detector within this dataset. CONTAMINATION, in the genome sense, DEFINED, is the
inheritance of an INVALID or already-REFUTED claim through a lineage edge — a hard rule that was
later found wrong, a fitness pattern that was later REFUTED (Part VI §6.4's REFUTED case, applied
here across a lineage boundary rather than within one system's own measurement) transferred
forward into a descendant's genome as if it were still validated. This dataset's obligation,
consistent with the fold, is narrower than building the detector: every genome transfer this
Part specifies (§20.2, §20.5) must carry its SOURCE EVIDENCE LEVEL forward per-fact (mirroring
IAS-F1 Part IV §4.3's provenance discipline), so that the global SQI_CONTAMINATION baseline,
whichever mechanism implements it, has the per-fact evidence trail it needs to detect a
contaminated inheritance without this dataset needing to duplicate that detection logic itself —
the specific, minimal sense in which this Part strengthens SQI_CONTAMINATION rather than
rebuilding it.

20.7 Why genome and lineage matter for every mechanism this dataset has already specified. Looking
backward across Parts I-XIX, the genome and lineage concepts this Part formalizes were already
IMPLICITLY invoked at every genome-transfer point — Part XI §11.6 (speciation inheritance), Part
XIII §13.7 (merger inheritance), Part XV §15.3 (fossil-record extraction), Part XVII (continuity-
of-law across succession) — and this Part's contribution is not a new mechanism but the FORMAL
NAMING and TOPOLOGY that makes those four scattered specifications into one coherent, traceable
system: without Part XX, a reader could reasonably ask whether Part XI's inheritance rule and Part
XIII's inheritance rule were meant to be the same mechanism or two different ones; with Part XX,
both are confirmed as instances of one genome-transfer discipline operating over one lineage
graph, differentiated only by which named event type produced the edge.

20.8 Lineage graph queries: the two traversal directions and what each answers. Beyond storage
(§20.1-20.4), the lineage graph supports two structurally distinct query directions an
implementer should expect to need. FORWARD TRAVERSAL, from an ancestor toward its descendants,
answers "what became of this system's validated law after it was retired or absorbed" — the query
Part XV §15.3's fossil record and Part XVII §17.8's re-runnable audit both depend on. BACKWARD
TRAVERSAL, from a descendant toward its ancestors, answers "why does this system's capability
signature include this specific sub-capability" — the query Part XVII §17.6 names as closing a
provenance gap the bare capability signature cannot answer on its own. Because the graph is
bipartite-event rather than a simple tree (§20.4), backward traversal from any given node is
well-defined and terminates (every edge has exactly one event, every event has a bounded, named
set of predecessors), while forward traversal can fan out across an unbounded number of
descendants over a long enough history — an asymmetry any implementation should anticipate when
budgeting the traversal's own computational cost, backward queries being bounded and cheap,
forward queries requiring an explicit depth or breadth limit to remain tractable over a mature,
long-lived population.

20.9 Why the lineage graph is, itself, subject to the extinction-pathway's asymmetric-
irreversibility concern, and the specific safeguard this implies. Because a lineage edge records a
HISTORICAL fact (§20.3), the lineage GRAPH ITSELF must never be pruned or garbage-collected the way
a retired system's own bytes are (CO-06's cleanup, Part XV §15.1) — a lineage graph that shrank
over time as ancestor nodes "aged out" would defeat the entire continuity-of-law purpose this
dataset builds Parts XV, XVII, and XX around, since the whole point of surviving a system's
extinction is that its CONTRIBUTION remains traceable indefinitely, not merely for a bounded
retention window. NORMATIVE: the lineage graph is specified as an APPEND-ONLY, non-pruned store,
structurally distinct from every other store this dataset names (the fitness landscape, Part III,
IS expected to be recomputed and effectively superseded cycle over cycle) — the lineage graph is
this dataset's one genuinely permanent artifact, and any implementation that applies ordinary
storage-lifecycle policies (retention windows, cold-storage eviction) to it without this explicit
exemption would be silently reintroducing the fossil-loss failure mode (Part XV §15.2) at the
graph-storage layer rather than the extraction layer.

FINAL LAW — PART XX. The genome is the heritable, evidence-leveled, curated subset of a system's
validated properties and principles; the lineage is the bipartite-event, append-only, never-pruned
graph of speciation fan-outs and merger fan-ins that carries the genome forward across generations
and supports both bounded backward provenance queries and unbounded forward impact queries; and
genome contamination — an inherited, already-refuted claim — is caught by carrying per-fact
evidence levels across every transfer, feeding the global SQI_CONTAMINATION baseline rather than
duplicating it.

===============================================================================================
PART XXI — LINEAGE AS EVIDENCE: EXPECTED FITNESS FROM ANCESTRY, AND ITS LIMITS
===============================================================================================

21.1 Why lineage is not merely archival. Part XX established lineage as a historical record; this
Part specifies its FORWARD-LOOKING use, because a purely archival lineage graph — interesting to
consult, never consulted by a live decision — would be a weaker artifact than the Compounding
Graph's own standard demands of every IAS mechanism (a producer with no consumer is an orphan,
per the Compounding Graph's own admission law, generalized here to a mechanism WITHIN a dataset
rather than a dataset within the corpus). Lineage's forward-looking use, DEFINED, is EXPECTED
FITNESS FROM ANCESTRY: a newly-produced system (via speciation or merger) can be assigned a
PROVISIONAL fitness estimate, in the environments its genome's inherited fitness history covers,
DERIVED from its ancestors' confirmed fitness in those same environments, rather than starting
from a bare UNDEFINED landscape the way a wholly novel system would (Part VIII §8.2's young-system
exemption).

21.2 Why the provisional estimate is capped, never treated as OBSERVED. Following Article V
exactly, an expected-fitness-from-ancestry figure is, at best, DERIVED — it follows by stated
reasoning (the genome-transfer rules of Parts XI, XIII, XX) from OBSERVED ancestral fitness — and
it can never be promoted to OBSERVED status for the NEW system itself until that system has been
independently measured across at least one full evaluation cycle of its own operation (mirroring
Part VI §6.1's MEASURED stage exactly, applied here to a newly-produced descendant rather than to
a newly-activated symbiosis edge). This cap exists because inheritance of a validated PRINCIPLE
(Part XX §20.2, a durable, largely context-independent fact) is a stronger claim than inheritance
of a validated FITNESS LEVEL (a claim that is, by Part III's own definition, relative to the
CURRENT population and CURRENT environment demand — conditions that may have shifted between the
ancestor's last measurement and the descendant's birth), and conflating the two inheritance
strengths would systematically overstate a descendant's confidence.

21.3 The lineage-decay discipline: how many hops before ancestral evidence stops informing
provisional estimates. A direct parent's confirmed fitness is reasonably informative for a
descendant's provisional estimate; a great-grandparent's, filtered through two intervening
speciation-then-merger events each with their own apportionment and evidence-capping, carries
progressively less reliable signal, both because each hop introduces its own DERIVED-not-OBSERVED
discount (§21.2) and because the intervening events themselves may have altered which sub-slice
of the original ancestor's niche actually transferred forward (Part XX §20.5's apportionment
discipline). This dataset specifies the DIRECTION of this decay — provisional-estimate confidence
strictly decreases with lineage distance, never increases or stays flat — without fixing a
numerical decay rate, which Part XVI's evaluation protocol (§16.6, referenced generally; the full
evaluation treatment is Part XXV-XXVI's territory) would need to calibrate empirically before any
implementation could assign a specific confidence-per-hop figure.

21.4 Worked example: a provisional estimate assigned to System GG-alpha, and its subsequent
MEASURED reconciliation. Returning to Part XX §20.5's System GG-alpha (produced by speciation from
GG, which was itself produced by merger from HH and II), suppose GG-alpha inherits, via its
genome, a confirmed HIGH fitness reading GG held in one specific environment prior to speciation.
GG-alpha's provisional estimate for that SAME environment is assigned HIGH-DERIVED (not HIGH-
OBSERVED) at the moment of its own admission, distinct from an environment GG-alpha's divergence-
based apportionment did NOT carry forward (where GG-alpha correctly starts UNDEFINED, per Part
III §3.6, exactly as a wholly novel system would). One evaluation cycle later, GG-alpha's own
landscape is independently measured: if the provisional HIGH-DERIVED estimate is corroborated by
the fresh OBSERVED reading, the entry is promoted to HIGH-OBSERVED and the calibration record
(mirroring every prior calibration loop this dataset specifies) logs a CONFIRMED provisional
estimate; if the fresh reading diverges substantially — say, MODERATE rather than HIGH — the
provisional estimate is marked REFUTED, and this specific refutation feeds the lineage-decay
discipline's own calibration (§21.3), tightening future confidence assignments at that same hop
distance.

21.5 Why a REFUTED provisional estimate is informative, not embarrassing. Consistent with the
epistemic honesty this dataset has modeled at every prior refutation point (Part VI §6.4's
symbiosis REFUTED case, Part XII §12.6's re-merger learning event), a REFUTED provisional estimate
is not a failure of the lineage mechanism to be minimized or hidden — it is DIRECT EVIDENCE that
either the environment's demand has shifted between the ancestor's measurement and the
descendant's own operation (an environment-level explanation, requiring no change to the lineage
mechanism itself) or that the apportionment rule (Part XI §11.6) mis-assigned which sub-slice of
ancestral genome the descendant should have inherited (a mechanism-level explanation, requiring
recalibration of the apportionment rule). Distinguishing these two explanations is itself a
diagnostic task the calibration record must support, by checking whether OTHER descendants at the
same hop distance from OTHER ancestors show a similar refutation rate (suggesting a mechanism-
level problem) or whether the refutation is isolated to environments independently known to be
volatile (suggesting an environment-level explanation).

21.6 The limit of lineage's forward-looking value: it never substitutes for independent
measurement. This Part closes with the same restraint this dataset has applied consistently: a
provisional, ancestry-derived fitness estimate accelerates a descendant's INITIAL positioning in
the landscape (avoiding a cold start, per §21.1's stated purpose) but never substitutes for the
MEASURED stage's independent confirmation, and no downstream mechanism — not IAS-C1's portfolio
allocation, not IAS-G1's topology decisions (Part XXVI) — is licensed to treat a provisional,
DERIVED estimate as equivalent in confidence to an OBSERVED one, a discipline this dataset states
NORMATIVELY here because the temptation to shortcut a new system's evaluation period using
ancestry alone is real and would, if permitted, quietly erode the evidence-level discipline every
other mechanism in this dataset has been built to protect.

21.7 A boundary case: a descendant with MULTIPLE, conflicting ancestral readings for the same
environment. Where a merger produces a descendant whose two parents held DIFFERING confirmed
fitness readings for the same shared environment (System HH reading MODERATE where System II read
HIGH, in a hypothetical extension of Part XX §20.5's worked trace), the provisional estimate cannot
simply average the two — averaging would silently assert a precision neither parent's individual
reading supports, and would hide from the descendant's initial record the genuine fact that its
own ancestry was ITSELF divided on this environment's demand. This dataset specifies the honest
resolution: the provisional estimate is recorded as a RANGE (MODERATE-to-HIGH) rather than a point
value, with both parents' contributing readings preserved in the lineage record (§20.8's backward-
traversal query surfaces both on inspection) — a range-valued provisional estimate is a weaker,
less immediately actionable starting point than a point estimate, but a fabricated point estimate
that erased genuine ancestral disagreement would be a worse epistemic error than the honestly wider
range, consistent with this dataset's repeated preference (Part VIII §8.7's mixed-cause case, Part
III's UNDEFINED-versus-zero distinction) for an honestly imprecise output over a falsely precise
one.

21.8 Interfaces: what expected-fitness-from-ancestry hands to the biodiversity governor's young-
environment exemption. A descendant carrying a strong, narrow-range provisional estimate for a
specific environment is, per Part XVIII §18.8's environment-age gate, still subject to that gate's
protection regardless of how confident its provisional estimate looks — the environment-age
exemption is about the ENVIRONMENT's own maturity, not about any individual occupant's estimated
fitness, and a descendant's inherited confidence must never be read as grounds to WAIVE the young-
environment protection early, because a confident provisional estimate about the DESCENDANT's own
performance says nothing about whether the ENVIRONMENT itself has matured enough for a monoculture
reading to be meaningful — two independent axes of uncertainty (the system's own evidence maturity,
this Part's object, and the environment's own structural maturity, Part XVIII's object) that this
dataset keeps deliberately unconflated throughout.

FINAL LAW — PART XXI. A descendant's ancestry licenses a provisional, DERIVED fitness estimate —
recorded as a range rather than a false point value where ancestral evidence itself conflicts —
that accelerates its initial positioning in the landscape, with confidence strictly decreasing by
lineage distance and never crossing into OBSERVED status until the descendant's own operation is
independently measured, and never substituting for the environment's own independent maturity
gate — ancestry informs the starting point, it never substitutes for the measurement or for the
environment's own age.

PART XXII — GOVERNED EVOLUTION: EVIDENCE THRESHOLD, CANARY, AND ROLLBACK
===============================================================================================

22.1 Governed evolution as the cross-cutting discipline binding every mechanism in this dataset.
Per Article IX of the CPP-IAS Constitution ("no IAS system may recommend self-modification of the
estate without an evidence threshold, validation, versioning, and rollback") and the Strengthening
Matrix's explicit naming of self-safe-evolution and MVI as parents this dataset strengthens, this
Part specifies the SHARED discipline every prior mechanism's PROPOSED-to-ACTIVATED (or PROPOSED-
to-EXECUTED) transition must satisfy, rather than re-deriving evidence-threshold, canary, and
rollback logic independently for symbiosis activation (Part VI), speciation (Part XI), merger
(Part XIII), and extinction (Part XV) each in isolation — the same non-duplication discipline this
dataset asks of the wider estate, applied reflexively to its own internal architecture, exactly as
Part VII §7.6 already did for the symbiosis-and-parasitism detector pairing.

22.2 The evidence threshold, generalized. Every mechanism this dataset specifies already carries
its own PART-SPECIFIC evidence bar — symbiosis's complementarity test (Part V §5.5), speciation's
divergence-confirmed corroboration (Part XI §11.2), merger's multi-environment overlap
confirmation (Part XIII §13.4), extinction's cause classification (Part VII §7.5). This Part names
the SHARED STRUCTURE those bars all share: EVIDENCE THRESHOLD, DEFINED, is the minimum evidence
level (per Article V's vocabulary) and minimum evidence BREADTH (multi-cycle for speciation,
multi-environment for merger, multi-check for parasitism adjudication) a candidate record must
carry before it is eligible to leave the PROPOSED stage and enter execution via D2A or
evolution_engine. The threshold is deliberately MECHANISM-SPECIFIC in its concrete criteria
(because speciation's and merger's risks are asymmetric in kind, per Parts XI-XIV) but UNIFORM in
its STRUCTURAL requirement: no PROPOSED record crosses into execution on HYPOTHESIZED evidence
alone, ever, across any of the seven mechanisms this dataset specifies.

22.3 Canary, defined and applied across mechanisms. A CANARY, DEFINED in this dataset's usage, is a
BOUNDED, REVERSIBLE, PARTIAL execution of a proposed ecological action — activating a symbiosis
edge for a LIMITED subset of the affected environment's traffic rather than all of it, or, for
speciation and merger (where a partial split or partial fusion is structurally harder to bound
cleanly), a SHORTENED initial MEASURED-stage window with an explicit, pre-committed rollback
trigger rather than the full evaluation-cycle window otherwise specified. The canary's function is
to let the MEASURED stage (Part VI §6.1, Part XI §11.4, Part XIII §13.4) begin generating real,
OBSERVED evidence sooner, and at lower risk, than committing the full population immediately would
allow — directly serving the MVI (minimum-viable-increment) discipline this dataset strengthens by
name, per the Strengthening Matrix: MVI's own object, the estate's spec_gate tiering discipline
(the ledger's Family O fold), governs how large an increment of change may proceed without
additional governance overhead, and this dataset's canary mechanism is the ecology-specific
INSTANTIATION of that same minimum-viable-increment principle applied to population-level actions
rather than to a single code change.

22.4 Rollback, defined and bound to the reversibility asymmetry already established. Following
Part XV §15.6's explicit naming of extinction's REVERSIBILITY ASYMMETRY, ROLLBACK, DEFINED, is the
pre-committed, named procedure for undoing an ACTIVATED or EXECUTED ecological action when its
canary or full MEASURED-stage evidence resolves REFUTED rather than CONFIRMED. For symbiosis, the
rollback is straightforward: remove the activated feeds or strengthens edge, restoring the
population's prior relation graph exactly (Part VI §6.4's REFUTED case already assumes this).
For speciation and merger, rollback is the RE-MERGER (Part XII §12.4) and RE-SPECIATION (Part XIII
§13.4) mechanisms already specified — this Part's contribution is confirming those two mechanisms
ARE, formally, this dataset's rollback procedure for their respective forward actions, not a
separate corrective afterthought. For extinction, per Part XV §15.6's own honest admission,
NO SYMMETRIC ROLLBACK EXISTS — which is precisely why Article IX's rollback requirement is
satisfied, for extinction specifically, by the MANDATORY FOSSILIZATION requirement (Part XV
§15.2-15.3) standing IN PLACE of a true rollback: fossilization is this dataset's answer to
Article IX's rollback mandate for the one mechanism where literal reversal is structurally
impossible, an explicit, honest substitution this Part names rather than leaving Article IX's
requirement silently unmet for the extinction pathway.

22.5 The versioning requirement, applied to the ecology's own mechanisms. Article IX also requires
VERSIONING. Following IAS-F1 Part XVII's precedent (the additive-versus-revisory version boundary
this dataset's foundation already establishes), every calibration adjustment this dataset's
mechanisms make to themselves — the divergence test's evidentiary bar (Part XII §12.4), the
succession tracker's projection accuracy (Part XVII §17.5), the fitness composition weighting
(Part IV §4.3) — is itself a VERSIONED change to the ecology's own detection logic, carrying a
version marker distinct from the ordinary genome/lineage versioning (Part XX) applied to the
POPULATION's systems. A recalibration that silently overwrote a prior threshold, with no version
record distinguishing "detection logic version 3's divergence bar" from "detection logic version
4's divergence bar," would make Part XII's own re-merger learning loop uninterpretable — a re-
merger attributed to a threshold that had, unbeknownst to the record, already been tightened
before the re-merger occurred would misattribute the calibration signal to the wrong version of
the detector.

22.6 Governed evolution as the mechanism that lets D2A and CO-06 trust this dataset's
recommendations without re-verifying them from scratch. The complete practical payoff of this
Part, read together with Parts VI, XI-XV's individual lifecycle specifications, is that any
ecological recommendation surfaced to D2A, evolution_engine, or CO-06 arrives ALREADY carrying:
its evidence threshold satisfaction (§22.2), its canary results where applicable (§22.3), and a
pre-named rollback procedure (§22.4) — meaning the consuming system's own decision burden is
reduced to confirming the package's completeness and rendering its own authority-protected verdict
(IAS-F1 Invariant Three), rather than independently re-deriving whether the underlying evidence
was sufficient. This is the concrete, checkable mechanism by which this dataset strengthens self-
safe-evolution: it is not a parallel safety mechanism duplicating self-safe-evolution's own
object, but a POPULATION-GRAIN evidence-and-canary discipline self-safe-evolution's own event-
grain safety checks can consume as a pre-verified input, precisely the STRENGTHENS relation (IAS-F1
Part VI §6.6) this dataset's Strengthening Matrix row requires.

22.7 Why the canary mechanism itself requires a MAXIMUM duration, not merely a minimum evidence
bar. A canary (§22.3) that runs indefinitely, never converting to full commitment or being rolled
back, is itself a failure state this dataset names explicitly: a PERPETUAL CANARY, DEFINED, is a
partial activation left running past the point where its own MEASURED-stage evidence should have
resolved it one way or the other, typically because the governance surface responsible for
converting a successful canary to full commitment (or rolling back a failed one) has simply not
acted — a governance-action gap structurally identical to the PROTECTED-PARASITE pattern (Part
XXIII §23.4-23.5) but occurring at the canary stage rather than the extinction-adjudication stage.
PREVENTION: this dataset specifies a maximum canary duration (again, per Article VIII, a required
gate rather than a fixed number) after which an unresolved canary is AUTOMATICALLY surfaced as its
own distinct flag type — PERPETUAL-CANARY-CANDIDATE — using the identical cost-visibility mechanism
Part XXIII §23.5 already specifies for protected parasites, converting an invisible, indefinitely-
running partial commitment into a visible, dated, accumulating-cost fact.

22.8 The relationship between this Part's governed-evolution discipline and the estate's own
spec_gate tiering, made concrete rather than merely named. Building on the MVI-strengthening claim
already stated at §22.3 and §22.6, the concrete correspondence is: spec_gate's tiers (S/M/L/XL,
referenced via HR-SPEC-001's precedent) gate how much GOVERNANCE OVERHEAD a given code change
requires before shipping; this dataset's canary mechanism gates how much POPULATION EXPOSURE a
given ecological action requires before full commitment — the two are structurally parallel
(both scale required scrutiny to the blast radius of the action) but operate over different units
(code-change size versus population-relation-graph exposure), and this dataset's contribution to
MVI specifically is demonstrating that the SAME minimum-viable-increment PRINCIPLE — start small,
gather real evidence, commit further only once that evidence confirms — generalizes cleanly from
the code-change grain MVI already governs to the population-action grain this dataset governs,
without requiring MVI itself to be extended or modified to cover the new grain.

FINAL LAW — PART XXII. Every one of this dataset's seven mechanisms shares one structural
discipline — an evidence threshold below which no candidate executes, a time-bounded canary that
lets real evidence accrue at bounded risk before full commitment and is itself flagged if left
unresolved past its own maximum duration, a named rollback (literal for symbiosis, speciation, and
merger; fossilization-as-substitute for the irreversible case of extinction), and a versioned
record of every self-calibration — and this shared discipline, the same minimum-viable-increment
principle MVI applies at the code-change grain now generalized to the population-action grain, is
what lets D2A, evolution_engine, and self-safe-evolution consume the ecology's recommendations as
pre-verified inputs rather than re-deriving safety from scratch.

PART XXIII — FAILURE MODES: PREMATURE EXTINCTION, PROTECTED PARASITES, AND THE WIDER CATALOGUE
===============================================================================================

23.1 Why a dedicated failure-mode Part, beyond the failure modes already named inline. This
dataset has named several mechanism-specific failure modes inline as they arose — fragmentation
(Part XII), forced consolidation and scope creep (Part XIV), fossil loss (Part XV §15.2),
biodiversity-washing (Part XIX §19.7, elaborated further below). This Part collects the two
failure modes the task brief names explicitly by title — PREMATURE EXTINCTION and PROTECTED
PARASITES — gives them the dedicated treatment their severity warrants, and completes the
catalogue with modes not yet named, so that a reader consulting failure modes has one complete
reference rather than needing to reassemble the catalogue from scattered inline mentions.

23.2 PREMATURE EXTINCTION, defined and distinguished from a correctly-adjudicated extinction. A
PREMATURE EXTINCTION, DEFINED, is an extinction recommendation (Part XV) executed against a system
whose apparent parasitism was, in fact, one of the false-positive shapes Part VIII's adjudication
protocol exists to catch (young-system misread, foundation misread, seasonal-demand misread) or
whose true cause was DECAY-driven rather than DEMAND-driven (Part VII §7.5) — meaning the niche
itself remained genuinely valuable and a REPAIR, not a retirement, was the correct disposition.
Premature extinction is the SINGLE MOST COSTLY failure mode this dataset can produce, precisely
because of the asymmetric irreversibility Part XV §15.6 and Part XXII §22.4 both establish: unlike
a fragmented speciation (correctable via re-merger) or a forced-consolidation merger (correctable
via re-speciation), a premature extinction's only correction is REBUILDING the retired system's
capability from whatever the fossil record preserved — and if the extinction ALSO failed to
fossilize properly (a compounding failure, fossil loss stacked on premature extinction), the
correction requires rebuilding from nothing.

23.3 Why premature extinction is specifically a failure of the ADJUDICATION stage, not the
detection stage, and what that implies for prevention. The parasitism DETECTOR (Part VII) is
expected, by design, to produce false positives — that is exactly why the adjudication protocol
(Part VIII) exists as a SEPARATE, mandatory stage rather than folding its checks directly into
detection. Premature extinction, therefore, is diagnostically a failure of the adjudication stage
being SKIPPED or RUSHED, not a failure of the detector producing a wrong signal in the first
place — the detector did its job (flagging a candidate); the adjudication protocol's four checks
(age gate, foundation exemption, trend-length, cause classification, Part VIII §8.6) either were
not run, were run against insufficient data, or were overridden. PREVENTION, stated normatively:
the governed-evolution discipline of Part XXII §22.2's evidence threshold must, for the extinction
pathway specifically, be interpreted as REQUIRING all four adjudication checks to have PASSED and
been RECORDED before an extinction candidate is eligible to enter the PROPOSED stage at all — an
extinction candidate arriving at D2A without a recorded, passed adjudication package is, by this
dataset's own discipline, an incomplete package that should not have been surfaced, and this Part
names that incompleteness as the specific, checkable signature of a premature-extinction risk
in progress, before the irreversible action occurs.

23.4 PROTECTED PARASITES, defined and distinguished from adjudication failure. Where premature
extinction is a failure of ACTING on insufficient evidence, PROTECTED PARASITISM (introduced at
Part VIII §8.5) is the inverse failure: a system that PASSES adjudication cleanly — genuinely
sustained, genuinely cost-exceeding-return, genuinely demand-driven or decay-driven per §7.5's
clean cases, not a mixed-cause ambiguity — and is STILL not acted upon, because of factors outside
the evidence record entirely: institutional attachment, a maintainer's continued investment,
uncertainty about second-order effects the evidence record does not capture, or simple inertia.
Protected parasitism is a failure this dataset's own mechanisms CANNOT fully solve, because it is
not a detection or adjudication defect — the record is correct — it is a GOVERNANCE ACTION defect
occurring entirely downstream of this dataset's output, at D2A's or CO-06's own decision point.

23.5 What this dataset CAN do about protected parasites, honestly bounded. Consistent with the
event-grain-authority-preserved restraint this dataset has maintained throughout (Part I §1.4),
this dataset does not, and structurally cannot, force D2A or CO-06 to act on a correctly-
adjudicated parasitism record — Invariant Three (IAS-F1 Part V §5.4) reserves that authority to
D2A alone. What this dataset CAN and DOES do is make protection VISIBLE and MEASURABLE rather than
silent: a parasitism record that has been ADJUDICATED (passed all four checks) but remains
UNACTIONED across MULTIPLE subsequent evaluation cycles is flagged, distinctly, as a PROTECTED-
PARASITE-CANDIDATE, with its cost accumulation across the unactioned period tracked explicitly —
converting an invisible, ongoing cost into a visible, accumulating, dated figure that makes the
COST of protection impossible to ignore even though this dataset cannot compel the correction
itself. (NORMATIVE: this is the maximum honest intervention a population-grain, authority-
respecting mechanism can make against a governance-level failure; the actual correction remains,
correctly, an Owner-or-D2A-level decision, per the estate's existing Owner-decide precedent, IAS-F1
Part X §10.5.)

23.6 The wider catalogue, named briefly for completeness. Beyond fragmentation (Part XII),
forced consolidation and scope creep (Part XIV), fossil loss (Part XV §15.2), premature extinction
(§23.2), and protected parasites (§23.4), three further modes complete the catalogue.
SPECIATION CHURN (a system oscillates between speciation and re-merger repeatedly across many
cycles because the divergence test, even after Part XII's calibration feedback, remains poorly
tuned for that specific system's landscape shape — a signal that the system's own niche is
genuinely ambiguous in a way the general-purpose divergence test cannot resolve, requiring a
manual, Owner-level review rather than further automated calibration attempts). BIODIVERSITY-
WASHING (named at Part XIX §19.7: building trivially-differentiated systems to inflate an apparent
diversity count without genuine complementary capability — caught by the anti-proliferation pole's
substitutability test, Part XIX §19.3, which is specifically designed to see through cosmetic
differentiation). CALIBRATION DRIFT (the various calibration loops this dataset specifies — Part
VI §6.2, Part XII §12.4, Part XVII §17.5, Part IV §4.3 — each depend on a STEADY STREAM of
CONFIRMED and REFUTED outcomes to stay accurate; a population that goes many cycles without enough
ACTIVATED or EXECUTED events to generate fresh calibration data will see its thresholds drift
stale relative to the population's actual current behavior, a slow-onset failure this dataset
names but does not fully solve, because its correction — more frequent evaluation cycles, or a
minimum calibration-refresh cadence — is an operational decision belonging to whichever mechanism
implements this specification, not a fact this dataset can assert without calibration evidence of
its own).

23.7 A worked composite trace: how a single system could, in the worst case, chain THREE failure
modes across its lifetime, and why the catalogue's individual preventions still hold at each link.
Consider a fully synthetic, illustrative-only trace: a system undergoes CALIBRATION DRIFT (§23.6's
third named mode) because the population generated too few events over several cycles to keep its
governing thresholds current; the stale threshold subsequently lets a SCOPE-CREEP condition
(Part XIV §14.3) go undetected past its usual review point because the cumulative-absorption
threshold itself had drifted upward without recalibration; and the resulting overloaded system
eventually shows a genuine, adjudicated parasitism signal that a rushed, understaffed review cycle
processes without the full four-check protocol (Part VIII §8.6), producing a PREMATURE EXTINCTION
of what was, in fact, a repairable, merely overloaded system. This composite trace is presented not
because any single one of this dataset's preventions failed on its own terms — each individual
mechanism (calibration-loop health monitoring, Part XXVI §26.1's Protocol Four; the scope-creep
detector, Part XIV §14.3; the four-check adjudication protocol, Part VIII §8.6) would have caught
its OWN link in the chain had it been running with fresh, uncompromised inputs — but to demonstrate
that this dataset's own calibration-loop-health requirement (Part XXII §22.5's versioning, Part
XXVI §26.1's Protocol Four) is not a peripheral concern: a population that lets its calibration
loops go stale is a population where every DOWNSTREAM prevention this dataset specifies inherits
that staleness silently, which is the specific, concrete argument for why Protocol Four's
calibration-loop-health check is placed FOURTH, not omitted, in the evaluation protocol's ordering.

23.8 Why this dataset does not attempt to enumerate every possible composite failure chain
exhaustively, and what that restraint implies for an implementer. The three-link chain of §23.7 is
illustrative of a general risk (staleness propagating downstream through dependent mechanisms), not
an exhaustive catalogue of every possible chain a real population could produce — this dataset does
not attempt that enumeration, consistent with Article VI's prohibition on padding a dataset with
combinatorial variations that add no new mechanism, only new instances of an already-named pattern.
NORMATIVE: an implementer's correct response to this Part is not to build a detector for every
conceivable failure-chain permutation, but to ensure each INDIVIDUAL mechanism's own prevention
(the four-check adjudication, the scope-creep review, the calibration-loop-health monitor) is
implemented with genuine rigor and kept genuinely fresh — a population where every individual
prevention holds cannot, by construction, produce the composite failure §23.7 illustrates, which is
the precise sense in which this dataset's failure-mode catalogue is complete at the MECHANISM level
even though it is deliberately incomplete at the COMBINATORIAL-INSTANCE level.

FINAL LAW — PART XXIII. Premature extinction is an adjudication-stage failure with no full
correction once executed, preventable only by requiring all four adjudication checks recorded and
passed before an extinction candidate is even eligible for the PROPOSED stage; protected
parasitism is a governance-action failure entirely outside this dataset's authority to force,
correctable only by making the cost of protection visible and accumulating rather than silent; and
because staleness in any one mechanism's calibration can silently propagate into every downstream
mechanism that depends on it, correct detection is necessary but never sufficient for correct
outcomes — the calibration loops themselves must stay fresh, or every prevention built on top of
them inherits their staleness invisibly.

===============================================================================================
PART XXIV — ADVERSARIAL CASES: GAMING THE ECOLOGY'S OWN MECHANISMS
===============================================================================================

24.1 Why the ecology needs an adversarial catalogue distinct from its failure-mode catalogue. Part
XXIII's failure modes arise from HONEST error — a detector miscalibrated, an adjudication skipped
under time pressure, a governance decision deferred out of inertia. This Part treats a different
threat: a system, or the agent that maintains it, DELIBERATELY shaping its own metrics, capability-
signature language, or reported behavior to score favorably against this dataset's mechanisms
without genuinely earning the favorable score — GAMING, in the precise sense IAS-F1 Part VIII §8.6
already names for the ensemble-level boundary test, now applied to the ecology's own population-
dynamics mechanisms specifically.

24.2 GAMED SYMBIOSIS: manufacturing feeds edges to appear mutualistic without genuine
complementarity. Because the symbiosis detector's complementarity test (Part V §5.5) is, at
bottom, a comparison of DECLARED capability-signature input and output TYPES, a system's own
capability-signature description could be worded to CLAIM an output type matching a target
system's declared input type, without the underlying implementation genuinely producing output the
target could productively consume — a wiring that passes the complementarity test on paper while
producing, in practice, low-value or unused data flow. PREVENTION: the MEASURED stage (Part VI
§6.1) is the specific structural defense — a gamed symbiosis, activated, will show NO measurable
fitness gain for either party once real usage data accrues, and the REFUTED outcome (Part VI §6.4)
catches it within one evaluation cycle; the deeper prevention is that this dataset never promotes
a symbiosis claim past HYPOTHESIZED status on capability-signature analysis alone (Part V §5.6
already states this explicitly), which means a gamed symbiosis can, at most, achieve a temporary
FLAGGED or ACTIVATED status, never a durable CONFIRMED one, bounding the gaming's maximum payoff
to one canary-scale activation before detection.

24.3 GAMED FITNESS: inflating relational position through low-value edges. Because relational
position (Part III §3.2) counts inbound feeds and strengthens edges as a proxy for load-bearing
value, a system could seek to accumulate MANY low-value feeds edges specifically to inflate this
component of its fitness score, independent of whether the edges represent genuine value to their
recipients. PREVENTION: this is precisely why relational position is only ONE of the three fitness
components (Part IV §4.1), composed at the LOWEST evidence level among the three (§4.1's
discipline), and why demand-satisfaction (measuring whether CONSUMED output is ACCEPTED rather
than overridden) is weighted as an independent, harder-to-game signal — a consumer overriding or
ignoring low-value output it nominally "consumes" via a gamed edge will show low demand-
satisfaction for that same relationship, and the composed fitness value, capped at the lowest
supporting evidence level, does not reward the inflated relational-position component in isolation.

24.4 GAMED BIODIVERSITY: the two-directional attack on the governor's two poles. Named already at
Part XIX §19.7 in brief, this attack deserves fuller treatment given its two-directional nature.
BIODIVERSITY-WASHING (inflating apparent diversity by building trivially-differentiated systems to
avoid a monoculture flag) is caught by the anti-proliferation pole's substitutability test (Part
XIX §19.3), which specifically measures whether occupants' OUTPUTS are interchangeable regardless
of how differently their capability signatures are WORDED — a textual-differentiation attack fails
against a substitutability test that ignores wording and measures actual output overlap. The
INVERSE attack, CONSOLIDATION-WASHING (merging genuinely distinct systems specifically to reduce
apparent system count and avoid a proliferation flag, at the cost of genuinely losing the narrower
specialist capability one of the merged systems held), is caught by the merger evidence protocol's
own multi-environment overlap-confirmation requirement (Part XIII §13.4) combined with the
post-merger MEASURED stage (§13.4): a consolidation-washing merger, by definition executed against
systems that were NOT genuinely convergent, will re-trigger the BIMODAL landscape signature (Part
III §3.4) within a cycle or two, exactly as an honest forced-consolidation error would (Part XIV
§14.1) — the detection mechanism cannot distinguish DELIBERATE gaming from HONEST error at the
signal level, and this dataset's honest position is that it does not need to: both produce the
same detectable, correctable signature, and the re-speciation rollback (Part XXII §22.4) applies
identically regardless of the original merger's motive.

24.5 SELECTIVE REPORTING: suppressing REFUTED outcomes to inflate apparent detector accuracy.
Named at Part VI §6.4 and Part XII §12.6 as the specific failure this dataset's calibration
loops are designed to resist, this adversarial case deserves explicit naming here: an agent
maintaining a system, or maintaining the ecology's own detection mechanisms, could be incentivized
to under-report REFUTED symbiosis, speciation, or merger outcomes — reporting only the successes —
to make the underlying mechanism appear more accurate than it is, which would corrupt every
downstream calibration loop this dataset depends on (Part IV §4.3, Part XII §12.4, Part XVII
§17.5). PREVENTION: this dataset specifies that the lifecycle record (Part VI §6.1, and its
analogues at Part XI §11.4 and Part XIII §13.4) is a MANDATORY, append-only log of every stage
transition, including REFUTED outcomes, distinct from any summary report a maintaining agent might
separately produce — the calibration record's source of truth is the lifecycle log itself, never a
self-reported summary, which is the structural defense against selective reporting: an agent
wishing to suppress a REFUTED outcome would need to suppress it AT THE LOG LEVEL, a materially
harder and more detectable act than simply omitting it from a narrative summary.

24.6 THE IMPERIAL-ECOLOGY CASE: the ecology mechanism itself overreaching its own boundary. The
final adversarial case is reflexive: could the ecology's OWN mechanisms, in the course of
detecting parasites, monocultures, or proliferation, themselves overreach into territory IAS-F1's
Invariant Four (the boundary invariant, Part V §5.5) reserves for other systems — for instance, a
biodiversity governor recommendation that implicitly asserts authority over WHICH specific system
gets built to fill a niche vacancy, rather than merely naming the vacancy (Part IX §9.7's explicit
restraint)? This dataset's answer, consistent with every mechanism specified across Parts V through
XXIII, is that every one of the seven mechanisms' outputs is explicitly bounded to DETECTION,
EVIDENCE-GATHERING, and RECOMMENDATION — never EXECUTION — and this restraint is not incidental
caution but the specific application of Part I §1.4's founding principle (the ecology strengthens
D2A and evolution_engine by supplying context, never by seizing their authority) to every
subsequent mechanism this dataset has built on that foundation; an implementation that let any of
this dataset's mechanisms cross from recommendation into execution would violate this dataset's
own design intent as surely as it would violate IAS-F1's Invariant Three.

24.7 A seventh case, distinct from gaming by a system's own maintainer: ADVERSARIAL FOSSILIZATION.
Where §24.2 through §24.6 concern an agent gaming a system's OWN metrics to appear favorable,
ADVERSARIAL FOSSILIZATION, DEFINED, is the inverse: an agent maintaining a system slated for
extinction attempting to inflate the fossilization package (Part XV §15.3) with LOW-VALUE or
FABRICATED "validated principles" specifically to make the retirement appear more costly than it
is, hoping to delay or block the extinction recommendation on the grounds that "too much knowledge
would be lost." PREVENTION: this dataset's own evidence-level discipline is the direct defense — a
principle claimed for fossilization must already carry an independent OBSERVED or DERIVED evidence
level from the estate's existing hard_rules or ACIS registries (Part XV §15.3's explicit sourcing
requirement) BEFORE the extinction candidacy is even raised, meaning a principle cannot be
freshly minted at the moment of extinction review specifically to inflate the package — a
fabricated, last-minute "principle" would arrive with no prior OBSERVED provenance and would fail
the same per-fact evidence-level check Part XX §20.6 already specifies for genome-contamination
detection, making adversarial fossilization structurally indistinguishable, at the detection layer,
from the ordinary contamination case this dataset already defends against.

24.8 Why this dataset's adversarial catalogue is deliberately closed at seven named cases rather
than left open-ended. Consistent with §23.8's restraint against exhaustive combinatorial
enumeration, this Part names seven specific, structurally distinct gaming vectors — not because no
others could exist, but because each of the seven targets a genuinely DIFFERENT component of this
dataset's architecture (the complementarity test, the relational-position fitness component, each
of the two biodiversity poles, the lifecycle log's completeness, the ecology's own authority
boundary, and the fossilization package's provenance), and a future case that does not map onto any
of these seven components is, by that fact alone, evidence of an architectural gap this dataset has
not yet named — which is itself a useful diagnostic: an implementer encountering a NEW gaming
vector should first ask which of the seven existing defenses it evades, and if the answer is
genuinely none, that finding is itself a candidate for a future revision of this Part, following
IAS-F1's own additive-versus-revisory versioning discipline (Part XXII §22.5) rather than an ad hoc
patch.

FINAL LAW — PART XXIV. Every adversarial case this dataset can foresee — gamed symbiosis, gamed
fitness, biodiversity-washing in either direction, selective reporting, the ecology's own
mechanisms overreaching their bounded recommendation-only role, or adversarial fossilization
inflating a retirement's apparent cost — is defended not by a separate anti-gaming layer bolted on
afterward, but by the SAME evidence-level discipline, MEASURED-stage falsifiability, and
authority-preserving restraint this dataset already specifies for honest operation; a mechanism
this rigorously bounded resists gaming as a structural consequence of being correctly bounded, not
as an additional feature.

PART XXV — METRICS AND ANTI-GOODHART COUNTERMETRICS
===============================================================================================

25.1 Why every metric in this dataset ships with a countermetric. Following the depth contract's
own requirement and this dataset's own repeated warnings against single blended scores (Part III
§3.1, Part XIX §19.7) and gameable targets (Part XXIV throughout), this Part specifies the
ecology's headline metrics NEVER in isolation, but always paired with the countermetric that
detects the metric being optimized in a way that defeats its own purpose — the general discipline
Goodhart's observation names (a measure that becomes a target ceases to be a good measure) applied
concretely to seven metric pairs, one per major mechanism this dataset specifies.

25.2 METRIC: Confirmed-symbiosis rate (CONFIRMED outcomes / total ACTIVATED symbiosis flags per
cycle). COUNTERMETRIC: canary-to-full-activation ratio and average time-to-REFUTED. A rising
confirmed-symbiosis rate, alone, could indicate either genuine improvement in the detector's
complementarity test OR that the detector has been quietly tuned to only flag OBVIOUS, low-risk
pairs, avoiding the genuinely uncertain, higher-value dormant pairs that carry more risk of
refutation but also more potential gain (§5.3's whole point was surfacing NON-obvious, dormant
pairs). The countermetric — is the detector still finding pairs that required real analysis to
surface, evidenced by a healthy, non-zero rate of canary-stage activity and a non-trivial average
time before a REFUTED outcome resolves (rather than refutations resolving suspiciously fast,
suggesting trivially-obvious-and-safe flags) — catches a detector that has drifted toward
conservatism disguised as improved accuracy.

25.3 METRIC: Parasite count declining over successive cycles. COUNTERMETRIC: adjudication-pass
rate and mean cost-accumulation-before-flag. A declining raw parasite count is ambiguous on its
own — it could mean genuine population health improving, OR it could mean the DETECTOR's
sensitivity has been quietly narrowed (the exact zero-cannot-fall pathology this dataset's own
authoring context has independently observed elsewhere: an unrecognized or under-detected
condition reads as zero, and zero cannot fall further, hiding a real problem behind an
apparently-improving number). The countermetric tracks whether the ADJUDICATION PASS RATE (Part
VIII §8.6 — the fraction of raw detector flags that survive all four adjudication checks) has
remained stable or risen (evidence the detector's raw sensitivity is intact and adjudication is
doing real filtering work) versus collapsed toward near-100% or near-0% (either extreme suggesting
the detector or the adjudication protocol has been miscalibrated to trivially pass or trivially
reject everything), paired with mean cost-accumulation-before-flag (are parasites being caught
EARLIER, the genuinely healthy signal, or is the population simply generating fewer NEW parasitic
conditions while existing ones go undetected for LONGER — a distinction the raw count alone cannot
make).

25.4 METRIC: Vacancy registry size (Part IX §9.6). COUNTERMETRIC: vacancy age distribution and
escalation-cost trend. A shrinking vacancy registry could mean genuine gap-filling (healthy) or
could mean the registry's severity threshold (Part IX §9.4) has been quietly raised, filtering out
genuine but lower-severity vacancies from the count. The countermetric tracks the AGE DISTRIBUTION
of remaining vacancy entries (are old, long-standing vacancies actually being resolved, or does the
registry's apparent shrinkage come entirely from newer, lower-severity entries while old, high-
severity vacancies persist untouched) and the ESCALATION-COST TREND (Part IX §9.4's third
component) across the population as a whole, independent of registry size — a population where
Owner-escalation events are rising even as the named vacancy registry shrinks is a population where
real gaps are going undetected, not resolved.

25.5 METRIC: Speciation/merger event count per cycle. COUNTERMETRIC: re-merger/re-speciation
(rollback) rate as a fraction of total events. A rising event count alone is not informative
without its rollback rate, per Part XXII §22.4's rollback discipline — a healthy population shows
SOME non-zero rollback rate (evidence the evidence thresholds are appropriately calibrated to
allow genuine, if imperfect, action rather than being set so conservatively that only certain-
to-succeed candidates ever proceed) but a rollback rate that is RISING over successive calibration
cycles, despite Part XII §12.4 and Part XIII §13.4's calibration feedback loops, is the direct
signal that the underlying divergence or overlap tests require more than routine threshold
tightening — the SPECIATION CHURN failure mode (Part XXIII §23.6) this metric pair exists
specifically to surface.

25.6 METRIC: Monoculture-risk-flag resolution rate (Part XVIII §18.5). COUNTERMETRIC: mean
dependency-concentration trend across the population, independent of flag resolution. A high
resolution rate (flagged monocultures receiving deliberate-redundancy recommendations that are
subsequently built) could still coexist with a population whose OVERALL structural concentration is
worsening, if new monocultures are forming faster than flagged ones are being resolved. The
countermetric — the population's mean dependency-concentration trend, tracked independent of
whether any individual flag has been "resolved" — catches this net-negative case, ensuring the
metric being watched is the population's actual structural health, not merely the governor's own
throughput.

25.7 METRIC: Genome-transfer completeness (Part XVII §17.2's continuity-of-law cross-check pass
rate). COUNTERMETRIC: fossil-record retrieval rate — how often a fossilized principle or boundary-
knowledge fact is actually CONSUMED by a later mechanism (Part XV §15.3's stated purpose — the
Cognitive Multiplier or a future niche-filling system reusing it) versus sitting unretrieved. A
100% transfer-completeness rate is worthless if nothing downstream ever reads the transferred
genome or fossil record — the countermetric confirms the preserved law is actually being
REUSED, not merely archived, which is the specific test that distinguishes genuine institutional
memory from ceremonial record-keeping.

25.8 An eighth pair, cutting across all seven: MEASUREMENT COVERAGE versus MEASUREMENT
CONFIDENCE, the meta-metric this dataset applies to itself. Beyond the seven mechanism-specific
pairs (§25.2-25.7), this dataset names one final, cross-cutting pair that applies to the LANDSCAPE
substrate every other metric ultimately reads from. METRIC: the fraction of the population's
(system, environment) cells holding a DEFINED landscape value (Part XXVI §26.1's Protocol One,
LANDSCAPE COVERAGE). COUNTERMETRIC: the AVERAGE EVIDENCE LEVEL of those defined cells — a rising
coverage rate achieved by lowering the bar for what counts as "defined" (accepting HYPOTHESIZED
or CONTESTED readings as though they were DERIVED or OBSERVED) would show healthy-looking coverage
growth while actually degrading the confidence every downstream mechanism places in that coverage,
exactly the same narrowed-sensitivity-disguised-as-improvement pattern §25.3's parasite-count
metric already warns against, now applied to the substrate layer itself rather than to any single
mechanism built on top of it. This eighth pair is what makes Protocol One and the evidence-level
discipline of Article V mutually reinforcing rather than independently specified: coverage without
confidence is not genuine landscape health, and this dataset's own metrics discipline would be
incomplete if it audited every mechanism's OUTPUT metrics while leaving the shared INPUT substrate
itself unaudited.

25.9 Reporting cadence: why these eight pairs are read at every evaluation cycle, never on an ad
hoc or triggered-only basis. Consistent with the cycle discipline established at Part II §2.8,
every metric-countermetric pair this Part specifies is computed at EVERY evaluation cycle, not
merely when a governance surface happens to request a report or when an anomaly has already been
noticed by some other means — a metrics regime that only computes on request is structurally
vulnerable to never being requested during exactly the period a gaming vector or a genuine decline
is active, the same invisible-until-queried failure Part XXIII §23.5 already names for protected
parasitism, generalized here to the measurement layer itself. NORMATIVE: the eight pairs form a
STANDING DASHBOARD, computed unconditionally every cycle and stored as part of the same append-only
calibration record (Part XXIV §24.5) this dataset's other mechanisms already depend on, ensuring a
retrospective audit can always reconstruct the metrics' own trajectory across the population's full
history, not merely their value at whatever moment someone happened to look.

FINAL LAW — PART XXV. No metric in this dataset stands alone; each of the seven mechanism-specific
headline metrics, plus one cross-cutting coverage-versus-confidence pair applied to the shared
landscape substrate itself, is paired with a countermetric specifically designed to detect the
metric being satisfied through narrowed sensitivity, conservative flagging, or ceremonial record-
keeping rather than through genuine population health, computed unconditionally every cycle rather
than on request — because a metric this dataset cannot show resisting its own gaming is a metric
this dataset has not actually specified, only named.

D1. DECISION: Draw the ecology's boundary against D2A and evolution_engine on TEMPORAL-POPULATION
GRAIN (Part I) rather than on a new relation type, a new authority class, or a broader definition
of "duplication." ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED: (a) define the ecology as governing a seventh relation
type beyond IAS-F1's six; (b) define the ecology as holding a new, standing authority class
alongside D2A's duplicates authority. WHY THIS: IAS-F1 Part VI §6.1 closes the relation-type set
deliberately, and introducing a seventh type here would duplicate F1's own object rather than the
ecology's intended target; a new standing authority class would violate Invariant Three (exactly
one final authority per decision class) the moment it overlapped D2A's existing duplicates
authority. Grain, not kind, is the only boundary that adds a genuinely new dimension without
touching either parent's existing authority. TRADE-OFFS: a grain-based boundary is less viscerally
intuitive to state in one sentence than a new-relation-type boundary would have been, and requires
Part I's full argument to land. REVERSAL CONDITIONS: if a future sibling dataset needs a relation
type this dataset's six inherited types genuinely cannot express, that gap belongs to a proposed
IAS-F1 revision, never to a workaround built inside this dataset.

D2. DECISION: Require every one of the seven core mechanisms (symbiosis, parasitism, niche,
speciation, merger, extinction, biodiversity) to stop at RECOMMENDATION and hand execution to D2A,
evolution_engine, or CO-06, rather than letting the ecology execute any action directly.
ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED: (a) grant the ecology direct execution rights for at least the
lower-risk actions (symbiosis activation) while reserving higher-risk actions (extinction) for
D2A; (b) build the ecology as a fully autonomous population manager. WHY THIS: any execution right
granted to the ecology, however narrowly scoped, would create a second final authority over a
decision class D2A or evolution_engine already holds, violating Invariant Three outright; the
uniform recommendation-only boundary is the only version of this dataset compatible with the
ensemble's existing authority structure. TRADE-OFFS: every action this dataset recommends carries
an extra handoff latency (PROPOSED-to-executed is never automatic), which is a real cost against
the tightening-loop value the Compounding Graph describes. REVERSAL CONDITIONS: none anticipated —
this decision is close to load-bearing as any in the dataset, on par with IAS-F1's own D3 (per-fact
provenance); reversing it would require rewriting Invariant Three itself, an IAS-F1-level change
far outside this dataset's own scope.

D3. DECISION: Hold speciation and merger to asymmetrically higher evidence bars than symbiosis
activation — multi-cycle divergence corroboration for speciation, multi-environment overlap
corroboration for merger — rather than a single uniform evidence bar across all three. ALTERNATIVES
CONSIDERED: (a) one uniform evidence threshold applied identically to all PROPOSED-stage actions;
(b) scale the bar purely by estimated cost of the underlying operation, independent of the specific
failure mode each action risks. WHY THIS: fragmentation and forced consolidation (Parts XII, XIV)
are structurally harder to reverse and more consumer-disruptive than a REFUTED symbiosis edge,
which reverses by simply removing one edge; a uniform bar would either under-protect against
fragmentation/consolidation or over-burden the comparatively low-risk symbiosis pathway. TRADE-
OFFS: asymmetric bars require an implementer to correctly classify which failure-mode risk profile
a given action belongs to before applying the right bar, a design complexity a uniform bar would
have avoided. REVERSAL CONDITIONS: if Protocol Three's red-team evaluation (Part XXVI §26.1) finds
the asymmetry does not track actual observed reversal costs on the real estate, the bar-assignment
logic (not the asymmetry principle itself) is a revisory candidate.

D4. DECISION: Make fossilization MANDATORY, not optional, for every extinction recommendation this
dataset produces, at the cost of adding a pre-verdict extraction step to every retire pathway.
ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED: (a) make fossilization OPTIONAL, recommended but not required, deferring
to D2A's own judgment on a case-by-case basis; (b) fossilize only systems above a certain fitness
or lineage-depth threshold, treating low-value systems as not worth the extraction effort. WHY
THIS: extinction's asymmetric irreversibility (Part XV §15.6) means an optional or threshold-gated
fossilization would, by construction, silently lose the law of every system that happened to fall
below whatever threshold was chosen — and a threshold chosen wrong is unrecoverable in exactly the
same way an un-fossilized extinction is. TRADE-OFFS: mandatory fossilization adds real overhead to
every extinction recommendation, including ones where, in retrospect, little of value existed to
extract. REVERSAL CONDITIONS: none anticipated; this is the direct application of Article IX's
rollback mandate to the one mechanism where literal rollback is structurally impossible, and
weakening it would leave Article IX's requirement unmet for the extinction pathway specifically.

D5. DECISION: Fold Family H (dataset genome/lineage, candidates 71-80) into this dataset as Parts
XX-XXI rather than treating genome and lineage as separate, standalone concerns bolted onto the
end. ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED: (a) leave genome/lineage as scattered, implicit mentions at each
genome-transfer point (Parts XI, XIII, XV) with no unifying Part; (b) build genome/lineage as an
appendix reference section rather than full Parts with their own worked examples and FINAL LAW.
WHY THIS: the Non-Duplication Ledger's own routing ("Genome/lineage folded into IAS-D1") is an
explicit instruction, not a suggestion, and Part XX §20.7 demonstrates directly why scattering the
concept across four Parts without a unifying formalization would leave a reader unable to confirm
whether Part XI's and Part XIII's inheritance rules were the same mechanism. TRADE-OFFS: two full
Parts dedicated to genome and lineage is a substantial share of this dataset's total depth budget
for a concept the ledger itself calls a "fold" rather than a "build," a proportionality question a
future audit might reasonably raise. REVERSAL CONDITIONS: if a future audit finds genome/lineage
content duplicates a Family-H-adjacent dataset's own treatment (none exists as of this sealing,
per the ledger's REFERENCE-not-BUILD disposition for Family H), the two Parts are a candidate for
compression, not deletion, since the fold instruction itself is not reversible.

D6. DECISION: Specify seven metric-countermetric pairs (Part XXV) rather than a single composite
ecology-health score. ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED: (a) one blended ecology-health index combining
fitness, biodiversity, parasitism, and vacancy trends into a single number for easy executive
reporting; (b) metrics without paired countermetrics, trusting implementers to add anti-gaming
logic later as needed. WHY THIS: Part III §3.1's anti-blending discipline and Part XIX §19.7's
explicit refusal to assert a target biodiversity number both establish, independently, that this
dataset's own founding stance is hostile to single blended scores precisely because they are
gameable from multiple directions at once and hide WHICH direction a decline is coming from; adding
countermetrics at specification time, rather than deferring them, ensures no implementation ships
a metric without its anti-Goodhart pair already named. TRADE-OFFS: seven metric pairs are harder to
report at a glance than one composite score, a real cost against executive legibility that a future
dashboard-layer specification (outside this dataset's own scope) would need to solve without
collapsing the pairs back into one ungameable-in-appearance number. REVERSAL CONDITIONS: a
composite score remains permissible as a DERIVED, presentation-layer aggregation for reporting
purposes, provided the seven underlying pairs remain the authoritative, individually-inspectable
record beneath it — the reversal this decision forecloses is treating a composite as the PRIMARY
artifact, never its existence as a secondary view.

pending real operational data.

ANTI-PATTERNS
===============================================================================================

ANTI-PATTERN 1 — TREATING A LANDSCAPE-SHAPE SIGNAL AS SUFFICIENT EVIDENCE FOR SPECIATION OR
MERGER, SKIPPING THE INDEPENDENT CORROBORATION STAGE. The most likely implementation error, given
how visually compelling a bimodal fitness landscape or a high overlap-percentage reading is once
computed, is an implementer or a governance surface treating the landscape signal ALONE as
sufficient grounds to propose a split or a fusion, skipping Part XI §11.2's change-history
corroboration or Part XIII §13.4's multi-environment corroboration because the landscape reading
already looks decisive. This is dangerous specifically because it is the exact precondition for
fragmentation (Part XII) and forced consolidation (Part XIV §14.1), both of which this dataset
demonstrates are detectable only AFTER the costly, hard-to-reverse action has already been taken.
PREVENTION: implement the CANDIDATE-to-DIVERGENCE-CONFIRMED (or CANDIDATE-to-OVERLAP-CONFIRMED)
transition as a HARD GATE that cannot be bypassed by a compelling-looking landscape shape alone —
the corroboration stage is not a formality to expedite when the initial signal looks strong, it is
precisely there because strong-looking signals are the ones most likely to tempt a skip, and Part
XIV §14.1's own naming of forced consolidation as the merger-side mirror of fragmentation confirms
this failure recurs identically on both the split and fuse pathways.

ANTI-PATTERN 2 — READING A SINGLE BIODIVERSITY-GOVERNOR FLAG IN ISOLATION FROM THE OTHER POLE,
PRODUCING A CORRECTIVE ACTION THAT WORSENS THE OPPOSITE FAILURE. Because the two poles (Parts
XVIII-XIX) are specified as separate, labeled outputs rather than one blended score (Part XIX
§19.6), a likely implementation error is a governance surface consuming ONLY the monoculture-risk
flag stream, or only the proliferation-risk flag stream, and acting on it without checking the
OTHER pole's current state for the same niche — building deliberate redundancy to resolve a
monoculture flag (Part XVIII §18.5) without first confirming the same niche is not ALSO showing a
proliferation flag, producing exactly the substitutable-occupant proliferation the anti-
proliferation pole exists to catch, or vice versa: merging two systems to resolve a proliferation
flag without checking whether one of them held a monoculture-mitigating redundant path the merger
would eliminate. PREVENTION: the biodiversity governor's output contract must be consumed as a
PAIR, per niche, always — any implementation that reads one pole's flag stream without the other,
even for a single niche's single decision, has re-introduced the exact tension Part XIX §19.6
exists to resolve, by resolving it silently and incorrectly instead of explicitly and jointly.

ANTI-PATTERN 3 — LETTING THE LIFECYCLE LOG BECOME A SUMMARY REPORT, DEFEATING THE SELECTIVE-
REPORTING DEFENSE. Part XXIV §24.5 specifies that the calibration record's source of truth must be
the mandatory, append-only lifecycle log itself, distinct from any narrative summary a maintaining
agent might separately produce. The third likely implementation error, especially as a population
scales and the raw lifecycle log grows large, is an implementer building a convenient SUMMARY VIEW
of the log for dashboard or reporting purposes, and then, over time, having downstream calibration
mechanisms (Parts IV, VI, XII, XVII) come to read the SUMMARY rather than the underlying log
directly — at which point any lossy compression the summary view applies (dropping REFUTED entries
that look uninteresting, rounding evidence levels for display) silently reintroduces the exact
selective-reporting vulnerability the append-only-log design was built to prevent, not through
malice but through an entirely reasonable engineering shortcut taken for performance or
readability. PREVENTION: calibration mechanisms must be specified, and implemented, to read
directly from the append-only lifecycle log or a lossless index over it — never from a summary
view, however convenient — with the summary view treated strictly as a downstream, human-facing
artifact with no calibration-consuming role.
