DFP-02 — KNOWLEDGE INFRASTRUCTURE MODE
Dataset First Protocol — the corpus-lifecycle axis of Claude Power Pack
Version 1. Founded 2026-07-12. Binding ontology: CANONICAL_ONTOLOGY.md (DFP family).
Composes, and never re-narrates: DRK (decisions), ACIS (epistemic levels E0 through E7), SQI (verification of executable reality), D2A (duplicate detection), graphify (the knowledge graph), spec_gate (task tiers and the specification gate sealed as HR-SPEC-001), hard_rules (invariants).


PART I — MISSION: WHY A CORPUS NEEDS A LIFECYCLE, AND THE MEASURED EVIDENCE THAT THE FAMILIES HAVE NONE

1.1 The subject of this dataset is a corpus: a body of institutional knowledge, authored deliberately, intended to govern an implementation that does not exist yet. A corpus is not a document, and the difference is the whole reason this dataset exists. A document is finished when its author stops writing. A corpus is finished when it can be handed to a stranger who will build against it, and the stranger will build correctly, and the corpus will still be answering questions after the stranger has gone. Between those two conditions lies an entire lifecycle, and the Power Pack has been operating without one.

1.2 The claim that the lifecycle is absent is not rhetorical, and it is not an aesthetic complaint about inconsistency. It is a measurement, taken at this family's own architecture stop, across the sealed corpora the repository already holds. Ten and more dataset families exist on disk: the epistemic family, the decision kernel, the verification family, the frontier family, the cost family, the graph family, the design-intelligence family, the duplicate-handling family, the specification operating system, and the session-execution family. Every one was built by the same estate under the same doctrine, and every one invented its own fabrication rubric from scratch. There is no shared standard because there is no owner of the standard, and there is no owner because nobody ever declared that how a corpus is built is itself a governed surface.

1.3 The consequence is measurable and it is an order of magnitude. The decision-kernel family ships datasets between two thousand five hundred and four thousand four hundred and five words. The verification family's fabrication contract mandates twenty-four thousand to thirty thousand words per dataset, and its first two sealed datasets landed at twenty-five thousand seven hundred and eighty-two and twenty-six thousand nine hundred and eighty-nine. Both were authored inside the same repository, under the same governance, by the same kind of process, and each believed it had a standard. Between them sits a factor of roughly ten. The reference band this family adopted at its architecture stop — eight thousand to fifteen thousand words per dataset, eight hundred to fifteen hundred per Part, eight to fifteen Parts — was derived by measuring quality references rather than by asserting a preference, and it matches neither extreme. Ten families, ten private rubrics, one order of magnitude, no arbiter.

1.4 Density is the symptom that is easy to measure, but it is not the disease. The disease is that three stages of a corpus's life are entirely unowned. No family defines Knowledge QA — the mechanical, executable, deterministic check that a corpus meets the form it declared for itself. No family defines Knowledge Certification — the recorded assertion, made by an authority that is not the author, that the corpus reached its standard, naming precisely what it governs and what it does not. No family defines Knowledge Freeze — the moment after which the corpus is immutable except by recorded amendment, so that a downstream implementation can be built against a text that will not move underneath it. Without those three, a corpus is a folder of essays with a good intention: it cannot be audited, it cannot be trusted by a consumer, and it cannot be finished.

1.5 A lifecycle solves three problems that no amount of authorial care can solve. Comparability: two corpora built to a declared shared standard can be reviewed by the same reviewer, gated by the same gate, budgeted by the same estimate, and compared for value, while two corpora built to private standards can be compared only by taste, and taste is not a governance instrument. Consumability: an implementation that begins against a corpus still in motion is building on sand, and the freeze is what converts the corpus into a substrate. And terminability, which this dataset treats as its centre of gravity. A body of knowledge with no defined end state does not end. It accretes. It becomes the work rather than the enablement of the work, and at that point the discipline meant to protect the estate from premature concrete has become the thing preventing any concrete at all.

1.6 This dataset owns the lifecycle and nothing else. It does not decide whether a corpus is necessary — that is the sufficiency verdict defined in the family's canonical ontology, reviewed as a decision by DRK. It does not assign an epistemic level to any claim; ACIS owns the ladder from E0 to E7 and this family reads it without ever writing to it. It does not verify executable reality; SQI owns that axis, and a certified corpus makes no claim about running code. It does not detect duplicates; D2A owns that surface and supplies the per-candidate verdicts the architecture stage consumes. It does not maintain the knowledge graph; graphify owns navigation, and the reuse measurements used here are read from graphify's edges. It does not classify task tiers or demand specifications; spec_gate owns that and its gate is already sealed as an invariant. All of the above is cross-referenced, and a Part that re-explains any of them has failed its own review criteria.

1.7 The mode is named Knowledge Infrastructure Mode because it is a mode in the strict sense: a temporary operating state, entered under a stated condition, exited under a stated condition, and inert the rest of the time. When the sufficiency verdict says the knowledge already exists, this dataset has nothing to say and the build proceeds without it. When the verdict says the governing science does not exist yet, the mode activates, the corpus is routed through eight gated stages, and the mode ends. The ending is not an afterthought appended to the design. It is the design's most load-bearing property, and Part IX exists to make the ending as hard to avoid as the starting.

1.8 The failure this mode must avoid is precisely the failure that would justify every objection to it. A protocol that always demands a corpus is as destructive as one that never does, because it becomes a tax, and a tax is routed around, and a rule that is routed around is not a rule but a fiction consuming review cycles while governing nothing. The most dangerous version of that failure is not refusal; it is compliance without termination. A team that enters the mode sincerely, authors diligently, reviews carefully, and never reaches certification has not been governed. It has been stalled by an instrument it trusted. The mode's authority depends entirely on its ability to hand back control, on schedule, with a certificate and a frozen text, and to be seen doing so repeatedly.

1.9 Done criteria for the mission are three checkable statements: that the estate's corpora are demonstrably built to incompatible standards, which the density measurement establishes; that Knowledge QA, Knowledge Certification, and Knowledge Freeze have no owner anywhere in the repository, which a search establishes by returning nothing; and that the absence has a cost, which the inability to compare, consume, or terminate a corpus establishes every time a family finishes by declaring itself finished. If a future audit falsifies all three, this dataset is retired, and DFP-05 is the mechanism that makes that retirement reachable rather than unthinkable.

— PART I FINAL LAW —
A corpus that declares its own standard, judges its own compliance, and ends when its author decides it has ended is not governed knowledge but a private essay with institutional pretensions, and ten families measured an order of magnitude apart in density is the receipt.


PART II — THE EIGHT STAGES: ENTRY GATES, EXIT GATES, AND WHY IMPLEMENTATION IS UNREACHABLE BEFORE CERTIFICATION

2.1 The lifecycle is a state machine with eight states, one legal forward edge between each adjacent pair, exactly one legal backward edge in the whole machine, and no other transitions. The states, in order, are ARCHITECTURE, ONTOLOGY, AUTHORING, REVIEW, QA, CERTIFIED, FROZEN, IMPLEMENTATION. The single legal backward edge runs from FROZEN to REVIEW and is called amendment; it is governed in Part VIII and it is the only way a sealed text ever changes. Every other backward motion — an author reopening AUTHORING after REVIEW has begun, a family slipping from QA back into AUTHORING to add material a gate disliked, a certification revisited because an implementer found the corpus inconvenient — is illegal, and each has a name and a countermeasure here because each is the natural, sincere, well-intentioned thing to do.

2.2 The stage of a corpus is a fact recorded in its manifest, not a description of how the authors feel about their progress. The manifest is the single state carrier: family, current stage, the immutable count of Parts planned, the count of Parts sealed, the fabrication standard the corpus bound itself to, the certificate once one exists, the freeze timestamp once one exists, and the siblings this corpus composes. Anything not in the manifest is not part of the corpus's governed state. This matters because the most common way a lifecycle degrades is that its stages become adjectives — a family is basically in review, a family is nearly done — and an adjective cannot be gated. A stage that is not written down is a stage that cannot refuse to advance.

2.3 The entry gate of a stage is a condition that must hold before the corpus may occupy it; the exit gate is a condition that must hold before it may leave. They are not one check performed twice. An entry gate protects the stage from work not ready for it; an exit gate protects the next stage from work that is not finished. A family that checks only exits will fill authoring with Parts written against an ontology never sealed, and discover at review that half the corpus uses two names for one object. A family that checks only entries will let unfinished work advance because the next stage was willing to accept it. Both exist at every boundary, and both are mechanical wherever a mechanical form of the check exists.

2.4 ARCHITECTURE is entered when a knowledge sufficiency verdict returns the mandatory-corpus class — a judgment produced outside this dataset and reviewed as a decision by DRK. It is exited when a Reality Scan has assigned every candidate dataset a verdict of new, extend, or reference, and the count of Parts planned has been declared. Part III owns this stage in full.

2.5 ONTOLOGY is entered when the architecture is approved. It is exited when every canonical object the corpus intends to define has both a named producer and at least one named consumer, and every term has exactly one definition. Part IV owns it, and its exit gate is the one most often skipped, because an ontology feels like preamble and authors are impatient to write the substance.

2.6 AUTHORING is entered when the ontology is sealed and exited when every planned Part exists on disk and meets the declared fabrication standard. That exit is a count and a form, both mechanically checkable, and it says nothing about quality; quality is the next stage's business. Conflating them is how an authoring stage comes to have no bright line at which it ends.

2.7 REVIEW is entered when authoring is complete and exited when every Part has been checked against the rejection criteria by someone who did not author it, and every rejected Part rewritten and re-checked. QA is entered when review is clean and exited when the mechanical gates pass hermetically three times with identical output. The separation between them is deliberate and non-negotiable: review judges meaning and cannot be automated; QA judges form and must be. A family that runs only review has no gate that can refuse without a human in the loop. A family that runs only QA has a corpus that is perfectly shaped and possibly vacuous.

2.8 CERTIFIED is entered when QA is green three times hermetically, and exited only into FROZEN. Certification is an assertion made by an authority that did not author the corpus, and it names what the corpus governs and, with equal precision, what it does not. FROZEN is entered on certification and exited only into IMPLEMENTATION, or laterally into REVIEW through the amendment edge. Part VIII owns both.

2.9 IMPLEMENTATION is the terminal state, and its unreachability from any earlier stage is the entire protocol. When the sufficiency verdict was the mandatory-corpus class, code may not be written against the corpus until the corpus is frozen. Not should not — may not, as a gate, because the point of the apparatus is that a permanent decision must not be encoded in an artifact before the science governing the decision exists. If implementation may begin from AUTHORING, the corpus is a companion document rather than a governing one, and every incentive acting on an author under deadline will drag it into becoming a retroactive description of what was built. That is the exact inversion the protocol exists to prevent, and the gate is the only thing preventing it.

2.10 The unreachability has a limit, and stating it is not a weakening. The gate applies to the class of build that triggered the mode. It does not freeze the estate: other work proceeds, bugs are fixed, sibling families ship. A mode that spreads its block beyond its subject has become the bureaucracy of Part IX and must be caught by the same detectors.

2.11 Every gate in this machine fails open in one direction and closed in the other, and the asymmetry is intentional. The advisory surfaces of this family — classifier, sufficiency engine, detectors — fail open: an error yields silence and the session continues, because an advisor that crashes into a block has become an unelected authority. The lifecycle gates fail closed: if the QA gate cannot run, the corpus does not advance, because a gate that passes when it cannot execute is worse than no gate, having converted an unknown into a green. That is the difference between a recommendation and a contract, and this dataset governs contracts.

2.12 Stage transitions are recorded, not merely performed: each advance writes the stage, the timestamp, the evidence that satisfied the exit gate, and who authorized it. Two later questions then have answers — how long each stage took, which feeds the anti-bureaucracy metrics of Part X, and whether any stage advanced without its gate, which catches a family that narrated the lifecycle rather than executing it.

— PART II FINAL LAW —
The stage of a corpus is a recorded fact with an entry gate and an exit gate, never an adjective an author applies to their own progress, and implementation is unreachable before certification because a corpus that may be implemented while still in motion inevitably becomes a description of what was built rather than the science that governed it.


PART III — THE ARCHITECTURE STAGE: THE REALITY SCAN AND THE IMMUTABLE DENOMINATOR

3.1 The architecture stage answers two questions and refuses to answer any others: what datasets must exist, and how many Parts there will be. Everything else — what the datasets say, how they are structured internally, what the ontology contains — belongs downstream. A narrow architecture stage matters because architecture is where enthusiasm is highest and evidence is lowest, and a stage that lets the enthusiastic specify content produces a plan the authoring stage spends its life failing to satisfy.

3.2 The entry gate is a sufficiency verdict of the mandatory-corpus class, and the verdict must name at least one missing kind of knowledge. The canonical ontology enumerates seven: foundational science, governance, ontology, taxonomy, protocol, benchmark, evaluator. A corpus convened without naming which kind is missing was convened on a feeling, and a feeling of largeness is not a signal. The requirement is load-bearing in both directions: it prevents a corpus being demanded for a task that merely looks large, and it tells the stage what the corpus is for. A corpus missing an evaluator and a corpus missing a foundational science are different artifacts with different Parts, and a scan that does not know which it is building will produce the generic table of contents that Part VI rejects.

3.3 The Reality Scan is the stage's central mechanism, and its output is a per-candidate verdict, never a global one. For each dataset the source specification proposes, the scan asks what the repository already holds and returns exactly one of three verdicts. NEW: the surface is genuinely unowned and the dataset must be authored. EXTEND: a parent already owns the surface and the correct delivery is a reinforcement of that parent — usually code, occasionally an amendment to the parent's own corpus — never a sibling dataset competing with it. REFERENCE: the surface is owned so completely that the correct delivery is a cross-reference and nothing else. The per-candidate granularity is what makes the scan honest. A scan returning one verdict for a whole proposed family will return new, because at least one candidate always is, and the rest will be smuggled in behind it.

3.4 The scan is not performed by opinion. It consumes D2A's duplicate verdicts against the family registry, and D2A owns that surface entirely; this dataset consumes the result and routes on it. The one contribution architecture makes to that pipeline is a demand: the registry D2A searches must contain the sealed families, or the scan is searching an empty room and will return new for everything. A duplicate detector that cannot see the parents is not a detector; it is a rubber stamp with a plausible interface.

3.5 The evidence that the scan is not ceremonial comes from the two families that have run it. This family's own specification proposed seven datasets. The scan found the central thesis already partly law in the specification gate, and four of the seven substantially owned by existing parents. Three were approved; four were routed to D2A and recorded as do-not-build verdicts with the reasoning attached, and the numbering gaps in the family index are deliberately preserved so that a reader who asks where the missing datasets went is answered by a document rather than by silence. The verification family's specification proposed seventeen; its scan approved fourteen and then scoped the active build to four. In both cases the scan removed a substantial fraction of the proposed corpus before a word was authored, and that fraction is the difference between a mode that terminates and one that does not.

3.6 The exit gate is the declaration of the count of Parts planned, and that count is immutable for the life of the corpus. The mechanism is simple: the count is written into the manifest at architecture and can never be raised. Completion is measured as Parts sealed over Parts planned, and a denominator that can grow is a completion metric defeatable by a single act of good intentions. Every corpus in mid-authoring discovers material it did not anticipate. That discovery is normal, valuable, and must not be permitted to move the finish line.

3.7 The declaration is a forecast and will sometimes be wrong, so the stage states what happens when it is. Shrinking is always legal: a corpus that finds a planned Part unnecessary may seal fewer than it planned, provided the reduction is recorded with a reason and the certificate names the Parts dropped. A corpus that shrinks has learned something and its certificate is honest about it. Growing is never legal in flight. Material discovered during authoring that genuinely exceeds the planned scope has two legal destinations: compressed into an existing planned Part, or recorded as a candidate for a sibling family with its own architecture stage, its own scan, and its own denominator. The asymmetry is not arbitrary: shrinking terminates the corpus sooner, growing terminates it later, and only one of those directions can run away.

3.8 Three characteristic failure modes, each observed. The scan as formality: the scan is run, every candidate returns new, and the architecture is approved in the time it takes to read the specification back. The detector is arithmetic — a scan whose new-verdict rate is one hundred percent is either the first family in an empty repository or a scan that did not search, and after ten sealed families the first explanation is unavailable. The vanity denominator: a family declares twenty Parts because twenty sounds substantial, then spends authoring manufacturing material for a number chosen before it knew anything; the countermeasure is the density band of Part V, which makes an inflated Part count visible as thin Parts rather than as impressive scope. Architecture-as-content: the stage produces a detailed outline of what each Part will say, authoring discovers half of it is wrong, and the corpus spends its life either obeying a bad plan or silently abandoning it. The stage's charter is a count and a set of verdicts. It is not a draft.

3.9 The structured example, on synthetic data. A family proposes six datasets; the sufficiency verdict names two missing knowledge kinds, protocol and evaluator. Candidate one has no parent and touches both: new. Candidate two restates a sealed parent's taxonomy in different words: reference, delivered as a cross-reference paragraph inside candidate one. Candidate three would build a second detector against a surface an existing engine owns: do-not-build, routed to that engine as an extension. Candidates four and five are one idea split in two by an author who mistook length for scope: merged into a single new dataset. Candidate six is genuinely new but addresses neither missing kind: deferred, because a dataset that fills no named gap has no claim on the mode's budget. Two datasets are approved, the counts of Parts planned are declared at ten and eleven, and the stage is over.

3.10 Done criteria: a sufficiency verdict on file naming at least one missing knowledge kind; a per-candidate verdict for every proposed dataset, with the do-not-build verdicts recorded rather than deleted; a declared, immutable count of Parts planned for each approved dataset; and a written list of the siblings this corpus will compose, which becomes the non-duplication contract that Part VI enforces at review.

— PART III FINAL LAW —
Every candidate dataset receives its own verdict against what the repository already holds, the do-not-build verdicts are recorded rather than deleted, and the count of Parts planned is declared once and is immutable thereafter — because a corpus whose denominator can grow has no finish line, only a direction.


PART IV — THE ONTOLOGY STAGE: EVERY OBJECT NEEDS A PRODUCER AND A CONSUMER

4.1 The ontology stage exists because a corpus is a shared language before it is anything else, and a language with two words for one thing, or one word for two things, produces an implementation with two implementations of one thing and one implementation of two. The stage is short, unglamorous, and the most often skipped, because it stands between an approved architecture and the writing everyone wants to do. Skipping it does not remove the work. It relocates it into review, where it appears as a dozen Parts that must be rewritten because each coined its own vocabulary for the same object.

4.2 The entry gate is an approved architecture. The exit gate is a single condition, the strictest in the lifecycle: every canonical object the corpus defines has a named producer and at least one named consumer. Not a hypothetical producer. Not a producer to be built later. A named producer and a named consumer, both written down, both traceable to a module, an agent, a gate, or a Part of the corpus itself.

4.3 The condition has two failure modes, mirror images of each other. The orphan object has a consumer and no producer: something reads it, nothing writes it. The estate has met this exactly, and the memory is precise — a field defined in a schema, consumed by working code, exercised by a passing test, and null across every live record, because no producer ever wrote it. The consumer ran. The consumer was correct. The consumer was reading a value that had never existed. Nothing failed, nothing alerted, and the capability the field was supposed to enable was dead by starvation while every signal reported health. The detector is cheap: for every object, ask what writes it, and demand a name.

4.4 The dead object is the reverse: a producer and no consumer. Something writes it, nothing reads it. This is the orphan module in a different costume — a module that imports cleanly, passes its unit tests, and has zero references anywhere in the estate's settings, commands, or agents. It is not broken; it is unconnected, and a capability that is not connected is documentation. The estate has met this more than once, and the pattern is always the same: the build was verified in isolation, isolation was mistaken for integration, and the wiring was assumed rather than gated. This stage forbids the assumption by demanding the consumer's name at the moment the object is defined, when the demand is cheap, rather than when the capability is needed and the discovery is expensive.

4.5 The rule is therefore a deletion rule rather than a documentation rule, and the severity is deliberate. An object defined with no producer, or with no consumer, is deleted from the ontology — not annotated, not deferred, not tracked as future work. It is removed. An ontology is a contract about what exists, and an entry that nothing writes or nothing reads is a lie about the corpus's own shape; a lie in the contract is more dangerous than a gap in it, because the gap is visible and the lie is not. If the object is genuinely needed and its producer is genuinely future work, then the honest form of that statement is that the architecture was wrong and the producer must be planned — an amendment to the architecture, not a footnote in the ontology.

4.6 The second condition is naming: one term, one meaning, one definition, one place. The definition lives in the family's canonical ontology document, binding on every author, and a Part that redefines a term defined there, or coins a synonym for it, is rejected and rewritten rather than patched. Synonym drift is not a stylistic problem. It is the mechanism by which two modules come to name one concept twice and then diverge, and it is invisible until the divergence produces a defect whose cause is a word.

4.7 The stage inherits rather than invents wherever an inherited term exists. To speak about strength of belief in a claim, it uses the ACIS ladder from E0 to E7 and reads that level rather than assigning one; a family that grades its own evidence commits exactly the self-promotion the epistemic family exists to prevent. To speak about whether a decision is sound, reversible, proportional, or evidenced, it uses DRK's vocabulary and DRK's verdicts. To speak about whether executable reality is verified, it uses SQI's verdict ontology and invents no parallel word for green. To speak about task tiers or whether a specification is required before coding, it uses spec_gate's tiers and the sealed invariant governing them. Inheritance is not politeness toward siblings; it is the only defence against an estate in which each family speaks a private dialect and no cross-family gate can be written at all.

4.8 The stage's outputs are two artifacts, both mechanical. The producer-and-consumer table, which for every canonical object names what writes it and what reads it. And the composition declaration, which names every sibling family this corpus depends on and, for each, states the boundary: what that sibling owns, what this corpus takes from it, and what this corpus will therefore never say about it. The composition declaration is the input to the review stage's cardinal check, because a Part that re-narrates a sibling is caught by comparing what it says against what the declaration promised it would only reference.

4.9 The characteristic adversarial case is the retrofit. An ontology written after the Parts are authored is not an ontology; it is an index of the vocabulary the authors happened to use, and it will faithfully document every inconsistency it was supposed to prevent. It will also pass every mechanical check, because the terms it defines are by construction the terms in use. The detector is temporal and recorded in the manifest: the ontology stage's exit timestamp must precede the authoring stage's first Part. A corpus whose ontology was sealed after its Parts were written has not run this stage; it has narrated it.

4.10 The second adversarial case is the aspirational ontology: a document defining the objects the family wishes it had, each with a planned producer and an intended consumer. Every entry is honest as an intention and false as a contract. The producer-and-consumer rule kills it by demanding names rather than plans, and the deletion rule enforces the killing. An ontology that describes the future is a roadmap, and a roadmap mistaken for a contract is how an implementation comes to be written against objects that do not exist.

4.11 Done criteria: a producer-and-consumer table in which every canonical object has both, with no exceptions; a definitions list in which every term appears exactly once; a composition declaration naming every sibling and its boundary; an exit timestamp preceding the first authored Part; and a recorded count of objects deleted here, because a stage that has never deleted anything has almost certainly never refused anything.

— PART IV FINAL LAW —
An object with no producer is dead by starvation and an object with no consumer is documentation wearing the costume of a capability; both are deleted from the ontology rather than annotated, because a contract that lies about what exists is more dangerous than one that admits a gap.


PART V — THE AUTHORING STAGE: THE FABRICATION STANDARD AND THE DENSITY BAND

5.1 The authoring stage is where a corpus is written, and its governance is a single artifact: the fabrication standard, declared in the manifest, binding on every Part, shared across the estate rather than invented per family. The standard states the form of a dataset — one file, prose, numbered subsections, a fixed number of Parts, each closed by a stated law — and the density band each Part and each dataset must land within. The reference band derived at this family's architecture stop is eight hundred to fifteen hundred words per Part, eight to fifteen Parts, eight thousand to fifteen thousand words per dataset. A family may bind itself to a different band with a stated reason recorded in its manifest, and the verification family did exactly that. What no family may do is fail to declare one.

5.2 The band is a band and not a floor, and the ceiling is the half usually missing and always needed. A floor alone rewards volume, and volume is the cheapest thing a competent writer can produce. The ceiling exists because a Part that runs to four thousand words has almost certainly stopped being one Part; it has become two Parts sharing a heading, and the reader who needed only the second must now read the first. The ceiling is also a scope alarm. When an author cannot say what a Part must say inside it, the usual cause is not an unusually rich subject but a Part carrying material belonging to a sibling Part, or re-narrating a parent system, or padding. All three are review rejections, and the ceiling surfaces them at authoring time when they are cheap to fix.

5.3 Word count is a proxy, and this dataset states what it is a proxy for, because a proxy whose target is unstated will be optimized directly and will then measure nothing. The target is mechanism density: the count of distinct, load-bearing statements a Part makes that a reader could act on, be surprised by, or check. Words correlate with mechanism density only while the author is trying to explain a mechanism, and they decorrelate completely the moment the author is trying to reach a number. The countermetric is the restatement ratio of Part X, enforced at review: a Part inside the band with a high ratio has passed the proxy and failed the target, and review rejects it.

5.4 Why a shared standard beats ten private ones rests on four properties, none aesthetic. Comparability: two corpora at the same standard can be assessed against each other, so the estate can ask which family produced more governing knowledge per unit of effort, and can answer. Review transferability: a reviewer trained on one family's rejection criteria can review another's Parts without relearning what good looks like, which is the only thing making independent review affordable. Gate reuse: a mechanical QA gate that checks Part count, density, form, and contamination is written once and pointed at every family, whereas ten private standards require ten gates and will get one. Budgeting: a corpus of ten to fifteen Parts at eight hundred to fifteen hundred words is an estimable unit of work, so the mode can declare its cost at architecture and be held to it — the precondition for the terminating condition of Part IX. A family that will not declare a standard cannot be budgeted, and a mode that cannot be budgeted cannot be forced to end.

5.5 The authoring order is prescribed because the alternative produces divergence. The index is written first and fixes what each Part owns and what it does not. One exemplar Part is authored next, in full, to the standard, and is reviewed before the rest of the corpus is begun — an exemplar authored badly will be replicated badly. The remaining Parts are then written against the index and the exemplar. When authoring is delegated across parallel workers, each receives the index, the exemplar, the ontology, and the composition declaration, and each is required to overshoot the density floor rather than approach it, because a draft under the floor costs a rewrite and a draft over the ceiling costs an edit.

5.6 Each Part carries, where the subject admits it, a fixed set of dimensions, and their absence is a review rejection rather than a stylistic note: mission and the problem solved; principles and invariants; its own ontology and taxonomy where the canonical ontology delegates objects to it; actors and authorities, meaning who may do what and who may refuse; inputs, outputs, and data contracts; state model and lifecycle; engines, registries, and detectors, described as mechanisms and never as source code; evaluators and scoring; policies and gates; governed feedback loops; interfaces to sibling datasets, stated as cross-references; failure modes and adversarial cases; anti-patterns with evidence rather than assertion; metrics, each with a countermetric that detects it being gamed; structured examples on synthetic data; and done criteria. A Part that names no failure mode has not been thought about adversarially. A Part that names a metric with no countermetric has issued a target and invited its own corruption.

5.7 The stage forbids source code inside a dataset absolutely. A dataset is doctrine, and doctrine describes mechanisms so that many implementations can satisfy them; code is one implementation, and a doctrine that ships code has silently narrowed itself to that implementation and will be wrong the moment it changes. The prohibition is mechanically checkable, which is why it is a QA gate rather than a preference.

5.8 Padding is a rejection, and the stage defines it precisely because every author accused of it will sincerely disagree. Padding is not verbosity and not repetition for emphasis. It is the assertion of content carrying no mechanism: a paragraph restating the Part's own thesis in different words, a sentence naming a principle already named, a list of considerations with no consequence attached to any of them, an example illustrating nothing the reader did not know. The test is subtractive: remove the paragraph and ask whether any downstream reader would now do something differently. If nothing changes, the paragraph was padding, and its only function was to move a number.

5.9 The characteristic failure mode is the standard declared and then quietly relaxed to accommodate a draft. A family authors its Parts, runs its density check, finds seven Parts under the floor, and lowers the floor. Every step is reasonable and the result is a corpus with no standard at all, because a criterion that moves to fit the result being measured is not a criterion. The verification family met this exactly and refused it: its gate failed on first execution against seven Parts under the floor and one contamination hit, and both were repaired rather than legislated away. That refusal is the most valuable single event in the estate's corpus history, because it is the proof that the gate can fire.

5.10 Done criteria: every planned Part exists on disk; every Part is inside the declared density band; the dataset is inside the declared total band; the count of Parts equals the immutable count of Parts planned, or is fewer with a recorded reason; no dataset file contains code; and the exemplar Part was reviewed before the remaining Parts were authored, with the review recorded.

— PART V FINAL LAW —
A fabrication standard is shared or it is nothing, the density band is a proxy for mechanism density and must never become the target, and a criterion relaxed to accommodate the draft it was measuring has ceased to be a criterion and become a decoration.


PART VI — THE REVIEW STAGE: THE REJECTION CRITERIA AND THE CARDINAL SIN OF RE-NARRATION

6.1 Review is where a corpus is judged on meaning, by a party that did not produce it, against criteria written before the corpus existed. All three conditions are load-bearing. On meaning, because form is QA's business and a corpus can be perfectly formed and entirely empty. By a party that did not produce it, because an author reviewing their own Part is performing the second half of the authoring they already did, and their agreement with themselves carries no information. Against criteria written beforehand, because criteria invented while looking at the artifact are shaped by the artifact, and a rubric that fits the thing it grades is a certificate of nothing.

6.2 The rejection criteria are seven and closed. A Part is rejected and rewritten — never patched — if it is repetitive; if it is generic; if it is padded to a word count; if it is a summary rather than a mechanism; if it is missing its data flows, failure modes, interfaces, governance, or evaluation; if it is disconnected from its siblings; or if it re-narrates a parent system instead of cross-referencing it. The rewrite-rather-than-patch rule exists because six of the seven defects are structural. A Part that summarizes instead of explaining cannot be repaired by adding detail to a summary; the summary is the wrong artifact and its shape survives every local edit. Patching a structurally defective Part produces a longer structurally defective Part, and the density band will then certify it.

6.3 The seventh criterion is the cardinal sin, and the stage devotes the rest of its authority to it. Re-narration is the act of explaining, inside this corpus, a system a sibling family already owns. It is the most seductive defect in the catalogue because every instinct of a good author pushes toward it: the reader might not know what the decision kernel does, the Part will read better self-contained, a cross-reference is a discourtesy to a reader who now has to go and look. Every one of those instincts is correct about the reader and catastrophic about the estate.

6.4 Re-narration is catastrophic because it forks doctrine. Once this corpus contains a paragraph explaining a sibling's reversibility taxonomy, the estate has two texts describing one mechanism. They agree on the day they are written. They will not agree in a year, because the sibling will evolve its mechanism and update its own corpus, and nothing will update the paragraph in this one. From that moment there are two authorities, and a reader who consults the wrong one is not misinformed by accident but by a document the estate certified. The certification is what makes it dangerous: an uncertified wrong description is a mistake; a certified wrong description is an institution.

6.5 The cross-reference contract is therefore precise. When this corpus needs a sibling's capability, it names the sibling, states the boundary — what the sibling owns and what this corpus takes from it — and moves on. It does not summarize the sibling's mechanism for convenience, restate its taxonomy briefly, or explain its rationale so the reader has context. Each is re-narration with an apology attached. The correct form is a sentence: this capability is owned by that family, this corpus consumes its output and never re-derives it. The reader who needs the mechanism reads the family that owns it, and reads the current version, which is the entire point.

6.6 The detector runs at review rather than at QA because it requires judgment at the boundary. For every sibling named in the composition declaration, the reviewer searches this corpus for passages that explain rather than reference it. The signal is descriptive verbs about a sibling's internals — how it computes, what its stages are, what its taxonomy contains — where a legitimate cross-reference is attributive: it owns, it produces, this corpus consumes. A corpus dense in descriptive verbs about its siblings has forked doctrine and does not know it yet.

6.7 The second-most-common defect is genericity, and it survives every other check. A generic Part is one whose sentences would be true of any corpus in any domain: knowledge should be organized, standards should be clear, quality matters. Such a Part sits inside the density band, contains no code, cross-references correctly, closes with a stated law, and is worth nothing. The test is substitution: replace the family's subject with an unrelated one and ask whether the paragraph is still true. If it is, the paragraph is about writing in general rather than about this family, and it is deleted however well written it is.

6.8 The independence requirement is not a suggestion, and there is an authority to compose rather than invent: the verification family owns separation of powers for the verification axis, and its principle transfers exactly — an agent may not certify its own change. Applied to knowledge: the author of a Part may not seal it, and the certifier of a corpus may not have authored it. When separation is genuinely unavailable, the honest response is not to simulate it by having the author narrate a review of their own work, which produces the appearance of independence and licenses a stronger claim than the arrangement supports. The honest response is to record that separation did not occur and cap the certificate's strength accordingly. An unseparated review is not invalid; it is weaker, and the weakness must be visible in the certificate rather than concealed by ritual.

6.9 Review produces one of three verdicts per Part and no others. SEAL: the Part meets the criteria and contributes to the numerator. REWRITE: it fails one or more criteria and is authored again from the index, not edited. AMEND-SCOPE: it is well made but carries material belonging to a sibling Part or a sibling family, and the correction is a boundary change rather than a rewrite. The deliberate absence of a patch verdict is the stage's most important design choice, because a patch verdict is where a rejected Part goes to survive.

6.10 The characteristic adversarial case is reviewer capture, and it arrives politely. Reviewer and author work the same corpus under the same deadline toward the same certificate, and every rejection costs them both a rewrite. The gradient points at leniency, and nobody has to be dishonest for it to be followed. The countermeasure is the metric of Part X: a review stage with a zero rejection rate is not evidence of an excellent corpus but of an inert reviewer, and past a certain corpus size the two hypotheses are no longer equally likely. The estate's own history supplies the calibration — the verification family's mechanical gate refused seven Parts on its first execution, and those Parts had been authored by a competent process. A review that refuses nothing, on a first draft, is reporting on the reviewer rather than on the corpus.

6.11 Done criteria: every Part carries a recorded verdict; every rewrite has been re-reviewed; every named sibling in the composition declaration has been checked for re-narration; the reviewer is not the author of the Parts reviewed, or the absence of separation is recorded; and the rejection count is recorded, because it is the only evidence the review actually happened.

— PART VI FINAL LAW —
Re-narrating a parent system forks the doctrine into two texts that agree today and diverge forever, and the certification is what makes the divergence lethal — so a sibling's capability is named, bounded, and left where it lives, and a reviewer who refuses nothing is reporting on themselves.


PART VII — THE QA STAGE: THE MECHANICAL GATES AND THE GATE THAT HAS NEVER REFUSED

7.1 Knowledge QA is the stage the estate has never had, and its definition is narrow on purpose. QA judges form, mechanically, deterministically, without a human in the loop. It does not judge whether the corpus is right, useful, or well written; review already did all three and QA cannot do any of them. What QA can do is refuse to let a corpus advance while it violates a check a program can perform, and that single power converts a fabrication standard from an aspiration into a contract.

7.2 The gates are eight, and each is a program that can fail. The depth gate: every Part inside the declared density band, and the dataset inside the declared total band. The structure gate: the count of Parts equals the immutable count of Parts planned, the Parts appear in order, and every Part is closed by its stated law. The no-code gate: the file contains no fenced code blocks, because doctrine that ships an implementation has narrowed itself to that implementation. The form gate: the dataset is prose, carrying none of the structural markup a governance document uses and a dataset does not. The contamination gate: zero occurrences of the quarantined vocabulary — the entities, ecosystems, and commercial terminology from domains this corpus is forbidden to import. The cross-reference gate: every sibling family named actually exists on disk, so no reader is sent to a document that was never written. The reality gate: zero occurrences of the vocabulary the estate reserves for unwritten content, because a corpus that ships an unfinished paragraph with an apology inside it has shipped a defect with a label on it. And the hermetic gate: the suite runs three times with identical output and a clean exit, so a green result is a property of the corpus rather than of the moment it was measured.

7.3 The cross-reference gate deserves its own sentence, because it is what keeps composition honest. A corpus that cross-references rather than re-narrates is only better if the reference resolves. A dangling reference is worse than a re-narration: a re-narration is at least a text the reader can read, whereas a dangling reference is an instruction to consult a document that does not exist, and the reader concludes the knowledge exists somewhere and that they failed to find it. The gate is trivial to implement and it is the difference between a composition contract and a bibliography of intentions.

7.4 A gate is mechanical only if it satisfies three properties, and a check failing any of them is a review criterion masquerading as a gate. Deterministic: the same input produces the same verdict every time, which the hermetic triple-run establishes. Executable without judgment: a check requiring a human to decide whether a Part is sufficiently deep is not a gate but an opinion with a threshold attached. And capable of refusing: a check whose only possible outcome is approval is a decoration, and decorations accumulate in QA suites precisely because nobody notices a check that never fires.

7.5 The most important property of a gate is that it has been observed to refuse, and this is not a philosophical flourish. A gate that has never refused anything is in the epistemic position of a regression test never seen to fail: it may be working, it may be inert, and from outside the two are indistinguishable. The estate has a real datum. The verification family's done-gate, on its first execution against a real draft, failed two checks against real defects: seven Parts below the density floor, and one contamination hit. Both were repaired and the corpus re-verified. That failure is worth more than a hundred green runs, because it is the only evidence the estate possesses that the gate is connected to the corpus at all.

7.6 From that follows a standing obligation on every family: before a QA suite is trusted, it must be shown to fail. The demonstration is cheap. Author a synthetic Part below the floor and confirm the depth gate refuses. Insert a quarantined term into a scratch copy and confirm the contamination gate refuses. Name a sibling that does not exist and confirm the cross-reference gate refuses. A suite that passes on a corpus and also passes on a deliberately broken corpus is not protecting anything; it is reporting on nothing, and the green it produces is worse than no signal because it has been believed.

7.7 QA is not review and the two may never be merged, however tempting the economy looks. Review judges meaning and cannot be automated; QA judges form and must be. A family that runs only review has no check that can refuse without a human, and humans under deadline are exactly the population the gate exists to protect the corpus from. A family that runs only QA has a corpus perfectly shaped, correctly cross-referenced, inside every band, and possibly vacuous, because no mechanical check can detect that a Part says nothing. The two catch disjoint defect classes. The verification family's experience makes the point in one line: its mechanical gate caught seven Parts under the density floor, which no reviewer had flagged, and it could not have caught a single instance of re-narration, which no program can see.

7.8 Two characteristic failure modes, both movements of a threshold. The gate tuned to the draft: the gate fails, the threshold is lowered, the gate passes, and the corpus is certified against a standard edited by the artifact it was measuring. The countermeasure is procedural: a threshold may be changed, but never by the party currently blocked by it, never during the run it is blocking, and never without the change being recorded with its reason. A threshold loosened calmly, with a stated reason, outside the blocked task, is governance. The same threshold loosened by the author it is blocking, in the moment it blocks them, is an escape, and it is indistinguishable from one.

7.9 The second failure mode is gate accretion without gate exercise: a suite that grows a check per incident and never verifies that any of them still fire. It becomes large, slow, universally green, and unfalsifiable, and its size is mistaken for rigour. The countermeasure is the refusal ledger: every gate records when it last refused something, and a gate that has never refused anything on any corpus is a candidate for deletion rather than a source of comfort.

7.10 The synthetic example: a corpus of ten planned Parts enters QA and the depth gate refuses one Part at six hundred and forty words against a floor of eight hundred. The author's instinct is to add two paragraphs of context, which satisfies the proxy and fails the target. The stage instead mandates a return to review, because a Part that cannot reach the floor without padding has too little to say and is either mis-scoped or belongs merged into its neighbour. It is merged, Parts sealed becomes nine against a planned ten, and the certificate records the reduction with its reason. The denominator did not move. The corpus shrank, honestly, and terminated.

7.11 Done criteria: all eight gates executed; all eight green; three consecutive identical runs with a clean exit; each gate demonstrated capable of refusal on a deliberately broken input; and the refusal ledger updated.

— PART VII FINAL LAW —
A knowledge gate must be deterministic, executable without judgment, and capable of refusing — and until it has been observed to refuse a deliberately broken corpus it is indistinguishable from a decoration, because a green from an inert gate is not weak evidence but false evidence that has been believed.


PART VIII — CERTIFICATION AND FREEZE: WHAT A CERTIFICATE ASSERTS, WHAT IT REFUSES TO ASSERT, AND HOW A FROZEN CORPUS CHANGES

8.1 Certification is the act by which a corpus stops being a work in progress and becomes a substrate. It is performed by an authority that did not author the corpus, it is recorded, and it is the entry gate to the only stage from which implementation is reachable. Everything about it is designed around one risk: that a certificate will be read as asserting more than it asserts, and that an implementation will then be built on a claim the corpus never made.

8.2 A certificate asserts exactly four things, all of them statements about process. First, that the corpus met the fabrication standard it declared for itself at architecture — not a standard chosen afterward, and not one adjusted to fit the result. Second, that the QA gates passed, hermetically, three times, against a named and immutable version of the corpus. Third, that the count of Parts sealed reconciles against the immutable count of Parts planned, either by matching it or by falling short with a recorded reason naming each Part dropped. Fourth, that the corpus's scope is named: the surface it governs, stated positively, and the surfaces it does not govern, stated with the same precision.

8.3 A certificate explicitly does not assert that the doctrine inside the corpus is true. This is the most important sentence in the Part and the one a reader in a hurry will misread. Certification states that a body of knowledge was built to a declared standard, reviewed independently, and mechanically verified for form. It does not state that the knowledge is correct. A corpus can be beautifully structured, independently reviewed, perfectly gated, certified, frozen, and wrong, and nothing in this lifecycle can prevent that, because no lifecycle can. The estate has an authority for strength of belief and it is not this one: the ACIS ladder from E0 to E7 owns epistemic level, and a certified corpus may still sit low on that ladder, meaning its doctrine is stated, coherent, and entirely unproven. Certification and epistemic level are orthogonal axes, and the certificate must say so on its face, in words, so that no reader can mistake process compliance for proof.

8.4 The certificate refuses three further assertions, each with a consumer who would otherwise be misled. It does not assert that the implementation governed by the corpus will succeed; a correct science does not guarantee a correct build, and SQI owns whether the executable reality is verified. It does not assert that the corpus is complete for all time; it asserts completeness against the denominator declared at architecture, which is a much smaller and much more honest claim. And it does not assert that the corpus is the only knowledge relevant to the build; the composition declaration names the siblings it depends on, and a reader who consults only this corpus is reading a fragment by design.

8.5 Freeze follows certification immediately and is what makes the corpus usable. A frozen corpus is immutable: its text does not change, its Part boundaries do not move, its terms do not acquire new meanings, and an implementation begun against it on Monday is still governed by the same text in six months. Without the freeze, every consumer is building against a moving target, and the rational response to a moving target is to stop consulting it — which returns the estate to the state the mode was convened to fix.

8.6 The freeze is not permanence, and the amendment protocol keeps it from becoming one. Knowledge is sometimes wrong, and a doctrine that cannot be corrected is a doctrine that will be quietly ignored. An amendment is the single legal backward edge in the state machine, from FROZEN into REVIEW, and it carries five obligations. It is a recorded decision, travelling through the estate's decision authority — DRK owns that surface, and this dataset consumes its verdict rather than building a parallel court. It names what changed and why, inside the corpus, so that a reader of version two can see what version one said and why the estate stopped believing it. It carries a version, so an implementation can state which version it was built against. It re-enters QA, because an amended corpus is an unverified corpus and the gates must fire again. And it is re-certified by an authority that did not author the amendment.

8.7 The cardinal freeze violation is the silent edit: a frozen corpus changed without an amendment record, because the change is small, obviously correct, and tedious to route through a protocol. Every clause of that justification is true, and the result is still a corpus whose version number no longer identifies its content, which means no implementation can ever again state what it was built against. The countermeasure is a content hash recorded in the certificate; a frozen corpus whose hash no longer matches has been silently edited, and the detection is arithmetic. The rule carries no softening clause: an unrecorded change to a frozen corpus invalidates its certificate, and the corpus returns to REVIEW.

8.8 Amendment churn is a governed signal rather than a forbidden act. A corpus amended once has learned something. A corpus amended repeatedly, in short succession, against the same Parts, has not learned; it was certified prematurely, and the certification authority is what failed. The stage records an amendment count per corpus and treats a rising count as evidence about the certifier rather than about the authors. The inversion is deliberate: it is the only mechanism in the lifecycle holding the certification authority accountable, and without it the certifier is the one actor whose work is never graded.

8.9 The adversarial case is certification of an unimplementable corpus, and it is subtle because every gate passes. The corpus is well formed, densely written, independently reviewed, mechanically green, and describes a science no implementer can act on, because it never descended from principle to mechanism. QA cannot see this, and review can miss it when the reviewer shares the author's conceptual frame. The only detector is downstream, and it is the metric of Part X: implementation follow-through. A corpus certified, frozen, and never implemented is a false positive of the entire protocol, and DFP-05 exists precisely to count them and move the thresholds when the count is high.

8.10 The synthetic example: a corpus of eleven planned Parts seals eleven, passes QA hermetically, and is certified. The certificate names its scope, names the four siblings it composes, states that it makes no claim about the correctness of its doctrine and none about any implementation, records the content hash, and is signed by an authority that authored none of the Parts. Three months later an implementer finds one Part's stated invariant unsatisfiable in practice. The implementer does not edit the corpus. A decision is recorded, the amendment enters REVIEW, the Part is rewritten, QA fires again, and version two is certified with a note naming what version one asserted and why it was withdrawn. The implementation states which version it was built against, nothing is hidden, and the corpus is more trustworthy than before it was found to be wrong.

8.11 Done criteria: a certificate naming standard, gates, denominator reconciliation, scope, and non-assertions; a content hash; a certifier who is not an author; a freeze timestamp in the manifest; and an amendment protocol exercised at least once against a synthetic correction, because a path never walked is a path discovered to be broken at the worst possible moment.

— PART VIII FINAL LAW —
A certificate asserts that a corpus was built to its declared standard, independently reviewed, and mechanically verified — and it asserts nothing whatever about whether the doctrine inside it is true, because process compliance is not proof, and a frozen text changed without an amendment has invalidated the only thing its certificate was ever able to guarantee.


PART IX — THE TERMINATING CONDITION: HOW THE MODE IS FORCED TO END

9.1 This Part is the reason the dataset exists, and its thesis is one sentence: a mode that cannot terminate is not a mode, it is a permanent blocker wearing a lifecycle's clothes. None of the preceding stages matter if the apparatus, once entered, becomes the work. A discipline convened to prevent premature concrete, which prevents all concrete, has replaced one failure with a slower and more respectable one.

9.2 The failure has a name and a mechanism. The name is corpus inflation. The mechanism is that authoring a corpus is intrinsically rewarding, open-ended, and safe: every gap discovered suggests another Part, and no Part is ever refuted by reality, because no reality has been built yet. Compared with implementation — difficult, visibly failing, producing defects with the author's name on them — the corpus is a warm room, and the door out of it opens onto weather. The mode must be built to push its participants out, because nothing in their incentives will.

9.3 The first mechanism is the immutable denominator, declared at architecture and never raised. Completion is Parts sealed over Parts planned. A denominator that can grow is a completion metric defeatable without a single dishonest act: the family authors ten of its ten planned Parts, discovers three more that genuinely ought to exist, raises the plan to thirteen, and is now at ten of thirteen. Every one of those three Parts is a good idea. The corpus is further from completion than when it started, and will remain so indefinitely, because the same discovery process that produced three will produce three more. Immutability does not deny that the three are good. It denies them a home in this corpus, and routes them to a sibling family with its own architecture stage and its own denominator, where they must justify themselves from scratch against a fresh scan.

9.4 The moving-denominator detector is arithmetic and it is the mode's primary alarm. The manifest records the count of Parts planned at architecture; any subsequent write that raises it is a violation, recorded and surfaced, not silently accepted. The detector also watches a subtler form: Part splitting. A family that cannot raise its denominator can still inflate its numerator by dividing one planned Part into two authored Parts, sealing both, and reporting progress. The countermeasure is the density band's floor and ceiling working together: two Parts carved from one will each sit near or below the floor, and the depth gate will refuse them.

9.5 The second mechanism is the declared budget. The mode states, at architecture, what it will cost: how many datasets, how many Parts, and therefore how much work. The declaration is a forecast and will be imperfect; its imperfection is not the point, its existence is. A mode with no declared cost cannot overrun, a mode that cannot overrun cannot escalate, and a mode that cannot escalate has no mechanism by which anyone outside it is ever told that it is not ending. When the work exceeds the budget by a stated margin, the escalation goes to the Owner as a decision with three options: extend the budget with a reason, shrink the corpus to fit, or abandon the mode. What it must never do is silently absorb the overrun into more corpus, which is the failure disguised as diligence.

9.6 The third mechanism is the single-open-corpus rule. The family's signal table carries a deferral signal that fires when another family is mid-build with unsealed Parts, because concurrent open lifecycles compete for the same review attention, the same certification authority, and the same Owner, and none of them finishes. The rule is not absolute and this family is the counterexample: it was founded on a day when another corpus was open with zero sealed Parts, under an explicit Owner override. The override was recorded as the family's first calibration datum, so that if parallel open corpora later prove to degrade both, the evidence that the estate knew and chose is on file. A protocol whose first act is to exempt itself silently is a fraud; one that exempts itself loudly and writes the receipt is a science.

9.7 The fourth mechanism is the asymmetry of scope changes, and it makes the others enforceable. Shrinking is always legal and cheap: fewer Parts, recorded reason, certificate names the reduction. Growing is never legal in flight. The asymmetry is structural rather than a preference for small corpora: shrinking moves the corpus toward termination and growing moves it away, and a system whose escape hatches all open in the same direction will eventually be pushed through one. Make the cheap direction the exit.

9.8 The seven gaming signals are enumerated because a governance mode is gamed by sincere participants rather than by cynics, and the sincerity is what makes the signals necessary. The moving denominator. Part splitting. Word-count padding, caught by the restatement ratio at review. Self-certification, caught by the separation requirement and the certificate's recorded signatory. The perpetual review loop, a corpus cycling between review and authoring indefinitely, each cycle producing genuine improvement and never a certificate, caught by time-in-stage against the declared budget. The one-more-Part reflex, the discovery at certification that one further Part would complete the corpus — which is always true, of every corpus, forever, and is therefore not information. And certification of an unimplementable corpus, caught only downstream by counting how many certified corpora are never implemented.

9.9 The forced end is a rule with teeth. The only exit that counts is IMPLEMENTATION. A corpus that reaches CERTIFIED and FROZEN and is never implemented has not succeeded; it is a false positive of the protocol, and the protocol must count it as one. The clause is deliberately harsh, because it is the only one connecting the corpus to a cost. Without it, the mode's success metric is corpora certified — a metric the mode can satisfy by writing corpora, which is the tautology every bureaucracy eventually becomes. With it, the metric is implementations governed, which the mode cannot satisfy alone and must hand back to the estate. The calibration family owns the counting; this dataset owns the demand that the count exists.

9.10 The abort path is legal, honourable, and recorded. The mode may end in abandonment at any stage: the sufficiency verdict was wrong, priorities moved, the science proved smaller than expected, or an experiment answered the question the corpus was convened to research. Abandonment writes the outcome into the necessity record that opened the mode, and that record becomes calibration evidence — an abandoned corpus is data, not waste. A mode with no honourable exit produces dishonourable ones: corpora quietly stopped and never recorded, which teaches the estate nothing and hides the protocol's error rate from the only mechanism capable of correcting it.

9.11 The routing-around test is the mode's ultimate self-check, and it inverts the usual direction of blame. If the Owner or an agent begins bypassing the mode — building without a corpus for work the protocol says needs one, and being right to do so — the defect is in the mode, not in the party bypassing it. The correct response is never to enforce harder. It is to measure the bypass rate, hand it to the calibration science, and let the thresholds move. If the evidence eventually shows the mode's demands never paid for themselves, the protocol must be able to recommend its own retirement, and that verdict must be reachable by some possible evidence set — a property the family's gates assert rather than assume.

9.12 Done criteria: an immutable denominator, recorded and audited; a declared budget with an overrun margin and an escalation path naming the Owner; a deferral signal for concurrent open corpora, with any override written down; a shrink path cheaper than a grow path; a detector for each of the seven gaming signals; an implementation follow-through count; and an abort path that writes an outcome rather than a silence.

— PART IX FINAL LAW —
Knowledge Infrastructure Mode must be as good at ending as it is at starting: the denominator is immutable, the budget is declared, the only exit that counts is implementation, abandonment is recorded as data rather than hidden as failure, and a mode that is routed around has been proven defective by the party routing around it — because a lifecycle that cannot terminate is a permanent blocker wearing a lifecycle's clothes.


PART X — FAILURE MODES, ADVERSARIAL CASES, METRICS WITH COUNTERMETRICS, AND DONE CRITERIA

10.1 The failure modes divide into three families, each with a different detector. Failures of entry: the mode is activated when it should not have been, or not activated when it should have been. Failures of process: the mode runs but a stage is narrated rather than executed. Failures of exit: the mode runs correctly and will not stop. Nearly all the attention a governance system receives goes to the first family, and nearly all the damage comes from the third.

10.2 The entry failures are two. The false positive is a corpus demanded for a task a specification would have governed perfectly well — the bureaucratic failure, the one the estate feels as a tax. Its detector is downstream: a corpus certified, frozen, and then found by the implementer to have added nothing a specification would not have supplied. The estate already owns the specification gate and its tiers, sealed as an invariant, and a specification-sized task routed through an eight-stage lifecycle is the purest form of this failure. The false negative is the reverse: a build proceeds directly, encodes a permanent decision, and the estate later finds it had no science with which to make it; its detector is the rework, expensive because the decision is already encoded in artifacts. Both rates are counted by the calibration family, and neither is countable unless the necessity record carried a prediction — which is why a record without one is inadmissible as evidence.

10.3 The process failures are the narrated stage. A family that reports itself in review, but whose reviewer authored the Parts. A family that reports its ontology sealed, but sealed it after the Parts were written. A family that reports QA green, from a gate that has never refused anything. In each case the artifact of the stage exists and the stage did not happen. The detector is that every stage here leaves a recorded residue — a timestamp, a signatory, a rejection count, a refusal ledger — and a stage whose residue is missing or implausible did not run. A review with zero rejections on a first draft; an ontology whose exit timestamp follows its corpus's first Part; a QA suite with an empty refusal ledger. Implausibility is the signal.

10.4 The adversarial cases are those where a sincere participant defeats the system without lying. The author who reaches the density floor by restatement, believing they are adding clarity. The reviewer who approves a peer's Part because rejecting it costs them both a week. The architect who declares twenty planned Parts because the subject seems large, committing the corpus to manufacturing material for a number chosen in ignorance. The family that cross-references, in good faith, a sibling expected to exist soon. The certifier who is also an author because nobody else was available. The frozen corpus edited by one character to fix a typographical error, invalidating its hash and therefore its certificate, unnoticed because the edit was obviously correct. Not one requires bad intent, every one is caught by a mechanical residue check, and none is caught by asking the participant whether they did it.

10.5 The metrics are stated with their countermetrics, and a metric shipped without one is a target issued with an invitation to corrupt it. Words per Part measures nothing alone; its countermetric is the restatement ratio, the proportion of a Part's assertions already appearing elsewhere in the corpus, and a Part inside the band with a high ratio has optimized the proxy and abandoned the target. Parts sealed over Parts planned measures completion; its countermetric is the denominator immutability audit, because the ratio is defeatable by raising the denominator. Time in stage measures throughput; its countermetric is the rejection rate, because a corpus that clears review in an afternoon with zero rejections has not been reviewed. Cross-reference count measures composition; its countermetric is the resolvable-cross-reference ratio, because the first is maximized by naming siblings that do not exist. Reuse events, read from the knowledge graph, measure whether a sealed corpus is consulted; its countermetric requires the reuse to be by a sibling rather than by the family itself, because a family citing its own corpus proves only that it remembers writing it. Certification rate measures the mode's output; its countermetric is implementation follow-through, because a mode that certifies corpora nobody implements has optimized its throughput while producing nothing — and that single countermetric is the difference between a discipline and a bureaucracy.

10.6 Two further metrics govern the authorities rather than the authors. Amendment churn per certified corpus grades the certifier: a corpus amended three times in a month against the same Parts was certified prematurely, and the failure belongs to whoever certified it. Gate refusal recency grades the gate: a check that has refused nothing on any corpus across the estate is a candidate for deletion. Both exist because every other metric here grades the people doing the work, and a governance system that grades only the governed is auditor capture with better manners.

10.7 The structured example, on synthetic data, walks a clean pass. A family proposes five datasets; the scan returns two new, two extend, one reference. Two datasets are approved at ten and twelve planned Parts. The ontology stage defines nine objects, deletes two for having no producer, and names a consumer for each of the seven that remain. Authoring produces the index, one exemplar Part reviewed before the rest, then the remaining twenty. Review seals nineteen, rewrites two, and amends the scope of one that had drifted into a sibling's territory. QA refuses once, on a Part below the floor; it is merged rather than padded, and the corpus certifies at twenty-one sealed against twenty-two planned, with the reduction named. Freeze is recorded with a hash, implementation begins six days after the mode was entered, and the necessity record's prediction awaits an outcome the calibration family will score.

10.8 The counter-example, same family, run without this dataset. The scan is skipped and all five datasets are approved as new. No denominator is declared. Authoring produces material of uneven density, some Parts at four hundred words and some at four thousand. Two Parts explain a sibling's mechanism in full, helpfully. Review is performed by the authors, who approve everything. There is no QA, because there is no declared standard to check against. The corpus is declared finished by the person who wrote it, and never frozen, so an implementer consulting it in month two is reading a text that changed in month three. Nothing here is dishonest and every step is the natural thing to do, which is precisely why the lifecycle must be a gate rather than an intention.

10.9 The interfaces to the siblings, restated once as a closing contract. The decision to convene a corpus travels through DRK. The epistemic level of anything the corpus asserts is read from ACIS and never assigned here. Whether an implementation built against the corpus works is SQI's jurisdiction, and no certificate this family issues makes any claim about it. Whether a proposed dataset duplicates an existing one is D2A's verdict, consumed at architecture. Where knowledge lives, how it is navigated, and the reuse counts these metrics depend on are graphify's. Whether a task needs a specification before coding is the specification gate's, sealed as an invariant, and a task that gate already governs must never be routed through this mode. This dataset composes all six and forks none.

10.10 The done criteria for the mode itself. The eight stages exist, each with an entry gate and an exit gate that is either mechanical or leaves a residue proving it ran. The denominator is declared once, immutable, audited. Knowledge QA, Knowledge Certification, and Knowledge Freeze now have definitions, mechanisms, authorities, and detectors. A certificate names what it asserts and what it refuses to assert. A frozen corpus changes only through a recorded amendment, and the hash proves it. The mode has a declared budget, an escalation path, an abort path that writes data rather than silence, and a success metric it cannot satisfy alone. Every metric ships with a countermetric. If a future audit finds any one of those false, the corpus is amended rather than defended, because a lifecycle that will not submit to its own protocol has no standing to impose it on anyone.

— PART X FINAL LAW —
Every metric in this mode ships with the countermetric that detects it being gamed, every authority is graded by a metric it does not control, and the mode's own success is measured not by the corpora it certifies but by the implementations they govern — because a discipline whose success metric it can satisfy alone has already become the bureaucracy it was built to prevent.
